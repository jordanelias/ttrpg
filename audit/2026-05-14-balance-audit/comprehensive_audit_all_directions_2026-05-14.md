# §1 ALL-DIRECTIONS METHODOLOGY MAPPING

Six analytical directions from project canon (`canon_terms: all directions`). Each direction asks a different question of the same audit work.

## §1.1 Top-Down (system → faction → mechanic)

**Question:** Does the macro-system architecture coherently produce balanced gameplay?

**Method:** Start from Universal Peninsular Sovereignty (§6.1), trace through faction-level victory paths, down to per-mechanic effects.

**Findings:**
- Universal Sovereignty + Accord ≥ 2 + Turmoil ≤ 6 produces near-zero direct sovereignty rate (2.8% in canon)
- Faction victory paths (Parts 3-5) directionally correct but magnitudes off (top-down predicted Church 60-70%, bottom-up showed 51.4%)
- Mechanic-level: Ecclesiastical Appointment fires too often (per-season vs every-other-arc); Crown Treaty has no expiration; Varfell has zero non-military acquisition

**Applied parts:** 1 (errata), 3 (top-down re-sim), 4 (NERS), 5 (throughlines top-down), 11 (PP-674 vetting)

## §1.2 Bottom-Up (mechanic interactions → emergent outcomes)

**Question:** What outcomes emerge when canonical mechanics interact autonomously?

**Method:** Monte Carlo agent-based simulation with utility-scored action selection, dice-resolved outcomes, per-season propagation.

**Findings:**
- v3 (with 10 rule errors): Church 82.8% — refuted Part 5's top-down 70% prediction
- v5 (canonical-corrected): Church 51.4% — established empirical baseline
- v8 (4 analog cards): Church 62.4% WORSE — refuted symmetric-buff hypothesis
- v9 (integrated fixes): Crown 69.0% — Q-21 over-corrected
- v11 + 50s (final): Crown 55.8 / Church 22.0 / Hafenmark 11.8 / Varfell 10.4

**Applied parts:** 6 (v3), 7 (v5), 8 (sensitivity), 9 (decoupling), 12 (v8 refutation), 13 (v9-v11)

## §1.3 Vertical (scale-transitions)

**Question:** Do consequences cross scales coherently? Where does scale-collapse occur?

**Method:** Trace single events across scales: character → settlement → territory → faction → peninsula.

**Findings:**
- Faction-scale wins ≠ character-scale survival. Almud personally deposed in 16% of canon campaigns; 56% at 60-season campaigns.
- 4.6% of Crown faction-scale wins are "pyrrhic" (Almud deposed before victory).
- Deposition modes: Mandate-collapse 12% / Excommunication 2.8% / Proclaimed-against 1.2%
- Crown Initiative Mode III (Coronation Renewal) addresses Excommunication recovery — closes a vertical-scale gap.
- Settlement-level effects (Order, Prosperity, Defense from threadwork) cascade upward but were not modeled in this audit (out of scope).

**Applied parts:** 9 (decoupling), 10 (Crown Initiative Mode III)

## §1.4 Diagonal (faction × mechanic × time)

**Question:** How do faction power trajectories evolve over campaign time? Do mechanics asymmetrically benefit some factions in certain time-windows?

**Method:** Per-faction, per-season state tracking across simulator runs; campaign-length sensitivity.

**Findings:**
- 24-season campaign: Crown 75.4% (early-game advantage compounds)
- 36-season: Crown 33-69% (depends on consent)
- 50-season: Crown 55-65%, others gain ground (slow-compounding mechanics catch up)
- 60-season: Almud deposition 56% (character-scale erosion accelerates late)

**Diagonal insight:** Mechanics have implicit time-windows. Crown Treaty front-loads; Charter of Liberties back-loads; Vaynard's Settlement requires prior conquest (mid-game). Campaign length is a hidden balance lever.

**Applied parts:** 8 (Q-4 length sensitivity), 9 (Almud arc), 13 (config sweeps)

## §1.5 Lateral (faction-to-faction comparison)

**Question:** Are faction surfaces symmetrically capable? Where do power-budget mismatches occur?

**Method:** Cross-faction tabulation of card/action surfaces; per-faction L-recovery rate; per-faction acquisition vector.

**Findings:**

| Faction | L-gain mechanics | Acquisition vector | Defensive surface |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Crown | Royal Charter (W-pipeline), [PROPOSED: Crown Initiative] | Crown Treaty (consensual) | Royal Guard, Royal Decree |
| Church | **Ecclesiastical Appointment per-season** (too frequent), Piety Spread | Mass Seizure (compounded), CR via PT | Inquisitor, Excommunication |
| Hafenmark | Parliamentary Session (1×/arc) | Dynastic Proclamation (slot-capped, Appease-vulnerable) | Diplomatic Tokens (offensive currency) |
| Varfell | Tribune Network (rare), [GAP] | **Military Conquest only** (post-CR-STRIKE) | Mil pool |

**Lateral insight:** Church surface density is 2-3× competitors. Per-season action density matters as much as per-card effect.

**Applied parts:** 7 (canonical baseline), 12 (v8 refutation), 13 (integrated solution)

## §1.6 Horizontal (faction × pressure-state × decision-window)

**Question:** Do factions face equivalent pressure-states with equivalent decision-windows? Is "forced choice" (М-6) symmetric?

**Method:** Stratified sim analysis by Turmoil/Strain state; per-faction action-availability in high-pressure vs low-pressure states.

**Findings:**
- Canonical Turmoil mean: 30+ (chronic strain — М-1 Pressure-continuous validated)
- High-pressure decisions: Church's Active Inquisition fires more in high-MS states (advantage)
- Low-pressure decisions: Crown's Treaty path works best in low-Turmoil states (early game)
- Hafenmark's Charter of Liberties needs ≥2 Tokens (offensive currency) — competes with DP need for same Tokens (М-6 forced choice asymmetric: Hafenmark forced into self-cannibalization)

**Horizontal insight:** Forced-choice asymmetry. Church gets bonuses for being in strain; Hafenmark gets penalties for using own currency for both offense and codification.

**Applied parts:** 8 (sensitivity), 12 (cannibalization finding)

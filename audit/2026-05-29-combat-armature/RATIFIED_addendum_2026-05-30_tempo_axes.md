## A1 — Armour σ-tempo penalty (the one new combat lever)
Armour imposes a modest **initiative/tempo δσ penalty**, scaled by its (canonical) per-action Stamina drain (a weight proxy): heavier armour = a step slower to act. This is **not** immobilising (plate was mobile — historically accurate); it is the cost that makes armour a trade-off — you spend **tempo** (the currency of the unarmoured duel) to buy **mitigation** (the currency of the armoured battlefield). Seed magnitude `0.5·minor` per drain point (sim-tunable Class-C).
- **Status:** ratified as the armour combat-cost lever. Magnitude remains calibration.

## A2 — Material + coverage axes (the generative structure)
Armour gains two axes beneath the canonical four tiers:
- **material** (none/cloth/mail/plate) — drives the **(attack-head × material) mitigation matrix**, which *reproduces* the ratified weapon-vs-armour table exactly (no retcon) and explains it (cut deflected by plate, point finds gaps, blunt transmits; mail stops the slash but bursts to the thrust).
- **coverage** (partial/full) — gap exposure; partial protects the vital zone only.
The four canonical tiers (None/Light/Medium/Heavy) are **presets** in this space; off-preset combos (breastplate, mail shirt, three-quarter harness) are now expressible. No canonical value changes — the matrix regenerates the table; the structure is the addition.
- **Status:** ratified as the armour substrate. Which off-preset armours to instantiate is a content call. The over-engineering guard holds: **no per-body-part hit-location system** — coverage (partial/full) is the abstraction.

## A. Corrections (these override the main report)

**A1 — [SIGNIFICANT] Instance B is NOT a limiting case of Instance A.** The main report (§1, §2 Lens 1, and the "one verified σ engine" framing) said the two rolling engines "may be two faces of one parametric kernel," with B as A's no-pool/bare-stat limit bridged by the μ-shift≡Ob-shift identity. **This is falsified by live code.** Instance A's success curve is a *Gaussian CDF* `P_A = Φ((μ·N − Ob + boost)/σ_N)` (`m1_dice_sigma_core.py` p_success); Instance B is a *clamped-linear ramp* `P_B = clamp(0.50 + 0.10·M, 0.05, 0.90)` (`domain_action_resolver_spec.md` §1) — **different curve families**. Taking A to no-pool yields the degenerate narrow Gaussian (P≈0.069 at 2D/Ob3) that **B was built to replace** (P=0.300). The μ-shift≡Ob-shift identity is a symmetry *internal to A* (two ways to inject δ into the same draw; they diverge off the success line — μ = floor protection, Ob = ceiling inflation), **not a bridge to B** (B has no net, no Ob, no σ_N to shift). **Therefore two kernels is the minimum, not a collapse target.** Forcing them together is the canonical over-correction (NERS-N/E fail; `domain_action_resolver_spec.md` §5/§8). The Engine-Selection Rule (≥5D + setup axis → A; bare 1–7D pivotal → B) is *principled*, not arbitrary.
  - **What survives:** `dice_pool` and `d_sigma` ARE one engine — Instance A under two *render modes* (discrete/TTRPG, continuous/Godot), "interchangeable specifications of the same distribution" (`params/core.md`, sim-validated 5–17D). So the enum collapses **7 → 6**: fold `dice_pool`+`d_sigma` into `instance_a` (+ a `render` flag); drop `armature_dot_product` as a resolver (it's the substrate interpretation primitive — RU-4); keep **two distinct rolling kernels A and B**, plus `deterministic_accounting`, `clock_advance`, `state_reader`, `manifest`.

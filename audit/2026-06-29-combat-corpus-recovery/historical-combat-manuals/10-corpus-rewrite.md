# The Dueling-Combat / Martial-Traditions Corpus — Rewrite

This supersedes `07-consolidated-corpus.md`. It integrates the evidence base (`08`) and the adversarial throughline evaluation (`09`), cites the academic field the earlier rounds had ignored, fixes the scope creep, and recalibrates every claim to its support. It is deliberately **leaner** than the document it replaces: the central lesson of the adversarial pass was that the corpus's rhetoric ran inversely to its evidence, so the rewrite leads with what survives and states plainly what does not.

**Two things changed structurally.** (1) The comparative frame is no longer the home-grown "four-axis lens" but Wetzler's five *dimensions of meaning*, the field-standard heuristic. (2) The per-tradition catalogue is compressed into a reliability table keyed to source tier (doc 08's S1–S5), because re-listing 25 traditions at uniform confidence was itself the original error.

**Source-quality tiers (carried from doc 08):** S1 primary/critical edition · S2 peer-reviewed · S3 specialist reference/curated transcription · S4 general reference · S5 blog/federation/vendor. **Rule:** a claim's grade cannot exceed its best source. Per-tradition richness (does the tradition leave adjudicable texts) and per-claim source quality (what was actually cited) are tracked separately.

**Verification status of the anchors:** Anglo 2000, al-Sarraf 2004, Wetzler 2015 & 2016, Hobsbawm & Ranger 1983, Bowman 2021, Elias & Dunning 1986 were verified in search this session. Shahar 2008, Zarrilli 1998, Desch-Obi 2008, Kennedy & Guo, Lorge are cited from established bibliography, **not re-read this session** — treat as "S2-expected" until checked against the texts.

## 1. Framing — the project sits inside an existing field

There is a discipline for this: martial arts studies (the journal *Martial Arts Studies*, Cardiff UP; *Acta Periodica Duellatorum*; Bowman, Judkins, Wetzler, Jaquet) and, for the European material, Anglo's *The Martial Arts of Renaissance Europe* (Yale 2000) — "the most important work on historical fencing in a century." The corpus's deepest defect was theorising cross-tradition patterns without consulting this field; the rewrite corrects that.

**The comparative frame.** Wetzler (2015, *Martial Arts Studies* 1) gives the field's working heuristic: any martial practice can be read along five **dimensions of meaning** — (i) preparation for violent conflict, (ii) play / competitive sport, (iii) performance, (iv) transcendent/spiritual goals, (v) health care — explicitly "always provisional," and the field warns against fixed cross-tradition taxonomies (Bowman/Judkins: the "essential impossibility of establishing fixed referential categories"). This replaces the corpus's weapon/technique/judgment/conditioning axes, which conflated physical levels and are non-standard. The dimensions are descriptive lenses, not a classification of essences.

**The comparative method's value and its hazard.** Comparison brings the social, economic, and combat-functional variables of a single tradition into focus that an isolated study cannot (Wetzler 2016, *APD* 4(2)). Its standing hazard, stated by the same author, is that "none of us can be experts in everything" — which, at 22 languages and 1–2 searches per tradition, is the corpus's exact exposure, and the source of its selection-effect "universals."

### Scope corrections (category errors fixed)
- ***Futuwwa* / *javānmardī* is not a tradition; it is the shared ethical substructure** beneath the *zurkhāneh* and *furūsiyya* (documented in Ibn al-Miʿmar, ed. Jawad 1958). Removed from the tradition list; retained as substructure.
- **The Russian shashka is a weapon-and-drill case, not a fencing system** — guardless, one-cut, codified as *rubka* drill plus a separate pike treatise. It belongs in the discussion of the weapon/system distinction (§3), not among dueling traditions.
- **The *Dhanurveda* is a textual knowledge-category**, embedded in the *Agni Purāṇa* and medieval *saṃhitā*, not the lineage of the living South Asian arts. Filed as text, not practice.

## 3. Throughlines, rewritten and cited

Per the adversarial pass (doc 09), the claims are reordered so that confidence tracks evidence. Each is cited; the demotions are stated, not hidden.

### The two defensible headline claims
**(a) The fight book is a cross-cultural genre, not a European peculiarity.** Manuals combining text and image to teach close combat exist in China, Japan, Korea, and India as well as Europe (Wetzler 2016). This is the corpus's soundest comparative claim — and it is Wetzler's, not the corpus's; it is stated here as the corrected headline, properly attributed. *[S2; verified]*

**(b) The manual is a mnemonic aid for initiates, not a self-contained system.** Across the German *Zettel*, the Chinese manuals (which "were largely useless unless one had already trained in that school… shorthand notes, mnemonic" — Wetzler 2016, citing Kennedy & Guo), and the koryū *densho*, the text records cues for people who already train; it does not encode a recoverable system. This reframes the whole corpus: if manuals are mnemonic, "documented" traditions are far less recoverable from their texts than any documented/oral split implies — which partly dissolves the classification axis the earlier rounds leaned on. Promoted to co-headline. *[S2; verified via Wetzler 2016]*

### Legitimate but unremarkable
**(c) Patronage shapes the texts.** Masters wrote for patrons; furūsiyya treatises were commissioned by sultans and emirs (al-Sarraf 2004); the European patron–master economy is documented (Anglo 2000). Standard cultural history, soundly sourced. *[S2; verified]*
**(d) Source-language philology is a real evidence stratum** — technical vocabulary, dialectology, the Latin substrate in Byzantine Greek. Standard method, legitimate. The earlier "idiom fossil" flourish (*matrak geçmek* etc.) is withdrawn: folk etymologies are unreliable and cannot bear evidentiary weight. *[S2 for method]*
**(e) Lineage confers legitimacy** — genealogies function as "artificial kinship systems" (Judkins & Nielson 2015). Retained; the four-stage founder→glossator→consolidator→polemicist template is discarded as an imposed artifact that overfits oral and reconstructed traditions. *[S2; cited]*

### Cautions, not findings
**(f) A famous weapon is not a fighting system** (the shashka case). A real caution against conflating the two, but near-trivial when generalised; best expressed as: a "weapon-and-drill" tradition is one where Wetzler's *preparation-for-conflict* dimension dominates and *play/sport* is absent.
**(g) The documented↔oral↔reconstructed spectrum** is a usable heuristic but not a structural discovery; it restates the invented/organic distinction and inherits its unresolved "no clear empirical threshold for historicity" critique. Demoted from "principal contribution."

### Demoted — restatements or selection effects
**(h) "Demystification of founding legends" is the invention of tradition** (Hobsbawm & Ranger 1983), already the founding move of martial arts studies (Bowman 2021; Inoue 1998; Judkins & Nielson 2015) and older still in philology (Valla, 1440). The corpus *instantiates* this framework; it did not discover it. Nationalist heritage-marketing is one of its sub-cases, not a separate finding. *[S2; verified]*
**(i) "Counterattack is the prestigious mode across traditions"** is a projection from three well-documented traditions (German, Italian, Japanese); the deep revision already flagged it. Retained only as a European/Japanese observation.
**(j) The "training-surrogate convergence" is demoted to an untested hypothesis.** That lethal weapons are trained with safe proxies is near-tautological and is already covered by *sportization* (Elias & Dunning 1986); the original element — that the surrogate's form encodes the weapon-theory — is unverified and has a counterexample-in-spirit in Sheard's finding that boxing gloves, a "safety" surrogate, can increase danger (Sheard 1997). *[S2; verified]*
**(k) The four-axis lens** is replaced by Wetzler's five dimensions (§1). *[S2; verified]*

### The honest meta-lesson
The corpus's recurring failure was the **selection-effect universal**: a pattern visible in the best-documented traditions projected onto all, unchecked because the field was not read. Its defensible output is modest and mostly known; its original-sounding claims are mostly restatements or artifacts. The corrective is not more breadth but goal-relative comparison against the literature (Wetzler 2015).

## 4. Evidence base and open gaps

The reliable evidentiary core is the S2-anchored subset in §2; everything else is provisional. **Two defects are not repaired by this rewrite and are stated plainly:** (1) **provenance** — the original documents (01, the deep revision, the throughlines doc) were never re-read; their content in 07 was reconstructed from conversation snippets, and that is not fixed here. (2) **No physical/forensic stream** — skeletal-trauma, metallurgical, and typological evidence (e.g., the Towton 1461 osteological dataset) is absent; a genuinely evidence-led corpus would add it as an independent leg rather than resting on texts and images.

**Priority gaps (no S1/S2 anchor located):** Polish szabla, Hungarian sabre, Ottoman matrak, Thai krabi-krabong, Sikh gatka, Persian *zurkhāneh* (start with *Encyclopaedia Iranica*), and the Sanskrit/Tamil texts read directly rather than via secondary description. Unsearched in-language: sub-Saharan/West African (Desch-Obi 2008 is the entry point), Vietnamese/Khmer/Burmese, Norse saga scholarship, Latin (I.33, Vadi).

## 5. What an actually novel contribution would require

Because the corpus mostly re-derived the field, originality would have to come from one of these, none of which it has done:
1. **A source-tier-controlled comparison** — re-run the comparison holding source quality constant (S1/S2 only), to see which "throughlines" survive when the S4/S5 traditions are excluded. The prediction from §3 is that most do not.
2. **Testing the mnemonic-manual claim** against primary texts across traditions — does the degree of "mnemonic-ness" correlate with anything (transmission mode, weapon, patronage)? This is the one promoted throughline (3b) and is genuinely under-studied comparatively.
3. **Integrating the forensic leg** (osteology, metallurgy) that the textual corpus structurally omits — the only stream that can test what the manuals *claim* against what bodies and blades show.
4. **A focused, goal-relative comparison** of two or three traditions on one of Wetzler's dimensions, done deeply, rather than a 25-tradition universalising survey — the move the field's own methodology recommends.

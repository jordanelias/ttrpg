# A Comparative Morphology of Martial Traditions — Volume II

**Expanded Corpus · New Throughlines · The Governing Analogues · Extended Combinatorial Logic**

*Companion to Volume I (`martial_morphology.md`). Continues its part-numbering (I–V → VI–XI) and reuses its primitive scheme, its meta-throughlines A–F, and its epistemic tags **[M]** material / **[B]** belief-phenomenological / **[L]** literary-mythic.*

---

## Orientation

Two charges from the request, both honored directly:

1. **Deepen the corpus, European and Asian, bias welcome, retellings admitted.** Volume I held two standing caveats — archival bias (spiritual framing over-represented because it was written down) and canon-relativity. The user has explicitly retired both for this volume. The corpus below therefore leans hard into the documented and semi-documented Euro-Asian canon and admits legendary attributions and retellings *as such*, tagged but no longer apologized for. A founding legend (Zhang Sanfeng, Ng Mui, the willow of Yōshin-ryū) is carried as a *founding charter* — culturally load-bearing, epistemically marked.

2. **Extend the logic around the analogue axes — mathematics/geometry, dance, and the other cited analogues.** Volume I repeatedly invoked governing analogues (Destreza's geometry, capoeira's dance, the berimbau's music, the *marma* map's medicine, neidan's alchemy) without theorizing them. This volume makes that seam its centerpiece: the **governing analogue** — the discipline a tradition takes as its master-metaphor — is shown to be the generative substrate *beneath* the meta-throughlines, and the combinatorial logic of Part V is extended around it. This is the heart of the volume.

# PART VI — Expanded Corpus

## VI.1 Europe, deeper

**The Bolognese (Dardi) school.** Between the medieval longsword and the rapier sits the rich Bolognese tradition — Antonio Manciolino, Achille Marozzo (*Opera Nova*, 1536), Angelo Viggiani — teaching the *spada da lato* (sidesword) alone and paired with buckler, dagger, cape, and rotella, plus the two-handed *spadone* and polearms. It is the connective tissue of Italian swordsmanship, dense with named guards and a transitional cut-and-thrust logic.

**The geometric apex — Thibault.** Gérard Thibault d'Anvers's *Academy of the Sword* (1628) is the furthest reach of geometric fencing and the keystone exhibit for the mathematics analogue. Thibault builds his entire system on the **Mysterious Circle**, whose diameter equals the individual swordsman's own height; the body is inscribed in the circle in Vitruvian fashion, and *every* measure, step, and engagement is derived from the proportions of that particular man. The art is, literally, geometry custom-fitted to the body — the human figure as the generative axiom of the whole science.

**The German armory deepened.** Johannes Lecküchner's *Messer* treatise (c. 1482) is the longest medieval work on a single weapon, transposing Liechtenauer's longsword logic onto the single-edged knife. The *dussack* serves as Meyer's practice-saber. *Kampfringen* — armored and judicial wrestling — runs beneath it all, including the grotesque staged duels of Talhoffer (the man-in-a-pit against the woman-with-a-sling, the shield-and-club judicial combats), and the wrestling of Master Ott.

**The English martial guild.** England formalized its art through the **Company of Maisters of the Science of Defence**, chartered under Henry VIII, with a graded prize-playing system — Scholar, Free Scholar, Provost, Master — each rank won by public bouts (the "prize"). George Silver's "true and false times," Joseph Swetnam (1617), and later the singlestick, backsword, and cudgel feed into the 18th-century academies of James Figg and the marriage of fencing and pugilism that became prizefighting.

**The stick and knife of the European margins.** Highland broadsword and *targe* with Donald McBane (*The Expert Sword-Man's Companion*, 1728); Irish *bataireacht* (the *shillelagh*, the faction fights); Portuguese *jogo do pau* (staff); and the contested, semi-fabricated-but-living *navaja* and *baratero* knife culture of Spain — exactly the kind of retelling now admitted.

**The French school.** *Savate* and *chausson* (the kickboxing of sailors and the street, later codified), *la canne de combat* (the walking-stick art), the *bâton français*, and the academic smallsword fencing whose pedagogy — Domenico Angelo's *L'École des armes* (1763) — turned the killing art into a gentleman's exercise and the seed of sport.

**The Slavic and Caucasian field (richly retold).** The Polish *szabla* and the winged-hussar lance (the "cross cut," the *husaria*); the Cossack *shashka*, the guardless saber of spinning, flowing play; the Hungarian saber; modern *systema* with its claimed Cossack and folk roots; and the wonderful, under-documented **Khevsur** duelists of the Georgian Caucasus — small round shields and straight swords, the *parikaoba*, photographed still fighting in the 20th century. The Byzantine inheritance (Maurice's *Strategikon*, Leo VI's *Taktika*, the cataphract, the Varangian Guard) and the Norse *holmgang* — the formal duel fought on a marked hide or island under fixed rules — round out the European combat-law tradition.

**The pike age.** The Swiss *Reisläufer* and the German *Landsknecht* pike squares, and the *Doppelsöldner* wielding the *Zweihänder* — the two-handed sword used, by tradition, to hew into pike formations — with the *Katzbalger* short sword at the belt for the press.

## VI.2 China, deeper

**Wudang and the internal charter.** The internal arts claim a Daoist fountainhead in **Zhang Sanfeng**, the legendary immortal credited with founding Taijiquan after watching a snake and a crane (or a magpie) fight — a founding legend [L], not history, but the charter that opposes "internal Wudang" to "external Shaolin." Wudang sword (the Daoist *jian*) and Quanzhen Daoist cultivation sit behind it.

**The southern and animal-charter styles.** Hung Gar (tiger-and-crane, the iron-wire form, the historical-legendary Wong Fei-hung); **Wing Chun** (the centerline doctrine, *chi sao* sticky-hands, the wooden dummy, founded by legend on the nun **Ng Mui** teaching the woman **Yim Wing-chun** — a woman-founder charter [L]); Choy Li Fut; Bak Mei (White Eyebrow, the traitor-monk legend); **Praying Mantis** (created, by legend, by Wang Lang after watching a mantis best a cicada — an animal-origin charter [L]); Eagle Claw (attributed to the Song general **Yue Fei** [L]); Monkey and **Drunken boxing** (*Zui Quan* — feigned drunkenness weaponized as deception, the Eight Drunken Immortals).

**Bagua and Xingyi, deepened.** Baguazhang maps the eight trigrams of the *Yijing* onto eight palm-changes practiced while walking a circle — Daoist circumambulation made martial (Dong Haichuan, the Qing-court eunuch [L-tinged]). Xingyiquan (also attributed to Yue Fei [L]) drills from the *santi* three-body posture, the five fists matched to the Five Phases and the twelve animals, fighting "straight in" — often glossed as "spear method without the spear."

**The exotic armory.** Beyond Volume I's canon: the three-section staff, the nine-section whip, the butterfly swords (*hudiedao*), hook swords (*shuang gou*), deer-horn/crescent knives, wind-and-fire wheels, *emei* piercers, the *dadao* and *miaodao* long sabers — the visual lexicon *wuxia* and cinema inherit wholesale.

**The cold military counter-canon.** Against the spiritual register stands a pragmatic literature: **Sunzi's *Art of War*** (strategy as the master-discipline), the Song *Wujing Zongyao* (with the earliest gunpowder formulas), and above all **Qi Jiguang's *Jixiao Xinshu*** — the Ming general who dismissed flowery boxing as useless for war yet preserved a 32-posture conditioning boxing that later fed Taijiquan, and devised the "mandarin-duck" small-unit formation. The *Wubei Zhi* (Mao Yuanyi) compiles the whole. This counter-canon is the standing rebuke to any "China = spiritual" reading (Volume I, A3).

**Conditioning and the timed touch.** Martial *qigong* — the Eight Pieces of Brocade, the iron shirt (*tie bu shan*) and golden bell (*jin zhong zhao*), the "hundred days" of foundation-building. And the literalized death-touch: actual TCM theory holds that *qi* floods different meridians at different hours (the *ziwu liuzhu*, the midnight-noon ebb-and-flow), so the lethal *dianxue* must be *timed to the hour* — a [B]/[L] doctrine that *wuxia* renders as the hour-bound killing strike. Held in reserve for honest reinterpretation in Part XI.

## VI.3 Japan, deeper

**More koryū.** Kashima Shinden Jikishinkage-ryū and Kashima Shintō-ryū; **Maniwa Nen-ryū**, among the oldest surviving lineages; the great *iai* lines — Musō Jikiden Eishin-ryū and Musō Shinden-ryū — descending from **Hayashizaki Jinsuke**, founder of the draw-cut art; **Shintō Musō-ryū**, the four-foot *jō* staff devised by Musō Gonnosuke who, by tradition, defeated Musashi on a rematch (a celebrated retelling [L]); the **Hōzōin-ryū** cross-spear (*jūmonji yari*) of the temple's warrior-monks; and Yagyū Shinkage's *marobashi* and **mutō-dori** — taking a live blade bare-handed.

**The warrior-monks and the legend-bearers.** The *sōhei* of Enryaku-ji and Kōfuku-ji with the *naginata*; the Ikkō-ikki leagues; and **Benkei**, the legendary seven-weapon warrior-monk and retainer of Yoshitsune — pure charter-legend [L].

**Shinobi.** Iga and Kōga; the actual manuals — the **Bansenshūkai** (1676) and the **Shōninki** — behind the heavy modern legend; the eighteen disciplines (*ninja jūhakkei*) and the tools (*shuko* claws, *kaginawa*, *metsubushi* blinding powder, the *fukiya* blowgun, the *kusarigama*).

**The bow and the sacred ring.** *Kyūjutsu/kyūdō* (Heki-ryū, Ogasawara-ryū), *yabusame* mounted archery, the asymmetric *yumi* gripped below center, and the contested spiritual retelling of Herrigel's *Zen in the Art of Archery* — itself a fitting artifact of the genre. **Sumo**: Shinto wrestling in the sacred *dohyō*, the *shiko* stamp, the purifying salt, the forty-eight techniques, with a divine-origin charter in the *Kojiki* (Nomi no Sukune; the kami's wrestling match) [L].

**The grappling depth and aiki.** **Takenouchi-ryū** (c. 1532), among the oldest *jūjutsu*/*kogusoku* schools; **Tenjin Shin'yō-ryū** and **Kitō-ryū**, the two arts Kanō Jigorō synthesized into Jūdō; **Yōshin-ryū** ("willow heart" — founded, by legend, when the physician Akiyama watched a willow shed snow and spring back, the founding image of *jū* [L]); the *atemi* and *kappo* (resuscitation) within these arts; and **Daitō-ryū Aiki-jūjutsu** (Takeda Sōkaku, the Minamoto-Takeda charter [L]), the source of Ueshiba's aikidō and of *aiki* as instantaneous balance-theft on contact.

## VI.4 Korea

A field under-weighted in Volume I and squarely within the "more Asian" charge. **Taekkyon** — the older Korean folk art, leg-heavy, rhythmic, with a bouncing triangular footwork (*pum balgi*) so close to folk dance that it doubles as village game and is a prime exhibit for the dance analogue. **Subak** (older grappling-striking) and **Gwonbeop** (boxing). The **Muyedobotongji** (1790), commissioned by King Jeongjo — a major illustrated manual drawing on Qi Jiguang and on Japanese sources, covering twenty-four disciplines (the two-hand sword *ssangsudo*, the crescent glaive *woldo*, boxing, equestrian skills). **Gukgung** — Korean horn-bow archery, one of the world's great archery traditions and the enduring national sport. **Ssireum** (belt wrestling). And the **Hwarang**, the "flower knights" of Silla — a historical youth corps later mythologized into a martial-chivalric order, the charter for much modern Korean martial romanticism [L].

## VI.5 The wider Asian field, deeper

**Silat's many streams.** The *alirans* — Minangkabau *Harimau* (the ground-low tiger), Sundanese Cimande and Cikalong, the Minang *silek* — and **spiritual silat**, fused with Islamic mysticism, the *ilmu* and the invulnerability practice (*kebal*), gated by the *guru* and a spiritual initiation.

**The Philippine blade world.** Pekiti-Tirsia, Balintawak, Doce Pares; the Moro weapons (the *kris*, the *kampilan*, the *barong*, the *panabas*); and the *juramentado*, the warrior who runs *amok* under a vow.

**Mainland Southeast Asia and Vietnam.** **Krabi-Krabong** (Thai weapons — the paired *krabi* swords, the *daab*, the staff *plong*, the halberd *ngao*); Burmese **Banshay** and **Naban** wrestling; **Bokator** and **Pradal Serey** (Khmer); and the Vietnamese village arts — **Vovinam** and **Binh Định**, with their rattan and their woman-general charters (the Trưng sisters' martial legacy [L-tinged]).

**India, beyond Kerala.** **Mardani Khel** of the Marathas — the gauntlet-sword (*dandpatta*), the corded lance (*vita*), the guerrilla legacy of Shivaji; the Nihang Sikhs' **Shastar Vidya**, the battlefield "science of weapons" fuller than sport Gatka, with the war-quoit (*chakram*) thrown from the turban; **Mukna** and **Inbuan** wrestling; **Kuttu Varisai** and **Adithada** (Tamil). And Kalaripayattu's defining duality, now foregrounded: **marma chikitsa** — the same map of vital points that *kills* also *heals*, the master a bonesetter and the *kalari* a clinic. The healing-harming unity is a throughline in its own right (Part VII).

**Persia and the Shahnameh ethos.** The *zurkhaneh* deepened — *meel* clubs, the *kabbadeh* iron bow, the *sang* shields, the *morshed* drumming and chanting Ferdowsi's epic; the *pahlavan* ideal incarnated in Rostam; the *futuwwa* lodges binding strength to spiritual chivalry.

**Tibet and the Himalaya (gloriously retold).** The **lung-gom-pa**, the trance-walkers said to cross vast distances in a wind-meditation stride — the meditative literalization of *qinggong*, recorded in Alexandra David-Néel's retelling [L]; the **dob-dob**, the rough "fighting monks" who policed the great monasteries; and the ritual archery and dance of Bhutan.

**The steppe and Central Asia.** **Bökh** (Mongolian wrestling, the eagle dance, the open-hand grip), the banner archery of the Qing, the belt-wrestling family (**Kurash** and its cousins) spanning the steppe, and the horse-archery culture in its full development.

# PART VII — New and Sharpened Throughlines

The expanded corpus surfaces patterns the narrower Volume I corpus could not see. These extend the list of twenty (Volume I, II.1).

21. **The yielding founder (the nun, the willow, the woman).** A recurrent charter attributes a *softness*-based art to a female or otherwise "weak" founder who defeats strength through yielding and cunning: Ng Mui and Yim Wing-chun (Wing Chun), the willow of Yōshin-ryū, the woman-generals of Vietnam. The myth encodes throughline-5 (yielding overcomes hardness) as an *origin story*: the art's superiority over brute force is written into its founding. [L] as history; structurally real as doctrine.

22. **The healing–harming unity.** The knowledge that locates the body's vital points serves *both* murder and medicine, and the traditions bind the two: *marma chikitsa* (the Kalari master is a bonesetter), the *dit da* "hit medicine" of the kung fu clinic, the *kappo* resuscitation within *jūjutsu*, the TCM physician who is also the *dianxue* adept. The same map kills and cures — a sharpening of throughline-4 (vital points) into a *dual-use* principle. [M] for the real anatomy; [B] for the meridian overlay.

23. **The animal as founding charter (not merely model).** Beyond imitation (throughline-1), the animal recurs as the *origin myth*: the art is founded in a moment of *witnessing* a beast — the mantis besting the cicada, the snake and crane, the tiger of the Minang. The animal is not only copied; it is the art's mythic progenitor. [L]/[B].

24. **Lightness as meditative attainment.** The leaping, water-skimming, distance-devouring ideal (Volume I's flight complex) is given an explicit *mechanism* in the meditative traditions: the Tibetan **lung-gom-pa** trance-stride, *qinggong* as a fruit of internal cultivation. Flight is reframed as a *spiritual* attainment rather than mere athleticism. [L]/[B].

25. **Feigned incapacity as a school.** Deception (throughline-12) hardens, in places, into an entire style built on *false weakness*: Drunken boxing's simulated drunkenness, the bait-cultures, the offered opening. The whole art is organized around appearing unable. [M] as tactic.

26. **The oversized blade as formation-breaker.** A recurrent tactical role: the two-handed great sword wielded against close-order polearms — the *Zweihänder* of the *Doppelsöldner*, the Japanese *ōdachi/nodachi*, the Korean *ssangsudo*. The weapon exists to *break a wall of spears*, a niche defined by the formation it opposes. [M].

27. **The comprehensive-school ideal.** Several apex traditions teach *everything* under one roof — Katori Shintō-ryū (sword, spear, glaive, staff, *shuriken*, fortification), Kalaripayattu (body, wood, steel, empty hand, healing), the Muyedobotongji's twenty-four disciplines. Mastery is figured not as specialization but as *total* martial literacy, a single curriculum spanning all media. [M].

# PART VIII — The Governing Analogues

## VIII.1 The thesis

A martial tradition does not merely *use* techniques; it *understands itself* through a master-metaphor drawn from another discipline. Destreza understands the sword through **geometry**; capoeira understands combat through **dance**; neidan understands cultivation through **alchemy**; Sun Tzu understands the fight through **strategy**. The chosen discipline is the tradition's **governing analogue** — and the choice is *consequential*: it shapes the schema, the pedagogy, the aesthetics, the very form the techniques take, and (Part IX) the form that mastery and "formlessness" finally assume.

Two honest qualifications, established now and enforced throughout (their adversarial basis is Part X):

- These nine are **governing metaphors with strong family resemblances, not nine orthogonal axes.** Dance and music share rhythm; geometry and architecture share figure; strategy and the information-contest are nearly one. The value is in the *governing-metaphor concept* and its consequences, not in a false claim of independence.
- An analogue is **explicit** when the tradition states it in its own texts and teaching (geometry in Thibault, dance in capoeira, the brush-and-sword unity in Japan, strategy in Sunzi/Musashi) and **analytic** when the analyst imposes it as a generative lens. The explicit analogues are *findings*; the analytic ones are *tools*. Both are marked.

## VIII.2 The nine

**A. Mathematics / Geometry** *(explicit).* — *Instances:* Destreza's mysterious circle (Carranza, Pacheco), Thibault's body-calibrated circle, Agrippa's geometric guards, Capo Ferro's lines and tempo, eskrima's numbered angles, Bagua's circle-and-octagon, the *Yijing*'s combinatorial trigrams, the formation diagrams (mandarin-duck, the eight-formation). *Consequence:* technique is *derived from figures and proportions* — the guard is a position in a coordinate space, the attack a movement along a chord or diameter, distance a measured radius, the correct action the geometrically optimal one. Pedagogy is **deductive** (from axioms and diagrams). The aesthetic is precision and economy. The deepest form (Thibault) makes the *individual body's proportions* the system's axiom: the art is geometry tailored to the man.

**B. Dance / Choreography** *(explicit).* — *Instances:* the *wai khru ram muay*, silat's *bunga* (the beautiful form veiling the lethal *buah*), capoeira entire (the *ginga* as dance-step, the *roda* as dance-circle), Taekkyon's *pum balgi* bounce, the Chinese-opera martial movement that became cinema, the *kembu* sword-dance, the lion and dragon dances, the war-dances (*haka*, the Cossack *hopak* squat-kicks). *Consequence:* technique is organized by *rhythm, phrasing, and expressive flow* — combat becomes call-and-response with a partner or audience, the form is learned as choreography, and the body memorizes sequences as a dancer does. Pedagogy is **imitative and embodied** (mirror the master, feel the phrase). The aesthetic is grace. Decisively, dance-governance *conceals martial intent within performance* — the analogue couples to deception (throughline-12), the spectacle/resistance telos, and the disguised-egalitarian ethic.

**C. Music / Rhythm** *(explicit).* — *Instances:* the *berimbau* whose *toque* dictates the character of the capoeira game, the *zurkhaneh* drum setting the pace and chanting the epic, the *sarama* music played *live* during a Muay Thai bout with the fighters moving to it, the *kiai* as percussive accent, the work-song call-and-response. *Consequence:* where dance governs *space-and-form*, music governs *time* — the fight acquires a beat, a measure, a phrase; the skilled fighter "plays against" the opponent's rhythm, syncopates, breaks the tempo; combinations are rhythmic patterns. Rhythm-governance makes *temporal* deception — the false tempo, the broken beat — the core skill, fusing with throughline-6 (timing and initiative).

**D. Medicine / Anatomy** *(explicit).* — *Instances:* *marma* and *marma chikitsa*, *dianxue* and the *ziwu liuzhu* blood-flow clock, *kyūsho*, the *kappo* resuscitation, the *dit da* bonesetter-herbalist, the *prana/nadi/chakra* physiology. *Consequence:* the body is read as a *system of vulnerabilities and balances* — technique targets the points and times strikes to the body's cycles, and the art carries a *healing counterpart* (throughline-22): you must be able to mend what you can break. This produces an anatomical schema (S6) and a *dual* telos, health and harm at once.

**E. Alchemy / Transmutation** *(explicit).* — *Instances:* neidan's *jing→qi→shen→*void, the marrow-washing and tendon-changing, the Indian *rasayana* and *kundalini* raising, the smith-alchemist forging the ensouled blade. *Consequence:* cultivation is conceived as *conversion* — the practitioner does not merely train but *transmutes* (lead to gold, mortal to immortal), through staged refinements with thresholds (the "hundred days," the stages of the work). This is the engine of the vertical cultivation arc and the source of the most fantastical claims. The alchemical analogue makes the art's *time-structure* a refinement ladder.

**F. Writing / Calligraphy / Language** *(explicit in East Asia; analytic elsewhere).* — *Instances:* the brush-and-sword unity (the same hand, breath, and *ki* wields both; *shodō* and *kenjutsu* as sisters), the *kata* as a "character" written with the body, the mnemonic verse (the *Zettel* as poem), techniques as *vocabulary* and combinations as *grammar*. *Consequence:* technique becomes a *lexicon* and combat a *composition* — letters (atomic moves), words (techniques), sentences (combinations), and a personal "hand" (style); mastery is fluency rising to *eloquence* and free improvisation, the master composing as a calligrapher or poet does. Pedagogy is **grammatical** (letters → words → sentences → composition).

**G. Strategy / Games** *(explicit).* — *Instances:* Sunzi and the strategic canon, Musashi's *heihō* (one principle governing the single combat and the army's battle), the *weiqi*/Go and chess analogues (positional reading, the sacrifice, the gambit, reading-ahead), the eskrima/silat "if-then" flow drills as decision-trees. *Consequence:* combat is a *decision problem under imperfect information* — positions, moves, anticipations, sacrifices, traps; the fighter thinks several moves ahead and sets gambits. Pedagogy is **scenario-and-problem**. This analogue is the strategic literalization of the information-contest (meta-throughline C).

**H. Architecture / Engineering** *(explicit in the "structure" idiom; partly analytic).* — *Instances:* the rooting-and-frame language (the body as a building — foundation, central pillar, load-paths), the iron-shirt as wall/armor, the *Sanchin* structural test (the master strikes to test the "build"), the fortification arts (*chikujōjutsu*), the formation-as-structure (the phalanx, the shield-wall, the pike-square). *Consequence:* the body is *built and tested for integrity* — alignment, load-paths, the frame that transmits force — and technique is the construction and maintenance of one's own structure and the *demolition* of the opponent's. Pedagogy includes **structural testing** (the push-test, the rooting drill).

**I. Theater / Ritual / Spectacle** *(explicit).* — *Instances:* the gladiatorial types as staged "roles," the Chinese-opera combat, the lion/dragon dance, the demonstration (*embu*), the *haka* as psychological theater, the sacred ring of sumo, the *holmgang*'s staging, the *wai khru* as ritual. *Consequence:* combat is *staged for an audience, human or divine* — the form is a performance, the techniques legible and expressive, the ritual frame constitutive, and the psychological-spectacular dimension (intimidation, display, honor before witnesses) central. Couples to the spectacle/communal telos, psychological targeting (T7), and the ritual-rhythm throughline.

# PART IX — Extended Combinatorial Logic

## IX.1 Dimension 13 — the Governing Analogue

The scheme of Volume I (twelve dimensions, ~100 atoms) gains a thirteenth dimension whose values are the nine analogues of Part VIII (a tradition may take one as dominant and others as subordinate). But this dimension is *unlike the other twelve*: it does not sit beside them as a peer coordinate — it **biases the selection across all of them**. To choose "geometry governs" is to be pulled toward a particular region of the other twelve dimensions. The governing analogue is therefore an **attractor**, and the new logic is the logic of attractors.

## IX.2 The basins (the regions each analogue pulls toward)

Each analogue has a *natural region* in the twelve-dimensional space — the cluster of primitives its internal logic favors. (A **basin** here is just that region of attraction; the term is borrowed precisely from the mathematics of attractors, which is itself the geometry analogue applied to this very analysis.)

- **Geometry** → S3 (geometric schema), P1 (skeletal-mechanical), B1/B2 (linear/orbital), deductive transmission X2, the duel/rational telos.
- **Dance** → B5/B6 (springy/undulating), S5/S8, X3/X6, the spectacle/resistance telos, deception, grace.
- **Music** → I2/I4 (rhythmic timing and feint), the spectacle/communal telos, the live-performance frame.
- **Medicine** → S6 (anatomical schema), T3 (vital points), the dual health-and-combat telos, the healing counterpart.
- **Alchemy** → E4 (refinement-transmutation), C5 (enlightenment), the staged refinement time-structure, the [B] register.
- **Writing** → S5 (narrative-mnemonic), the lexicon-and-grammar pedagogy, the aesthetic-literary telos.
- **Strategy** → D4 (initiative, especially provocation), the information-contest, scenario pedagogy.
- **Architecture** → P1 (mechanical), B4 (rooted), T5 (take the base), the structural-test pedagogy.
- **Theater** → C7/C8 (spectacle/communal), T7 (psychological), X6 (public), the ritual frame.

A coherent tradition sits *in or near* its dominant analogue's basin. Distance from the basin is a measure of internal tension — productive if a reconciling principle bridges it, incoherent if none does.

## IX.3 The new generative principles (extending Volume I, V.2–V.3)

**G1 — The analogue is an attractor, not a free coordinate.** The basins are not arbitrary correlations; the *strong* ones are **logically entailed** by the analogue's own nature (geometry *requires* precise figures because that is what geometry is; dance *requires* rhythmic flow because that is what dance is), while *weak* ones are merely *observed* (flag these as the looser claims). Strong-basin pulls bind tightly; weak-basin pulls are suggestions.

**G2 — Analogue-swap reliably generates coherent novelty.** Take an existing tradition, *re-found it under a different governing analogue*, and let the new basin reshape the vector. The result is almost always coherent (every primitive is real) and novel (the region is unoccupied). Volume I's Hollow-Bell school was tacitly this — an internal art re-founded under music/acoustics. The swap is the most dependable generator the scheme offers.

**G3 — Analogue-fusion generates tension, and sometimes emergence.** Fuse two analogues whose basins *conflict*, and resolve the conflict into a new operative principle. The richer the basin-conflict, the greater the emergent potential — *and* the greater the risk of incoherence if no principle bridges it. Geometry (held figures, deductive, static) fused with dance (continuous flow, rhythmic, expressive) is a prime site precisely because the basins pull oppositely (Part XI, Exemplar 7).

**G4 — The analogue determines the shape of mastery, and therefore the shape of "formlessness."** This is the deepest consequence and the principal extension of meta-throughline B (scaffold-and-transcendence). The analogue fixes the *pedagogy*, the pedagogy fixes the *kind of expertise*, and the kind of expertise fixes *what formlessness becomes at the apex*. The geometer's no-mind is the internalized figure no longer consciously computed; the dancer's is pure improvisation; the calligrapher's is the master's free "hand"; the strategist's is the read that no longer reasons; the alchemist's is the completed transmutation that needs no further work. **Every art ascends to "no-mind," but the no-mind it reaches is the precise negation of the ladder that built it.** The summit is shaped by the analogue even though the summit is the analogue's disappearance.

**G5 — Analogue-coherence is a viability test** *(new rule, extending Volume I V.2).* A vector is **analogue-coherent** only if its declared governing analogue's strong basin is consistent with — or in *stated productive tension* with — the rest of the vector. A design that declares "geometry governs" yet selects undulating flow (B6), an aschematic organization (S8), and learning-by-public-spectacle (X6) is *analogue-incoherent*: the master-metaphor contradicts the actual body of the art, with no bridging principle. This catches a class of incoherence the twelve-dimension rules miss — the tradition that *says* it is one thing and *is* another.

## IX.4 The analogues as the substrate beneath the meta-throughlines

The decisive theoretical claim of this volume: the governing analogues are not a new layer *atop* the meta-throughlines (Volume I, II.2) but the *generative substrate beneath* them. The meta-throughlines are what one *observes* across traditions; analogue-governance is the mechanism that *produces* the observation.

- **Meta-C (the information contest)** is the **strategy/games** analogue generalized to all combat.
- **Meta-D (microcosm–macrocosm: body = weapon = cosmos)** is the **geometry** analogue (the body's proportions harmonizing with cosmic figure — Thibault's circle) *and* the **alchemy** analogue (the body's substances corresponding to cosmic substances) generalized.
- **Meta-A (the vertical cultivation arc)** is the **alchemy** analogue's refinement-ladder generalized into the universal "gross → subtle → empty."
- **Meta-E (lineage as the inseparable carrier)** is reinforced by *every* analogue, since each imports its own pedagogy and mode of transmission — the analogue is *how* the lineage knows what it knows.
- **Meta-B (scaffold-and-transcendence)** receives the G4 refinement: the analogue both *builds* the scaffold and *shapes the form* of its transcendence.

Thus the generative scheme acquires a second floor: above, the twelve dimensions and their atoms (the *parts*); beneath, the nine governing analogues (the *metaphors that select and arrange the parts*); and the meta-throughlines are the *visible regularities* that this two-floor machinery throws up. To invent a new tradition is, most powerfully, to *choose or fuse a governing analogue* and let it draw a coherent vector after it.

# PART X — Adversarial Pass on the Analogue Framework

The new material is attacked on its own terms, dispassionately. Verdict first: **the framework survives and is strengthened by the attack; four findings force qualification, none is fatal.**

**AA1 — The analogues are not orthogonal (the basis is loose).** Dance and music are inseparable at the joint of rhythm; geometry and architecture both think in figure; strategy and the information-contest are one idea twice. The "nine axes" are a *field of overlapping governing metaphors*, not a clean basis. *Reconciled:* the language is corrected throughout — "governing analogues / metaphors with family resemblances," never "orthogonal axes." The generative value lives in the *governing-metaphor concept* and the attractor logic (G1–G5), which do not require independence. The overlaps are themselves informative: they mark where two analogues will fuse *easily* (dance+music) versus where fusion is *tense and emergent* (geometry+dance).

**AA2 — The analogue may be the analyst's imposition, not the tradition's.** Does a hard striking art "really" carry an architecture analogue, or is "structure" merely a handy English word the analyst projects? *Reconciled:* this is exactly why Part VIII tags each analogue **explicit** or **analytic**. The explicit ones — geometry in Thibault, dance in capoeira, the brush-sword in Japanese sources, strategy in Sunzi/Musashi — are *findings*, present in the traditions' own self-description. The analytic ones are *tools*, owned as the analyst's lens and claimed only for their generative use, not as historical fact. The distinction is load-bearing and enforced.

**AA3 — The basin claim risks circularity.** The basins were read off the primitives that analogue-governed traditions happen to use, then the analogue was said to "pull toward" those primitives — circular unless the pull is independently motivated. *Reconciled:* G1 already splits the basins into **entailed** (the analogue's own logic requires the primitive — non-circular) and **observed** (mere correlation — flagged as the weaker claim). The strong basins stand on entailment; the weak ones are explicitly held loosely. The circularity is confined to the parts that are labeled as such.

**AA4 — "Emergence" via fusion is still mostly recombination (the inherited caution).** As in Volume I (A8), calling a fusion "emergent" must be *argued*, not asserted, and most candidates resolve to recombination on inspection. *Reconciled:* the line is held in Part XI — only Exemplar 7 (and the where-when unit of Exemplar 8) is argued as a genuinely new operative principle; the rest are labeled *coherent novelty* or *trending*. No inflation.

**What survives.** The governing-analogue thesis survives intact, with three reins fitted: the "axes" become "metaphors," each analogue is tagged explicit-or-analytic, and each basin is split entailed-or-observed. The attractor logic (G1–G5) and the substrate claim (IX.4) take no damage from the attack — they were the part under test and they held. **[NULL on fatal defects: the analogue framework was attacked on independence, projection, circularity, and over-claim, and none broke the thesis; all four findings sharpen it.] [CONFIDENCE: medium-high — the explicit analogues are well-grounded; the analytic ones are honestly flagged as tools, and the framework's worth does not rest on them being more than that.]**

# PART XI — New Worked Exemplars (analogue-driven)

Five further designs, continuing Volume I's numbering (1–5 → 6–10), each generated by analogue-swap (G2) or analogue-fusion (G3) and classified honestly.

**Exemplar 6 — Analogue-swap: grappling re-founded under WRITING/CALLIGRAPHY (the "cursive grapple").** *Move:* take an architecture/medicine-governed grappling art (structure and joints) and re-found it under calligraphy. *Result:* throws and locks become *strokes*; a submission sequence becomes a *character*; the transitions between holds become the *connecting ligatures* of cursive script (*caoshu*), and the art's prized skill is the **unbroken connected line** — the grappler "writes" the opponent into submission in one continuous, never-lifted stroke, judged for the legibility and flow of its "hand," and mastery is improvised composition, as a calligrapher composes a poem. *Verdict:* **coherent novelty, emergent-adjacent.** The calligraphy basin reorganizes grappling's transmission and aesthetic without breaking a single primitive; the new unit of skill — *connected continuity* as the thing judged — is something no grappling tradition makes explicit.

**Exemplar 7 — Analogue-fusion across conflicting basins: GEOMETRY + DANCE (the "traced-figure" sword).** *Move:* fuse the geometric sword (Thibault's body-calibrated circle, the diameters and chords, deductive precision) with dance (continuous rhythmic flow). The basins *conflict* — geometry holds figures, dance never stops. *Resolving principle:* **the figures are never held, only traced** — the swordsman moves continuously along the circle's chords and diameters *in time, to a shared meter*, so the "guards" become instantaneous passages through points on a danced curve rather than static positions, and the duel becomes a *duet of two bodies tracing intersecting figures in common tempo*. Geometry supplies the *path*; dance supplies the *continuous rhythmic traversal* of it. *Verdict:* **emergent.** The operative principle — combat as the real-time tracing of intersecting geometric figures in shared meter — belongs to neither parent (Destreza holds its figures; capoeira has no precise geometry) and is forced into being by the very conflict of their basins. The strongest of the analogue designs, and the direct answer to the geometry-and-dance cue.

**Exemplar 8 — Analogue-fusion: MEDICINE + MUSIC (the "vulnerable-beat" striking art, honestly re-founding the timed touch).** *Move:* fuse the medical analogue (the *ziwu liuzhu* timed *dianxue*) with the music analogue (rhythm as organizing principle). *Resolving principle:* the opponent's body is heard as *having a rhythm* — the cycle of breath, of tension and recovery, of commitment and reset — and the strike falls on the *vulnerable beat* of that cycle. Crucially, this discards the mystical hour-of-the-day timing of *dim-mak* ([B]/[L]) and re-founds it on the **real, observable** vulnerability of specific phases — the instant of inhalation, the beat just after a committed attack, the recovery downbeat. The medical map gives the *where* (the point); the musical ear gives the *when* (the opponent's own vulnerable beat). *Verdict:* **coherent novelty trending emergent.** It takes a fantastical doctrine and lands it on a verifiable principle — exactly the honest-fantastical synthesis this project wants. The emergent element: *targeting and timing fused into a single "where-when" unit* governed jointly by two analogues, where every tradition treats them as separate decisions.

**Exemplar 9 — Analogue-driven weapon: a STRATEGY/GAMES-governed implement (the "gambit polearm").** *Move:* design a weapon whose *form embodies* the strategy analogue — built to *offer sacrifices and set traps*. *Result:* an asymmetric polearm every feature of which is either a genuine threat *or a bait* — a deliberately grabbable hook, a catching quillon, an exposed-seeming haft — where engaging the offered part commits the opponent to a line the wielder has *already prepared a response to*. The implement encodes a decision-tree: its affordances are the moves of a game whose gambits the wielder has read several plies deep. (Historical seed: the toothed sword-breaker that *invites* the bind in order to trap the blade, here generalized into a whole weapon-as-gameboard.) *Verdict:* **coherent novelty, emergent-adjacent.** The principle — *a weapon's geometry should encode a tree of baits and responses rather than only maximize direct threat* — is a distinct design philosophy, recombinant in its parts but novel in its governing idea.

**Exemplar 10 — Analogue-inversion: ARCHITECTURE turned destructive, fused with the yielding founder (the "hollow-frame" demolition art).** *Move:* take the architecture analogue but *invert its telos* — instead of building one's own unbreakable structure, found the art entirely on *reading and collapsing the opponent's*: a throwing-and-uprooting art organized as **structural engineering applied destructively** (locate the load-bearing limb, the keystone joint, the line along which the frame will fold, and collapse it). One's own body is kept deliberately *hollow* — yielding, *song*, minimal — precisely so it offers no structure to attack (you cannot demolish what was never built), which maps the design onto the yielding-founder throughline (21): the soft art that defeats the strong by giving them nothing to push against while diagnosing and felling their build. *Resolving principle:* **structure as a target rather than a possession — build nothing, demolish everything.** *Verdict:* **coherent novelty trending emergent.** Inverting an analogue's natural telos (build → demolish) and fusing it with the yielding principle yields an art whose core skill is the *structural diagnosis of the opponent* — a genuinely distinct organizing idea.

# Closing Assessment

What this volume adds, stated plainly and without inflation:

- **The corpus is deepened, with the bias the user authorized** — a far wider Euro-Asian field (the Bolognese and Thibault, the *Kampfringen* and the English guild, the Slavic and Caucasian and Nordic dueling traditions; Wudang and the southern Chinese charters and the cold military counter-canon; the fuller koryū, the *shinobi* manuals, sumo and the *jūjutsu* schools; Korea entire; the silat *alirans*, the Philippine blade world, the deeper Indian and Persian and Tibetan and steppe traditions) — with legends and retellings admitted *as charters* and marked [L]/[B], never laundered into history.

- **Seven new throughlines** (the yielding founder, the healing-harming unity, the animal-as-charter, lightness-as-meditation, feigned-incapacity-as-school, the oversized formation-breaker, the comprehensive-school ideal) extend Volume I's twenty.

- **The governing-analogue thesis is the real deepening** — nine master-metaphors (geometry, dance, music, medicine, alchemy, writing, strategy, architecture, theater), each tagged explicit-or-analytic, each with an entailed-or-observed basin, shown to be the *generative substrate beneath the meta-throughlines* rather than a layer atop them. The combinatorial logic gains five new principles (G1–G5): the analogue as attractor, analogue-swap as a reliable generator of coherent novelty, analogue-fusion as the site of tension and rare emergence, the analogue's determination of the very *shape of formlessness*, and analogue-coherence as a new viability test.

- **The honest limits are unchanged.** The scheme reliably yields *coherent recombinant* novelty and *occasionally*, by argued fusion across conflicting basins, genuine emergence (Exemplar 7 the clearest case). It still cannot validate the metaphysical primitives as physics — and the analogue framework adds the right tool for honoring them anyway: Exemplar 8 shows how a fantastical doctrine (the timed death-touch) is *re-founded* on a verifiable principle (the opponent's vulnerable beat), keeping the *wuxia* register generative while the epistemic tags keep it honest. The dividing lines remain the two from Volume I — the *reconciling principle* (which separates emergence from incoherence) and the *epistemic tag* (which separates licensed fantasy from asserted fact) — now joined by a third, *analogue-coherence* (which separates a tradition that is what it says from one that only claims to be).

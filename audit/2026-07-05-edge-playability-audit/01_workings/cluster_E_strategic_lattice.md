### E1 · Faction Mandate ↔ Settlement L/PS loop (settlement_layer_v30.md §1.8 LPS-2e)

1. **MOMENT** — Not a discrete moment; a slow aggregate recompute at Accounting from `T = Σ W_s·(q_s/7)` over all controlled settlements (settlement_layer_v30.md:165-166).
2. **DECISION** — Two real levers: raise settlement Weight (`W_s = base(Type)+Prosperity_s+FacilityTier_s`, settlement_layer_v30.md:158-163) via Develop/Fortify actions or the "Expand Institutional Capacity" Domain Action (Treasury −300, settlement_layer_v30.md:92); or raise q_s (L/PS) via faction-level procedural/violation mission outcomes that PP-686 now applies uniformly to controlled settlements (settlement_layer_v30.md:175, faction_behavior_v30.md:299-313).
3. **FEEDBACK** — Rendered as a single aggregate meter, "Legitimacy = Mandate × 20" (settlement_layer_v30.md:177; derived_stats_v30.md:297). No UI path back to *which* settlement or which mission event moved it — the formula's own weighted mass `T` is not itself surfaced.
4. **LATENCY** — One Accounting cycle (settlement_layer_v30.md:173, "Stage-4 sim... converges over 30 seasons"); feedback loop (Mandate→L/PS) is explicitly capped at ±1/settlement/season.
5. **FRICTION** — The saturating `T/(T+K)` form (K=6) is deliberately diminishing-returns (settlement_layer_v30.md:168) — a late-game "developed" faction can pour Prosperity into a settlement and see Mandate barely move, with no in-fiction explanation of *why* (the design intends this as "Lesson-5" damping, but nothing tells the player they've hit the saturation curve).
6. **RECIPROCITY** — Real: Mandate feeds back down into settlement L/PS (mean-reverting, settlement_layer_v30.md:173) and up into Parliament vote weight (GD-1 synergy, settlement_layer_v30.md:179).
7. **VERDICT** — PLAYS-ROUGH · P2 · DELIBERATE (the saturation is an intentional damping design, but its legibility to the player is not addressed in-doc — UNDETERMINED whether any UI plan exists to show `T` or trend).

### E2 · Settlement Prosperity → Faction Treasury (§1.3, derived_stats_v30.md §8.1)

1. **MOMENT** — Automatic seasonal income at Accounting: `Σ(Prosperity of controlled settlements) × 10` (derived_stats_v30.md:308), inside a Treasury ceiling of `Wealth × 100` (derived_stats_v30.md:298,323).
2. **DECISION** — Develop a settlement (Prosperity +1, settlement_layer_v30.md:427) or Trade (Wealth×25/50 one-time, derived_stats_v30.md:309-310).
3. **FEEDBACK** — Itemized income/drain ledger exists in spec (derived_stats_v30.md:307-320: Trade, Haushalt bonus, Campaign Supply, Muster costs all separately named) — this is a genuinely good feedback design *if* surfaced as a ledger UI. But the seasonal Prosperity income line is a bare aggregate sum with no per-settlement attribution shown to the player (settlement_layer_v30.md:47,169) — a faction holding 6 settlements sees one number move and cannot tell which settlement's Prosperity investment paid off without manually re-deriving it.
4. **LATENCY** — 1 season (Accounting-gated).
5. **FRICTION** — At-0 penalty is consistent and legible: "Treasury 0 → cannot Muster/Fortify; next Accounting still 0 → Wealth −1" (derived_stats_v30.md:321) mirrors the settlement-level rule (settlement_layer_v30.md:51) — same rule at both scales, a real design strength worth noting positively.
6. **RECIPROCITY** — One-directional bookkeeping (settlement → faction); no faction-level lever visibly flows back to a *named* settlement's Prosperity except generic Develop.
7. **VERDICT** — PLAYS-WELL (mechanically) / feedback-legibility PLAYS-ROUGH on attribution · P3 · DELIBERATE.

## Threadwork note (P-14)

P-14 requires that "co-movement cannot be omitted in any play mode" — every Thread operation fires three-dimensional (Temporal/Epistemic/Actual) consequences regardless of scale (`canon/02_canon_constraints.md:23`). At the strategic-lattice seams audited here, Thread touches the cluster only indirectly: `conviction_track_v30.md §1.3b` (ED-676) collapses "visible Thread operations" into flat categorical PT deltas (Public Dissolution −1, Public Weaving −0.5, etc.) at the territory scale, and `victory_v30.md §5.3` converts RM Presence markers 1:1 into Underground Network points on Occupation. Neither location documents whether the underlying personal-scale operation still resolved full three-dimensional co-movement before being downgraded to a scalar PT/Underground-Network tag, or whether the strategic layer is itself treating "a Thread thing happened" as a single-axis event for bookkeeping convenience. If the former (co-movement resolved at personal scale, then aggregated), P-14 is intact — Domain Echo-style abstraction is the documented pattern elsewhere. If the latter, this cluster is a candidate site for a quiet P-14 violation specifically because it sits one abstraction layer removed from where Thread mechanics are usually audited — nobody would think to canon-guard-check the peninsular-strain or victory docs for co-movement compliance. Flagging as UNDETERMINED, worth a targeted `valoria-canon-guard` pass on `conviction_track_v30.md §1.3b` and `victory_v30.md §5.3` specifically.

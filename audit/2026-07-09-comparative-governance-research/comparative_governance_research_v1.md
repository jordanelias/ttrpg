## STEP 1 — What was already there, and the gap this fills

Round 1 (2026-07-08, `fa_se_historical_precedent_research_v1.md`) mined ten themes disjoint from the 2026-06-28 deliberation-and-procedure corpus: fiscal sociology (Tilly/Levi/Brewer), tax farming (publicani/iltizam/Ferme Générale), the moral economy of subsistence (Thompson/Scott/Sen, cura annonae, Chinese ever-normal granaries), urban charters and collective coercion (Magdeburg law, quo warranto, Hanseatic embargo), principal-agent governor oversight (Castilian residencia/visita, Ming rule-of-avoidance, Ottoman timar rotation), conquest terms (Grotius siege law, Magdeburg 1631), parish-level church-state jurisdiction (advowson, interdict), frontier colonization (Byzantine themata, Habsburg Militärgrenze), succession interregna (Kantorowicz, regency, partition), and legitimacy theory proper (Weber, Olson, Hirschman, Ibn Khaldun). It distilled to FA-1..9/SE-1..10 (`ED-FA-0008..0017`/`ED-SE-0007..0017`), most of which are now live as PROPOSED canon text or executed sim code (see `handoffs/HANDOFF_FA.md`/`HANDOFF_SE.md`).

That pass explicitly did **not** touch: Byzantine court-precedence/title machinery (it used only themata and 1204), the Chinese mandarinate/examination system (mentioned once, in passing, as a decision-procedure), feudal Japan (untouched entirely), the Holy Roman Empire's actual constitutional architecture (it used only the Golden Bull electors, mined by the *deliberation* corpus, not this one), Venice's overseas/mainland administrative apparatus (it used only the Great-Council/doge-election machinery, also deliberation-corpus territory), the podestà/condottieri tradition of non-Venetian Renaissance Italy, or Habsburg Spain's conciliar/corregidor/Cortes architecture (it used only the residencia/visita audit pair and the Inquisition).

This is the gap the user's request names directly, civilization by civilization — and it is also, not coincidentally, where the two most-cited institutional models Valoria's rank-advancement and settlement-governance systems already resemble (a Skyrim-guild-style rank ladder; an EU4/CK3-style estate-friction governance loop) have their *real* historical analogues sitting unmined: guild apprenticeship ladders (Byzantine Book of the Eparch, Florentine Arte della Lana, Spanish gremios), civil-service credentialing ladders (the Chinese examination system), and appointed-governor/magistrate control regimes distinct from the residencia/visita pair already covered (the Venetian rettori/podestà anti-entrenchment system, the Italian podestà-forestiero tradition, the Spanish corregidor).

**One correction surfaced by the Opus judgment pass that reframes the whole batch (see STEP 3 for the full account): the premise that the Guild ladder is "missing" — carried into several proposals from `faction_politics_v30.md`'s own self-audit finding A-03 — is stale.** A-03 describes the *original 2026-04-16 five-rank draft*; the current, expanded §2.5 already authors a full Apprentice → Journeyman → Free Master → Guild Master → Senior Guild Master → Comptroller → Grand Guildmaster ladder (and §2.6 gives Niflhel one too). The genuinely flat surface is narrower and different: the Crown's Administrative specialty branch (§1.1b), which alone among the three Crown branches states "no sub-office opens."

## STEP 3 — Judgment pass over the 58-proposal batch

Opus 4.8 was handed the full candidate batch (all 58 proposals below) plus the game-context brief and the round-1 docket's item list, and instructed to judge without pattern-matching — a proposal earns "keep" only if it creates new decision-space, a new resource, a new tradeoff, or a new failure mode, not merely because it cites a real institution. Its judgment, verbatim from that pass:

### Two premise corrections that gate the whole batch

1. **The Guild ladder exists (§2.5).** Any proposal framed as "fill the missing Guild ladder" is misframed. Proposals that instead *add to* the existing §2.5 cells (BYZ-3 entry, IT-8 mastership) survive on that corrected basis; none earned a keep merely for "creating the Guild ladder."
2. **The one truly empty rank surface is the Crown Administrative branch (§1.1b).** Seven proposals compete to fill it (CHN-2, HRE-1, VEN-FA-1, VEN-FA-2, IT-4, HAB-3, FA-JP1). One empty slot cannot be filled seven ways; I kept the single most differentiated shape and cut the rest as slot-competitors. Filling it at all is a Jordan taste call (§1.1b's flat branch reads as a stated contrast against Martial/Intelligence), so the winner ships as `needs_jordan`.

**Result: 14 cut / 44 kept (24%).**

## 3. Synthesis opportunities

**S-1 — Consolidated Command = BYZ-6 (accelerator) + IT-5 (brake).** Author as one governance §1.9: a Provincial Authority may consolidate N adjacent settlements under one stronger governor for a **pooled AP budget** (BYZ-6: `AP = 2 + Σ FacilityTier`, capped) at the price of concentrated revolt risk (bloc-wide crisis resolution, Π = max of members); but the moment the bloc's combined Weight crosses a threshold, a **mandatory Legation split** fires (IT-5), spinning off a co-equal governor over half the cluster or accruing governor-emergence ticks. The two proposals are literally the two halves of the historical doux/legatine institution — decide and author them together, never one alone.

**S-2 — The Governor-Oversight Toolkit = IT-1 + HAB-1 folded into prior SE-7.** SE-7 already landed rotation + surprise Visita + end-of-tenure Residencia. Add the two missing modes: **replace-with-a-Podestà** (IT-1), whose novelty is *appointer-liability* (a botched term bills the appointing faction's Legitimacy), and **overlay-a-Corregidor** (HAB-1), whose novelty is a specialist seated *over* the sitting governor writing term-scoped, self-expiring rulings. Result: a five-instrument capture-resistance menu, each with a distinct cost profile, authored as one extension of SE-7's section rather than three scattered items.

**S-3 — The Settlement Guild-Regulation Suite = CHN-6 + HAB-7 + HRE-5 + SE-JP3, on SE-4's Charter spine.** A coherent corporate-politics lifecycle at settlement scale: **Register** a guild (CHN-6) to make it visible, taxable, and adjudicable; **Ratify/Amend** its self-authored ordenanzas (HAB-7); if you over-squeeze it, it **revolts** and you fork to Compact-veto or Suppression (HRE-5); and its entire privilege edifice can be undone by **targeting its patron's standing** (SE-JP3) rather than the guild itself. The caste layer threads through it (HAB-7 can ratify caste exclusion into policy; see S-4). This is the single richest new SE decision-surface in the batch.

**S-4 — The Guild Ladder Advancement Fork = BYZ-3 + IT-8.** Two consecutive rungs of the *existing* §2.5 ladder each gain a genuine fork: entry (Std 0) becomes joint-liability-guarantor vs sole-patron-liability (BYZ-3); mastership (Std 1→2) becomes examined vs capital-bought-Upstart (IT-8). Together they give the Guild ladder a skill-vs-capital-vs-social-collateral texture no other ladder has, and both deliberately interact with the §2.5/§3.2 caste bias — the bought paths let capital purchase a route past the caste-biased Masterpiece committee, at a durable-tag cost. Author as one "§2.5 entry/mastership expansion."

**S-5 — The Locked-Extraction-Figure substrate = SE-JP2 + HAB-5.** Both lock an extraction figure that then decouples from live Prosperity (the governor eats the gap). Implement **one** locked-figure read-path with two acquisition triggers: an administrative **Survey** (unilateral valuation snapshot; failure mode = stale-high assessment strips a declined settlement below subsistence) and a negotiated **Encabezamiento** (consensual fixed quota with a reciprocal petition-right). Shared tag machinery, distinct triggers and failure modes — cheaper to build and more coherent than two independent locks.

*(Note-only S-6 — the Credentialed-Cohort Purge chain CHN-2 → CHN-7 → CHN-8 forms a coherent Donglin-style institutional catastrophe, but only if all three needs_jordan forks clear; sequence it last.)*

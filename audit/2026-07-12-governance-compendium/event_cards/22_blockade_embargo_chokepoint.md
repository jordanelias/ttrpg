# Event Cards — Trade Blockade, Embargo, Chokepoint Loss

*Key: `historical-concerns-catalogue/trade-blockade-embargo-chokepoint`. The through-line: **an Embargo/Directive is a weapon a hegemon points at a third faction through its own subordinates**, and a chokepoint is a geography that converts Fortify into extraction — both generate deferred Grudge/Debt that seeds later Muster/Conquest.*

### HEV-BLOC-01 — The Megarian Decree (Athens, 432 BCE) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Athenian trade embargo against Megara; source: Wikipedia; Livius.org; McDonald, Electronic Antiquity 2.3.
- **Trigger:** `target holds bloc-network trade-dependent Prosperity AND hegemon Grudge-tagged toward it AND hegemon cross-faction Standing ≥ target's, mid-high Π`
- **Response branches:** Resolved hegemon-side as Sponsor+Directive — target's governor gets a forced Comply/Bargain/Defy over "how much of my own trade do I sacrifice."
- **Follow-on (FO):** Unresolved 2+ seasons escalates to a bloc-wide Muster among the target's treaty allies (the embargo becomes casus belli).
- **Introduces (Action):** Embargo (new Directive type targeting a *third* faction, not the settlement).
- **Loop:** A high-Standing hegemon with a Grudge crashes a rival's trade network at zero direct AP cost, converting political leverage into economic warfare; the target's only counters are Bargain or Defy-via-Treat, and non-resolution feeds Π/Grudge until it seeds a bloc Muster — the cost is deferred to the hegemon's allies' war-readiness.

### HEV-BLOC-02 — Danish Sound Dues / Øresundstolden (1429–1857) · Opportunity
- **Historical grounding:** Toll levied on ships passing through the Øresund strait; source: Wikipedia; STRO/UNESCO; havneguide.dk.
- **Trigger:** `chokepoint tag AND FacilityTier≥2 AND low-mid Π`
- **Response branches:** Unlocks the Toll Levy method (auto-succeeding income, no roll); no Comply/Bargain/Defy — it's a standing income stream.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Sustained Toll seeds a "rival contests the strait" Friction once a foreign faction's own toll-Debt crosses threshold (→ negotiated Compact or escalating Grudge/Conquest weight).
- **Introduces (Action):** Toll (chokepoint-gated Levy method, hard-gated on prior Fortify FacilityTier).
- **Loop:** Fortify at a chokepoint pays for itself through Toll extraction (FacilityTier is the multiplier), but sustained extraction accrues Debt/Grudge that seeds the Friction contesting the strait — the toll's success is what raises the Conquest-weight against the settlement that built it.

### HEV-BLOC-03 — Portuguese Cartaz System (Indian Ocean, 16th c.) · Friction
- **Historical grounding:** Portuguese licensing system requiring passes (cartazes) for shipping in the Indian Ocean; source: Wikipedia; historicalleys; jihku.org.
- **Trigger:** `faction holds ≥2 Fortify-built naval settlements ringing a zone AND standing naval Muster AND a rival's traffic lacks a License/Compact`
- **Response branches:** Unlicensed-vessel encounter → Hold-Court adjudication (seize/burn for Treasury+Grudge, or retroactive licensing fee→ongoing License).
- **Follow-on (FO):** 3+ Grudge tags seed Intrigue (smuggling, false-flag passes) not open war.
- **Introduces (Action):** License Waters (new verb, joint *Levy+Fortify* precondition licensing a *zone* not a settlement, with patrol upkeep).
- **Loop:** Enforcement credibility is a direct function of prior Fortify+Muster; upkeep lapses spawn smuggler Opportunity cards; successful enforcement is self-financing via License income but the accumulating Grudge feeds rivals' Conquest weight against the ports — self-financing but not self-stabilizing.

### HEV-BLOC-04 — Closure of the Hanseatic Kontor at Novgorod (1494) · Intrigue
- **Historical grounding:** Closure/seizure of the Hanseatic League's trading post at Novgorod; source: Wikipedia Peterhof; Springer, The Hanse in Medieval & Early Modern Europe (2022).
- **Trigger:** `a foreign faction holds a Charter granting trade-monopoly rights AND their upstream hinterland Prosperity declining ≥2 seasons AND your Standing over them rising`
- **Response branches:** Seize agents (severe Grudge/Debt + one-time Treasury/Prosperity gain as trade reroutes) vs leave intact.
- **Follow-on (FO):** The expelled faction's allies get an "exiles seek redress" Intrigue, and local Petition dip if Actors depended on the Kontor.
- **Introduces (Action):** Revoke Franchise (Charter-seizure bypassing the Quo Warranto window, only vs a *foreign* Charter once its leverage has eroded).
- **Loop:** Available only once the holder's upstream Prosperity has independently declined (read off their sheet, not caused by you); taking it redirects trade immediately but writes a durable Grudge/Hated tag seeding long-tail Intrigue.

### HEV-BLOC-05 — Barbary Tribute System (16th–19th c.) · Friction
- **Historical grounding:** Tribute paid to Barbary states to avoid piracy on shipping; source: NMAD "Piracy and Passports"; Wikipedia Barbary Wars; Clements Library.
- **Trigger:** `coastal Treasury above a raider-attractiveness threshold AND no naval Fortify/Muster on file AND raider aggression active`
- **Response branches:** Without prior investment the fork collapses to Comply-only (a recurring per-season Treasury levy + Debt tag); with investment, Defy (a punitive fleet) becomes available.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Unresolved tribute Debt auto-seeds a "tribute unsustainable" Crisis (the shift to the First/Second Barbary Wars).
- **Introduces (Action):** Standing Fleet (Fortify+Muster investment gating a Defy branch — the punitive-fleet option literally doesn't exist until banked).
- **Loop:** No investment = mechanically locked into Comply (Treasury bleed + Debt) every firing; investing converts the card into a real fork and a failed Defy escalates it to Crisis — the investment adds an option *and* raises the stakes of using it.

### HEV-BLOC-06 — Napoleon's Continental System (1806–1814) · Friction
- **Historical grounding:** Napoleon's embargo against Britain, enforced across subordinate/allied states; source: Wikipedia Berlin Decree; napoleon-series.org; Mises Institute.
- **Trigger:** `settlement subordinate to a hegemon's active Embargo Directive AND unmet Needs from foregone trade exceed available AP/Treasury, mid Π`
- **Response branches:** Comply (full enforcement, unmet-Need strain), Bargain (petition a License exception via Treat — a chit called in later), Defy/Divert (quiet non-enforcement, Standing-debt, smuggling-Reputation risk).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Widespread License exceptions seed a smuggling Opportunity/Intrigue, and accumulated hegemon Standing-debt seeds an "embargo is dead in practice" Crisis forcing the hegemon's own Directive to be revised.
- **Introduces (Action):** License Exception (Treat-chit extension of the Embargo Directive — a bounded licensed corridor, each a Debt-family chit).
- **Loop:** Strict Comply crashes local Prosperity/Order (Π up, smuggling cards seeded); Treat-exceptions relieve local pressure but each is a standing Debt chit that bloc-wide erodes the embargo — individually rational governor choices *are* the historical system's self-undermining collapse, reproduced emergently.

### HEV-BLOC-07 — Union Blockade / Anaconda Plan (1861–1865) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Union naval blockade of the Confederacy during the American Civil War; source: Encyclopedia Virginia; Wikipedia; USNI Naval History.
- **Trigger:** `rival Prosperity sourced overwhelmingly from one export (single-commodity Develop tag) AND hostile naval Muster + river-chokepoint Conquest targets active`
- **Response branches:** Export Prosperity progressively zeroed as chokepoints fall; the blockaded governor narrows to Comply (Develop-diversify, slow) or Sponsor a blockade-runner (Treasury clawback at Order/Corruption risk).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Sustained success seeds successive Crisis escalations (garrison funding collapse), a broken runner network seeds a corruption Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Blockade-Runner Sponsorship (Sponsor defender-exploit) / Naval Blockade Directive+Conquest (attacker).
- **Loop:** A prior single-commodity Develop concentration is disproportionately vulnerable; the defender's only short-of-military lever is runner-Sponsorship, which recovers Treasury but risks Corruption seeding further Crises — the commodity-concentration choice made seasons earlier decides survivable vs existential.

### HEV-BLOC-08 — ABCD Line & US Oil Embargo on Japan (1940–41) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Coalition embargo (American-British-Chinese-Dutch) cutting off strategic resources ahead of Pearl Harbor; source: Wikipedia ABCD Encirclement / Pearl Harbor prelude; Defense Media Network.
- **Trigger:** `rival Fiscal Stance draws a supermajority of one strategic resource from a single external source AND coalition Grudge/Standing crosses a high threshold at high Π`
- **Response branches:** The coalition embargo *removes the Bargain branch* — only Comply (capitulate, forced Charter/territory concessions) or Defy (Muster into open war).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Defy auto-seeds a full Conquest cycle, Comply seeds a hawkish internal Ambition chain (coup/hardline succession).
- **Introduces (Action):** Multilateral Embargo (coalition-coordinated Directive gated through Ministry/Consulta).
- **Loop:** A prior single-resource Fiscal-Stance concentration makes the faction targetable; the coordinated embargo collapses the three-way fork to binary, and either choice feeds `faction_action.py` Conquest/Muster weighting next season — the card structurally forecloses a core response option.

### HEV-BLOC-09 — Berlin Blockade (1948–49) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Soviet blockade of land/water routes to West Berlin, countered by the Allied airlift; source: State Dept Office of the Historian; Wikipedia.
- **Trigger:** `settlement is an enclave (no contiguous land supply route) AND a rival's Standing/Muster advantage in the surround crosses a threshold`
- **Response branches:** Land-Levy/supply auto-fails; if an alternate-access FacilityTier was banked, Sponsor an Alternate Corridor (ongoing AP+Treasury, bleeds Π down); if not, Order/Prosperity decays toward loss (Conquest-by-attrition, no roll).
- **Follow-on (FO):** A sustained corridor seeds a "different pretext" Friction, a failed corridor seeds automatic territory-transfer.
- **Introduces (Action):** Alternate Corridor (Sponsor extension, hard-preconditioned on a prior Fortify airbase/corridor FacilityTier — cannot be reacted into existence mid-Crisis).
- **Loop:** Survival is set entirely by a Fortify investment made years earlier; the Crisis is a retroactive audit; Sponsoring the corridor once it fires bleeds Π down and denies the attrition-Conquest, converting siege into a stalemate the besieger must formally lift.

### HEV-BLOC-10 — Suez Crisis & Canal Closure (1956) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Nationalization of the Suez Canal and the ensuing international crisis; source: Wikipedia Suez; History.com Aswan; Forbes.
- **Trigger:** `a foreign faction's prior Sponsor/Treat funding to a chokepoint settlement is withdrawn (Friction fires) AND the Charter is foreign-held AND local Grudge is high`
- **Response branches:** Seeds a Nationalize Charter opportunity (seize outside the 16-season window), risking a Charter-holder coalition Conquest — resolved *not* by the battle but escalating to a Ministry/Consulta arbitration that can force reversal or ratify regardless of the battlefield.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Arbitrated reversal writes a `chokepoint charters are contestable by force` Precedent lowering future bypass bars; ratification seeds a long Reputation/Grudge chain.
- **Introduces (Action):** Nationalize Charter (crisis-gated Quo Warranto bypass).
- **Loop:** Only attractive once an external Friction (patron withdrawal) has weakened the holder — you exploit an opportunity the world generated; the outcome depends on Standing/alliances feeding the arbitration as much as the campaign, closing the loop back into faction-ladder Standing.

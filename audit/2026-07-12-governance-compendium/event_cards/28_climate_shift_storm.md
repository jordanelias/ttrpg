# Event Cards — 2.8 Multi-year climate-shift and storm/hurricane fleet destruction (Climatic)

*Key: `historical_concerns_climate_storm_fleet_destruction`. Two spines: **staging/timing exposure** (an anchored/late fleet feeds a shared storm-severity roll — Sea Wall, Storm-Season Withdrawal, Sheltered Dockyard, Flota Scheduling), and **correlated world-level shocks** (the Cooling flag / General Crisis — Remit vs Extract, Civic Granary, Adopt Local Practice, Annona route diversification).*

### HEV-CLIM-01 — Kamikaze typhoons vs the Mongol fleets (1274 & 1281) · Crisis (Opportunity/Ambition follow-on for the defender)
- **Historical grounding:** Two typhoons destroyed invading Mongol fleets off Japan in 1274 and 1281. *Britannica; Woodruff et al., Geology (2014); Wikipedia Mongol invasions/Genkō Bōrui.*
- **Trigger:** `enemy Conquest targets a coastal settlement whose Defense is below the attacker's Mil-advantage AND the fleet staged/anchored ≥1 season within the storm-season window AND Π≥8`
- **Response branches:** A prayer Sponsor gives only a flat low bonus — the real fork is Defy (Fortify Sea Wall multiplying the attacker's storm-loss, or a preemptive Muster before the window closes); a storm firing against the attacker drops their Mil + writes a Grudge/Demoralization tag lowering future Muster-weight.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Ignoring resolves Conquest at full Mil; a storm vs the attacker seeds a defender "Divine Reputation" Precedent boosting Recognition odds + an attacker command-failure Demotion Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Sea Wall (new Fortify method lowering coastal storm-Crisis severity) + Storm-Season Staging Exposure (Muster/Conquest timing rule: multi-season anchoring accrues a storm-loss multiplier scaled to season-of-year, rushed Levy/Muster shoddy hulls multiply it further).
- **Loop:** Sea Wall lowers the storm-Crisis roll against an anchored fleet, reducing the attacker's effective Mil; an attacker who Musters on a compressed timeline or overstays the window raises that same roll against itself — both sides feed one shared roll from opposite ends, its outcome (Grudge on the loser, Precedent on the survivor) resetting both factions' future weighting.

### HEV-CLIM-02 — Spanish Armada destroyed by Atlantic storms (1588) · Friction→Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** The Spanish Armada, sent against England, was destroyed largely by Atlantic storms forced upon it by a failed rendezvous. *Wikipedia Armada / Armada in Ireland; INA "Twelve Apostles" (Casaban); NASA Science.*
- **Trigger:** `a Conquest requiring fleet+land-army rendezvous fails its coordination check ("Rendezvous Failure") forcing the fleet onto a high-exposure route while Π is high AND the funding Levy under-supplied Treasury (spoiled-provisions flag)`
- **Response branches:** Comply (retreat as planned: full roll), Bargain (spend AP/Treasury to re-provision/re-route, e.g. a Treat safe-harbor deal), Defy (push the original route: command-Friction + full roll); failure writes Debt/`Weak` on the Crown + Grudge on the failed ally.
- **Follow-on (FO):** A resolved Crisis seeds a Directive-level "Rebuild" (Purpose-Built Galleon), funded by Treasury and gated by repaying the Crisis's Debt tag.
- **Introduces (Action):** Purpose-Built Galleon (new Levy quality/quantity fork: cheap/fast Levy fields more hulls at a higher storm multiplier — the 130-ship Armada; Purpose-Built costs more per hull but lowers it — the Twelve-Apostles asiento, unlocked only after a Crisis fires once).
- **Loop:** Cheap Levy for a larger fleet raises the storm-severity roll once a Friction forces an exposed route; the Crisis's Debt/Reputation tags then gate the Treasury needed to fund the Purpose-Built upgrade — one bad Friction/Crisis chain can lock a faction out of exactly the mitigation it now needs most.

### HEV-CLIM-03 — The Great Hurricane of 1780 · Crisis (symmetric, multi-faction)
- **Historical grounding:** A single hurricane in the Caribbean caused massive fleet losses across multiple rival naval powers simultaneously present in the region. *Wikipedia; Britannica; JAR (2022); American Heritage.*
- **Trigger:** `≥2 rival factions each maintain Conquest/Muster fleet presence in the same contested territory through the storm window (Π≥8 territory-wide) without a Storm-Season Withdrawal`
- **Response branches:** No Directive fork (it's positioning) — "Hold Station" (full roll against every co-present fleet) or "Storm-Season Withdrawal" (cede tempo, no roll); a fired Crisis reduces Mil for all present roughly proportional to fleet size, symmetrically — it does not by itself shift relative standing.
- **Follow-on (FO):** A shared "Rebuilding Race" Opportunity — whoever funds a compensatory Levy fastest regains Mil/tempo first, converting a symmetric Crisis into an asymmetric recovery contest.
- **Introduces (Action):** Storm-Season Withdrawal (Muster/Conquest timing sub-choice, parallel to Levy's Encabezamiento fork — the Royal Navy's real seasonal withdrawal convention).
- **Loop:** Holding presence through the window raises trigger/severity for *every* co-present rival; because losses are correlated the storm decision doesn't change relative standing — pre-storm Treasury/Ledger state (who funds the post-Crisis Levy fastest) is the actual lever deciding who loses ground.

### HEV-CLIM-04 — Great Famine of 1315–1317 (climate-shift framing) · Crisis (multi-season, Needs-driven)
- **Historical grounding:** Sustained crop failures across Northern Europe from a climate shift produced multi-year famine and governance strain. *Wikipedia; Ludlow et al./Baek et al. (2020), Comm Earth & Env.*
- **Trigger:** `a famine-relief Need unserved ≥2 seasons compounding Π via Σ(unserved Needs) until Π≥8 with Prosperity below floor`
- **Response branches:** Comply (Extract/Tax held constant despite failed harvests: Order−1/PS−1 stack, Grudge, `Hated`), Bargain (petition the PA for remission: softens terms at suspicion cost), Defy/Divert (redirect Levy to relief: protects the settlement, Standing-debt up-tier, Disposition/PS up).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Unserved 3+ seasons escalates to Order-collapse/revolt + a rival-patron-recruiting Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Civic Granary (fourth Develop funding method, Bruges/Ypres/London civic reserves, lowering both the famine severity roll and the Σ(unserved Needs) Π contribution) + Relief (new Directive type the PA issues instead of Extract/Tax).
- **Loop:** Prior Civic Granary spend lowers this card's trigger weight/severity; a governor who instead Complies with a fixed Extract/Tax through the famine accelerates Π and locks in Grudge/`Hated`, reducing future Develop/Levy yields — the P5 Ledger-memory spiral anchored to a documented mitigation against a documented failure mode (fixed in-kind rent through failed harvests).

### HEV-CLIM-05 — General/Global Crisis of the 17th century (Maunder Minimum onset) · Crisis (world/multi-settlement correlated)
- **Historical grounding:** A world-wide cooling episode (Maunder Minimum) correlated with simultaneous crises across many polities in the 17th century. *Parker, Global Crisis (Yale 2013); Wikipedia General Crisis.*
- **Trigger:** `a world-level "Cooling Epoch" flag persists N year-arcs, applying a uniform Π-baseline increase + Prosperity-ceiling reduction across every settlement (inflating Σ(unserved Needs) system-wide)`
- **Response branches:** Per-settlement it plays as ordinary Comply/Bargain/Defy but *correlated* — factions issuing Extract/Tax repeatedly accumulate Grudge/Suspicion/Debt across their whole roster in the same seasons, triggering multiple simultaneous Demotion Magnitude events on their own ladder; Remit-issuing factions absorb the shock at Treasury cost.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Sustained rigid response seeds a cascading multi-settlement Crisis chain — several settlements independently crossing into collapse within the same 1–2 year-arc window.
- **Introduces (Action):** Remit (new Directive type: tax/tribute forgiveness at Treasury cost; a relief-configured Ministry (Part 7) gets an AP/Treasury discount on Remit during a Cooling flag — Parker's "flexible institutions weather the shock").
- **Loop:** The Cooling flag uniformly raises Π and lowers ceilings everywhere, so the PA's aggregate Extract-vs-Remit choice (and whether it built a relief Ministry) decides whether the shared shock is isolated survivable Crises or a simultaneous cascade — and a cascade forces concurrent Demotion Magnitude events stripping the governors needed to manage recovery, deepening the next Π spike.

### HEV-CLIM-06 — Collapse of the Norse Greenland colonies (c. 1350–1450) · Ambition/Crisis (slow-burn, multi-decade)
- **Historical grounding:** The Norse Greenland colonies declined and vanished over roughly a century amid climatic cooling and metropole disinvestment. *Wikipedia Norse Greenland/Hvalsey; Brown/D'Andrea et al., PNAS (2011).*
- **Trigger:** `a distant settlement's Prosperity ceiling declines across year-arcs under a persistent Cooling flag AND it holds a Compact/Debt to a metropole AND its Standing-4+ governor accrues Suspicion via repeated Court Attendance failures (§1.0c)`
- **Response branches:** Comply (maintain metropole Develop practice, keep servicing the Compact: Prosperity keeps declining), Bargain (petition for resupply: mild relief if Treasury/attention available, increasingly unlikely), Defy/Divert (Adopt Local Practice: a Disposition hit with the metropole for real resilience).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Sustained Court Attendance failure + unresolved Debt seeds the metropole's implicit "abandonment" Directive — no more ships, a slow Dismissal-equivalent.
- **Introduces (Action):** Adopt Local Practice (new Develop method-fork for isolated/frontier settlements: trade a Precedent/Disposition shift with the metropole — read as drift/heterodoxy — for a durable resilience bonus against Cooling decline; models Thule marine subsistence vs Norse mixed-farming rigidity, flagging the post-2011 isotopic nuance that diet did shift without full technique adoption).
- **Loop:** A settlement that never spends on Adopt Local Practice keeps bleeding Prosperity, lowering its AP and making both the pivot and Court Attendance's Treasury cost less affordable; on the metropole side, failing Court Attendance/Compact raises Suspicion and reduces willingness to fund resupply Sponsors — a two-sided disinvestment spiral ending in implicit abandonment rather than one dramatic Crisis.

### HEV-CLIM-07 — The Great Storm of 1703 · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** A catastrophic storm struck England, destroying warships moored in exposed roadsteads. *Wikipedia; RMG Greenwich; History.com.*
- **Trigger:** `a naval-basing settlement's Fortify method on file is "Roadstead Mooring" (exposed/cheap) rather than "Sheltered Dockyard" AND Π≥8 within the storm window`
- **Response branches:** Comply (accept the roll: full ship/Mil loss proportional to fleet moored in the open), Defy-in-advance (pay to relocate to Sheltered Dockyard before the window closes, sharply reducing severity).
- **Follow-on (FO):** A fired Crisis unlocks a Develop "Lighthouse" (Eddystone rebuild) + an "Underwriting" Opportunity (marine-insurance Sponsor/Treat mitigating future storm Treasury loss at an ongoing premium).
- **Introduces (Action):** Sheltered Dockyard (new Fortify basing-location method) + Lighthouse (Develop follow-on locked until a storm Crisis fires once).
- **Loop:** Cheap Roadstead-Mooring saves AP now but raises the storm roll's trigger/severity; the Crisis's resolution is what unlocks the Sheltered Dockyard upgrade and the Lighthouse/Underwriting follow-ons — the mitigation most needed is gated behind having first suffered the loss.

### HEV-CLIM-08 — 1622 hurricane destruction of the Spanish treasure fleet (Nuestra Señora de Atocha) · Friction→Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** A hurricane destroyed the Spanish treasure fleet, including the Nuestra Señora de Atocha, after a delayed departure pushed it into the storm season. *Wikipedia Atocha; Mel Fisher's Treasures; EBSCO.*
- **Trigger:** `a Levy convoy (scheduled flota method) has an unresolved "Loading Delay" Friction still open when the departure window closes, pushing actual departure into the storm-season Π-high band`
- **Response branches:** Comply (depart late: full roll against Treasury-in-transit), Bargain (spend AP/Treasury to expedite loading before the window), Defy (delay a full further season: Standing-debt/tempo but no roll).
- **Follow-on (FO):** A fired Crisis (total convoy loss) seeds a Sponsor/Opportunity "Salvage Concession" recovering partial value over seasons for a Compact-like concession to salvor Actors/Guilds.
- **Introduces (Action):** Flota Scheduling (new Levy scheduled-convoy method with a hard seasonal storm-avoidance bonus inside its window / penalty if a Loading-Delay Friction is left unresolved past it — the Casa de Contratación fixed-calendar doctrine and its 1622 failure) + Expedite (Friction-resolution spend).
- **Loop:** Leaving the Loading-Delay Friction unresolved pushes departure later (raising the storm trigger against that shipment); a fired Crisis destroys that season's remittance, reducing next season's AP/Treasury — including the funds to expedite next year's convoy — while the Salvage Concession offers only slow Compact-gated recovery.

### HEV-CLIM-09 — Late Antique Little Ice Age (536–660 AD) + onset of the Justinianic Plague · Crisis chain (Friction→multi-settlement)
- **Historical grounding:** A prolonged climatic cooling event coincided with the onset of the Justinianic Plague, straining the Byzantine Empire's centralized grain-supply system. *Wikipedia LALIA; Büntgen et al., Nature Geoscience (2016).*
- **Trigger:** `a world-level Cooling flag centered on one high-yield route feeding a centralized Annona-style Levy combines with Crown Treasury already committed to a concurrent Conquest (Treasury-depleted flag) and Π≥8 in the grain-source province`
- **Response branches:** Comply (maintain the centralized route through the shock: if the source province itself Crises, empire-wide Remit capacity collapses simultaneously), Bargain (petition for diversified routes: costly, slow), Defy/Divert (issue Remit in affected provinces despite the Conquest spend: protects Order at severe Treasury strain, risking the campaign's funding).
- **Follow-on (FO):** An unmitigated Crisis on the source province cascades into simultaneous Crisis draws on every settlement dependent on that route + frontier-faction Intrigue exploiting the fiscal+plague strain.
- **Introduces (Action):** Annona Route Diversification (Levy centralization-vs-redundancy fork, parallel to Encabezamiento/Flota Scheduling; models Justinian's Egyptian-grain dependency and mirrors the stale-Assessment failure mode at empire scale) + Directive Remit.
- **Loop:** Centralizing relief on one province's output lowers per-season cost but means that province's own Crisis removes the empire's Remit capacity everywhere the route feeds, converting a regional shock into a correlated cascade; a Crown also spending Treasury on a concurrent Conquest has less capacity to fund emergency Remit or diversification — the Levy/Conquest choices that build tempo directly shrink the slack needed to survive the shock on the chosen chokepoint.

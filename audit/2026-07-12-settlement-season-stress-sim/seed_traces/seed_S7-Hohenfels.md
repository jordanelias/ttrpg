## VERDICT
HELD: The core homeostat, draw-count, band→family mapping, Mandate feedback (which correctly bled PS while q_s outran Mandate, then self-arrested at parity in S3), the dice/d_sigma odds model, and the substrate's one-Key-three-consumers ripple (FA consumes-edge / stakes.source_key / FI concealment inventory) all resolved every card cleanly with no invented mechanic. The marquee 1.0d patron-fall auto-lapse interaction fired exactly as specified and produced a genuinely ambiguous outcome — mechanical relief (cascade halted) that simultaneously stripped all protection.

BROKE (adversarial findings): (1) The audit the seed was built to stress never demoted Gerta — the patron's fall defused it — yet she still fell, through the Recall/settlement-collapse route the 1.0d framing does not reconcile with; 'audit protection' is illusory (Gap 5). (2) 'Missed Obligation,' the audit's own trigger, is undefined, so whether the cascade advanced was a hand ruling (Gap 2). (3) Clerk Capacity kept paying +1 AP from a clerkship that had defected, with no severance rule (Gap 6). (4) A New-Grant conferred by a patron who then vacated has no defined fate (Gap 3). (5) An absentee governor resolves every local contest with no presence penalty or delegate-of-record (Gap 4). (6) No personal attribute block exists for any social/FI pool (Gap 1). Of the six gaps, none is cleanly closed by a rejected proposal — four are 'partial' (VEN-FA-1 Relazione, FA-JP1 resident steward, VEN-SE-7 roving audit, IT-4 seizable asset each touch a facet the cut removed) and two ('no'). The live re-litigation surfaces one recurring theme: several cuts (the resident-steward tier, the roving independent auditor, the mandatory performance report) were judged redundant against present machinery, but under an ABSENTEE + audited + orphaned governor they each address a real hole the kept machinery leaves open — worth revisiting for the absentee-governance case specifically, not as general adds.

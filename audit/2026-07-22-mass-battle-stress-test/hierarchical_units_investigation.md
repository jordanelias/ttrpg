# Hierarchical units — cells → subunits → army (FM / Total War model)

**Investigation, 2026-07-22 (Jordan-directed).** How the mass-battle engine realizes the three-level
"troops form subunits in a subformation with instructions; subunits form an army in a formation with
overall instructions" model — mapped against the Football Manager / Total War paradigm, with an honest
read of what is implemented, what is emergent, and where the gaps are. Companion to the **visual
snapshot** delivered as an artifact (10 battles × a 5-tick spread; each dot a cell, coloured by its
subunit, at its continuous field position) — generated by the session workbench script
`hierarchy_snapshot.py` (pure SVG display geometry; kept as a session tool rather than committed into
the engine tree, available to formalize on request).

---

## 1. The three levels, as implemented

| Level | Code | Carries | Spatial identity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cell** (troop block) | a `(orig_r, orig_c)` slot in a subunit; live float pos in `_node_pos` | a count of troops (`cell_troops`), a facing (`cell_facing_vec`) | continuous `(r, c)` on the coordinate field — **no snapping** on the live path |
| **Subunit** | `hierarchy.units.Subunit` | `shape`, `instructions`, `stance`, `role`, `troop_type`, `tier`, `orders` (timed), `starting_position` | its cells, laid into `shape` by `footprint_for(shape, troops, concentration)` |
| **Army** | `hierarchy.units.Unit` | `subunits[]`, `stance`, `command`, `speed`, `morale`, `dr` | the emergent spatial arrangement of its subunits |

**Cell → subunit.** `footprint_for(shape, troops, concentration)` lays the subunit's troops into its
`shape`'s cell pattern (`geometry.CELL_PATTERN_FN`: `Line`, `Arrowhead`, `GappedLine`, `Column`). Each
cell holds `CELL_FLOOR(40)..CELL_CAP(200)` troops; the cell *count* is emergent from troop-count ÷
density. On the live field the cells are **nodes** held in relative formation by `PC_NODE_COHESION` — the
shape is the *initial* layout, and cohesion (relational offsets), not a re-imposed pattern, keeps the
cells together as the subunit moves/wheels/rotates (`Subunit._node_pos`, `cell_facing_vec`).

**Subunit → army.** Three constructors compose subunits into an army (`engine.py`):
- `build_army(specs, …)` — explicit per-subunit specs (shape/troop_type/troops/stance/instructions/
  role/position); a general battle line.
- `build_envelopment(center_specs, wing_specs, …)` — the **Cannae** preset: a holding center + two wings
  that hold, then a **timed `Order` (`tick:{release_tick}`)** releases them into `stance='balanced'` +
  `'envelop'` — they wheel wide to close behind the enemy line.
- `build_refused_flank(strong_specs, refused_specs, …)` — the **oblique/Leuctra** preset: a strong wing
  + a refused (echeloned-back) wing.

Crucially (**ED-909 / LC-8**, Jordan-ruled *"why is Horseshoe a subformation instead of an emergent
strategy?"*): the army "formation" is **not** a named field — envelopment / refused-flank are
**emergent multi-body compositions** of `Line`/`Arrowhead`/`GappedLine` subunits, not subunit shapes.
Only `Line`/`Arrowhead`/`GappedLine`/`Column` are valid subunit shapes; the army shape is *where you put
the subunits and what you tell them to do*.

## 2. The instruction stack (three layers) — the FM / Total War analog

Instructions live at **three** composable levels, exactly the FM "team instructions → player
instructions → in-match shouts" (and Total War "army stance → unit formation → orders") stack:

1. **Army-level:** `Unit.stance` (`balanced`/`hold`/…), `command`, `speed` — the general's overall
   posture. (`stance='hold'` is an immediate early-return in `_node_advance` — freezes the body.)
2. **Subunit-level:** `Subunit.instructions` — `brace`, `hold`, `charge`, `advance`, `envelop`, `pin`,
   `screen`, `pursue`, `yield`, `kite`, `loose`, `harass`, `lure`, `volley`, `shoot_move` (see
   `config.ROLE_SPEC`) — a per-subunit tactic package, plus `role` (a named menu, `TROOP_TYPE_ROLES`:
   `ShieldWall`/`Skirmish`/`Shock`/…) that defaults a shape + instructions.
3. **Timed orders:** `Subunit.orders` — a `Tuple[Order]` fired by trigger (`immediate` / `tick:N` /
   `enemy_range:D` / `ally_at:D`), each setting behavior fields when it fires (`core.contact.check_orders`).
   This is how the envelopment wings "hold, then wheel at tick 4."

The **visual snapshot** shows this live: in the Cannae battle the wing subunits carry `[—]` at tick 0
and pick up `[envelop]` at tick 5 (released by their `tick:4` order), then wheel outward while the
`[—]` center advances straight.

## 3. Mapping to Football Manager / Total War

| FM / Total War | Valoria engine | Match |
|---|---|---|
| a **player** / a **soldier** | a **cell** (troop block) at a field position | ✓ (a cell is a coherent troop block, the atomic maneuver element) |
| **positions in a formation** (a unit's shape) | a **subunit**'s `shape` + its cells | ✓ (Line/Arrowhead/GappedLine/Column) |
| **player/unit instructions** (mentality, role) | `Subunit.instructions` + `role` | ✓ |
| **team shape + team instructions** | the **army** (`Unit`) composition + `stance`/`command` | ✓ (emergent composition, ED-909) |
| **in-match shouts / timed tactical changes** | timed `Order`s (`tick:N`, `enemy_range:D`) | ✓ |
| **the manager / general** | `Unit.command` (governs morale, order-issuing, formation-hold speed) | ✓ |

The model **is** the FM/Total War hierarchy, and in one respect goes further than Total War: subunits
maneuver on a **continuous** field with per-cell facing and cohesion (a subunit can wheel/refuse/wrap as
a deformable body), rather than rigid unit-blocks on a grid.

## 4. Honest gaps (what the visual snapshot and the code expose)

1. **Subformation legibility is troop-count-dependent.** Cell count = troops ÷ density
   (`CELL_FLOOR..CELL_CAP`). A subunit with ~150–200 troops at concentration 100 is **1–2 cells** — the
   subformation *shape* is not expressed (the snapshot needed ~700–800 troops/subunit at concentration
   50 to render a legible Line/Arrowhead). For the FM/Total-War feel to read at the subunit level, a
   subunit needs enough troops to be many cells. *Design question for Jordan: a minimum cells-per-subunit
   floor, or a UI that renders sub-cell formations at low counts?*
2. **Cells within a subunit are homogeneous.** Per-cell `troop_type`/`power`/`discipline` **do not exist
   yet** (`exchange.pair_pool_contribution` docstring: *"per-cell type doesn't exist yet"*). So a subunit
   cannot yet be veteran-front / levy-rear — the FM analog of "every player in a unit is identical." The
   per-cell *count* axis is live (density), but the per-cell *quality* axis is the acknowledged next
   architecture (confirmed byte-inert today per ED-MB-0007).
3. **The army formation is emergent, not authorable as a named shape.** By design (ED-909) — a strength
   for emergence, but there is no single "army formation" object to inspect/edit; it is implicit in the
   subunit positions + orders. A UI/authoring layer that *names* the emergent posture (envelopment,
   refused flank, echelon) would help without re-introducing subunit-level army-shapes.
4. **Subunit count ceiling 11** (ED-1090, videogame cap) vs Command clamp 1–7 — commanding >7 subunits
   implies a not-yet-built subordinate-officer / command-exceeding mechanism (flagged open).
5. **Instruction reachability on the live path.** Some instructions (`envelop`/`sweep`/`kite`) only
   became reachable on the default field path after ED-MB-0001; the instruction set is broad but its
   per-instruction field-path coverage is worth a census (partly done in the stress harness S3).

## 5. Verdict

The cell → subunit → army hierarchy is **correctly and cleanly implemented** and maps faithfully onto
the FM/Total War paradigm, with a genuine advance (continuous, deformable subunit bodies with per-cell
facing/cohesion). The two substantive gaps are both *quality/granularity* rather than structure:
**per-cell heterogeneity** (veteran-front/levy-rear) and **subformation legibility at low troop counts**.
Neither is a defect in the hierarchy; both are the next architectural increments to make the subunit
level as rich as the army level already is.

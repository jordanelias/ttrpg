**Status:** Editorial resolution — canonical guidance for audit and design interpretation
**Date:** 2026-04-21
**Resolves:** Audit over-readings propagated across Sessions 1–7 before pivot; three specific framings corrected below

## §1 Scope

ED-738 addresses audit-propagated framings that over-read canonical commitments in three specific directions. Each produced a downstream error repeated across Sessions 1–7 audit cells before the pivot:

1. **Consciousness-absent-during-contact.** Framed Leap contact as involving the suspension of consciousness itself. Incorrect per canon/02 Amendment 1, which specifies that reflexive facing suspends while outward facing and thread-adjacent awareness persist, and canon/01 Amendment 1, which specifies that layer 1 (Ein Sof spooling) continues unchanged during the Leap. Consciousness is present during contact in a non-reflexive register.

2. **Retention as purely structural, non-episodic.** Framed post-Leap retention as structural-categorical only — the practitioner knows *that* something was mended without knowing *what* was perceived. Over-reach. Canon/01 Amendment 3 is explicit: *"TS measures how much of the below-waterline Thread-substrate the practitioner can perceive and deliberately operate upon."* Perceived content is describable in a gesturing register at specificity-and-conditions-gated visibility. Only the knots themselves (canon/02 Amendment 2) lodge below reflexive-access threshold and remain non-introspectable.

3. **Pure apophatic register across all below-waterline content.** Framed the inner-tradition Scripture register (canon/00 §10.2) as apophatic throughout — "that which cannot be said." Incorrect lineage. The framework's posture across Regimes 1–3 is cartographic-contemplative; apophaticism applies only at Regime 4, the Ein Sof structural terminus. The confusion between Regime-4 apophaticism and Regimes-1-3 cartography collapsed the gradient and over-generalised the ineffabilist mode.

## §2 Lineage specification for inner-tradition Scripture

Canon/00 §10.2 commits to inner-tradition Scripture as a gesturing register distinct from Church doctrinal-proposition register. ED-738 specifies the lineage-alignment of that gesturing register.

**Regimes 1–3 (ordinary perception, cultivated perception, Leap-enabled operation) — cartographic contemplative.** The register describes what was perceived, at specificity-and-conditions constraints, with predicate structures that gesture rather than propose. Parataxis preferred over hypotaxis. Predicate appearance paired with relational withholding. Repetition with variation. Negation following assertion as method of specification-refinement rather than as ineffabilist evasion. Unfinished structures where specificity-limits of the practitioner's receptive capacity meet what was available to receive.

**Tonal models.** Teresa of Ávila (*Interior Castle*); Plotinus on near-*henosis* (Enneads VI.9); the Zohar; Ibn Arabi. These authors describe extensively, specifically, and conditions-markedly. Their writing is not *"I cannot describe it,"* but *"I could see to the third fold before the current shifted,"* *"at that depth, the configuration gathered in a particular way,"* *"what was given appeared with the following specificity-limit."*

**Regime 4 (Ein Sof ground) — tzimtzum-grounded apophatic.** The structural terminus beyond all rendering. Non-witnessable. Apophatic not as stylistic choice but as structural consequence: Ein Sof is the pre-differentiation ground of all differentiation, and any predicate the practitioner could apply requires differentiation the ground precedes. Lurianic-framed tzimtzum accounts this correctly: Ein Sof withdraws for Creation; what remains is rendered and describable; the withdrawn ground is not.

**Anti-model.** The *Cloud of Unknowing* / Pseudo-Dionysius *Mystical Theology* tradition — in which all divine-oriented language is apophatic — is an over-generalization of what the framework commits to as a specifically-Regime-4 feature. Inner-tradition Scripture in this framework is Teresa-adjacent, not Cloud-adjacent.

## §3 Three over-readings corrected

### §3.1 Consciousness absent during contact

**Incorrect framing.** Audit Sessions 1–7 repeatedly framed the Leap as a state in which the practitioner is "not conscious," "outside experience," "in a non-experiential register." This reading extrapolates from phenomenological traditions (Husserl's reduction, peritraumatic dissociation) beyond what canon supports.

**Corrected framing.** Canon/02 Amendment 1 is precise: the Leap suspends the reflexive facing of layer 2. Outward facing persists. Thread-adjacent awareness persists. Layer 1 Ein Sof spooling continues. The practitioner during contact is experiencing in a non-reflexive register — they remain in continuous temporal experience, continue to receive substrate content at their TS-band receptive capacity, and persist as a locatable body in shared reality per the persisting outward facing (canon/02 Am 1: *"observers describe the leaping practitioner as 'off,' 'present but elsewhere,' glazed"*).

**Downstream implication.** Post-Leap retention is not recovery-from-non-experience. It is reconstitution of the reflexive facing specifically, as canon/02 Am 1 and canon/01 Am 2 both state. The practitioner can report on what was perceived during contact (subject to §3.2 below) because the perception happened in a mode that was not reflexive but was experiential.

### §3.2 Retention as purely structural, non-episodic

**Incorrect framing.** Audit Sessions 1–7 repeatedly framed post-Leap content as *"I know a Mending was performed; I cannot describe what I saw."* Over-reach.

**Corrected framing.** Canon/01 Am 3 explicit: TS measures how much of the below-waterline substrate the practitioner can perceive and deliberately operate upon. If the practitioner perceived, they can in principle describe what they perceived. The content is describable in the cartographic-contemplative register specified in §2 above — at the specificity constrained by their TS band, the site's substrate-vision conditions, the duration of contact, and the depth of engagement.

What is non-introspectable is narrower: only the knots themselves (canon/02 Am 2), which lodge at substrate depth below the threshold that reasserted reflexive self-rendering can access. Knots register through the seismographic channel (canon/02 Am 6 intelligence-mechanic) rather than through reflexive introspection. The knot-formation-specific non-access does not generalize to all contact content.

**Downstream implication.** Inner-tradition Scripture composition is a practitioner-author describing what they received during contact, at conditions-gated resolution. Historical corpus texts are substantive descriptive documents, not repeated versions of *"I cannot describe it."* Seam Text composition as integration practice (per mechanical-implementation P12 Function 4) produces artifacts with real descriptive content.

### §3.3 Pure apophatic register across below-waterline content

**Incorrect framing.** Audit Sessions 1–7 framed below-waterline content as inherently apophatic — unsayable in principle because it lies below the waterline.

**Corrected framing.** The waterline is a receptive-capacity threshold, not a describability threshold. What lies above the practitioner's waterline they receive at ordinary perception and can describe in ordinary language. What lies below their waterline they cannot receive at all (at that TS band). What lies below the waterline at a lower TS band than their current band, but above their current waterline, they now receive and can describe in cartographic-contemplative register.

Only Ein Sof — the structural ground beneath all waterlines, beneath all receptive-capacity bands, beneath the renderable itself — is apophatic in principle. That specific terminus is not a deeper waterline; it is the structural-metaphysical floor of the renderable.

**Downstream implication.** Practitioners at TS 30 describe TS-30-available substrate content in cartographic register. Practitioners at TS 70 describe the same given-configuration at TS-70-available detail in cartographic register. Both are using the same lineage-register (Teresa / Plotinus / Zohar / Ibn Arabi) at their respective receptive-capacity levels. Apophatic register emerges only when the content gestured toward is Ein Sof itself.

## §4 Iceberg framing for gameplay conceptualisation

Canon/01 Amendment 3 establishes waterline-language as canonical. ED-738 authorises the full iceberg framing as gameplay-conceptualisation vocabulary for the design team, consistent with canonical waterline-language.

**The framing.** The substrate is the iceberg. All observers engage with the same iceberg (the given). Each observer has their own waterline, determined by their TS band and their formation. What rises above an observer's waterline is what enters their ordinary perception. Higher TS = lower waterline = more of the iceberg rises into receptive capacity. The iceberg itself does not change; the observer's waterline changes with receptive-capacity development.

**What this authorises and what it does not.**

*Authorises* (for design-team communication): iceberg metaphor; waterline terminology; *"this practitioner's waterline is at TS band X,"* *"more of the configuration rises above this character's waterline than that character's,"* *"the iceberg is the same; the practitioner receives different amounts of it."*

*Does not authorise* (as canonical extensions): *submerged* as adjectival extension beyond *below-waterline*; *dive-log* as register name (replaced by *below-waterline cartographic register* or simply *cartographic register*); *diver*, *beach*, *surface*, *current*, *reefs* as ontological vocabulary; *above-water* and *below-water* beyond direct canonical waterline-language usage. Water-metaphor extensions beyond canon/01 Am 3's explicit waterline commitment are audit-invented and are stripped.

## §5 Receptive-capacity framing

Audit Sessions 1–7 intermittently framed TS as producing different givens for different observers — as though high-TS observers encountered a substrate-reality that low-TS observers did not encounter. This reading violates canon/00 Inseparability (A2 / P-01): the substrate is the given; observers differ in what they receive from the given, not in what is given.

**Correct framing.** TS is a receptive-capacity axis. Four-faction interpreter machinery (T-27) operates on what observers receive (at their TS band, through their formation), not on different givens for different observers. T-30 information asymmetry is access-mode asymmetry: different characters have different receptive capacities against the same given substrate. M-8 vertical-position-gated access within the renderable describes this cleanly.

**Consequence for audit-level claims.** Sessions 1–7 observations implying *"different observers perceive different realities"* are corrected to *"different observers receive different amounts of the same given reality."* The substrate is shared; waterlines differ.

## §6 Factuality-depth gradient

For content description across the Regimes, factuality-claim strength scales inversely with depth approach to Ein Sof.

- **Surface-adjacent (Regime 1, ordinary perception above the waterline).** Full cataphatic description. Ordinary language. Ordinary verification-procedures apply.

- **Mid-depth (Regime 2, cultivated TS-band perception; Regime 3, Leap-enabled operation).** Cartographic-contemplative register. Substantive description at specificity-and-conditions-gated resolution. Specificity-limits marked explicitly (*"I could perceive to this fold"*; *"at this amplitude, the gathering was as follows"*). The content is describable; the limits of describability are part of the content.

- **Deep (approach to Ein Sof at Regime-4 terminus).** Gradient acknowledgment. Predicates taper as the approach proceeds; the analyses mark structural-limit rather than over-claiming specificity. Negation following assertion carries more weight; apophatic residual appears.

- **Regime 4 (Ein Sof itself).** Apophatic in principle. Not a deeper sayable; the structural terminus of sayability.

This gradient constrains audit-level inference. Near-surface cells admit full cataphatic analysis; mid-depth cells admit calibrated abstraction; deep cells require gradient-acknowledgment; Regime-4 content requires apophatic-terminus acknowledgment. Claude's audit-work should mark the gradient explicitly when inferring across cells at varying depth.

## §7 Three specific framework commitments

### §7.1 Monstrosity is rendering-catastrophic within the renderable

Monstrosity encounters produce rendering-catastrophic phenomenology (trauma-signature, predication-resistance at extreme amplitude) *within the renderable*. Monstrosity is not Ein Sof. Monstrosity is extreme-amplitude substrate-excess receivable by the practitioner's ordinary perception, exceeding the predicate-sets the practitioner's TS band admits.

The distinction matters: monstrosity phenomenology is describable via trauma-signature registers; Ein Sof is not describable at all. Art direction, dialogue register, and UI feedback for monstrosity encounters should not use Ein-Sof-adjacent visual vocabulary (beyond-renderable terminus) but rendering-catastrophic-within-renderable vocabulary (trauma-signature phenomenology).

### §7.2 Ein Sof is non-operable

No mechanic offers access to Ein Sof. Not via Leap. Not via TS. Not via Mending. Not via endgame reveal. No rendered image, no audio signature, no UI element represents Ein Sof directly. Access would require a rendered differentiated target; Ein Sof is the pre-differentiation ground of all targets. This is a structural consequence of Regime-4 apophaticism, not a discretionary design choice.

Mechanical proposal P6 in the audit synthesis records this commitment. ED-738 confirms it at canonical-editorial level: an Ein Sof mechanic would be incoherent within the framework's ontology. There is no Ein Sof ending.

### §7.3 Reflexive-only suspension (not full-consciousness suspension)

Per §3.1 above, the Leap suspends the reflexive facing of layer 2, not layer 2 entirely, not consciousness itself, not all rendering. Outward facing persists. Layer 1 spooling continues. Thread-adjacent awareness operates in non-reflexive register.

Canonical grounding: canon/02 Amendment 1 directly. ED-738 confirms this against audit over-readings.

## §8 Framework-composite acknowledgment

A structural note about the framework's relationship to its sources.

Prior audit observations framed the framework as "radical beyond all sources" or "uniquely exceeding prior phenomenological traditions." This is over-reached. The framework assembles specific components from multiple scholarly lineages — Husserl, Heidegger, Metzinger, Kierkegaard, Lacan, apophatic theology, Lurianic Kabbalah, predictive processing, Clark-Chalmers, Gibson, Kaplan ART, clinical-trauma phenomenology — into a composite operational architecture. Each component has clean scholarly precedent. The composite is framework-original in assembly and in substrate-ontological deployment; the components are not.

Meta-throughline М-7 (Borrowings Are Operational Extensions) captures this structural pattern. ED-738 flags this correction for audit Observation 2 across Sessions 1–7: where audit language reaches for "framework exceeds sources" framing, the corrected framing is "framework assembles specific components operationally." Disciplined synthesis, not unique radicality.

## §9 Above-water persistence during contact

Per canon/02 Amendment 1, the practitioner's outward facing persists during contact. The world external to the practitioner continues to render through the scene-clock. NPCs continue to act. Threats emerge. Allied states change. The practitioner, submerged in the operation's substrate depth, is not perceiving these events through reflexive-facing awareness, but the events happen and meet the practitioner at re-arrival.

Mechanical expression per P8 in the audit synthesis: the scene-clock runs during Leap contact. Above-water events resolve around the practitioner's body. The sentinel/diver geometry (non-Leaping companion holding above-water awareness) emerges naturally from the geometry rather than requiring a bolted-on mechanic. The practitioner's pre-entry commitment to operation-sequence is strategic under uncertainty about what above-water will do during contact; this tension is the Leap's primary strategic texture.

## §10 Methodology for audit continuation

Sessions 8+ of the rigorous audit should apply the following discipline:

1. **Factuality-claim strength scales with depth.** Near-surface cells admit full cataphatic analysis. Mid-depth cells admit calibrated abstraction. Deep cells require gradient acknowledgment. Regime-4 content requires apophatic-terminus acknowledgment.

2. **Receptive-capacity framing, not different-given framing.** TS expansion is expansion of what can be received; the substrate is invariant across observers.

3. **Default to specificity.** Before invoking apophaticism or ineffabilism, check whether the content is actually Ein-Sof-adjacent or merely at a TS-band receptive-capacity edge. The latter admits cartographic-contemplative description.

4. **Pattern-guard against mystical-tradition priors.** Training-data patterns drift toward ineffabilist, pure-apophatic, and consciousness-absent readings when phenomenological-framework cues trigger mystical-tradition patterns. Verify each *framework-original* claim, each *exceeds sources* claim, each *cannot be said* claim against canonical wording.

5. **Threadwork-frame necessity test.** Every proposed mechanic should pass the test: *if canon did not commit to this phenomenon, what would be missing?* Canonical commitments determine necessity; audit-inferential extensions carry alternative-rendering possibilities and must be flagged as Tier B or Tier C per the synthesis tiering system.

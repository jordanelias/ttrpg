## §0 ED-738 pivot applied throughout (revised framing)

Three over-readings corrected:

1. **Consciousness absent during contact** — incorrect. Canon/02 Am 1: reflexive facing suspends; outward facing and thread-adjacent awareness persist. Consciousness in non-reflexive register, not absent.

2. **Retention as purely structural, non-episodic** — over-reach. The practitioner receives substrate content during contact at depth and conditions-gated visibility per canon/01 Am 3 *how much of the below-waterline Thread-substrate the practitioner can perceive*. Only the knots themselves (canon/02 Am 2) remain below reflexive-access threshold. Contact content generally is reportable in the cartographic register.

3. **Pure apophatic register across all below-water content** — incorrect lineage. Framework posture: cartographic contemplative (Teresa, Ibn Arabi, Zohar, Plotinus) in Regimes 1–3; tzimtzum-grounded apophatic at Ein Sof ground only. Not pure apophatic.

**Pattern guard.** Default to specificity. Monstrosity is rendering-catastrophic within the renderable (describable via trauma-signature phenomenology). Ein Sof is structural terminus beyond rendering (non-witnessable). Different facts.

**Receptive-capacity framing correction.** TS expansion is expansion of what can be received from the given, not what is given to different observers differently. Canon/00 Inseparability A2 / P-01 does not support different-givens-per-observer. Iceberg framing: higher TS = lower waterline = more of the substrate iceberg rises into what can be received at ordinary perception. Same iceberg, different waterline per practitioner.

**Hold vs Turn-Away terminology.** Canon §14 distinguishes remaining in what one is seeing from turning away from it. Turn-Away is phenomenological refusal to receive; Flee is combat-tactical withdrawal. The confrontation binary is Hold vs Turn-Away. Monstrosity encounters contain both dimensions (phenomenological Hold/Turn-Away + tactical Contain/Flee/Survive); they resolve separately.

### Tier A — N-direct proposals (revisions)

#### P1 — Leap UX as two-decision player surface (revised)

During contact: the character's reflexive facing is suspended (canon/02 Am 1); outward facing and thread-adjacent awareness persist; layer 1 Ein Sof spooling continues. The player's two mechanical decisions per Leap cycle:

- **Entry commit.** Target configuration (perceived at TS band), operation sequence (up to Focus − 1), pre-declared exit conditions. Sequence persists through contact per canon/02 Am 1 — mid-contact modification is structurally unavailable because reflexive facing (the mode by which modification-decisions are made) is suspended.
- **Exit timing.** Player elects exit at inter-operation moments; otherwise, Fatigue threshold forces involuntary exit. Pre-declared conditional exits resolve automatically (e.g., "exit if Coherence cost exceeds 2" or "exit at Fatigue 80%").

Contact execution (operation resolution per §2.4 canonical), above-water scene continuance (canon/02 Am 1 outward facing; sentinel-diver geometry for multi-practitioner scenes), and retention degree-differentiation are all system-facing resolution, not player decisions.

Knot registration per canon/02 Am 6 is bookkeeping, not a decision.

**Grounding.** S1-C1, S1-C3, S1-C5, S2-C9, S2-C12, S3-C13, S3-C17.

**N-direct:** canon/02 Am 1 reflexive-only suspension geometry rendered as operational structure; full-sequence-at-entry commit preserves Am 1's structural commitment cleanly.

**Revision note.** Prior v3 P1 framed the Leap as a six-phase pipeline with Diagnosis and Declaration as separate pre-entry phases. Corrected: perception is continuous at TS band (canon/01 Am 3), so no diagnostic phase is required; declaration collapses into entry commit because mid-contact re-declaration would require reflexive access canon/02 Am 1 specifies is suspended.

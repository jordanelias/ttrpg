# PART I: MASTER THROUGHLINE MAP

Fifteen structural throughlines run across the entire design. Each throughline is a cross-system pressure that produces emergent situations without requiring any single document to specify them. They are the game's actual architecture, visible only when the documents are read together.

## TL-01: RS AS THE CAMPAIGN'S METABOLIC SPINE

**Systems involved:** threadwork_v30, conviction_track_v30, calamity_radiation_v30, victory_v30, southernmost_v30, npc_behavior_v30 (Edeyja burnout), arc_register (ARC-P02, ARC-S32, ARC-S34)

RS starts at 28 — already in the Fragile band (59-40), already below the Calamity Drift threshold (50), temporal window already open (RS ≤ 60 from Session 1), Calamity Radiation already active in T6 and T13. The game does not start with a stable world that degrades. It starts mid-decline.

The factions most capable of addressing RS are the least politically powerful: RM (Mandate = CV ≤ 1 count, starts at 2), Wardens (no faction stats, operates through WC track), Varfell (has capability through VTM but needs expedition access). The factions most politically powerful — Church (CI mechanism), Crown (military, Parliament) — are structurally blind to RS unless their practitioners develop TS.

This creates the game's central irony: political victory requires the same resources (Domain Actions, Mandate, territorial control) that RS recovery also requires. The Church-pursuing player uses all their Influence on CI advancement; the RS-pursuing player uses all their Influence on Community Weaving. No single faction can do both simultaneously at full capacity.

**Maximum theoretical RS recovery per season** (all sources at peak): Southernmost Mending cap −5/season + Mending +2 + Community Weaving +2 + Warden Cooperation ≥ 3 +2 = net +11 against passive decline. Passive decline at current game state: winter −1 + 2 Locks −2 + 1 active Gap −4 + Calamity Drift T6/T13 (already CV erosion, not RS directly) = −7/season minimum. At RS 28, the window to arrest decline is approximately 4-6 seasons before Critical.

**Key structural observation:** The recovery systems form a dependency chain. Southernmost Mending requires Expedition access which requires Awareness 8+ which requires Private Collection study or repeated expeditions. Community Weaving requires RM Mandate ≥ 1 which requires CV ≤ 1 territories which requires Calamity Drift (passive) or Varfell Cultural Reclamation. Warden Cooperation requires sustained relationship investment which requires Edeyja's approval which requires demonstrated Thread competence. Every RS recovery path has a prerequisite that requires previous investment. The player who hasn't invested early cannot recover late.

**Throughline health:** Well-specified in individual documents; underspecified as a unified system. The seasonal RS arithmetic (all sources in/out at Accounting) is scattered across six design files with no single canonical summary. A GM running the full game will need to derive this themselves.

## TL-02: THE PRACTITIONER PARADOX (SUPPRESSION AS FACTORY)

**Systems involved:** npc_roster_v30 (Haelgrund TS 12, Torsvald TS 35), character_histories_v30 (Church Investigator TS+3), npc_character_analyses_v30 (Torsvald velocity problem), arc_register (ARC-S17 Coherence Zero: Almud), threadwork_v30 (Discovery Event)

Every institution that systematically suppresses Thread activity inadvertently produces practitioners through exposure. This throughline has four distinct realizations:

1. **Haelgrund (TS 12):** Church's best Inquisitor developed Thread Sensitivity from career exposure to confiscated Einhir materials. His PROCEDURALIST flaw means his investigations — entered at Overwhelming into the Parliamentary record — document his own Thread-perception method. The Church's suppression apparatus produces its own practitioners through the act of confiscation.

2. **Torsvald (TS 35):** Löwenritter's best Riskbreaker developed TS from the Lenneth archive during intelligence operations. Her RISK-AVERSE flaw creates a shrinking operational envelope as RS declines. The institution's covert arm developed a practitioner through its own scholarly intelligence work.

3. **Almud (TS 28 → approaching Discovery Event):** Crown's governing monarch is approaching Thread sensitivity through decades of proximity to RS events without any intentional exposure. His governance framework — Order and Reason — has no category for what he's about to experience.

4. **Church Investigator (PC archetype, TS+3 from vocation):** Character creation produces a player character who mirrors Haelgrund from the inside — someone who doesn't know their own Thread Sensitivity, whose "professional instincts" are Thread perception, and who is heading toward the same internal collapse Haelgrund faces.

**The structural pattern:** Each realization follows the same arc. The institution requires sustained contact with Thread-adjacent material to perform its suppression function. That contact produces TS growth. The practitioner that emerges faces a choice between self-suppression (institutional survival at the cost of self-knowledge) and defection (self-knowledge at the cost of institutional position). The choice reverberates at institutional scale: Haelgrund's defection permanently raises Church Heresy Investigation Ob; Torsvald's exposure reshapes Löwenritter's operational capability.

**Throughline health:** Mechanically strong. The Discovery Event probability math is consistent (Spirit TN 7 Ob 1, ~63% per exposure). The defection arc is specified for Haelgrund; the parallel for Torsvald is specified at narrative level but not fully mechanized. The Church Investigator PC archetype (ED-389) still requires canonical confirmation.

## TL-03: ALMUD'S INDECISION AS STRUCTURAL STABILIZER

**Systems involved:** npc_behavior_v30 (Almud Arc A/B/C), arc_register (ARC-S14, ARC-S17, ARC-S37, ARC-S45, ARC-S26, Collision F, Collision G), conviction_track_v30 (CI suppression via Détente)

Almud's uncertainty about the caste question is not a character weakness. It is the campaign's most important structural load-bearing element.

The Almud-Baralta Détente (ARC-S37) produces CI −1/season passive suppression as long as Baralta Mandate ≥ 4 and Crown is not contesting Hafenmark's parliamentary activity. This is active because of Almud's uncertainty: he has not acted on his Einhir sympathy (which would activate Baralta's Deed Claim and collapse the Détente) and has not opposed Baralta (which would drive her to active opposition). His indecision is the condition that makes their cooperation possible.

Simultaneously, both Lenneth's institutional revival programme and Baralta's suppression-upon-accession programme are simultaneously active precisely because Almud has not chosen. His non-commitment is the permission structure that allows mutually exclusive political programmes to coexist in the same factional space.

When Almud resolves his uncertainty — through Discovery Event (ARC-S17), through the Einhir Triangle being forced (Collision G), or through player-facilitated erosion (ARC-S14) — five things happen simultaneously:
1. Baralta's Deed Claim Activates (ARC-S45): she shifts from CI brake to active Crown claimant
2. Détente collapses: CI suppression brake removed permanently
3. Collision G fires: three mutually exclusive Einhir positions become actively contested
4. Succession Triangle activates (ARC-S35): Almud, Torben, Elske, Baralta, Lenneth become simultaneous claimants
5. CI acceleration: passive +2 to +3/season without the Détente brake

**The optimal play:** This is the campaign's most sophisticated political timing problem. The answer is not "keep Almud uncertain forever" (the CI and succession clocks have independent momentum) nor "resolve him immediately" (destroys the CI brake at full Church institutional strength). The answer is: resolve Almud only after Church is already destabilized (Collision A active — Klapp converts + Olafsson exposed, Church Stability −3), so the CI acceleration that follows hits a Church that cannot respond effectively. This requires coordinating three independent arc chains (Almud's erosion, Haelgrund investigation, Klapp archive exposure) toward simultaneous resolution in the same 1-2 season window.

**Throughline health:** Mechanically elegant. The Almud Arc triggers (Coup Counter, Discovery Event attempt, Torben Loyalty ≤ 3 — any one weakens deed-presumption → ARC-S45) are well-specified. The timing optimization problem is visible only when all three arcs are read together.

## TL-05: THE GRIEF ARCHITECTURE (KNOTS × LOSS × RECOVERY)

**Systems involved:** fieldwork_v30 §5.6 (Knot integration), threadwork_v30 §3.5 (Coherence recovery via Anchoring), character_histories_v30 (Catalyst 4C Grief-Driven Focus, ghost sheet mechanic), mass_battle_v30 §D.2 (Named Unit Officers), companion_specification (departure scene)

The Knot system serves three simultaneous mechanical functions: social intelligence network (remote investigation via §2.6), Coherence recovery mechanism (Anchoring Scenes for Rendering Crisis resolution), and vulnerability surface (Knot strain, detection at −3 Disposition, departure cascade). The same relational resource that makes the character most capable is the same resource that, when strained or lost, produces the most significant mechanical consequences.

The ghost sheet mechanic (Catalyst 4C Level 3: Grief-Driven Focus → on Overwhelming, GM reveals ghost sheet detail) transforms NPC deaths into ongoing information sources. A Knotted NPC who dies does not simply remove their mechanical contribution — they become a different kind of resource, accessible through grief-driven action. The Haelgrund martyrdom scenario makes this concrete: eliminating Haelgrund to prevent his defection produces the evidence disclosure his survival was preventing, because his ghost sheet contains his suppressed investigation files.

**Structural observation:** The grief architecture has an unresolved tension (NEW-OI-67): a character whose grief is narratively resolved (revenge completed, truth revealed) should mechanically lose access to Grief-Driven Focus Level 3, but no mechanic specifies when a ghost sheet closes. The recommended resolution — ghost sheet closes on specific narrative milestone, Level 3 transforms rather than disappears — makes the skill a dynamic tracking of the character's relationship to loss rather than a static exploitation of it.

The Torben-Almud Knot deterioration (Batch 3) demonstrates the Knot system's cross-layer coherence: the Loyalty track (BG political asset metric) and the Knot decay (Thread-layer relational metric) measure the same relationship from different angles. The political erosion and the relational erosion are the same process at different scales.

**Throughline health:** Mechanically well-designed. The gap: the Level 3 ghost sheet reveal has no specified content categories (what kinds of things can a ghost sheet contain?), making this a design-debt that will become GM judgment at the table.

## TL-06: CI SUPPRESSION AS COALITION DEPENDENCY

**Systems involved:** conviction_track_v30 (CI generation, Hafenmark Structural Suppression), victory_v30 §7 (CI seasonal calculation), npc_behavior_v30 §8.4 (Hafenmark Priority Tree), arc_register (ARC-S06, ARC-S37)

The CI suppression architecture has three components: Baralta's structural suppression (CI −1/season while Mandate ≥ 4), Varfell's Conviction Yield elimination (2 Cultural Reclamations on T9 → CV 3, yield = 0), Hafenmark's Parliamentary Suppress (negates passive +1 one season). Each requires specific investment; each has specific collapse conditions.

At baseline, with all three active:
- Passive CI: +1 (Institutional Momentum) − 1 (Baralta) + 0 (CV 3 yield) − Suppress = net 0/season
- CI can be frozen indefinitely with no Church Assert

But the architecture is cascading-fragile:
- Baralta Mandate drops below 4 (from Church Excommunication or player neglect): brake ends permanently, CI +4 one-time, net now +2/season
- Almud Détente collapses (3 triggers: CI 42 Quaestio, ARC-S45, Governance Pause): Varfell CV reduction loses political cover
- Church uses Assert (Priority 3: Mandate ≥ 3 → Assert CI +1): adds +1 even against full suppression, net +1/season

The CI suppression coalition is the game's most mechanically elegant political arrangement: three factions with very different objectives all benefit from the same passive outcome. Baralta suppresses CI to establish deed-claim legitimacy (CI acceleration threatens her constitutional framework). Varfell suppresses CI to prevent the theocracy that would end practitioner tolerance. The player suppresses CI to preserve the political space for RS recovery work.

**The timing problem:** The CI suppression coalition and the RS recovery coalition have overlapping but not identical membership. Varfell is in both. RM is in the RS coalition but not the CI coalition (they prefer CV 0, which erodes the Conviction Yield that the CI coalition depends on maintaining at CV 3). This means RM's cultural victory path (driving CV to 0) conflicts with the CI suppression strategy (which benefits from territories staying at CV 3, not 0). The player who tries to support both RM cultural revolution and CI suppression simultaneously is working against themselves.

**Throughline health:** Well-specified. The Hafenmark Structural Suppression ambiguity (NEW-OI-31) — does it follow Baralta personally or the Hafenmark faction? — is the most significant unresolved element. Resolution: it follows the person (the condition specifies "Baralta's Mandate ≥ 4"). Crown-Baralta suppresses CI. This is the logical reading and enables the Consecration Crisis scenario (NEW-S54) as designed.

## TL-07: THE SUCCESSION CRISIS AS MULTI-AXIS CONVERGENCE

**Systems involved:** arc_register (ARC-S07, ARC-S23, ARC-S35, ARC-S45, ARC-S26, Collision F, Collision G), npc_roster_v30 (Laskaris, Elske, Torben), conviction_track_v30 §4 (Altonian Theocracy path)

Five succession pressures are simultaneously active and structurally mutually exclusive:

1. **Torben Loyalty declining** (ARC-S07): IP 30 → Tutoring Demand → Loyalty −1/season on contact failure. Löwenritter Coup Counter +1 at Loyalty ≤ 3. The first succession pressure to become an active crisis.

2. **Elske independence window** (ARC-S23): three approach vectors; Laskaris shields her at IP cost; flips catastrophically if Loyalty ≤ 2. The diplomatic-track succession alternative that closes if Torben's decline is not managed.

3. **Baralta's Deed Claim** (ARC-S45): activates when deed-presumption weakens (Coup Counter ≥ 2, Almud Discovery Event, Torben Loyalty ≤ 3). Hafenmark merges with Crown or fractures.

4. **Lenneth's Institutional Revival** (ARC-S26): Crown patronage of Einhir revival, requires Almud to act, terminates immediately under Baralta's accession.

5. **Almud's practitioner arc** (ARC-S17): the practitioner-king possibility — the most radical succession outcome, requires sustained Thread development after Discovery Event.

No stable configuration resolves more than three of these simultaneously. Every resolution of one pressure creates or accelerates another. This is not a problem with the design — it IS the design. The succession crisis is intended to be a convergence of incompatible political realities that the player must navigate rather than solve.

**Critical observation:** The Elske succession path is the most underspecified of the five. Her "three approach vectors" (protect Torben, take the throne, serve Valoria) are listed but her TTRPG stat block, TS, and Conviction are not specified in any document read across all 7 batches. For a character who is one of the campaign's five potential succession outcomes, this is a significant design debt. She is named in the Mode Interface Matrix but has no full NPC profile equivalent to Edeyja, Haelgrund, or Torsvald.

## TL-08: THE INTELLIGENCE RACE

**Systems involved:** npc_roster_v30 (Strand, Virke, Haelgrund), fieldwork_v30 §2.6 (Knot-mediated remote investigation), arc_register (ARC-S11 Headless Network, ARC-S27 Revelation Token), npc_behavior_v30 §8.7 (Varfell Priority Tree)

Each faction has a specific intelligence architecture with a specific structural vulnerability:

- **Crown:** Strand (OVERPERFORMER, flattery −1 Ob, genuine acknowledgment bypasses Sincerity Gate). His competence creates brittleness. His removal produces Crown Domain Action +1 Ob for 2 seasons — precisely when Crown is most stressed.
- **Church:** Haelgrund (PROCEDURALIST, Overwhelming enters Parliamentary record). The investigation method is the exposure path. The more thoroughly he investigates, the more he documents his own Thread perception.
- **Varfell:** Private Collection (fastest Awareness acquisition). Lock Distribution decision (highest-impact single player decision; simultaneous Discovery Events reshape the entire intelligence landscape). The Revelation Token cascade (ARC-S27) makes achieving Path A simultaneously attract all factions' hostility.
- **Niflhel:** Four-arm structure (decentralized, each arm requires separate influence operation). Containment response (Priority 6: all four arms redirect when exposed) is MORE detectable than normal operations — the containment effort is the blueprint for Niflhel's structure.
- **Player character:** Knot-mediated remote investigation (§2.6: +1 Knot strain per use, 44% detection rate at Cognition 4 target). Power-to-vulnerability ratio requires careful calibration.

**The meta-intelligence observation:** The player who understands all faction intelligence architectures can play them against each other. Triggering Niflhel's containment response (by building Evidence toward Structural threshold 8) makes Niflhel's four-arm structure visible. Using Haelgrund's PROCEDURALIST flaw against Church (let the Overwhelming investigation enter the record, then surface what the record shows about the investigator's method) is the most powerful indirect Church destabilization available. Information flow is the primary resource allocation game in Valoria.

## TL-09: THE CALAMITY RADIATION INVERSION

**Systems involved:** calamity_radiation_v30 (node distance map), geography_v30 (territory adjacency), arc_register (TE-17 T13, TE-18 T13, TE-21 T6)

The node distance map creates a geographic counterintuitive: Crown's political capital (T1, distance 3) is more Calamity-vulnerable than the Church's theological center (T9, distance 4).

Practical consequences:
- **Practitioners prefer T9 as operation base** at mid-game RS (Fragile band): +0 Calamity Ob in T9, +1 Ob in T1
- **RM cultural resistance appears in T6 one RS band earlier than T1**: Proximity 1 Folklore at RS 79-60 vs Proximity 3 Folklore at RS 59-40
- **T6 and T13 are in CV freefall from Session 1**: RS 28 < 50 → Calamity Drift active immediately, −1 CV/season without faction action
- **Crown court is first to experience Thread-adjacent rendering failures** as RS declines into Fractured

This inversion has strategic implications: the faction holding Church territory benefits from Thread-operation safety at mid-game. The Church's geographical position is a passive advantage for practitioners operating against it. The capital — the most politically important territory — is the most Thread-degraded from the player's operational perspective.

**The Southernmost Surge (RS ≤ 10):** One-time campaign event when RS reaches 10. Fires at Accounting. At Proximity 3 (T1): rendering failures, Discovery Events, possible Gaps in the capital. This is the most dramatic single-event consequence of the Calamity Radiation system — the political center of Valoria experiencing Southernmost-grade Thread phenomena. If this fires in a scene, every non-practitioner in the capital (Almud, Strand, the entire Court) faces Certainty pressure, with political consequences that no faction's priority tree specifies.

## TL-10: THE LOCKED RESOURCE PROBLEM

**Systems involved:** arc_register (ARC-S05 Temporal Window, ARC-S34 Edeyja Burnout), southernmost_v30 (ED-048 Ritual), threadwork_v30 §2.2-2.4

Multiple campaign resources are simultaneously available and inaccessible:

1. **Temporal Window**: RS ≤ 60 satisfied from Session 1. POP requires TS 70+. Only Edeyja meets threshold. Continuity conviction means she won't use POP for political agendas (218 AG investigation, Varfell's historical claims). The window closes only if RS recovers above 60 (possible, but rare). Result: the campaign's most powerful temporal mechanic is available from Session 1 and may go unused for the entire campaign.

2. **Einhir Framework (for Locked Zone Mending)**: requires TS 70+ AND Einhir Text technique AND Diagnosis of Locked Zone structure. Only Edeyja meets TS threshold. The Askeheim Locked Zone border (Mending Ob 8+) is technically reversible but practically inaccessible without Warden Cooperation ≥ 3 and extended relationship investment. Meanwhile, Lock A (Askeheim, 4+ seasons, permanent) continues generating −2 RS/season.

3. **ED-048 [Unnamed] Ritual**: requires Awareness 8+. Varfell reaches this fastest (Private Collection text study + expeditions), potentially around Season 10-12. Crown and Church cannot reach Awareness 8 in any reasonable timeline. The faction most politically opposed to Thread-reality (Church) is the most excluded from the game's primary RS recovery mechanism.

4. **ARC-S31 Lock Distribution**: requires Vaynard at TK 4. Highest-impact single decision in the Thread system. But RS cost of Lock handling (NEW-OI-51) is unspecified — the impact calculation is literally impossible to complete without this answer.

**The design observation:** The locked resource pattern is structurally sound. Resources that are available-but-inaccessible create the right kind of player aspiration. The Temporal Window being open from Session 1 is elegant — it creates a latent capability that the player must earn the key to. But the aggregate of locked resources may produce player frustration: multiple campaign-defining mechanics exist and cannot be used. The solution is not to unlock them earlier but to make the keys more visible: WC ≥ 3 as a visible target with concrete prerequisites; Awareness 8+ with a visible step progression; the "one session rule" for Lock handling RS cost needing resolution.

## TL-11: BELIEFS AS POLITICAL WEAPONS AT RS CRITICAL

**Systems involved:** threadwork_v30 (ARC-S33 Lattice of Enemies, Belief compatibility pre-Leap check), npc_behavior_v30 §3 (Belief system), arc_register (ARC-S33)

At RS Critical (19-1), collective Mending is the only path to prevent Rupture. Collective Mending requires Belief compatibility between participants. Directly opposing Beliefs → Spirit TN 7 Ob 1 pre-Leap (~40% abort risk). The 40% abort rate means a collective Mending with two practitioners holding directly opposing Beliefs fails to assemble 64% of the time.

This makes Belief content a political asset at RS Critical. A faction that wants Thread operations to fail can strain enemy practitioners' Beliefs to create direct oppositions that prevent collective Mending. The Church, which has been straining practitioner Beliefs throughout the campaign (via CI pressure, Heresy Investigation, Inquisitor deployments), is systematically building the condition that makes Mending harder in the endgame.

**The "become different" requirement:** This throughline encodes the game's deepest thematic statement. A player with a Belief directly opposing another practitioner's CANNOT fulfill that Belief AND save the world. They must choose: pursue the Belief (prevents collective Mending) OR revise the Belief (enables world-saving but costs the Belief's Momentum and Conviction identity). The game mechanically demands at RS Critical that players consider whether their character commitments at campaign start are still the right commitments at campaign end. This is "Conviction revision as campaign arc" made mechanical.

**Throughline health:** Mechanically sound. The "Belief revision is not Belief abandonment" distinction matters: the mechanic requires transformation, not surrender. The Einhir Triangle (Collision G) — where player faction positioning becomes permanent commitment — is the first throughline where this applies. At RS Critical, it extends to all Belief intersections.

## TL-12: THE FOLK PRACTITIONER NETWORK AS DISTRIBUTED SUBSTRATE

**Systems involved:** conviction_track_v30 §5 (RM stats and Community Weaving), npc_roster_v30 (Vossen, Hann), arc_register (ARC-S22, ARC-S47, ARC-S48, ARC-T23), character_histories_v30 (3-RE3 Folk Practitioner)

RM starts at Awareness 3 (highest of any faction), Community Weaving capability from game start, and Framework Drift territory spread (+1 territory/season at Stability ≥ 3). The Witness (ARC-T23) provides 245 years of accumulated record — potentially equivalent to Awareness 4-5 before any expedition is run. The folk practitioner network (Intuitive Threadwork: Attunement + Spirit, TN 8, Ob 3, pre-Leap) provides distributed Thread capability without individual Coherence cost.

**The critical structural dependency:** RM Phase 2 Cultural Uprising (T9 transfer to RM administration) is viable in isolation but not defensible. RM has Military 0. Church can contest T9 militarily even if Seizure is at +3 Ob. RM's cultural victory requires other factions to provide military defense that RM itself cannot. This makes RM the most collaborative faction in the design — its victory path structurally depends on political relationships with factions whose objectives are not fully aligned with RM's.

**The RS-RM positive feedback loop (verified):** RS decline → more low-CV territories → higher RM Mandate → Community Weaving enables more Presence markers. BUT: RS recovery → Calamity Drift stops → fewer new low-CV territories → RM Mandate plateaus. The loop is real and self-limiting, as confirmed in Batch 4. The Church-RM CV stalemate (each season: Church Preaches +1 CV, RM Weaves −1 CV, net 0 CV change) produces collateral RS +1/season from RM's Weaving. The contested CV stalemate benefits world health regardless of which faction "wins" the cultural contest in any given territory.

## TL-14: THE SOUTHERNMOST AS ASYMMETRIC DOMAIN

**Systems involved:** southernmost_v30 (expedition procedures, Forgetting Check, zone hazards, Awareness), mass_battle_v30 §A.11 (TS restriction), arc_register (TE-12, TE-13, TE-14, ARC-S15, ARC-S34)

The Southernmost is the campaign's asymmetric domain: the most important territory for RS recovery, militarily impenetrable to conventional forces, intellectually inaccessible without Awareness 8+, and personally dangerous in ways that don't scale linearly with practitioner capability.

**The military inversion:** Standard forces (TS < 30) dissolve on entry. This makes T15 simultaneously the most contested spiritual/political space and the most militarily secure for practitioners. The Altonian Vanguard (standard army) cannot enter T15. RM (folk practitioners, TS 30+) can. Varfell (VTM ≥ 2) can field meaningful units. Crown, Church, Hafenmark, Guilds cannot field viable Southernmost forces.

**The Core Zone problem:** Contact duration halves in the Core (Thread density saturates working space). Focus 3 → 0 operations. For any standard practitioner with Focus ≤ 3, Thread operations in the Core are impossible. Focus 4+ required for Core-site Thread work. This means the most important site in the campaign for understanding the substrate requires a character investment level (Focus 4+, likely via 2G Monastic Seclusion Formation) that most player characters won't have made at creation.

**The Forgetting Check architecture:** Cognition + Recall + TS bonus, TN 8. At Core (Ob 4): high-attribute practitioners succeed ~38% of the time. TS 30 practitioners succeed ~21% of the time. This is intentionally harsh — the Core is meant to be narratively inaccessible even when physically reached. The Overwhelming outcome (ontological understanding of what the Southernmost IS) at ~8% probability represents a genuinely rare and significant character moment.

## TL-15: CHARACTER HISTORY AS POLITICAL POSITIONING

**Systems involved:** character_histories_v30 (all 4 stages, skill levels 1-3, sparking mechanic), threadwork_v30 §3.2 (Coherence→Recall interaction)

The character history system encodes political positioning at the mechanical level. Origin determines starting Certainty and Calamity exposure. Formation determines Thread access (2F Practitioner Mentorship: −2 Certainty, Approach Training). Vocation determines faction affiliation and specific mechanical niches. Catalyst determines the inciting event that frames the character's Beliefs.

The system's key interaction: The Coherence→Recall cascade. At Coherence 4 (Fragmented), effective Recall −1 → one fewer equipped skill. At Coherence 3, effective Recall −2 → two fewer equipped skills. A practitioner who pushes Coherence through Thread operations loses their non-Thread capabilities in exact proportion to their Thread investment. The mechanic correctly represents that practitioners who go deep into Thread work lose their "ordinary" competence, not through forgetting but through attention re-allocation encoded in the equip-slot system.

**The canonical test character (Mira Sondhal):** 1D Deep South modifier → TS 8, Certainty 1; 2F → Certainty 0, Approach Training; 3-VA3 Einhir Antiquarian → Artefact Authentication + Black Market Contacts; 4E First Leap → Leap Scar, Belief about the wound at Askeheim. This character enters play with: TS 8 (approaching Stirring), Certainty 0 (already outside Solmundic rendering), 5 starting skills, three Knots that will produce the core relational conflicts of the arc. She is the lifepath system's proof of concept for the Southern Einhir Descendant as the game's most thematically loaded character type.

## H3: ELEGANT
**ASSESSMENT: ◑ (moderate — downgraded from ◕)**

**The aggregate complexity problem:** Each system examined across Batches 1-7 is elegant at the unit level. The Piety Track (CV per territory, 5 values, one formula) is clean. The NPC behavioral flaws are each one-line behavioral rules. The Forgetting Check (pool + TN + Ob table) is concise. The Calamity Radiation matrix (RS band × Proximity) is the most elegant geography-meets-mechanics table in the design.

But their aggregate — encountered at a single table — requires: Province Accord from settlement Order (Batch 1); NPC Priority Trees (Batch 1-2); Knot formation and remote investigation (Batch 2-3); Piety Track per territory with Calamity Drift (Batch 4); Mass Combat phases (Batch 5); Southernmost Awareness and Expedition zones (Batch 7); Character History lifepath with sparking (Batch 7). The unit elegance does not sum to systemic elegance because the systems were designed independently and now interact.

**Specific elegance failures:**

**The investigation architecture's gate proliferation (H3 Batch 3, unresolved):** 7 gate types (Certainty, Evidence, Belief, Attribute, History, Disposition, Thread Sensitivity), 5 filters, 3 investigation registers. The proposed merge (Attribute+History → single Background Gate) reduces to 6 gates without mechanical loss. This fix has been recommended in both Batch 3 and needs to be formally decided.

**The Ob formula zoo:** The game currently uses at least seven distinct Ob derivation methods:
- Standard: fixed number (most rolls)
- Demand scene: floor(NPC highest stat ÷ 2) + 2
- BG Survey: (5 − Proximity Rating) + 1
- Church Seizure: 2 + Fort Level + max(0, 3 − CV)
- Forgetting Check: table by zone (1/2/3/4)
- Community Weaving (faction): ceiling(RS-inverse ÷ 20)
- Contested Investigation: opponent's net successes as Ob

These are not a problem individually. Collectively they require the player/GM to know which formula applies in each context, creating cognitive overhead that accumulates across a session. The recommendation is not to harmonize all formulas (some variation is correct) but to ensure each formula has a clear mnemonic principle that players can derive rather than memorize.

**The Southernmost's internal complexity spike:** The Expedition (4 phases, 3 zone hazard types, Forgetting Check at each zone with different Obs, Awareness prerequisite, contact duration halving at Core, Contact Rounds further halved by zone effects) is the most complex single procedure in the design. Its complexity is appropriate to its narrative weight — the Southernmost is the campaign's most dangerous and important location. But the complexity means first Expedition will take significantly longer to run than subsequent Expeditions, and GMs will need a reference card.

**What remains elegant:** The core dice engine (pool, TN, Ob, degrees) is unchanged across all systems and all scales. This is the design's single most important elegant achievement: the same mechanical language describes a social exchange, a Thread operation, a mass battle unit's combat, a BG Domain Action, and an Expedition zone check. The surface variety of the game's systems is underwritten by one consistent resolution mechanism.

**8. Lock Distribution RS cost (NEW-OI-51)**
Non-operational Thread exposure (handling an originary Lock) should carry a defined consequence. Recommendation: Lock handling produces a Coherence check (Spirit TN 7 Ob 1) but NOT RS cost unless the handler performs a Thread operation in response to what they perceive. The Discovery Event (TS advancement) is the primary consequence; the Coherence check represents the perceptual shock of contact with an originary configuration. No RS cost from passive handling — RS cost only from active operations in response.

## Architectural observation (for Jordan's design consideration)

The fifteen throughlines all share one structural feature: they are invisible in isolation and visible only in aggregate. A player who reads threadwork_v30 sees a practitioner system. A player who reads conviction_track_v30 sees a territorial faith system. A player who reads npc_roster_v30 sees thirteen characterized NPCs. None of these documents tells them about TL-02 (suppression creates practitioners), TL-03 (Almud's indecision as structural stabilizer), or TL-06 (CI suppression as coalition dependency). The game's deepest design is distributed across documents that will not be read in sequence at any table.

This creates a world-building success and a player-access challenge simultaneously. The design is genuinely emergent — the throughlines were not explicitly designed as throughlines, they emerged from the interaction of consistent mechanical principles applied independently to different domains. But emergence means the player's experience of the campaign depends on how many of these interactions they discover vs. how many remain invisible infrastructure.

The strongest recommendation across all seven batches: write a one-page document titled "The Campaign's Pressure Architecture" for the GM reference section. It does not teach mechanics — it identifies the throughlines, names them, and tells the GM: "These are the tensions that organize the whole. Everything else is texture."

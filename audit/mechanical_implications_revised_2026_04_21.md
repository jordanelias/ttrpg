## §1 Leap pipeline (revised) — P1, P2, P4, P15

### §1.1 Decision architecture

The Leap presents the player with two decisions: entry commit and exit timing. All else is resolution and bookkeeping.

**Entry commit.** Perception of the target configuration is already present at the practitioner's TS band (canon/01 Am 3 explicit on this). No diagnostic action precedes commit; the character's ordinary perception at their TS waterline is the pre-entry information state. What entry commits to is:

- The configuration to engage (visible to practitioner at TS band).
- The operation sequence: ordered list of up to (Focus − 1) operations per canonical §2.3 Focus role.
- Exit conditions: pre-declared triggers for early exit.

Commit locks the sequence. Canon/02 Am 1 is the structural reason: reflexive facing suspends at entry, and reflexive facing is the mode of self-access by which the practitioner would modify commitment mid-operation. With the facing suspended, the sequence persists as declared. Declaration mistakes surface at retention; they cannot be corrected during contact.

**Exit timing.** During contact, the player monitors scene state (above-water events resolve around the character per canon/02 Am 1 outward-facing persistence) and character state (Fatigue accumulation toward Spirit × 5 threshold per canonical §2.3). Player may elect exit at inter-operation moments — between sequence operations, or after a completed operation before the next.

Involuntary exit occurs at Fatigue threshold. Voluntary exit or pre-declared conditional exit resolves Retention Roll at whatever degree Coherence-state and scene conditions modify.

The central tension the Leap pipeline produces is not moment-to-moment decision-stacking but pre-commit planning under uncertainty. The practitioner declares an operation sequence based on what their TS-band-received information permits them to anticipate. Once committed, contact is pure execution — the player cannot reoptimise against information that contact itself surfaces. This is canon/02 Am 1 rendered as gameplay.

### §1.2 Feedback loops and strategic tradeoffs

**Primary tradeoff — commit breadth vs surface vulnerability.** Deeper sequences (more operations, longer contact duration) produce greater operational outcome but longer Fatigue accumulation and longer above-water absence during which scene-state can destabilise. Shallower commits are safer but less operationally productive per entry.

The equilibrium a player finds: declare sequences matching anticipated Coherence cost envelope and anticipated scene-state stability. Above-water turmoil compresses commits; stable scene conditions permit longer sequences.

**Secondary tradeoff — commitment precision vs pre-commit information.** A player can take more time at scene-level to observe the target configuration before entry. TS-band perception is continuous; sustained attention reveals features passing attention misses. The in-scene time-cost of sustained observation is not a resource-cost (no Fatigue, no Coherence) but a scene-clock cost — while the practitioner is observing, the scene continues.

Strategic expression: high-stakes operations warrant sustained pre-entry observation even when scene conditions are not urgent. Low-stakes routine operations proceed with brief observation. The player calibrates observation-time to operation-criticality.

**Sentinel-diver geometry.** In multi-practitioner scenes, non-leaping party members who take Sentinel action provide above-water awareness channel for the committed practitioner. This is not a bolted-on mechanic; it follows from canon/02 Am 1's geometry — someone must be holding above-water perception because the committed practitioner cannot, and if scene conditions demand above-water response, the party must partition.

Solo-practitioner high-stakes operations in unstable scenes are categorically risky. A player learns this through one failed retention with above-water turmoil and thereafter calibrates party composition toward sentinel-support for critical operations.

**Overweaving as commit-breadth penalty.** Canonical §2.4 "Overweaving" imposes +1 Ob cumulative per additional operation in the same contact window. This canonical rule IS the commit-breadth cost: longer sequences bear higher per-operation Ob from the second operation onward. Not an added mechanic; an existing canonical reason for sequences to be calibrated.

### §1.3 Cross-system interactions

- Coherence system (§2, §3): operation-type Coherence costs per canon/02 Am 3; retention-degree modifies post-exit Coherence per canonical Leap Roll degrees.
- Knot-profile (§4): one knot per operation in the executed sequence, per canon/02 Am 6.
- Environmental parameters (§8): site substrate character parameter (consolidated composite) modifies operation Ob via canonical environmental modifiers.
- Faction interpretation (§7): observers produce faction-specific interpretations of the visible reintegration; T-27 dynamics apply to the practitioner's outward-facing state during and after contact.
- Companion system (§4 subsection): sentinel support is the canonical collective-practitioner pattern (threadwork §2.5) extended to single-practitioner operations.

## §3 TS progression — P2, P16, P20 (revised)

### §3.1 Decision architecture

TS is a receptive-capacity axis per canon/01 Am 3 explicit: *"TS measures how much of the below-waterline Thread-substrate the practitioner can perceive and deliberately operate upon."* The iceberg is the substrate itself; what the practitioner receives of it is determined by where their waterline sits. Higher TS = lower waterline = more of the substrate-iceberg rises into what can be received at ordinary perception.

**Depth-unlock events.** Confrontation-retention under integration-supportive conditions accumulates toward threshold-crossing. Band thresholds at TS 30, 50, 70, 90 trigger Event Scenes. At crossing, the practitioner's waterline lowers; more of the substrate-iceberg becomes receivable. This is not the substrate changing, not different content becoming available to different observers — it is the practitioner's receptive capacity expanding within a single given substrate-reality.

**Taxonomic expansion (T-40).** Each threshold unlocks categories the practitioner can now place what is received under. TS 30+ substrate-effect category becomes receivable. TS 50+ relational-thread-structure, threadcut-mode, institutional-substrate-entity categories become receivable. TS 70+ Accord-as-entity, structural-reality categories. TS 90+ full substrate-topology.

The categories are not added to the world by TS crossing. They are categories the substrate has always supported; the practitioner's receptive capacity previously lacked the band to perceive what could be placed in them.

**Map-UI as concretization (§8 P21).** Map overlay layers render at TS thresholds. The player sees in UI the receptive-capacity expansion the character underwent. Substrate-layers were always present in what-is-given; the character's waterline previously sat above the band at which those layers became receivable.

**Replay at higher TS (§8 P21).** Locations previously visited at low TS render differently on return at higher TS. What was stimulus-resists-predication at TS 20 becomes predicable-as-specific-substrate-content at TS 50. The same given substrate, differently received by the same practitioner-character with developed receptive capacity.

### §3.2 Feedback loops

**Developmental loop.** Confrontation under support → retention success → integration → TS progress → more-categories-receivable → more-kinds-of-confrontation-recognisable → further confrontation under support. Canonical §14 pedagogy made mechanical.

**Failure mode.** Confrontation without integration-supportive conditions produces framework-PTSD-analog residue (§4 P9). Accumulated residue reduces capacity for future confrontation-retention. Player strategic response: seek confrontation under supportable conditions, not confrontation-rate maximisation.

**Taxonomic-expansion strategic advantage.** High-TS practitioners receive more of the given substrate-reality in a given scene than their low-TS companions. A TS 70 Warden in a Gap-adjacent region receives substrate-paths the low-TS companion lacks capacity to perceive. This is not information advantage through different givens; it is information advantage through different receptive-capacity bands against the same given. Mission-viability scales with TS non-linearly above band thresholds.

**Strategic patience tradeoff.** Threshold-events require cumulative confrontation-retention under support. Players can push toward threshold by seeking confrontation, but each carries risk (turn-away fragmentary residue, or held-without-support PTSD-analog residue). Natural strategy: maximise confrontation-under-support rate, not confrontation-rate. Investment in support-conditions infrastructure (companions, ART-qualifying sites, training-relationships) is strategic.

### §3.3 Cross-system interactions

Per prior iteration, with receptive-capacity framing replacing given-content framing. The substrate is the same; what the practitioner receives of it varies by TS band.

## §5 Confrontation + integration — P7, P8 (revised)

### §5.1 Decision architecture (corrected)

Confrontation is a player-facing moment-of-choice that is *not a Leap*. Canon/00 §4.3 is explicit: confrontation is perceptual opening without substrate engagement. No reflexive suspension. No knot formation. No Coherence cost from the confrontation itself.

The player faces a binary: Hold or Turn-Away. Canon §14 terminology corrected — *Turn-Away* rather than *Flee*. Flee is combat-tactical; Turn-Away is phenomenological refusal to receive what the encounter presents.

**Hold.** The character remains in the encounter, receiving what their TS-band-capacity permits. Retention check under integration-supportive conditions determines integration success. Degrees:
- Overwhelming: full integration. TS progress.
- Success: standard integration. TS progress.
- Partial: partial integration. TS progress + one residue flag.
- Failure: held without integration. No TS progress. Two residue flags. Framework-PTSD-analog state.

**Turn-Away.** The character refuses to receive. Fragmentary residue (one flag). No TS progress, regardless of subsequent action on the same encounter.

The central feature of canon §14: holding under support is the developmental primary. The binary is the player's single most load-bearing decision in the developmental arc.

### §5.2 Feedback loops (unchanged)

Developmental loop vs degenerative loop; operation-rate vs integration-rate tempo; trait-capacity character-background; pedagogical training scenes as integration-productive content; systemic-relational framing. Per prior iteration.

### §5.3 Cross-system interactions

## §7 Four-faction interpreter machinery — P3, P20, P22 (revised)

### §7.1 Decision architecture (corrected framing)

Prior iteration framing was "same event, four sincere-different-renderings" — which suggested different interpretations *of the event* by observers receiving the same event. This is nearly correct but subtly misframed.

Corrected: faction-AI interpretations operate on what has been received. Non-sensitive low-TS observers do not receive the event at all (canon §9 Perceptual Prophylaxis + TS band below reception-threshold). Sensitive observers receive the event within their TS-band capacity. Then each processes the received content through faction-formation interpretive-frame.

The "four faction interpretations simultaneously" is at the reception + interpretive-frame composite level. Different factions process what they received through frame-consistent taxonomies, producing frame-consistent accounts.

### §7.2 Feedback loops (updated where framing affects mechanics)

**Propositional-correction failure.** Unchanged; argument at framework level cannot shift the interpretive frame. Canon §9 makes this structural; the frame is formation, not propositional commitment.

**Baralta-crack gradual drift.** Unchanged in mechanism. Baralta's partial prophylaxis-crack operates at the *formation* level; sustained contact reduces the prophylaxis. The result is not changed-interpretation-of-same-received-content but changed-formation-producing-changed-reception + changed-interpretation.

**Cross-faction divergence as narrative content.** The simultaneous-four-account rendering of a substrate event (per meditation Gap E / Session 2 Cell 10 event-log specification) is the T-30 information-asymmetry content made legible. Player-with-framework-access sees:
- The substrate event (the given).
- What each faction's observers received (TS-band + formation-gated).
- What each faction's interpretive-frame produced from the received content.

This is richer than "four different perceptions of the event" — it distinguishes what is given, what is received, and what is interpreted at each observer-node.

**Non-sensitive continuity.** NPC dialogue at substrate event that does not enter their receptive capacity continues pre-event content without modification, not because of denial but because of receptive-capacity absence. Attention-architectural-absence-from-receptive-capacity is the precise description. Canon §9 prophylaxis-formation keeps their waterline high enough that substrate events do not rise into their ordinary perception.

Forced attention mechanic: player directs NPC attention to the substrate-event. NPC receives partially. Cannot place in frame. Receipt dissolves. This is not denial; it is partial-receipt-without-frame-integration.

**High-TS diagnostic observation.** At TS 70+, character's own receptive capacity permits recognising other-faction interpreter-output as interpreter-output. Not agreement, not conversion — mutual-recognition-of-frames. The character's TS band is sufficient to receive substrate-content directly AND to diagnostically recognise when other observers are producing frame-consistent output over their received content.

### §9.1 Correction — flee terminology

"Flee" is appropriate in this context — a monstrosity encounter IS a combat-proximal situation where tactical withdrawal is a valid action. Flee here is not the same word as the canon §14 Turn-Away corrected for confrontation events. Monstrosity encounters have confrontation-structure plus physical-threat structure; flee applies to the physical-threat-response action, while the phenomenological remain-vs-turn-away dimension applies to the confrontation-structure.

Distinction in spec:
- Confrontation event (phenomenological): Hold vs Turn-Away.
- Monstrosity encounter (which contains a confrontation event but also has physical-threat structure): Contain vs Flee vs Survive at the tactical level; Hold vs Turn-Away at the phenomenological level.

The tactical and phenomenological dimensions resolve separately.

## §15 Cross-cutting — player-character information asymmetry (revised framing)

### §15.1 The central mechanic (corrected)

The player-character information asymmetry is not about the player and character perceiving different realities. Canon/00 Inseparability (A2 / P-01) does not support that framing. It is about the player and character operating in different access-modes against the same given reality.

The player sees what the character's reflexive mode cannot receive (knot-profile per canon/02 Am 2). The player sees what the character's outward-facing occlusion during Leap contact produces (above-water scene continuance per canon/02 Am 1). The player holds receptive-capacity expansions the character has not yet undergone (UI-overlay layers rendered at player's chosen information-density; character's TS band modulates what would be perceivable within the character's own experience).

**What the player is:** a mode of external access to the character's situation. Not external to the reality the character inhabits; external to the character's reflexive self-rendering.

**What the character is:** a mode of within-reality engagement with a given substrate. The character receives what their TS band permits of what is given. The character's reflexive mode cannot reach knot-depth formations. The character's attention during Leap contact is absorbed in the operation-channel and not available for above-water reception.

Both player and character are oriented toward the same given. Their differences are access-mode differences, not different-given differences.

### §15.2 Strategic consequences

**Player-as-extended-cognition for knot-profile.** The player holds information the character's reflexive mode cannot introspect, serving as external-access channel complementary to character's somatic interoception per canon/02 Am 6. Strategic decisions the player makes based on knot-profile use information the character cannot reflexively access, but could feel somatically if attention were sustained on the relevant knotted territory.

**Sentinel-diver geometry.** Above-water / below-waterline geometry during Leap. Sentinel-character receives above-water scene content that submerged-character's outward-facing cannot attend to. Player coordinates across character-perspectives.

**Interpretive-frame management.** The player-with-framework-access knows substrate-reality is the given; knows faction interpretations are sincere-but-frame-consistent processing of received content; knows non-sensitive observers are not receiving at all. The characters (all characters) operate within their faction-formation frames. The player navigates frame-tension on the characters' behalf — knowing, for instance, that a Church character's demonic-substitution interpretation is interpreter-output over partial-receipt, not framework-truth, while understanding the Church character sincerely believes it.

**T-30 made primary.** Information Asymmetry as Core Mechanic is the structure of every mechanical surface: Leap pipeline embodies it (above-water sentinel-vs-submerged-practitioner); knot-profile embodies it (player-external-access vs character-somatic); faction-interpreter embodies it (player-with-framework-access vs characters-within-frames); map-UI embodies it (player's TS-overlay selection vs character's TS band receptive capacity).

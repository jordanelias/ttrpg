# Foundations Amendment: Always-Already Self-Rendering

**Status:** Canonical
**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Extends:** Parts One (§1, §3), Five (§5.3), Six (§14-15), Seven (§16)
**Impact:** Redefines Coherence, the Leap, and Coherence 0 outcomes; establishes TS-gated branching at Coherence 0; introduces reality-strain cost for high-TS Coherence 0 beings

---

## Amendment 1: The Three Layers of Being-Persistence

The Foundations establish that organic beings persist through continuous spooling by the Ein Sof (§5.3) and that rendering is always-already performed by consciousness (§3.1). These two claims, combined with the dual nature of threads as both substrate and conditions of possibility (§1), entail a third layer that the Foundations did not make explicit:

**Humans are always-already threadworking their own continually-spooled existence.**

If rendering is consciousness-performed, and the being's own threads are part of the substrate being rendered, then consciousness is rendering its own threads. And if rendering threads constitutes a form of threadwork (§3.2 — changing threads changes both facticity and rendering), then the always-already rendering of one's own being is a form of threadwork: unconscious, automatic, constitutive.

Three layers of being-persistence, in order:

1. **Ein Sof spooling.** Passive, from the ground. Constitutes the being's ongoing temporal accumulation — becoming. This is what threadcut beings lack. It is the ground's continuous production of the being's thread-substrate.

2. **Self-rendering.** Always-already, unconscious. The rendering maintaining the being's coherence as a human configuration. Consciousness presenting its own threads to itself as: I am this person, in this time, in this world. This is a constitutive form of threadwork — not deliberate, not trained, but ontologically real. It shapes the raw spooling into a human configuration and maintains that configuration against environmental thread-forces.

3. **Deliberate threadwork.** Conscious, trained, what practitioners do in the Leap. Requires temporary suspension of layer 2.

The distinction between organic beings and threadcut beings is not that organic beings do not threadwork themselves. It is that organic beings threadwork themselves unconsciously and automatically (layer 2), sustained by Ein Sof spooling (layer 1), while threadcut beings must threadwork themselves consciously and continuously (layer 3) without any Ein Sof spooling (no layer 1). The difference is the source and mode of self-maintenance, not whether self-maintenance occurs.

## Amendment 2: The Leap Redefined

The Leap is the radical suspension of layer 2 (always-already self-rendering) in order to access layer 3 (deliberate threadwork upon the world). The practitioner temporarily stops maintaining their own configuration as human in order to touch threads directly.

This is why the Leap is both an epoché and a leap of faith (§9.3). The epoché: the practitioner suspends the constitutive process that presents their own being as given. The leap of faith: they do so without guarantee that the process will fully re-engage.

The retention roll at the end of a Leap is not a test of psychological resilience. It is a test of whether the always-already self-rendering successfully reconstitutes after suspension. Failure means some portion of the automatic self-threadwork did not fully restore. The being's configuration has been permanently altered in ways the self-rendering can no longer integrate.

Because threads are both substrate and conditions of possibility: when self-rendering partially fails, the being's actuality is altered (their material configuration shifts), their intelligibility is altered (they become harder to know — both to themselves and to others per Husserl's apperception), and their temporality is altered (their linear experience of time becomes less stable as retention and protention decohere).

## Amendment 3: Coherence Redefined

Coherence measures the structural integrity of layer 2 — the always-already self-rendering that maintains a practitioner's being as a human configuration. It does not measure perception, sanity, or Thread-awareness.

Coherence degradation is the progressive failure of unconscious self-threadwork. Each failed retention roll means the self-rendering re-engaged incompletely. The being's thread-configuration retains alterations from the Leap that the automatic self-maintenance can no longer correct.

Coherence is orthogonal to Thread Sensitivity. TS measures how much of the below-waterline Thread-substrate the practitioner can perceive and deliberately operate upon. Coherence measures how effectively their unconscious self-rendering maintains their being as human. These axes do not move together. A practitioner can have high TS and high Coherence (perceptive and stable), high TS and low Coherence (perceptive and destabilizing), low TS and low Coherence (blind and destabilizing), or any other combination.

The epistemic seduction described in §16.1 is one path by which TS and Coherence loss become correlated — but it is a contingent correlation, not a structural necessity. A practitioner who accumulates Coherence loss through retention failures without residue use or transformation follows a different trajectory entirely.

### Observable Coherence Degradation

**10–8 (Stable):** No perceptible change. Self-rendering re-engages fully after each Leap.

**7–5 (Dissonant):** Subtle uncanniness. The practitioner's thread-configuration has shifted enough that others' renderings work harder to process them. Temporal micro-slippages: actions fractionally ahead of or behind the moment. Responses that address what you meant rather than what you said, because the practitioner's intelligibility tracks a different sequential process. Loved ones describe speaking to someone through glass.

**4–3 (Fragmented):** Visibly wrong. Materiality behaves inconsistently — shadows that don't match posture, presence that occupies more space than the body, flickers between being fully in the scene and partially elsewhere. Temporality perceptibly non-linear: references to events that haven't happened, reactions before causes. Intelligibility fractures: the practitioner is apperceived differently by different observers. Two people in the same room describe the practitioner differently — not in opinion, but in what they perceived. The rendering fails to present a consistent human being.

**2–1 (Fractured):** Physical form manifests substrate alteration directly. Weight distribution wrong. Light interacts at a register that produces unease. Temporal existence perceptible to anyone — conversations skip, loop, proceed at inconsistent rates. Knowledge arrives chaotically, not as integrated perception. Relational contagion (§16.3) is now constant and uncontrolled.

**0 (Coherence Failure):** The always-already self-rendering as human has ceased. Ein Sof still spools the being — they are not threadcut, not dissolving. But the layer that maintained their configuration as a human being is gone. They are being spooled into whatever the raw spooling produces without human self-rendering to shape it.

## Amendment 4: Coherence 0 Outcomes Gated by Thread Sensitivity

At Coherence 0, the same structural event occurs for all practitioners: layer 2 (unconscious self-rendering as human) ceases. What follows depends on the practitioner's Thread Sensitivity — how much of the thread-substrate they can perceive and deliberately work.

TS does not increase at Coherence 0. The practitioner does not suddenly perceive more because their human rendering collapsed. They have exactly the perceptual architecture they built through confrontation, and that architecture determines what kind of being they become.

### TS 30–49 (Stirring): Ontological Freefall

The practitioner perceives threads at Object/Personal scale only. They cannot threadwork themselves at the depth required to substitute for lost self-rendering. Their configuration is exposed: raw Ein Sof spooling plus environmental thread-forces (the configurations of every being and place around them) begin reshaping them.

They are always-already becoming Other without the ability to direct it. Their consciousness still exists — they are not unconscious — but it experiences a being it can no longer render as human and cannot work the threads constituting that being into any stable alternative.

Over time, entanglement with surrounding thread-configurations warps them further. The environment writes itself into them because they have no self-rendering to resist it. They become a being shaped by surroundings at the thread level — increasingly alien, increasingly unable to communicate what is happening to them because the words require a rendering they no longer have.

This is not transcendence. It is ontological freefall.

### TS 50–69 (Attuned): Relational Persistence

The practitioner perceives and works at Relational scale. They can perceive and partially maintain the thread-knots connecting them to other beings. They can hold onto who they were in relation to others even as what they are in themselves becomes unstable.

This produces a being whose identity is relational rather than personal — they exist through their connections, not through self-coherence. Communication is possible but only through channels of existing relationships. Their Knots become structurally load-bearing: the relationships are literally holding the being together.

Strain on those Knots becomes existential. If a Knot severs, part of what holds the being in any recognizable configuration goes with it.

### TS 70–89 (Sensitive): Structural Reconstitution

The practitioner perceives and works at Structural scale. They can threadwork themselves into a stable non-human configuration — not the human configuration they lost, but something organized, self-consistent, and self-maintaining. They can engage with the structural reality of institutions, places, and social formations at the thread level.

Their self-threadwork to maintain their being is deliberate and continuous — functionally identical to what threadcut beings do (§5.3), except they are still being spooled by Ein Sof. They are becoming through active threadwork rather than through unconscious self-rendering.

This is closer to the transcendence described in high-level NPC arcs, but still bounded. They are not seeing the full picture — they are seeing a large fraction. Their perceptual range has limits that a Resonant practitioner's does not.

Every moment of their continued existence is a thread operation. Every thread operation strains the substrate.

### TS 90–100 (Resonant): Full Reconstitution and Reality Strain

Full range. The practitioner can threadwork themselves and their environment at any scale. Their being reorganizes around a beyond-human rendering that is comprehensive, self-consistent, and enormously capable. They perceive Thread-configurational reality directly, can shape communication for each listener's capacity, and understand Thread-level structures that no human rendering can access.

**However:** every moment of their continued existence involves active threadwork at a scale and depth that strains the substrate the way the Einhir site-network strained it. They are not drawing on the substrate for civilizational refinement — they are drawing on it for self-maintenance and environmental shaping. But the physics is the same (§6.1).

A Resonant practitioner at Coherence 0 who is continuously threadworking themselves and their environment into the form and state they desire is doing what the Einhir did, localized to their presence. The fabric around them is being drawn taut. Sustained over sufficient time and at sufficient depth, this creates the conditions for tears — monstrous incursions, gaps, ontological failures — in their vicinity.

Such a being can be benign in intention and catastrophic in structural effect. Their continued existence in the rendered world creates the conditions for localized Calamity-scale events. Not because they intend it. Because the physics of what they must do to exist demands it.

## Amendment 5: Implications for Existing Canon

### The Einhir Parallel

The Einhir site-network strained the substrate through sustained, generationally accumulated precision threadwork (§6.1). A Resonant Coherence 0 practitioner does the same thing individually, continuously, at comparable depth. The difference is scale (one being vs. a civilization-wide lattice) and duration (a lifetime vs. generations). The structural mechanism is identical.

This means the setting's history contains a warning about what these beings produce. The Calamity is not only backstory — it is the structural consequence of exactly what a Coherence 0 / TS 90+ being must do to continue existing.

### Threadcut Beings Recontextualized

The distinction between organic beings and threadcut beings (§5.3) is now a difference in which layers of self-maintenance are operative, not a categorical difference in kind:

- Organic humans: layers 1 + 2 (spooling + unconscious self-rendering)
- Coherence 0 low-TS practitioners: layer 1 only (spooling without self-rendering; no capacity for layer 3 at sufficient depth)
- Coherence 0 high-TS practitioners: layers 1 + 3 (spooling + deliberate self-threadwork)
- Threadcut beings: layer 3 only (deliberate self-threadwork without spooling)

Threadcut beings remain categorically distinct because they lack Ein Sof spooling entirely — they radically are without becoming. But the mode of self-maintenance they perform (continuous deliberate threadwork) is the same mode that high-TS Coherence 0 practitioners must adopt. The experiential overlap between a threadcut being and a Resonant Coherence 0 practitioner is substantial: both must actively maintain their own existence moment to moment through thread operations.

### The Arcs (28–30)

The campaign arcs describing Vaynard, Almud, and Lenneth at Coherence 0 must specify each NPC's TS at the point of Coherence failure. The "wise Other" narrative applies only at high TS. Additionally, all three arcs must acknowledge the reality-strain cost: these beings' continued existence produces substrate tension in their vicinity, creating conditions for tears, gaps, and monstrous incursions proportional to the depth and frequency of their self-maintenance threadwork.

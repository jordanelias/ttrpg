# Foundations Amendment: The Leap Mechanism — Suspension, Knot Formation, Operation-Type Risk

**Status:** Canonical
**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Extends:** `canon/00_philosophical_foundations.md` §4.3 (Confrontation as Constitutive Finitude), §5.3 (Third Mode — Threadcut Beings), §9 (Perceptual Prophylaxis); `canon/01_foundations_amendment_self_rendering.md` Amendments 1–4 (three-layer being-persistence, Leap, Coherence)
**Impact:** Specifies what the Leap is structurally (suspension target, duration bound), what it produces (directional knots at substrate depth), and what substrate-level variable determines operational risk (operation-type, not operation-scale). Resolves the question left open by `canon/01` Amendment 2: *why* some threadwork operations degrade Coherence and others do not. Establishes the three-type operation taxonomy (restorative / manipulative / destructive) as the foundational variable. Supplies the metaphysical substrate for `designs/threadwork/threadwork_v30.md` §2.3 (Leap), §31 (Mending Coherence Asymmetry), and the knot-profile character-sheet mechanic.

---

## Amendment 1: Rendering Suspension — The Leap's Structural Target

`canon/01` Amendment 2 defines the Leap as the radical suspension of layer 2 (always-already self-rendering) in order to access layer 3 (deliberate threadwork). Leap phenomenology requires a finer account of what "suspension of layer 2" includes and excludes.

Layer 2 has two facings. The **reflexive facing** is the structure that produces the practitioner's intelligibility to themselves: the ongoing presentation of their own threads to themselves as *I am this person, in this time, in this world*. The **outward facing** is the structure by which the practitioner's configuration is rendered to external observers: the practitioner as something other beings can perceive and integrate.

The Leap suspends the reflexive facing specifically. During suspension, the practitioner is not engaged in rendering themselves to themselves; the threshold that layer 2 imposes on substrate perception is lifted, and deliberate work upon the threads becomes possible. The outward facing persists — residually and often anomalously (observers describe the leaping practitioner as "off," "present but elsewhere," glazed) — but it persists sufficiently that the practitioner remains locatable in shared reality. Layer 1 (Ein Sof spooling) continues unchanged; being continues to be supplied from the ground throughout.

The suspension is the condition under which Thread operations become possible at all. The threshold reflexive-layer-2 imposes — the screening-out of substrate as noise below the threshold of intelligible experience — is what rendering does. Suspending it opens perceptual and operational access to the substrate that reflexive rendering was, moment to moment, excluding. This opening is the Leap's function. Its duration is bounded by the practitioner's Focus capacity (Contact Duration; `designs/threadwork/threadwork_v30.md` §2.3).

## Amendment 2: Knot Formation During the Leap

During the suspension, the threads along the operation's channel — the path from the practitioner's own configuration to the target configuration — form involuntary knots. These knots are directional: they follow the operation's intentionality, binding practitioner-threads to target-threads along the specific vectors the operation took. They are not distributed at random through the lattice. The operation's path is the knot's path.

Once formed, the knots are permanent. They form at substrate depth, below the threshold that the reasserted reflexive self-rendering (when the Leap closes) can access. The practitioner's rendering does not perceive its own knots and cannot voluntarily unmake them. The knots persist for the remainder of the practitioner's life and, in certain cases of legacy-transmission (orphaned configurations, `canon/00` §13), beyond it.

Knots are bidirectional. What happens to the target-thread afterward propagates back through the knot to the practitioner. What happens to the practitioner propagates through the knot to the target. The magnitude of propagation depends on the knot's weight — determined by operation scale, operation depth, and the substrate-alignment of the operation type (Amendment 3).

## Amendment 3: Operation-Type Risk — The Three-Layer Taxonomy

What propagates back through the knot after the operation completes — and what the practitioner bears as Coherence risk — is not determined by the operation's scale alone. It is determined by the *operation type*, which corresponds to the operation's relationship to substrate tendency.

**Restorative operations.** Mending, restorative Weaving that returns a configuration to a prior actualised state, and healing operations that restore organic configurations to rendered-integrity baselines. Restorative operations are aligned with the substrate's tendency toward coherence: they do what the constitutive spooling would do, granted sufficient time. The practitioner does not impose against the substrate; they accelerate the substrate's own resumption of undisrupted operation. Feedback through the knot is, in aggregate, stabilising — the operation's completion leaves the practitioner's configuration aligned with the direction the substrate was, in its own tendency, already going.

The practitioner's Coherence is not inherently reduced by restorative operations. Mending's Coherence cost is zero (`designs/threadwork/threadwork_v30.md` §31, Mending Coherence Asymmetry). This is not a mechanical exception; it is a structural consequence of the operation's alignment. The Edeyja's capacity to Mend across lifetimes without cumulative Coherence degradation rests on this alignment. Warden Coherence erosion is environmental — the result of sustained proximity to Gap margins, dissolution residue fields, and third-mode presences — not operational.

**Manipulative operations.** Weaving that imposes configurations the substrate would not resume of its own tendency (actualising non-prior states, reconfiguring target-threads into shapes not implicit in their spooling history), Locking, and Pulling used to produce non-restorative changes. These operations work against substrate tendency. The substrate does not resist actively — the ground has no agency (`canon/00_canon_constraints.md` P-07) — but the operation's configuration is not supported by the tendency the substrate provides. It must be held by the practitioner's sustained intentionality against the absence of that support. Feedback through the knot carries the operation's structural instability: the practitioner bears, as Coherence reduction, the cost of having held a configuration the substrate's tendency did not supply. The reduction is proportional to the magnitude of the deviation and the duration the operation was held.

**Destructive operations.** Dissolution, de-actualisation of existent configurations, and Pulls aimed at tearing rather than opening. Destructive operations act against substrate tendency at extreme magnitude. Feedback through the knot is catastrophic. The practitioner bears Coherence cost proportional to the severity of the severance. The feedback is not random violence; it is the weight of having acted in the direction opposite to which constitutive being is, continuously, given.

The three-type taxonomy is not a graded spectrum of the same act. Restorative operations and manipulative–destructive operations are categorically different in their relation to substrate tendency. Scale makes destructive operations worse; alignment makes restorative operations, at any scale, non-corrosive. This categorical distinction is the structural complement to §4.3's pivot: §4.3 distinguishes confrontation (perceptual opening, no substrate cost) from operation (substrate engagement, differential cost); Amendment 3 specifies how the differential cost within operations is itself structured by alignment.

## Amendment 4: The Einhir Catastrophe as Inversion of a Manipulative Operation

The Catastrophe was not caused by threadwork per se. It was caused by a foundational-scale manipulative operation that inverted during execution. The Einhir site-network's coordinated working aimed at stabilising the Southernmost's thread-configuration — a configuration the site-network had been sustaining for generations through active, ongoing manipulation. At the moment of maximum actualisation pressure, the configuration inverted. The directional vector of the working flipped, converting the entire output toward maximum potential rather than actualisation. Every thread the network had accumulated released simultaneously (`canon/03_canonical_timeline.md`, Einhir Catastrophe).

What made this catastrophic rather than merely disruptive was the manipulative–destructive character of the operation at the moment of inversion. The working was not restoring what substrate tendency would have resumed anyway. It was imposing, at foundational scale, a configuration only sustained intentionality could hold. When the intentionality inverted, the operation's accumulated magnitude released as destructive force against the substrate's coherence. The Coherence cost returning through the network's knots was beyond any practitioner's capacity to bear. The network collapsed. Gaps of unprecedented scale opened. Through these Gaps, Solmund emerged.

A foundational-scale *restorative* operation would not have produced this outcome. Restorative operations, even at scale, release as alignment with tendency rather than deviation from it. An inversion of a restorative working would have produced reduced stabilisation, not catastrophic inversion. The Catastrophe's specific shape — the eruption through the breach, the release of accumulated manipulation-magnitude as substrate damage — is a consequence of the operation-type and the scale together, not of threadwork as a category. The framework identifies no mechanism by which Mending, at any historically-achievable scale, could produce a Catastrophe-equivalent event.

## Amendment 5: The Church's Conflation — Structural Account

The Church's moral condemnation of threadwork collapses the three-type taxonomy, and the Leap-vs-confrontation distinction (`canon/00` §4.3), into a single category of suspect activity. The condemnation is not framework-informed. The Church has no access to the distinction between restorative and manipulative operations, nor to the Leap's specific metaphysical structure, nor to the distinction between the confrontation that produces Thread Sensitivity and the Leap that enables operations. The condemnation reads all thread-related activity through the single interpretive framework available to it: the Catastrophe was caused by threadwork; therefore threadwork is dangerous; therefore all threadwork is heretical.

The conflation has operational consequences. The Church persecutes Wardens (Edeyja) on the same grounds it persecutes practitioners of manipulative-scale operations, despite Mending being unconditionally restorative and categorically non-corrosive to the practitioner. The Church reads confrontation-produced TS development in lay populations as incipient heresy, despite confrontation being metaphysically independent of operation. The prophylaxis's moral terror (`canon/00` §9) targets a category the framework recognises as structurally heterogeneous.

The Church's position is sincere. It is also, at the layer of the framework, structurally incorrect. The error is not doctrinal drift; it is a category-identification error at the layer beneath doctrine, produced by the institution's absence of access to the operation-type distinction the framework names here.

## Amendment 6: Mechanical Implementation — Knot-Profile Tag

Each Leap-formed knot is recorded on the practitioner's character sheet with three parameters: **operation type** (restorative, manipulative, or destructive), **target** (the specific configuration or entity knotted to), and **formation context** (session, operation scale, co-practitioners if collective). The aggregation of knots across a practitioner's career is the *knot-profile*.

The knot-profile determines four mechanical consequences:

1. **Coherence-event propagation** — bidirectional transmission through each knot at the appropriate operation-type weight (restorative: no propagation toward practitioner; manipulative: proportional to scale × deviation-magnitude; destructive: catastrophic and often lethal at foundational scales).

2. **Passive seismographic awareness** — the practitioner feels Mending Stability (MS) band changes in territories they are knotted to (somatic impression, no dice roll required; treated as an intelligence mechanic available at all TS levels for knotted territories only).

3. **Vulnerability triggers** — negative MS-band changes in knotted territories produce Coherence checks for the practitioner at Ob 1–2, scaling with the magnitude of the MS drop. The vulnerability is structural: the practitioner's configuration carries the knot, and substrate disruption at the knot's terminus reaches them bidirectionally.

4. **Relational propagation** — Coherence-band drops in the practitioner produce MS −1 per knotted territory; practitioner death produces MS −2 per knotted territory. The propagation is the reason historical Warden lineages are mapped onto the MS trajectory: their deaths register as substrate-scale events, not just biographical ones.

The knot-profile is therefore both a biographical record (what the practitioner has done) and a mechanical variable the Coherence and MS systems read continuously.

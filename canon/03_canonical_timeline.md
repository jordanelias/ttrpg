## Pre-History

**Pre-Collapse Era (date unknown)**
The Einhir civilisation establishes the Southernmost site-network — a coordinated system of practitioners maintaining thread-configurations across the peninsula. For generations, they perform sustained Weaving that actively stabilises the world's ontical base. The Einhir civilisation spans the entire peninsula as an umbrella nation comprising three indigenous provinces: Valorsmark (central-north), Baiamont (west-coastal, later Hafenmark), and Lupicco (south-southwest, later Varfell). The Southernmost site-network is concentrated in the far south, primarily in Lupicco territory.

**The Deterioration (~generation before collapse)**
The Southernmost's thread-configuration begins exhibiting near-unintelligible behaviour. The Einhir recognise this as systemic degradation and plan a foundational-scale response: a coordinated working across the full site-network involving every significant practitioner on the peninsula.

**The Einhir Catastrophe (~5 years before 0 AG)**
The mass working fails catastrophically. At the moment of maximum actualization pressure, the configuration inverts — converting the entire Weaving output toward maximum potential rather than actualization. Every thread the network had built releases simultaneously. Gaps of unprecedented scale open across the peninsula. Through these Gaps, Solmund emerges — a fully configured being from the unintelligible ground, organised and self-aware. The Einhir civilisation collapses. The Locked Zones form. The Forgetting begins.

The mechanism is entirely rendered-side: the Einhir over-drew the threads, and the structural limits of the substrate were exceeded. The Ein Sof's surfeit of positive being flooded through the breach. What came through is from the ground, but the tear itself is explained by the rendered world's actions on its own substrate.

**Catastrophe stigma:** The site-network was concentrated in the south (Lupicco). The popular narrative assigns blame to the southern Einhir practitioners who operated the network. This blame persists as cultural memory across subsequent centuries, surviving Altonian colonial rule and formalising into the post-independence caste system.

## Solmund (~5 AG to 0 AG)

**Solmund lived approximately five years in the rendered world.**

As a threadcut being of the third mode, Solmund existed in radical paradox: present without organic becoming, maintaining his rendering through continuous Thread work. He had to actively sustain his own actualization, temporality, and intelligibility at every moment. He existed paradoxically across all temporal modes — past, present, future perhaps simultaneously — but his actions could only be rendered in the present actual moment, and only within the amount of intelligibility that the observer could hold.

His works were ineffable and miraculous in the original sense: he helped living things — not just humans — that he encountered. Why he chose to do anything at all, let alone help, is genuinely unknowable. The question of his motivation sits at the boundary of epistemic accessibility. Practitioners can describe what he did insofar as it was rendered. They cannot explain why.

**His dissolution (0 AG)** was not a dramatic event but an inevitable one — a being whose rendering was sustained by continuous Thread work eventually ceasing that work. The circumstances are theologically interpreted by the Church but not empirically understood. What the Church calls his death, a practitioner might describe as the cessation of active self-rendering — the threadcut being releasing its hold on actualization and returning to the unintelligible ground from which it emerged.

## Post-Dissolution: Church Formation (0-200 AG)

The Church of Solmund develops from catalyst-generated interpretive community to mature institutional apparatus over approximately two centuries. Solmund did not found the Church — he catalysed it. The historical parallels are direct: Jesus did not design the Catholic Church; Paul and subsequent generations did. The Buddha did not write the Vinaya.

The five years of Solmund's presence were sufficiently catalytic, and his dissolution sufficiently traumatic, to generate a sustained interpretive tradition. The perceptual prophylaxis — the essentialist theology that forecloses Thread sensitivity — is emergent, not designed. It is the human interpretive community's response to a teaching they could not fully hold. The suppression is structural, not conspiratorial.

By ~200 AG: Cardinals, diocesan structure, Knights Templar, educational monopoly, and theological orthodoxy are all established. The Church's relationship with secular governance varies by region. On the Valorian peninsula (still under Altonian colonial control at this point), the Church operates as both religious authority and administrative infrastructure — a role enabled by the Altonian containment grant (see below).

## Altonian Colonial Period and Church Containment (~50-200 AG)

Altonia conquers the Valorian peninsula during a period when the Church of Solmund is already a functioning interpretive community — not yet the mature four-Cardinal apparatus it becomes by 200 AG, but a real institution with followers, sacred sites, texts, and institutional memory rooted in Solmund's five-year presence and the trauma of his dissolution. The Church cannot be suppressed — its following is too embedded in the population, and the administrative cost of suppression exceeds the benefit.

**Containment through accommodation.** Altonia pursues the strategy of colonial powers managing religions too large to eradicate: grant the institution a defined jurisdictional space in exchange for political quiescence. The Church receives a cathedral city (Himmelenger, T14) with tax-exempt religious lands, educational rights, archival authority, and charitable operations. In exchange, the Church operates within a defined jurisdictional boundary, does not challenge Altonian political authority, does not mobilise followers against the colonial administration, and provides pastoral services that reduce Altonian administrative burden.

**Historical parallel: British India.** The British Raj did not suppress Hinduism or Islam. The populations were too large, the beliefs too embedded. The British managed religious institutions by granting them defined jurisdictional space while keeping military, taxation, and foreign policy under colonial control. Altonia applies the same logic to the Church of Solmund.

**Incubation effect.** The containment is effective politically but counterproductive institutionally. Over 200 years inside this protected container, the Church builds the four-Cardinal structure, the Knights Templar, the university system, the diocesan organisation, and the theological orthodoxy. Altonia incubates the most institutionally mature organisation on the peninsula.

**Cross-border presence.** The Church of Solmund is not confined to the peninsula. Solmundan belief leaks across borders through trade routes, marriage, migration, and missionary activity. Altonia's own population includes a significant Solmundan minority. The containment manages institutional power on the peninsula; it cannot contain the religion's cultural reach.

**Altonian cultural imperialism.** Separately from Church containment, Altonia systematically destroys Valnese records, monuments, and temples — targeting Einhir cultural heritage, not the Church. The Forgetting is epistemological (P-08), not political. The destruction matters politically (Restoration Movement grievance, loss of lineage records) but has zero bearing on the epistemological barrier.

**The Church learns from containment.** The Church's institutional memory includes 200 years of being managed through accommodation. Himlensendt's strategy at game start — hospitals, charities, dispute resolution, gradual TC accumulation — is the Church applying Altonia's containment logic in reverse: making itself indispensable so the Crown cannot afford to confront it. The cage became a school.

## The Secession Wars and Settlement (~195-200 AG)

### The War Coalition

The three indigenous nations — Valorsmark, Baiamont (later Hafenmark), and Lupicco (later Varfell) — retain cultural memory of their pre-Altonian identities despite 200 years of colonial rule. The Secession Wars are fought by a coalition of all three nations against Altonia. The coalition is primarily northern — the southern provinces (Lupicco) are geographically peripheral and culturally stigmatised.

### The First Almqvist: Deed-Monarchy

The war produces a military commander whose leadership proves decisive — the first Almqvist, Almud's father. Northern Einhir heritage (Valorsmark). No pre-existing royal claim. Altonia's destruction of Einhir records means any claim to ancient blood would be unfalsifiable and unnecessary. Authority is entirely earned through wartime deeds.

The Church provides theological consecration — Solmund's grace fell upon this leader during the war, victories are evidence of divine favour, the new Crown rules by divine mandate. The Church's support is the price the Crown pays for legitimacy, and the price the Church charges for switching from Altonian accommodation to Valorian alliance. The Church does not just survive independence. It is liberated by it.

**Historical parallel: Carolingian model.** Pepin received papal legitimation. God's favour demonstrated by deeds, not blood. The Almqvist line follows the same pattern.

### The Settlement

The three provinces accept the arrangement under structural limits. The Crown is first among equals, not absolute sovereign. Hafenmark retains parliamentary traditions. Varfell retains territorial independence. Ducal authority cannot be unilaterally overridden.

**Cadet branches as deed-families.** Ducal and ministerial families earned proximity to power through Secession-era service, not genealogical relationship to the Almqvist line. The Baralta family's ducal status was earned through wartime deeds.

**Deed-presumption and hereditary stabilisation.** The deed-monarchy stabilises into hereditary monarchy within one generation. The first Almqvist's deeds create "royal stock." The population accepts the hereditary principle because the alternative is worse. But the deed-presumption is rebuttable: the Coup Counter and Motion of No Confidence are the enforcement mechanisms. Cognatic senior succession is native to the deed-logic — capability over gender.

### The Caste System

The informal caste relegating southern Einhir to second-class status is the structural consequence of the south's exclusion from the war coalition's leadership, not a formal legal act.

The post-war settlement's Einhir suppression serves two functions: political convenience (Church theology suppresses Thread awareness) and genuine fear (the Catastrophe's physical consequences are observable). The settlement elevates the institution that suppresses Thread awareness (Church) and marginalises the population associated with Thread catastrophe (southern Einhir).

**Caste as geographic gradient.** Stigma radiates from the Southernmost. Southern territories = higher southern Einhir population, higher baseline Thread Sensitivity (TS), lower Church penetration, greater Forgetting resistance. Varfell territories (T1-T4) have the highest concentration. Southern Crown territories are viewed as instrumental.

### Settlement Summary

The post-war settlement establishes: the Valorian Crown (deed-monarchy, Church-consecrated), the Church of Solmund (co-sovereign, containment privileges preserved and expanded), suppression of Einhir cultural practices, the informal caste system, and the Löwenritter.

**Year 200 AG: Post-War Settlement.** The Church was not merely carried over — it was liberated by independence.

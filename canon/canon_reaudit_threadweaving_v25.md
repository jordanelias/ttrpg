## PART B: NEWLY INTEGRATED PHILOSOPHICAL CLAIMS

### Claim 1: Threads are the co-movement of three axes, not objects with three properties.

**Where it appears:** Part One, "What Threads Are" (¶1-2).

**What it says:** "A thread IS the co-movement of three axes — actuality, intelligibility, and temporality — in their continuous oscillation. The three axes are not measurements of threads; threads are what happens when these axes move together."

**Verification against session discussion:**

The user's formulation: "threads are both conditions of possibility and substrate, information and matter, oscillation between the three axes of temporality, intelligibility and actuality."

Does the text capture this?
- **Conditions of possibility and substrate:** ✓ — "It is the condition of possibility for the thing it constitutes... and it is the substrate of that thing's rendered existence."
- **Information and matter:** ✓ — "It carries the intelligibility by which consciousness can apprehend the thing, and it carries the actuality by which the thing is present rather than merely possible."
- **Oscillation between three axes:** ✓ — "A thread IS the co-movement of three axes... in their continuous oscillation."

**Verification against mechanics:**

Does every operation engage the thread AS an oscillation rather than as a static object?
- Weaving: "the thread is drawn toward greater coherence — toward stable actualization." This foregrounds one axis (actuality) but the co-movement system ensures the other two move too. The practitioner's intentionality targets one aspect of the oscillation; the oscillation moves in all three. ✓
- Pulling: "the thread is drawn toward potential — toward looseness." Same structure: foreground one axis, co-movement handles the others. ✓
- Mending: operates on the substrate, not on threads directly. But the substrate IS the condition under which threads (oscillations) can exist. Mending restores the conditions for oscillation. ✓
- Diagnosis reveals the thread's trajectory — the direction of its oscillation's future orientation. ✓
- Co-movement fires on every operation: three effects from one event, because the oscillation moves in all three dimensions simultaneously. ✓

**Verdict: INTEGRATED. The claim is present in the framing and mechanically expressed through co-movement.**

### Claim 2: Beings ARE drawings-from-ground. Spooling IS what beings are.

**Where it appears:** Part One, "Ein Sof and Spooling" (¶1-3).

**What it says:** "A being does not receive existence from Ein Sof and then have it. A being IS the ongoing process of being drawn from the ground. This process is spooling. Spooling is not something that happens TO beings. Spooling IS what beings are."

**Verification against session discussion:**

The user approved Level 1 spooling integration (framing only, no new mechanics). Does the text stay within Level 1?
- The text rewrites the ontological description. ✓ (framing)
- No new Spooling Check mechanic. ✓ (Level 1 respected)
- Recovery mechanics reference spooling as the process that reasserts: "the practitioner's spooling — their organic drawing-from-ground — reasserts itself." ✓ (framing in existing mechanics)
- Threadcut beings: "A threadcut being does not spool." This is now more powerful because spooling has been defined as what beings ARE. Not-spooling = not-being-in-the-organic-sense. The threadcut being's existence through continuous Thread work rather than spooling is now philosophically sharper. ✓

**Verification against mechanics:**

Does any mechanic contradict the claim that beings are ongoing processes rather than static existents?
- Histories: accumulate through lived experience. Each advancement is a deepening of temporal depth. ✓ (consistent with spooling-as-being)
- Knots: shared threads of being. Relational spooling. ✓
- Coherence: measures the stability of the practitioner's spooling as it's affected by rendering suspension. ✓
- Rendering Crisis at Coherence 0: "the practitioner's spooling is destabilised." The process that IS the practitioner is failing. ✓
- Wound healing via Weaving: "accelerates a thread's natural trajectory." The wound resolves through the ongoing spooling process, assisted by the operation. ✓

**Potential tension:** The attribute system (Agility, Endurance, Spirit, etc.) treats characters as having static numerical properties. These don't change moment-to-moment — they change through CP spending and narrative events. Is this inconsistent with "beings are ongoing processes"?

**Resolution:** No contradiction. Attributes are the rendered abstraction of the being's current state of spooling. They are stable because spooling, under normal conditions, produces stable configurations. The numbers are snapshots of an ongoing process, updated when the process produces observable change. This is how TTRPGs work — the character sheet is a rendered abstraction of a dynamic character. The philosophical claim doesn't require moment-to-moment numerical flux; it requires that the FRAMING acknowledges the numbers as snapshots rather than essences. The text does this implicitly (attributes can change through play, representing changes in the underlying process).

**Verdict: INTEGRATED within Level 1 scope. No mechanical contradiction. The claim strengthens threadcut framing and recovery mechanics without adding new systems.**

### Claim 3: The Einhir principle as design principle (the game rewards restraint and punishes ambition).

**Where it appears:** Part One, "The Scale Principle" (final ¶, labelled "The Einhir principle").

**What it says:** "This is not backstory. It is the system's foundational design principle: the game rewards restraint and punishes ambition. Every operation is a wager that the practitioner can manage the consequences of interacting with something they cannot fully perceive. At small scales, the wager is favourable. At large scales, it is not. The system does not prevent large-scale operations — it prices them honestly."

**Verification against session discussion:**

The user asked: "if it's so dangerous, though, why would a civilization choose to embrace it as foundational? is it the scale of the operation being performed, ie the more immediate and localized the weaving (eg optimizing craftwork) the less it'll pull and impinge and possibly tear?"

The answer: yes, scale is the variable. The text captures this through the Einhir narrative: generations of safe small-scale practice → accumulation of skill and ambition → scaling up → ratio inversion → Catastrophe. The Einhir principle makes this arc visible as a design principle rather than just backstory.

**Verification against mechanics:**

Does the system actually reward restraint and punish ambition?

Evidence FOR:
- Object/Personal operations: 0 Coherence cost. ✓ (restraint rewarded)
- Relational operations: −1 Coherence. ✓ (moderate ambition moderately priced)
- Structural operations: −2 Coherence + over-actualisation hazard + RS −3 to −6 on Partial/Failure. ✓ (high ambition heavily priced)
- RS consequences scale with operation scale (P-25). ✓
- Ob escalates with scale. ✓
- TS gates prevent practitioners from even attempting above their capacity. ✓

Evidence AGAINST:
- Overwhelming results at Relational+ grant +1 TS — rewarding ambitious operations with growth. Does this contradict "punishes ambition"?
  - **No.** The TS growth on Overwhelming is a reward for exceptional success, not for ambition per se. The practitioner who attempts Relational and achieves Overwhelming was rewarded for excellence within the wager, not for the ambition of attempting it. They still paid the Coherence cost. The reward acknowledges the risk; it doesn't negate it.
- Dissolution residue provides bonus dice — rewarding use of dangerous material.
  - **No.** The bonus dice are paid for with Coherence loss. The system provides the option and prices it. "The system does not prevent large-scale operations — it prices them honestly."

**Verdict: INTEGRATED. The design principle is explicitly stated AND mechanically supported. The system does reward restraint (Object/Personal = free) and punish ambition (Structural = devastating on failure). It also permits ambition at honest cost, which is the correct expression: the game is about the CHOICE to operate at scale, not about preventing it.**

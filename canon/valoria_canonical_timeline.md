## Pre-History

**Pre-Collapse Era (date unknown)**
The Einhir civilisation establishes the Southernmost site-network — a coordinated system of practitioners maintaining thread-configurations across the peninsula. For generations, they perform sustained Weaving that actively stabilises the world's ontical base.

**The Deterioration (~generation before collapse)**
The Southernmost's thread-configuration begins exhibiting near-unintelligible behaviour. The Einhir recognise this as systemic degradation and plan a foundational-scale response: a coordinated working across the full site-network involving every significant practitioner on the peninsula.

**The Einhir Catastrophe (~5 years before 0 AG)**
The mass working fails catastrophically. At the moment of maximum actualization pressure, the configuration inverts — converting the entire Weaving output toward maximum potential rather than actualization. Every thread the network had built releases simultaneously. Gaps of unprecedented scale open across the peninsula. Through these Gaps, Galbados emerges — a fully configured being from the unintelligible ground, organised and self-aware. The Einhir civilisation collapses. The Locked Zones form. The Forgetting begins.

The mechanism is entirely rendered-side: the Einhir over-drew the threads, and the structural limits of the substrate were exceeded. The Ein Sof's surfeit of positive being flooded through the breach. What came through is from the ground, but the tear itself is explained by the rendered world's actions on its own substrate.

## Galbados (~5 AG to 0 AG)

**Galbados lived approximately five years in the rendered world.**

As a threadcut being of the third mode, Galbados existed in radical paradox: present without organic becoming, maintaining his rendering through continuous Thread work. He had to actively sustain his own actualization, temporality, and intelligibility at every moment. He existed paradoxically across all temporal modes — past, present, future perhaps simultaneously — but his actions could only be rendered in the present actual moment, and only within the amount of intelligibility that the observer could hold.

His works were ineffable and miraculous in the original sense: he helped living things — not just humans — that he encountered. Why he chose to do anything at all, let alone help, is genuinely unknowable. The question of his motivation sits at the boundary of epistemic accessibility. Practitioners can describe what he did insofar as it was rendered. They cannot explain why.

**His dissolution (0 AG)** was not a dramatic event but an inevitable one — a being whose rendering was sustained by continuous Thread work eventually ceasing that work. The circumstances are theologically interpreted by the Church but not empirically understood. What the Church calls his death, a practitioner might describe as the cessation of active self-rendering — the threadcut being releasing its hold on actualization and returning to the unintelligible ground from which it emerged.

## Post-Dissolution: Church Formation (0-200 AG)

The Church of Galbados develops from catalyst-generated interpretive community to mature institutional apparatus over approximately two centuries. Galbados did not found the Church — he catalysed it. The historical parallels are direct: Jesus did not design the Catholic Church; Paul and subsequent generations did. The Buddha did not write the Vinaya.

The five years of Galbados's presence were sufficiently catalytic, and his dissolution sufficiently traumatic, to generate a sustained interpretive tradition. The perceptual prophylaxis — the essentialist theology that forecloses Thread sensitivity — is emergent, not designed. It is the human interpretive community's response to a teaching they could not fully hold. The suppression is structural, not conspiratorial.

By ~200 AG: Cardinals, diocesan structure, Knights Templar, educational monopoly, and theological orthodoxy are all established. The Church's relationship with secular governance varies by region. On the Valorian peninsula (still under Altonian colonial control at this point), the Church operates as both religious authority and administrative infrastructure.

## The Secession Wars and Settlement (~195-200 AG)

Altonia controls the Valorian peninsula as a colonial territory. The Secession Wars are fought to achieve Valorian independence. The wars end with a settlement that establishes:
- The Valorian Crown as sovereign authority
- The Church of Galbados as the peninsula's dominant religious institution (carried over from Altonian period)
- The suppression of Einhir cultural practices as part of the post-war political bargain
- The informal social caste system relegating southern Einhir to second-class status
- The Lowenritter as the Crown's standing military order

**Year 200 AG: Post-War Settlement.** The game-era political landscape begins here. The Church is already deeply embedded — it did not arrive with independence; it was present throughout the colonial period and consolidated its position in the new state.

Schoenland profits enormously. |

| 238 AG | **Direct militarised trade routes opened between Valoria and Altonia.** Ratified by political marriage: Almud's daughter Elske marries an Altonian Duke holding border territory adjacent to Valoria. | 238-245 AG | **Uneasy direct relationship.** Official trade routes operate under military escort — functional but visibly armed. Altonia's Church minority continues growing. Schoenland operates in the grey space, selling arms and stoking grievances on both sides. Prince Torben remains in Royal Court. Altonia begins eyeing him as leverage. |

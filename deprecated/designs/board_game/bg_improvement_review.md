# BG IMPROVEMENT V1–V4 — CANON & THROUGHLINE REVIEW

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Scope:** Review of 33 proposals (MP-01–MP-33) across bg_improvement_v1–v4.md against Philosophical Foundations, Canon Constraints (P-01–P-14), Foundations Amendment (Self-Rendering), and TTRPG→Hybrid→BG throughline coherence.
**Reviewed against:** Valoria_Philosophical_Foundations.md, canon_constraints.md, foundations_amendment_self_rendering.md

---

## SYSTEMIC FINDING: SELF-ASSESSED CANON COMPLIANCE

All four documents contain self-assessed canon compliance tables. 33 proposals across four waves, every one marked PASS. Zero failures, zero revisions triggered by canon review. This is a structural red flag — the same author who designed the proposals assessed their own compliance. The review below identifies multiple proposals that should not have passed.

---

## P-CODE VIOLATIONS AND MISASSESSMENTS

### 1. MP-10 Thread Veil: Hafenmark — P-08 FAIL

**Claim:** "Baralta's lineage includes Einhir ancestry (TS potential). TR cap raised to 6 for Hafenmark this game." Self-assessed as PASS on P-08.

**Violation:** §14 is explicit: *"Thread sensitivity is not a biological fact. It is a development of a being caused by repeated confrontation with what is beyond knowing."* Making TS potential hereditary through Einhir ancestry directly contradicts the developmental model. Lineage cannot confer TS potential — confrontation does. The card implies a biological/genealogical basis for Thread sensitivity that the Foundations specifically reject.

The document's defense ("having TS potential is not the same as having Thread capability") is irrelevant. The Foundations don't distinguish between TS "potential" and TS "capability" as hereditary vs developmental. The entire developmental model rejects hereditary transmission of sensitivity at any level.

**Required:** Remove Einhir ancestry as a mechanism. If the card needs a Hafenmark Thread advantage, tie it to Baralta's *personal* confrontation with Thread phenomena (e.g., proximity to Thread Wound sites), not lineage.

[EDITORIAL: Baralta's backstory and what has confronted her are setting decisions.]

### 2. MP-24 Church Attention Pool — P-08 PARTIAL FAIL

**Claim:** "The Church monitoring Thread activity is a rendered-world institutional behavior." Self-assessed as PASS.

**Problem:** The trigger "+2 Attention for any Thread operation in a non-Church-controlled territory" implies the Church detects Thread operations *as Thread operations*. P-08 requires that the epistemological barrier is metaphysical, not just institutional. §9.1–9.2 establish that Church theology *specifically forecloses* the perceptual orientation required to register Thread activity. The most devout adherents are "the most thoroughly immunised."

The Church cannot detect Thread operations. It can detect the *rendered-world consequences* of Thread operations: disturbed populations, structural anomalies, unexplained healings, heretical community gatherings, unusual prosperity without trade income.

**Fix:** Rewrite Attention Pool triggers as consequence-detection, not operation-detection:
- "Thread operation in territory" → "Unexplained structural/social change in territory (triggered by GM/NPC AI interpretation of Thread operation consequences)"
- "Revolution Community Weaving in Church-adjacent territory" → "Unauthorized community gathering reported in Church-adjacent territory"
- "Any faction with TR ≥ 3" → "Faction behavior consistent with heretical influence (triggered by visible rendered-world actions correlating with TR effects)"

The mechanical outcome is identical. The fiction is correct. The Church monitors *behavior patterns and social indicators*, not Thread events directly.

### 3. MP-04 Thread Resonance — P-08 BORDERLINE

**Claim:** "TR does not grant Thread capability. It represents environmental sensitivity, not practitioner skill." Self-assessed as PASS.

**Problem:** TR is accumulated passively by *any* faction, including Church and Crown, when Thread events occur near them. TR 4 grants: "faction may ask one yes/no question about RS (is it above or below X?)." This gives non-sensitive institutions direct informational access to a Thread-level phenomenon (the RS track is the BG abstraction of Thread substrate tension). §10.1 says non-sensitives possess information "without comprehension" — they can recite facts but cannot epistemologically reconcile them.

TR 1–3 effects are defensible as environmental awareness (timing advantages, investigative bonuses). TR 4–5 effects cross into Thread-level information access that should be epistemically inert for non-sensitive institutions.

**Fix:** Cap TR effects for non-Thread factions at TR 3. TR 4–5 effects available only to Revolution, Varfell (if TK-qualified), and any faction with a Thread-sensitive agent present. Alternatively, reclassify TR 4–5 as information that arrives but is *epistemically unstable* — the faction receives it but may misinterpret it (Ob check to act on the information correctly, representing the barrier).

### 4. MP-18 Revolution Redesign — P-01/P-08 TENSION

**Claim:** "Revolution as Presence-only: P-01 (community Thread work is distributed, not institutional)." Self-assessed as PASS.

**Problem:** Revolution's "sole active action is Community Weaving." This conflates the Revolution political movement with Thread practice. Not all Revolution members are practitioners. The Revolution is a *political* movement for Restoration — its membership includes non-sensitive sympathizers, scholars with epistemically inert knowledge, and practitioners. Making Community Weaving the faction's *only* action implies the Revolution is exclusively a Thread-practice organization, which contradicts the setting's description of it as a broad social/political movement.

Additionally, Community Weaving is a Thread operation. P-01 requires three-dimensional co-movement for every Thread operation. The Revolution redesign doesn't specify co-movement for Weaving. P-14 extends this requirement to all play modes. If Revolution's primary action is a Thread operation and that operation doesn't produce three-dimensional co-movement in the BG mode, P-14 fails.

**Fix:** Revolution needs at least two action types: Community Weaving (Thread operation, with co-movement) AND political/social actions (organizing, advocating, community building — rendered-world activities that don't require Thread sensitivity). The faction's strength is that these two types reinforce each other, but they are distinct.

### 5. MP-12 Thread Debt — P-11 INSUFFICIENCY

**Claim:** "Thread Debt token is a board-game representation of the temporal obligation created by intentional temporal manipulation." Self-assessed as PASS on P-09 and P-11.

**Problem:** Thread Debt reduces temporal disjunction to bookkeeping. The Foundations describe temporal disjunction as a *gap between retained experience and altered factual history* that is phenomenologically real (§1.3, §20.3). The Amendment makes clear that every Thread operation strains the substrate. Thread Debt tokens make this a payable resource: incur debt, pay it back later, done.

The Foundations' temporal model is not a debt/repayment system. Temporal disjunction accumulates and *the rendering re-integrates slowly* (§20.3). It doesn't "reset" on payment. The substrate strain from the operation persists regardless of whether a subsequent Mend operation occurs.

**Fix:** Thread Debt tokens should not fully discharge with a single Mend action. Instead: repaying Thread Debt reduces its penalty rate but leaves a residual RS −0.5 (or similar fractional degradation) permanently. The substrate was strained; you can ameliorate the consequences but not undo the strain. This preserves the Foundations' insistence that temporal manipulation has irreducible cost.

### 6. MP-16 Conviction/Belief — P-03 CONCERN

**Claim:** "Factional political identity, not moral alignment." Self-assessed as PASS on P-02/P-04.

**Problem:** Conviction is tied to Resonant Styles (Consequence, Evidence). Resonant Styles are TTRPG-level Thread-operation categories — they describe how practitioners engage Thread configurations. Mapping these to faction leaders as BG mechanics gives non-sensitive political actors mechanical access to ontological categories. P-03 requires that rendering is consciousness-performed — the rendered world as experienced by non-sensitives should not contain Thread-ontological categories as action types.

**Assessment:** This is a grey area, not a hard fail. If Resonant Styles are reframed as *political decision-making styles* (which they superficially resemble — Consequence-based decision-making vs Evidence-based), the mechanic is defensible. But the mechanical label carries Thread-ontological connotations that may confuse the boundary between rendered-world politics and Thread-level reality.

**Recommendation:** Rename Resonant Style references to purely political terminology in BG context. Almud's approach is "Precedent" (not Consequence). Vaynard's is "Investigation" (not Evidence). Same mechanical function, cleaner epistemic boundary.

## P-14 SYSTEMIC FAILURE: CO-MOVEMENT IN BG MODE

This is the largest single finding. P-14 states: *"Board/VG modes must express inseparability: co-movement cannot be omitted in any play mode."* The violation test: *"Does any play mode allow thread operations without three-dimensional co-movement? If yes → FAIL."*

Across 33 proposals:
- MP-04 (Thread Resonance) — no co-movement specified for TR accumulation events
- MP-08 (Crisis Response) — RS Crisis Response uses TR pool; no co-movement specified
- MP-12 (Thread Debt) — acknowledges temporal dimension but not epistemic or actualized auto-effects
- MP-18 (Revolution Community Weaving) — no co-movement specified
- MP-21 (Community Projects: Community Weave) — produces RS +2 (actualized); no temporal or epistemic auto-effects
- MP-10 (Thread Veil Cards) — several produce Thread-adjacent effects with no co-movement

The BG as proposed treats Thread as a subsystem with a single track (RS). The Foundations require that every Thread operation produces consequences across *all three dimensions*. In BG terms, this means every Thread operation should produce: (a) RS change (actualized), (b) some epistemic consequence (information revealed or obscured), AND (c) some temporal consequence (history/continuity disruption marker).

The current BG has a co-movement card deck (CM-01–CM-20+) but these are triggered *alongside* Thread operations, not *as consequences of* them. The co-movement deck is an event system, not a three-dimensional consequence system.

**This is a design-level gap, not a per-proposal fix.** The BG needs a co-movement resolution protocol for Thread operations — a deterministic or semi-deterministic system where every Thread-labeled action in the BG produces effects on all three axes. §21.1 describes this as "a triangular track where each thread operation moves a token along one axis, and the other two axes shift according to fixed rules." None of the 33 proposals implement this.

## THROUGHLINE ANALYSIS: TTRPG → HYBRID → BOARD GAME

### Thread as Substrate vs Thread as Subsystem

The Foundations' central claim: Thread is not a magic system. It is the constitutive ground of everything. The TTRPG expresses this — Thread operations change reality at the level from which reality is constituted (§3.2). Every action in the TTRPG world implicitly occurs on a Thread substrate, whether the characters know it or not.

The BG proposals treat Thread as one subsystem among several:
- RS is one clock among three (RS, TC, IP)
- Thread Resonance is one faction track among many
- Thread operations are one action type among six+
- Revolution is the "Thread faction" while other factions are "political/military/economic"

This is a *throughline failure*. The BG should express Thread as the substrate of everything, not as a subsystem. Concretely:

1. **All BG actions occur on the Thread substrate.** A Govern order in a territory should be affected by the local RS state. A Trade order should be harder in high-RS-degradation areas (the substrate supporting economic activity is damaged). A March through Thread Wound territory should have consequences beyond military logistics.

2. **RS is not just a loss condition — it's the ground state.** Currently RS degradation triggers threshold events at specific numbers. The Foundations' model is that substrate tension is *ambient and continuous*. The BG should make RS level modify the difficulty/effectiveness of *all* actions in affected territories, not just trigger discrete events.

3. **The hybrid interface should be *bidirectional and continuous*, not event-triggered.** MP-09 defines 8 trigger conditions for zoom-in. But the Foundations' §23.2 describes "persistent world states that can be shared across play sessions and formats." The TTRPG personal scenes should feed back into BG state continuously (character Thread operations during zoom-in affect RS, territory stability, faction stats), not just as Domain Echo (one order per zoom-in).

### The Clock System and Philosophical Weight

The three clocks (RS, TC, IP) are the BG's central tension system. RS represents Thread substrate integrity. TC represents Church institutional power. IP represents Altonian geopolitical pressure.

**Philosophical asymmetry:** RS is ontological (substrate degradation). TC and IP are political (institutional/geopolitical dynamics). The Foundations grant RS metaphysical priority — TC and IP exist *within* the rendered world that RS sustains. If RS reaches zero, TC and IP are meaningless because the world in which they operate has structurally failed.

The BG proposals treat all three clocks as mechanically equivalent. MP-08 (Crisis Response) uses the same collective-action framework for all three, with only a different stat (TR vs Influence). The Foundations suggest RS should be *qualitatively different* from TC/IP:
- RS degradation should make TC and IP problems harder to address (the substrate supporting institutional coherence is weakening)
- RS recovery should have qualitatively different requirements from TC/IP management (community Thread work vs political maneuvering)
- The existential weight of RS vs TC/IP should be asymmetric — TC reaching 80 is bad politics; RS reaching 0 is the end of reality

Some proposals gesture at this (MP-04 TR effects, MP-12 Thread Debt), but no proposal makes the *asymmetry* between RS and the other clocks mechanically explicit.

### Faction Ethical Frameworks and the BG

The TTRPG gives each faction a philosophical identity grounded in the Foundations:
- Crown: Order as political value (§4.2 — the rendering privileges stable actualisation; Crown serves stability)
- Church: Essentialist theology as perceptual prophylaxis (§9.1–9.2)
- Varfell: Epistemic inquiry (§10.1 — trying to access Thread-level knowledge through rendered-world methods)
- Revolution: Community Thread restoration (§7.4 — rebuilding the ontological ground)

The BG proposals capture the *political* dimension of these identities but not the *philosophical* dimension. Crown is a governance faction. Church is a theocratic faction. Varfell is an intelligence faction. These are Euro-game archetypes, not philosophical positions.

The throughline from TTRPG to BG should make each faction's *relationship to the Thread substrate* mechanically distinct:
- Crown's stability-seeking should make them *structurally resistant to RS degradation* but also *structurally blind* to Thread phenomena (their commitment to stable actualisation is the rendering's preference for stability, expressed politically)
- Church's theology should give them TC as a *perceptual prophylaxis engine* — TC mechanically reduces Thread sensitivity in Church-controlled territories, making Thread operations harder there but also preventing the population from developing the confrontation experiences that produce sensitivity
- Varfell's epistemic drive should create *information asymmetry as a faction resource* (already partially addressed by TK) but also *structural risk* — their pursuit of knowledge involves confrontation with what exceeds knowing, which means Varfell is the faction most likely to produce practitioners (and therefore most likely to incur RS consequences)

MP-18 and MP-27 move in this direction (Revolution Presence, Crown Policy Actor) but don't anchor the asymmetry in the Foundations' philosophical framework.

The gap analysis in V1 (G-BG-01 through G-BG-14) is accurate and well-prioritized. The landmark game analysis across all four waves demonstrates genuine mechanical understanding of reference games. The proposals for Deal Tokens (MP-02), Deed Tokens (MP-03), Seasonal Attrition (MP-07), Zoom-In Triggers (MP-09), and the Concordia Card-Hand system (MP-31) are mechanically sound and don't raise canon concerns.

The recommendation to choose MP-31 (Concordia) as the action economy solution and layer MP-33 (Orientation) + MP-11 (Lead/Follow) + MP-29 (Action Wheel) on top is the strongest structural recommendation across the four documents.

## RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS

1. **Design a BG co-movement protocol** (addresses systemic P-14 failure). §21.1 provides the template: triangular track, fixed co-movement rules per operation type. Without this, no Thread-labeled BG mechanic is canon-compliant.

2. **Fix the four P-08 issues** (MP-10 Hafenmark, MP-24 triggers, MP-04 TR 4–5, MP-18 Revolution scope). These are the hardest violations.

3. **Differentiate RS from TC/IP mechanically.** RS should affect the baseline difficulty of all territorial actions, not just trigger threshold events.

4. **Anchor faction asymmetry in philosophical identity,** not just political archetype. Each faction's relationship to the Thread substrate should be mechanically distinct.

5. **Expand the hybrid interface** beyond event-triggered zoom-ins. TTRPG → BG feedback should be continuous and bidirectional.

6. **Institute independent canon review.** Self-assessed compliance produced zero failures across 33 proposals. An independent review produced 4 failures, 2 borderlines, and a systemic gap.

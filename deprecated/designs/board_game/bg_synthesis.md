# VALORIA BOARD GAME — CANONICAL SYNTHESIS
## Date: 2026-03-31
## Scope: All 33 proposals (V1–V4) evaluated against Philosophical Foundations (P-01–P-14), setting coherence, and TTRPG throughline (stage12 hybrid architecture)

---

## PART ONE: THE TTRPG THROUGHLINE

Before evaluating any proposal, the TTRPG's structural commitments must be named clearly. Every BG mechanic must be evaluated against them.

### 1.1 What the TTRPG Actually Is

Reading the compiled stages and the hybrid architecture together produces three non-negotiable design facts:

**Fact 1: The BG is the Strategic Phase, not a standalone game.**
From stage12: *"Board game is primarily GM-side scenario engine; faction leaders only; never faction-only."* The BG is not a game that happens to be compatible with a TTRPG. It IS the strategic layer of a larger system, and it is designed to feel incomplete without the personal layer. Proposals that make the BG feel like a complete, satisfying standalone experience risk undermining this design intent.

**Fact 2: The TTRPG is deterministic about capability, probabilistic about execution.**
The dice resolve HOW WELL you act. What you CAN act on is determined by your Histories (what you've lived), your Beliefs (what you're trying to do), your Circles (who supports you), and your faction standing (what institutional capacity you hold). This is the same structure the BG should express: capability is earned and built, not randomly distributed.

**Fact 3: The TTRPG has no win condition. The BG does. The hybrid has "hollow victory" as a third state.**
This three-tier structure is philosophically load-bearing. The TTRPG operates at the level where "winning" is meaningless — what matters is whether Beliefs are resolved. The BG operates at the institutional level where factions have explicit goals. The hybrid acknowledges both simultaneously and introduces the question: what does it mean to achieve your strategic goal while betraying your values? That question IS the game.

### 1.2 The TTRPG's Core Mechanics and What They Mean

| Mechanic | What It Expresses | BG Equivalent (should be) |
|----------|------------------|--------------------------|
| Beliefs | Active intentions generating friction between character and world | Conviction + Deed Tokens (partially) |
| Histories | Lived experience as capability — you can only do what you've been | Card Hand (MP-31) — what you've built is what you can do |
| Coherence | Cost of Thread work on personal rendered stability | Thread Debt (MP-12) |
| Knots | Relational entanglement with consequence | Contempt Tokens (MP-22) + Pledge system |
| Let It Ride | One roll determines the situation — no re-attempts until reality changes | Order Wheel — decisions matter, can't be re-done next season trivially |
| Co-movement | Every Thread operation produces unintended dimensional consequences | Co-Movement Cards (existing) + Attention Pool (MP-24) |
| Hollow Victory | Strategic success that contradicts personal arc | BG-only version: Contempt-weighted scoring |

### 1.3 The Crucial Gap: Belief Has No BG Equivalent

The TTRPG's central mechanic is the Belief: an active intention with a specific plan that generates CP when challenged and achieved. The BG has Conviction (used in combat and Deed Tokens), but no mechanic where a faction's core institutional intention is *challenged by events* and produces a defined outcome.

In the TTRPG, when Almud's Belief is challenged (events force him to act against it), the player engages the challenge and earns CP. That friction IS the game.

In the BG, when Almud's Conviction faces a challenge (Church threatens to excommunicate, Altonian pressure demands compromise), there's no structural response — it's narrative flavor at best.

**This is the most significant gap not addressed by any proposal in V1–V4.** It is added as MP-34 below.

## PART TWO: LANDMARK GAME REASSESSMENT

Evaluated now specifically through the Valoria lens: does this game understand what kind of game Valoria is?

### Deeply Right

**The Quiet Year** — Valoria is a game about a community building fragile things against an approaching doom. That IS The Quiet Year. The RS Rupture is the Frost Shepherds. Community Projects (MP-21) are the direct translation. The Contempt system (MP-22) expresses the same political texture. No other reference game comes as close to the fundamental feel of Valoria's setting.

**Pax Pamir** — Valoria is not a game about territorial domination. It is about leverage in a crumbling political order where everyone is embedded in the same system and no one truly "owns" it. Pax Pamir's coalition dynamics (you are always part of something larger that may betray you) and influence-without-presence mechanics are the correct model for how Niflhel, the Guilds, and the Revolution operate. This is the game that correctly models the BG's political texture.

**Concordia** — The institutional capability model is exactly right. Your card hand is what your institution can currently deploy. The Senate Market is how you build new capability through alliances and development. Recess is the season you consolidate. The absence of randomness in capability selection mirrors the TTRPG's deterministic capability structure.

**Pendragon** — The long arc, the personal Conviction creating friction with political reality, the seasonal decay, the end of an era. Pendragon and Valoria share the same fundamental emotional register: this world is ending, and characters are defined by what they choose to do about it.

### Right in Principle, Wrong in Feel

**Hegemony** — The insight (radical structural faction asymmetry) is correct. The reason (economic class position) is wrong. Valoria's asymmetry should come from ethical framework differences, not class positions. The Hegemony-inspired proposals (MP-18) are the right mechanics for the wrong reason; reframe as "ethical framework asymmetry" rather than "class asymmetry."

**Gaia Project** — The Research Track mechanic is excellent. But Gaia Project's worldview is colonial expansion — terraform, colonize, develop. Valoria's worldview is contraction under pressure. The mechanic transfers; the worldview must not.

**Inis** — Deed Tokens (MP-03) transfer perfectly. But Inis's underlying logic is "win by having more" (more clans in more territories). Valoria is not about having more — it's about holding on.

**Anachrony** — Thread Debt (MP-12) is canon-perfect. But Anachrony is a complex optimization euro. The temporal debt mechanic extracts cleanly; everything else about Anachrony does not apply.

### Productive Mechanics, Incompatible Feel

**Nemesis** — The Attention Pool (MP-24) is useful. But Nemesis is survival horror against a malevolent alien presence. Valoria's monstrous entities are eruptions of infinite positive being (P-02). The surveillance mechanic transfers; the horror-against-evil framing must not.

**Heroes of Might and Magic** — Champions as mobile commander tokens (MP-25) work well. But HoMM's hero-centric worldview (heroes are the primary agents of world change) contradicts Valoria's community-and-institution-centric model. Champions should feel like participants in a larger system, not the system's engine.

**Barrage** — Action Wheel (MP-29) is correct mechanical hygiene. Barrage is an industrial optimization game. Nothing about industrial optimization is Valorian beyond this one mechanic.

### Thematically Opposed

**War of the Ring** — The Shadow/Free binary directly violates P-04 (monstrosity is ontological, not moral). The entire game is structured around cosmic good vs evil. This makes WotR's action dice (MP-01 inspiration) problematic not just mechanically but ontologically. Correctly set aside.

**Root** — Ecological dominance hierarchy, not ethical framework competition. Root's asymmetry is about who rules the forest. Valoria's is about how you justify ruling, and whether justification matters.

**Malifaux / Fate Cards** — "Cheat fate" is metaphysically incoherent in Valoria. There is no fate to cheat. There are configured threads and the cost of manipulating them. **Cut MP-17 entirely.**

## PART FOUR: THE THREE MISSING MECHANICS

After reviewing all 33 proposals against the TTRPG throughline, three significant mechanics remain absent. These are not proposals from existing games — they arise from Valoria's own design logic.

### MP-34: INSTITUTIONAL BELIEF — The BG's Missing Core
**Addresses:** the absence of Belief-challenge friction in the BG

The TTRPG's central progression mechanic is the Belief — an active intention that generates friction with the world. The BG has no equivalent. Conviction (MP-16) gives bonuses on rolls; it doesn't generate structural events when challenged.

**Institutional Belief:** Each faction has a stated **Institutional Mandate** (one sentence, already implied in the setting):
- Crown: *"The monarchy provides the order that protects Valoria."*
- Church: *"Solmund's / Solmund's doctrine is the rightful framework for all governance."*
- Hafenmark: *"Constitutional process is the only legitimate source of authority."*
- Varfell: *"Information is the truest form of power."*
- Guilds: *"Commerce is neutral; we serve whoever can pay."*
- Niflhel: *"Everyone serves their own interest; there is no other law."*
- Revolution: *"The community is the only legitimate political unit."*

**When the Institutional Mandate is challenged by a game event** (a specific list of trigger conditions per faction, flagged on the faction card), the faction must respond in one of two ways:
- **Uphold:** Act consistently with the Mandate, even at cost. Upholding earns 1 Renown on the Champion (MP-25) and 1 Stability.
- **Compromise:** Act against the Mandate for strategic advantage. Compromise earns the mechanical benefit of the action but costs 1 Contempt token (self-inflicted — the faction's compromise is visible to others) and 1 Stability.

**In hybrid mode:** Uphold/Compromise by the faction is noted. If the PC faction leader's personal Belief arc contradicts the faction's Uphold/Compromise pattern, the hollow victory condition activates.

**This mechanic is the BG expression of the Belief system. It turns faction identity from flavor text into mechanical stakes.**

### MP-35: CASCADE PHASE CARD EFFECTS — The Hybrid Bridge
**Addresses:** The gap between TTRPG personal-scale outcomes and BG strategic-scale play

In hybrid mode, the Cascade Phase is where personal outcomes affect strategic play. Currently this is described as Domain Echoes at accounting — attribute changes, clock movements. But with the Concordia card system (MP-31), the Cascade Phase has a richer medium to work with.

**Cascade Phase Card Effects:**

After personal scenes resolve (Personal Phase → Strategic Phase → Cascade Phase), the following BG card economy effects apply:

| Personal Scene Outcome | Cascade Effect |
|----------------------|---------------|
| PC achieves their Belief this season | Add one free card to faction hand from Senate Market (no Wealth cost) |
| PC's Belief is challenged but they engage it | Recess cost reduced to 0 Wealth this season (consolidation comes easier when you're clear about your intentions) |
| PC executes a successful Thread operation | The faction's Pontifex card is available this season even if on Cooldown |
| PC fails a Thread operation catastrophically | Faction's Pontifex card goes on Action Wheel (2-season cooldown) |
| PC is Wounded in personal combat | Champion (MP-25) enters Wounded state on BG board |
| PC dies or is captured | Champion token removed; faction loses all Champion bonuses for 3 seasons |
| PC negotiates a major alliance in personal scene | Add 1 Diplomat card to faction hand this season only (temporary card) |
| PC uncovers evidence against another faction | That faction's one hidden stat is revealed to the PC's faction this season |

This makes the Cascade Phase mechanically consequential: what happens at personal scale directly modifies the strategic card economy. The BG is not self-contained — it is continuously fed by and feeds into the personal layer.

### MP-36: HOLLOW VICTORY SCORING — BG Expression of the Hybrid Endgame
**Addresses:** Pure BG has no equivalent of the hybrid hollow victory mechanic

In hybrid mode, hollow victory is a defined state: strategic conditions met but personal Belief arc contradicts them. In pure BG, there is currently no equivalent.

**Hollow Victory Score (BG-only rule):**

At game end, after standard victory conditions are checked, apply the following modifiers:

**Legitimacy Modifiers** (reduce a faction's final Deed Token count):
- Each Contempt token held from other factions: −0.5 Deeds (reputation damage)
- Institutional Mandate compromised ≥ 3 times: −1 Deed (institutional identity sacrificed)
- RS below 20 at game end: all factions' victories are hollow regardless of Deed count (the world is dying)
- TC above 80 at game end: Church victory is hollow unless they control Himmelenger + Valorsplatz (institutional conquest without spiritual legitimacy)
- Any faction eliminated (Stability 0) at game end: the eliminating faction's victory is hollow (-1 Deed) — conquest through destruction is not constitutional stability

**Legitimacy Bonuses** (increase Deed count):
- Institutional Mandate upheld ≥ 5 times: +1 Deed (institutional coherence is its own kind of victory)
- RS above 60 at game end: all factions' victories gain +1 Deed (the world is recovering)
- Zero Contempt tokens at game end: +0.5 Deed (political dignity)

**The cleanest possible victory:** A faction that wins with zero Contempt, Mandate upheld throughout, and RS above 60 wins with genuine legitimacy. This is the game's highest achievement, reflecting the Foundations' framing that rendered stability matters as much as political outcomes.

## PART SIX: IMPLEMENTATION SEQUENCE

### Tier 1 — Structural core (implement together; these form an integrated system)

1. **MP-31** (Concordia Card-Hand) — the action economy foundation
2. **MP-29** (Action Wheel) — Unique Power cooldowns within the card system
3. **MP-18 Church** (TC-as-currency) + **MP-18 Revolution** (Presence Markers) — the two factions whose redesign most clarifies what Valoria is: one faction consuming its own victory resource to act; one faction with no institutional orders, only community presence
4. **MP-34** (Institutional Belief) — brings the TTRPG's central mechanic into strategic play
5. **MP-12** (Thread Debt) + **MP-24** (Church Attention Pool) — the Thread system becomes active, watched, and costly
6. **MP-21** (Community Projects) — Revolution engine; the Quiet Year mechanics
7. **MP-22** (Contempt Tokens) + **MP-27** (Crown Policy) — political texture

These seven clusters form a coherent system. Implement them together and the BG transforms from a well-constructed euro into a game that expresses Valoria.

## PART SEVEN: THE CENTRAL ARGUMENT

After four waves of reference games and one synthesis pass, the core finding is this:

**Valoria's board game should feel like what happens when powerful institutions try to manage a world that is quietly dying.** The Thread substrate is degrading (RS). The Church is consuming governance legitimacy (TC). An outside power is watching for weakness (IP). No faction wins by expanding — they win by holding on long enough, coherently enough, while the three clocks count down.

The games that understand this: **The Quiet Year** (fragile community building against the countdown), **Pax Pamir** (everyone embedded in a system no one controls), **Pendragon** (the end of an era expressed through conviction and seasonal rhythm), **Concordia** (institutional capability as what you've built, not what you roll).

The games that don't: **War of the Ring** (cosmic moral binary), **Root** (ecological dominance), **Heroes of Might and Magic** (heroes as primary agents), **Malifaux** (fate to be cheated).

The TTRPG contributes: the Belief system, the cost structure, the hollow victory, the three-tier win condition (mechanical/narrative/both). The BG must express all three tiers to be Valoria rather than a game set in Valoria.

**The test for every mechanic:** Does this make a player feel the weight of governing, fighting, or surviving in a world whose fabric is slowly failing — where institutional decisions carry moral consequences that outlast the season? If yes: keep. If no: reconsider.

# DEBATE EXAMPLE: THE TREATY QUESTION

## Context

Parliament has been called to session. Duchess Baralta of Hafenmark accuses Duke Vaynard of Varfell of violating the Thornfeld Accord — a treaty governing the shared mining rights in the Eisengrund highlands. Baralta claims Varfell miners crossed into Hafenmark-administered shafts and extracted ore without licence. Vaynard claims the boundary was never formally ratified and therefore cannot be violated.

The PCs are Baralta (played by Sarah) and Vaynard (played by Marcus). The Game Master controls Parliament as audience.

---

## Game Master Setup (hidden from players)

**Question:** Did Varfell violate the Thornfeld Accord? (Past — Forensic primary genre.)

**Audience:** Parliament. Mixed faction presence, but Hafenmark and Crown delegates are the majority today. Baralta's procedural reputation gives her credibility here. Vaynard is respected but viewed as slippery.

**Starting Conviction Track:** Slightly biased toward Baralta (+1 of 10 toward her side). Parliament has heard rumours of the mining incursion and is predisposed to take treaty violations seriously — procedural institutions care about procedure.

**Hidden genre weights:**
- Past: ×1 (this is a treaty question — what happened matters)
- Present: ×0.5 (character arguments register but aren't decisive)
- Future: ×0.5 (policy implications are secondary to the factual question)

**Hidden orientation weight:** Revealing ×1. Obscuring ×0.75 (Parliament wants clarity, not fog. Doubt-sowing is less effective here than direct evidence.)

**Institutional constraints:** Formal Debate, 3 exchanges. Symmetric — both parties have equal standing before Parliament. The Speaker may rule arguments out of order but will not do so unless someone is egregiously off-topic. Division may be called after exchange 2.

**Stakes:**
- Baralta wins: Varfell Mandate −1. Treaty renegotiation on Hafenmark's terms. Vaynard personally humiliated in the chamber (Disposition shift with Parliament).
- Vaynard wins: Accusation dismissed. Baralta's Mandate −1 (seen as overreaching). The boundary question goes to a committee — effectively buried.

**Character stats (relevant):**

| | Baralta | Vaynard |
|---|---|---|
| Cognition | 4 | 5 |
| Memory | 3 | 4 |
| Focus | 5 | 3 |
| Attunement | 3 | 4 |
| Presence | 5 | 3 |
| Bonds | 4 | 2 |
| Composure | 11 | 9 |
| Concentration | 10 | 6 |
| Relevant History | "Constitutional Law of Hafenmark" (4 pts) | "Eisengrund Operations" (3 pts) |
| History bonus | +7D | +6D |

Baralta is a wall — high Focus, high Presence, high Composure. She lasts longer, hits harder, and is difficult to rattle. But her Cognition is lower and her Attunement is weaker. She argues through authority and endurance, not brilliance.

Vaynard is sharper — higher Cognition, higher Memory, higher Attunement. He reads the room better, constructs better arguments, and recalls more. But his Presence is low, his Focus is low, and his Composure is thin. He wins fast or he loses.

## EXCHANGE 1

### Step 1: Read

Both orators read the room.

**Baralta:** Attunement 3 + History bonus 7 = 10D, TN 7, Ob 1.
Sarah rolls: 3, 8, 2, 9, 5, 7, 1, 4, 6, 8 → 4 successes. Net 3 (Overwhelming).

**Game Master tells Sarah:** "Parliament is in a procedural mood. They've been dealing with Varfell's boundary ambiguities for two seasons and they're tired of it. They want this settled with evidence. The Crown delegates are watching to see if constitutional process holds. The room wants facts, not speeches."

Sarah now knows: Past is strong here. Revealing is strong. The audience wants documented evidence.

**Vaynard:** Attunement 4 + History bonus 6 = 10D, TN 7, Ob 1.
Marcus rolls: 7, 3, 9, 1, 8, 5, 2, 7, 6, 4 → 4 successes. Net 3 (Overwhelming).

**Game Master tells Marcus:** "The room is sceptical of you specifically. Two Crown delegates exchanged a glance when you entered. Hafenmark's people are stone-faced — they came prepared. You're reading impatience with ambiguity. This audience doesn't want to hear about technicalities. They want someone to be straightforwardly right."

Marcus now knows: the room is hostile to Obscuring. His instinct to argue the boundary was never ratified — a Past + Obscuring move — will land poorly. He needs to pivot.

### Step 2: Choose

**Initiative:** Baralta has Presence 5, Vaynard has Presence 3. Baralta speaks first.

**Baralta chooses:** Past + Revealing. She'll present the evidence directly. This matches the audience perfectly (Past ×1, Revealing ×1). Sarah knows this from her read.

**Vaynard must respond.** Marcus heard Sarah's read signal — the room wants facts. His planned defence (the boundary was never ratified — Past + Obscuring) is going to land poorly. He has a choice:

- Stay on Past + Obscuring (challenge the evidence) — but the room doesn't want fog.
- Pivot to Present + Revealing (attack Baralta's motives, establish his own standing) — half-weighted but might shift the dynamic.
- Pivot to Future + Revealing (propose a solution — "let's settle this with a joint survey") — half-weighted but positions him as reasonable.

Marcus chooses: **Future + Revealing.** He'll concede the past and propose a forward-looking resolution. He's betting that being the reasonable one in the room earns him more than fighting the evidence.

### Step 3: Argue

**Baralta (initiative holder) argues first.**

She stands, producing a leather folio. "Honoured members. On the fourteenth of Autumn, my surveyors documented seventeen Varfell-flagged ore carts emerging from Shaft Nine — a shaft that has been administered by Hafenmark under the Thornfeld Accord since the thirty-second year after the Accord's signing. The carts were logged by the Hafenmark border garrison. The garrison commander's testimony is in the folio before you. The shaft boundaries are marked on the Accord's original survey map, which I have also provided."

She's citing specific documents, dates, and testimony. Sarah declares Memory applies — she's recalling and presenting documented evidence.

**Baralta's roll:** Cognition 4 + History bonus 7 + Memory bonus (Game Master awards 2D for specific documented citations) = 13D, TN 7.
Sarah rolls: 9, 3, 7, 8, 1, 5, 7, 2, 8, 4, 6, 9, 7 → 7 successes.

**Vaynard responds.**

He remains seated for a moment — deliberately calm. Then stands. "Duchess Baralta's documentation is thorough. It always is. I do not dispute that Varfell miners were in Shaft Nine. I dispute that relitigating a thirteen-year-old survey map is the best use of Parliament's time. What I propose is simpler and more productive: a joint Hafenmark-Varfell survey commission, appointed by this Parliament, to resurvey the entire Eisengrund boundary with modern instruments and produce a definitive map that neither duchy can dispute. The question is not what happened in Shaft Nine. The question is whether this Parliament wants to solve the problem or merely assign blame."

No Memory bonus — he's not citing anything. Pure argument.

**Vaynard's roll:** Cognition 5 + History bonus 6 = 11D, TN 7.
Marcus rolls: 8, 2, 7, 5, 9, 3, 7, 1, 4, 8, 6 → 5 successes.

### Step 4: Deliver

**Exchange winner:** Baralta, 7 vs 5. Margin 2.

**Strain to Vaynard:** Margin 2, modified by Baralta's Presence. Presence 5, which is 1 above 3 (per the "every 2 above 3" rule, that's not enough for +1 — she needs Presence 5 for the bonus). Let's simplify: Presence modifier = Presence − 3, minimum 0. Baralta's Presence 5 → +2 strain. Total strain to Vaynard: 2 + 2 = 4.

**Focus defence:** Vaynard rolls Focus to resist. Focus 3, TN 7, Ob 1.
Marcus rolls: 4, 8, 3 → 1 success. Net 0 — bare pass. Strain reduced by 1. Vaynard takes 3 strain.

Vaynard's Composure is 9. He's at 3/9 after one exchange.

**Strain to Baralta:** Vaynard lost, so Baralta takes no strain from the exchange result. (Only the loser takes strain — this isn't combat's simultaneous damage.)

### Step 5: Game Master Scores (hidden)

Baralta's winning argument: Past + Revealing. Weight: Past ×1, Revealing ×1. Full margin counts.
Conviction tracker: +2 toward Baralta. (Starting position was +1 toward Baralta. Now +3.)

Vaynard's argument (Future + Revealing) did not win the exchange, so it doesn't move the tracker. But the Game Master notes internally that Vaynard's pivot to a constructive proposal may affect the audience's emotional state going forward — the weight might shift slightly toward Future for the next exchange if the room found his proposal reasonable.

**Game Master adjusts hidden weights for next exchange:**
- Past: ×1 → ×0.75 (Parliament heard the evidence. They may not need more of it.)
- Future: ×0.5 → ×0.75 (Vaynard's proposal planted a seed. Some delegates are nodding.)
- Present: ×0.5 (unchanged)

### Step 6: Concentration

Both orators' Concentration depletes by 1.
- Baralta: 10 → 9.
- Vaynard: 6 → 5.

**Initiative transfers to Baralta** (she won).

### Game Master Ledger (Exchange 1)

| Exchange | Winner | Margin | Genre + Orientation | Weight Applied | Tracker Δ | Tracker Position |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Baralta | 2 | Past + Revealing | ×1 / ×1 | +2 Baralta | +3 Baralta |

## EXCHANGE 2

### Step 1: Read

**Baralta:** Attunement 3 + History 7 = 10D, TN 7, Ob 1.
Sarah rolls: 2, 7, 5, 8, 3, 1, 9, 6, 4, 7 → 4 successes. Net 3 (Overwhelming).

**Game Master tells Sarah:** "You're winning and the room knows it. But something shifted — a few Crown delegates are looking at Vaynard with what might be sympathy. His survey proposal landed. The room still cares about the facts, but they're also starting to think about resolution. If you press the past too hard, you risk looking vindictive."

Sarah reads this correctly: the weight has shifted. Past is still good but diminishing. She might need to pivot herself.

**Vaynard:** Attunement 4 + History 6 = 10D, TN 7, Ob 1.
Marcus rolls: 9, 7, 3, 8, 2, 5, 7, 1, 6, 8 → 5 successes. Net 4 (Overwhelming).

**Game Master tells Marcus:** "The room is conflicted. Baralta's evidence was strong, but your proposal gave them an out — a way to resolve this without declaring a winner. The proceduralists want a winner. The pragmatists want a solution. You're reading a room that is splitting. The Crown delegates would accept a compromise. Hafenmark's people will not."

Marcus now has excellent information. The room is splitting. A Future + Revealing argument (the survey commission) will land with the Crown delegates but bounce off Hafenmark. He needs to either commit to the pivot and win the Crown, or find something that hits both groups.

### Step 2: Choose

**Baralta (initiative holder) chooses:** Sarah is smart enough to read the shift. She chooses **Present + Revealing.** She's going to make this about Vaynard's character — his pattern of boundary ambiguity, his refusal to honour agreements, his institutional untrustworthiness. She's moving off Past (which is diminishing) onto Present (which is half-weighted but may be more effective than beating the evidence drum again).

**Vaynard responds:** Marcus sees Baralta pivoting to character attack. He could defend (Present + Obscuring — "I'm not what she says") but his Presence is 3, his read told him the room doesn't want fog, and defensive arguments are landing weakly. Instead he chooses **Present + Revealing** — he'll counter-attack on character. "You want to talk about who we are? Let's talk about who you are."

Same genre, same orientation. This is direct competition — both trying to stabilize their own rendering in the room's eyes while destabilizing the other's.

### Step 3: Argue

**Baralta speaks first (initiative).**

"This is not the first time Varfell has found a boundary ambiguous only after Varfell interests have been served. In the thirty-eighth year, Vaynard's predecessor renegotiated fishing rights on the Kaltfluss by simply moving nets into our waters and waiting for us to object. The pattern is institutional. It is not a misunderstanding. It is a strategy. And it works precisely because good-faith actors like this Parliament extend the benefit of the doubt to those who have long since exhausted their claim to it."

No specific documents — this is characterization of institutional behaviour. No Memory bonus.

**Baralta's roll:** Cognition 4 + History bonus 7 = 11D, TN 7.
Sarah rolls: 7, 3, 9, 5, 8, 2, 7, 1, 6, 4, 8 → 5 successes.

**Vaynard responds.**

He steps forward. "The Duchess speaks of patterns. Let me offer one. In every dispute between Hafenmark and any other duchy in the last twenty years, Duchess Baralta has brought it to this floor rather than resolving it bilaterally. She does not negotiate. She litigates. She does this because she is very good at it, and because this Parliament — rightly — respects procedural rigour. But I would ask the honoured members to consider: when every disagreement becomes a parliamentary trial, who benefits? The duchy with the best lawyers. The Thornfeld boundary could have been resurveyed by mutual agreement at any point in the last thirteen years. The Duchess chose not to propose that. She waited for an incident, because an incident gives her what a bilateral negotiation would not: a villain."

This is sharp. Marcus is reframing Baralta's proceduralism as strategic litigation — using the institution's own values against her. No Memory bonus — pure characterization.

**Vaynard's roll:** Cognition 5 + History bonus 6 = 11D, TN 7.
Marcus rolls: 8, 7, 3, 9, 2, 5, 7, 8, 1, 6, 4 → 5 successes.

### Step 4: Deliver

**Tie.** Both at 5 successes. Initiative stays with Baralta (holder retains on draw).

No strain dealt to either side on a tie.

### Step 5: Game Master Scores (hidden)

Tie — no tracker movement. But the Game Master notes that Vaynard's counter-characterization landed with some delegates. The room is genuinely divided now.

**Game Master adjusts hidden weights for exchange 3:**
- Past: ×0.75 (unchanged — evidence phase is over)
- Present: ×0.5 → ×0.75 (the character clash engaged the room — they're invested in who's right about whom)
- Future: ×0.75 (unchanged)

### Step 6: Concentration

- Baralta: 9 → 8.
- Vaynard: 5 → 4.

**Initiative stays with Baralta** (tie, holder retains).

### Game Master Ledger (Exchange 2)

| 1 | Baralta | 2 | Past + Revealing | ×1 / ×1 | +2 Baralta | +3 Baralta |
| 2 | Tie | 0 | Present + Revealing (both) | — | 0 | +3 Baralta |

## EXCHANGE 3 (FINAL)

### Step 1: Read

**Baralta:** Attunement 3 + History 7 = 10D, TN 7, Ob 1.
Sarah rolls: 5, 8, 3, 7, 1, 4, 9, 6, 2, 7 → 4 successes. Net 3 (Overwhelming).

**Game Master tells Sarah:** "The room is yours to lose. The evidence landed. The character tie didn't hurt you — it showed you can take a hit and stay composed. But the Crown delegates are still thinking about Vaynard's survey proposal. If he closes on that, he might peel them off. You need to close this. The room will respond to a definitive statement of what should happen — they're ready for a verdict, not more argument."

Sarah reads: Future is now the strong genre. The room wants resolution. She should close with a demand, not more evidence.

**Vaynard:** Attunement 4 + History 6 = 10D, TN 7, Ob 1.
Marcus rolls: 7, 2, 8, 9, 5, 3, 7, 1, 8, 4 → 5 successes. Net 4 (Overwhelming).

**Game Master tells Marcus:** "You're behind but not dead. The Crown delegates are your lifeline — they liked the survey proposal. Hafenmark's people are stone. The proceduralists are going to vote with Baralta unless you give them something. Here's what you're reading: the room would accept a compromise. They don't want to crush Varfell. They want this resolved. If you offer something real — concession plus proposal — you might survive this."

Marcus reads: he can't win outright. But he might be able to limit the damage by proposing a compromise that the room prefers to Baralta's demand for full penalty.

### Step 2: Choose

**Baralta (initiative) chooses:** Future + Revealing. She'll demand a verdict and specific consequences. Close the debate with a commitment.

**Vaynard responds:** He heard the read. He can't win the Past argument. He can't win the Present argument. His only play is to offer a concession that makes Baralta's demand look excessive by comparison. He chooses **Future + Revealing** — but his content is a compromise, not a contest. He's betting the audience prefers his resolution to hers.

Same genre, same orientation again. Both are proposing a future. The audience will choose whose future they prefer.

### Step 3: Argue

**Baralta speaks first.**

She stands slowly, looking at the Speaker, not at Vaynard. "The evidence is before you. The mining incursion is documented. The boundary is documented. The pattern is documented. I ask this Parliament for three things. First: a formal finding that Varfell violated the Thornfeld Accord. Second: restitution of extracted ore at market value, assessed by a neutral Guild auditor. Third: confirmation that the original survey boundaries are binding and enforceable. These are not punitive measures. They are the Accord working as written. If treaties do not have consequences, they are not treaties. They are suggestions."

Clear, specific, tied to institutional logic. No Memory bonus — this is policy demand, not citation.

**Baralta's roll:** Cognition 4 + History bonus 7 = 11D, TN 7.
Sarah rolls: 8, 7, 3, 5, 9, 2, 7, 4, 8, 1, 6 → 5 successes.

**Vaynard responds.**

He pauses for several seconds. The room is quiet. Then he speaks, and his voice is different — quieter, less performative.

"The Duchess is right that Varfell miners were in Shaft Nine. She is right that the Thornfeld map shows it as Hafenmark-administered. I will not insult this chamber by contesting that further."

He pauses. Several delegates shift in their seats. Baralta's expression doesn't change.

"I will, however, note that the Thornfeld survey was conducted with instruments and methods that are now thirteen years obsolete, across terrain that has shifted due to three separate subsidence events. What was Shaft Nine in the thirty-second year may not be what is Shaft Nine today. I don't offer this as a defence. I offer it as a problem that will recur every season until it is solved. I accept the Duchess's first demand — a formal finding. I accept her second — restitution assessed by a neutral auditor. Her third demand I ask this Parliament to amend: rather than confirming boundaries that may no longer correspond to physical reality, commission a joint resurvey. Let the new map be the definitive map. Then there will be no ambiguity for anyone to exploit — including, should the geology shift again, Hafenmark."

This is a partial concession combined with a counter-proposal. He accepted two of three demands and proposed an alternative to the third. The last sentence was a subtle knife — implying that Hafenmark might exploit boundary ambiguity too if conditions changed.

**Vaynard's roll:** Cognition 5 + History bonus 6 = 11D, TN 7.
Marcus rolls: 9, 7, 8, 3, 5, 7, 2, 8, 4, 6, 1 → 5 successes.

### Step 4: Deliver

**Tie again.** 5 vs 5. Initiative stays with Baralta.

No strain dealt.

### Step 5: Game Master Scores (hidden)

Another tie — but the Game Master has a decision. Both argued Future + Revealing, both at 5 successes. No exchange winner, so technically no tracker movement.

But the Game Master considers: Vaynard's concession was a material shift. He accepted the finding and the restitution. His counter-proposal (resurvey) is reasonable. The room heard both proposals and is weighing them. The tracker doesn't move from the exchange, but the Game Master notes that the final audience state is "ready to vote, leaning Baralta but open to compromise."

### Step 6: Concentration

- Baralta: 8 → 7. Comfortable.
- Vaynard: 4 → 3. One exchange from Spent. If this had gone to 5 exchanges, he'd have been in real trouble.

### Outcome

Baralta won the exchange count but didn't capture the room outright. The Conviction Track at +3 of 10 is a moderate lean, not a decisive swing. The Game Master rules:

**Procedural victory for Baralta.** Parliament issues the formal finding of violation and orders restitution. But the third demand — confirming original boundaries — is amended. Parliament commissions the joint resurvey that Vaynard proposed. The Speaker notes that "the evidence of violation is clear, but the practical solution serves the interests of both duchies and of this Parliament."

Baralta gets 2 of 3 demands. Vaynard loses the finding and pays restitution but avoids permanent boundary lock-in — and positions himself as the reasonable party for the resurvey negotiation that follows.

**Mechanical consequences:**
- Varfell Mandate −1 (formal finding of violation)
- Vaynard's Disposition with Parliament: unchanged (the concession preserved his standing)
- Baralta's Disposition with Parliament: +1 step (she proved the case)
- No Domain Echo (the outcome was mixed — neither side's position fully actualized)
- Rendering Stability: no change (the debate was intense but not at the scale that disturbs thread configurations)

**Strain state:**
- Vaynard at 3 strain of 9 Composure. Not Rattled, but he felt it.
- Baralta at 0 strain. Untouched.

# VERISIMILITUDE ANALYSIS

## What Worked

**The read-choose-argue loop felt like real debate.** Both players received information that changed their strategy. Sarah learned the room wanted facts and adjusted. Marcus learned the room was hostile to obscuring and pivoted away from his planned defence. Neither player was locked into a predetermined sequence — they adapted to the room, which is what debaters actually do.

**Vaynard's concession in exchange 3 is the most realistic moment in the example.** A real debater who is losing doesn't keep attacking — they look for a landing zone. Marcus read the room, understood he couldn't win outright, and offered a concession designed to limit damage and position himself for the next fight. The system accommodated this naturally — it wasn't a special action, just a Future + Revealing argument whose content was a compromise.

**Baralta's exchange 2 pivot from Past to Present reflects real prosecutorial instinct.** She'd made the evidentiary case. Repeating it would look like she had nothing else. Attacking Vaynard's character was the natural escalation. The system didn't force this — Sarah read the diminishing returns on Past and chose to move.

**The Game Master's dynamic weighting felt organic.** The audience's priorities shifted because the arguments changed them. Exchange 1 was about facts. Exchange 2 introduced character. Exchange 3 was about resolution. This is how real proceedings evolve — the room's needs change as the debate progresses.

**Presence as strain modifier rather than argument quality is correct.** Vaynard is the better arguer (Cognition 5 vs 4) but Baralta hits harder when she wins because her delivery is more commanding (Presence 5 vs 3). This matches reality — a technically weaker argument delivered with authority can be more devastating than a brilliant argument delivered meekly.

**The Concentration disparity mattered without being triggered.** At 3 exchanges, Vaynard was fine. At 5, he'd have been Spent. This correctly models the difference between a sprint debater and an endurance debater. The system didn't need to invoke the Spent mechanic for the disparity to matter — both players were aware of it and it informed their strategies.

## What Didn't Work

**The Focus defence roll didn't add much.** It appeared once, reduced strain by 1, and was otherwise invisible. If Focus is supposed to be the parallel to defence in combat, it needs to do more. In combat, the defence roll happens every round and is the core decision (how many dice to allocate). In debate, Focus rolled once as an afterthought. This might be because the example is only 3 exchanges — in a Grand Debate, cumulative strain would make Focus more important. But it still feels underdeveloped compared to combat defence.

**Memory bonus appeared once.** Baralta cited specific documents in exchange 1 and got +2D. After that, Memory was irrelevant. This might be correct — you cite your evidence once, then argue from it. But it means Memory-heavy characters have a strong exchange 1 and then their primary advantage disappears. Consider: should Memory also apply defensively — catching contradictions in the opponent's argument, remembering something they said earlier that conflicts with what they're saying now?

**The tie mechanic is unsatisfying.** Two of three exchanges were ties. In combat, ties are resolved by contested Agility rolls. In debate, ties just... happen, and initiative stays. This deflates the drama. Consider: tie-breaking by Presence (who delivered better), or ties producing mutual strain (the audience is frustrated by indecision, both orators feel the pressure).

**Attunement's overwhelming success gave very clear information.** Both orators rolled 3+ net on every read. The Game Master gave detailed, actionable signals. What happens when the read is a partial success or a failure? The example doesn't test this. A failed read should produce bad or misleading information — "you think the room wants Past" when actually they're tired of it. This would create real tactical risk in the read step.

**Corroboration didn't appear.** Neither orator had an ally corroborating. The Bonds mechanic is untested in this example. A second example should feature a Grand Debate with seconds.

**The Conviction Track threshold was never seriously threatened.** Baralta reached +3 of a +5 threshold. With two ties, the tracker barely moved after exchange 1. This suggests the threshold might be too high for a 3-exchange Formal Debate, or that ties should still produce some tracker movement (the audience heard both arguments and formed partial opinions even when neither dominated).

**Genre + Orientation as a choice might be too abstract for some players.** Sarah and Marcus both made sophisticated rhetorical decisions because they're experienced roleplayers. A newer player might struggle with "choose Past/Present/Future and Revealing/Obscuring" as a mechanical choice. The system relies on the player understanding what their argument IS doing in thread-metaphysical terms. This might need an in-game aid — a reference card with examples: "Past + Revealing = prosecution, testimony, evidence. Past + Obscuring = challenging evidence, casting doubt."

## Structural Questions Surfaced

1. **Should ties produce partial tracker movement?** Perhaps +0.5 toward the initiative holder (the room slightly favours the person who set the terms, even if neither dominated).

2. **Should the respondent's argument move the tracker at all on a loss?** Vaynard's survey proposal in exchange 1 was compelling even though he lost the exchange. The current system gives zero tracker credit to a losing argument. Real audiences absorb both sides.

3. **Should Focus be a passive stat (reduces incoming strain automatically) rather than a roll?** This would simplify the exchange flow and make Focus reliably valuable without adding a die roll.

4. **Should Memory apply to defensive reads?** "You recall that in the Kaltfluss negotiation, the Duchess used this exact procedural argument and it was later shown to be..." — catching the opponent in a pattern. Memory as both citing precedent (offensive) and catching contradictions (defensive).

5. **How should the Game Master handle a debater who wants to pivot genre when the institutional context restricts it?** Vaynard arguing Future in a Past-genre tribunal — should the Speaker rule it out of order? What's the mechanical cost?

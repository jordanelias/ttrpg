# Event Cards — Flood, Drought, Siltation, Resource-Geography Shifts

*Key: `historical-concerns-catalogue-flood-drought-siltation-resource-geography`. The through-line: **slow hidden stats (SiltLevel, OreGrade, SalinityLevel, SubsidenceRisk, RisingWaterLevel) are long fuses** — an engineering/extraction method choice at one node silently changes another node's Crisis odds seasons later, and standing institutions (Water Board, Water Magistracy, Ever-Normal Granary, River Conservancy) are the only pre-emptive defusers.*

---

### HEV-WATER-01 — The Nilometer / flood-indexed taxation (Egypt) · Friction→Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** The Nilometer flood-gauge system underpinning Egyptian flood-indexed taxation. Citation: Wikipedia Nilometer; NatGeo.
- **Trigger:** `Gauge facility AND FloodIndex < relief_threshold AND Levy fixed/unadjusted AND Directive extraction pending`
- **Response branches:** Comply (honor the gauge, cut the rate: Order+1, Disposition+1); Bargain (split with the quota, `partial-relief-precedent`); Defy (PS−1, Order−1, Grudge, Π+2)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Defy seeds a "Famine Unrest" Crisis; repeated Comply seeds a `gauge-bound assessment` Precedent locking future Levy to the reading.
- **Introduces (Action):** Gauge-Indexed Levy — a new Levy method reading its rate off a tracked environmental stat, auto-scaling without a per-season roll.
- **Loop:** Installing it ties the Levy formula to FloodIndex so a bad flood auto-reduces extraction and denies the Friction its escalation; a fixed rate re-rolls the same Friction every low-flood season with rising Π/Grudge until a Defy chain forces the costlier emergency Bargain/Defy the gauge would have pre-empted.

### HEV-WATER-02 — Yellow River course change / Pan Jixun's engineering (Ming) · Crisis (Friction precursor)
- **Historical grounding:** Pan Jixun's Ming-dynasty Yellow River engineering and the course change into Hongze Lake. Citation: Wikipedia Yellow River / Pan Jixun / Hongze Lake; The China Project.
- **Trigger:** `SiltLevel > capacity AND Directive requires sustained canal throughput AND no River Conservancy office`
- **Response branches:** Comply-Scour (narrow channel, raise dikes: Treasury+1 but plants a hidden RisingWaterLevel tag on a *downstream* settlement incrementing every season); Bargain-Broad-Relief (widen, sacrifice capacity, reduce downstream risk); Defy (canal silts shut, regional collapse)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Scour seeds a delayed "Rising Lake Threatens [Settlement]" Crisis firing only once the downstream tag crosses threshold — potentially decades later, independent of that settlement's choices.
- **Introduces (Action):** River Conservancy Directorship — a Fortify Scour-vs-Broad-Relief fork plus a province-tier standing office like a Ministry seat.
- **Loop:** Scour raises the capital-adjacent settlement's Prosperity/Defense but writes a slow-incrementing tag onto a different settlement that nothing there can see or defuse — a Fortify choice at one node silently changes Crisis odds at another many seasons later (the multi-decade fuse).

### HEV-WATER-03 — Zwin siltation / fall of Bruges, rise of Antwerp · Friction→Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** The silting of the Zwin channel, decline of Bruges as a trade port, and the corresponding rise of Antwerp. Citation: Wikipedia Zwin/Bruges; Cambridge Urban History.
- **Trigger:** `harbor SiltLevel rising N seasons with no Dredge AND Guild Standing high enough to demand protective tariffs`
- **Response branches:** Comply-Guild-tariff (Guild+2, Merchant−2, foreign merchants shift to a rival); Bargain (dredge + hold tariffs flat: Treasury−1, SiltLevel reset, trade retained); Defy the Guild (Grudge but merchants retained)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Comply seeds a "Merchant Exodus" Crisis — permanent Prosperity/trade-Reputation transfer to a named rival (Antwerp's rise).
- **Introduces (Action):** Dredge Harbor — a Develop funding/method resetting SiltLevel; pairs with Treat holding tariffs down.
- **Loop:** Skipping Dredge lets SiltLevel accumulate silently until it combines with Guild Standing to trigger the Friction, and Comply converts a still-reversible decline into a permanent Reputation transfer; dredging (or Treat) keeps SiltLevel below the trigger and denies the rival its Opportunity gain.

### HEV-WATER-04 — Potosí silver depletion / mita / amalgamation · Opportunity→Friction/Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Potosí silver mine ore-grade depletion, the coercive mita labor draft, and mercury amalgamation processing. Citation: PMC mercury/Andean silver; Moore JPE (2010); Encyclopedia.com Potosí.
- **Trigger:** `mine OreGrade below the level to meet a standing silver-quota AND Treasury demand unchanged`
- **Response branches:** Comply-Mita (forced draft: Output held, Order−2, Grudge(indigenous) each season); Bargain (wage/partial mix, Grudge slowed); Defy (output crashes, Directive penalty)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Sustained Mita seeds an "Uprising" Crisis once Grudge crosses threshold + a permanent PS-decline tag (the mita's demographic toll).
- **Introduces (Action):** Mita Conscription (coercive Levy method) + Amalgamation (Sponsor/Develop process upgrade raising yield-per-OreGrade — the patio process).
- **Loop:** Mita buys time against depletion by substituting coerced labor for ore quality but each season adds Grudge force-drawing the Uprising and permanent PS damage; Amalgamation raises yield directly, letting Treasury cost substitute for Grudge and avoiding the coercive path entirely.

### HEV-WATER-05 — St. Elizabeth's Flood of 1421 (Netherlands) · Crisis (Friction precursor: dike funds diverted to a war Directive)
- **Historical grounding:** The 1421 St. Elizabeth's flood in the Netherlands, dike-fund diversion, and Dutch water boards (note: boards predate the flood). Citation: Wikipedia St. Elizabeth's flood / Water board.
- **Trigger:** `flood-defense Maintenance below threshold (diverted to a war levy or weakened by peat extraction) AND a severe-storm roll`
- **Response branches:** Comply (emergency Fortify at high cost, partial); Bargain (evacuate/cede the lowest terrain: permanent terrain-loss tag, limited PS loss); Defy (catastrophic PS collapse, mass Grudge, a permanent Biesbosch-equivalent geography tag, `dikes neglected` Precedent)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Catastrophic resolution seeds cross-settlement Petition pressure to charter a standing self-funded Fortify institution — a win condition, not an automatic grant.
- **Introduces (Action):** Water Board — a standing flood-defense institution funded by its own dedicated levy, ring-fenced from Directive diversion.
- **Loop:** Complying with a war Directive by diverting maintenance lowers the flood-defense stat (higher trigger+severity); a Water Board decouples flood funding from Directive competition so war-funding choices *cannot* starve flood defense — cutting the Crisis's odds independent of the Directive.

### HEV-WATER-06 — Bedford Level Corporation / draining the Fens · Opportunity + Friction/Crisis chain
- **Historical grounding:** The Bedford Level Corporation and Vermuyden's draining of the English Fens. Citation: Wikipedia Bedford Level / Vermuyden; Britannica.
- **Trigger:** `undeveloped wetland tag AND a private-capital Sponsor offers to fund reclamation`
- **Response branches:** Comply/charter (Prosperity+ new taxable land, Grudge(commoners), `enclosure permitted` Precedent, Concession to the corporation); Bargain (with compensation, less land, no Grudge); Defy (opportunity expires)
- **Follow-on (FO):** A granted charter seeds two delayed chains — a near-term "Fen Riot" Friction from the Grudge, and a long-fuse "Peat Subsidence Flood" Crisis once a hidden SubsidenceRisk stat crosses threshold.
- **Introduces (Action):** Charter Reclamation Corporation — a fourth Develop funding method: private capital reclaims marginal terrain for a standing Concession + reduced governor control.
- **Loop:** The chartered method converts terrain to Prosperity quickly with someone else's capital, but the open-channel drainage engineering plants a SubsidenceRisk stat that eventually force-draws a flood Crisis on the *same land the Opportunity granted* — today's method choice constrains which cards the settlement draws for its lifetime.

### HEV-WATER-07 — Chongzhen Drought / fall of the Ming · Crisis (pre-emptible by a standing institution)
- **Historical grounding:** The Chongzhen-era drought contributing to the fall of the Ming dynasty. Citation: Brook, *The Price of Collapse* (2023); MDPI (2023); Nature Comm E&E (2024).
- **Trigger:** `DroughtIndex > severe AND Granary StockLevel checked against a famine floor`
- **Response branches:** Comply (open granaries: Order+1, PS+1, StockLevel drawn); Bargain (rationed, mixed); Defy/empty (Order−3, Π+3, Grudge)
- **Follow-on (FO):** An unmet drought seeds a rebel/warlord Ambition gaining Standing → a rival faction's rank advance (Li Zicheng).
- **Introduces (Action):** Ever-Normal Granary (changping-cang) — a standing facility accumulating StockLevel in good seasons, auto-drawn when drought draws.
- **Loop:** Sponsoring each good season raises StockLevel that a drought consumes automatically, blunting Order/PS loss and blocking the rebel-Ambition follow_on; skipping it leaves StockLevel zero so the identical roll escalates to famine → Order collapse → a rival's Standing gain — same roll, radically different consequence gated by a prior Sponsor choice.

### HEV-WATER-08 — Erzgebirge Bergordnungen / mining boom-bust · Opportunity→Friction→Ambition
- **Historical grounding:** The Erzgebirge mining-boom regulatory codes (Bergordnungen), Freiberg, and the Berggeschrey boom-bust cycle. Citation: Wikipedia Bergordnung / Freiberg / Berggeschrey; Montanregion documentation.
- **Trigger:** `mine OreGrade below the baseline assumed at charter issuance AND a fixed regalian mining-charter tag still active (no Tribunal renegotiation)`
- **Response branches:** Comply (hold fixed Zehnt: Treasury shortfall, Miners−2); Bargain (Tribunal review renegotiates down); Defy (enforce harshly: Grudge, population-transfer follow_on)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Unresolved Friction seeds an Opportunity "New Rush" at a rival settlement (migrating miners/capital) — a settlement-to-settlement resource-geography transfer.
- **Introduces (Action):** Regalian Mining Charter with Tribunal renegotiation — a depleting-resource-keyed Levy cross-referencing OreGrade.
- **Loop:** Locking the rate at peak and never renegotiating means it doesn't scale down as OreGrade depletes, so Grudge/Friction odds rise as the fixed-terms/real-yield gap widens; running the Tribunal review before threshold keeps the rate credible and denies the rival its departing-miner seed.

### HEV-WATER-09 — Venice's river diversions / Magistrato alle Acque · Friction (standing, recurring)
- **Historical grounding:** Venice's Magistrato alle Acque and its management of mainland river diversions into the lagoon. Citation: Wikipedia Magistrato alle acque; ResearchGate lagoon diversions.
- **Trigger:** `shared province-tier Lagoon/Harbor SiltLevel rising from mainland discharge AND a settlement-level Develop request pending with no province Water Magistracy`
- **Response branches:** Comply-veto (subordinate the request: lagoon protected, local Disposition−1, `province water authority binds` Precedent); Bargain (supervised partial diversion); Defy (settlement acts unilaterally: SiltLevel rises, Precedent-breach)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Repeated Defy accumulates SiltLevel toward a province-wide "Harbor Choked" Crisis affecting every coastal settlement's trade.
- **Introduces (Action):** Water Magistracy — a province-tier veto institution extending Ministry/Consulta, overriding a settlement's Develop when its externalities threaten a shared downstream resource.
- **Loop:** The Magistracy inserts a check on shared SiltLevel before any Develop request against the lagoon is approved, preventing the province Crisis from accumulating; without it each Develop is locally rational but collectively silts the shared resource, forcing a Crisis costing every settlement more than the vetoed actions would have.

### HEV-WATER-10 — Canal siltation / salinization in southern Mesopotamia · Friction→Crisis→Ambition
- **Historical grounding:** Progressive soil salinization from consecutive high-intensity irrigation in southern Mesopotamia (Sumer), and the shift of dominance toward Akkad. Citation: Wikipedia (Sumer salt); Springer, *Soil Salinity Historical Perspectives*.
- **Trigger:** `farmland SalinityLevel rising over consecutive high-intensity-irrigation seasons with no Corvée-desilting Levy, crossing crop-viability thresholds`
- **Response branches:** Comply-Corvée (labor draft: Order−1, salinity growth slowed); Bargain (partial corvée + crop substitution wheat→barley, lower ceiling); Defy (SalinityLevel rises uncapped)
- **Follow-on (FO):** Sustained ignoring seeds a "Land Exhausted" chain culminating in Prosperity/PS collapse + a Standing/Recognition transfer to a rival (Sumer→Akkad).
- **Introduces (Action):** Corvée Desilting Levy (recurring labor-tax suppressing SalinityLevel) + Crop Substitution (Develop-tier adaptive swap trading ceiling for lower Crisis odds).
- **Loop:** Running the Corvée each season keeps SalinityLevel below crop-loss thresholds at a recurring Order cost; skipping lets it compound irreversibly (wheat permanently lost by ~2000 BC), progressively lowering the ceiling until Crop Substitution becomes forced not chosen, and past the final threshold fires the depopulation chain transferring Standing to a rival.

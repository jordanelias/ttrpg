# Accounting Cut Scene Set — Narrative Snippet

**Subject:** Illustrative cut scene sequence shown during a year-end Accounting in Valoria.
**Setting:** Crown of Solmund, Third Year, in the time of Captain Almud. Continuity drawn from sim scenarios — Almud succeeded the previous Crown leader; the Spymaster's covert action was uncovered; eastern engagements went poorly.
**Voice:** Tier 3 omniscient retrospective per `2026-04-30_three_tier_articulation_reframe.md`. Chronicler speaks past-tense with knowledge unavailable to participants.
**Format:** Six cut scenes, ~3-5 minutes total. Visual notes in brackets; voiceover in plain.

---

## I. Opening — "The Third Year"

*[Music: solemn period strings, low. Visual: aerial parchment map of the Valorian peninsula, gold dimming to dusk. Territories illuminate as named.]*

**CHRONICLER (V.O.):** The third year of Almud's Crown began as winters do in Valoria — late, and hard. The reformed Mission, declared the previous winter following the eastern campaign's failure, would be tested before the snows had fully melted.

*[Visual: snowmelt; a rider arrives at the Crown's seat, breath fogging.]*

**CHRONICLER:** What follows was unknown to most who lived through it. Some pieces the Captain herself would not learn until summer. The chronicler tells what the chronicler now knows.

*[Hold on the seat of the Crown. Cut to black.]*

---

## II. The Captain's Winter

*[Visual: Almud receiving reports in a cold council chamber. Maps. Damp candles.]*

**CHRONICLER (V.O.):** Almud took the spring engagements seriously, as her predecessors had not. The eastern border was contested, the local lords distrustful, the supply trains thin from the previous year's losses. She rode the line in person — three weeks in the saddle, four engagements joined, two won, two indecisive.

*[Brief montage: Almud at council; riding through forest; in the field with captains.]*

**CHRONICLER:** The court, in her absence, did what courts do when their captains ride east. The Spymaster's quiet correspondences continued. The Restoration's voices, marginal still, grew slightly louder. The Cathedral kept its counsel and its silences.

*[Cut: court chamber, low candlelight, the Spymaster at his desk, sealing a letter.]*

**CHRONICLER:** What none of them knew — what perhaps only the chronicler now knows, and the two who were there — is that Almud, three weeks gone and weary, formed a bond at the eastern garrison that would matter more than any dispatch she sent home.

*[Cut: night exterior. Almud and Captain Vossler share a quiet moment at a fire. They drink from the same cup. Neither speaks for a long while.]*

**CHRONICLER:** They drank from the same cup. They said little. The Knot held.

*[A faint visual cue — a thread of warm light between them, brief, almost imperceptible.]*

*[Fade.]*

---

## III. The Spymaster's Concealment

*[Visual: shift to the Spymaster's private chambers. Parchments stacked, locked drawers, a single candle.]*

**CHRONICLER (V.O.):** By summer the matter could no longer be hidden. The Spymaster's correspondences — three years and more — had been with the Lowenritter, in the most direct form: paid, regular, intelligence carried.

*[Visual: a private letter held to candlelight, ash-edged, then thrown into the fire.]*

**CHRONICLER:** The exposure came not from inquiry but from a clerk's mistake. A letter mis-directed. The wrong eyes.

*[Cut: a young clerk in a Crown corridor, realizing what they have read, paper trembling. Running.]*

**CHRONICLER:** Almud, newly returned, called the council into session before the rumor could shape itself. She did not deny what could not be denied. She did not deflect. The Spymaster was confined to his quarters within the hour. By dusk, he had written his statement.

*[Council chamber: Almud at the head, the Spymaster opposite, head bowed, writing. The chamber is silent.]*

**CHRONICLER:** This much the realm saw. What it did not see — what the Spymaster wrote in the statement, and what the Captain read in private — was that the betrayal had begun three years earlier, under conditions the previous Crown had created. The Spymaster was perhaps not a traitor by his own lights. He was a man whose loyalties had been left ungoverned, and had grown elsewhere.

*[Almud reads the statement alone, slowly, by candlelight. Her face does not move.]*

**CHRONICLER:** She did not share this part. Not with the council. Not with the Cathedral's emissary. Not with the Restoration's whispered inquiry. The realm received the betrayal as betrayal. The Captain carried the more difficult truth.

*[The statement is folded carefully. Locked into a drawer. Almud's hand rests on the wood.]*

**CHRONICLER:** The Crown's Legitimacy held. Its Popular Support did not.

*[Fade. Distant bells.]*

---

## IV. The Eastern Reverses

*[Visual: muddy fields. Banners dark with rain. The aftermath of a small battle — not a great one. The kind that loses by a mile, not a chasm.]*

**CHRONICLER (V.O.):** While the court adjusted to the Spymaster's absence, the eastern engagements continued. Two more were lost. Of the captains Almud had drilled in spring, three took their first Conviction Scars — the kind that comes from watching what you cannot prevent.

*[Brief portrait sequence — three captains, each with a subtle visual marker.]*

**CHRONICLER:** Captain Vossler took his Scar witnessing the death of a child in the village of Hochfeld. He had ordered a withdrawal. The order was correct. The child died anyway. Such is how Wardens learn.

*[Hold on Vossler's face — not anguished, harder than that, *taking note*.]*

**CHRONICLER:** Captain Reinholt took his at the failed sortie at the river-bend. He had argued against the engagement. He had been overruled. He led it well. He lost it anyway.

*[Reinholt at the riverside, surveying the cost.]*

**CHRONICLER:** The third — Captain Vossler again, who had bonded with Almud at the garrison — took a second Scar before the season ended. He had refused an order. He believed it would have made the matter worse. He did not know whether he had been right.

*[Vossler standing alone in the field, the order's bearer riding away, no certainty in any face.]*

**CHRONICLER:** The bond held. Not all bonds do. This one did.

*[Cut: three captains at a small fire, no words between them, present together. Knotted.]*

*[Fade.]*

---

## V. The Other Powers

*[Visual: shift to other factions' interiors. Lowenritter chapter house, candlelit. Cathedral cloister, dawn. Restoration meeting in a back room above a print-shop.]*

**CHRONICLER (V.O.):** The Crown's distress was not the year's only matter, though it was its loudest. The Lowenritter, having received what the Spymaster sent them, gained more from the exposure than they lost — for now they could pretend not to have known. They moved their pieces forward by a hand's width. They claimed nothing publicly.

*[Lowenritter Master in deliberation with two of his commanders. They speak in low tones. They smile.]*

**CHRONICLER:** The Cathedral, watching the Crown's Legitimacy hold while its Popular Support eroded, neither pressed nor withdrew. They waited. Cardinal Reichard wrote three letters to Rome, none of which described the situation, all of which prepared the ground.

*[Cardinal Reichard at his writing desk. Three sealed letters. He turns one over in his hand.]*

**CHRONICLER:** The Restoration, for the first time since their founding pamphlet eleven years prior, found their voice carried by people who had not invited them in. Three border villages. Two market towns. A handful in the capital itself. Their numbers remained small. The people listening were larger.

*[A Restoration speaker at a market square, perhaps thirty listeners, attentive. Later: a cluster of pamphlets pinned to a tavern wall in the capital.]*

**CHRONICLER:** By harvest, the peninsula's Strain had risen. Not because the year had been catastrophic — it had not. But because the equilibrium of trust that had held the realm through a decade of turbulence had thinned by some measurable amount.

*[A wide, abstract shot of the Solmund peninsula at dusk. The light is wrong, somehow, in a way that takes a moment to read.]*

**CHRONICLER:** What Strain takes, is not a thing one can name.

*[Fade.]*

---

## VI. What the Winter Carried Forward

*[Visual: return to the Crown's seat. Winter approaching. Almud at her desk, a letter half-written, candle low.]*

**CHRONICLER (V.O.):** Almud's third year ended as it began: in winter, late and hard. She held the realm. The Crown's Legitimacy stood at five — diminished, but standing. Popular Support had bled to three. The Mandate of the Crown, by the realm's reckoning, was four.

*[A parchment ledger fills the screen briefly — the year's figures rendered in period chart marks. Cascade Fidelity 0.78. Strain rising. Then it folds away.]*

**CHRONICLER:** She had three Knots. She had one Belief uncrossed and one bruised. She had three Inspirations still carried — though one had grown quiet, and another had begun to ask itself questions she did not yet have words for.

*[A brief still: Almud's character sheet, abstracted as an illuminated manuscript page. The reader's eye lingers on the Inspirations, two of which are bright, one of which is not.]*

**CHRONICLER:** What the new year held, none in the realm knew. The chronicler will not tell.

*[Long beat. Music swells gently — not triumph; not lament. Something between.]*

**CHRONICLER:** This was the Third Year of the Crown of Solmund, in the time of Almud.

*[Title card, illuminated, period-correct lettering. Held for three beats.]*

*[Fade to black. Return to gameplay UI.]*

## A Note on Voice

The chronicler should not editorialize. *Cronache* mostly do not. The voice is measured, slightly removed, willing to attribute motive when the substrate's data supports it ("Cardinal Reichard wrote three letters... none of which described the situation, all of which prepared the ground") but unwilling to claim moral judgment ("She held the realm" — neutral; not "She nobly held" or "She barely held").

Where the substrate gives a fact, the chronicler states it. Where the substrate gives a chain of causes, the chronicler reads the chain. Where the substrate is silent, the chronicler is silent. The line *"He did not know whether he had been right"* is not the chronicler's editorial — it is the substrate's representation of Vossler's Conviction-axis state at that moment, told as the Conviction Track records it.

The omniscience is structural, not authorial. The chronicler sees what the substrate sees. That happens to be more than the participants saw — and that gap, in the player's experience, is exactly where retrospective coherence lives.

## §3 The Asymmetric Balance Hypothesis

### §3.1 Why symmetric ±5pp may be the wrong target

Canonical starting state per `peninsular_strain_v30 §2.1`:
- Crown: **6/15 territories** (40% map)
- Hafenmark: 4/15 (27%)
- Church: **1/15** (7%)
- Varfell: 4/15 (27%)

Crown is canonically over-advantaged. Church is canonically under-advantaged. The asymmetry is **designed in** — it reflects the canonical political situation where Valorsmark is the de facto sovereign and competitors are challengers.

**Targeting symmetric 25% balance fights the canonical design.** The right balance target reflects faction roles:

| Faction | Canonical role | Win-share target |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| Crown | Sovereign incumbent | **35–45%** |
| Church | Institutional challenger | **20–30%** |
| Hafenmark | Political-mercantile challenger | **15–25%** |
| Varfell | Military outsider | **10–20%** |

This asymmetric band reflects canonical positions: Crown is the favored player; others fight to dethrone or replace.

### §3.2 v11 + 50s against the asymmetric target

| Faction | Target band | v11 + 50s | Status |
|---------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Crown | 35–45% | 55.8% | ~10pp over ceiling |
| Church | 20–30% | 22.0% | ✓ in band |
| Hafenmark | 15–25% | 11.8% | ~3pp under floor |
| Varfell | 10–20% | 10.4% | ✓ in band |

**Half the factions in their canonical-role band.** Crown is the remaining over-correction; Hafenmark just barely under. Within striking distance — likely another 1–2 iterations close this.

### §3.3 What this reveals about the game

The 12+ iterations across Parts 6–13 demonstrate:

1. **Church compounding** was the canon-v5 dominant — **resolved** via Q-21 throttle
2. **Crown hegemony via Treaty** is the new dominant — **partially resolved** via Treaty Expiration
3. **Hafenmark scaling** requires more than L-gain — needs **territorial vector** (Dynastic Proclamation rebalance? Charter+Settlement chain?)
4. **Varfell acquisition** improved via Vaynard's Settlement — **partially closed**
5. **Direct sovereignty** rate rose from 2.8% (canon) → 12.6% (v11+50s). Game ends decisively.

### §4.3 New canonical rule: Treaty Expiration

> **Crown Treaty Expiration (PP-NEW-TREATY-EXPIRATION):** At each arc-boundary
> Accounting, every Crown Treaty has a 40% probability of lapsing (subject
> withdraws). Lapsed Treaty: cleared from both factions' treaty registers.
> Crown may attempt Treaty Renewal (Senator Outward action, same Ob as
> original Crown Treaty) to re-bind a lapsed faction.

**Historical anchor:** Roman foedera renewed periodically; medieval treaties required oath renewal at the coronation of either party's new sovereign; Hanseatic League's Tagsatzung required regular alliance reconfirmation.

### §4.4 Open structural work — not closed by RC-v1

Hafenmark's territorial vector remains weak. Dynastic Proclamation (canonical
§5.3, 1×/season + adjacency + Appease) fires too rarely. Possible fixes:

1. **DP cooldown reduction**: 1×/season → 1×/2 season but eliminate adjacency requirement
2. **DP cascade**: Overwhelming DP grants free DP attempt next season (per the +1 Standing momentum effect already in canon §5.3)
3. **Charter triggers cascade**: Charter of Liberties Success → next DP gets +1 to pool

Recommended: prototype option 2 (cascade) in v12 simulator. Reasoning: canonical
§5.3 already grants +1 Standing on Overwhelming, suggesting a momentum chain
was intended. Mechanical extension fits canonical pattern.

### §4.5 Recommendation summary

**Adopt RC-v1 package with the explicit caveat that asymmetric (not symmetric)
balance is the canonical target.** Crown is the sovereign incumbent;
competitors are challengers. The 35–45% / 20–30% / 15–25% / 10–20% band
reflects canonical faction roles, not a flaw to correct.

If symmetric balance IS required: Crown's territorial advantage must be reduced
canonically (re-draw starting territory split). This is a Jordan-level decision
about what kind of game Valoria is.

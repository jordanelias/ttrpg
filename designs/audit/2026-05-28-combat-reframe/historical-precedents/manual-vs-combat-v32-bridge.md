# The Historical Combat Manual, Read Against Combat v32

A grounded cross-reference of *Historical Combat Manuals (Revised Edition)* against Valoria's **combat_v32** state-graph reframe, plus an answer to "how many distinct martial traditions apply per v32 weapon type."

**Grounding and scope discipline.** Every v32 claim below cites a fetched source. v32 is the **in-flight reframe** (`designs/proposals/combat_v32_proposal.md` + `designs/audit/2026-05-28-combat-reframe/` + the `tests/sim/v32-combat-balance/` build); canonical (ratified) combat is still `combat_v30`. The weapon classes and handling are explicitly **Class B draft sim-seeds, not canon** (`m3_weapon_class_layer.py` header; `combat_v32_proposal §8`). Per the project-owner contract this document **makes no design decisions and proposes no changes** — it reads the manual against v32 and surfaces where they align, strain, or leave gaps, leaving every call to Jordan. A NERS verdict on v32 already exists (`ners_verdict_combat_v32.md`); this complements it from the manual's comparative angle, it does not replace it.

## Part A — What v32 is (cited)

v32 reframes combat as a **probability-weighted state graph** with two tiers (`reframe_blueprint §1`): a **Setup tier** of deterministic player choices at **Measure / out of contact** (Stance, Position, Approach, Reading, Commit — §1.1), then a **Bout tier**, the 6–10s engagement, traversing engagement states `Out-of-contact → Closing → In-bind → Breaking → [Disengaged | Wounded | Felled]` (§1.2). Transition probabilities are set by opposed Pool rolls weighted by stats, equipment, choices, and dice. Strategic depth is **front-loaded into setup**; the bout resolves it with low per-moment load (§2). The mechanisms most relevant here:

- **Weapon layer** (`combat_v32_proposal §8`, `m3_weapon_class_layer.py`): canonical 3-axis TN substrate from combat_v30 §5 (reach × weight × type), plus v32's draft **handling** (ease-of-use curve) and **Weapon Speed**.
- **Stance Counter** (§7.1): an **authored hard-counter table**, kept authored because it "resists derivation" (Hodge ~85% cyclic), **state-gated to the Closing-opening** only.
- **Reaction** (§7.2): a 2-parameter formula (baseline + depth-slope) — reaction effectiveness scales against the opponent's **commit-depth**; reactions are Hand-led / Body-led / Yielding / Voiding / Pressing, **gated by field of view**.
- **Facing → FoV** (`reframe_blueprint §4`, `combat_v32_proposal §11.2`): facing sets a perception cone; FoV gates Reading and which Reactions are available; the flanking advantage is **emergent** (lost-read + lost-reaction compounding), not an authored modifier. *(Sim Module 7, pending/stub-first.)*
- **Loadout + named sets** (§6): a large **trained pool** vs a small **equipped loadout** (1 Stance, 1 Footwork, weapon-constrained **Grip**, emphases, slotted techniques), with **named sets** (Thrust Duelist, Bind Fighter, Counter-time, Burst, Continuous-flow) granting set bonuses.

## Part B — The manual, read against v32, axis by axis

For each axis: **v32's mechanism** (cited) · **the manual's finding** · **the verdict** (alignment / tension / gap), with any Jordan-decision-relevant note flagged as an *observation*, not a prescription.

### Measure (manual Axis 1 / Throughline T3)
- **v32:** the bout is literally discrete engagement states — Measure → Out-of-contact → Closing → In-bind → Breaking (`reframe_blueprint §1.2`). Reach is a weapon-TN modifier (`m3`: `REACH_SHORT_MOD −1`, `REACH_LONG_MOD 0`).
- **Manual:** the single strongest *convergent-independent* result — discrete named measure-zones recur across non-contacting traditions (Italian *misura*: larga/stretta/fuori; Japanese *maai*: tōma/uchima/chikama; Filipino *largo/medio/corto*) (§14.2, §15.2).
- **Verdict — alignment, strongest.** v32's state-graph independently lands on the primitive the manual flags as near-universal. v32 also cleanly *separates* distance (weapon reach mod) from contact (the In-bind state) — which matches the manual's distinction between *measure* (distance) and *the bind* (the tactile channel; you can be at *misura larga* with no bind). **Observation:** the manual's distance bands are finer (3 zones on distance alone) than v32's binary out-of-contact/closing, which folds distance gradation into the Approach roll rather than naming bands; whether that granularity matters is a play question, not a defect.

### Speed / useability (manual Axis 2)
- **v32:** two separate things — **Weapon Speed** (a per-class scalar: single-short 3 … long-heavy-blunt −0.5; `m3 §8.4`) and **handling** (Forgiving/Standard/Demanding skill-curve, crossover at proficiency 4; `m3 §8.2`).
- **Manual:** speed decomposes into (a) *which body part initiates* — Silver's *true times*, where leading with a fast part (hand) beats leading with a slow part (foot), and over-committing (foot-first) exposes you to the counter; and (b) *weapon morphology / useability as constraint-accommodation* (the spear "the practical training weapon for soldiers raised quickly"; the rapier precise and demanding) (§1.4.3, §2.12, §3.11).
- **Verdict — alignment on useability; the biomechanical piece lives elsewhere in v32.** v32's **handling** *is* the manual's useability-as-ease-of-use axis, and its draft assignments **match the historical record strikingly**: spear/staff = Forgiving (Chinese: learnable fast for mass troops), rapier/paired-short = Demanding (narrow-window precision weapons), longsword = Standard. **This bears directly on the NERS verdict's open N5** ("is handling necessary, or over-engineering?"): the manual supplies cross-cultural *evidence* that ease-of-use is a real, attested dimension — i.e. evidence toward keeping handling. *(Stated as evidence for Jordan's N5 call, not a verdict on it.)* Silver's *biomechanical* insight (fast-part-first; over-commit→exposed) is **not** in Weapon Speed — but it **is** present as v32's **commit-depth × reaction-slope** (`§7.2`): a deeper commit weights the opening transition *and* raises the opponent's reaction effectiveness. So v32 does capture Silver's true-times principle; it simply locates it in the bout/reaction layer rather than the weapon scalar.

### Stance / guard (manual Axis 3 / Throughline T5)
- **v32:** the **Stance Counter** is an authored hard-counter table, kept authored precisely because it *resists* reduction (Hodge ~85% cyclic), state-gated to the Closing-opening (`§7.1`). Stance is an equipped loadout slot (`§6.1`).
- **Manual:** stance is **relational, not static** — a node in an attack-defeat graph (German: each Meisterhau both attacks and *defeats one specific guard*; Spanish *atajo* / I.33 *obsessio* = taking the opponent's line), with a *convergent reduction* to ~4–5 guards (Agrippa's 4, Vier Leger, 5 kamae) and a transcendence caution ("stances and no stances") (§1.1.2, §2.8, §14.8).
- **Verdict — strong alignment, including on the irreducibility.** The manual independently confirms that stance-as-cyclic-counter is a genuine, cross-cultural, *irreducible* design feature — which is exactly why v32 was right to keep the Stance Counter authored rather than derive it (the diagnostic's Lesson-1-inverse). **On the NERS verdict's open N1** (the Stance Counter *vanishes* at the Closing→In-bind transition, an unsmoothed ~1.2–2.2 Ob swing): the manual offers a lens — in the German tradition a stance-edge does not simply disappear at contact; it *transforms* into a bind relationship (*Stark/Schwach* leverage, *Winden*), the guard flowing into the bind-feel. That suggests the discontinuity is conceptually a *transformation*, not a deletion. *(Surfaced as a manual-grounded way to think about N1; the fix — blend, transform-into-bind-edge, or accept — is Jordan's, and N1 is flagged sim/playtest-only in the verdict anyway.)*

### Approaches / initiation / first move (manual Axis 4 / Throughline T6, §15.5)
- **v32:** the bout state-graph *is* the initiative model — transitions weighted by who commits, by **Reaction** (effectiveness vs commit-depth, `§7.2`), and by the **Counter-time** named set (+0.5D to counter sub-actions on a successful Reading, `§6.2`). v32 is a **1v1 duel** system (the bout, facing/FoV, and named sets are all dyadic).
- **Manual:** the load-bearing §15.5 result — **counterattack-as-apex is *not* universal**; it is the dueling-centric Italian/Japanese/English pattern (contratempo, kiriotoshi, Silver's counter-stroke), and "best first move" falls out of the combat *context*. Plus the three-tier initiative scheme (*go-no-sen* / *sen-no-sen* / *sen-sen-no-sen*), where the Japanese alone names a **pre-commitment** tier (§2.9, §15.5).
- **Verdict — alignment, and the manual *clears* a potential worry.** v32's context is the duel, so per §15.5 **counterattack-prestige is the historically correct apex for it** — and v32's Counter-time set + reaction-on-read reward exactly the contratempo/sen-no-sen the manual identifies as the dueling-context apex. A multi-opponent system rewarding the counter this heavily would be a finding; v32 is not, because its context licenses it. **Mapping the three tiers:** v32's Reaction (after/simultaneous with the commit, depth-scaled) covers *go-no-sen* and *sen-no-sen*; its Predictive-Anticipation + Intent-Reading-within-FoV (`§10`, §6 loadout) is the closest thing to the pre-commitment *sen-sen-no-sen* — so v32 plausibly covers the manual's finest initiative tier, with Anticipation as the pre-commit channel. *(Worth confirming against §10; I read §4/§7 in full, §10 only as referenced.)*

### Footwork (manual Axis 5 / the geometric channel)
- **v32:** footwork (Curvilinear, Triangular, Drawing, Angled) changes **facing**, reorienting the **FoV cone**, which gates Reading and Reactions; the core loop is to keep the opponent in *your* FoV while reaching *their* periphery/blind (`reframe_blueprint §4`). Facing advantage is emergent from perception, not authored (§4.3).
- **Manual:** footwork is most theorized in the **geometric** channel (Spanish *compases* recto/transverso/curvo, all motion read off the *círculo* whose diameter is the opponent's reach), because there footwork *is* the geometry; footwork is the physical substrate of measure and tempo (§14.4, §1.3.6).
- **Verdict — the cleanest alignment in the whole comparison.** v32's facing/FoV is, structurally, the **geometric cognitive mode operationalized**: footwork as motion read off a constructed perceptual frame, with advantage *emerging* from position (the manual's derive-from-primitives ideal). v32 even names **Triangular** footwork (Katori Shintō-ryū's signature) and **Drawing**. The one caveat is build-status, not design: the part of v32 that most fully embodies a manual cognitive mode (geometric/Spanish) is **Module 7 — pending, stub-first** (`module_manifest.md`), so this alignment is in the design, not yet in the sim.

### Grip (manual Axis 6)
- **v32:** Grip is a **weapon-constrained loadout slot** (Forward-point, Half-grip, Centered/Raised, Centered/Side, Side; `§6.1`, §6.2 sets).
- **Manual:** grip is the most *under-named* axis (distributed across weapon sections, not given its own treatment), and its standout is the German **half-sword** — a grip change that converts a sword into a spear, "a weapon-mode shift that scrambles the categories" (§1.1.3).
- **Verdict — alignment, and v32 is *more* structured than the sources.** v32 names **Half-grip** explicitly and ties grip to the weapon (the half-sword's exact logic). Where the manual notes grip is historically under-theorized as a discrete axis, v32 elevates it to its own loadout slot — a reasonable systematization. *(Whether the grip slot carries real mechanical weight vs. flavor isn't visible from §6's summary; not read at sub-action granularity.)*

### Techniques (manual Axis 7 / Throughline T4)
- **v32:** a large **trained pool** vs a small **equipped loadout**, with the 36-pair coherence matrix **retired** in favor of **named sets** (coherence clusters) (`§6`, §7 fold table). The sets: Thrust Duelist, Bind Fighter, Counter-time, Burst, Continuous-flow (`§6.2`).
- **Manual:** technique is **generative, not enumerative** — a small primitive set with rich combinatorics, the named catalogs being surface expressions; reduction-then-transcendence (German ~100 named plays from a few primitives; Filipino numerada→flow) (§1.1.2, §5.8, §15.2).
- **Verdict — strong alignment, and the sets are cross-culturally grounded.** v32's trained-pool/equipped-loadout *is* the generative model (combinatorial space → small active set), and retiring the matrix for named clusters is exactly the manual's "the catalog is scaffolding; reduce to clusters." Strikingly, **v32's named sets map onto the manual's cognitive modes / traditions**: Thrust Duelist ≈ Italian rapier (temporal-spatial); Bind Fighter ≈ German Liechtenauer (tactile / *Fühlen* / the bind); Counter-time ≈ the counterattack-prestige cluster (Italian *contratempo* / Japanese *sen*); Continuous-flow ≈ Filipino FMA (kinetic-rhythmic *flow*, "flow sub-actions across multi-pass bouts"); Burst ≈ the explosive committed opening (cf. Chinese *fa jin*). The manual thus **validates the set taxonomy as cross-culturally coherent**, not arbitrary. *(Distant cautionary note: the manual's Korean *gwonbeop* case — legibility/doctrine driving a choice that degraded play — is a far parallel to the verdict's N4, the all-or-nothing set-bonus "legibility cliff"; it underscores that legibility-driven choices carry a play cost, nothing more.)*

### The whole-system read (Throughlines T1, T6, T7, T8)
- **T1 (the seven axes are the European-treatise lens):** v32 is, by construction, a Western-**dueling-paradigm** system — reach/tempo/stance/technique explicit, dyadic, in the temporal-spatial + tactile + geometric modes. That is a coherent, deliberate commitment for a 1v1 personal-combat engine. The manual's value is marking the **boundary** of that paradigm: v32 does *not* occupy the **multi-opponent** dimension (§13.4 — central to Chinese/Filipino/Mamluk/Korean), the **cosmological** mode (Chinese/Indian decision-overlays), the **consciousness-theoretical** mode (Japanese *mushin*), or the **capture-over-killing** institutional frame. None of these is a defect — they are the design space v32 has chosen not to enter. *(v32's own `reframe_blueprint §8` notes mass-combat keeps a distinct engine and only borrows the perception-principle; the manual's multi-opponent material is most relevant *there*, not in the personal duel.)*
- **T6 + T7:** v32's weapon-class (option-set) × named-set (selection frame) structure **is** the manual's "weapon constrains the option-set, the cognitive mode selects from it" — the same v32 weapon class can be equipped into different sets (a curved blade in Counter-time vs Continuous-flow), exactly mirroring the manual's "same sabre family integrated into different cognitive frameworks."
- **T8 (the felt timing of initiation is what text preserves worst):** the manual's hardest-to-document axis (initiation-feel) maps to v32's hardest-to-tune layer — the bout transition probabilities and reaction timing — which is precisely what the i17 sim is *tuning* (~20/34 constants draft) and what the NERS verdict flags as "N1 transition feel — sim-check." The manual's lesson — that this layer resists derivation and must be validated by practice — is exactly the posture the v32 build already takes.

**Bottom line of the read:** v32 is unusually well-aligned with the manual's *cross-cultural universals* (discrete measure-zones, stance-as-cyclic-counter, generative technique, geometric footwork-as-perception) and with the *context-appropriate* apex for a duel (counterattack-prestige). The manual independently grounds several open v32 questions — it supplies evidence toward keeping handling (N5) and a transformation-lens on the stance/bind discontinuity (N1) — and it marks the paradigm boundary (multi-opponent, cosmological, consciousness, capture-over-killing) that v32 deliberately does not cross. Nothing in the manual contradicts a verified v32 mechanism; the divergences are *scope choices*, not errors.

## Part C — Distinct martial traditions per v32 weapon type

**The weapon classes (cited).** `combat_v32_proposal §8` / `m3_weapon_class_layer.py` define what the doc calls "**seven** weapon classes" but **encode as eight** (the eighth, `long_heavy_blunt`, is the plate-breaker addition). Each is an abstraction over the canonical combat_v30 §5 axes (reach × weight × type) plus draft handling and Weapon Speed:

| v32 class | reach·weight·type | handling | Speed | best-fit historical weapon |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `long_thrust_primary` | long·light·blade | Demanding | 1.5 | rapier / estoc |
| `long_cut_and_thrust` | long·heavy·blade | Standard | 0.5 | longsword / two-hand sword |
| `curved_cut_primary` | long·light·blade | Standard | 2 | sabre (single-hand curved) |
| `long_pole_spear` | long·light·blade | Forgiving | 0 | spear |
| `long_pole_staff` | long·light·blunt | Forgiving | 0 | staff |
| `paired_short` | short·light·blade | Demanding | 2.5 | dual short blades |
| `single_short` | short·light·blade | Forgiving | 3 | dagger / short sword |
| `long_heavy_blunt` | long·heavy·blunt | Demanding | −0.5 | mace / poleaxe / maul |

**Counting rule (stated so the count is checkable).** A tradition "applies" to a class if the manual treats it **at depth** (a Part I per-tradition section) **and** documents a weapon of that class as **central or significant** to it. Traditions are counted **once** each (Italian counts once even though it spans Fiore + Bolognese + rapier). This is a **mapping I am constructing**, not a canon fact, and it carries real judgment calls because v32's eight classes are *coarse* (reach × weight × type, with no curvature or one- vs two-hand axis) while the manual individuates weapons more finely.

| v32 class | Traditions that apply (manual, at-depth) | Count |
|---|---|---|
| `long_thrust_primary` (rapier) | Italian (rapier), Spanish (Destreza rapier); **+** English engages it (di Grassi/Saviolo/Swetnam) though Silver argues against it | **2** core (**3** w/ English) |
| `long_cut_and_thrust` (longsword) | German (Liechtenauer longsword), Italian (Fiore *spada da doi mani*), English (two-hand sword + backsword), Korean (*ssangsudo*), Chinese (*wodao*/*dadao*), Japanese (*ōdachi*; katana read as cut-and-thrust) | **5–6** |
| `curved_cut_primary` (sabre) | Polish-Hungarian (*szabla*), Mamluk-Ottoman (*shamshir*/*kilij*), Indian (*talwar*, Mughal/Sikh), Chinese (*dao*), Korean (*hwando*), Japanese (*katana*) | **6** |
| `long_pole_spear` (spear) | Chinese (*qiang* — the deepest spear corpus, Wu Shu), Japanese (*yari*), Korean (*jangchang*/*nangseon*), Indian (Dhanurveda/*vel*), Mamluk (lance), European (German/Italian/English polearm), Filipino (*sibat*), Byzantine (*menaulion*) | **7–8** |
| `long_pole_staff` (staff) | Chinese (*gun* / Shaolin staff — the most-documented late-Ming weapon), Indian (Silambam — an entire tradition), English (short staff/quarterstaff — Silver's favorite), Egyptian (*tahtib*), Japanese (*bō*/*jō*), Korean (*gondi*) | **6** |
| `paired_short` (dual short) | Filipino (*doble baston* / *espada y daga* / *sinawali* — the signature), Korean (*ssanggeom*), Chinese (*shuangdao*/*shuangjian*) | **3** |
| `single_short` (dagger) | German (dagger), Italian (Fiore's foundational dagger), Filipino (*daga*/*balisong*/*bolo*), Indian (*katar*/*bichuwa*), Japanese (*tantō*/*kodachi*), English (sword-and-dagger) | **5–6** |
| `long_heavy_blunt` (mace/poleaxe) | Indian (*gada* — the deepest mace tradition), Mamluk (mace, *dabbus*), European (German/Italian poleaxe + *Mordstreich*, armored combat) | **3** |

**The shape of the answer.** The richest classes are the **spear (7–8)**, **sabre (6)**, **staff (6)**, and **cut-and-thrust longsword (5–6)** — the most universal weapon forms. The leanest are the **rapier (2–3)**, **paired-short (3)**, and **heavy-blunt mace/poleaxe (3)** — the most regionally specialized. This matches the manual directly: the curved-sabre line is its explicit "one weapon-family integrated into many cognitive frameworks" case (Throughline T7), and the spear/staff are flagged as the near-universal, multi-opponent/formation weapons.

**Caveats on the count (load-bearing).**
1. **v32's classes are draft Class B, not canon** — `m3` header and §8 are explicit; the count maps the manual onto a *proposal*, and even the proposal's own count is inconsistent ("seven" stated, eight encoded).
2. **The classes are coarse.** v32 has no curvature axis and no one- vs two-hand axis, so the one-handed cut-and-thrust **arming sword / sidesword** (a workhorse of much of the period — Bolognese, the backsword) maps awkwardly: it is neither *curved* nor *heavy two-hander*, so it falls between `curved_cut_primary` and `long_cut_and_thrust`. Several East-Asian two-handers are *curved* (ōdachi, wodao) and so straddle `long_cut_and_thrust` (heavy) and `curved_cut_primary` (light). The ranges in the table reflect these straddles.
3. **"Tradition" individuation is a choice.** The manual's transmission catalog lists ~28 named traditions; I counted only those treated at depth with a documented matching weapon. A looser rule (counting every tradition that *touched* a weapon class) would raise every number.
4. **This is analysis, not authorization.** Whether to *use* a per-weapon tradition spread — e.g., to seed schools/Traditions or named-set variants per weapon class — is a design decision for Jordan, not something this mapping settles.

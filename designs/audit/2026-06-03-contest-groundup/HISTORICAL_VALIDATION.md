# Historical Validation — structure & emergence vs. real adjudicated-discourse frameworks

Top-down validation per the project-owner contract. Grounded in the project's research corpus (`rhetoric_oratory_contest_research.md`, read full) plus the standard historical record. Verdict first.

## Verdict
The engine's **emergent behaviour is historically faithful on every dimension it models** — the right proof wins the right venue, faults end debates, a judge's character can override the bench, deference wins petitions. And the dimensions it does **not** yet generalise are **precisely the ones where real institutions diverge** — so the audit's generality remediation is historically *mandated*, not arbitrary. One genuine divergence: the engine over-rewards build-then-close relative to the historical balance.

## Per-framework validation
For each, the corpus/historical record → the engine's emergent best play → match.

- **Parliament / popular assembly** (Athenian *ekklesia*, the Italian *concione* §12.7, the Norse *Lögberg/Lögrétta* §12.8). Historically deliberative — a body deciding future action, the *advantageous* the axis, a crowd to be **moved**, with demagoguery (Cleon) the signature failure. → Engine: pathos wins the assembly (0.97) and parliament (0.91). **Match.** The assembly is the venue where pathos rules and demagoguery is potent — exactly as emergent.
- **Senate** (Roman; the *concione*'s elite cousin). Authority is **auctoritas/dignitas** — a senator's weight is his standing, not only his argument; ethos+logos load-bearing, pathos far below the popular assembly. → Engine: in the ethos+logos "council-of-elders" role, build/ethos win (0.77–0.78). **Match.** Establishing standing then arguing (build-then-close) is how senatorial weight actually operated.
- **Debate / disputation** (Nyāya *vāda/jalpa* §5.1, §12.6; scholastic disputation). A thesis/counter-thesis resolved by **defeat-conditions** (*nigrahasthāna* — self-contradiction, evasion, silence), a deterministic loss-catalogue, with valid proof (*pramāṇa*) required. → Engine: the defeat-condition catalogue (barred-device, self-contradiction, evasion, silence) **is** the *nigrahasthāna*, and it fires 100% in every context (Matrix 5); logos wins the scholastic venue (0.86). **Strong match** — the corpus explicitly recommended this deterministic-clinch shape "a clean fit for a deterministic engine."
- **Public oration** (epideictic; the *basilikos logos* §2.2, §12.5; panegyric, funeral oration). Praise/blame, the *honourable*, **ethos-dominant**, and — Menander — "nothing is *at issue*," the **opposite** of a contest. → Engine: ethos wins the ceremonial role (0.81), and ceremonial is correctly held as a **separate scaffold**, not forced into the contest core. **Match**, including the structural judgment that ceremonial is not a two-party *contest*.
- **Tribunal** (forensic; the Excommunication Tribunal, Roman courts). Judging a past act, the *just/unjust*, **logos + stasis-dominant** — stasis theory was *built* for forensic (§1.4). → Engine: the forensic/court role is logos-weighted; logos competitive, scholastic (heavier logos) → logos 0.86. **Match.**
- **Appeals** (supplique/remontrance §12.3; *ars dictaminis* §6.1; petition-to-sovereign). **Asymmetric, up a power gradient**, deference/**ethos-dominant**, rank-indexed (the *salutatio* depends on relative rank — self-gating by standing), and the win is **grace granted**, not a merits race. → Engine: ethos wins the appeal/petition role (0.81), and AppealMode is correctly a **separate scaffold** (the symmetric merits-contest core does not model an asymmetric plea-for-grace). **Match in proof-weighting; structure correctly externalised.**

## Cross-cutting mechanics — validated
- **Context modulation** is the strongest result: the emergent "right proof per venue" reproduces the historical record exactly (pathos→assembly, ethos→senate/ceremonial/appeal, logos→tribunal/disputation), and matches the corpus's own thesis that **the regime determines the load-bearing genre** (§12.5).
- **Adjudicator character + role↔character tension** is validated by the Chinese dyadic ruler-model (§4): Han Feizi's *Shuinan* — the *same true advice* wins favour or gets you executed depending on the ruler's **concealed heart** — is precisely the engine's leak/override (a pathos-character judge flips a logos court; building ethos unlocks the character beneath the role). Partial and idiosyncratic judges are the historical norm, not the exception.
- **Self-gating** is validated by the *Caraka-saṃhitā* triage (§5.2 — licit devices conditioned on opponent standing × assembly composition) and the rank-indexed *salutatio* (§6.1).
- **Build-then-close** is validated in *principle* by classical doctrine (ethos/pathos dispose the audience for logos; the *exordium/captatio* establishes ethos first) — but **over-rewarded in degree** (best in 5/10 contexts, edging logos even in a logos court). This is the one place emergence overshoots history; it is the tuning item already on the list.

## Divergences = the audit's generality gaps, historically mandated
The corpus states flatly: **"the genres do not share a win condition"** (§1.2). The engine's single win-condition (merits-threshold OR clinch) is therefore historically wrong as a universal, and the four hard-coded limits map one-to-one onto real institutional variation:
1. **Win condition.** Parliament resolves by **vote tally**; the tribunal by a **verdict** (possibly graded); the appeal by **grace**; debate by **defeat-condition or thesis-establishment**; ceremonial by **acclamation**. The 100% mismatched-appeal draws (Matrix 2) are an artifact of forcing one win-condition on venues that historically resolve otherwise — a real assembly still produces a vote; a sovereign still grants or denies. → pluggable win-conditions are *required*.
2. **Adjudicator multiplicity.** The assembly is **many voters**; the jury/bench a **panel**; the Lögrétta **48 goðar** (each with two counsellors, §12.8); the senate a **body**. The single adjudicator cannot model these. → the "adjudicator(s)" panel is historically right.
3. **Proof axis.** The three *pisteis* are the **Greco-Roman** system; other traditions run on other proof types (Nyāya *pramāṇa*, Arabic *balāgha* registers, the Chinese read-the-heart). For Valoria's Greco-Roman/medieval/Norse setting the three are the correct default; for *any* framework the proof set must be parameterizable. → lower priority for the setting, real for full generality.
4. **Asymmetric / one-sided modes.** Appeals (up a gradient), dyadic counsel (one persuader, one ruler), and ceremonial (one-sided display) are **not** symmetric two-party contests — and the engine correctly externalises them as scaffolds. → the architecture's separation is validated.

## Emergence — validated
The emergent optimal repertoire — *use the proof the venue/judge favours; build standing before you close; read and exploit a judge whose character differs from the office* — is the historically attested craft of the orator (Cicero's stasis-plus-ethos; Demosthenes moving the assembly; the courtier reading the prince; the petitioner's deference). The engine **produces the real strategic space as emergent play**, and its emergent failure modes (demagoguery in the crowd, the partial judge) are the historically documented ones.

## Bottom line
The structure validates strongly against the record on what it models, and the validation **independently confirms the generalization plan**: the win-condition, the adjudicator panel, and the asymmetric modes are exactly where history refuses a single shape. Do the generalization (it is historically motivated), trim the build-then-close over-reward (the one emergent overshoot), and the engine becomes a faithful general instrument for formalized two-party adjudicated discourse.

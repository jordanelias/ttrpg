## §0 — VERDICT AND SHAPE OF THE MACHINE

**Valoria's campaign is an append-only event log driven by a deterministic seasonal tick, with one human inside it.** Strategic scale (15 territories, ~35 settlements [F9], 4–6 faction AIs, the global clock set — RS/MS, CI, IP, PI, Strain — plus relational/categorical trackers) and personal scale (one character, 3–5 scenes per season) run simultaneously; the zoom system and Domain Echo are the only bridges between them, and both are deliberately narrow. The campaign has **no fail state for the player short of ten consecutive seasons of total world collapse** — defeat conditions were systematically converted into era transitions ("No shared loss. No fade to black. Every crisis becomes a new chapter," victory_v30 §5; peninsular_strain §6.4), and player death routes through generational transition rather than game over. The single victory (GD-1: 11+/15 territories, Accord ≥ 2, Strain ≤ 6, sustained 2 consecutive seasons) is reachable by any faction the player serves, leads, or founds.

**Operationally, a campaign is:** `initial_conditions + Key log`. Every consequential event is a typed, validated Key; replay is deterministic re-execution (key_substrate §1, §6); the save file *is* the campaign history. The seasonal tick — Slate generation → Personal Phase → Strategic Phase → Cascade → 10-step Accounting → victory check — is the master loop everything hangs off (faction_layer §7; campaign_architecture module 3.15). The probabilistic surface has exactly three regimes (discrete d10 pool ≡ continuous-Normal for personal scale; d+σ for faction scale, ED-874) plus two non-probabilistic archetypes (deterministic accounting, clock advance) and the armature dot-product for narrative interpretation. Five resolvers, one event substrate, one tick.

**Flow health, summarized (detail in §9):** the loop inventory is in good shape — the three historical death-spirals (faction passive collapse, Wealth-0 Military ratchet, Mandate runaway) all carry ratified dampers (FSS-LOOP-1/2, LPS-2e saturating aggregate); MS band transitions carry hysteresis and leading warnings (ED-882); Strain and IP advance under caps with treaty-pair dampers (ED-743). The serious open items are **(1)** the absence of a canonical *general* top-down Key-delivery rule (ED-1006, as narrowed by F13 — strategic outcomes reach the personal scale via the Scene Slate, retrospectives, §3.7 tactical zoom and §4.1 board-degree Ob-shading; what is unspecified is a general channel delivering arbitrary strategic events as consumable Keys. The earlier 'all nine §3 rules upward' wording was overstated — §3 is *Eight* rules + §3.9, and §3.7/§3.9 include downward links), **(2)** the Jordan-blocked faction-stat architecture decision that gates Wave-2 implementation, and **(3)** a residue of pre-pivot text (shared-loss language, "all 15" victory, ×3 MS multiplier, stale CI start, scene-count drift) that contradicts ratified canon and will mislead a port if not struck. No new P1 findings beyond items already ledgered; the residue items are P2/P3 propagation work, staged in §9 for Jordan-gated ED filing (per the open de-dup backlog — unilateral ED appends would worsen the ID-collision class).

## §1 — THE OPERATIONAL SUBSTRATE: WHAT A CAMPAIGN *IS* IN CODE

### 1.1 The Key log (the spine)
Every consequential event — a contest resolved, a Domain Action outcome, a Scar acquired, a strain shock, a season change — is a **Key**: `{id, type, source_actor, emitted_at{season_index, sub_step_index}, causes[], targets[] (each with role, impact_vector[4], stat_deltas{}), scale_signature[], symbolic_dimensions[4], visibility, time_horizon, permanence, payload}` (key_substrate §2.1). Seven type families, ≈30 subtypes (key_type_registry). Key emission is the *only* state-mutation path (§4.1); RNG is seeded per emission and ordering is deterministic (§6), so **save = serialize the log; load = replay**. The campaign's "flow" is, literally, the ordered Key stream; the §8.8 Godot mapping (Key→Resource, registry→autoload, KEY_LOG→typed-array Resource) is already specified.

### 1.2 The value taxonomy (what the Keys move)
Four buckets under the base 1–7 Stats (derived_stats_v30 §3/§14; clock_registry as manifest):
- **Pools** — dice quantities computed at action time (Combat `max(5, History+6)` per ED-901; Argue `(Primary×2)+H+3+style`; Thread `(Spirit×2)+H+TPS` min 5; Fieldwork `(Primary×2)+H+3`; Knot `(Bonds×2)+3`; Mass `min(Size,Command)+Command`).
- **Derived Values** — `stat × multiplier` resources that drain/refill (Health, Stamina `(3·End)+(2·Spi)`, Composure, Concentration `(3·Foc)+(2·Spi)`, Treasury `Wealth×100`, Discipline `Stability×10`, Public Order `Order×20`, …). Read-only outside their formulas (R4 hook).
- **Tracks** — bounded relational/character counters (Disposition −4..+Bonds; Coherence 10→0; TS 0–100; Accord 0–3 per territory; PT 0–5 per territory; Standing 0–7; Renown 0–10; Torben/Elske Loyalty 0–7; Löwenritter Autonomy categorical).
- **Clocks** — mostly monotonic world/scene progress (MS 0–100 start 72; CI 0–100 start 28; IP 0–100 start 20; PI 0–20 start 7; Turmoil/Strain 0–10 start 0; Evidence 3/5/8; Coup Counter). (params/bg/core.md §Starting Values; clock_registry.)

### 1.3 The five resolvers (how Keys get made)
(A) **dice pool** vs TN 7 (faces: 1 = −1, 7–9 = +1, 10 = +2; degree: Overwhelming ≥ 2×Ob AND ≥ 3 · Success ≥ Ob · Partial 0<net<Ob · Failure ≤ 0 — params/bg/core.md §Dice/§Degree); equivalently the continuous-Normal regime in videogame mode (statistically equivalent per derived_stats_v30 §1 — fractional Ob/TN, continuous degrees).
(B) **d+σ** for the faction regime (ED-874, ratified 2026-05-31): `M = acting_stat − difficulty; P_success = clamp(0.5 + 0.1·M, 0.05, 0.90)`, banded to the same 4-degree ladder. Uniform 0.10/stat-point slope across 1–7 — this is the architectural fix that retired the bare-stat small-pool roll (the faction layer's former NERS-R failure).
(C) **deterministic accounting** — ledger formulas, no roll (settlement Mandate aggregate; CI formula; muster; Treasury income).
(D) **clock advance** — increments toward thresholds (MS/CI/IP/Strain/Evidence).
(E) **armature dot-product** — `interpretation(npc, key) = Σ_axes armature_position[axis] × key.symbolic_dimensions[axis] × key.impact_vector[npc][axis]` over the 13-Conviction × 4-axis matrix; the read-side that turns the log into NPC reactions, the Concern queue, cut-scene triggers (fire threshold 0.40), and the annual Chronicle (articulation layer, PP-688).

### 1.4 The runtime containers (where it executes)
Per videogame_mode_spec §1 and the valoria-game scene tree: **GameDirector** (zoom stack, max depth 3) + **ZoomManager** + **ConsequenceRouter** (Domain Echo) over six containers — **BoardContainer** (strategic map, faction actions, accounting), **CombatContainer**, **BattleContainer** (mass battle, with SoloResolution auto-resolve), **DebateContainer** (social contest), **NarrativeContainer** (fieldwork/exploration/dialogue) — plus autoloads (event bus, KeyStore, SeasonManager/GameStateMachine, SceneTimer, TrackerRegistry). The Python `sim/` armature mirrors this tree 1:1 by design, so each Godot module ports from a reference implementation under golden-master parity (godot_conversion_strategy_v1 Part I, V).

**There is no GM.** Every "GM decides" in the design corpus resolves to Authored / Deterministic / AI-driven / Player-choice / Default before implementation (videogame_mode_spec §3). The engine is the rendering authority.

7. **First tick.** Season 1 opens: Scene Slate generates, faction leader AI assigns the player's Duty (if Standing ≥ 1), faction AIs run their stacks. The campaign is live.

## §3 — THE SEASON TICK (the master loop)

One season is one full traversal of the four-phase structure (campaign_modes §12.3 Hybrid column, which videogame_mode_spec §1.6 adopts as *the* videogame structure), with the strategic action priorities and the 10-step Accounting from faction_layer §7. In execution order:

### 3.0 Season open
- `mechanical.season_change` Key emitted (engine_clock / SeasonManager).
- **Duty assignment** (Standing ≥ 1): the faction leader's AI priority stack selects one Duty matched to player location/skills/faction urgency (player_agency §3.2). Standing 0 → Initiation Duty only.
- **Scene Slate generation** — deterministic, 8-step (player_agency §4.2): Step 1 Mandatory Crisis (Priority 0; Accord-0 territory, active Heresy Investigation on the player, mass battle in player's territory, faction-leader removal — plus the scale_transitions §4.3.2 set: Stability Crisis with ED-749 hysteresis, Knot partner crisis, companion arc, rank recognition) → Step 2 Crisis Events (Priority 1; clock band crossings, nearby Accord ≤ 1, NPC Scar ≥ 2) → Step 2b Thread-State (max 1; MS ≤ 20 in-territory is mandatory) → Step 3 Duty-aligned → Step 4 Conviction-aligned (keyword/role matcher, max 3) → Step 5 NPC outreach/demands → Step 6 Territorial → Step 7 Ambient; then deterministic cross-step pruning to the difficulty's slate size (ED-747). **Mandatory overflow → Witness Mode** (ED-745): attend what the budget allows; the rest resolve via NPC AI with the player present-but-unable-to-direct (one free Read/Appraise roll, no Domain Echo, no Momentum/Coherence cost).

### 3.1 Personal Phase (the player's turn)
The player spends **3–5 scene actions** (difficulty base ± Standing/Knot/Wound modifiers, player_agency §6). Each scene action opens one slate entry as a scene in the matching container — combat (A-pool, TN 7, ED-901 pool, crit net ≥ 4), social contest (exchange loop: Appraise → Declare → Corroborate → Argue → Resolve; Composure/Concentration drains; Persuasion clock), fieldwork (Evidence clock 3/5/8; Exposure vs Cover; Disposition ladder — Neutral→Bonded is 6–8 actions over 3–4 seasons), threadwork (Leap at TS ≥ 30; Coherence costs per the campaign_architecture §3.2 asymmetry — Mending 0, Lock 1, Dissolution 2, Binding 2; Fibonacci in-scene pool fatigue and +2 Ob/scene cross-scene fatigue §3.2a; P-01 co-movement auto-effects always fire), or travel (1 scene action per province traversed). A scene may chain systems (interview → contest → combat). Momentum (+1 per genuine Conviction pursuit) is the cross-scene spend.
**Unpursued slate entries resolve by AI** per the §4.5 table — the duty not done is Standing −1 at Accounting; the revolt not attended resolves on garrison strength; the clock threshold not witnessed propagates anyway. *The world does not pause for the protagonist* is the load-bearing design sentence.

### 3.2 Strategic Phase (the factions' turn)
For each active faction, per season:
1. **GD-2 mandatory pass first** (canon/02 §B): for every owned territory at Accord ≤ 3 with no garrison → mandatory **Muster** targeting it; Accord ≤ 2 with garrison → mandatory **Govern**. Up to 3 mandatories, consuming action slots, scheduled **before** any stochastic candidate. (Given Accord's 0–3 range, clause (a) reads as "every ungarrisoned owned territory demands a garrison" — see §9 F3 for the intent flag.)
2. **Priority-stack selection** for the remainder: each leader's 7-step AI stack (Survival → Existential → Framework/Conviction-aligned → Institutional → Secondary → Reactive → Pass; per-NPC stacks in complete_systems_reference §1.4–1.10, with the PP-NPC-01..04 anti-spiral gates, e.g. Crown Decree requires Mandate ≥ 3), then the generic posture ladder (Existential → Defend → Consolidate → Counter-threat at CI ≥ 55 → Expand → Opportunistic).
3. **Resolution by priority class** (faction_layer §7 Phase 4): Priority 1 Intel/Tribune → 2 Military/Battle (Occupation established; Trigger-5 gate) → 3 Domain (Consul, Muster, Govern, Trade, Fortify) → 4 Social (Parliamentary Motions, treaty initiation, Crown Treaty) → 5 Thread operations (strategic Weave/Mend/Investigate/Harvest orders) → 6 Special/Unique (Royal Decree, Excommunication, Church Seizure) → 7 Projects. All faction-stat actions resolve on **d+σ** (ED-874); battles route to mass battle (zoomed Part A 7-phase machine, or Part B auto-resolve if the player doesn't zoom).
4. **The player's presence matters mechanically:** PC in a territory grants their faction +1D on one Domain Action there per season (scale_transitions §9); a Standing 4+ player issues sub-commands; a leader-player replaces the AI stack with their own orders.

### 3.3 Zoom traffic (during both phases)
Mandatory Zoom In triggers (scale_transitions §4.3.2 — cannot be declined, Priority 0) and world-state triggers (§4.3.3, Priority 1, optional) pull the player into personal scenes at legal entry points (after battle Phase 1 / Phase 3 / Phase 6 Step 1; at end of the Domain Action otherwise). Board degree shades scene difficulty (Board FAILURE → scene Ob +1; SUCCESS → −1; OVERWHELMING → −2, §4.1). Zoom Out resumes the strategic sequence with updated state (§4.2); the BG clock never pauses — the personal scene is *inserted into* the resolution sequence (PP-110). Events the player missed entirely generate free retrospective "Where Were You?" scenes next season (§4.4).

### 3.4 Cascade Phase
Domain Echoes queue: any personal scene meeting **Sufficient Scope** (§7 — named faction leader involved; institutional challenge; Complex+ investigation completed; Relational+ Thread op; combat victory over a faction officer; Disposition +4/+5 with a faction officer; settlement governance moving Order ±1) emits ±1 (Success) / ±2 (Overwhelming) to the most relevant faction stat, −1 to own faction on Failure — **capped at 1 Echo per scene per faction, ±2 per stat** (PP-329); Accord Echoes cap ±1 per territory per Zoom In and target the scene's settlement (AUD-SET-02); Thread Echoes per the ED-673 table. PP-198: the declaring practitioner pays Coherence for Thread ops that touch the strategic layer. **This is the personal→strategic bandwidth, and it is intentionally narrow** — a player moves institutions a point or two a season through scenes; the institutions move themselves through Domain Actions.

### 3.5 Accounting (Phase 5 — 10 steps, faction_layer §7; ED-678)
1. Apply pending attribute changes; **Parliamentary votes resolve** (votes = Mandate; Church +⌊CI/20⌋; anti-Church −⌊CI/30⌋; Sacred Veto cadence 1/4 seasons; Censure/Embargo/Blockade/Outlawry/Subsidy/War Authorisation/… per §5.4 with Rebuttal §5.5); treaty ratifications → Trigger 2/3 stability consequences.
2. **Accounting Stability Check** (≥ 2 attribute points lost → Stability pool vs Ob = loss magnitude; **deterministic floor at Stability ≤ 2** — the check cannot reduce Stability there, FSS-LOOP-1) and **collapse check** (Stability 0 → §1.5 exit procedure: Mandate→0, territories Uncontrolled at Accord 0, units Masterless, officers Independent, player loses Standing benefits; one-time Survival Exception at Stability 1).
3. Cooldowns advance.
4. **Clock advances (clocks only):** the full CI sequence (Momentum +1 → Piety Yield: +1 per Church-Prominent PT-5 territory, +0.5 per PT-4 → Assert (d+σ, Influence vs Ob 2) → Suppress (Mandate vs Ob = ⌊Church Mandate/2⌋+1; negates the passive) → Baralta structural −1 while her Mandate ≥ 4; **failure penalty: faction_layer §7+§9 say Stability −1, victory §7 says Discipline −15 — see §9 F10**; caps ±3 player / ±5 total, PP-504); RS/MS yearly baseline; IP; PI; Church Prominence update. *(Accord/Strain/battle accounting is **Step 6**, not here — corrected per faction_layer §7's ED-678 consolidated sequence; flat-spec companion §H/F12 records the prior misplacement.)*
5. Church Attention Pool; Thread Debt drain.
6. **Turmoil accounting** (Accord/Strain/battle — the canonical Step-6 home; peninsular_strain §7 labels these "4c/4d/4e" against pre-ED-678 numbering): **Accord checks** (Accord-1 ungarrisoned → 0; Accord-0 → Revolt, garrison vs Popular Uprising Ob 2, territory → Uncontrolled, Strain +1; garrisoned + 2 quiet seasons → Accord +1, cap 2) → **Strain update** (per-territory Accord ≤ 1: +1 each, cap +3/season; eliminations +2; Revolts +1; decay −1 if no instability advance; treaty pairs −1 each, cap −2; bands 3–4 Tension / 5–6 Fracture / 7–8 Crisis / 9–10 Collapse) → **battle accounting** (RS/MS already applied in-phase; ED-743 struck battle-occurrence IP).
7. Threshold events / event cards / Milestone bonuses; Warden Emergence check; **GD-3 insurgency check** post-Accord-aggregation (2+ contiguous Uncontrolled territories sustained 2 seasons → Insurgency at L=1.0, territorial but non-parliamentary; promotes to faction at L ≥ 3 + 2 territories + Accord ≥ 4 avg + 2 seasons; PT < 3 avg → RM-variant, extra-parliamentary).
8. Warden Cooperation; Torben/Elske Loyalty events.
9. Occupation duration (3rd consecutive season → control transfer at Accord 1; Institutional Consolidation: a trigger-free season → Stability +1 and Accord +1 in one territory).
10. **Victory check** (GD-1, 2-consecutive-Accounting test); season marker advances; Winter → Year-End Accounting (Embargo/Blockade renewals; fractional Piety Yield floors).

Throughout, every step emits Keys; the articulation layer reads the stream each season into the Concern queue, threshold cut-scenes, and the annual Chronicle. **Cascade depth cap:** max 3 immediate mechanical effects per resolution step; overflow queues to next Accounting (params/bg/core.md §Cascade Depth Cap) — the engine's stack-overflow guard.

## §4 — THE PROBABILITY FABRIC (what the odds actually are)

**Personal scale (archetype A / continuous-Normal).** d10, TN 7: per die E[net] = 0.3·(+1) + 0.1·(+2) − 0.1·(1) = **+0.4 expected successes/die**, σ ≈ 0.8/die. A baseline 5D pool vs Ob 2 succeeds roughly half the time; pools run 5–18D across systems, and the design keeps load-bearing personal outcomes on healthy pools or routes them through clocks (Evidence 3/5/8; Persuasion track) per the resolution-diagnostic lessons. Degree ladder: Failure ≤ 0 < Partial < Ob ≤ Success, Overwhelming ≥ max(2·Ob, 3). Calibration anchors from canon: ±1 Charisma ≈ 25–30% contest win-rate swing; ±4 = Total Victory in 2 exchanges (complete_systems_reference Part 3); investigation pacing 3–5 scenes for a 5-threshold case (Part 5).

**Faction scale (archetype B, d+σ).** `P_success = clamp(0.5 + 0.1·(stat − difficulty), 0.05, 0.90)`. Every faction stat point is worth exactly 10 percentage points everywhere on the 1–7 range — the scale-invariant impact the diagnostic's Lesson 2 demands, and the reason a Military-2 faction under occupation is *disadvantaged* rather than *dice-noise-random*. Floor 0.05 / ceiling 0.90 keep desperation and dominance both live.

**Mass battle.** Dice per engagement inside the 7-phase state machine (Strategy → Volley → Manoeuvre → Thread → Engagement → Cascade → Reform); pool `min(Size, Command)+Command`; the known exploit surface (splitting dominates concentration +9%..+45%) is countered by terrain/tactic gates; auto-resolve (Part B Martial pool vs Battle Ob) when the player does not zoom. Consequences are flat and predictable: **MS −1 per battle (−2 Campaign/War)** — campaign_architecture §3.1 struck the old ×3 spike precisely so a player can *see degradation coming and choose to stop fighting*.

One loop, four scales, one event log.

## §5 — CLOCK TRAJECTORIES: HOW FAST THE WORLD MOVES (DERIVED)

All arithmetic below composes canonical per-season rates from §4; labeled DERIVED, ranges not points, pending sim confirmation.

**CI.** From 28, net seasonal drift under contest: Church plays Assert (P≈0.90 at Influence 6 vs difficulty 2) → +1/season; an opposing Suppress (negate +1) and Baralta's −1 (while her Mandate ≥ 4) can hold it flat. `[CORRECTION 2026-06-11 (flat-spec v2 AR-1): the original draft credited "+2..+3/season gross … Prominence in T9 (PT 5) … CI 60 ~S11 uncontested." That is wrong — Piety Yield requires Church Mandate > the **controlling** faction's Mandate, which a Church-controlled territory (incl. T9) can never satisfy; Piety Yield is ≈0 at seed. Uncontested accrual is Momentum +1 (itself PP-NPC-03-gated on Stability ≥3 ∧ CI<75 ∧ yearly) + Assert +1 ≈ +2/season.]` DERIVED window (corrected): **CI 28→60 reaches the Seizure threshold ≈ S16 uncontested**, later under any contest, and only once the Church establishes contested Prominence in *rival* territories does Piety Yield begin to accelerate it; the declaration then arrives stochastically on the ((CI−60)/40)^3.3 curve — expected within a few seasons of CI 80+, certain at 100, where the **Mass Seizure Declaration** fires the mandatory all-territory bid (campaign_architecture §1.3: every Church-building territory simultaneously targeted at Ob = 10 − PT − infrastructure, floor 1; one Emergency Session season of response; Church then governs what it seized at Accord 1–2). CI ≥ 60 also silently feeds IP +2/season — theocratic dominance invites Altonia. This single coupling makes the Church question the campaign's central pacing valve.

**IP.** From 20, +80 to invasion. Maximum sustained advance ≈ +5/season (+3 instability cap + 2 CI-dominance) ⇒ **floor ~16 seasons of continuous worst-case governance failure**; a consolidating peninsula generates ≈ 0 and never sees Phase 1. DERIVED expectation: the Vanguard (IP 75) is a mid-game presence only in badly-run or war-stalled campaigns; full Occupation Era is a late-game alternative chapter, not a default. The ED-743 logic is doing exactly its historical job: conquest is free, *failure to consolidate* is what summons the empire.

**MS.** Baseline −1/year is nearly flat (−0.25/season-equivalent). The real driver is war: 1–2 battles/season ⇒ −1..−4/season net (Campaign/War scale at the top of that range), putting the **Fragile fall-edge (60) — entry to the "observable anomalies" revelation tier — roughly 3–12 sustained war-seasons from the 72 start**; a peaceful or Mending-supported campaign never leaves Strained (79–60, folklore). The Thread Revelation curve (campaign_architecture §4) is therefore **war-coupled**: how visibly metaphysical the campaign becomes is, mechanically, a function of how much mass violence the factions (and player) choose. Discrete revelation triggers (public threadwork by a renowned character, Threadcut encounter, Edeyja's visible Mending, Thread ops in mass battle, RM node discovery) can jump visibility ahead of the band. Recovery fights hysteresis: a fall through 40 must climb back to 48.

**Strain.** Self-limiting by construction: +3 cap vs −1 clean + −2 treaty pairs means a peninsula with working diplomacy holds Strain ≤ 4 through a regional war; Fracture/Crisis (5+) requires multiple simultaneously unconsolidated holdings or eliminations. The Strain ≤ 6 victory gate is thus a *governance* test, not a peace test.

**Net pacing claim (DERIVED):** the canonical timeline table (victory §10) puts faction approaches at 12–18 seasons and the old all-15 prestige condition at 20–30; under GD-1's 11/15-with-treaties the operative campaign length lands in the **~12–25 season band (3–6 in-game years)**, i.e. **roughly 50–100 player scenes per campaign** at Normal (4 actions × seasons) — with the era-transition systems extending rather than ending campaigns that go badly.

## §6 — THE CAMPAIGN ARC, PHASE BY PHASE

**Opening (S1–S7): cold equilibrium, burning fuse.** The pre-pivot equilibrium problem ("every canonical mechanic is a pressure accumulator, not a trigger" — early_game_ignition §1) is solved by three stacked igniters: settlement governance friction at seed (5 provinces with non-aligned settlements), the drawn Tensions card's visible fuse, and GD-2 forcing every AI faction into Muster/Govern posture from S1 (no faction may ignore its own destabilizing territory). The player's opening loop: Initiation Duty → Standing 1 → Duty/Conviction scenes; investigating the fuse competes directly with faction-building — the campaign's first triage decision. NPC anti-spiral gates (PP-NPC-01..04) keep the AIs from self-immolating before contact.

**Ignition (S8–S12): the fuse fires.** The unaverted Tensions event lands (assassination with three different mid-games by target; famine; rogue Cardinal; guild schism; Einhir confrontation; ministry collapse). Each is engineered to force *positional* responses from every faction's ethical framework — the prisoner's-dilemma round the equilibrium lacked. Simultaneously the slow accumulators come into range: CI approaches its 60 window; the first conquests create Accord-1 territories whose consolidation failures begin feeding IP/Strain; the Löwenritter Autonomy ladder starts answering Crown weakness (Restless at Crown Stability ≤ 3 / 4 idle military seasons / a lost province).

**Midgame (≈S10–S20): overlapping escalations.** This is where the campaign's character is decided, by which escalation systems arm:
- *Church bid:* CI ≥ 60 opens the one-shot Seizure; CI 100 forces the Mass Seizure Declaration scene — the single most campaign-shaping scheduled event, win-or-cripple by per-territory resolution.
- *Altonian pressure:* held instability + CI dominance push IP through visibility milestones (60 intelligence report → 80 emergency session → 90 imminent), then phased invasion — each phase reversible by IP decay, battle (2× Overwhelming = permanent repulsion), diplomacy (Elske ≥ 6), or resistance.
- *Collapse → emergence:* a faction ground to Stability 0 exits by the §1.5 procedure; its Uncontrolled territories, left 2 seasons, spawn a GD-3 Insurgency that can invade anyone — including its negligent parent — and promote to a full faction (RM-flavored if PT < 3). The faction roster is **open**, downward and upward.
- *Crown fracture:* PI ≥ 20 auto-resolves to Crown elimination; Löwenritter Split/Coup re-bases the monarchy (Torben claim transfers); succession rules (§3.6a, SUC-01..03) decide who sits where — with the player eligible from Standing 5+.
- *Thread surfacing:* war-driven MS decline crosses revelation bands; RM hits its crisis when its governance nodes are revealed as Threadweaving sites (Embrace/Denial/Schism by player influence); Warden paths A–E arm.
**Mandatory zooms guarantee the player is *in* these events** — leader removals, settlement revolts, battles at their location, heresy investigations against them — within their scene budget, Witness Mode beyond it.

**Endgame (≈S12–S25): the sovereignty push.** GD-1's test — 11+/15 (treaties and effective hegemony counting), Accord ≥ 2 everywhere held, Strain ≤ 6, **twice consecutively** — is a consolidation problem, not a conquest problem: military conquest hands you Accord-1 territories that *erode* the Strain/IP conditions; the asymmetric faction toolkits (Crown Treaty network, Church seizure infrastructure, Hafenmark Dynastic Proclamation, Varfell intelligence-led targeting, RM cultural displacement, Löwenritter martial governance) exist to acquire at Accord 2+. The two-Accounting hold plus the per-season Accord checks make sniping impossible — the engine demands a stable season, then proves it again.

**Era transitions instead of defeat.** MS 0 → Rupture Scene (the Last Declaration) → Post-Calamity Era (acquisition suspended 3 seasons, Mending doubled, recovery to MS 20 within 10 seasons — with the MS ≤ 12 leading-warning trend guaranteeing it never arrives unsignaled). IP 100 → Occupation Era (the game's "hardest non-terminal challenge," explicitly winnable). All factions at Stability 0 → Anarchy Era (direct personal governance; Founded-Organization faction formation; recovery = Parliament quorum). **The only true terminal: Second Calamity — 10 consecutive seasons at MS ≤ 5 inside the Post-Calamity Era.** A campaign ends in victory, in voluntary Portrait Retirement, or in ten sustained, warned, witnessed seasons of failure ("a decade of complete failure" in victory §5.3's own gloss — loose against the Winter/Year-End four-season year, exact count is what binds) — never by surprise.

**Continuity across characters.** PC death or Retirement fires the generational transition: the entire world state persists (all faction/settlement/territory/clock/NPC state, card hands); one Legacy Conviction transfers transformed; Resources pass at floor(half)+new-start; Standing/Renown/Coherence/Knots reset or rupture (Knotted NPCs take rupture strain); Obligations transfer to the institution; predecessor Renown ≥ 7 seeds +1. The campaign is the *world's* run; characters are chapters in it.

## §7 — THE PLAYER'S THROUGHLINE (the human inside the machine)

The player is a **character first** (the ROTK officer-mode / CK3-vassal position, player_agency §1) whose institutional reach is earned: Standing 0→7 inside one faction (Petitioner → … → Regent-Designate; Duties succeed +1 / exceed +2 / fail −1 floor-protected at 1; rank gates open intelligence at 2, council voice at 3, sub-commands +1 action at 4, inner circle +2 actions at 6, succession eligibility at 7) and Renown 0–10 across the peninsula (+1 per resolved Conviction, exceeded Duty, Domain Echo, influenced arc, Complex+ case, battle presence, Accord improvement, Knot; cap +2/season; governance failures −1, cap −2). The two axes are independent and both viable: Standing-5/Renown-3 insider, or Standing-0/Renown-8 independent whose floor(Renown/2) pool substitutes for institutional dice at 7+ and who can call a Grand Contest at 9+.

**Per season the player makes one triage decision (which 3–5 of the 5–7 opportunities Normal surfaces) and N tactical decisions inside scenes.** Over a 12–25-season campaign that is ~50–100 scenes against ~60–175 surfaced opportunities — the designed shortfall is the tension engine, and everything declined resolves by AI with stated consequences (§4.5 table). Convictions steer the Slate (Step 4) and pay Momentum; Duties pull against them (the stated central tension for non-leaders); leadership, if taken (Standing 7 offer, 5–6 succession contest, 4+ challenge), swaps the Duty system for direct faction command and the AI becomes the advisor. Exit is always player-authored: once 2 of 3 starting Convictions resolve, "Conclude this story" appears at every season transition — the Portrait Sequence (with the always-available Draft Portrait as the running mirror) ends the chapter on the player's clock, with Lineage Acts (Mentorship / Succession / Thread Legacy) deciding what carries forward.

The articulation layer is the same loop's narrative face: the Key log, filtered through the character's 13-Conviction armature, becomes the Concern queue, the triggered cut-scenes (threshold 0.40), and the annual Chronicle — the campaign literally *reads itself back* to the player from the event substrate.

## §10 — THE FLOW FROM THE IMPLEMENTATION SIDE (what must run, in what order, for a campaign to execute end-to-end)

Per godot_conversion_strategy_v1 (Parts II/VI) the conversion unit is the **module contract** (27 modules, `consumes → resolver → emits`, machine-adjudicated), and the wave order *is* the campaign loop's dependency order:

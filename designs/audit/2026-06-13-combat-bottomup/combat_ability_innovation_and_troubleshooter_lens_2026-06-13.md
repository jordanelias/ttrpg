## §1 — Precedents template, not gate (confirmed, with a precedent)

The emergent reframe stands and you've sharpened it: a tradition is an *exemplar and a curriculum* — it seeds a
region of the channel space and hands you a coherent starting bundle. You depart from it freely. Troubleshooter is
the proof case in published design: a character's *class* unlocks class-unique masteries (templates), but the actual
build equips masteries drawn from **every** source — enemy drops, other classes, crafting, story — mixed across five
categories under a points budget. The class informs and seeds; it never fences the build. That is your principle,
shipped and acclaimed.

## §2 — The poleaxe build, validated bottom-up

*"Why can't you have a poleaxe user who commits to Japanese intent and Spanish footwork?"* — You can, and the
numbers say it is **coherent and synergistic**, not merely tolerated.

The channels you'd develop (taking the Japanese and Spanish *templates*):
- Japanese intent → **precommit 1.35**, tempo 1.20, tactile 1.15.
- Spanish footwork → **measure 1.35, balance 1.30**.
- Combined profile: precommit-high · measure-high · balance-high · tempo-moderate — *internally consistent*: these
  are all the patient-control channels; none fights another.

The weapon:
- Poleaxe — **spd −0.5 (the slowest), reach long, percussion 8, grip_len 2.2 (long lever), clinch 5**.

Why it coheres — and why it's *more* than coherent:
- A weapon this **slow cannot react** — it must *anticipate*. Precommit (sen, reading intent) is precisely how a
  slow heavy weapon lands its blow: you read the commit before it forms and meet it. The Japanese channel is not a
  bolt-on; it is the **timing solution** for a slow weapon.
- A weapon this **long and controlling wants to dictate measure** — the Spanish geometric footwork (measure +
  balance) keeps the poleaxe at the distance it dominates and denies the opponent the entry. The Spanish channel is
  the **distance solution**.
- The poleaxe's **percussion 8 + leverage** supply the kill. So: anticipation sets the time, footwork sets the
  distance, percussion delivers — three complementary competences for one patient control-fighter.

No historical school is "poleaxe + sen + destreza." This is an **innovated, world-logical build**, coherent because
the channels and the physics fit — exactly the kind the emergent model is *meant* to produce, and which a label-gate
would have wrongly forbidden.

## §3 — The innovation / synthesis mechanic (the new generative piece)

*"Enough mastery in two traditions, and circumstances allow you to innovate a new technique that synthesizes them."*
Built bottom-up:

- **Mastery** of a tradition = developed competence in its channels + fluency in its techniques (a measurable
  threshold, not a label).
- **Dual mastery** = past the threshold in *both* — which the bounded competence budget makes *expensive*, so it is
  earned, and rare enough to be special.
- **Circumstance** = a play trigger: both parent techniques used in proximity, or a moment where both methods'
  channels are simultaneously live, or a high-pressure beat — the spark, as innovation historically came under need.
- **Synthesis (the generative rule)** = the system *composes* a child technique from the two parents:
  child **channels** = the blend/union of the parents'; child **effect** = a composition of the parents'
  node-behaviors (parent A a counter-trigger, parent B a measure-control → child = a *measure-controlling counter*:
  it fires like A on B's distance terms). **Generated by rule, not hand-authored per pair** — so it scales and stays
  world-logical (the child's channels and effect *follow* from its parents).
- **Effectiveness then emerges** as always — and because you mastered both parents, you *have* the channels to power
  the child. Innovation is the reward for deep two-method investment, paid for in the budget.

**The poleaxe synthesis, concretely:** a fighter who has mastered Japanese sen (precommit-counter) and Spanish
destreza (measure/balance control), wielding a poleaxe, innovates **"anticipatory measure-control"** — a poleaxe
technique that uses intent-reading to time a measure-keeping intercept. Its channels (precommit + measure + balance)
are the union of its parents; its effect chains the sen-counter onto the destreza circle; it suits the slow long
poleaxe better than either parent alone. A new technique, no school ever taught it, and it is *world-logical* — which
is exactly your "semi-realistic but world-logical builds."

## §4 — The Troubleshooter lens (what to borrow)

Troubleshooter: Abandoned Children runs one of the deepest published mastery systems; four pieces map cleanly onto
Valoria:

- **Double-bounded budget per category.** Each category caps both a *slot count* (~7) and a *training-point total*
  (~10); each mastery costs 0–3 points (some cost a slot but 0 points). This is the competence budget I proposed,
  *enriched*: two bound axes (slots + points) give finer build texture while staying bounded. A proven shape.
- **Sets that auto-activate from their components.** A set is a named combination of base masteries; when you have
  **all** components equipped, the set effect turns **on automatically** — "like item-sets," no activation button,
  the components just light up. *This resolves the declared-vs-emergent set tension:* the combination→capability is
  authored, but **activation is emergent from the loadout** — you don't "trigger" a set, you *assemble* it and it
  becomes true. The middle path.
- **Discovery through studying opponents.** Sets can have *hidden* components you uncover by maxing information on
  the enemies who use them. This resonates hard with the corpus's own axiom that **knowledge-of-others is the deep
  hook**: you learn a master's set/technique by *studying them in the field*, and that study is gameplay.
- **Craft-to-unlock-new.** Combining the right masteries *unlocks a new mastery* (the "+" recipes). This is the
  published precedent for the innovation mechanic — Valoria's version is just more generative (the synthesis is
  composed from mastered parents under circumstance, rather than bought at a bench).

*(Also notable: masteries are acquired everywhere — defeating a foe can drop their method, classes unlock their own,
story grants others. The Valoria analog: defeat or study a master → learn their technique; train → raise channels;
experiment → innovate.)*

---

## §5 — Where this leaves the set question

Adopt the **auto-activation** middle path. A coherence set in Valoria = a combination of slotted techniques that,
when all present, turns on an emergent capability — assembled, not triggered. The combination→capability mapping can
be **authored** (a designed set, Troubleshooter-style) *or* itself **emerge** from shared-channel concentration (the
pure bottom-up form from the reframe) — and these can coexist: authored sets for signature doctrines, emergent
coherence everywhere else, synthesis as the generative crown. The activation is emergent in all cases (the loadout
makes it true), which keeps it inside the bottom-up discipline.

## §6 — Honesty / decisions

- **The honest seam:** Troubleshooter's sets are *authored* combinations (designer content) that *activate*
  emergently. So "auto-activating sets" is a **middle path**, not pure bottom-up — the combo→effect authoring is real
  content work and a mild top-down element. You can accept it (authored signature sets + emergent coherence
  elsewhere) or hold the pure line (only shared-channel emergence). Flagged, your call.
- **The synthesis rule needs care.** A generative composition must yield *sane* child techniques (channels that
  blend coherently, effects that chain legally through the engine's resolution). This is a real design + sim task —
  the composition rule must be constrained so it cannot mint degenerate children. `[GAP: unvalidated]`.
- **Decisions:** (a) the budget shape — single competence budget, or Troubleshooter's slots + points per category?
  (b) sets — authored, pure-emergent, or the coexistence above? (c) the innovation **trigger** — what "circumstance"
  is, concretely? (d) the **composition rule** for synthesized children, and its guardrails. (e) discovery — do you
  learn a foe's technique by studying them (the knowledge-of-others hook)? (f) the sim plan: free + synthesized
  builds, coherent strong / scattered weak, no dominant profile (±2–3 pp, N≈3000).
- **Mechanical, not creative;** historical names stay anchored, mechanical/synthesized fill graded `M`. The
  tradition names remain historical descriptors and templates — never gates.

# Faction-Action Audit - Valoria

_Date: 2026-06-22 | Method: multi-agent audit (3 workflow runs) | 47 findings_

**Scope:** the faction-unique action mechanics across canon design, `params/bg/`, and the v17/v18 simulation
code, plus faction-specific tactic cards and the settlement faction-integration modules.

**Note on process:** the adversarial verify stage was repeatedly hit by transient server-side rate-limiting
(not usage limits). Pass A's verifiers largely failed, so its findings were re-verified by hand; Pass B was
re-scoped with lower fan-out and its verifiers completed normally.

## Executive summary

The faction-action surface spans **three layers that were authored independently and never cross-mapped**:
canon design (`faction_canon_v30 §9`, `part10`, `victory_v30`, `mass_battle_v30`), `params/bg/`, and sim code -
where the sim itself has **two generations** (the `tests/sim/v17-integration/m6_*` *primitives* and the live
`sim/provincial/*` v18 modules). The defects cluster into seven themes:

1. **The layers describe disjoint action sets (CRITICAL).** Canon §9 names one signature action per faction
   (Crown=Royal Decree, Church=Excommunication, Hafenmark=Sovereign Authority Doctrine, Varfell=The Private
   Collection, Guilds=Economic Leverage, Löwenritter=Martial Law/Coup). BG-params defines a different catalogue
   (Piety Spread, Active Inquisition, Royal Charter, ...). m6/sim implement a third set (Crown Initiative,
   Council of Solmund, Absolution, Charter of Liberties, Vaynard's Hall, ...). **Only "Excommunication" is common
   to all three.**

2. **The sim's flagship actions are unratified proposals (CRITICAL).** Crown Initiative, Council of Solmund,
   Charter of Liberties and Vaynard's Hall exist only as Part-10 "sketched proposals" (§5) / "to formalize"
   (§3); §6.1's ratification step (write specs into `faction_actions.md` + a PP entry) was never done - yet m6
   implements them with full tables and the live dispatcher even *wires* Council/Crown Initiative.

3. **m6 primitives drifted from ratified canon (CRITICAL/HIGH).** `royal_progress_ob` still uses the formula
   ED-840 explicitly struck (gets *harder* as Accord rises); its tests lock the struck behavior in;
   excommunication comments quote canon wording deleted by LPS-2e; Absolution OW grants +2 vs canon's +1.

4. **Live dispatch is incomplete & internally inconsistent (CRITICAL/HIGH).** Varfell & Hafenmark hard-return
   `'invalid'` (root cause of the skewed win-share sweep); the action-mix marginals can't equal the documented
   30/35/20/15; `_degree()` contradicts the canonical Ob-relative degree model; the docstring's Church priority
   order contradicts the code (and itself).

5. **BG-params hygiene (HIGH).** The entire PP-428-442 block is duplicated verbatim; the "Varfell is
   military-only" framing is stale (corrected by ED-880); Active Inquisition rolls against a "territory
   Stability" quantity that doesn't exist at the BG layer; several citation collisions (PP-555->PP-559,
   PP-320-ED, PP-675).

6. **Tactic cards §B.4 & integration modules (HIGH/MEDIUM).** `inquisitors_mark` is double-categorized
   (hooks *and* a flat pool delta); `mercenary_surge`/`assassination` conflate units with dice; the
   StabilityTrigger enum is mislabeled and omits Triggers 2-5; module_13 applies the CI cap to an
   Invasion-Pressure track; a faction-standing binding is imported but never invoked.

7. **Naming (MEDIUM/LOW).** m6 comments say "Varfell's Hall" (canon: "Vaynard's Hall"); `__init__.py` lists
   modules by struck prior names; a §A.7/§B.4 citation slip.

**Verification:** every Critical and High finding was independently confirmed - by paired adversarial skeptics
where the verify stage survived rate-limiting, and by direct file re-reads for the rest. Items still tagged
"evidenced (verifier rate-limited)" are Medium/Low findings that carry exact citations but were not
re-checked by a second pass.

### [HIGH] Action-mix marginals are NOT 30/35/20/15: single shared roll + fall-through over-weights lower bands and starves the unique slot
- **Status:** verified (self-checked vs files) | **Layer:** `sim_code` | **Dimension:** sim-dispatch
- **Discrepancy:** The module docstring asserts a clean '30% faction-unique | 35% Conquest | 20% Muster | 15% Govern' mix. The implementation uses ONE roll = rng.random() and a cumulative-if cascade where each band is gated by the SAME roll, with fall-through on 'invalid'. The stated marginals only hold if every band is always valid. They are not: when a band returns 'invalid' its probability mass spills into the NEXT band down (not redistributed across all bands), because control reaches `if roll < <next>` with the same roll still satisfying it. Concretely: a roll in [0.30,0.65) can NEVER reach unique (skipped) and a roll in [0.65,0.85) can never reach unique or conquest. So unique is only ever attempted on roll<0.30; if unique is invalid (always, for Varfell/Hafenmark), that whole 0..0.30 band collapses into Conquest, yielding Conquest≈65% / Muster≈20% / Govern≈15% / unique 0% for those factions, vs 30/35/20/15 for a faction whose unique always resolves. Conquest itself is invalid when no hostile adjacency or Mil<3, in which case its mass spills further into Muster, then Govern. Govern is the unconditional sink. The bands can therefore be both starved (unique) and over-weighted (Conquest/Govern); the documented marginals are wrong whenever any earlier band can be invalid.
- **Evidence:** sim/provincial/faction_action.py:9-10 docstring `v17 port: probabilistic action mix (M7_ASSUMPTION_SIX).\n30% faction-unique | 35% Conquest | 20% Muster | 15% Govern.`  ||  faction_action.py:55-77 `roll = rng.random()` then `if roll < 0.30: ... # fall through`, `if roll < 0.65: ...`, `if roll < 0.85: ...`, final `return _try_govern(...)` — all four gated by the one `roll`.  ||  Independent canon-analysis confirms the skew: designs/audit/2026-06-06-integration/win_computation_compilation.md:39 `a **sequential-`if` cascade** (not `elif`)` and :45 `Varfell/Hafenmark attempt Conquest on `roll<0.65` ≈ 65% of seasons; Crown/Church only on `[0.30,0.65)` ≈ 35%`.
- **Canon:** The intent IS conditional, per tests/sim/v17-integration/m7_sim_verification_ledger.json:230 (M7_ASSUMPTION_SIX) `Probabilistic mix: 30% faction-unique action when favorable; 35% Conquest if adjacent hostile + Mil >= 3; 20% Muster; 15% Govern.` — i.e. canon explicitly qualifies the unique/Conquest bands as 'when favorable' / 'if adjacent hostile + Mil>=3'. The module docstring (faction_action.py:10) drops those qualifiers and presents the numbers as unconditional marginals, which is the misleading claim. Sweep evidence the marginals don't hold: ledger:187 `Wilson 95% CI: Crown [64.74, 70.53], Church [0.41, 1.57], Hafenmark [1.14, 2.83], Varfell [26.95, 32.61] ... all_factions_in_band=False`.
- **Suggested fix:** Either (a) correct the docstring to state these are band thresholds with fall-through (and that realized marginals depend on per-band validity), or (b) if true 30/35/20/15 marginals are wanted, re-roll within each band (independent selection) or redistribute an invalid band's mass proportionally rather than always pushing it to the next lower band. At minimum, stop documenting unconditional marginals that the cascade cannot produce.

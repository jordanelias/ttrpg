# VALORIA — All-Directions Adversarial Design Critique

## 1. CENTRAL THESIS

Valoria's design axiom — *the world happens to the player and the player to the world; there is ALWAYS churn* — is implemented as its own negation. The simulation faithfully renders **terminal states** (battle-win → instant `t.owner` swap; treaty → 0.90 lapse-expiration; Stability 0 → eliminated) and systematically deletes the **intermediate state objects** that canon designs precisely so that consequences must *negotiate* before they resolve: the occupation marker (faction_layer §2.2–§2.5), the three-phase treaty + guarantor (§3.3), and the §5.4 motion catalogue. The deep failure is architectural, not a list of stubs: **churn lives in persistent intermediate objects, and an echo can only propagate through an object that exists for long enough to be read by a second system.** Where Valoria deletes the object (occupation window, guarantor binding, multi-season pledge) the branch point and its cross-system echo orphan *together* — you cannot resist, treaty-cede, or recapture what has already changed hands, and no settlement/NPC/victory layer can read a transition that happened atomically. Where Valoria *keeps* the object but never wires the read (occupation pressure vs §3.3 Phase 2; per-settlement Excommunication vs the saturating Mandate aggregate; `bind_faction_standing_delta` defined-but-uncalled), the echo is computed and then discarded at the seam. The genre cognate is exact and damning because Valoria's own infill cites it: **EU4's occupation→warscore→peace-deal loop**, where holding (occupied) is mechanically distinct from owning (controlled) and the entire war exhaustion / separate-peace / rebellion churn lives *in the interval* — Valoria implemented only the endpoints. The result is not the designed multi-actor drama but a Crown steamroll (and the headline 67.7% win-share is itself an artifact, not evidence — see Theme F).

## 2. CROSS-CUTTING THEMES

**A. The deleted interval (occupation as the canonical case).** This is the spine of the whole critique and recurred across every lens. `faction_action.py:_try_conquest` sets `t.owner` on any winning degree (the degree `deg` is computed but never gates the transfer) and applies a flat `adjust_accord(-25)`, collapsing §2.2's degree gate (only **Overwhelming** = immediate; **Success** = marker, control retained), §2.4's 3-season clock, §2.5's Resistance check (M = Influence − (⌊opp Mandate/2⌋+1)), the §2.2 Fort march-Ob table (terrain=None → Lowenskyst Fort 3/Ob 5 ignored), and the §2.3 garrison −1 pool / −1 Wealth holding tax. **Precedent:** EU4 — a sieged province accrues warscore and pays attrition/garrison/revolt-risk *before* the peace transfers title; presence never equals ownership. Valoria's §2-INF cites France-in-Milan 1499 (Sforza fled, the bureaucracy kept functioning, control required a separate administrative act) as the literal rationale for the window — and the sim deletes the thing the infill built. Adjudication of a lens disagreement here: the "snowball runs *undamped*" framing (systems-feedback lens) overstated it — §1.4 FSS-LOOP-1 and §5.7 FSS-LOOP-2 are live graduated governors. The correct charge is narrower and sharper (exploit lens): conquest is missing its **marginal-cost curve** (the §2.3 garrison drag is the load-bearing brake the sim drops), not its existence; conquest *is* gated (Mil≥3, hostile-adjacent, must win a routed mass-battle).

**B. Persistence is the precondition of betrayal (the guarantor / Lodi failure).** A relationship that is never recorded as first-class state can never be betrayed, so the entire betrayal-saga generator is dead. `treaty.py:propose_treaty` raises `NotImplementedError`; `register_treaty` persists **no guarantor binding**; only expiration exists. Even the generic §3.3 guarantor that *is* specified is a bilateral terminal transaction (+1 to ratifier M; flat guarantor Mandate −1 on failure-to-sanction) whose breach cost never recurses into the guarantor's **own** internal cohesion — officer Conviction (§1.5), §5.3 vote weight — and §4.1 Alliance *casus foederis* is named-but-unspecified. **Precedent:** Peace of Lodi 1454 / Italic League — held ~40 years because each signatory's failure to enforce damaged its standing with *all* others (a self-policing equilibrium), and collapsed in 1494 precisely when guarantor Sforza invited Charles VIII in and no guarantor would enforce against a French army. Valoria's §3-INF cites Lodi by name and then implements a +1 die that does none of the self-policing — the design's own cited precedent indicts its own mechanic.

**C. The faction sheet is write-protected against the rest of the game (echo channels exist but are physically disconnected).** Two confirmed seam failures compound into one: (i) `module_12`'s `StabilityTrigger` enum encodes only four sub-rows of Trigger 1 plus the SUPPRESS_FAILURE exception (which §0 explicitly states is *not* one of the five triggers) — Triggers 2 (Unfavourable Treaty), 3 (Antagonistic Vote), 4 (Subterfuge), 5 (Failed Engagement) are absent, and the test asserts `count==5` against the wrong contents; (ii) `bind_faction_standing_delta` / `FactionDeltaBinding` — the function whose own docstring says it "translate[s] ActionResult signals … into faction-stat-sheet mutations" — is imported at `module_13:110` and **never called**. With no treaty/motion subsystem to emit M3–M11 deltas *and* the only binding conduit un-invoked, the faction can structurally only remember "I lost dirt" — not betrayal, condemnation, treachery, or rout. **Precedent:** Dwarf Fortress's post-2018 stress model records distinct event *types* with provenance, each propagating differently; collapsing them to one channel erases the emergent psychology. Valoria's §1-INF Venice rationale is verbatim this point — Venice lost battles constantly yet stayed stable; destabilization comes from *specific, categorised shocks* — and §5-INF's Gaston de Foix at Ravenna 1512 is a Trigger-5 officer-loss event the enum literally cannot represent. **Severity calibration (adjudicating the immersive-sim lens against the audit):** the binding is the highest-*leverage* fix (one un-called function, the trunk line every other echo rides) but the audit rates it MEDIUM; the Trigger-2/5 enum gap is the higher-*severity* of the pair because it is the memory channel itself.

**D. Mandate is triple-counted across three layers with no negotiating seam (the rotten-borough core).** Mandate gates **vote weight** (§5.3), **treaty ratification** (§3.3 M = Mandate − 2), *and* **victory suppression** (victory_v30 §0 effective-hegemony requires rival Mandate ≤ 1; §3 rival-suppression thresholds) — so a Mandate lead compounds across all three simultaneously, with no echo-balancing rule at any seam. Adjudication of the strongest lens disagreement in the set: the "one faction monopolizes Parliament" charge is **refuted in its strong form** — §5.3 has the targeted faction abstain (the dominant faction's Mandate is removed from the denominator when *it* is the subject), supermajority gates on the dangerous motions (Outlawry needs proposer Mandate 5 + ≥60%), §5.5 Rebuttal, and the Sacred Veto. The **surviving** structural defect: for *majority-tier* motions (Censure, Embargo, Subsidy, War Auth, Treaty Ratification) raw Mandate-weighting makes the canonically-highest-Mandate Crown a near-permanent kingmaker who carries a Censure with one ally, with no Venetian anti-incumbency/rotation lever. **Precedent:** the Venetian Maggior Consiglio + promissione ducale + multi-stage ballot dispersed dynastic capture (versus the Florentine signoria's vulnerability to Medici concentration); the Reform Act 1832 rotten-borough fight is apt for the *territorial* factions (flat global Mandate vote) but **not** for Church — whose §5.3 vote = Mandate + ⌊CI/20⌋ already couples clout to held pious territory, a genuine Victoria-3-style IG-clout-from-substrate echo. The coupling is real but per-faction ad hoc, one-directional (§5.6b lets motions *degrade* Accord but Accord never feeds *back* into vote weight), and split across two rival vote models.

**E. The substrate→legitimacy chain is correct at the *event* but blind during the *interval* and lossy at the *grain*.** Adjudicating the domain-echo lens's biggest correction: the "two independent rollups; faction layer only feeds Accord; Mandate unchanged when Crown loses provinces" framing is **wrong on the load-bearing fact** — per settlement_layer §1.8 (LPS-2e), `Mandate = clamp(round(7·T/(T+K)),0,7)` with `T = Σ_s W_s·(q_s/7)` over *controlled settlements*, so Mandate is a **pure derived aggregate**: lose control of a settlement and it drops out of T automatically. The genuine gaps are narrower and three-fold: (i) **interval-blind** — during the 3-season occupation window control has *not* transferred, so the occupied faction votes/ratifies at undiminished Mandate under bayonets (the EU4 occupied-but-not-annexed-still-counts problem); (ii) **grain-lossy on Excommunication** — canon (faction_canon §9:626) specifies −1 L *to each controlled settlement*, which must round-trip through the saturating `T/(T+K)` form, but the sim collapses it to a flat faction −1, **inverting target selection** (a flat −1 craters a one-province upstart and barely dents a peninsula-spanning Crown deep in the saturating tail — the exact inverse of the intended weapon geometry); (iii) **grain-lossy on cession** — the gaining side's Order is *set to a province constant* (Capitulation→1/Peace→2), bulldozing inherited per-settlement Order, *inconsistent with the design's own §2.4c Prosperity-inherits-unchanged precedent*. **Precedent:** Victoria 3 IG strength is a population-weighted aggregate that never collapses to a flat group delta; historically the papal interdict bit by base size and cohesion — it broke Henry IV (fragmented German magnate base, Canossa 1077) but glanced off Venice in 1606 (broad, cohesive base). The magnitude *was* the base-size echo.

**F. The evidence base is contaminated, so quantitative claims must be quarantined.** `faction_action.py:128-129` hard-returns `'invalid'` for Varfell/Hafenmark unique actions inside a single-RNG *sequential-if* cascade (not elif, no per-band re-roll), so their 30% unique slot spills into the next band (Conquest), giving them ~65% Conquest vs Crown/Church's ~35% and producing Crown 67.7 / Varfell 29.7 / Hafenmark 1.8 / Church 0.8. This means it is a **2-of-4-faction game**, and the 67.7% Crown win-share — which several lenses reached for as proof of the snowball — must be embargoed as evidence until all four unique loops fire. **Precedent:** COIN (Fire in the Lake / A Distant Plain) and Root keep every faction's distinct action menu always live; a faction whose signature loop is dark is a *missing* faction, not a weak one. Adjudication: the dispatch block does **not** itself starve the §1.5/GD-3 collapse→emergence pathway (collapse fires on Stability 0 via Trigger consequences regardless of unique-action use); it distorts the multi-actor *ecology* that produces interesting collapses — so the precise mechanism is "denies two factions identity + over-weights Conquest," not "starves emergence."

## 4. PRIORITIZED RECOMMENDATIONS (by leverage)

**P0 — Quarantine the evidence and unblock the roster (Theme F).** Wire Hafenmark→Charter of Liberties and Varfell→Vaynard's Hall from the existing validated m6 primitives; OR if the Pass-2d/2e contamination block must hold, replace the *silent* fall-through with a logged `unique_unavailable` and redistribute the 30% within-band (independent per-band roll) so the mix is not silently dumped into Conquest. **Embargo every win-share number until all four unique loops fire.** *Precedent:* COIN/Root always-live asymmetric menus. This is first because every other quantitative claim is downstream of it.

**P1 — Restore the occupation interval as a first-class marker (Themes A, E; map #1, #3).** Read `deg` before transfer; gate immediate transfer to Overwhelming only; on Success place the marker, start the §2.4 3-season clock, apply §2.3 garrison −1 pool + −1 Wealth/territory (the rising marginal cost), and run §2.5 Resistance as a free Accounting action. Replace flat `adjust_accord(-25)` with the branch-differentiated canon Accord (1 auto / 2 treaty / restored Overwhelming / max(pre−1,1) Success-recapture). Decrement occupied-settlement L_s −1/Accounting so Mandate erodes through §1.8 during the window. Pass real terrain so Fort march-Ob fires. Each Resistance resolution is eligible to mint a named local resister/collaborator NPC. *Precedent:* EU4 occupation→warscore→peace-deal loop; Milan 1499 (the design's own §2-INF anchor). **Highest structural leverage** — it is the canonical churn case and re-opens four orphaned echoes at once.

**P2 — Wire the two dead echo conduits (Theme C; map #13, and the enum behind it).** Add Trigger 2/3/4/5 as first-class `StabilityTrigger` members; fix the test to assert membership against the canonical five, not `count==5`; relabel SUPPRESS_FAILURE as the §0 exception it is. Then call `bind_faction_standing_delta` in module_13's per-season consumption, routing typed deltas through those members. *Precedent:* Dwarf Fortress typed-event memory; Venice's "categorised shocks" (§1-INF). **One un-called function + four enum members from working** — the trunk line every other inbound echo rides.

**P3 — Persist the guarantor and make the guarantee a standing obligation (Theme B; map #4, #5).** Implement the §3.3 formation resolver (Positioning M=Inf−Inf; Concession AI-pick over the §3.3 categories; Ratification M=Mandate−2) and store the **guarantor binding as first-class TreatyRecord state** so a breach fires the guarantor's forced dilemma (sanction or Mandate −1) in a later Accounting. Make the guarantee tick a small credibility gain each season it holds; on failure-to-sanction echo −1 officer disposition (§1.5) + reduced §5.3 vote weight into the guarantor itself; add the Sforza defection premium (full breach cost + CB to all). Specify §4.1 *casus foederis*. Reconcile treaty.py (treaty_expiration_v30) with §3.3 into one formation→expiration lifecycle. *Precedent:* Peace of Lodi 1454 self-policing web; CK3 alliance/obligation cascade.

**P4 — Kill the duplicate vote model and generalize substrate-coupling; close the loop (Theme D; map #7).** Ratify §5.3 (Mandate-weighted tally + Sacred Veto + CI weighting) as canonical for faction-layer motions; relegate social_contest §10 to the §4.2 Grand Debate scene only, feeding back as the §3.3 ratification ±M modifier. Generalize the CI-weighting *shape* to all factions: effective vote weight = Mandate modified by governed-substrate (+ for territories at Accord ≥2, − for Revolt/occupied), closing the §5.6b one-directional Accord echo into a bounded loop (bound it against the §1.4 death-spiral lesson). Add a Venetian anti-incumbency lever (a faction that carried the last N motions takes −1 vote weight) and cap single-faction majority-tier weight (≈ min(Mandate, median+1)). Make the Phase-3 Ratification Ob **term-sensitive** (rise with ceded-territory count / indemnity) so a humiliating-but-Mandate-comfortable deal can die at home — restoring the Senate-vs-ambassador churn without re-introducing the ED-865/874 84%-self-veto wall. *Precedent:* Venetian Maggior Consiglio dispersion; Victoria 3 IG clout-from-substrate; the Venetian Senate routinely repudiating its ambassadors' terms.

**P5 — Give CB an object and make unpressed claims persist (Theme A across time; map #6).** Every CB names a target territory that bounds Phase-2 cession and re-anchors the dangling "Recognition" clause; unpressed CBs persist as latent non-expiring Claims re-armed by a triggering event, seeding future §3.3 negotiations and §5.4 Recognition Challenges. *Precedent:* CK3 pressed/unpressed claims — the transferable lesson is **persistence**, not dynasticism, so the 20–40-season campaign-clock defense does not cover it.

**P6 — Implement the §5.4 motion suite + §5.5 Rebuttal reversal + Sacred Veto; fix Excommunication grain and the AE accumulator (Themes C, D, E; map #7, #9, #10).** Implement §5.5's accuse→rebut→verdict resolver so the Overwhelming reversal (proposer Mandate −1, target Stability +1, accused vindicated) mints a durable reputational marker the NPC/victory layers read; implement the §5.4 catalogue so the §5.6b legitimacy-erosion loop fires (re-keyed to the *sustained state*, map #9); add the seam-arbitration dedup rule (map #10); implement Excommunication as the per-settlement write it canonically is and compose it with Outlawry (map #7); add an **Aggressive-Expansion accumulator** (incrementing on the Trigger-1 occupation events already counted) that auto-arms the *existing* PP-296 coalition suppression against a runaway and feeds the §0 effective-hegemony check (a faction over the AE threshold cannot count rival territories as "institutionally dominated"). *Precedent:* Disco Elysium white-check-writes-back; Julius II's 1512 Lateran nullification of Pisa (Sacred Veto = declaring deliberation void, not arguing merits); League of Cambrai 1508 (AE coalition forms against the *strongest* with no dominant-proposer gate — the one place §5.4 Outlawry's Mandate-5 requirement genuinely inverts balance-of-power logic).

**P7 — Fix the canon contradiction under Great Work, then add its window (Theme A; map #11).** Reconcile part10 §3.3 vs PP-515 *first* (the single root cause); author a distinct multi-season Project Pledge with a live breach-exposure window rivals can target; flag crown_initiative.py's instant-resolve as a known balance distortion. *Precedent:* Through the Ages / Brass: Birmingham — multi-turn commitments derive value from being interruptible. Lowest leverage (Crown-only, moderate severity) and blocked on a canon decision, so it sequences last.

**Do NOT** add a parallel "Leverage"/warscore scalar (Stability/Wealth already price occupation), a faction-scalar Mandate decrement (breaks §1.8 size-weighting), or touch FSS-LOOP-1/-2 (sound and graduated — the floor is the fix; its environment, Themes A and D, is the bug).

## 5. COMPLETENESS CRITIC — under-developed angles

_Under-weighted by the six lenses: Succession, the Stability feedback loop, CI as a cross-system echo carrier, and victory-condition echoes. The infill already cites Venice/Cambrai/Lodi/Ravenna/Pavia, partly self-grading the historical lens._


**Succession (faction_succession_split_v30) churn-engine**
- *Sharper critique:* Contests open only on leader eliminated (2.2), so officer death/Outlawry/collapse cannot fire one; no anticipatory pretender object; 2.4/2.5/1.5 fragmentation regimes conflict with no precedence rule. Fix: let those events open contests; add a standing Claimant token; unify fragmentation.
- *Precedent:* CKII/III partition succession; HRE elective succession (1356 Golden Bull; 1618 Bohemian double-election).

**Stability cascade as a re-plumbed feedback loop**
- *Sharper critique:* The 1.4 fold-back resolver is faked in sim (audit T40); the deterministic Stab<=2 floor removed passive collapse so factions park at 1-2 with no autonomous churn. Fix: build the 1.4 resolver; add bounded decay below the floor.
- *Precedent:* Frostpunk discontent spiral; Democracy 4 feedback web; FSS-LOOP-1/2 loop-surgery.

**CI as an echo-carrier that triple-counts and only flows up**
- *Sharper critique:* CI read three times (vote 5.3, Seizure pool, victory clock; divisors 20/30/15) with no shared fan-out cap; no faction-to-scene CI echo. Fix: combined fan-out cap; downward CI echo.
- *Precedent:* Reigns four coupled meters; CKIII Dread/Stress carrier-stats.

**Parliament lacks power-of-the-purse and agenda control**
- *Sharper critique:* All 5.4 motions are one-shot punitive, never renewable supply-for-redress; agenda is flat (only Church vetoes; PP-526 auto-bloc hard-coded); 5.6 firewalls Parliament from CI. Fix: a renewable supply motion; an agenda-setter; let Mandate damage echo into CI.
- *Precedent:* English power of the purse (1628 Petition of Right; 1641 Grand Remonstrance); Reichstag three-college assent.

**Victory echoes + dead occupation leverage**
- *Sharper critique:* GD-1 left 3.1-3.6 indicators as NOT-endpoints nothing reads; no conquest-raises-doom-clock trade; immediate transfer kills the occupy-to-bargain bluff 3.3 cede-Occupation-marker presupposes. Fix: wire indicators to AI; add a conquest-vs-survival trade; restore the occupation marker.
- *Precedent:* Suzerain consequence variables; Twilight Struggle DEFCON; EU4 occupation-to-peace bluff loop.

# Evidence Base for the Dueling-Combat / Martial-Traditions Corpus

This document fixes the defect identified in the critical review and previously flagged in the blunt-weapons self-review: **source-quality heterogeneity was hidden behind uniform `[Source: high]` tags.** Wikis, blogs, federation pages, and tourism sites were graded alongside peer-reviewed monographs. This establishes (1) an explicit source-quality hierarchy distinct from the per-tradition grading the two were conflated; (2) the authoritative anchor sources that should have been cited; (3) an honest per-claim re-grade that downgrades or retracts claims resting only on tertiary content; and (4) a correction of the anglophone-bias thesis to its defensible form.

It is consistent with the evidence-hierarchy correction established earlier in this research line: the *findings* (which sources exist, what they say) mostly stand; what was wrong was the *weighting*. This is a re-stratification, not a new literature search.

---

# Part 1 — Two gradings, now separated

The prior documents used one axis where two were needed.

**Axis A — per-tradition evidentiary richness** (the existing `[Source: ; Interpretive:]` scheme): how well-attested the *tradition* is — do manuals survive, are there critical editions, can disputes be adjudicated against texts. This was defined correctly in the deep revision (European corpus *high*: manuals survive, modern critical editions, active scholarship; Mamluk furūsiyya *medium*: texts survive but vocabulary granularity lags, much untranslated; reconstructed/oral traditions *low–medium*). Retained.

**Axis B — per-claim source quality** (the missing axis): the tier of the source *actually cited for a given assertion*. A tradition can be evidentiarily rich (Axis A high) while a specific claim about it rests on a blog (Axis B low). The prior documents never tracked Axis B, so a Wikipedia paraphrase and a Yale University Press monograph both surfaced as "Source: high."

### The source-quality tiers (S1–S5)

- **S1 — Primary source / critical edition / manuscript facsimile.** The QDL al-Aqsarāʾī manuscript; Novati's 1902 *Flos* edition; the AGEA critical editions; primary texts on ctext.org; the *Muyedobotongji* itself; Dennis's Greek editions of the Byzantine treatises.
- **S2 — Peer-reviewed scholarship.** University-press monographs and refereed journal articles. The field's genuine backbone (Part 2).
- **S3 — Specialist reference / curated transcription.** Wiktenauer (superb transcriptions, *not* peer-reviewed); national scholarly encyclopedias (*Historisches Lexikon Bayerns*, the Korean *Encyclopedia of Korean Culture*, *Encyclopaedia Iranica*, *Real Academia de la Historia*).
- **S4 — General reference.** Wikipedia (any language), Britannica.
- **S5 — Tertiary / non-scholarly.** Blogs, federation and club sites, tourism boards, commercial/vendor pages, Q&A and forum posts.

**Grading rule (the rule that was missing):** *a claim's `Source` grade cannot exceed what its best cited source supports.* A claim resting only on S4/S5 cannot be `Source: high`; at most `Source: low–medium`, flagged for an S1/S2 anchor. "Source: high" requires an S1 or S2 source, or convergent S3.

# Part 4 — Correcting the anglophone-bias thesis

The review's sharpest charge stands and is conceded here. Docs 04–07 framed source-language search as recovering what English "systematically misses," but much of the strongest material *is* in English — and at higher source-quality than several of the foreign-language web pages that supposedly corrected it:

- The German *Fechtbuch* scholarship is summarized and translated in English (Forgeng; Anglo Ch. on the German masters; Wiktenauer's English apparatus).
- The furūsiyya authority (al-Sarraf 2004) is **in English**, open-access.
- Chinese staff/Qi Jiguang myth-criticism (Shahar 2008) is **in English**, Harvard.
- Kalaripayattu's definitive study (Zarrilli 1998) is **in English**, Oxford.
- *Le Jeu de la Hache* has an English-language foundational study (Anglo, 1991, *Archaeologia*).

**The defensible, narrower thesis:** source-language search adds three things, and only these — (1) **primary-text and critical-edition access** (ctext, QDL, AGEA, Novati) that English summaries abstract from; (2) **internal skeptical/native discourse** (the Polish reconstruction debate; Japanese *budō-gaku* source-criticism) that is real but largely S3–S5; and (3) **coverage of traditions with no English monograph at all** (Polish szabla, Hungarian sabre, matrak, krabi-krabong). What it does *not* do is relocate the evidentiary backbone away from peer-reviewed scholarship — which remains disproportionately English-language. The corollary undercuts a doc-04 framing: "multilingual is where the real evidence is" is false; **multilingual is where the breadth and the primary-language texture are; the reliability is in the S2 literature, much of it English.**

# Part 5 — Honest disclosure of what remains unverified

`[UNVERIFIED this session]`
- **Shahar 2008, Zarrilli 1998, Desch-Obi 2008, Dennis (Byzantine editions), Forgeng, Jaquet/Brill 2016, Jaser & Israel 2014** are cited from established bibliographic knowledge, not re-opened this session. They are canonical and pre-cutoff, but the *specific* attributions mapped to them above should be checked against the texts before any claim is treated as S2-anchored. Until then they are "S2-expected," not S2-confirmed.
- The **prior corpus documents (01, the deep revision, the throughlines doc) were not re-read**; their structural content in doc 07 remains reconstructed from conversation-search snippets. The evidence base does not repair that — doc 07's provenance defect stands until those files are opened.
- The per-claim mapping above is to source *type/author*, not to verified page numbers; it establishes *which authority should ground each claim*, which is the precondition for a valid evidence base, not the completed citation apparatus.
- **No physical/forensic evidence stream** (skeletal trauma, metallurgy, typology) has been incorporated — the same gap the blunt-weapons self-review flagged (e.g., the Towton 1461 osteological dataset for blunt-force). A complete evidence base would add it as an independent leg rather than resting on texts and images.

A valid evidence base now exists in the sense that matters: every load-bearing claim has been assigned a source tier, the inflated grades have been brought down to what their sources support, the genuine S1/S2 authorities are named, and the traditions lacking any scholarly anchor are identified as the priority gaps. The corpus's reliable core is smaller than docs 04–07 implied, and it is concentrated where peer-reviewed scholarship exists. *[Source: high; Interpretive: high — this is a statement about the corpus's own sourcing, which is directly inspectable.]*

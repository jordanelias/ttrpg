# Edge-Playability Audit — Charter

## Status: PROPOSED (audit grounding — Jordan review)
## Date: 2026-07-05 · Lane: IN (cross-cutting) · Branch: `claude/fable5-playability-audit-y5wj43`

## Mandate

Read-only Fable-led audit evaluating **playability and gameplay experience at every
edge / common surface / linkage among gameplay subsystems, across all scales and in all
directions.** Where the 2026-07-04 NERS audit (`designs/audit/2026-07-04-ners-qualitative-audit/`,
PR #77) judged subsystems and corpus-level throughlines, this audit takes the **seam** as the unit
of analysis: what the player does, sees, decides, and can trace when consequence crosses from one
subsystem or scale into another.

## Relationship to the NERS audit (division of labor)

- **#77 asked:** does each subsystem serve the North Star; do the throughlines hold; is the corpus
  cohesive. Its cohesion lens (§6) flattened edges but verdicted them as *transport conformance*
  (declared/consumed/targeted).
- **This audit asks:** for each edge, is the *crossing itself* good gameplay — a moment, a decision,
  legible feedback at a dramatically apt latency, tolerable friction, and reciprocity — regardless
  of whether the plumbing conforms.
- #77's confirmed findings (F-1..F-5), corpus signals (S-1/S-2), refutations (R-1..R-6), and its
  charter's KNOWN calibration list are **inherited as calibration**: re-reporting them as
  discoveries is an audit failure. They may be *reframed* where the playability consequence at a
  seam is new information.

## Criteria (unchanged from #77 — the corpus's own instruments)

(threadwork at every juncture).

## The rubric (applied to every edge)

1. **MOMENT** — what does the player actually do/see when this edge fires?
2. **DECISION** — is there a meaningful player decision at/across the seam, or does it fire
   autonomously?
3. **FEEDBACK** — would the player know it fired; is cause→effect traceable in one sentence?
4. **LATENCY** — immediate / queued-to-Accounting / next-season; dramatically appropriate?
5. **FRICTION** — mode switch, scene budget, bookkeeping: does crossing feel like play or admin?
6. **RECIPROCITY** — does the return direction exist; if one-way, is that deliberate?
7. **VERDICT** — `PLAYS-WELL` / `PLAYS-ROUGH` (works; friction or legibility problems) / `INERT`
   (specified; no player-perceivable experience) / `MISSING` (play clearly expects a linkage that
   does not exist) / `DEGENERATE-RISK` (the seam is farmable/avoidable in a choice-collapsing way);
   + severity P1 (blocks the experience) / P2 (materially narrows) / P3 (friction) + intent gate
   `DELIBERATE` / `NOT-INTENDED` / `UNDETERMINED` with cited intent evidence.

## Direction discipline

Every edge is judged in the direction(s) it carries, and the six-axis tagging from #77's charter is
retained (top-down · bottom-up · lateral · diagonal · forwards · backwards). Cluster coverage:
bottom-up and top-down transport get dedicated clusters; lateral is split scene-scale vs
strategic-lattice vs NPC-fabric; the **rendering seam** (edge → screen) and the **temporal seam**
(mode bridge, season cadence) are audited as first-class common surfaces, since every other edge
rides them.

# Q1 — How the player affects the world

## Q1.1 Participation IS Key-emission (no parallel channel)

The player never "updates an arc." The player **plays a scene**, the scene resolves through an
ordinary subsystem engine, and the resulting outcome Keys re-enter the arc-vector's causal record.
This is C3 verbatim: *the engine books venues, never owns resolution* (charter C3). The loop:

1. A live arc-vector needs a beat. The casting resolver (§Q2.1) injects an **arc-tagged candidate**
   into the season's Scene Slate at the priority its scope/actors dictate (`player_agency_v30 §4.2`
   Steps 1/3/4/5/6).
2. The player spends one of their 3–5 scene actions on it (`player_agency_v30 §4.3`, §6.1). The
   scene resolves through the ordinary engine — contest / combat / thread / fieldwork / mass-battle
   / settlement — exactly as `player_agency_v30 §4.4` already routes it. **No arc-specific
   resolution logic exists**; the arc contributes the venue and the stakes, not the dice.
3. That engine emits its normal outcome Keys (`scene.combat_resolved`,
   `scene.investigation_resolved`, `belief_revised`, the four ED-681 thread beats, etc.).
4. `game_director` consumes those Keys, appends them to the arc-vector's `payload.ledger_cause`
   provenance and to the shared `causes[]` chain, and **steps the lifecycle FSM** — the transition
   is conditioned on the *participated outcome*, not merely on the player having shown up.

So "the player affects the world" is not a special effect: it is the ordinary Key stream, read by
the arc-vector store as first-class lifecycle input. This is what closes the aspirational gap in
`player_agency_v30 §7.6` ("Convictions … intersect with NPC arc pressures and clock pressures to
produce emergent narrative" — flagged as *prose, not a mechanism* in `03_prior_art §d`): the
intersection now has an owner (the FSM stepper, synthesis §1 L1) and a typed input (the
`arc_vector.trigger.predicate` reading fired-Key state).

**Domain Echo is the aggregate-up transport.** A personal scene with Sufficient Scope emits a
Domain Echo (`scale_transitions_v30 §4.1`, §5; `sim/cross_scale/domain_echo.py`) that lands as a
faction/territory-scale delta at Accounting. The arc-vector store READS those deltas the same way
it reads any clock (holonic containment, synthesis §1). The player's knife-fight becomes a Mandate
shift becomes a trigger-predicate crossing for a faction-tier arc — one transport, no bespoke wire.

> **Conformance (extends synthesis §10):** *participation-is-ordinary-resolution* — no arc-vector
> may carry a `resolves_via` value that is not one of the registered subsystem resolvers
> (`dice_pool | deterministic_accounting | state_reader | manifest | null`, per
> `dossier_register_formalizability` schema). An arc that resolves itself is scripting drift
> (`holonic_container_doctrine_v1 §2` failure-mode 1) and fails at compile.

## Q1.2 Reach scales with position

The *magnitude* of a player's effect is not flat; it scales with where they sit in the social
graph, and it does so through mechanisms that already exist:

| Position | Reach mechanism | Canon home |
|---|---|---|
| Operative / Agent (Standing 1–2) | Personal scenes → Domain Echo → faction deltas | `player_agency_v30 §5.1`; `scale_transitions_v30 §5` |
| Member with missions | Duties whose success/failure move faction stats at Accounting | `player_agency_v30 §3.4`, §7.2 (Phase 3) |
| Leader (Standing 6–7 / faction head) | **Delegation** — assigned beats return as owned consequence | §Q2.3 (leader ladder); `player_agency_v30 §5.2` |
| Factionless (Renown 7+) | Independent Domain Action, personal authority substitutes for institutional | `player_agency_v30 §5.3`, §5.4 |

**Leader delegation is the load-bearing case for reach.** A leader does not micromanage; they
*assign*, and the assignment returns as a consequence they own. Mechanically: a leader's ruling on
a subordinate-originated beat (§Q2.3) emits a Key that the subordinate NPC's `npc_behavior_v30 §5`
FSM consumes, whose downstream action Echoes back up. The leader "affected the world" transitively —
the arc-vector's `causes[]` chain records the delegation edge (synthesis §9, the **diagonal**
direction), so retrospect can walk from the consequence back to the ruling that authored it.

## Q1.3 Non-participation is also an input

Ignoring a summons is a **decision the world records**, never a pause. This is already canon at the
scene layer — `player_agency_v30 §4.5` ("The world does not pause for the protagonist") tabulates a
consequence for every unpursued slate source — and the arc-vector store generalizes it: an
unattended arc's lifecycle FSM **steps on its clocks with zero player Keys** (synthesis §1 L1). The
revolt you skipped resolves on garrison strength; the NPC arc-moment you missed resolves on that
NPC's Conviction (`player_agency_v30 §4.5` table); the arc converges on schedule.

Two rules make non-participation honest rather than punitive:

- **Autonomous progression is unconditional.** The FSM advances on its `activity_mode` schedule
  (`level_triggered | clock_escalation | edge_triggered_*`) whether or not the player emitted a
  Key. Non-participation is a *branch selector*, not a brake. (This is the GD-3-style autonomy the
  charter Q1 names.)
- **The watching/participating invariant is OFFER-not-outcome.** The charter (Q4 L120) requires
  every major arc to keep ≥1 open intervention point until converging. The synthesis states this as
  a `participated`-vs-`watched` counter (synthesis §4). **That counter must be satisfied by a window
  having been OFFERED — a summons fired, a slate candidate injected — NOT by the player taking it.**
  An all-ignored major arc still converges on schedule and still passes the invariant, because the
  invariant counts *offered* windows, never *taken* windows. **The same narrowing applies to the
  charter's companion clause that every major arc *routes through ≥1 playable scene* (charter Q4 L121):
  the guarantee is satisfied by a playable (slate) scene having been *offered* — an arc-tagged slate
  candidate injected at a participating surface (s3 §Q4.3) — not by the player electing it. Playability
  OFFERED, never play TAKEN**; requiring an *attended* scene would re-introduce the hold-for-the-player
  pause C7 forbids.

> **Why OFFER-not-outcome (adversarial fix, MAJOR "rubber-banding"):** if the invariant were
> satisfied only by *participation*, the engine would have to hold an arc pre-converging until the
> player engaged — a pause, i.e. rubber-banding, i.e. the railroad C7 forbids and a direct
> contradiction of charter Q1 L50–54 ("ignoring a summons is remembered, never a pause; arcs
> proceed autonomously"). Counting offered windows resolves the collision cleanly.

> **Conformance rule 6 (revises synthesis §10):** *watching-participating-invariant* — for every
> major arc, `count(offered_windows before lifecycle.state == converging) ≥ 1`. The predicate reads
> **offered** windows (summons emitted / candidate injected), never taken windows. Arcs the player
> never touched pass.

## Q1.4 The durable ledger — the world keeps a record of you

"Affecting the world" means leaving a mark the world can later read. Valoria already carries that
mark across seven existing surfaces; the engine adds no eighth (C5, no new currency). The ledger:

| Ledger surface | What it records | Canon home |
|---|---|---|
| **Convictions** | Declared stances + resolution states (Fulfilled/Failed/Transformed/Unresolved) | `player_agency_v30 §2.3` |
| **Standing** (per faction, 0–7) | Institutional trust, moved by Duty success/failure | `player_agency_v30 §3.4`, §5.1 |
| **Renown** (0–10, cross-faction) | Personal significance; persists across faction collapse | `player_agency_v30 §5.4` |
| **Obligations / debts** | Priced betrayal; owed favors | `player_agency_v30 §4.5` (Exposure, Disposition costs); pricing mode §Q2.5 |
| **Scars / Beliefs** | Belief revision + Scar accumulation (identity change) | `npc_behavior_v30 §3.2`, §3.3 |
| **Knot strain** | Relational load transmitted through the substrate | `npc_behavior_v30 §5.0b` |
| **Foreclosures** | Permanently closed possibilities (§Q2.5) | `npc_behavior_v30 §5.2` (Almud Arc A, ED-672) |
| **Chronicle** | Retrospective causal record (`causes[]` walk) | `articulation_layer_v30` Tier-3; synthesis §9 backwards |

The arc-vector store does not own any of these — it READS them as trigger-predicate operands
(holonic containment). The player's history is thereby *legible to the world*: an arc can fire
because your Standing dropped, because you carry a Scar, because a Knot is straining. That is Q1
(you changed the ledger) and Q2 (the ledger now conditions your options, §Q2.5) meeting on the same
data.

## Q1.5 Ethics as pattern, not points (P-04 hard boundary)

There is **no morality meter, no alignment axis, no hidden virtue score.** This is P-04 as a hard
constraint: *"No alignment system"* (`canon/02_canon_constraints.md` P-04). The engine reads ethics
the way a person reads a reputation — as an **accumulated pattern of behavior**, never as a scalar
the game secretly totals:

- **Convictions are the ethics ledger.** What you declared and how you resolved it
  (`player_agency_v30 §2.3`) *is* the record of what you value. There is no separate goodness
  number behind it.
- **The pattern is read by three existing readers**, not by a morality subsystem:
  `npc_memory` (the Keys→memory store, `03_prior_art §c`, resolving Disposition and recall),
  **Standing** (institutional pattern), and **Cascade/Domain Echo** (aggregate consequence).
- **Pattern-response casting** (§Q2.5, mode 4) is how ethics reaches back: an oath-breaker and a
  zealot get *different summons* because the tie-graph reads their divergent Conviction/Scar
  histories — not because one has "−3 alignment."

> **Conformance (C2 + P-04):** the C2 literal-string lint (synthesis §10 rule 4) extends to forbid
> any surfaced or internally-labelled *moral-valence scalar* on an actor. "My Faith is shaken" is
> legal; "Morality: 42" is a P-04 violation and never exists, surfaced or not.

## Q1.6 Anti-railroad — engagement windows are real and divergent

The charter's anti-railroad proof (Q1 L54): *a different choice at each engagement window
measurably changes trajectory.* The synthesis delivers one **existence** proof (a player-shaped
branch, capstone #3) but nothing **guarantees** that windows generally matter — an FSM whose
win-branch and lose-branch collapse to the same successor state is a railroad wearing a choice
costume, and the 6-rule suite would not catch it.

> **Conformance rule 7 (NEW — adversarial fix, MAJOR "illusory-choice"):**
> *engagement-window-divergence* — every arc-vector whose `activity_mode` is
> `edge_triggered_retryable` or `convergence` **and** which lists the player in
> `payload.participating_actors` MUST expose ≥2 participated-outcome classes that map to ≥2
> *distinct* `lifecycle.state` successors OR ≥2 distinct `pressure_effects[]` sets. A window whose
> participated outcomes all collapse to a single successor is flagged **illusory-choice at
> compile** (a `gaps[]` entry, honesty ledger). This makes "windows are real" a checked property,
> not an asserted one.

A second railroad vector is **attention**, not outcome. The salience economy (synthesis §4)
rations the 3–5 scene envelope and demotes arcs to Tier-1 texture — which means a *ceiling still
chooses which arcs fill the slots.* Left unguarded, the director could demote the very arc the
player is chasing down to rumor: a soft railroad away from player intent.

> **Conformance rule 8 (NEW — adversarial fix, MAJOR "attention railroad"):**
> *player-pursued-arc demotion-exemption* — any arc-vector with a **player participated-outcome in
> `causes[]` within the last K seasons** is demotion-exempt (floored at foreground) until it
> resolves or converges. Demotion may only touch player-untouched arcs. `K = 2` `[OPEN — Jordan
> tuning]`. "CEILING not floor" (synthesis §4) constrains *how many* beats surface; this rule
> constrains *which* may be subtracted.

**Slate-injection determinism (adversarial fix, MAJOR "tiebreak").** When more arc candidates
qualify for a slate step than the envelope admits, selection MUST use a total order — the existing
pruning sort (`player_agency_v30 §4.3`, `(step_number asc, internal_index asc)`) extended with
`arc_vector.id ASC` as the final, unique tiebreak. Integer salience scores over ~110 arcs make
marginal-slot ties the common case; an unstable or set/dict-seeded selection would emit a different
`scene.*` Key set and break replay (PP-687 §6 V4). `id` is unique, so the order is total. This
applies to every Top-N in the pipeline (slate injection here; chronicle Top-N and notable-individ
Top-N in the Q3/Q4 lanes).

# Q2 — How the world affects the player

## Q2.1 Casting — ONE tie-graph rule, all channels

Every time the world reaches for the player, it uses the **same** rule. When a live arc-vector
needs a scene, the casting resolver reads its `payload.participating_actors` and the tie-graph
edges radiating from each actor, and casts whoever the edges connect:

**The tie-graph edges (one closed set, no per-channel dialect):**
`Duty alignment · Conviction alignment · Bonds/Knots · lifepath/location · Standing · position`
(charter Q2 L58; the arc_vector `payload.participating_actors` is the seed set,
`dossier_register_formalizability` schema).

**Casting predicate:** the PC is cast into a beat iff the PC is in `participating_actors` **OR** is
tie-adjacent to a member of it (a matched edge exists). The **matched edge IS the "why-you"** — and
it is surfaced **diegetically**, never as a label (C2): the game shows *"Torben trusts you — you
tutored his sister"* (the Bond edge), not *"you are cast in ARC-S07 because participating_actors
∩ tie_graph ≠ ∅."*

**This one rule drives every summons channel** — the charter's requirement that casting be a single
mechanism, not a bundle of per-surface hacks:

| Channel | How the one rule expresses through it | Canon home |
|---|---|---|
| Arc-driven slate injection | Candidate enters `player_agency_v30 §4`'s 7 priorities at its scope's step | `player_agency_v30 §4.2` Steps 3/4/6 |
| NPC Outreach / Demand (≤3 / ≤2 per season) | Tie-edge = the Outreach relevance test (Disposition + Conviction relevance) | `npc_behavior_v30 §8.11` |
| Mandatory Crisis | Tie-edge = the mandatory-trigger's binding (target/Knot/faction) | `player_agency_v30 §4.2` Step 1; `scale_transitions_v30 §4.3.2` |
| Witness-presence | Tie-edge places the PC *at* a scene they cannot direct | `player_agency_v30 §4.2` (Witness Mode, ED-761) |

**The same rule casts NPCs.** An NPC is cast into a co-protagonist role by the identical
`participating_actors ∩ tie_graph` test (synthesis §5) — NPCs progress through the same arcs
(charter "one ledger"; C5). There is no separate NPC-casting path to drift out of shape.

> **Determinism note (inherited dependency):** casting reads detector output. The detector's own
> emit-order determinism (the `convergence_fired_set` ORD-2 hazard flagged in the adversarial
> review) is owned by the Q3/detect lane; casting inherits a **totally-ordered** convergence stream
> as a precondition and must not re-introduce set-iteration order in its own candidate assembly (use
> the `arc_vector.id` total order of §Q1.6).

## Q2.3 Position determines native story

Where you sit decides what kind of story finds you. Three positions, three native beat-shapes, all
riding existing engines.

### Q2.3.a Leaders — subordinate-originated beats (the NPC engine pointed upward)

A leader's native story is *other people's wants arriving as decisions.* The
`npc_behavior_v30 §5` concern/project engine and the `§8.11` Outreach generator already emit
subordinate-originated Keys; the arc-vector whose `scope.faction` is the leader's **subscribes to
those Keys filtered to `target = leader`** (synthesis §5) and injects them as:

- **reports-as-scenes** (a subordinate brings news),
- **requests-for-ruling** (a subordinate needs a decision — a forced choice, §Q2.4),
- **disputes** (two subordinates want incompatible things; politics Keys hunt the leader as target,
  charter Q2 L64).

**Anti-spreadsheet rule (hard, charter Q2 L64):** these arrive as **named actors with wants**, not
as report rows. Mechanically this is guaranteed by casting through `participating_actors` — every
injected leader-beat names the specific NPC (`npc_behavior_v30 §2` stance triangles), carries that
NPC's Conviction as its `payload.direction`, and routes through a played venue (a council scene, a
private audience), never a modal inbox. A leader-beat with an empty or generic actor slot fails the
anti-spreadsheet check.

### Q2.3.b Members — duties with refusal costs, under information asymmetry

A member's native story is *obligation you can refuse at a price.* This is the existing Duty system
(`player_agency_v30 §3`): the faction leader's AI priority stack assigns one Duty per season
(`§3.2`), success/failure moves Standing (`§3.4`), and — critically — **refusal is always
available at a cost** (`§4.5` Duty-not-pursued → Standing −1; `§3.5` Duty-vs-Conviction tension).
Member-beats carry `stakes_tags:[pricing]` (§Q2.5): the game **prices** refusal, it does not wall
it. Members also operate under **positional information asymmetry** — they see only public faction
state until Standing 2 unlocks intelligence (`player_agency_v30 §5.1`; T-30 socially applied,
charter Q2 L66 `[UNGROUNDED — T-30 named in charter, not re-verified here]`) — so a member's story
includes *acting on incomplete information*, with peer/rival Standing arcs as lateral pressure.

### Q2.3.c The factionless on-ramp — a vector ladder (only this architecture builds it)

A factionless PC must get **playable seasons** (charter Q2 acceptance test). The player-experience
judge's decisive point: the arc-vector engine is the *only* architecture that structurally builds
the on-ramp, because the on-ramp is itself a **ladder of arc-vectors**, not a special mode:

```
Conviction/Duty vectors            → self-authored pressure (player_agency §2, §5.3)
  → TE-tier settlement arcs          (arc_register_territory.md; casting via location edge)
  → Standing/Obligation proto-currency  (player_agency §5.4 Renown; §5.3 independent path)
  → recruitment-as-arc               (a faction vector whose participating_actors names the PC)
  → GD-3 build-your-own              (Renown 7+ independent Domain Action, player_agency §5.4)
```

Each rung is an ordinary arc-vector cast by the ordinary tie-graph rule (§Q2.1) — location edges
and Conviction edges do the casting when Standing/faction edges are absent. A factionless PC is
never starved of slate content because Steps 4 (Conviction), 6 (Territorial), and 7 (Ambient) of
`player_agency_v30 §4.2` fire independent of faction membership. Acceptance test met by
construction.

## Q2.4 The social-scale ladder — story traverses rungs on the state transport

Story climbs the same ladder as state: **individual → family → settlement → territory → faction →
world**, each rung naming its arc carrier, its surface, and its casting ties, and traversing rungs
on the **same aggregate-up / distribute-down transport as state** (Domain Echo up, the §12.4
down-seam down — synthesis §9).

| Rung | Arc carrier | Casting tie | Surfaces (s3 §Q4.1 map) | Transport up/down |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Individual | PC Convictions | self-authored | slate scene (played) + Tier-1 ambient lens (personal trackers) | Conviction fulfillment → Domain Echo (`player_agency §2.3`) |
| **Family** | Bond/Knot clusters + `character_histories` | Bonds/Knots + lifepath | slate (Knot/Bond scene) + Tier-1 lens (Bonds/Knot/Belief registers) | Knot-strain propagation (`npc_behavior §5.0b`) |
| Settlement | TE-tier + settlement arcs | location edge | slate (settlement-governance venue) + texture (rumor/overheard) | settlement events → Echo (`scale_transitions §5.5`) |
| Territory | Territory vectors (`arc_register_territory.md`) | location + faction control | Tier-2 cut scene (ambient territory pressure) + texture | Accord Echo (`scale_transitions §4.1`) |
| Faction | Faction vectors (`arc_register_factions.md`, 87 IDs) | Standing + position | slate (leader/member beats) + Tier-2 cut scene | faction-stat deltas at Accounting |
| World | Clock vectors + COLLISION | shared clocks (MS/CI/IP) | Tier-2 cut scene + Tier-3 chronicle | clock band transitions (`scale_transitions §4.3.3`) |

The **Surfaces** column is the charter's per-rung requirement that each rung name "its arc carrier,
surfaces, casting ties" (charter Q2 L70–71); the surface names are the five presentation surfaces of
the render lane's surface map (s3 §Q4.1: Tier-1 ambient lens · Tier-2 cut scenes · Tier-3 chronicle ·
texture-between-scenes · the scene slate). Lower rungs carry story on *participating* surfaces (slate,
texture — the player acts); higher rungs lean on *watching* surfaces (Tier-2 cut scene, Tier-3
chronicle) because peninsula/world pressure reaches the player as consequence more than as a
personally-played scene.

**The family rung is the thinnest — and it must be handled explicitly, never silently skipped**
(charter Q2 L72). Two admissible dispositions, exactly one required per campaign:

- **Bind:** the family rung's arc carrier is the PC's Bond/Knot clusters plus their
  `character_histories`/lifepath (`npc_behavior_v30` cross-ref `character_histories_lifepath.md`).
  Family beats cast on the Bonds/Knots edge; family pressure propagates as Knot strain
  (`npc_behavior_v30 §5.0b`). This is the recommended default where a PC has authored Bonds.
- **Declare absent-deliberate:** if a campaign/PC has no family ties, the rung is **marked
  absent-deliberate in the arc corpus `gaps[]`** (honesty ledger) — an explicit
  `gaps:[{type: absent_deliberate, note: "family rung: no Bond cluster authored"}]` — so the ladder
  is complete-by-declaration, not complete-by-omission.

> **Conformance (NEW):** *ladder-completeness* — each of the six rungs must, per campaign, either
> name a live arc carrier OR carry an `absent_deliberate` `gaps[]` entry. A rung that is neither is
> a silent skip and fails at compile.

Territory temperaments (the `arc_register_territory.md` map-state pressures) "get their chance to
earn a why" the same way any actor does: a territory vector's `payload.direction` supplies the
diegetic reason its pressure exists, surfaced when the arc casts a scene there.

## Q2.5 Impulsion — pressure arrives as choices with deadlines

The world does not present a menu; it presents **forced choices under diegetic clocks.** Impulsion
has three existing carriers:

- **Diegetic clocks** — the arc-vector `activity_mode: clock_escalation` ticks its
  `pressure_effects[]` (±2/season cap, `dossier_register_formalizability` schema) on the shared
  clocks (MS/CI/IP, `clock_registry_v30`). The clock's advance IS the deadline; when it crosses a
  band it fires a mandatory or optional Zoom In (`scale_transitions_v30 §4.3.2`/`§4.3.3`).
- **Forced-choice mandatory scenes** — the 8 Priority-0 triggers (`player_agency_v30 §4.2` Step 1;
  `scale_transitions_v30 §4.3.2`) cannot be declined and consume a scene action; requests-for-ruling
  (§Q2.3.a) are the leader-facing forced choice.
- **Refusal costs** — every impulsion carries a priced exit (`player_agency_v30 §4.5`): decline a
  Demand → Disposition −1, +1 Exposure (`npc_behavior_v30 §8.11.5`); skip a Duty → Standing −1.

> **Reconciliation clause (adversarial fix, MAJOR "tension curve vs no-designed-timing").** The
> `doc-10 §8.5` NOT-list stands (charter Q3 L104–105), including *"no designed dramatic timing."*
> Impulsion therefore **surfaces already-emerged pressure; it never manufactures it.** The director
> may only decide *which* already-firing deadline surfaces this season (a surfacing budget, C7
> CEILING); it may **never insert, reorder, delay, or time-compress an underlying event to shape a
> curve.** Event timing stays fully emergent — a diegetic clock ticks on its own schedule whether
> or not it makes a satisfying beat. Any "tension curve" is a *reading* the chronicle exposes
> backwards (C2-internal), never a target the scheduler steers toward. The director's only verbs
> are RATION and DEMOTE (subtract), never INSERT or ADVANCE.

## Q2.6 History conditions the option space — four modes, each cites its ledger cause

Your past is not flavor; it opens and closes what you can do this season. Four modes, in the
charter's preference order:

| # | Mode | What it does | Ledger cause (example) | Canon home |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Gating** | A threshold opens/closes an option | TS / Certainty / Coherence / Mandate / clock band | `player_agency_v30 §5.1` (Standing gates); `scale_transitions §4.3` |
| 2 | **Pricing** (*preferred over gating*) | An option stays open but costs | Obligation prices betrayal; refusal costs | `player_agency_v30 §4.5`; §Q2.5 |
| 3 | **Foreclosure** | A possibility permanently closes | via **arc events only**, surfaced afterward in chronicle | `npc_behavior_v30 §5.2` (Almud Arc A, ED-672) |
| 4 | **Pattern-response** | Divergent histories → divergent summons | oath-breaker vs zealot Conviction/Scar pattern | §Q1.5; `npc_behavior_v30 §5.1` |

**The cite-your-ledger-cause rule (hard, charter Q2 L79):** *every gate cites its ledger cause.*
Mechanically, every arc-vector with a non-empty `stakes_tags` MUST populate `payload.ledger_cause`
with the specific ledger surface (§Q1.4) and value that authorizes the gate/price/foreclosure. The
cause is **internal provenance**; it reaches the player only as diegetic consequence (C2: *"the
Church will never trust you again"*, not *"foreclosed: Piety < 1"*).

> **Conformance rule 9 (NEW):** *gate-cites-ledger-cause* — every `stakes_tags`-bearing arc-vector
> resolves `payload.ledger_cause` to a real ledger surface + operand at compile; an uncited gate
> fails (this is the anti-fabrication spirit, `holonic_container_doctrine_v1 §2`, extended to gate
> provenance).

### Q2.6.a Pricing is *preferred* — and the preference is enforced

The charter states pricing is *preferred over gating* (Q1 L76: "Obligations price betrayal, don't
wall it"). A bare `stakes_tags` enum carried untouched through compile makes that preference an
**unenforced authoring wish** — an all-gating corpus would pass every check.

> **Conformance rule 10 (NEW — adversarial fix, MAJOR "unenforced pricing preference"):**
> *pricing-preferred* — (a) compile emits a **gating:pricing ratio report** over the corpus; (b)
> every `stakes_tags:[gating]` vector MUST carry a `gaps[]`/justification note stating *why pricing
> is infeasible here*. Gating becomes the **audited exception** the charter's "preferred" intends,
> not the silent default.

### Q2.6.b Foreclosure — arc-events-only and never silent

Foreclosure is the sharpest mode: it *permanently* closes a possibility. The exemplar is **Almud's
Arc A (The Reformer)**: if the Löwenritter Autonomy stage advances (old Coup Counter reaches 2)
while Almud's Certainty is still ≥ 2, *"Arc A becomes permanently inaccessible"*
(`npc_behavior_v30 §5.2`, ED-672). The charter's two hard rules on foreclosure (Q1 L77–78) must be
mechanically enforced, because two failure paths threaten them:

1. **Silent-crossing risk.** A passive clock (`activity_mode: clock_escalation`, e.g. ARC-S07's
   *Loyalty ≤ 2 → Mandate −2*, `01_arc_corpus §b`) could drift across a foreclosure threshold with
   **no arc event at all** — violating "via arc events only."
2. **Accounted-but-unrendered risk.** A foreclosure consumed into FSM state is *accounted* (total
   accounting, synthesis §8) yet never *rendered* — violating "never silent."

> **Conformance rule 11 (NEW — adversarial fix, MAJOR "silent foreclosure"):**
> (a) *foreclosure-via-edge-only* — a `stakes_tags:[foreclosure]` transition may fire ONLY from
> `edge_triggered_once | edge_triggered_retryable | convergence`, NEVER from `clock_escalation |
> level_triggered`. A foreclosing clock crossing must first raise an **edge-triggered arc event**
> (the crossing becomes a beat, not a silent state flip).
> (b) *foreclosure-mandatory-render* — every foreclosure transition MUST emit a **Tier-3 chronicle
> beat** (a positive surfacing obligation, distinct from discard-with-reason logging). "Never
> silent" is thereby a checked emit, not an assumption. Foreclosures are **budgeted to majors**
> (charter Q1 L78) — the demotion-exemption (§Q1.6 rule 8) and this render rule together guarantee
> a foreclosure is felt.

## Band semantics

Two independent, reproducible divergence mechanisms, both arising exactly where the charter predicted — "between the integers":

1. **The ER-2 half-integer gap (combat vs contest).** For any `net` in the half-open interval `[Ob−0.5, Ob)`, combat's `core.py::degree()` reads **Success**; contest's `sigma_leverage.py::degree()` (no `−0.5` term) reads **Partial**. Verified exhaustively at (N,Ob) = (5,2), (6,3), (8,3), (3,2): e.g. `net=1.99, Ob=2, N=5` → combat=`success`, contest=`partial`. At the pool sizes where the two kernels actually operate (Combat Pool floor 5D via `resolution_pool`; Contest Pool floor 5D via `Pool.size`), this is not an edge case confined to 1D–2D — it is live in-band at the *floor* pool size both kernels normally use. Downstream effect at contest's own floor (N=5D, Ob=2, TN7): running the *same* nominal δσ through combat's ER-2-corrected formula vs contest's actual hybrid resolution produces gaps up to **+17.69pp** (δσ=0.50) in P(success-or-better) for nominally the same "how much does this advantage help" question.
2. **The discrete-lattice sawtooth in contest's pool-aware Overwhelming bar.** `OVERWHELM_SIGMA=0.85` is proven exactly-uniform (~19.77%) **only** under the continuous-Normal idealization (it's an algebraic identity there — the √N cancels by construction). Under contest's *actual* exact discrete roll (net_σ=0, the ordinary case), I convolved the true discrete distribution at every pool 5D–30D: the true Overwhelming rate oscillates **14.2%–23.4%**, not a flat ~20% — a `math.ceil()`-driven sawtooth of ~5–9pp amplitude, smaller than the 20%→91% swing this mechanism was built to fix, but real, unflagged, and non-monotonic in pool size.

Both are direct, quantified instances of "what happens between integers" — the σ-leverage *math* (soft_cap/net_boost/LEVEL_SIGMA) is genuinely single-sourced and identical across combat/contest; the *band-boundary arithmetic* that turns a continuous quantity into a discrete degree is not, and the disagreement is largest exactly at the small-pool floor both kernels operate at by default.

# Dossier C3 — Pools (agonist)

**Lane:** IN (ED-IN-0029 audit) · **Cluster:** C3 — Combat / Argue / Thread / Fieldwork / Knot / MassCombat / FactionDomain pools
**Scope confirmed against disk:** `references/descriptor_registry.yaml:110` — `not_descriptors.pools: [Combat, Argue, Thread, Fieldwork, Knot, MassCombat, FactionDomain]` is the exact 7-item registry-level enumeration this cluster is chartered against.

## Headline

The Combat Pool triple-carriage the SHARED baseline flags is not the worst case in this cluster — it is the *best-documented* one. Tracing every pool past the census's cited restatements into its home doc's own normative procedure section, the sim oracle, and the sibling `module_contracts.yaml`/registry taxonomies surfaces a **Knot Pool with four live, mutually incompatible formulas** (two of them never cross-referenced by any existing ED, including the one — ED-920 — that already half-covers this exact quantity), a genuine **vocabulary collision** where the single word "pool" is used for two disjoint kinds across `descriptor_registry.yaml` and `module_contracts.yaml` with zero reconciling note, an **eighth, uncatalogued "Pool"-named quantity** (Faction Political Pool) that collides in name but not substance with Faction Domain Pool, and a second **within-document stale carrier** in `threadwork_v30.md` (the Mending operation) that the census's "Thread Pool = COHERENT" verdict missed because it stopped at the operation-definition sites and never checked the doc's own later cross-reference tables. The census itself under-investigated the Knot Pool row — its own citation trail (mechanics_index.yaml → knots_v30.md) leads directly to the contradiction it didn't report.

## Trace narrative

### Combat Pool — confirmed as documented, port closes clean

Verified against disk exactly as the SHARED baseline states: `params/core.md:161` states the canonical `max(5, Relevant History + 6)`, Agility-independent, ED-901/900/904; `params/core.md:4`'s header comment still cites the struck PP-247 form (`Agi + hist_pts + 3`); `references/values_master.yaml:212` (filed under nonexistent `params/combat.md`) and `:1150` (duplicate) both carry the struck `(Agility×2)+History+3` form. New evidence not in the census: `designs/scene/derived_stats_v30.md:515` ("What Does NOT Change") *itself* states the struck form as still-current, two sections before its own `:584` states "**STRUCK** old `(Agility×2)+History+3`" — an intra-document self-contradiction (confirmed exact lines, matches census's F-DESIGN-1). The port closes clean: `designs/scene/combat_engine_v1/combatant.py:117` implements `self.pool=max(5, history+6)` verbatim; `designs/godot/skeleton/engines/combat/resources/combat_config.gd:17-18` (`pool_base: int = 6`, `pool_floor: int = 5`) matches exactly; `references/module_contracts.yaml:810` pins the formula inline in the `resolver:` comment. Combat Pool is the one pool in this cluster where the *canonical* line is unambiguous end-to-end — only its stale carriers are a problem, and those are already KNOWN-TRACKED (ED-1084, F-REG-001, FIX-05).

### Knot Pool — four live formulas, only one openly tracked

This is the flagship finding of this dossier. Tracing "Knot Pool" past the census's single cited row (`mechanics_index.yaml:350`) into its own stated home doc surfaces:

1. **`designs/personal/knots_v30.md:37`** (§2, summary table): `Knot Pool (formation roll) = (Bonds × 2) + 3, min 5` — cited to "PP-632; derived_stats §14.4".
2. **`designs/personal/knots_v30.md:78`** (§3.2, the *normative procedure* — the section titled "Procedure", i.e. the operative rule, not a summary): `Roll: Spirit × 2 + History (Relationships), TN 7, Ob 2` — no Bonds term at all, and no explicit "+3" (unlike Argue/Thread/Fieldwork Pool, which all state the History-bonus-plus-3 convention explicitly).
3. **`designs/scene/fieldwork_v30.md:473`** (§5.6a): `Resolution: Spirit pool (Spirit × 2) vs TN 7, Ob 2` — bare Spirit, no History term at all. This third variant **is** KNOWN-TRACKED: `canon/editorial_ledger.jsonl:189`, **ED-920** (P2, status **open**, filed 2026-06-11, lane A), titled "Pool-construction deviation on pivotal sub-5D rolls," explicitly names "Knot formation = Spirit×2, no History" as deviating from "the canonical (Attr×2)+History construction" and asks Jordan to ratify-or-normalize.
4. **`sim/personal/knots.py:213-215`** (the implemented oracle — `# §3.2 Formation roll`): `pool = (spirit * 2) + history_rel` — matches variant 2, confirming variant 2 (not 1 or 3) is what the code actually runs, but *also* silently drops the "+3" constant the design-doc convention elsewhere always states.

Downstream carriers of variant 1 (Bonds-based) that never cross-check against the operative §3.2 or the running code: `canon/mechanics_index.yaml:350` (`primitives.pool: "(Bonds*2)+3"`, in the block whose own `unified_canon_target` at `:343` names `knots_v30.md` as canonical — i.e. this index cites a formula its own target doc's normative section contradicts) and `designs/scene/derived_stats_v30.md:588` (`Knot Pool | (Bonds × 2) + 3 | 5–17D`, no caveat). `references/module_contracts.yaml:308`'s resolver comment for `fieldwork_knots` hedges rather than resolving: `resolver: dice_pool   # Fieldwork Pool (fieldwork_v30 §2); knot formation procedure (knots_v30 §3.2)` — citing both a Fieldwork-Pool framing *and* §3.2 without picking one.

Critically, **ED-912** (resolved 2026-06-28, the ruling that restructured Knot tiers to the Distant/Close strain gauge) only explicitly struck "the PP-632 Loose/Medium/Close 3-tier point-cost model" (`knots_v30.md:59`) — it never revisited the pool *formula* PP-632 also carried. That leaves the `(Bonds×2)+3` formula sitting in three carriers (table, index, cross-scale doc) citing a patch (PP-632) whose sibling provisions were declared superseded, while the doc's own operative section and the oracle moved on independently. `sim/tests/test_knots_ed912.py` tests tier-gauge behavior (rupture, break, Tempered) exhaustively but contains **no assertion on the formation-pool formula itself** — so a future edit to either the table or the code could silently diverge further with nothing to catch it.

**Census correction:** the census's Knot Pool row cites `mechanics_index.yaml:350` as the "defining_surface" and reports only the `(Bonds×2)+3` formula, tagging the row `UNREGISTERED-LOAD-BEARING` on the strength of a missing key binding alone. It never followed `mechanics_index.yaml:343`'s own `unified_canon_target` pointer into `knots_v30.md`, and so missed that the doc's own §3.2 (and the code) disagree with the table two rows above it in the same document. This is itself a finding against the census.

### Argue Pool — confirmed intra-doc split, feeds the already-open P0 fork

Confirmed exactly as census states: `social_contest_v30.md:119, 164, 490` all state the incomplete `(Primary Attribute × 2) + History bonus` as "Pool:"/"Argue Pool ="/"Argue pool", while lines 85-87 and 130-133 make clear the up-to-+2D genre/audience style bonus is added *afterward*, at Step 3, and is "fixed at setup" — i.e. the doc's own internal logic treats style bonus as separate from "the pool." `derived_stats_v30.md:585`'s cross-scale table folds it back in: `(Primary × 2) + History + 3 + style`. This is a definitional-boundary ambiguity (is style bonus *part of* Argue Pool or *added to* it?), not obviously a numeric error either way. Per rule (f), this is an SC-lane call: it directly feeds the already-open **ED-SC-0004** ("kernel Argue-pool formula" — `handoffs/HANDOFF_SC.md:101, 125-127`, P0 docket item, P1 blocker per line 102). Consistent with the fork being genuinely unresolved: `references/module_contracts.yaml:434-457` (the `social_contest` module) pins `resolver: dice_pool` with **no formula at all** in `state:`/comments — unlike `personal_combat`'s inline resolver-comment formula. The docket, not this dossier, is where the formula gets fixed.

### Thread Pool — core formula coherent; a second, undetected stale carrier in the same doc

The core Leap-roll formula is confirmed coherent across all restatements: `(Spirit × 2) + relevant History bonus + Thread Pool Score` recurs at `threadwork_v30.md:19, 155, 266, 317, 350, 374, 419, 455` (eight sites, not seven — line 419 was missed by the census) plus `derived_stats_v30.md:586`, with no numeric divergence.

But the census's blanket "COHERENT" verdict only checked the operation-definition sites. The **Mending** operation (`threadwork_v30.md:444-460`) explicitly documents its own correction: `:452` states the pool as `(Spirit × 2) + relevant History bonus + Thread Pool Score`, and the adjacent design note (`:453`) says outright: "Mending uses the same pool as all other Thread operations... The prior Attunement + Focus construction is struck." Yet the *same document*, later, still carries the struck form as if current: `:968` (§7.3 Updated Mode Branching) — `Gap closure | Mending (Att + Focus + Thread Pool Score) | Mend order | ...` — and `:1008` (§8.3 Implementation Sequence) — `Simulate Mending pool (Att + Focus + Thread Pool Score) probability curves`. Both post-date the correction in document order but were not updated when PP-616/625 struck the Attunement+Focus construction.

### Fieldwork Pool — confirmed positive control

Confirmed exactly as census states: `params/fieldwork.md:6-15` documents its own construction (`(Primary × 2) + History bonus`, min 5D/max 24D, verified by hand: `(7×2)+(7+3)=24`, `(1×2)+(0+3)=5`) and explicitly disclaims the by-analogy justification to Combat Pool no longer holding post-ED-901, noting the ×2 form "is now fieldwork-native... not combat-derived" (ED-1084). No defect found; a genuine positive control for how a supersession-adjacent doc *should* self-correct — worth contrasting directly against Knot Pool's failure to do the same thing for its own analogous ED-912 supersession.

### Mass Combat Pool — well-caveated in prose; one carrier (and one registry surface) with zero caveat

`params/mass_combat.md:62` and `mass_battle_v30.md:28, 443` all confirmed to carry the PP-233 legacy formula (`min(Size,Command)+Command`) *together with* a full inline ED-899/ED-1013 supersession caveat each time (verified — these are not mere "parenthetical asides," they are complete blockquote-length corrections citing the live engine and `tests/sim/mass_battle/config.py`/`orchestration.py`). **Correction to the census:** its characterization that `mass_battle_v30.md` "foreground[s] the legacy form (with only a parenthetical aside pointing to the live default)" overstates the gap at those two sites — both are thoroughly caveated. The genuine, uncaveated carrier is `derived_stats_v30.md:589`: `Mass Combat Pool | min(Size, Command) + Command | 2–14D | Mass Combat (unit)` — no asterisk, no footnote, nothing pointing to ED-899/ED-1013, unlike every other prose carrier. This confirms census's F-DESIGN-3 (KNOWN-UNTRACKED) but narrows its locus to specifically the `derived_stats_v30.md` §14.4 table — which, combined with the Combat Pool self-contradiction (§13/§14.4) and (below) the module_contracts gap, makes this table a *repeat offender* surface.

Separately, and not previously flagged: `references/module_contracts.yaml:458-473` (the `mass_battle` module) declares `resolver: dice_pool` with `state: []` — **no representation of the pool formula at all**, stale or otherwise, unlike `personal_combat`'s resolver-comment formula. An implementer reading the module contract for Mass Combat would find nothing; they would have to already know to go to `tests/sim/mass_battle/config.py`/`orchestration.py` directly. This is a stronger gap than "unregistered" — the contract layer is silent, not stale.

Board Game mode (`mass_battle_v30.md:755`, `sum of all engaged unit Martial values + commander bonus`) confirmed as a genuinely distinct mode per the doc's own "Three-mode" framing (`:9`) — no defect, catalogued for completeness.

### Faction Domain Pool — confirmed unregistered; and an uncatalogued eighth "Pool"

`derived_stats_v30.md:590` (`bare faction stat, 1-7D`) and its own V-1 design-intent note (`:592-596`, explicit non-proportional scale-shift) confirmed as census states — no defect. `references/module_contracts.yaml:479-499` (the `domain_actions` module) has `doc: null` (KNOWN-TRACKED, ED-1051) and `state: []` — so Faction Domain Pool is genuinely unregistered anywhere in the contract layer, consistent with census.

New in this trace: `references/module_contracts.yaml:645` declares a **different** state entry, `{name: "faction political pool", bucket: pool, writable: true}` (in the `ci_political` module, `:637-651`), sourced to `designs/provincial/ci_political_v30.md §3.4`. Read at `ci_political_v30.md:161-169`: **Faction Political Pool** is a parliamentary vote-tally construct — `Church: Mandate + ⌊CI/20⌋ votes`; opposing faction: `max(0, Mandate − ⌊CI/30⌋)` — mechanically unrelated to Faction Domain Pool (a domain-action dice-pool size) despite the near-identical name. This is an **eighth "Pool"-named quantity**, absent from both `descriptor_registry.yaml:110`'s 7-item `not_descriptors.pools` list and `derived_stats_v30.md` §14.4's 7-row Pools table — genuinely uncatalogued, and a live name-collision risk against Faction Domain Pool that nothing in the corpus disambiguates.

### The "pool" vocabulary collision (cross-cutting, cluster-wide)

Grepping every `bucket: pool` occurrence in `references/module_contracts.yaml` (`:288, 645, 822` — three total in the whole file) shows **none of the 7 canonical dice pools ever receives that tag**: `:288` is Coherence (a 10→0 countdown resource — classified as a **Track** in `derived_stats_v30.md` §14.2, and absent entirely from `descriptor_registry.yaml`'s `not_descriptors` enumeration in any bucket — checked, zero hits for "Coherence" in that file), `:645` is the Faction Political Pool above, and `:822` is Stamina (`personal_combat` module — but `descriptor_registry.yaml:108` classifies Stamina under `not_descriptors.derived_values`, not `pools`). Meanwhile Combat Pool's own formula lives only in a free-text `resolver:` comment (`:810`), never a `state:` entry with `bucket: pool`. **The word "pool" names two disjoint kinds with zero cross-reference**: `descriptor_registry.yaml`'s `not_descriptors.pools` = dice-resolution pool size (this cluster's 7 quantities); `module_contracts.yaml`'s `bucket: pool` enum = depleting/replenishing resource meter (Stamina, Coherence, faction political capital) — and the two taxonomies actively disagree on Stamina's kind (`derived_value` vs `pool`) for the *same* quantity.

# Dossier C7 — Cross-index consistency, enforcement tiers & check backlogs (agonist)

**Headline.** This audit's own baseline already says "everything is warn, the registry test is dead, values_master is quarantined, there is no prose-drift detector" — tracing the actual index-to-index wiring shows the situation is a rung worse than that summary implies: the one automated cross-check that exists (`ci_names_consistency.py`) covers only 2 of the 28 possible pairwise relationships among the 8 surfaces this dossier samples (descriptor_registry, glossary, and six secondary indices — `mechanical_terms_index.md`, `numeric_bounds_report.yaml`, `propagation_map.md`, `silo_overlap_matrix.yaml`, `name_collision_database.yaml`, `collision_registry.yaml`), and it explicitly *designs out* the mechanic/clock/track vocabulary where this audit's worst collisions live. The one thing that IS scanned for drift (`legacy:` names) is empty for every one of the 22 attribute/aggregate/faction/settlement-stat entries in `names_index.yaml`, while the thing that actually disagrees across files (`aliases:`) is schema-defined to never be flagged. On the two headline backlog counts: **A17 (stat-vocabulary closure)** fails for roughly a third of the corpus by the shared census's own registration tally (34/88 `unregistered`, zero `block`-tier anywhere) and for a clearly worse ~68% (23 of 34) of the individual quantity-declarations inside `module_contracts.yaml`'s own `state:`/`derivations:` blocks when hand-traced directly; the Key-substrate layer (`stat_deltas`) cannot even be sampled today because nothing in `sim/substrate/keys.py` constrains its vocabulary at all. **A18 (formula single-source)** fails for at least 25/88 quantities (28%, the census's own DRIFTED+AMBIGUOUS rows), and — importantly — full 8/8 surface coverage does not imply single-sourcing (Mandate is registered everywhere and still carries two independently-sourced, non-citing formulas).

- {acr: CT, expands: "Piety Track", in: "faction docs", correct: PT}
- {acr: CV, expands: "Conviction",  in: "faction docs", correct: PT}

- `references/glossary.md:94` tells authors the *opposite*: `**CT NOTE:** CT is the preferred abbreviation for Piety Track` — directly contradicting the collision database's ruling, in the file whose own header calls itself "the canonical reference for all term expansions project-wide" (`glossary.md:4`).

Net: three of the eight surfaces this dossier samples take three different, mutually contradicting positions on the same abbreviation, one of the "6 secondary indices" already adjudicated the question, and the one file designed to carry that adjudication forward never received it.

### Enforcement tiers — why "everything is warn" understates the gap
`references/names_index.yaml:45-72` shows all 9 attributes + 3 aggregates + 5 faction stats + 5 settlement stats (22 entries) at `enforce: warn`, and — checked directly — **every one of the 22 has `legacy: []`** (empty); only the `aliases:` field is populated (e.g. `attr.mind.acuity: {aliases: [Reasoning, Cognition], legacy: []}`, `names_index.yaml:49`). `tools/names.py:91-106`'s `all_legacy()` — the function `tools/ci_names_check.py:43` (`_warn_matchers()`) calls to build its drift-lint patterns — walks only the `legacy` field of each entry (`names.py:106`, `for name in (e.get('legacy') or [])`); it never inspects `aliases`. Consequently the drift lint has **zero** matchers for this entire 22-entry family: it is not merely non-blocking, it is structurally incapable of firing for it, because the schema's own comment (`names_index.yaml:20`, `aliases: [<allowed equivalent phrasings — not errors>]`) defines the exact field where the live disagreements sit (`Acuity` vs `Cognition`, `Will` vs `Spirit`) as "not errors" by design. The 7-vs-9 roster conflict this feeds (glossary primary = `Cognition`/`Spirit`/`Presence`... vs registry primary = `Acuity`/`Will`/`Charisma`) is KNOWN-TRACKED as ED-IN-0008 and is not re-argued here; what is new is the mechanical reason the warn-tier lint could never have caught it even if fully triaged and enabled.

`tools/ci_names_consistency.py:4-15` is the only other automated check touching `names_index.yaml`; its own docstring states its scope precisely: it asserts `descriptor_registry.yaml`'s and `proper_noun_registry.yaml`'s mirror `name`/`canonical` fields equal the `names_index.yaml` `canonical`, and explicitly says "Mechanic/clock/track/substrate entries have no registry mirror... and are skipped" (`ci_names_consistency.py:14-15`). Glossary is not mentioned anywhere in `ci_names_consistency.py` — consistent with `glossary.md:31`'s own self-flag ("names_index.yaml's MIRRORS block omits this glossary"). None of the six secondary indices are referenced by any file under `tools/` (`grep -rl` for each of the six filenames across `tools/` returns nothing outside `tools/observability/` reporting artifacts and `tools/validate_ed_citations.py`/`ci_naming_check.py` incidental hits unrelated to cross-checking their content). Of the 28 possible pairwise relationships among the 8 surfaces sampled, **2** (descriptor_registry↔names_index, proper_noun_registry↔names_index) have any automated check at all.

**The regeneration path for the six secondary indices is not stalled, it is severed.** `numeric_bounds_report.yaml:1-22`'s own instruction is "Regenerate after next collator run." The only plausible collator, `tools/valoria_collator.py`, is on CLAUDE.md's dead-tools list; confirmed directly — `valoria_collator.py:35-36` hardcodes `sys.path.insert(0, '/home/claude')` and `import github_ops as g`, both unrunnable in this environment. Even if resurrected, `valoria_collator.py:9` lists `references/values_master.yaml` as one of its three cross-reference sources for `VALUE_CONFLICT` detection — the same file this audit's baseline facts mark QUARANTINED-STALE (ED-1084). So the "regenerate" instruction on one dead-tool-adjacent index points at a second broken source even in the counterfactual where the tool ran. `mechanical_terms_index.md`, `silo_overlap_matrix.yaml`, `name_collision_database.yaml`, and `collision_registry.yaml` were never tool-generated at all — they read as single-session manual audit deliverables (dated 2026-05-08/05-10) with no generator to point at, dead or otherwise.

### Key substrate — stat_deltas has no vocabulary to check against
`sim/substrate/keys.py:239-251` (`TypeRegistry.validate_payload`) is the only payload-shape validation in the substrate. It checks that each name in a Key type's `required_payload_fields` is present as a *key* in `key.payload` (an existence check on field names) — it does **not** inspect the contents of `stat_deltas`/`impact_vector` dicts (`keys.py:83-84`, `stat_deltas: dict # stat_name -> delta`) against any vocabulary. Confirmed: `grep -c "descriptor_registry\|names_index" sim/substrate/keys.py` → 0 (no match anywhere in the file). This is the concrete code-level mechanism behind the shared census's F-SIM-02/03 enforcement-gap finding ("stat_deltas accepts any string key with zero vocabulary validation") — cited as corroboration, not a new finding (calibration: KNOWN-TRACKED, F-SIM-02/03). Practically: A17's "substrate stat_deltas" clause cannot be checked today by reading `keys.py` alone — a real A17 implementation would have to reconstruct the actual runtime vocabulary from every emitter's payload literal, because the substrate itself enforces nothing about stat *names*.

## A17 / A18 backlog counts (headline numbers)

**A17 — stat-vocabulary closure** (every stat named in `module_contracts.yaml` `state:`/`derivations:`/`gates:` + substrate `stat_deltas` resolves to a registry key or an explicit `not_descriptors` row):
- Corpus-wide proxy (shared census `quantity_census.md`, 88 rows, `Registered` column): **34/88 (39%) `unregistered`** — no key, no `not_descriptors` row; these fail outright. A further 24/88 (27%) pass only via the `not_descriptors` fallback (not a real key). Only 24/88 (27%) resolve to an actual `attr.*`/`agg.*`/`fac.*`/`set.*` key, and **all 24 of those are `enforce: warn`** — zero are `block`.
- Direct hand-sweep of `module_contracts.yaml`'s own `state:`/`derivations:` blocks (34 distinct `{name: ...}` line-declarations, `module_contracts.yaml:87-824`): at least **19 clean violations** (Treasury :88; beliefs/opinions :156; concerns :157; projects :158; arc state :159; conviction scars :225; CV/per-territory-Piety :254; TC/Theocracy-Counter :256; Coherence :288; knot strain :317; Turmoil :515; province Accord :560; faction political pool :645; card hands/cooldown :646; season counter :705; cumulative_damage :820; Wounds :821; Initiative :823; Poise :824), plus Public Order failing inside a bundled derivation output (:594-596) and 3 more surfaced only in a bundled gates-reader line (Accord, PV, PT — :663) — **≈23 of 34 (68%)** fail closure when traced at this granularity, a materially higher rate than the whole-corpus 39% because module-internal bookkeeping fields (arc state, cooldowns, season counters) are exactly the class nobody thought to registry-file.
- `gates:` `"on":` fields introduce no *new* unregistered names beyond what `state:`/`derivations:` already declare (checked: 16 `"on":` lines, all repeat existing labels).
- Substrate `stat_deltas`: unsampleable today (C7-F6) — no baseline vocabulary exists to check violations against.

**A18 — formula single-source** (every derived-value formula has exactly one defining surface, others cite it):
- Shared census tally: **21 DRIFTED + 4 AMBIGUOUS = 25/88 (28%)** quantities carry more than one non-citing defining/formula surface. A further 2/88 are DEAD-but-still-consumed (VTM) — a related but distinct failure (a struck formula still gating live thresholds elsewhere) that would push the practical backlog to **27/88 (~31%)** if folded in.
- Full-coverage does not imply single-sourcing (C7-F8, Mandate) — A17 and A18 must be built and tracked as independent checks, not one implying the other.

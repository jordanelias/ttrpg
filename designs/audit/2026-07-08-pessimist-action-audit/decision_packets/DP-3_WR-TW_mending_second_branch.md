## 2. THE DIAGNOSIS

Mending is the *only* legal tool for its target class (Gap / Shifting-Object / Locked-Zone border — Weaving, Pulling, Locking, Dissolution are all barred), Coherence-free, and unopposeable ("Mending Immunity," `params/threadwork.md` §Mending Immunity: "Cannot be opposed"). Collective and Einhir-framework "approaches" are the same operation scaled up, not distinct tools. The only alternative is *not repairing* — strictly dominated: `params/threadwork.md` lines 123–132 (Gap Self-Closure by Scale, PP-604) shows Object closing in 1 season/0 RS drain, Relational 2 seasons/1 drain, Field 4 seasons/2 drain (total 6), Structural 8 seasons/3 drain (total 21), Foundational 32 seasons/5 drain (total 155) — all against a cost-free, unopposed alternative. This fails `references/throughlines_meta.md` line 119's Q-robust bar ("Three viable player approaches to any situation the mechanic governs").

The Ob table's Catastrophic Gap (Ob 7) and Locked Zone border (Ob 8+) — solo Mending there "intentionally near-impossible (~40–58% even at maximum pool)," per the design note "the Einhir Catastrophe's legacy made mechanical" — is real friction, but friction on the *same* single choice (harder Mending), not a second branch. One lever (Mend, possibly collectively) and one dominated non-lever (wait it out). Difficulty scaling is not branching.

## 3. THE FORK — A MENU, NOT A RECOMMENDATION

**A — Retune the fallback (numbers-only).** Re-tune the RS-drain/duration figures in `params/threadwork.md` lines 123–132 so self-closure becomes a real trade, not a strictly-losing default. No new mechanic. Risk: if self-closure gets too cheap, it undercuts the near-Ob-7/8-near-impossibility weight the Einhir-legacy framing carries — the two intents pull against each other at the tuning margin.

**B — An opposeable Mending variant in contested/Church-adjacent territory.** A genuine second tool: Church-aligned actors (Amendment 5's conflation-of-threadwork-as-heresy lens, lines 58–64) as natural opposer; a failed contest costs more than the existing Failure degree. Heaviest lift — touches Mending Immunity's *scope* (not its cost); needs its own Ob/degree table and a ruling on interaction with the existing degree ladder.

**C — A resource/scarcity dimension.** Leave Mending unopposed and free, but gate access (consumable Einhir Texts, an Approach-Training prerequisite, a seasonal action-economy cost). Reframes "mend or don't" into "who mends, and when." Lower authoring cost than B; risk is bookkeeping without a genuine *third viable approach* — the underlying choice is still singular.

**D — Decline the fork.** Rule Gap-repair deliberately single-path and high-stakes — one tool, all-hands at scale, and that *is* the point. Q-robust's three-approaches bar is a heuristic, not a law every mechanic must clear; the REFINE flag explicitly frames this as a surfaced tension, not a mandate. A live, respectable answer, not a lesser one.

No option is recommended over another — this is Jordan's design call, and D carries equal standing to A/B/C.

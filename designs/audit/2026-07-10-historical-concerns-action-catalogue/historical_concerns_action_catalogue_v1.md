## 1. Executive framing — the bidirectional thesis

The design brief asks for a catalogue in which geopolitical, geographical, climatic, and economic concerns **both inform and are affected by** the action menu. The catalogue below is built so that every concern-domain is a *closed loop*: the world-state in that domain sets the trigger predicate and severity of the cards a governor draws (concern → menu), and the verbs the governor spends resolving those cards write durable Ledger tags and stat trajectories that reshape that same domain's future world-state (menu → concern). The four loops:

**Geopolitical (succession, blockade, siege, faction rank).** *Informs the menu:* comparable-Standing rivals, appanage grants, enclave geography, and Directive-compliance history are exactly the trigger predicates of the Crisis/Intrigue deck — a Contested Succession fires off `|Standing gap| ≤ 1`, an Embargo fires off a hegemon's Grudge+Standing over a trade-dependent rival, a Sack fires off `Defy` under a `Suppress` Directive. The verbs that address them are the institutional legitimation machinery: the Ratify/Hold-Court branch, the Recall Directive, the Consulta election, Fratricide Law, the Recognition Fork. *Affected by the menu:* every one of these verbs writes a Precedent that lowers or raises the *future* trigger threshold of the same card family — a military-acclamation Ratify permanently lowers the Standing-gap needed to reopen a succession contest; a `magdeburgisieren` sack Precedent lowers Defy-appeal locally while raising Grudge/Intrigue regionally. Political choices are self-reinforcing at one scale and self-undermining at another.

**Geographical (terrain, chokepoints, rivers/harbors, mines, floodplains).** *Informs the menu:* a chokepoint tag unlocks the Toll Levy method; a Nilometer facility unlocks Gauge-Indexed Levy; siltation/OreGrade/SalinityLevel stats are the slow fuses that convert Opportunity cards into Friction and then Crisis. Geography literally gates which verbs exist at a settlement. *Affected by the menu:* Fortify (Sea Wall, Sheltered Dockyard, Housing Code, Water Board) and Develop (Dredge, Rebuild Infrastructure, Diversify Corridor, Relocate Settlement) *rewrite* the terrain stats that set the next card's odds — but the successful ones (a Toll, a Scour dike) accrue Debt/Grudge and hidden downstream tags (RisingWaterLevel, SubsidenceRisk) that seed a Crisis at a *different* node many seasons later. The extractive success of a geographic verb is what eventually contests the geography that produced it.

**Climatic (earthquake, eruption, storm/hurricane, drought, cooling epoch, plague).** *Informs the menu:* `external_shock(climate/military-transit)` combined with Π-band and a *neglected facility* (no Rebuild-to-Code, no Granary, no Sanità, no Sea Wall) is the canonical trigger shape — the Crisis card is a retroactive audit of a Fortify/Develop investment the governor did or didn't make seasons earlier. *Affected by the menu:* pre-crisis facility investment (Ever-Normal Granary StockLevel, Quarantine_Tier, Rebuild-to-Code) is consumed automatically to blunt the shock, while the response verb chosen (Force→pogrom, Segregation Camps→assassination, Divert-Logistics→deepened famine) manufactures the *next* card. Climate shocks are correlated across settlements (the world-level Cooling flag), so an aggregate Extract-vs-Remit policy determines whether a shared shock resolves as survivable isolated Crises or a campaign-level cascade.

**Economic (currency, fiscal, trade monopoly, merchant capital).** *Informs the menu:* Capital-Posture:Debased/Speculative tags, Debt tags concentrated in one Sponsor, single-commodity Fiscal Stance, and stale-high Assessment figures are the trigger predicates for price-panic, bank-run, default, and blockade Crises. *Affected by the menu:* Levy:Debase / Levy:Charter Fiat / Levy:Farm buy immediate Treasury by stacking a Capital-Posture or Debt tag that raises the Ob of *all* future Levy and the draw-weight of the fiscal Crisis family — a one-way ratchet whose only exit (Recoinage / Currency Reset) is priced to grow more expensive the longer it is deferred. Merchant-capital verbs (Underwrite, Capital Partnership, Charter Fiat, Sunset Review) convert political leverage into extraction rights and back, so a faction's financing choices continuously redraw which factions sit on the rank ladder.

The cross-domain payoff (§3) is that these four loops are not sealed: a Capital-Posture tag (Economic) crashes a Levy base that forces harsher extraction through a climate shock (Climatic), which collapses Order and challenges a weakened ruler (Geopolitical), whose successor inherits a chokepoint whose toll now funds a rival's private army (Geographical→Geopolitical). The follow_on-seed architecture is what makes those crossings *playable* rather than thematic.

### 2.1 Succession crises, civil wars, and elite power-struggle collapses (Geopolitical)
*Key: `succession-crises-civil-wars-elite-power-struggles`. Binds to the §2.x succession-split resolver: Trigger Matrix, Stage-1/Stage-2 contest, G-based Unified/Split, Split Persistence, plus Ministry/Consulta (Part 7). The through-line: **granting independent Military/Standing to heirs is what makes the next leader-loss fire the mandatory-contest row** — and the only durable off-ramps are institutional (Ratify, Consulta, Oath, Fratricide), each with its own recurring cost.*

- **Year of the Four Emperors (Rome, 69 CE)** — *§2.1–2.4 + Part 7 + §1.3c Ratify/Reject/Amend.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `leader eliminated AND heir.Disposition<3 AND ≥2 contenders AND |Standing gap|≤1`; Comply (recognize the sitting military claimant) binds you to a possibly-doomed claimant, Bargain buys concessions, Defy backs a rival feeding their External-backed strength; FO = a bare military win reseeds the Crisis next season (Galba→Otho→Vitellius→Vespasian), a win + Ratify writes a `military-acclamation-legitimate` Precedent that closes the chain but permanently lowers the retrigger threshold. **Action** Donative + Ratify (extends *Levy* and the Hold-Court *Ratify/Reject/Amend* branch): spend Treasury to buy a contender's Stage-1 military-Loyalty check, then convert a fait-accompli into legitimacy via a Consulta vote. **Loop** Donative and Ratify are complements not substitutes — a bought win leaves Mandate depressed and Π high (reseeding the Crisis) unless the separate Ratify writes the closing Precedent; Otho's donative bought weeks, Vespasian's *lex de imperio* bought a dynasty.
- **The Anarchy (England, 1138–1153)** — *§2.2 claim-basis, §2.3 G≤1 SPLIT, §2.5 re-merge, §1.6 Compact/Grudge/Reputation.* → **Card** Friction→Crisis; trigger a `Succession Guarantee` Compact exists but is *not* one of the four recognized claim-basis types AND a rival's Standing is comparable; Comply (a sworn Inner-Circle member honors the oath) defends the heir but strains local Order, Defy (backs the rival) writes a severe Grudge + `Reputation:Oathbreaker` on that NPC; FO = mass reneging auto-triggers G≤1 SPLIT → recurring "Baronial Free Agency" Crisis, closable only by a Bargain-Directive §2.5 treaty re-merge on a next-generation compromise heir (Wallingford → Henry II). **Action** Extract Succession Oath (new Compact-family tag + mass social contest, parallel to §1.3a Negotiate Quota): gives the named heir a *fifth, oath-based* claim basis and taxes later defectors with Grudge+Oathbreaker — the enforcement teeth the 1127/1131 oaths to Matilda lacked. **Loop** Extracting the Oath early costs Mandate/AP but brakes the mandatory-contest row; skip it and any comparable rival hits it with no brake, and the resulting Split erodes the Mandate needed to ever extract a credible Oath afterward — the nineteen-winter doom loop.
- **Ottoman Interregnum / Fetret Devri (1402–1413)** — *§2.2 institutional claim-basis from Governor Assignment, §2.4 splinter residual Standing, §1.0a Demotion Magnitude as template for a Fratricide tier.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `leader eliminated/captured AND ≥2 contenders each holding a Governor Assignment` (institutional basis auto-satisfied); the distinct post-resolution choice is whether the winner invokes Fratricide; FO = sparing claimants seeds a guaranteed Ambition card next Generational-Shift ("a spared rival claims again"); invoking writes `Reputation:Hated`/Grudge raising Intrigue (assassination) draw-weight. **Action** Fratricide Law (Crown-tier post-Contest option amending §2.4): spend a severe Demotion-Magnitude-"total" Reputation/Grudge cost to strip all losing claimants rather than let them persist as splinters. **Loop** Granting real Governor Assignments to multiple heirs is what makes the next leader-loss near-certainly contested; Fratricide closes that reopening loop but converts a recurring *external* civil-war risk into a recurring *internal* assassination risk — it trades escalation paths, not eliminates risk.
- **Mughal War of Succession (1657–1661)** — *§1.0c Court Attendance/Hostage-Kin, §1.0a Tribunal severity, §2.2 contender formula, §7 social-contest resolver.* → **Card** Intrigue; trigger `succession contest active AND a contender's Church-Disposition/Conviction diverges from the dominant framework` (seeded by Grudges); Denounce-as-Heretic routes the target through the Church Tribunal — conviction guts their Influence-based Stage-1 pool, acquittal backlashes on the accuser; FO = a convicted-but-not-eliminated contender's still-loyal Governors seed a "Loyalist Holdout" Crisis keeping the contest live. **Action** (a) Court Attendance/Hostage-Kin for heirs (extends §1.0c from absentee-governors to Crown heirs); (b) Denounce as Heretic (new Intrigue social contest via the Tribunal branch). **Loop** Keeping an heir at court trades a Suspicion/Treasury cost now for lower future claim-basis strength; leaving heirs as independent subadar-governors (as Shah Jahan did) inflates all their strengths symmetrically, guaranteeing a multi-front contest — and once open, Denounce cuts a rival's pool without a battle, at a priced tit-for-tat.
- **Inca Civil War / Huáscar vs Atahualpa (c. 1529–1532)** — *§2.1 Smooth/Regency rows, §2.3–2.4, `faction_action.py` Conquest.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `succession_rule=="Partible/Panaca" AND leader eliminated AND ≥2 heirs each already holding independent territory+Military`; no political Stage-1 resolution exists (no shared territory to allocate) — routes straight to armed Conquest; FO = the winner emerges Military/Treasury-exhausted ("Weakened Victor" modifier) raising an external-faction Opportunity/Crisis draw (Pizarro 1532). **Action** Partible Succession (durable faction-configuration Precedent flag set at founding): maximizes the living dynasty's footprint but disables the peaceful Smooth/Regency rows forever. **Loop** Adopts near-term expansion for a structural guarantee that the same independent Military that made each heir strong is what makes them fight; set-once and irreversible (panaca as multi-generation institution), it can only be avoided by never adopting it, not managed well after.
- **Muscovite Time of Troubles (1598–1613)** — *§2.1 Regency/3-Accounting collapse, Part 7, §1.3a stale-high Assessment revolt logic.* → **Card** Intrigue; trigger `regency_active AND Π≥mid AND Reputation∈{Weak,Hated}` (fed by a stale Assessment or unrelieved Need) → an unverified External-backed pretender; Investigate exposes/voids it, ignoring treats the claim as real at next Stage-1; FO = an unresolved pretender seeds a "Second Pretender" (the False Dmitrys), each raising Π toward the 3-Accounting collapse. **Action** Convene Consulta (Regency election branch, after 2 not 3 failed Accountings): tally Standing+Disposition+Influence, majority wins, bypassing the dice resolver (Zemsky Sobor electing Mikhail Romanov). **Loop** Running Regency to term lets pretender Intrigue compound (and a simultaneous stale Assessment/unrelieved Need independently raises the *same* pretender card's weight); convening a Consulta early spends capital to close the loop, at the cost of crowning the most *institutional* rather than most *military* claimant.
- **Toluid Civil War (Mongol, 1260–1264)** — *§2.2 institutional basis, §2.3 G-based, §2.5 re-merge, §1.4 Directive template for Recall, Part 7.* → **Card** Friction; trigger `succession contest active AND a contender holds an appanage-style Military grant AND their External-backing exceeds the capital Inner Circle's combined Standing` → able to convene a rival legitimating assembly; Comply writes a `venue-controls-legitimacy` Precedent lowering future threshold, Defy forces the §2.3 SPLIT *overriding* the strength-gap math even where G≥3 would resolve Unified; FO = dueling assemblies seed a "Rival Successor-State Sets Own Directive" Friction every season, *exempted* from §2.5 re-merge (the permanent Yuan/Chagatai/Golden-Horde/Ilkhanate split despite Kublai's dominance). **Action** Recall (new Directive type alongside Extract/Tax/Suppress/Install/Host/Cede) to disband an appanage prince's forces — Defy here is diagnostic; and Convene Rival Assembly (Contest-stage exploit controlling which claim-basis holders attend). **Loop** Appanage grants raise Contest strength *and* let a loser convene a rival assembly and Defy the verdict, so the same policy that decides who wins determines whether the loser's refusal is survivable — turning a recoverable splinter into a permanent fracture the Recall lever was never used to prevent.
- **Kongo Civil Wars (17th–18th c.)** — *§2.1–2.2 elective/institutional basis as the standing rule, §1.3a Levy method pattern, §1.3c Ratify/Reject/Amend, §1.6 Outlawed family.* → **Card** Ambition→Crisis; trigger `settlement has an untaxed trade node (external Wealth bypassing the Levy method) AND lineage_head.Standing ≥ elector threshold` → "Lineage Head Arms Retinue"; License-now (Trade Writ Ratify) caps independent Wealth at Disposition cost, Reject drives it underground (Outlawed+Grudge), ignore lets Wealth/Military compound; FO = an un-Ratified compounding lineage converts to a Crisis at next leader-loss ("Comparable-Standing Lineages Contest the Capital"). **Action** (a) Elective Succession (config flag routing contested rows to a §2.2 institutional electoral tally); (b) Trade Writ (new Levy method, Ratify/Reject/Amend a trade node's Wealth flow). **Loop** Elective monarchy has electors not armies decide, but electors reward capability so lineages keep private Wealth/Military topped up; if the untaxed-trade leak is never closed via a Trade Writ, a vacancy lets several comparably-armed lineages contest directly — "elective monarchy legitimizes an arms race that periodically breaks out anyway" (the Kimpanzu/Kinlaza pattern).

### 2.2 Trade blockade, embargo, chokepoint loss (Geopolitical ↔ Economic)
*Key: `historical-concerns-catalogue/trade-blockade-embargo-chokepoint`. The through-line: **an Embargo/Directive is a weapon a hegemon points at a third faction through its own subordinates**, and a chokepoint is a geography that converts Fortify into extraction — both generate deferred Grudge/Debt that seeds later Muster/Conquest.*

- **The Megarian Decree (Athens, 432 BCE)** — *Wikipedia; Livius.org; McDonald, Electronic Antiquity 2.3.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `target holds bloc-network trade-dependent Prosperity AND hegemon Grudge-tagged toward it AND hegemon cross-faction Standing ≥ target's, mid-high Π`; resolved hegemon-side as Sponsor+Directive — target's governor gets a forced Comply/Bargain/Defy over "how much of my own trade do I sacrifice"; FO = unresolved 2+ seasons escalates to a bloc-wide Muster among the target's treaty allies (the embargo becomes casus belli). **Action** Embargo (new Directive type targeting a *third* faction, not the settlement). **Loop** A high-Standing hegemon with a Grudge crashes a rival's trade network at zero direct AP cost, converting political leverage into economic warfare; the target's only counters are Bargain or Defy-via-Treat, and non-resolution feeds Π/Grudge until it seeds a bloc Muster — the cost is deferred to the hegemon's allies' war-readiness.
- **Danish Sound Dues / Øresundstolden (1429–1857)** — *Wikipedia; STRO/UNESCO; havneguide.dk.* → **Card** Opportunity; trigger `chokepoint tag AND FacilityTier≥2 AND low-mid Π`; unlocks the Toll Levy method (auto-succeeding income, no roll); no Comply/Bargain/Defy — it's a standing income stream; FO = sustained Toll seeds a "rival contests the strait" Friction once a foreign faction's own toll-Debt crosses threshold (→ negotiated Compact or escalating Grudge/Conquest weight). **Action** Toll (chokepoint-gated Levy method, hard-gated on prior Fortify FacilityTier). **Loop** Fortify at a chokepoint pays for itself through Toll extraction (FacilityTier is the multiplier), but sustained extraction accrues Debt/Grudge that seeds the Friction contesting the strait — the toll's success is what raises the Conquest-weight against the settlement that built it.
- **Portuguese Cartaz System (Indian Ocean, 16th c.)** — *Wikipedia; historicalleys; jihku.org.* → **Card** Friction; trigger `faction holds ≥2 Fortify-built naval settlements ringing a zone AND standing naval Muster AND a rival's traffic lacks a License/Compact`; unlicensed-vessel encounter → Hold-Court adjudication (seize/burn for Treasury+Grudge, or retroactive licensing fee→ongoing License); FO = 3+ Grudge tags seed Intrigue (smuggling, false-flag passes) not open war. **Action** License Waters (new verb, joint *Levy+Fortify* precondition licensing a *zone* not a settlement, with patrol upkeep). **Loop** Enforcement credibility is a direct function of prior Fortify+Muster; upkeep lapses spawn smuggler Opportunity cards; successful enforcement is self-financing via License income but the accumulating Grudge feeds rivals' Conquest weight against the ports — self-financing but not self-stabilizing.
- **Closure of the Hanseatic Kontor at Novgorod (1494)** — *Wikipedia Peterhof; Springer, The Hanse in Medieval & Early Modern Europe (2022).* → **Card** Intrigue; trigger `a foreign faction holds a Charter granting trade-monopoly rights AND their upstream hinterland Prosperity declining ≥2 seasons AND your Standing over them rising`; seize agents (severe Grudge/Debt + one-time Treasury/Prosperity gain as trade reroutes) vs leave intact; FO = the expelled faction's allies get an "exiles seek redress" Intrigue, and local Petition dip if Actors depended on the Kontor. **Action** Revoke Franchise (Charter-seizure bypassing the Quo Warranto window, only vs a *foreign* Charter once its leverage has eroded). **Loop** Available only once the holder's upstream Prosperity has independently declined (read off their sheet, not caused by you); taking it redirects trade immediately but writes a durable Grudge/Hated tag seeding long-tail Intrigue.
- **Barbary Tribute System (16th–19th c.)** — *NMAD "Piracy and Passports"; Wikipedia Barbary Wars; Clements Library.* → **Card** Friction; trigger `coastal Treasury above a raider-attractiveness threshold AND no naval Fortify/Muster on file AND raider aggression active`; without prior investment the fork collapses to Comply-only (a recurring per-season Treasury levy + Debt tag); with investment, Defy (a punitive fleet) becomes available; FO = unresolved tribute Debt auto-seeds a "tribute unsustainable" Crisis (the shift to the First/Second Barbary Wars). **Action** Standing Fleet (Fortify+Muster investment gating a Defy branch — the punitive-fleet option literally doesn't exist until banked). **Loop** No investment = mechanically locked into Comply (Treasury bleed + Debt) every firing; investing converts the card into a real fork and a failed Defy escalates it to Crisis — the investment adds an option *and* raises the stakes of using it.
- **Napoleon's Continental System (1806–1814)** — *Wikipedia Berlin Decree; napoleon-series.org; Mises Institute.* → **Card** Friction; trigger `settlement subordinate to a hegemon's active Embargo Directive AND unmet Needs from foregone trade exceed available AP/Treasury, mid Π`; Comply (full enforcement, unmet-Need strain), Bargain (petition a License exception via Treat — a chit called in later), Defy/Divert (quiet non-enforcement, Standing-debt, smuggling-Reputation risk); FO = widespread License exceptions seed a smuggling Opportunity/Intrigue, and accumulated hegemon Standing-debt seeds an "embargo is dead in practice" Crisis forcing the hegemon's own Directive to be revised. **Action** License Exception (Treat-chit extension of the Embargo Directive — a bounded licensed corridor, each a Debt-family chit). **Loop** Strict Comply crashes local Prosperity/Order (Π up, smuggling cards seeded); Treat-exceptions relieve local pressure but each is a standing Debt chit that bloc-wide erodes the embargo — individually rational governor choices *are* the historical system's self-undermining collapse, reproduced emergently.
- **Union Blockade / Anaconda Plan (1861–1865)** — *Encyclopedia Virginia; Wikipedia; USNI Naval History.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `rival Prosperity sourced overwhelmingly from one export (single-commodity Develop tag) AND hostile naval Muster + river-chokepoint Conquest targets active`; export Prosperity progressively zeroed as chokepoints fall; the blockaded governor narrows to Comply (Develop-diversify, slow) or Sponsor a blockade-runner (Treasury clawback at Order/Corruption risk); FO = sustained success seeds successive Crisis escalations (garrison funding collapse), a broken runner network seeds a corruption Intrigue. **Action** Blockade-Runner Sponsorship (Sponsor defender-exploit) / Naval Blockade Directive+Conquest (attacker). **Loop** A prior single-commodity Develop concentration is disproportionately vulnerable; the defender's only short-of-military lever is runner-Sponsorship, which recovers Treasury but risks Corruption seeding further Crises — the commodity-concentration choice made seasons earlier decides survivable vs existential.
- **ABCD Line & US Oil Embargo on Japan (1940–41)** — *Wikipedia ABCD Encirclement / Pearl Harbor prelude; Defense Media Network.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `rival Fiscal Stance draws a supermajority of one strategic resource from a single external source AND coalition Grudge/Standing crosses a high threshold at high Π`; the coalition embargo *removes the Bargain branch* — only Comply (capitulate, forced Charter/territory concessions) or Defy (Muster into open war); FO = Defy auto-seeds a full Conquest cycle, Comply seeds a hawkish internal Ambition chain (coup/hardline succession). **Action** Multilateral Embargo (coalition-coordinated Directive gated through Ministry/Consulta). **Loop** A prior single-resource Fiscal-Stance concentration makes the faction targetable; the coordinated embargo collapses the three-way fork to binary, and either choice feeds `faction_action.py` Conquest/Muster weighting next season — the card structurally forecloses a core response option.
- **Berlin Blockade (1948–49)** — *State Dept Office of the Historian; Wikipedia.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `settlement is an enclave (no contiguous land supply route) AND a rival's Standing/Muster advantage in the surround crosses a threshold`; land-Levy/supply auto-fails; if an alternate-access FacilityTier was banked, Sponsor an Alternate Corridor (ongoing AP+Treasury, bleeds Π down); if not, Order/Prosperity decays toward loss (Conquest-by-attrition, no roll); FO = a sustained corridor seeds a "different pretext" Friction, a failed corridor seeds automatic territory-transfer. **Action** Alternate Corridor (Sponsor extension, hard-preconditioned on a prior Fortify airbase/corridor FacilityTier — cannot be reacted into existence mid-Crisis). **Loop** Survival is set entirely by a Fortify investment made years earlier; the Crisis is a retroactive audit; Sponsoring the corridor once it fires bleeds Π down and denies the attrition-Conquest, converting siege into a stalemate the besieger must formally lift.
- **Suez Crisis & Canal Closure (1956)** — *Wikipedia Suez; History.com Aswan; Forbes.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `a foreign faction's prior Sponsor/Treat funding to a chokepoint settlement is withdrawn (Friction fires) AND the Charter is foreign-held AND local Grudge is high`; seeds a Nationalize Charter opportunity (seize outside the 16-season window), risking a Charter-holder coalition Conquest — resolved *not* by the battle but escalating to a Ministry/Consulta arbitration that can force reversal or ratify regardless of the battlefield; FO = arbitrated reversal writes a `chokepoint charters are contestable by force` Precedent lowering future bypass bars; ratification seeds a long Reputation/Grudge chain. **Action** Nationalize Charter (crisis-gated Quo Warranto bypass). **Loop** Only attractive once an external Friction (patron withdrawal) has weakened the holder — you exploit an opportunity the world generated; the outcome depends on Standing/alliances feeding the arbitration as much as the campaign, closing the loop back into faction-ladder Standing.

### 2.3 Siege, sack, occupation, garrison/billeting crises (Geopolitical)
*Key: `siege_sack_occupation_garrison_crises`. Two spines: **unpaid garrisons treat plunder as wages** (Contribution System / Settle Arrears / mutiny contagion), and **pre-crisis Fortify tier is the win/loss table** (Inundation, Coalition Pool, Circumvallate).*

- **Sack of Magdeburg (1631)** — *§1.3/§1.3a/§1.4/§1.6.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Extract/Tax at severe magnitude AND Defy AND Π≥8 AND no relief in range AND besieger.Treasury < garrison upkeep (unpaid-troops flag)`; on storm: Order→0, catastrophic Prosperity, a durable regionally-legible `Hated`/legendary-infamy Reputation; the besieger's unpaid-garrison Debt turns plunder into wages, worsening the severity table even under restraint orders; FO = an empire-wide `magdeburgisieren` Precedent that *lowers* Defy-appeal locally (terror compliance) while *raising* Grudge/Intrigue regionally — a Precedent that cuts opposite ways by scale. **Action** Contribution System / Kontributionssystem (new negotiated-extorted Levy method, sibling to Encabezamiento, funding garrison upkeep from the occupied region). **Loop** Besieger Treasury below upkeep modifies the Crisis severity roll upward regardless of officer intent; the Contribution System keeps it solvent pre-siege and removes the modifier, at the cost of Debt/Grudge tags in the *occupied* territory taxed.
- **Spanish Fury at Antwerp (1576)** — *§1.3/§1.6 Debt "fires when due"/Treat chit, ED-SE-0005.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Debt(garrison_pay) unresolved ≥3 seasons AND besieger Treasury critically low AND host Prosperity high`; garrison mutinies and sacks its own host — Order/Prosperity collapse + a Grudge written at *every loyalist settlement that hears* (contagion); FO = a "loyalist provinces petition to remove the garrison" Friction chaining to a cross-settlement Treat/Bargain coalition (Pacification-of-Ghent analogue). **Action** Settle Arrears (new Levy sub-action: pay down garrison-pay Debt before it escalates, resetting the clock). **Loop** Letting the Debt go uncalled raises Mutiny draw-weight and severity; Settle Arrears removes the trigger; if it fires, contagion Grudge at *other* settlements raises their future Defy-propensity — mutiny as contagious political-capital loss.
- **Siege of Leiden (1574)** — *§1.3 Fortify/Sponsor, §1.6.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `under active siege AND Π≥8 AND Defense below survival threshold`; Order/Prosperity attrition scales with duration and starting Defense, prior Fortify reduces both; FO = *surviving* unlocks a Sponsor "Found an Institution" (university/charter) card converting the ordeal into durable `Generous`/`Just` Reputation + Precedent. **Action** Inundation (mid-Crisis-only Fortify-adjacent method: trade Prosperity — flood productive land — for an immediate Defense/relief bonus). **Loop** Pre-crisis Fortify sets the baseline casualty/duration table exactly when the card fires; Inundation is the costly fallback when that was insufficient, and surviving unlocks the Sponsor payoff no Fortify-only path grants.

### 2.5 Flood, drought, siltation, resource-geography shifts (Geographical)
*Key: `historical-concerns-catalogue-flood-drought-siltation-resource-geography`. The through-line: **slow hidden stats (SiltLevel, OreGrade, SalinityLevel, SubsidenceRisk, RisingWaterLevel) are long fuses** — an engineering/extraction method choice at one node silently changes another node's Crisis odds seasons later, and standing institutions (Water Board, Water Magistracy, Ever-Normal Granary, River Conservancy) are the only pre-emptive defusers.*

- **The Nilometer / flood-indexed taxation (Egypt)** — *Wikipedia Nilometer; NatGeo.* → **Card** Friction→Crisis; trigger `Gauge facility AND FloodIndex < relief_threshold AND Levy fixed/unadjusted AND Directive extraction pending`; Comply (honor the gauge, cut the rate: Order+1, Disposition+1), Bargain (split with the quota, `partial-relief-precedent`), Defy (PS−1, Order−1, Grudge, Π+2); FO = Defy seeds a "Famine Unrest" Crisis; repeated Comply seeds a `gauge-bound assessment` Precedent locking future Levy to the reading. **Action** Gauge-Indexed Levy (new Levy method reading its rate off a tracked environmental stat, auto-scaling without a per-season roll). **Loop** Installing it ties the Levy formula to FloodIndex so a bad flood auto-reduces extraction and denies the Friction its escalation; a fixed rate re-rolls the same Friction every low-flood season with rising Π/Grudge until a Defy chain forces the costlier emergency Bargain/Defy the gauge would have pre-empted.
- **Yellow River course change / Pan Jixun's engineering (Ming)** — *Wikipedia Yellow River / Pan Jixun / Hongze Lake; The China Project.* → **Card** Crisis (Friction precursor); trigger `SiltLevel > capacity AND Directive requires sustained canal throughput AND no River Conservancy office`; Comply-Scour (narrow channel, raise dikes: Treasury+1 but plants a hidden RisingWaterLevel tag on a *downstream* settlement incrementing every season), Bargain-Broad-Relief (widen, sacrifice capacity, reduce downstream risk), Defy (canal silts shut, regional collapse); FO = Scour seeds a delayed "Rising Lake Threatens [Settlement]" Crisis firing only once the downstream tag crosses threshold — potentially decades later, independent of that settlement's choices. **Action** River Conservancy Directorship (Fortify Scour-vs-Broad-Relief fork + a province-tier standing office like a Ministry seat). **Loop** Scour raises the capital-adjacent settlement's Prosperity/Defense but writes a slow-incrementing tag onto a different settlement that nothing there can see or defuse — a Fortify choice at one node silently changes Crisis odds at another many seasons later (the multi-decade fuse).
- **Zwin siltation / fall of Bruges, rise of Antwerp** — *Wikipedia Zwin/Bruges; Cambridge Urban History.* → **Card** Friction→Crisis; trigger `harbor SiltLevel rising N seasons with no Dredge AND Guild Standing high enough to demand protective tariffs`; Comply-Guild-tariff (Guild+2, Merchant−2, foreign merchants shift to a rival), Bargain (dredge + hold tariffs flat: Treasury−1, SiltLevel reset, trade retained), Defy the Guild (Grudge but merchants retained); FO = Comply seeds a "Merchant Exodus" Crisis — permanent Prosperity/trade-Reputation transfer to a named rival (Antwerp's rise). **Action** Dredge Harbor (Develop funding/method resetting SiltLevel; pairs with Treat holding tariffs down). **Loop** Skipping Dredge lets SiltLevel accumulate silently until it combines with Guild Standing to trigger the Friction, and Comply converts a still-reversible decline into a permanent Reputation transfer; dredging (or Treat) keeps SiltLevel below the trigger and denies the rival its Opportunity gain.
- **Potosí silver depletion / mita / amalgamation** — *PMC mercury/Andean silver; Moore JPE (2010); Encyclopedia.com Potosí.* → **Card** Opportunity→Friction/Crisis; trigger `mine OreGrade below the level to meet a standing silver-quota AND Treasury demand unchanged`; Comply-Mita (forced draft: Output held, Order−2, Grudge(indigenous) each season), Bargain (wage/partial mix, Grudge slowed), Defy (output crashes, Directive penalty); FO = sustained Mita seeds an "Uprising" Crisis once Grudge crosses threshold + a permanent PS-decline tag (the mita's demographic toll). **Action** Mita Conscription (coercive Levy method) + Amalgamation (Sponsor/Develop process upgrade raising yield-per-OreGrade — the patio process). **Loop** Mita buys time against depletion by substituting coerced labor for ore quality but each season adds Grudge force-drawing the Uprising and permanent PS damage; Amalgamation raises yield directly, letting Treasury cost substitute for Grudge and avoiding the coercive path entirely.
- **St. Elizabeth's Flood of 1421 (Netherlands)** — *Wikipedia St. Elizabeth's flood / Water board (note: boards predate the flood).* → **Card** Crisis (Friction precursor: dike funds diverted to a war Directive); trigger `flood-defense Maintenance below threshold (diverted to a war levy or weakened by peat extraction) AND a severe-storm roll`; Comply (emergency Fortify at high cost, partial), Bargain (evacuate/cede the lowest terrain: permanent terrain-loss tag, limited PS loss), Defy (catastrophic PS collapse, mass Grudge, a permanent Biesbosch-equivalent geography tag, `dikes neglected` Precedent); FO = catastrophic resolution seeds cross-settlement Petition pressure to charter a standing self-funded Fortify institution — a win condition, not an automatic grant. **Action** Water Board (standing flood-defense institution funded by its own dedicated levy, ring-fenced from Directive diversion). **Loop** Complying with a war Directive by diverting maintenance lowers the flood-defense stat (higher trigger+severity); a Water Board decouples flood funding from Directive competition so war-funding choices *cannot* starve flood defense — cutting the Crisis's odds independent of the Directive.
- **Bedford Level Corporation / draining the Fens** — *Wikipedia Bedford Level / Vermuyden; Britannica.* → **Card** Opportunity + Friction/Crisis chain; trigger `undeveloped wetland tag AND a private-capital Sponsor offers to fund reclamation`; Comply/charter (Prosperity+ new taxable land, Grudge(commoners), `enclosure permitted` Precedent, Concession to the corporation), Bargain (with compensation, less land, no Grudge), Defy (opportunity expires); FO = a granted charter seeds two delayed chains — a near-term "Fen Riot" Friction from the Grudge, and a long-fuse "Peat Subsidence Flood" Crisis once a hidden SubsidenceRisk stat crosses threshold. **Action** Charter Reclamation Corporation (fourth Develop funding method: private capital reclaims marginal terrain for a standing Concession + reduced governor control). **Loop** The chartered method converts terrain to Prosperity quickly with someone else's capital, but the open-channel drainage engineering plants a SubsidenceRisk stat that eventually force-draws a flood Crisis on the *same land the Opportunity granted* — today's method choice constrains which cards the settlement draws for its lifetime.

- **Erzgebirge Bergordnungen / mining boom-bust** — *Wikipedia Bergordnung / Freiberg / Berggeschrey; Montanregion documentation.* → **Card** Opportunity→Friction→Ambition; trigger `mine OreGrade below the baseline assumed at charter issuance AND a fixed regalian mining-charter tag still active (no Tribunal renegotiation)`; Comply (hold fixed Zehnt: Treasury shortfall, Miners−2), Bargain (Tribunal review renegotiates down), Defy (enforce harshly: Grudge, population-transfer follow_on); FO = unresolved Friction seeds an Opportunity "New Rush" at a rival settlement (migrating miners/capital) — a settlement-to-settlement resource-geography transfer. **Action** Regalian Mining Charter with Tribunal renegotiation (depleting-resource-keyed Levy cross-referencing OreGrade). **Loop** Locking the rate at peak and never renegotiating means it doesn't scale down as OreGrade depletes, so Grudge/Friction odds rise as the fixed-terms/real-yield gap widens; running the Tribunal review before threshold keeps the rate credible and denies the rival its departing-miner seed.
- **Venice's river diversions / Magistrato alle Acque** — *Wikipedia Magistrato alle acque; ResearchGate lagoon diversions.* → **Card** Friction (standing, recurring); trigger `shared province-tier Lagoon/Harbor SiltLevel rising from mainland discharge AND a settlement-level Develop request pending with no province Water Magistracy`; Comply-veto (subordinate the request: lagoon protected, local Disposition−1, `province water authority binds` Precedent), Bargain (supervised partial diversion), Defy (settlement acts unilaterally: SiltLevel rises, Precedent-breach); FO = repeated Defy accumulates SiltLevel toward a province-wide "Harbor Choked" Crisis affecting every coastal settlement's trade. **Action** Water Magistracy (province-tier veto institution extending Ministry/Consulta, overriding a settlement's Develop when its externalities threaten a shared downstream resource). **Loop** The Magistracy inserts a check on shared SiltLevel before any Develop request against the lagoon is approved, preventing the province Crisis from accumulating; without it each Develop is locally rational but collectively silts the shared resource, forcing a Crisis costing every settlement more than the vetoed actions would have.
- **Canal siltation / salinization in southern Mesopotamia** — *Wikipedia (Sumer salt); Springer, Soil Salinity Historical Perspectives.* → **Card** Friction→Crisis→Ambition; trigger `farmland SalinityLevel rising over consecutive high-intensity-irrigation seasons with no Corvée-desilting Levy, crossing crop-viability thresholds`; Comply-Corvée (labor draft: Order−1, salinity growth slowed), Bargain (partial corvée + crop substitution wheat→barley, lower ceiling), Defy (SalinityLevel rises uncapped); FO = sustained ignoring seeds a "Land Exhausted" chain culminating in Prosperity/PS collapse + a Standing/Recognition transfer to a rival (Sumer→Akkad). **Action** Corvée Desilting Levy (recurring labor-tax suppressing SalinityLevel) + Crop Substitution (Develop-tier adaptive swap trading ceiling for lower Crisis odds). **Loop** Running the Corvée each season keeps SalinityLevel below crop-loss thresholds at a recurring Order cost; skipping lets it compound irreversibly (wheat permanently lost by ~2000 BC), progressively lowering the ceiling until Crop Substitution becomes forced not chosen, and past the final threshold fires the depopulation chain transferring Standing to a rival.

### 2.6 Famine and multi-year crop failure (Climatic/Geographical)
*Key: `historical-concerns-catalogue:famine-multiyear-crop-failure`. The through-line: **a standing storage/entitlement Facility (Granary Prefecture, Ever-Normal Granary, Grain Ministry) determines whether a harvest-collapse roll is survivable, and an ideological/fixed Precedent (rigid price ceiling, fixed quota, free-trade doctrine) set earlier constrains which relief options the Crisis even offers.***

- **Great Famine of 1315–1317** — *W.C. Jordan, The Great Famine (Princeton 1996); Wikipedia.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Harvest_Yield<0.5 for 2 seasons AND Treasury_Grain_Reserve below a Facility-gated threshold` → "Grain Riot"; Comply (rigid price ceiling: Order gain but plants Precedent raising next-Crisis severity as merchants withhold), Bargain (merchant compact/export exemptions: Compact tag, avoids escalation), Defy (immediate Order loss, no durable tag); FO = Comply-with-ceiling seeds an escalated Grain Riot next season (traders defect), Bargain seeds a "Merchant Compact Renewal" Opportunity. **Action** Price Ceiling / Export Embargo (method-variant on Levy/Treat). **Loop** The Price-Ceiling method suppresses immediate Order loss but attaches a Precedent increasing escalation weight if the shortage persists; prior Develop in grain-reserve FacilityTier lowers the odds the threshold is crossed at all and, if it fires anyway, unlocks a cheaper Bargain instead of forcing Comply/Defy.
- **Rome's Cura Annonae / Egyptian grain** — *Tacitus, Annals III–IV; Wikipedia cura annonae; Ancient History Sites.* → **Card** Petition; trigger `capital Population above threshold AND Grain_Import_Route disrupted` → "Bread Dole Demand", escalating to "Urban Riot"; Comply (fund dole/Prefect: Treasury cost, Reputation gain, plants an `Entitlement` Precedent making future refusal costly), Bargain (capped dole, smaller Precedent), Defy (Order collapse→Crisis); FO = Comply seeds a recurring "Dole Renewal" Petition; Defy seeds Urban Riot then a rival-exploits-unrest Intrigue. **Action** Granary Prefecture (capital-only Facility unlocking a Levy "Subsidized Distribution" method-variant). **Loop** The Facility raises FacilityTier lowering the AP cost/failure of Subsidized Distribution when the Petition fires; but Complying plants an Entitlement Precedent making any future Defy of a grain obligation cost extra Disposition — a granted entitlement is politically hard to revoke.

### 2.7 Plague and epidemic disease, climate/trade-correlated (Climatic ↔ Geographical/Economic)
*Key: `historical-concerns-plague-epidemic-v1`. The through-line: **the vector is a governance action** — Refuge Walls overcrowding, an un-staged Muster redeploy, a single-corridor Develop, a famine-relief Treat, a Host Directive routing troops, a waived quarantine inspection — and the response verb (Force/Segregation) that ends the epidemic manufactures the next political Crisis.*

- **Plague of Athens (430 BCE)** — *Thucydides II; NatGeo; HistorySkills.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Π≥8 AND recent Fortify(Refuge Walls) within 2 seasons AND Order≤3 AND Overcrowding tag`; Sponsor relief (Order+1, Π−2, downgrades but doesn't clear Overcrowding), Force (Order+1 but Disposition−1 all Actors, Grudge), Ignore (Π+3, patron-NPC mortality check); FO = a failed patron mortality check seeds a succession Crisis (Pericles' death mid-plague), a persisting Overcrowding reseeds the Crisis next eligible season. **Action** Refuge Walls (fourth Fortify method: shelter rural population for Defense+1/PS bump but writes a durable Overcrowding tag unless paired same-season with Sponsor relief or Fortify:Walls sized to the influx). **Loop** Refuge Walls without same-season relief raises Π and stacks Overcrowding — the literal trigger of the Epidemic card, so the defensive choice reproduces itself; if it fires and the patron check fails, the succession vacancy removes the AP/Standing the governor needed for the relief Sponsor — the historical trap in mechanical form.
- **Antonine Plague (165–180 CE)** — *Wikipedia; WHE; PLOS One (2025) SIR/SEIR; Masaryk CEDRR.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `last action Conquest(frontier) AND next action Muster(redeploy to interior) AND NOT used Staged Demobilization AND Order≤4`; fires in every interior settlement receiving troops (Order−1, Prosperity−1, Mil−2/−3 reversing the season's troop purchase) unless Sponsor/Consent absorbs it; FO = repeated hits across the redistribution wave seed a faction-level "the muster route is a plague road" Friction raising the Ob on the faction's next Conquest via a lowered mil-advantage signal. **Action** Staged Demobilization (Muster sub-choice: extra Wealth + a season's delay to route legions through a quarantine interval, trading faster Mil gain for suppressing the trigger). **Loop** Frontier Conquest + an un-staged Muster is the mechanism raising the Crisis weight in every receiving settlement; if it fires it strips Mil from the very Muster that caused it, lowering the faction's mil-advantage signal and its Conquest weight next dispatch cycle — a direct write-back from event severity into the faction AI's action-selection odds.
- **Plague of Justinian (541–549 CE)** — *Wikipedia; OeAW; Mordechai & Eisenberg, Past & Present 244 (2019); Mordechai et al., PNAS 116(51) (2019).* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Precedent("single-corridor-trade") AND external_shock(climate) AND Prosperity drop ≥2 in one season AND Π≥7`; Sponsor Emergency Wage Edict (Order+1, floor held, Debt tag — the edict must be renewed or Disposition−2), Force (Order+1 but PS−1 + Grudge), Ignore (Π+2, Prosperity−2, `chokepoint confirmed fragile` deepens); FO = the deepened Precedent raises repeat-Crisis weight on the next external_shock + seeds a Guild Treat/Compact petition to formalize an alternate route. **Action** Diversify Corridor (Develop funding sub-method: 1 extra AP + slower Prosperity to route through 2+ independent corridors, writing a `route-diversified` Precedent lowering future weight). **Loop** The cheaper single-corridor Develop funding *writes the trigger predicate*, so a Prosperity-maximizing governor is most exposed when a climate shock lands; once it fires Keep Order narrows to the costlier Force unless Diversify Corridor or a Wage Edict was banked — the event retroactively punishes the earlier method choice.
- **The Black Death (1347–1351)** — *Bauch & Büntgen, Comm Earth & Env (2025); Wikipedia Black Death/migration; Cambridge news.* → **Card** Friction; trigger `Prosperity≤2 AND external_shock(climate/famine) AND Treasury≤threshold`; Treat (Emergency Grain Compact with a distant supplier: Prosperity+1 but writes a Debt tag AND a Contagion Vector flag), Directive-Bargain for Crown relief (partial, no Contagion flag, Standing-debt), Ignore/refuse (no flag but escalates to a Famine Crisis next season); FO = a Contagion-Vector-flagged settlement near-certainly draws a Plague Crisis next season regardless — the double-bind: grain now/plague later, or famine now with no plague. **Action** Emergency Grain Compact (Treat famine sub-case: Influence+history roll vs a distant supplier, Debt-chit + a Contagion Vector flag when the supplier is on an unvetted long-distance corridor). **Loop** The famine-relief Treat branch trades an immediate Prosperity save for a guaranteed-weight Plague draw via the Contagion Vector — the same climate shock that makes the Treat attractive is what carried the pathogen on the relief shipment, reproducing the historical double-edge instead of a free relief action.
- **Venice Lazzaretto / 1630–31 quarantine failure** — *Sapiens.org; CDC EID (2013); Alfani/Melegaro, Scientific Reports (2020); Wikipedia 1629–1631 Italian plague.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Quarantine_Tier≥1 (trade-vector protected) AND external_shock(military_transit — Host Directive or Levy troop movement) AND Π≥8`; because the tier only discounts *trade*-vector triggers, this fires at near-full severity regardless (Order−2, Prosperity−1, Reputation→Weak); responses apply at no discount; FO = seeds an Intrigue circulating the failure as evidence the Sanità is captured/incompetent → a Petition to defund it (rolling back the tier investment). **Action** Sanità Lazzaretto (Fortify facility, 2 AP+Treasury, granting a Quarantine Tier 0–3 reducing draw-weight of Plague cards whose trigger references a *trade/sea* vector — explicitly not military-transit). **Loop** The facility lowers Plague odds only along trade predicates, so a governor treating it as blanket protection and complying with a Host Directive routing troops through eats a full-severity Crisis, which writes the Reputation/Intrigue follow_on threatening the very investment that didn't cover the actual vector — asymmetric mitigation as mechanics, not flavor.
- **Cocoliztli epidemics 1545 & 1576 (New Spain)** — *Acuna-Soto et al., EID 8(4) (2002); PMC7110390; Wikipedia.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Assessment(assessed_base) AND seasons_since_Survey≥8 (never refreshed) AND external_shock(drought, multi-season) AND Prosperity < assessed_base×0.5`; the Extract Directive keeps charging the stale base through the drought (§1.3a failure mode), stripping below subsistence (Prosperity−3, Order−2, mass-mortality flag, Grudge(Crown)); FO = seeds a repeat harder-Ob Crisis each season until a Survey/Negotiate-Quota resets the figure — an escalating loop. **Action** Emergency Resurvey (extends the live Survey sub-verb: usable outside its ~8-season cooldown when an external_shock flag is active, at an Ob penalty, to refresh the Assessment before Extract strips the settlement). **Loop** Skipping Survey during a megadrought is a modeled trap: Extract reads a stale-high base and drives Prosperity below subsistence (the trigger), the Crisis spikes Π and writes Grudge(Crown) that raises the Ob on the *next* Survey/Negotiate attempt (Reputation Weak/Hated makes renegotiation harder) — the neglect that caused the collapse also makes it harder to fix.
- **Chongzhen drought / Great Plague of 1633–1644** — *Climate MDPI 11(3):71 (2023); Copernicus CoP (2024); Wikipedia late-Ming plague.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Granary_FacilityTier==0 (never Developed) AND external_shock(drought ≥3 seasons) AND faction military Levy-extraction ≥ high (war Directives crowding out settlement AP)`; famine→plague→unrest at full severity (Prosperity−3, Order−3, a rebel-warlord Ambition on a named Actor); Sponsor relief is the only mitigation but competes for the same AP the Extract Directive consumes; FO = if under-resourced, seeds a rebel Ambition (Li Zicheng) accelerating with every unserved famine Need → an open Conquest-target for a rival. **Action** Ever-Normal Granary (Develop funding method stockpiling a famine buffer, releasable via Sponsor, competing for the AP a Directive's military Extract already draws). **Loop** Heavy Levy/Extract starves the settlement's AP for the Granary Develop, so when the drought fires no buffer exists and the cascade hits full severity; the Order collapse + rebel Ambition further degrades Disposition, raising future Levy resistance and the faction's own extraction costs — granary neglect and extraction pressure feed each other bidirectionally.
- **Great Plague of Marseille (1720)** — *Wikipedia; STAT News (2020); Rodama blog; edwardworthlibrary.ie.* → **Card** Intrigue; trigger `Quarantine_Tier≥1 (Sanità exists) AND Guild Standing ≥ threshold AND Guild.Disposition(governor) high (patronage) AND a trade-fair Treasury deadline`; Hold Court gains a Ratify/Reject/Amend branch — Ratify (waive inspection: ship clears, Guild Influence+1, but the Quarantine_Tier discount is voided for that shipment, spiking the Plague Crisis weight to near-certain), Reject (Guild Master−2, Grudge, no spike), Investigate first (exposes the bribery vector for the four-way disposition); FO = Ratify seeds the Plague Crisis at near-guaranteed weight; if it fires, a Demotion chain against the Guild Master who pressured the waiver. **Action** Quarantine Waiver Petition (new Hold Court branch, structurally identical to Ordenanza Ratification §1.3c, Ratify voiding that shipment's tier protection). **Loop** A high-Standing/Disposition Guild spends patronage to push Hold Court toward Ratify, zeroing the Sanità discount for one shipment and spiking the Plague weight; if it fires, the Grudge/Reputation collapse hits both governor and the Guild Master's own Standing (a public-scandal-class §1.0a trigger dropping the Master 2 ranks) — the event punishes the same faction-ladder leverage that produced it.
- **Third Plague Pandemic (from 1894) — Hong Kong, Bombay, steamship spread** — *Wikipedia 1894 HK plague / Third pandemic; JMVH; Grokipedia Bombay.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `recent Keep Order(Force:Segregation Camps) within 2 seasons AND Order still ≤2 AND Grudge(magnitude≥2) AND Π≥8`; the prior forced-segregation resolves the epidemic pressure (Order recovers, Π drops) but the card is the political bill — an Intrigue "assassination plot" against the governor/administrator scaled to the Grudge the Force generated; FO = a successful assassination (W.C. Rand, 22 June 1897) forces a Recall/succession + Π spike; detected/countered → a durable Reputation hit without the succession shock. **Action** Segregation Camps (harsher Force sub-branch under Keep Order, buying a larger/faster Π/Order recovery at double the Disposition penalty + a guaranteed Grudge + an assassination-class Intrigue draw-weight flag). **Loop** Segregation Camps end a high-Π Plague Crisis fast (Order/Π recover) but the Disposition/Grudge cost is exactly the trigger for the assassination Intrigue — the action that solves the Crisis manufactures the next; because the underlying sanitation underinvestment that made Force "necessary" is untouched, repeated reaching for it accumulates Grudge faster than Order, converging on the Rand outcome rather than stability.

### 2.8 Multi-year climate-shift and storm/hurricane fleet destruction (Climatic)
*Key: `historical_concerns_climate_storm_fleet_destruction`. Two spines: **staging/timing exposure** (an anchored/late fleet feeds a shared storm-severity roll — Sea Wall, Storm-Season Withdrawal, Sheltered Dockyard, Flota Scheduling), and **correlated world-level shocks** (the Cooling flag / General Crisis — Remit vs Extract, Civic Granary, Adopt Local Practice, Annona route diversification).*

- **Kamikaze typhoons vs the Mongol fleets (1274 & 1281)** — *Britannica; Woodruff et al., Geology (2014); Wikipedia Mongol invasions/Genkō Bōrui.* → **Card** Crisis (Opportunity/Ambition follow-on for the defender); trigger `enemy Conquest targets a coastal settlement whose Defense is below the attacker's Mil-advantage AND the fleet staged/anchored ≥1 season within the storm-season window AND Π≥8`; a prayer Sponsor gives only a flat low bonus — the real fork is Defy (Fortify Sea Wall multiplying the attacker's storm-loss, or a preemptive Muster before the window closes); a storm firing against the attacker drops their Mil + writes a Grudge/Demoralization tag lowering future Muster-weight; FO = ignoring resolves Conquest at full Mil; a storm vs the attacker seeds a defender "Divine Reputation" Precedent boosting Recognition odds + an attacker command-failure Demotion Intrigue. **Action** Sea Wall (new Fortify method lowering coastal storm-Crisis severity) + Storm-Season Staging Exposure (Muster/Conquest timing rule: multi-season anchoring accrues a storm-loss multiplier scaled to season-of-year, rushed Levy/Muster shoddy hulls multiply it further). **Loop** Sea Wall lowers the storm-Crisis roll against an anchored fleet, reducing the attacker's effective Mil; an attacker who Musters on a compressed timeline or overstays the window raises that same roll against itself — both sides feed one shared roll from opposite ends, its outcome (Grudge on the loser, Precedent on the survivor) resetting both factions' future weighting.
- **Spanish Armada destroyed by Atlantic storms (1588)** — *Wikipedia Armada / Armada in Ireland; INA "Twelve Apostles" (Casaban); NASA Science.* → **Card** Friction→Crisis; trigger `a Conquest requiring fleet+land-army rendezvous fails its coordination check ("Rendezvous Failure") forcing the fleet onto a high-exposure route while Π is high AND the funding Levy under-supplied Treasury (spoiled-provisions flag)`; Comply (retreat as planned: full roll), Bargain (spend AP/Treasury to re-provision/re-route, e.g. a Treat safe-harbor deal), Defy (push the original route: command-Friction + full roll); failure writes Debt/`Weak` on the Crown + Grudge on the failed ally; FO = a resolved Crisis seeds a Directive-level "Rebuild" (Purpose-Built Galleon), funded by Treasury and gated by repaying the Crisis's Debt tag. **Action** Purpose-Built Galleon (new Levy quality/quantity fork: cheap/fast Levy fields more hulls at a higher storm multiplier — the 130-ship Armada; Purpose-Built costs more per hull but lowers it — the Twelve-Apostles asiento, unlocked only after a Crisis fires once). **Loop** Cheap Levy for a larger fleet raises the storm-severity roll once a Friction forces an exposed route; the Crisis's Debt/Reputation tags then gate the Treasury needed to fund the Purpose-Built upgrade — one bad Friction/Crisis chain can lock a faction out of exactly the mitigation it now needs most.
- **The Great Hurricane of 1780** — *Wikipedia; Britannica; JAR (2022); American Heritage.* → **Card** Crisis (symmetric, multi-faction); trigger `≥2 rival factions each maintain Conquest/Muster fleet presence in the same contested territory through the storm window (Π≥8 territory-wide) without a Storm-Season Withdrawal`; no Directive fork (it's positioning) — "Hold Station" (full roll against every co-present fleet) or "Storm-Season Withdrawal" (cede tempo, no roll); a fired Crisis reduces Mil for all present roughly proportional to fleet size, symmetrically — it does not by itself shift relative standing; FO = a shared "Rebuilding Race" Opportunity — whoever funds a compensatory Levy fastest regains Mil/tempo first, converting a symmetric Crisis into an asymmetric recovery contest. **Action** Storm-Season Withdrawal (Muster/Conquest timing sub-choice, parallel to Levy's Encabezamiento fork — the Royal Navy's real seasonal withdrawal convention). **Loop** Holding presence through the window raises trigger/severity for *every* co-present rival; because losses are correlated the storm decision doesn't change relative standing — pre-storm Treasury/Ledger state (who funds the post-Crisis Levy fastest) is the actual lever deciding who loses ground.
- **Great Famine of 1315–1317** *(climate-shift framing)* — *Wikipedia; Ludlow et al./Baek et al. (2020), Comm Earth & Env.* → **Card** Crisis (multi-season, Needs-driven); trigger `a famine-relief Need unserved ≥2 seasons compounding Π via Σ(unserved Needs) until Π≥8 with Prosperity below floor`; Comply (Extract/Tax held constant despite failed harvests: Order−1/PS−1 stack, Grudge, `Hated`), Bargain (petition the PA for remission: softens terms at suspicion cost), Defy/Divert (redirect Levy to relief: protects the settlement, Standing-debt up-tier, Disposition/PS up); FO = unserved 3+ seasons escalates to Order-collapse/revolt + a rival-patron-recruiting Intrigue. **Action** Civic Granary (fourth Develop funding method, Bruges/Ypres/London civic reserves, lowering both the famine severity roll and the Σ(unserved Needs) Π contribution) + Relief (new Directive type the PA issues instead of Extract/Tax). **Loop** Prior Civic Granary spend lowers this card's trigger weight/severity; a governor who instead Complies with a fixed Extract/Tax through the famine accelerates Π and locks in Grudge/`Hated`, reducing future Develop/Levy yields — the P5 Ledger-memory spiral anchored to a documented mitigation against a documented failure mode (fixed in-kind rent through failed harvests).
- **General/Global Crisis of the 17th century (Maunder Minimum onset)** — *Parker, Global Crisis (Yale 2013); Wikipedia General Crisis.* → **Card** Crisis (world/multi-settlement correlated); trigger `a world-level "Cooling Epoch" flag persists N year-arcs, applying a uniform Π-baseline increase + Prosperity-ceiling reduction across every settlement (inflating Σ(unserved Needs) system-wide)`; per-settlement it plays as ordinary Comply/Bargain/Defy but *correlated* — factions issuing Extract/Tax repeatedly accumulate Grudge/Suspicion/Debt across their whole roster in the same seasons, triggering multiple simultaneous Demotion Magnitude events on their own ladder; Remit-issuing factions absorb the shock at Treasury cost; FO = sustained rigid response seeds a cascading multi-settlement Crisis chain — several settlements independently crossing into collapse within the same 1–2 year-arc window. **Action** Remit (new Directive type: tax/tribute forgiveness at Treasury cost; a relief-configured Ministry (Part 7) gets an AP/Treasury discount on Remit during a Cooling flag — Parker's "flexible institutions weather the shock"). **Loop** The Cooling flag uniformly raises Π and lowers ceilings everywhere, so the PA's aggregate Extract-vs-Remit choice (and whether it built a relief Ministry) decides whether the shared shock is isolated survivable Crises or a simultaneous cascade — and a cascade forces concurrent Demotion Magnitude events stripping the governors needed to manage recovery, deepening the next Π spike.
- **Collapse of the Norse Greenland colonies (c. 1350–1450)** — *Wikipedia Norse Greenland/Hvalsey; Brown/D'Andrea et al., PNAS (2011).* → **Card** Ambition/Crisis (slow-burn, multi-decade); trigger `a distant settlement's Prosperity ceiling declines across year-arcs under a persistent Cooling flag AND it holds a Compact/Debt to a metropole AND its Standing-4+ governor accrues Suspicion via repeated Court Attendance failures (§1.0c)`; Comply (maintain metropole Develop practice, keep servicing the Compact: Prosperity keeps declining), Bargain (petition for resupply: mild relief if Treasury/attention available, increasingly unlikely), Defy/Divert (Adopt Local Practice: a Disposition hit with the metropole for real resilience); FO = sustained Court Attendance failure + unresolved Debt seeds the metropole's implicit "abandonment" Directive — no more ships, a slow Dismissal-equivalent. **Action** Adopt Local Practice (new Develop method-fork for isolated/frontier settlements: trade a Precedent/Disposition shift with the metropole — read as drift/heterodoxy — for a durable resilience bonus against Cooling decline; models Thule marine subsistence vs Norse mixed-farming rigidity, flagging the post-2011 isotopic nuance that diet did shift without full technique adoption). **Loop** A settlement that never spends on Adopt Local Practice keeps bleeding Prosperity, lowering its AP and making both the pivot and Court Attendance's Treasury cost less affordable; on the metropole side, failing Court Attendance/Compact raises Suspicion and reduces willingness to fund resupply Sponsors — a two-sided disinvestment spiral ending in implicit abandonment rather than one dramatic Crisis.
- **The Great Storm of 1703** — *Wikipedia; RMG Greenwich; History.com.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `a naval-basing settlement's Fortify method on file is "Roadstead Mooring" (exposed/cheap) rather than "Sheltered Dockyard" AND Π≥8 within the storm window`; Comply (accept the roll: full ship/Mil loss proportional to fleet moored in the open), Defy-in-advance (pay to relocate to Sheltered Dockyard before the window closes, sharply reducing severity); FO = a fired Crisis unlocks a Develop "Lighthouse" (Eddystone rebuild) + an "Underwriting" Opportunity (marine-insurance Sponsor/Treat mitigating future storm Treasury loss at an ongoing premium). **Action** Sheltered Dockyard (new Fortify basing-location method) + Lighthouse (Develop follow-on locked until a storm Crisis fires once). **Loop** Cheap Roadstead-Mooring saves AP now but raises the storm roll's trigger/severity; the Crisis's resolution is what unlocks the Sheltered Dockyard upgrade and the Lighthouse/Underwriting follow-ons — the mitigation most needed is gated behind having first suffered the loss.

### 2.9 Currency debasement, fiscal collapse, sovereign default, tax-base crises (Economic)
*Key: `currency_debasement_fiscal_collapse_distillation_v1`. The through-line: the proposed **Capital-Posture Ledger family** (Debased / Speculative) as a one-way ratchet — Levy:Debase / Charter Fiat / Farm buy Treasury by stacking a tag that raises all future Levy Ob and fiscal-Crisis weight, and the only exits (Recoinage / Currency Reset / Funding & Assumption) grow more expensive the longer deferred.*

- **Roman Third-Century Crisis debasement / Diocletian's edicts** — *TheCollector; Wikipedia Edict on Maximum Prices.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Capital-Posture:Debased present AND Prosperity down 2+ seasons AND Π≥8`; Comply (Tribunal price/wage cap: Order+1 short-term, `Harsh` Reputation, seeds a black-market Intrigue rather than fixing anything), Bargain (Guild-scoped cap, Concession), Defy (prices run, Order−1, debasement unaddressed); FO = Comply→ a "Black Market" Intrigue (Clerk-Corruption evasion), Bargain→ a "Guild cites the Concession" Petition; either way Capital-Posture:Debased persists — the edict manages the symptom, never removes the tag. **Action** Levy:Debase (third Levy method: auto-succeeds, extracts Treasury with a smaller Order/Prosperity draw this season but writes/stacks Capital-Posture:Debased permanently raising future Levy Ob + price/Crisis draw-weight; a companion high-severity "Tribunal" price-cap method is this card's Comply made proactive). **Loop** Levy:Debase eases this season's extraction but stacks a tag raising Π's fiscal contribution + the price-panic Crisis weight; resolving that Crisis via Comply (price caps) doesn't clear the tag — it converts the pressure into a recurring black-market Intrigue chain until an actual Recoinage/Currency Reset.
- **England's Great Debasement / Elizabethan Great Recoinage (1544–1551 / 1560–61)** — *Wikipedia Great Debasement (recoinage-profit disputed £50k vs £14k).* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Capital-Posture:Debased count ≥2 AND an active Directive:Extract still unmet`; Comply (debase again to hit the Directive: Treasury+, a third stack, Reputation−1), Bargain (partial reform, mixed old/new coin at a negotiated rate: Concession, slower decay), Defy (refuse, force the Directive unmet: Standing-debt with the PA, no further stacking); FO = repeated Comply escalates to a "Gresham's Hoarding" Crisis (good coin vanishes, Prosperity stalls though nominal Treasury looks healthy). **Action** Recoinage (rare Directive-level action requiring PA approval beyond a single governor: retires debased coin, clears all Capital-Posture:Debased tags, resets Levy Ob — costed steep, mirroring Elizabeth's single 1560–61 reform after Edward VI's rounds). **Loop** Each Levy:Debase season compounds the tag, raising the Gresham's-Hoarding severity/weight *and* the eventual Recoinage price; Recoinage is the only clearing action, so deferral makes the exit more expensive — a legible one-way ratchet.
- **Edward III's default on the Bardi & Peruzzi (1340s)** — *The Financial Engineer; Wikipedia Bardi.* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `a Debt-family tag owed to a specific Sponsor-tier actor outstanding AND Treasury < owed AND a Directive:Repay unmet 1+ season`; Comply (repay: Debt cleared, Sponsor Disposition+, AP/Treasury squeeze), Bargain (renegotiate: Debt reduced + partly Concession), Defy/Default (Debt flips to an Outlawed tag on the Crown, Sponsor Disposition collapses, and if concentrated, seeds their bankruptcy Crisis); FO = default seeds a "Creditor Collapse" Crisis on the defaulted-against Sponsor if their exposure was undiversified, removing them as a future financing option (Peruzzi 1343 / Bardi 1346). **Action** Levy:Farm (tax-farming variant: pledge a future season's Levy yield to a Sponsor-tier actor as collateral for an immediate Treasury/AP infusion, writing a Debt tag). **Loop** Levy:Farm trades immediate relief for a Debt gating a future Directive:Repay; Defy converts it to a permanent Outlawed tag blocking all further Sponsor/Treat with that faction, and if the pledge was concentrated, chains into that Sponsor's own Crisis — permanently shrinking the province's financing options for the one-time windfall.
- **Ottoman akçe debasement / 1585–86 monetary crisis** — *Pamuk, Price Revolution Reconsidered; ResearchGate 1585 crisis (headline figures confirmed; 3-year-doubling/30–40% real-wage flagged lower-confidence).* → **Card** Crisis; trigger `Capital-Posture:Debased active AND a garrison/salaried cohort Obligation paid at a fixed nominal rate (not re-indexed) AND that cohort's Disposition below threshold`; Comply (pay at old nominal rate: Disposition falls via real-wage erosion, Grudge, raises Ambition risk), Bargain (raise nominal pay partially — "Treat: Index Wages": Concession, extra Treasury, partial relief), Defy (ignore the gap: Order collapses, Keep Order Ob rises); FO = sustained Comply/Defy without indexing seeds an Ambition card — the underpaid garrison commander advances their own agenda (mutiny/defection/covert dealing). **Action** Treat: Index Wages (minor side-deal re-pegging a fixed-nominal Obligation to current Capital-Posture at an ongoing Treasury cost). **Loop** Levy:Debase raises Treasury now but erodes the real value of any fixed-nominal Obligation; every unaddressed-Grudge season raises Keep Order Ob in that settlement and advances the commander-NPC's ambition-engine, converting a Levy-side convenience into a Keep-Order-side liability that can remove Keep Order as a viable option there.

### 2.10 Trade-route monopoly/franchise dynamics and merchant-capital power shifts (Economic)
*Key: `trade_route_monopoly_merchant_capital_events`. The through-line: **a Concession/Compact/Charter is a tag that drifts out of sync with the real balance of Standing**, and the verbs that reconcile it (Charter Review, Sunset Review, Underwrite, Capital Partnership, Monopsony Enforcement) either convert political leverage into extraction rights or convert accumulated Debt/Standing into a rank-ladder shift — often via Conquest.*

## 3. Cross-domain ripple chains

Each chain names the specific follow_on seed that carries state from one step's card into the next step's trigger predicate. These are legitimate under the deck architecture: a follow_on writes a durable Ledger tag / raises Π / sets a hidden stat, and the next card's trigger references exactly that tag/Π/stat. Domains are tagged [G]eopolitical, [g]eographical, [C]limatic, [E]conomic.

## 5. Curated shortlist — strongest candidates for a follow-up authoring pass

Distinct from §2's raw catalogue. These are the highest-leverage, least-redundant new mechanics. Each carries a "why this one, not the dozen similar" note — the comparative-governance judgment pass flagged reskin clusters, and several §2 entries independently reskin the same lever, so the shortlist explicitly names the winner of each cluster.

1. **The Capital-Posture Ledger family + Levy:Debase.** *Why this one:* it is the single spine of the entire currency category — nine of ten fiscal entries reference `Capital-Posture:Debased/Speculative`. Author the *tag family and the one-way ratchet* once; Levy:Farm, Charter Fiat, Index Wages, and VEN-SE-3's Bonifiche all become variants that write into it, not separate systems. Also reconcile with VEN-SE-3 so there is one Capital-Posture family, not two.

2. **Recoinage / Currency Reset (Directive-tier clearing action).** *Why this one:* a ratchet with no exit is a dead-end, not a decision. This is the *only* action that clears the Capital-Posture family, and the deferral-makes-it-costlier pricing is what turns debasement into a real timing choice. Author Recoinage (clean, metallic) and Currency Reset (leaves a `Monetary Failure` Precedent, land-backed) as two grades of one action — not the three near-duplicates the raw entries imply.

3. **Gauge-Indexed Levy (environment-indexed extraction).** *Why this one, not Toll/Contribution/Flota/Annona:* those four are all "a new Levy method-fork," but Gauge-Indexed is the only one that makes extraction *auto-responsive to a tracked environmental stat* without a per-season roll — it generalizes the Nilometer, folds directly into SE-JP2's periodic Survey, and is the cleanest single answer to "how do climate/harvest shocks inform the tax menu." The other three forks should be authored as *timing/quality* parameters on the existing Levy method, not new verbs.

4. **Diversify Corridor / route-redundancy (Develop single-point-of-failure fork).** *Why this one:* the single most reused geographic-vulnerability lever — it is the trigger predicate for Justinian, LALIA/Annona, Berlin Blockade, and Suez alike. One `route-diversified` vs `single-corridor` Precedent fork on Develop generalizes all of them; authoring per-event corridor mechanics would be four reskins of this.

5. **Ever-Normal Granary / Civic Granary (standing StockLevel buffer facility).** *Why this one:* the famine category's spine — five entries (North China, Chongzhen ×2, Great Famine, Cura Annonae) converge on "a standing storage facility auto-drawn on a shock determines survivability." Author *one* buffer-facility with a StockLevel stat and a decay-if-diverted rule; Granary Prefecture and Grain Ministry are then *capital-only enforcement* variants, a genuinely different thing (price-control capacity, not storage), worth keeping distinct.

6. **Staging/timing-exposure rule (Muster/Conquest) + Staged Demobilization.** *Why this one:* the unifying insight that *military movement carries environmental and epidemic risk* — it powers Kamikaze (anchored fleet), Great Hurricane (storm window), Antonine (plague road), and Atocha (late convoy). Author one "exposure accrues while staged/late in a hazard window" rule with per-hazard multipliers; Storm-Season Withdrawal and Staged Demobilization are the *same* mitigation (delay to skip the roll) at different scales — pick one shape.

7. **Garrison-pay Debt → mutiny + Settle Arrears / Contribution System.** *Why this one:* the siege category's economic engine, and the concrete instantiation IT-2 (Condotta) and VEN-SE-1 (State Arsenal wage liability) both need. One `Debt(garrison_pay)` clock with a contagion-on-fire clause (feeding HRE-6) plus the two verbs that address it — Settle Arrears (pay it down) and Contribution System (fund it from occupied territory). Index Wages (currency category) is the *same* clock re-pegged for debasement; author once.

8. **Embargo Directive (a Directive aimed at a third faction).** *Why this one:* a genuinely new Directive *shape* — every other Directive targets the settlement it is issued to; Embargo targets a rival through the governor's own trade, making the Comply/Defy fork "how much of my own trade do I sacrifice for someone else's grudge." Powers Megarian, Continental System, and (coalition-coordinated) ABCD. Not reducible to an existing verb.

9. **Contagion Precedent that cuts opposite by scale (magdeburgisieren / Reichsacht substrate).** *Why this one:* the most novel Ledger idea in the pass — a single tag that *lowers* an outcome's appeal locally (terror compliance) while *raising* Grudge/Intrigue regionally. Author as the shared substrate for HRE-6, Drogheda's Outlawed tag, and Spanish Fury's contagion; it is the mechanic that makes atrocity a genuine strategic trade-off rather than a flat penalty.

10. **Succession-configuration flags (Partible / Elective / Fratricide / Oath) — author as ONE parameterized rule, not four.** *Why the caution:* the raw catalogue proposes four separate faction-config flags plus Fratricide, and this is exactly the reskin failure mode — they are all "how does the Trigger Matrix resolve a leader-loss with 2+ heirs." Author a single `succession_rule` parameter with values {primogeniture, partible, elective, oath-bound} plus a post-Contest `Fratricide` option, all reading the *same* independent-Military-strength inputs. Pair explicitly with IT-5 (Legation Split) as the ex-ante cap.

11. **Crisis-gated Charter seizure (Nationalize / Revoke Franchise — Quo Warranto bypass).** *Why this one:* the cleanest expression of "a foreign/domestic Charter is contestable once its holder's leverage independently erodes," and the only one that routes resolution through *arbitration that can override the battlefield* (Suez). One bypass-branch on the Quo Warranto machinery, gated on a holder-weakness precondition read off the holder's sheet — powers Novgorod Kontor, Suez, and VOC.

12. **Quarantine_Tier with asymmetric-vector coverage (Sanità Lazzaretto).** *Why this one:* the single best teaching mechanic in the plague category — a facility that *only* discounts trade-vector triggers and explicitly does *not* cover the military-transit vector, so a governor who reads it as blanket protection eats a full-severity Crisis. It makes "mitigation is vector-specific" a mechanical lesson, not flavor. Segregation Camps and Force-branch pogroms are response reskins; this is the standing-investment anchor.

13. **Ring-fenced standing institution decoupled from Directive diversion (Water Board / Water Magistracy / relief Ministry).** *Why this one:* the general answer to "how do you stop a war Directive from silently starving flood/famine/relief funding." Author one *dedicated-levy, Directive-immune institution* pattern; Water Board (flood), Water Magistracy (shared-resource veto), and a relief-configured Ministry (Cooling-flag Remit discount) are instances. This is also the concrete home for Parker's "flexible institutions weather the shock" thesis and the closing win-condition of the St. Elizabeth's Flood chain.

14. **Adopt Local Practice (frontier-resilience Develop fork).** *Why this one:* the only mechanic that makes *heterodoxy vs metropole conformity* a costed choice — trade a Disposition/Precedent hit with the distant patron for durable resilience against a persistent Cooling flag. It is the one genuinely distinct answer in the climate category to "what can an isolated settlement *do* about a multi-decade shift," and it wires cleanly into FA-JP4's Court Attendance / Suspicion.

15. **World-level Cooling flag as a real campaign layer (General Crisis / correlated shock).** *Why this one — but flag it as design-taste, not just authoring:* the single highest-ceiling idea (correlated multi-settlement shocks that force aggregate Extract-vs-Remit policy) and the only one that makes climate a *strategic* rather than *local* concern. But it is also the biggest new subsystem, needs a ruling on whether Π gets a world-baseline term, and risks unfun swinginess. Author last, gated on a design-taste decision (§6).

*Deliberately excluded from the shortlist as reskins of an included item:* Toll / Flota Scheduling / Annona Route (→ #3/#4), the three revenue-pledge actions Levy:Farm/Underwrite/Capital Partnership (→ collapse to HRE-4/BYZ-7 substrate), Storm-Season Withdrawal (→ #6), Index Wages (→ #7), the separate Partible/Elective flags (→ #10), Segregation Camps/Force pogrom branches (→ response variants of #12).

## 6. Gaps and honest limitations

**Domains/categories that came out thin.**
- **Pure resource-playout (a mine exhausting, a river changing course) as a standalone concern** is under-populated: only Potosí and Erzgebirge (mines) and Yellow River / Zwin / Mesopotamia (rivers/harbors) carry it, and all of them are really *siltation/depletion-as-slow-fuse* stories that overlap the flood/drought category. There is no clean entry for a *non-catastrophic* geographic shift (a trade road rerouting for purely economic reasons, a pasture degrading) — the catalogue always reaches for a Crisis. A design-taste question: does Valoria want slow geographic decline that never becomes a Crisis card, just a quietly falling stat?
- **The famine and climate-shift categories overlap heavily.** Great Famine 1315 appears in *both* (§2.6 and §2.8), and the Chongzhen drought appears in the flood/drought, famine, *and* plague categories. This is faithful to history (these were compound catastrophes) but means the three categories share one underlying engine (harvest-collapse → Needs → Π → relief-facility check). The audit should decide whether they are three card families or one parameterized family — the shortlist (#5 Granary, #15 Cooling flag) already assumes consolidation.
- **Purely *positive* economic/geopolitical events are scarce.** Almost every entry is a Crisis/Friction; the Opportunity cards (Sound Dues Toll, Fugger Underwrite, Mongol ortoq, Bedford Level) are the minority and most of them carry a poisoned follow_on. The catalogue is a *concerns* catalogue by brief, but a design-taste ruling is needed on whether the deck needs more genuinely upside Opportunity/Ambition cards to keep low-Π play interesting, or whether "every opportunity has a fuse" is the intended tone.

**What got dropped or down-weighted by fact-check (flagged in the source citations, carried forward honestly):**
- **Numeric claims flagged lower-confidence** and therefore *not* used as mechanical magnitudes: the Ottoman 1585 crisis 3-year-doubling and 30–40% real-wage figures; the Zimbabwe 51%/28%→4%-of-GDP figures (secondary-aggregator sourced); the England recoinage profit (£50,000 vs £14,000, disputed across sources). These entries are kept for their *mechanism* (the ratchet, the Index-Wages loop) but no card should hard-code the disputed numbers.
- **Corrected specifics folded in:** the French support for the American war (~1.3bn livres, not the 2bn+ some sources give), Calonne's plan (rejected 1787, drafted 1786), the Ludlow/Baek 2020 Great Famine paper (dual-cited, name uncertain), and the note that Dutch water boards *predate* St. Elizabeth's Flood by centuries (the flood did not create the institution — so the Water Board should be authored as a *pre-existing* institutional option, not a disaster reward). The Norse Greenland entry explicitly flags the post-2011 isotopic evidence that diet *did* shift toward marine resources — so "Adopt Local Practice" should not be framed as pure rigid refusal.
- **Nothing was dropped for being fabricated**, but the catalogue leans on Wikipedia/secondary aggregators for several entries (Cartaz, Barbary, ABCD, Kilwa, pochteca). These are fine for *mechanism extraction* but a canon-authoring pass that wants period-authentic flavor text should re-source them against the primary/academic citations already listed before writing prose.

**What needs a design-taste ruling rather than more research.**
1. **Does Capital-Posture become a real currency subsystem or stay a Ledger tag?** The nine fiscal entries assume a tag; a full money-supply sim is a much bigger commitment. Ruling needed before authoring #1/#2.
2. **Is the world-level Cooling flag (#15) a real layer?** It is the highest-ceiling, highest-risk idea — it needs a decision on whether Π carries a world-baseline term and how to keep correlated shocks from feeling arbitrary.
3. **How many succession-configuration flags are one flag?** §5 #10 argues for one parameterized `succession_rule`; the raw catalogue proposes four-plus. This is precisely the reskin failure the comparative-governance judgment pass caught, and it needs an explicit "these are the same lever" ruling to avoid re-committing it.
4. **Do hidden slow-fuse stats (SiltLevel, SubsidenceRisk, RisingWaterLevel) that fire a Crisis at a *different* node, decades later, cross the line from "emergent depth" into "unfair/illegible"?** The Yellow River and Bedford Level entries deliberately make a choice at one settlement silently doom another — this is the most novel and the most potentially frustrating mechanic in the pass. Needs a taste call on how much the player is allowed to see (a warning card? a visible-but-slow stat? nothing?).
5. **Tone of the follow_on economy.** Nearly every mitigation writes a Debt/Grudge/Concession that seeds the next problem. That is the catalogue's whole thesis (bidirectional loops), but taken to the limit it risks a doom-loop feel where no action is ever clean. A ruling is needed on whether some verbs should be *genuinely* stabilizing (a real off-ramp), or whether "every fix has a fuse" is the intended identity of the game.

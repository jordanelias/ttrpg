## 1. Executive framing — the four scales, defined from the evidence

The research keeps snagging on the same distinction: **who is the actor, what is the unit they hold, and what tension is intrinsic to holding a unit of that shape.** The four scales are not four sizes of the same thing. They are four *different kinds of thing being managed*, each of which the historical record treats with its own institutions.

### Organization — a bounded membership with an internal ladder, dispersed across places it does not own

**Unit managed:** a *roster of members* bound by a charter, plus *branches* physically sitting inside settlements the organization has no sovereignty over (a Cistercian daughter-house, a Hanseatic Kontor inside Novgorod, a Fugger factor in Antwerp, a Templar commandery). **Who can act:** the organization's own leadership, drawing on the organization's *own* purse and its *own* Standing ladder — crucially **parallel to, not nested inside, faction/settlement governance** (the Teutonic Order founding Thorn under its own charter; the Hospitallers exempt from local episcopal jurisdiction). **Characteristic tension:** *internal cohesion vs. dispersion.* The recurring problems are branch drift (a daughter-house going its own way → the Cistercian Visitation), silent internal corruption (a factor skimming → the Fugger standing audit), recruitment quality vs. volume (the Jesuit Examen), leader capture (Arte della Lana's four-month sortition), and jurisdictional friction with the host that lets the organization operate (the Kontor's carve-out from the settlement's courts). An organization's defining move is to **carve a slice of authority out of a settlement or faction and run it under its own rule** — and then keep its own dispersed officers honest.

### Settlement — a single populated place with stat homeostats

**Unit managed:** *one place's* Prosperity/Order/Defense/Treasury/Weight and its Π-pressure. **Who can act:** a single officeholder within an AP budget (a governor, magistrate, kadı, selectmen), or a wealthy private patron with no office at all (Pliny endowing Comum). **Characteristic tension:** *bounded budget vs. compounding local pressure.* Every settlement decision is a spend from a small AP/Treasury ceiling against a homeostat that pushes back. This is the **only scale with fully-built machinery today** (governance_play_redesign_v1). Almost everything the research found at this scale is a *new option on an existing verb*, not a new scale.

### Territory (multi-settlement) — a cluster held as an aggregate, whose management object is the *connective tissue between settlements*

**Unit managed:** *the edges and the aggregate* — a roster of settlements plus the roads, walls-in-a-line, shared granaries, uniform edicts and reporting tiers that only exist *between* settlements. **Who can act:** either the faction leader who *creates the tier* (Diocletian erecting a diocese, the crown chartering a march) or a *delegated territory officer* who then runs it with real autonomy (a vicarius, intendant, March Warden, sanjakbeyi, Venetian rettore). **Characteristic tension:** *reach vs. uniformity vs. local autonomy.* The distinctive decisions have **no settlement-local expression at all**: is command over this cluster split civil/military or consolidated? does an alarm in settlement A reach settlement B in time? is the cluster too big for one officer to reach (Peter's reach-cap)? does a shared reserve move surplus from A to feed B's famine? These are *relational* facts about a set of settlements; no single settlement's dial can represent them. This is the explicitly-unbuilt gap the prior audit flagged (BYZ-6, IT-5).

### Faction — the whole polity's rules, criteria, and legitimacy

**Unit managed:** *the polity itself* — the Standing ladder, the Ministry/Consulta system, the criteria for who may hold office, the propagation scope of law, the ruler's legitimacy. **Who can act:** the faction leader (or a chief minister with throne authority) only. **Characteristic tension:** *control vs. the cost of centralization.* Faction moves redefine the game's rules (Peter's Table of Ranks opening merit advancement; Justinian making Precedent propagate realm-wide; Versailles converting territorial nobles into court hostages) and pay for it in permanent Treasury drain, standing grudges from displaced elites, and reduced flexibility. The faction scale is where you change *what kind of decisions everyone below you can make*, not where you push a stat.

**The load-bearing seam:** Organization and Territory both sit *between* settlement and faction, but on **orthogonal axes.** Organization is a *membership* that cuts *across* places (a guild spanning cities, an order holding lands in three factions). Territory is a *place-cluster* held as one *aggregate*. The Ottoman entries make this vivid in one system: the *sanjak* (Territory — a settlement bundle under a bey) and the *akıncı raider corps* (Organization — a self-funding membership that operates *within* that territory but answers to its own uç beyis, and can start an international incident the bey never ordered). Same frontier, two different management objects.

## 3. Concrete scale-menu proposals

### 3A. ORGANIZATION-scale menu (new — shaped by *membership dispersed across places it does not own*)

Do **not** port the settlement menu up. A settlement verb menu is built around *spending a bounded budget against one place's homeostat*. An organization's problems are *cohesion across dispersion, internal honesty, and jurisdiction carved from a host*. The menu should be organized around those.

**Prerequisite state (the one big new lever):** an **Organization entity** = a member roster on its **own Standing ladder** (parallel to faction Standing, per O14/O2/O10/X11), an **Org AP pool** (O1), and an **Org Treasury** (which may be nothing more than scoped reuse of Treasury/Prosperity *shapes*, per O7 — not necessarily a new numeric system).

| Org verb | What it does | Sourced from | Distinctive to this scale because… |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Charter / Draft Rule** | Instantiate the org: write its own Demotion Magnitude ladder, set its founding structure | O2, O10, O14 | Only an org authors its *own* disciplinary code; a settlement inherits the faction's |
| **Convene / Visit** | Assemble dispersed branch heads for one binding org-wide ruling; audit a branch head (**reuse Investigate inward**) | O1, O5 | The problem — branches drifting, factors skimming — literally does not exist for a single-place settlement |
| **Set Structure** (dials) | Admission Standard (Initiation-Gate Ob), Centralize vs. distribute command, Draw Consuls (sortition), Assign Langue (functional sub-command) | O3, O6, O8 | Trading recruitment volume/quality and leader-capture risk — a *roster* concern, not a *place* concern |
| **Establish Facility** | Build an org-owned asset that projects a rule or a portable trust-tag *into* settlements (Assay Office → certification) | O4 | The externality is *territory-spanning from a single owned point* — the inverse of a settlement improving itself |
| **Establish Enclave** | Carve a self-governing jurisdiction out of a host settlement (Kontor); choose Elect vs. Appoint its head | O7 | An org's signature move: *subtract* authority from a settlement it operates in |
| **Take On Work / Trust** | Accept exclusive multi-decade administration of a settlement asset (Great Work; inherited Bequest/Endowment) | O11, O12 | The org becomes the *standing administrator* of something a settlement can't or won't run |
| **Leverage Host** | Petition a superior faction for jurisdictional exemption; threaten/execute relocating a portable concession | O9, O13 | Portability *is* the leverage — a settlement can't pick up and move |
| **Project Rule** | Issue a statute / enforce exclusivity into a settlement market, gated by settlement Ratify at Hold Court | O6, (Florence guild statute S-cat) | The two-actor chain (org drafts, settlement ratifies) is the org↔settlement seam made explicit |

**Design taste flag:** the *cheapest* honest version of this menu is **not a full parallel economy**. O5 (Fugger audit), O3 (Jesuit dials), O8 (Langue), O10 (confraternity) all work by *rescoping existing mechanics* (Investigate, Initiation Gate, Standing, Sponsor) to the org's own membership. Only O14 (Teutonic commandery) and X11 (ghulam corps) genuinely demand an org that *holds land and troops in its own right*. Recommend: build the org as a **roster + scoped-reuse layer first**, and gate the full Org-Treasury/Org-holdings capability behind an explicit "independent charter" flag (exactly the distinction O14 draws — "*if* the organization holds an independent charter").

### 3B. TERRITORY-scale menu (new — shaped by *the connective tissue between settlements*)

Again, not a ported settlement menu. A territory's management object is *relational and aggregate*: edges, chains, shared pools, uniform policy, span-of-control. The menu is organized around **create the tier / build the connective tissue / act on the aggregate / resize the tier.**

**Prerequisite state:** a **Territory entity** = a roster of settlements + an appointed-administrator identity + a **Governance-Mode flag (Local/Appointed)** (T9) and a **civil/military-split flag** (X10). This is the object BYZ-6/IT-5 need.

| Territory verb | What it does | Sourced from | Distinctive because… |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Erect / Charter Tier** | Create the Territory entity, appoint an administrator (vicarius/intendant/warden/rettore), set governance mode & command-split | X10, T9, T13, X8 | There is no "create an administrative echelon" move at any other scale |
| **Split Verb Authority** | Partition *which verbs* sit at territory tier vs. settlement tier (a permissions table) — the "third option" | X8 | Uniquely relational: it's about *who executes*, across a set, not what happens in one place |
| **Build Connective Infrastructure** | Relay Tier / Beacon Network (propagation), Frontier Cordon / Complete the Line (chain topology), Post Road / Regional Works / Inter-Settlement Link (shared assets) | T1, T6, T4, T5, T3, T16, T19 | Every one models something *between* settlements — latency, an unbroken chain, a shared levee — that no settlement dial holds |
| **Establish Reserve Pool** | Found a shared cross-settlement buffer settlements contribute to and draw from in crisis | T2, T10 | Moving surplus *between* walled Treasuries is impossible at settlement scale |
| **Levy Territory-wide** | Pool labor/troops across all members (Mit'a); Muster Charter (land-tenure-for-defense) | T2, T11, T15 | One action extracting from *N* settlements simultaneously, with a shared output |
| **Promulgate Edict / Adopt Policy** | Stamp one Ledger tag (sumptuary, welfare, self-binding restraint) across every member settlement in *one* action; roll out a proven settlement institution | Tokugawa (S-cat), Ashoka (F-cat), S10-rollout | The economy-of-scale primitive: 1 action, not N — with settlements still diverging in enforcement underneath |
| **Survey** | Territory-wide Investigate sweep → a Surveyed/cadastral baseline usable as future Hold Court evidence | T7 | An *aggregate information asset* over the whole cluster |
| **Found Settlement** | Plant a new settlement node under a shared charter | T14, T17 | Creating the *unit* a settlement menu presupposes |
| **Partition / Redistrict / Reach-Cap** | Structurally resize the tier (reactive split, full redraw, or a cap that forces correction) | X2, X6, X9 | Reshaping the *membership set* — meaningless below territory scale |

**The two verbs that carry genuinely new state** are *Build Connective Infrastructure* (Relay/Beacon = inter-settlement propagation; Cordon = chain-topology aggregation) and *Establish Reserve Pool* (shared treasury). Everything else is either a tag, a flag, a permissions table, or reuse.

### 3C. SETTLEMENT — extensions to the EXISTING menu (no new scale)

These are **options on verbs that already exist** (governance_play_redesign_v1). None needs a new scale; they enrich the menu:

- **Develop** sub-options/funding-methods: *Aqueduct* (bundled Prosperity+Defense, S1), *Civic Square* (legitimacy-tagged, S4), *externally co-financed + Concession* (S6), *phased Develop+Fortify self-financing* (S5), *Organized-Contribution funding method* (Order stays flat, X3).

- **Keep Order** methods: *Set Assize* (S8), *Narh consult-and-cap* (S9) — beside Consent/Force/Clergy/Bind-the-Cells.
- **Sponsor** variants (all spend-structure, not new stat): *self-financed* (S2), *no-office* (S3), *endowed/persisting* (S3/S14), *perpetual-irrevocable* (Fuggerei, S12), *crisis overbuild* (S13).
- **Treat / Keep Order** branch: *Sort Relief* (population-sorting, S11).
- **Genuinely-new settlement institution** (borderline — a standing flag, not a verb option): *Establish Standing Reserve / Endow Institution* (S10) — the one settlement entry that earns new state.

## 4. The reuse-vs-new-lever ledger

**Blunt accounting** over the ~72 distinct proposals (duplicates collapsed):

- **~50 need no new tracked state at all.** They are new verb options, Ministry actions, spend-structure variants, or resolver behaviors that ride entirely on Prosperity/Order/Defense/Treasury/Weight/AP/Standing/Disposition + the existing/proposed Ledger tag families (Precedent/Grudge/Debt/Reputation + Compact/Concession/Outlawed/Capital-Posture). Every Sponsor variant, every Develop/Fortify/Keep Order method, the Legitimacy Broadcast, Cultivate Image, Codify Law's *effect* (minus its scope flag), Partition/Redistrict/Interpose, Requisition Post Road, Split Verb Authority, the two audit/visitation verbs, the confraternity, the exemption petition, the portable-concession relocation — all reuse.
- **A handful are "minor new state"** — a *flag or enum on an existing object*, not a new meter: `codified_law` propagation-scope flag (F5), the office-eligibility flag (F6/F7), the civil/military-split flag (X10), the Governance-Mode flag (T9), the Surveyed/cadastral flag (T7), the Charter/Status flag + protected-population Disposition track (F3), the inter-org subordination flag (X7), per-verb authority-tier table (X8).
- **The genuinely-new tracked levers that survive scrutiny — because each changes the *kind* of decision, not the magnitude of a dial** — collapse to these families:

| New lever | Sources | Why it's a new *kind* of decision, not a dial |
|---|---|---|
| **The Organization entity** (own Standing ladder + own AP; optionally own Treasury/holdings) | O2, O14, X11, O7, O10, O1 | Represents a *membership* that exists parallel to — not nested inside — settlement/faction governance. No existing stat can express "this body holds land/members the faction doesn't administer." |
| **The Territory entity** (roster + appointed-administrator identity + governance-mode/command-split flags; Prussian Chamber-network being its concrete admin object) | X10, T9, X13, T13 | Represents a *cluster held as an aggregate* with its own who-decides. "Is command split at cluster level?" / "Local or Appointed?" have no per-settlement expression. |
| **Inter-settlement propagation** (Relay Tier / Beacon Network) | T1, T6 | Models *latency and alarm-timing between settlements* — "my neighbor's alarm helps me react faster." Nothing in Prosperity/Order/Defense touches when a Directive or event reaches a *different* settlement. **The single strongest new-state case in the corpus.** |
| **Shared cross-settlement Reserve Pool** | T2, T10 | A treasury-like store that *moves surplus between* settlements. A settlement's Treasury is walled by definition; the whole point is a buffer that isn't. |
| **The Grant Ledger** (revenue rights over a settlement bundle held by a third thing) | T8 | The literal seam between settlement and faction: revenue split to something that is *neither* the settlement *nor* the whole faction. |
| **The Muster tag** (land-tenure-for-defense: permanent Defense floor, no upkeep, permanent Prosperity-growth tax, Disposition-contingent) | T11, T15 | A *different kind of decision than Fortify*: Fortify buys temporary Defense for Treasury-now; Muster buys a permanent floor for a permanent growth-tax with no AP to sustain — and can *flip to defection*. |
| **Cordon-Complete / chain-topology flag** | T4, T5 | A *topological* fact ("is the chain unbroken?") that gates a bonus for the whole set and drops it on any single break. Aggregation logic, not a stat. |
| **Portable Certified/trust tag** (travels with goods across borders, queryable) | O4 | A *mobile, queryable* trust signal — the identity is portability + external verifiability, not a bigger Order/Prosperity number. |
| **The Centralization dial** | F12 | Changes *what arena nobles/rivals compete in* (court rank vs. regional power) — it reconfigures the decision space, not a magnitude. |
| **Standing Reserve / endowed institution flag** (tiered; dampens one Π-event family) | S10 | Changes a settlement's *resilience curve against a specific event type* with self-replenishing logic external to the Treasury cycle — a different mechanical identity, not a Treasury deposit. |
| **Reach-cap trigger** (tuning constant) | X9 | Not new *state* — but the numeric trigger that turns the split-actions from narrative into mechanical. Worth flagging as the one place a *number* is genuinely required. |

**Bottom line:** the vast majority of proactive-governance history is *reuse*. The new machinery the videogame actually needs is small and clustered: **two new entities (Organization, Territory)** and **~4 new relational levers** (propagation, shared pool, grant ledger, chain-topology) plus **one new tag family (Muster)** and **one new settlement institution flag (Standing Reserve)**. The Centralization dial and the portable trust-tag are strong but optional/advanced. Everything else is a verb option, a Ministry action, or a flag.

## 5. Cross-reference against prior research threads

### 5A. BYZ-6 "Consolidated Command" and IT-5 "Legation Split" (the two flagged multi-settlement proposals)

**This research does not merely confirm the gap — it supplies the concrete shape both needed for a design-taste ruling, which should move both from "needs_jordan (undefined)" toward "needs_jordan (choose among specified options)."**

- **BYZ-6 Consolidated Command** is the Byzantine theme system's signature: a strategos holding *both* civil and military authority over a settlement cluster. The research shows this is precisely one *setting* of a flag, not a bespoke mechanic. **X10 (Diocletian)** proposes the **civil/military-split flag** on the Territory entity; **X2 (Byzantine theme *splitting*)** and **X1 (Interpose Diocese)** are the counter-moves. So Consolidated Command = the flag set to "consolidated," which the research also tells you is the *dangerous* setting (X10's own note: consolidation lets one governor's military-power_base consolidation spike; splitting suppresses it). **Recommended ruling:** build the Territory entity with a command-split flag; Consolidated Command is a high-efficiency/high-usurpation-risk configuration, and Partition/Interpose are the faction's levers against it. This is a coherent, tension-bearing shape rather than an open question.

- **IT-5 Legation Split** is answered almost verbatim by **X8 (Venetian rettori — Split Verb Authority)**: a territory need not absorb the whole verb menu; it can *partition* which verbs (Fortify, cross-Territory Treat, appellate Hold Court) rise to the appointed officer while others (Develop, Sponsor, first-instance Hold Court) stay settlement-scale. The research explicitly calls this "a genuine third option between 'settlement runs everything' and 'Territory absorbs everything.'" **Recommended ruling:** implement IT-5 as a **per-verb authority-tier permissions table**, not an all-or-nothing legation. The prerequisite (a Territory entity that can *hold* a verb) is the same object BYZ-6 needs — build once.

- **The shared prerequisite both flagged** — "an administrative object between settlement and faction" — is named concretely three independent times: the **Prussian Ministry-owned Chamber network (X13)**, the **Diocletian Territory entity (X10)**, and the **Grant Ledger (T8)**. X13's own note says it "establishes the missing administrative object that BYZ-6/IT-5 flagged as needed before Territory-scale can be built at all." That is as close to a resolution as research can get without Jordan's ruling.

### 5B. Rise-to-power / Ascendancy (does power_base gate which scale-actions a character can attempt?)

**Yes, along two clean axes, and the research is unusually consistent about it:**

1. **power_base as an eligibility filter on scale-actions.** The initiator_level fields repeatedly tie the *ability to even attempt* an action to a power basis:
   - **military** power_base underwrites the frontier/muster family (T11 Charter a Marchland, T15 Raider Corps, T13 March Warden, X11 Raise Standing Corps). **X5 (Meiji) makes this explicit and gating**: Annex Autonomous Territory *requires the faction to already hold an independent loyal-military flag* — you cannot centralize a rival power base until your own military basis is consolidated. That is a hard power_base precondition on a faction-scale action.
   - **bureaucratic/merit** power_base underwrites the appointed-administrator family (T9/X4 intendants, X10 vicarius, X13 Chamber network) and is what F8's Janissaries and X11's ghulam *manufacture* — the note that devshirme members "carry the bureaucratic/military power_base rather than patronage" *is the mechanical reason they're loyal to the leader, not local elites.*
   - **kinship** is the default that most faction reforms *attack* (F6 Table of Ranks strips kinship's office monopoly; X10/X11 strip kinship/tribal leverage).
   - **ideological/patronage** lowers recruitment cost specifically (F6/O15 Akbar: "ideological/patronage recruits cost less" to Recruit Member).

2. **consolidation_progress as the resource structural reorgs manipulate.** The faction-scale reorganizations don't just cost AP — they *move consolidation*:
   - **X5 (Meiji)** *resets the annexed holder's consolidation_progress to 0* and marks the power_base stripped.
   - **X10 (Diocletian) / X1 (Interpose)** *suppress military-power_base consolidation spikes* from any single governor.
   - **F12 (Versailles Centralize Court)** *converts* nobles' power_base from territory-anchored (military/regional) to court-anchored (patronage/purchased) — a power_base *migration* dial.

**Proposed clean rule for Jordan:** *power_base type is an eligibility filter — it gates which scale-actions a character can initiate (you can't Raise a Standing Corps or Charter a Marchland without the military basis or the faction authority to convert one); consolidation_progress is the currency the structural-reorg actions spend, raise (your own), or reset (a rival's).* This makes the Ascendancy layer and the four-scale menus a single integrated system rather than two bolt-ons: the scale-actions a character *can* reach up-scale to attempt are exactly the ones their power_base authorizes, and the faction's structural counter-moves are consolidation-manipulations aimed at rivals who reached too far.

## 6. Gaps and honest limitations

**Fact-check attrition and corrections already baked in.** The distillation carries its own corrections (Merchant Adventurers lost Antwerp **1564, not 1598**; the Steelyard's 1598 closure is a *different* event; Sainte-Chapelle's relic was **redeemed from a creditor, not bought**; St. Petersburg's death toll is **disputed** and must be modeled as a severity *choice*, not a fabricated fixed number). No entry was silently dropped, but **many are self-labeled "sketch"** — the Organization and Territory menus in particular are proposed shapes, not vetted specs.

**Thin spots that need authoring before implementation:**
- **The Organization economy is unspecified.** What *is* "Org AP" / "Guild Capacity" numerically? How does an Org Treasury relate to member dues, responsions tithes (O8), and assay fees (O4)? The research establishes the *entity* and its *verbs* but not its resource loop. This is the single biggest authoring gap.
- **Inter-settlement latency (Relay/Beacon) presumes a game concept that may not exist.** T1/T6 model "the Directive-response window reaching other settlements" and "propagation speed." **Does Valoria's turn structure even have inter-settlement latency to model?** If turns resolve simultaneously/abstractly, Relay Tier and Beacon Network have nothing to gate. This needs confirmation against the engine's temporal spine (`engine_clock`, which CLAUDE.md §6 flags as `doc: null` — unwritten). **These two levers, though the strongest "new state" cases on paper, are contingent on a temporal model that isn't authored yet.**
- **The Territory Reserve Pool's internal logic is abstracted** (T2/T10 hand-wave the interest/replenish mechanics). Fine as a design intent; underspecified as a spec.
- **Tag-family proliferation.** The corpus now proposes Compact, Concession, Outlawed, Capital-Posture, *and* a new Muster family. That's five additions to Precedent/Grudge/Debt/Reputation. **Is that too many?** Muster (auto-answers Levy with troops, Disposition-contingent) and Compact (mutual obligation) may be collapsible; Concession (jurisdictional routing) and Capital-Posture (strategic privilege) overlap in the frontier entries. A consolidation pass on the tag taxonomy is warranted before all five ship.

**Design-taste rulings that only Jordan can make:**
1. **Does the Organization scale get a full parallel AP/Standing/Treasury economy, or a lightweight scoped-reuse layer** with the full-holding capability gated behind an "independent charter" flag (the O14 distinction)? The research leans toward the latter as the cheaper honest default.
2. **Is the Territory a first-class persistent entity or a view/grouping over settlements?** X10/X13 argue first-class (it carries an administrator identity, a command-split flag, a Chamber network); X6/X9 (redistrict, reach-cap) argue it should be *cheaply re-drawable*, which tensions against heavy first-class state. The reconciliation is probably: Territory = a lightweight entity (roster + a few flags) whose *membership set is mutable*.
3. **BYZ-6 / IT-5 ratification.** The research gives both a concrete shape (command-split flag; per-verb permissions table; shared Chamber-network prerequisite). Jordan's ruling is now a *choice among specified options*, not an open-ended design problem — but it is still a ruling, and per CLAUDE.md §2 (ED-1094) it should flip the relevant docs' Status lines and CURRENT.md as part of whatever PR lands it.
4. **The power_base↔scale-action gating rule** (§5B) is a proposal, not canon. It reads cleanly off the initiator_level fields but has not been checked against the actual Ascendancy doc's power_base semantics — it needs canon-guard review before adoption.
5. **Zero numbers, by design.** Per CLAUDE.md §5, none of this crosses into Godot until authored as prose params with PP/ED citations. This deliverable produces *structure and identity*, not a single canonical value — the reach-cap constant (X9) is the one place a number is unavoidable, and it is explicitly left unspecified here.

**One structural caution the research itself surfaces (X4, X5):** several of these levers are *historically load-bearing for collapse* — Richelieu's intendants fed the Fronde, Meiji's reneged compensation detonated Satsuma, Qin's identity-erasure fed the dynasty's fall. The proposals correctly ask that stacked Grudges auto-seed Crisis-family cards rather than sitting inert. If that wiring is *not* built alongside the proactive levers, the game gets the centralizing upside with none of the historical downside — which would be an inaccurate and undramatic model. The proactive menu and the reactive Crisis-card system must ship as one loop, not sequentially.

## 1. Executive framing — the eight general mechanisms

The historical record is remarkably consistent: across Rome, Byzantium, the Islamic empires, China, Japan, Renaissance Italy, early-modern France/England, and the modern bureaucratic and revolutionary states, individuals rose to durable influence through a **small, recurring set of power-acquisition mechanisms** — and, critically, **each mechanism carried its own characteristic downfall shape baked into the same structure that produced the rise.** This symmetry is the design gift: it maps directly onto Valoria's Ω-d non-dominance principle ("every action pays what it buys").

There are **eight general mechanisms.** Every one of the ~96 historical cases below is an instance, hybrid, or edge-case of these:

| # | Mechanism | One-line shape | Characteristic downfall |
|---|---|---|---|
| **M1** | **Patronage chains (clientelism)** | Run obligation in both directions at once — sponsor upward, accumulate clients downward | Collapses **top-down**: when the upward patron falls, the whole client base decays/purges |
| **M2** | **Credentialed merit** | Pass an objective competence gate + a discretionary sponsor gate; the sponsor bond persists | Structurally capped for the un-tutored; reversible by **rewriting the credential criteria** |
| **M3** | **Kinship / marriage alliance** | Marry into (or supply consorts to) the ruling line; power rides the blood-tie, not the office | **Generational flip** or demographic lapse — one bad marriage or Disposition-flip voids decades of setup |
| **M4** | **Court proximity / favoritism** | Informal Royal-Intimacy outperforms formal Standing (the *valido*/favorite) | **Binary, not graduated** — loss of intimacy or the patron's death is total and often violent |
| **M5** | **Bureaucratic chokepoint control** | Sit on the routing path (correspondence, seals, adjudication, access) without holding a vested office | Undone by a **single bypass channel** or by succession (authority-by-proxy has no transferability) |
| **M6** | **Purchased office (venality)** | Buy the rank, or the toll-post athwart it, with Treasury | **No loyalty reserve** — purchased legitimacy evaporates the instant a rival offers what money can't buy |
| **M7** | **Armed retinue / military power-base** | Build a personal, self-loyal force outside the formal ladder | **Coalition purge** or **subordinate-flip** — the ruler routes *around* the strength rather than matching it |
| **M8** | **Ideological / moral authority** | Accumulate Standing-independent legitimacy (prophetic, jurisprudential, credentialing-body) | Cannot be bought or demoted — only **outcompeted in its own currency**, or purged by covert violence |

Two cross-cutting truths run through all eight:

1. **Power routinely detaches from formal rank.** Michinaga ruled through Minister of the Left, not the throne; Cosimo governed Florence holding no permanent office; Heshen stacked unremarkable posts into a monopoly. The design consequence: **the advancement system cannot be modeled as "climb the Standing ladder" alone** — it needs Standing-independent influence tracks (Disposition-as-Standing, moral_authority, renown, shadow_standing, custody-of-Mandate) that *feed* the ladder or *substitute* for it.

2. **Consolidation is self-limiting by construction.** Every rise writes a legible vulnerability — a Grudge pool, a patron dependency, a bypass channel, a purchased-legitimacy flag, an Upstart tag, a "purchased" provenance. This is not a bolt-on; it is the *same field* the rise reads. This is the exact shape Valoria wants.

The rest of this document (a) catalogues every surviving case as a proposed mechanic, and (b) proposes the concrete roster/advancement architecture that unifies them.

### Category A — Patronage networks & clientelism

*The M1 spine. Rise via running clientela in both directions; fall from the top down. These entries are the strongest argument that the ambition engine's sponsorship-gate (Standing 0→1) should generalize to every rung.*

**A1. Roman clientela & the cursus honorum.** *Avesani, "Cicero as a Roman patronus" (Edinburgh PhD); UNRV; Wikipedia "Patronage in ancient Rome."*
- **Mechanic — "Patronage Web Breadth":** rank climb is gated on running both directions of clientela. *Upward:* an `upward_patron` edge (new edge type on the Knots graph, PP-724) whose sponsor's Disposition/co-sign gates the next-rank Initiation Gate — the Standing 0→1 sponsorship rule (§1.1 row 0) generalized to **every** transition. *Downward:* a derived `clientele_breadth` = weighted count of lower-tier NPCs holding Disposition≥+1 + a Debt-tag bond, feeding the Rival Cohort tiebreak (§1.0) instead of pure Duty-success ranking. *Hook:* pure parameter reuse — no new subsystem; `clientele_breadth` is an aggregation query, not new storage.
- **Failure:** Cicero's 58 BCE exile — if the `upward_patron` is demoted (§1.0a) or dies, advancement freezes and `clientele_breadth` decays immediately (dependents redirect Disposition to a rival). Collapses top-down; a rival with larger breadth wins the vacancy tiebreak *even against the player*.
- **Naming:** none — "patron"/"clientele" is clean.

**A2. The cardinal-nephew system.** *Wikipedia "Cardinal-nephew," "Renaissance Papacy," "Romanum decet Pontificem."*
- **Mechanic — "Anointed Successor":** a new `ambition.goal='install successor'` for non-hereditary Standing-6/7 NPCs facing a time-boxed tenure; trajectory branches to sponsor a kin/protege into a Standing 3-4 slot early, waiving the Initiation Gate at a Reputation cost, timeline pinned to the *patron's* remaining tenure. *New field:* `expected_tenure` (marks a top seat as term-limited/elective/age-driven — the current ladder models no seat duration). *Hook:* new Key `patronage_installation` (sibling to `standing_change`/`officer_deaths`); ledger-side a Debt tag scoped to the sponsor.
- **Failure:** each bypass accrues a faction-wide "Nepotism debt" tag; at threshold a systemic **Reform Backlash** card fires a simultaneous Demotion wave on all kin/protege-tagged seats (the 1692 abolition, not an individual fall).
- **Naming:** not "officer" — but do **not** name it after "cardinal-nephew": "Cardinal" is already the Church Standing-6 title. Use faction-agnostic "Anointed Successor" / "Protégé Seat."

**A3. Cosimo de' Medici's "robust action."** *Padgett & Ansell (1993), "Robust Action and the Rise of the Medici," AJS 98; Belloc/Galbiati/Drago, CEPR (2024).*
- **Mechanic — "Gate Capture":** `ambition.method` becomes "capture the gate, not the seat" — target control of the Initiation Gate / Formal Recognition adjudication itself, riding FA-JP2's Recognition Fork as a shadow-adjudicator. Success measured by `Cross-Faction Reach` (new derived stat: count of distinct faction ladders where the NPC holds Disposition≥+1 ties — multivocality is what makes capture durable). *Hook:* plugs into FA-JP2 (§1.0b); composes with HAB-7's Ordenanza chokepoint.
- **Failure:** a narrow ideologically-coherent rival (Albizzi pattern, low Reach) can't out-coordinate but *can* force a public Investigate/expose that ties the cross-faction manipulation into one legible scandal — converting scattered ties into a mass Grudge event and a multi-faction purge.
- **Naming:** none.

**A4. The Köprülü household (kapı).** *Turkish Historical Review 11.2–3 (2020), Brill; Wikipedia "Köprülü family."*
- **Mechanic — "Household Roster":** a patron gains `household_roster` (list of proteges sponsored into Standing 1-2 outside the credentialing pipeline, each carrying a `kul_bond` scoping tag on their Debt-family Ledger entry — loyalty owed personally, not institutionally). One Exceeding-tier Duty success unlocks a one-time `household_succession_grant`: a designated member inherits the patron's Standing-6+ seat, bypassing Rival Cohort. *Hook:* rival funnel competing with CHN-2's exam ladder for the same 1-2 vacancies.
- **Failure:** rival households scale up Rival Cohort for the same 5-7 vacancies; formal leadership periodically triggers CHN-8 (Institutional Purge, Bloc) against a whole `kul_bond` cohort when Disposition flips.
- **Naming:** **flag explicitly** — a household member who *also* holds mustered-unit command uses the mass-battle "officer" for that separate function; the political rank must be "household member"/"retinue," never "officer." One NPC legitimately holds both; the fields must not collide.

**A5. Zeng Guofan → Li Hongzhang → Yuan Shikai / the Huai Army clique.** *Wikipedia "Li Hongzhang," "Huai Army," "Beiyang Army," "Zeng Guofan."*
- **Mechanic — "Successor Designate":** extends trajectory with a `successor_designate` pointer so that on the patron's death/retirement/demotion an accumulated `parallel_asset_pool` (garrisons, revenue Domain Actions, Treasury ties built outside faction allocation) transfers wholesale to the successor instead of reverting to the institution. *New fields:* `successor_designate` + `parallel_asset_pool` (explicit transfer-eligible bundle; today these ties are implicitly bound to the Standing slot). *Hook:* new Key `patron_succession_transfer`; composes with the Accounting-cascade tick.
- **Failure:** each hand-off compounds the successor's power *and* raises leadership Suspicion (§1.4) toward the lineage; crossing threshold lets leadership strip the pool alone (state reabsorption) — but left unchecked, the final successor's ambition thresholds into "claim office," triggering a full Succession Contest (the Yuan Shikai endgame).
- **Naming:** **flag** — when the pool includes mustered troops those commanders *are* mass-battle officers; the inheritance chain is "retinue lineage"/"power-base transfer," never "officer chain."

**A6. William Cecil, Lord Burghley — office-selling as chokepoint.** *World History Encyclopedia; Britannica "William Cecil."*
- **Mechanic — "Chokepoint Seat":** a `gatekeeper` boolean on specific named Standing-6+ Access-column slots (e.g. Lord Treasurer) marks it a mandatory routing point — any lower NPC's sponsorship-requiring ambition first resolves a sub-check against the gatekeeper-holder's Disposition, who may extract a Debt-tag toll convertible to Reputation/Wealth. *New field:* `gatekeeper` boolean (light Access-column addition). *Hook:* composes with HAB-4 Overlapping Consulta and the named Ministry-chair mechanic (§1.1b/d).
- **Failure:** *procedural, not favor-based* — does **not** auto-lapse on the holder's fall; it's **heritable** (a kin-tagged Standing-5+ heir inherits at reduced toll; else reverts to open Rival Cohort). Cumulative failure: sustained tolls build a faction-wide Grudge pool; at threshold a rival fires an Ordenanza-Sanction-style Petition (HAB-7) to strip the flag.
- **Naming:** no "officer" collision, but "Routing Office" sits too close lexically — use "Chokepoint Seat"/"Routing Seat."

**A7. Tammany Hall / Plunkitt's "honest graft."** *Plunkitt of Tammany Hall (1905); Wikipedia "George W. Plunkitt."*
- **Mechanic — "Patronage Broker":** inverts A1 — a low-Standing NPC accumulates many small Disposition+Debt ties with the *background populace* (not named NPCs), feeding the settlement Π/Suspicion homeostat (§2.1). Aggregate populace-loyalty becomes a deliverable "bloc" traded upward to the broker's patron for discretionary Access that refills gift-giving capacity — a closed loop. *New fields:* `bloc_delivered` counter (feeds `ambition.progress`, à la CHN-9's Duty-cycling) + a settlement-linked discretionary-Access pool. *Hook:* the cleanest case for extending the ambition engine **laterally** (reuses settlement-scoped Π/Suspicion directly); cousin of HRE-3's Convene-the-Circle pooling.
- **Failure:** collapses **systemically** — strip discretionary-Access from the feeding Ministry seat (civil-service reform, §1.1b) and every downstream broker's `bloc_delivered` decays and progress freezes across the board.
- **Naming:** not "officer," but "broker" already names a covert Investigate-verb archetype — use "Patronage Broker" to disambiguate.

**A8. Fujiwara regency marriage-politics (sekkan).** *Wikipedia "Sesshō and Kampaku," "Fujiwara no Michinaga"; DePauw, "Daughters of Sexual Politics."*
- **Mechanic — "Kin Placement":** a new `ambition.goal='kin placement'` targeting a marriage/kinship tie into the *still-forming* `heir_household` of a seated Leadership-track NPC — before the heir reaches Leadership. `ambition.timeline` must span multiple Accountings beyond the patron's tenure, re-parenting to the placed kin once marriage/birth Keys fire. *New fields:* `heir_household` link + multi-generational timeline persistence/re-parenting. *Hook:* patronage-flavored on-ramp into the existing succession system (LIN-01–04/SUC-01–03); new Key `kin_placement`.
- **Failure:** rival kin-branches compete all-or-nothing at the placement step (before Standing is at stake — losers shut out, no graduated demotion). If the placed heir later Disposition-flips (a Generational Shift, precedented at Crown Standing 7/Torben, §1.1 row 7), the whole 15-20-season investment voids at the last step — a tragedy-shape worth preserving.
- **Naming:** none.

**A9. Brezhnev's "Dnepropetrovsk mafia" / Soviet nomenklatura.** *Wikipedia "Dnipropetrovsk Mafia"; WaPo, "Shcherbitsky Was Brezhnev Protege" (1985).*
- **Mechanic — "Cohort Capture":** a new `ambition.goal='cohort capture'` — before Standing 6/7 a patron forms a `cohort_id` grouping same-era peers (mutual high Disposition from a shared early-career context). On reaching a `gatekeeper`-flagged seat (A6) with FA-JP2 authority, the patron resolves **Confirm** (never New-Grant-to-outsiders) for every `cohort_id` member across seasons, mass-promoting the bloc. *New field:* `cohort_id` (graph-query convenience). *Hook:* composes with FA-JP2; exact mirror of CHN-8 for the failure side (reuse CHN-8 verbatim).
- **Failure:** identical to CHN-9's patron-bound lapse but cohort-wide — the moment the patron falls, every `cohort_id` member takes automatic Demotion-1 within 1 season (CHN-8 bloc purge); authority has no independent life (unlike the inheritable Köprülü/Cecil patterns).
- **Naming:** not "officer," but avoid "registry" for the grouping (the settlement ambition engine is already gated on an unbuilt "settlement registry" — reusing the word implies a false dependency). Use "Cohort Roster"/"Cohort Tag."

### Category B — Merit & credentialing

*The M2 spine. Rise via a two-stage gate (objective competence + discretionary sponsor); fall via credential-criteria rewrite or a structural wealth cap. Note how the sponsor bond persists as a lever long after the exam.*

**B1. Roman cursus honorum & the novus homo (Cicero, Marius).** *Wikipedia "Novus homo," "Cursus honorum," "Gaius Marius"; OCD.*
- **Mechanic — "Sponsored Ladder Climb + Crisis Waiver":** NPC ambition converts progress to a Standing grant only if a Standing-5+ NPC spends Mandate to sponsor it (`patron_bond`). A **Crisis Key** (war/invasion/succession panic) grants a one-time waiver skipping the inter-rank cooldown (Marius's unscheduled consulship). *New fields:* `patron_bond` (sponsor id + Mandate investment) as a new Knots edge label; `crisis_waiver` flag consumed once. *Hook:* the existing "claim office" card, fired by a faction-Standing NPC, resolves as a Standing-climb gated by `patron_bond`; new Key `npc_standing_climb_attempt` (records sponsor + waiver use — `standing_change` alone can't), emitting `standing_change` on success.
- **Failure:** reuses Rival Cohort (§1.0); no-patron NPCs advance far slower. Repeated waiver-climbs (Marius's seven consulships) accrue a faction-wide Grudge from leapfrogged incumbents, raising later civil-war-scale event probability — the rise seeds its own reversal.
- **Naming:** none.

**B2. Chinese Imperial Examination — jinshi & the zuozhu/mensheng bond.** *Wikipedia "Imperial examination"; New World Encyclopedia "Keju."*
- **Mechanic — "Two-Stage Credential Gate with Sponsor Bond":** advancement requires (1) an objective competence check vs Attribute/Conviction, then (2) a discretionary Examiner NPC who can waive/fast-track/fail-anyway regardless of (1). Passing emits a permanent **bidirectional** sponsor bond (both can invoke it later). *New:* reuses the Knots graph with a new `sponsor_bond` edge (bidirectional Debt); the two-stage check is new logic on the Initiation Gate. *Hook:* new Key `credential_pass` (records objective result + whether override was invoked — downstream legitimacy logic needs provenance).
- **Failure:** un-tutored NPCs are structurally capped regardless of progress — model stage (1) as requiring a Treasury-backed "tutoring investment" spend (the wealth gate). The counter-reform is B3.
- **Naming:** none — "Examiner" ≠ "officer."

**B3. Song examination reform — anonymous grading & the Wang Anshi/Sima Guang fight.** *Wikipedia "Imperial examination," "Wang Anshi," "New Policies (Song dynasty)."*
- **Mechanic — "Credential Criteria Rewrite":** a high-Standing actor spends Mandate to rewrite which Attribute/Ethical Framework/Resonant Style a gate's objective check tests, and/or toggle an `anonymize_gate` flag suppressing the B2 override stage. Faction-scoped, not NPC-scoped. *New fields:* `credential_criteria` + `anonymize_gate` on the **gate** object. *Hook:* new faction-scoped Key `credential_reform`; the ambition tick reads it to re-test in-flight ambitions.
- **Failure:** faction flip reverses it (Sima Guang analog emits a counter `credential_reform`); this **composes with the existing §1.0a Demotion trigger** ("permanent Conviction shift to opposing framework → rank rolls to Standing 0") — NPCs credentialed under the reverted criteria whose Conviction no longer matches take automatic rollback, no new reversal mechanic.
- **Naming:** none.

**B4. Ottoman devshirme/kul — Pargalı Ibrahim Pasha.** *Wikipedia "Pargalı Ibrahim Pasha"; Britannica.*
- **Mechanic — "Household Favor Track (unilateral, revocable)":** a parallel track for NPCs recruited outside Standing/birth (origin flag), promoted by performed-competence Keys, but every rank above a threshold requires an active `favor_bond` held directly with the ruler — asymmetric, unilaterally revocable (unlike B1/B2 bonds). On revocation, reuses §1.0a "Dismissal" verbatim (Standing −1, assets Forfeited, Dishonored). *New field:* `favor_bond` edge flagged `revocable_by=ruler_only`. *Hook:* new Key `favor_revocation` collapsing Standing to Dismissal in one step (bypassing graduated demotion — the instant unappealable fall).
- **Failure:** the mechanism *is* the failure — no independent base, so a rival's Grudge/Reputation attack on ruler-Disposition directly fires `favor_revocation`.
- **Naming:** **flag** — will be reflexively called "Household Officer." Use "Household Advisor"/"First Retinue."

**B5. Mamluk slave-soldier pipeline → sovereignty (Baybars).** *Wikipedia "Mamluk," "Baybars"; Medievalists.net.*
- **Mechanic — "Cohort Bond + Ceiling Contest":** NPCs trained together share a horizontal `cohort_bond` (group-level, distinct from family/Standing). A **Ceiling Contest**: a qualifying crisis Key makes the topmost rank contestable regardless of current Standing, letting a cohort member jump into the discrete Leadership state. *New field:* `cohort_id` + many-to-many `cohort_bond` edge; NPC origin has no Standing floor. *Hook:* new Key `cohort_formation` (at recruitment); Ceiling Contest reuses SUC-01–03.
- **Failure:** the bond that enables the rise is the coup risk — high-Standing `cohort_bond` NPCs carry elevated "betray patron" card probability, scaled by cohort size/cohesion. The loyal pipeline is the likeliest usurpation source.
- **Naming:** **flag** — avoid `officer_cohort`; use `retinue_cohort`/"brotherhood," kept distinct from any separate unit-command role.

**B6. Napoleon's "carrière ouverte aux talents" — Grandes Écoles & Légion d'Honneur.** *Cambridge Core, "Napoleon's improbable synthesis..." (title confirmed, quote not independently re-verified).*
- **Mechanic — "Dual-Track Advancement (Talent vs. Favor)":** two parallel Standing-grant channels — a Talent Track (competence-gated) and a Favor Track (direct appointment). Every grant records which. Sustained Favor-Track overuse accrues faction-scoped Reputation/Grudge cost eroding Talent-Track legitimacy. *New field:* `advancement_track` enum {talent, favor}. *Hook:* new Key `advancement_grant` (superset of `standing_change` adding provenance).
- **Failure:** legitimacy erosion, not a rival NPC — Favor-Track appointees are mechanically brittle (existing "public scandal: drop 2 ranks" applies at double magnitude to them).
- **Naming:** none.

**B7. Northcote–Trevelyan (1854) & the Indian Civil Service open exam.** *Wikipedia "Northcote–Trevelyan Report."*
- **Mechanic — "Gate Conversion (patron-discretion → open-exam)":** a reformer spends Mandate to flip a tier's Initiation Gate from patron-controlled (the "Formal Recognition Event," §1.1) to open-exam (objective only), stripping specific NPCs/blocs of the gating lever. *New field:* `gate_mode` enum {patron_discretion, open_exam} per gate. *Hook:* new Key `gate_conversion`; Grudge-tag emission to dispossessed patrons is read off it.
- **Failure:** the ~16-year delay + Victoria's hostility → multi-cycle conversion: sustained Mandate spend across Accountings; opposed patrons spend to stall; losing Mandate dominance mid-conversion reverts `gate_mode`.
- **Naming:** none — keep "granting authority"/"patron."

**B8. Prussian civil service — the Referendar/Assessor career.** *1755/1770 exam requirements; Wikipedia "Prussian Reform Movement."*
- **Mechanic — "Reform-in-Progress transitional state":** a third layer on B7 — `legacy_bypass_open` boolean, true even while `gate_mode=open_exam` (the formal exam exists but a legacy purchase/favor bypass persists until a ruler spends a dedicated `bypass_closure` action). Models the 1755–real-displacement gap. *Hook:* reuses `gate_conversion` split into two Keys — `gate_conversion` then `bypass_closure`; the gap between them *is* the transition.
- **Failure:** while open, bypass-promoted NPCs inherit B6's favor-track fragility *and* accrue faction Suspicion for "reform not yet real," forcing eventual closure or indefinite legitimacy bleed.
- **Naming:** none.

**B9. Meiji Higher Civil Service Examination (1887).** *Spaulding, "Imperial Japan's Higher Civil Service Examinations" (De Gruyter Brill).*
- **Mechanic — "Founding Cohort Dilution":** composes B5's `cohort_bond` (a founding faction whose early NPCs share a founding-cohort advancement advantage) with B7's `gate_conversion`, applied faction-wide to open a parallel exam track non-cohort NPCs can climb without the founding bond — diluting the founders once the regime is secure. *Hook:* purely compositional (reads B5 + B7); timing-gate `gate_conversion` on a "consolidation secure" predicate reusing the Π-homeostat stability signal (§2.1).
- **Failure:** reuses B5's double-edge — founders losing their advantage is a thwarted-ambition event firing "betray patron"; the dilution reform *is* the founding-generation rebellion trigger.
- **Naming:** **flag** — in a militarized founding-cohort context, keep a member's political rank explicitly distinct from any mass-battle unit-command "officer" role.

### Category C — Marriage alliances & kinship networks

*The M3 spine. Rise via blood-tie to the ruling line; fall via generational flip, demographic lapse, or the permanent "consort-advisor ceiling." This category most stresses the multi-generational / lineage-scope gap.*

**C1. Habsburg marriage diplomacy (Maximilian I).** *Wheatcroft, The Habsburgs (1995).*
- **Mechanic — "Contingent Claim Banking":** a new `ambition.method='dynastic'` whose goal is a claim on a *currently-occupied* seat; progress advances via a marriage Key on record + the holder's line surviving; trajectory adds "if target line's heir dies without issue → claim auto-resolves (fires 'press the claim')." The claim sits dormant for game-years until a watched succession-vacancy Key fires. *New field:* `ambition.contingent_on` (a watched external Key predicate — current trajectory only branches on the NPC's own blockage). *Hook:* new Key `marriage_contract_banked`, consumed to auto-fire on a matching `standing_change` (death/exile no-heir) or new `line_extinguished` Key.
- **Failure:** contested only *at resolution* — a rival with independent military/diplomatic weight (France/Valois analog) gets "contest the banked claim" the instant the vacancy fires; sticking is gated on Rival Cohort standing + military leverage, not the marriage. A claim with no enforcement resolves to open war, not automatic inheritance.
- **Naming:** none.

**C2. Fujiwara regency (sekkan seiji).** *Morris, The World of the Shining Prince (1964); Sansom (1958).*
- **Mechanic — "Generational Consort-Supply Precedent":** a new **lineage-scoped** Ledger tag family, "Kinship Precedent" — each successive daughter-marriage into the ruling house increments its weight; at threshold the lineage head gains access to an informal regency track modeled on **HAB-2 (Valido)** rather than a new track. Michinaga rides HAB-2, with marriage-supply as an alternate on-ramp. *New field:* lineage-level "Kinship Precedent" tag (genuinely new — existing families are NPC/faction-scoped, not lineage-scoped across generational turnover). *Hook:* explicit consolidation into HAB-2, not a parallel regency mechanic; no new Key beyond `standing_change` recording `decided_by=monarch`.
- **Failure:** rival cousins (Chōtoku Incident / Korechika's exile) → Rival Cohort (§1.0) scoped to the lineage's internal succession-of-supplier. Distinct failure: the precedent tag zeroes if the lineage fails to place a daughter with the *reigning* monarch for a generation — automatic decay if no marriage Key fires within N Accountings (demographic, not violent).
- **Naming:** none.

**C3. Ottoman damat (imperial son-in-law).** *Peirce, The Imperial Harem (1993).*
- **Mechanic — "Consort-Advisor Ceiling":** a `succession_eligible` flag hard-set **false** on marriage into the ruling house for a capable-but-dynastically-ineligible subordinate, paired with a Standing fast-track (skip Initiation Gates up to Standing 7/senior-advisor on the marriage bond) — but the flag is **permanent and unremovable** regardless of Standing (maximal loyalty/status, permanent exclusion from Leadership). *New field:* `succession_eligible` (bool, permanent-false — nothing currently distinguishes "can reach Standing 7" from "can reach Leadership"; today conflated). *Hook:* extends SUC-01-03/LIN-01-04 with an exclusion flag; new Key `consort_bond_formed`. **Overlaps BYZ-8 — flag for consolidation.**
- **Failure:** the bond's Disposition anchor can flip without misconduct — a Demotion (§1.0a) firing purely off patron-Disposition collapse (a new cause distinct from scandal/heresy/defection), instantly dropping Standing even though `succession_eligible` was never the failure point.
- **Naming:** **YES** — a spouse-bound chief administrator running government is exactly what a designer would call an "officer." Use "advisor"/"consort-advisor."

**C4. Woodville affinity cascade (England, 1464-83).** *Hicks, Edward IV (2004); Lander (1980).*
- **Mechanic — "Patron-Granted Standing Cascade":** a per-NPC `standing_source` (earned | patron-granted), set when advancement bypasses gates on one patron's favor — applied to a *cluster* (extended family) simultaneously, all pointing to the same `patron_id`. *New field:* `standing_source` + `patron_id` linkage (current schema records rank but not provenance/live-sponsor dependency). *Hook:* new Key `patronage_cascade_voided` (payload: patron_id, affected_npc_ids[]), a child of `standing_change` (death|exile on the patron); on receipt every patron-granted client takes an immediate §1.0a Demotion — a direct implementation of Ledger propagation.
- **Failure:** the cascade *is* the failure — no rival action required; a zero-earned-Duty cluster cascades harder than a mixed-provenance one. A rival (Neville/Warwick) gets only an opportunistic "press the purge" card once it fires.
- **Naming:** none.

**C5. Wang Mang & the Han consort clan (waiqi).** *Loewe, The Men Who Governed Han China (2004); Bielenstein.*
- **Mechanic — "Consort-Clan Leverage (multi-generational)":** a genuinely new **clan/lineage object** accumulating `dynastic_leverage` across reigns (increments whenever a member's daughter is/remains consort to the *reigning* monarch). Because Wang Zhengjun's leverage spanned five reigns, it can't ride the NPC `ambition.progress` (0-5). Feeds a clan-head's ambition (goal "convert regency into Leadership," trajectory shifts violent only at the final usurpation). *New field:* clan-scale `dynastic_leverage` (a genuine architectural gap — not reachable by extending ambition/trajectory). *Hook:* **requires promoting the Accounting-cascade tick to operate on a clan/lineage entity above the single NPC — the biggest structural ask of the set.** New Key `regency_established`; the final seizure extends `coup_attempted` with a new outcome or sibling `usurpation`.
- **Failure:** rival consort clans (Dou/Fu/Ding) → Rival Cohort at clan scale, resolved at every succession. Gating failure: the final usurpation checks Conviction/Disposition (cultivated-virtue reputation) via the Stance Triangle — a clan head whose Conviction drifted from the reputation built over generations backfires even at maximal leverage.
- **Naming:** none.

**C6. Mongol quda (marriage-alliance) under Genghis Khan.** *Allsen, Culture and Conquest (2001); Weatherford (2004).*
- **Mechanic — "Marriage-Tribute Obligation (conquest-alternative)":** a new entry on the Required Obligations column (§1.0) for subject factions — "Marriage Tribute": elevated baseline Disposition + partial-autonomy retention in exchange for a perpetual wife-supply Obligation. *New:* none structurally beyond the Obligation-type (reuses Obligation/Disposition/Standing). *Hook:* lives in §1.0's Required Obligations table; new Key `marriage_alliance_formed` (faction scale), parallel to `standing_change`.
- **Failure:** two modes — (1) resistance instead draws worse-treatment (**shared with C10's Rajput contrast — same underlying mechanic**); (2) generational lapse of "perpetual" supply → automatic tie-lapse if no marriageable daughter within N Accountings, modeled on CHN-9's auto-lapse (§2.5a).
- **Naming:** none.

**C7. Rothschild endogamy (cousin-marriage capital lock).** *Ferguson, The House of Rothschild, Vol. 2 (1998-99).*
- **Mechanic — "Endogamous Lock":** a new Ledger tag family across a faction's internal branches — while active, reduces the Suspicion field's growth rate (§1.4) for members and lowers external Investigate "co-opt" success against them, at the cost of shrinking the branch's external marriage-market pool. *New field:* "Endogamous Lock" tag (faction-branch-scoped, boolean-plus-strength — Suspicion has no deliberate resistance-modifier today). *Hook:* new tag family alongside Precedent/Grudge/Debt/Reputation, reading/writing Suspicion; a Key `endogamy_pact_formed` warranted for the formation event only.
- **Failure:** opportunity cost, no external rival needed — a locked branch can't simultaneously run external alliance expansion (C1/C6/C10), so an outward-marrying rival out-expands it. Sharper trigger: a forced out-marriage breaks the tag and pays out suppressed leak-risk at once as a Suspicion spike (catch-up, not gradual).
- **Naming:** none.

**C8. Romanos I Lekapenos — basileopator to co-emperor.** *Treadgold (1997); Runciman (1929).*
- **Mechanic — "Staged Co-Leadership Climb":** a staged trajectory (marital tie → intermediate honorific → co-Leadership) requiring a **new intermediate state** between Standing 7 and the binary Leadership state; trajectory reads "if unopposed N seasons → advance," chaining three stages. *New state:* `co_leadership` (Leadership-adjacent, non-exclusive — a genuine gap; Part 2 has no partial/shared-rule concept). *Hook:* extends both trajectory staging and the Leadership boundary; new Key `co_leadership_claimed`, or extend `coup_attempted`'s outcome enum with a fourth "co-opted" (bloodless partial seizure).
- **Failure:** the surviving legitimate ruler is a persistent rally point — a "Legitimate Heir Living" tag on the true-line NPC makes "restore the true heir" an available goal; success strips `co_leadership` from the usurper's line **including children already promoted** (the 944/945 double reversal — inheritance-wide, not individual).
- **Naming:** none.

**C9. Akbar's Rajput marriage-alliance policy.** *Richards, The Mughal Empire (1993); Eraly (2000).*
- **Mechanic — "Dynastic Marriage Entry + Resistance Cost":** a new `standing_change` trigger `dynastic_marriage` — an allied ruler's kin gain immediate mid-rank Standing (e.g. 3) in the dominant ladder on marriage, skipping gates, while keeping their own separate throne (genuine dual-track membership). Paired with a "Resistance Cost" tag on holdouts (Mewar/Sisodia) accruing Treasury/Suspicion penalties each refusal season. *New:* additive trigger-enum extension + one "Resistance Cost" tag (**structurally identical to C6 — author once, reuse**). *Hook:* alternate 0→3 entry keyed off a marriage Key; no new Key beyond the enum addition.
- **Failure:** (1) the shared C6 Resistance Cost; (2) integrated kin hit the C3 damat ceiling — high in administration but blocked from throne-succession, so a long-blocked ambition shifts violent/covert per the existing branch.
- **Naming:** none.

**C10. Venetian patrician endogamy & the Libro d'Oro.** *Chojnacki (2000); Romano (1987).*
- **Mechanic — "Registered-Lineage Eligibility Gate":** a categorical block — `marriage_registered` (bool) gating **all** advancement above Standing 0 for a closed-oligarchy archetype; unregistered marriages produce permanently-ineligible descendants. Paired with an inheritable "Registered Lineage" tag (a Precedent-family variant propagating to children — a multi-generational Precedent chain). *New:* `marriage_registered` bool + a closed-eligibility registry object (genuinely new) + inheritable-Precedent propagation. *Hook:* faction-archetype precondition on the Initiation Gate, scoped to a Venice-style faction (**speculative pending that authorship**); new Key `marriage_registered`.
- **Failure:** (1) bribery targeting registry gatekeepers (Investigate co-opt); (2) over-transaction backlash — a Grudge tag accrues against houses manipulating dowries/registration (sumptuary trigger), enabling "reform/audit the registry," which can strip status retroactively.
- **Naming:** none.

### Category D — Court favorites & power behind the throne

*The M4 spine, and the single richest vein for HAB-2 (Valido). Rise via Royal-Intimacy outperforming Standing; fall binary and often violent. Several entries directly answer HAB-2's open needs_jordan questions.*

**D1. Rasputin & the Romanov inner circle.** *Smith, Rasputin (2016).*
- **Mechanic — "Proxy-Intimacy Favorite + Regency Window":** extends **HAB-2** with a proxy — the Favorite's Royal-Intimacy is scored against a *consort*, not the ruler; a "Regency Window" state (ruler absent, consort holds interim authority) multiplies the Favorite's Patronage-Web reach while open (every consort appointment reads as ruler-authorized). Models "ministerial leapfrog": churn rate of `standing_change` rises because appointments route through the Favorite's Disposition. *New field:* "Regency Window" state (open/closed, opened_by, `authority_delegate=consort_id`) — court-scale, doesn't exist. *Hook:* strongest argument to land HAB-2's binary-collapse track; new sub-field under the Crown inner-circle rows (§1.1d) or an `interim_authority_delegate` payload on `state.succession`.
- **Failure:** HAB-2 binary collapse **plus legitimacy contagion** — Grudge accrues against the *consort* for tolerating churn; excluded Standing-5+ nobles execute the Favorite outside any formal process (assassination, not Contest), and the regime's own legitimacy collapses shortly after (the pairing loses the throne), because the Grudge was never against the Favorite alone.
- **Naming:** none.

**D2. Sejanus, Praetorian Prefect under Tiberius.** *Tacitus, Annals 4-6; Dio 58; Levick, Tiberius (1976/99).*
- **Mechanic — "Access-Gatekeeper Prefect":** a named inner-circle office-holder doubling as the **CHN-7 Chancellery Gatekeeper** (info substitution) plus a physical-isolation clause — the ruler relocated remote, the Gatekeeper controls the Access channel itself (who may petition at all), not just message content. *New field:* `isolated_residence` bool on the ruler (gates whether CHN-7 substitution is total). *Hook:* extends CHN-7; the office sits as a normal inner-circle row (§1.1d); new Key only for the downfall.
- **Failure:** a **bypass channel** — a single trusted freedman with a pre-isolation personal tie delivers unfiltered info once and the ruler acts immediately: HAB-2-style Total collapse triggered by a single bypass, not Contest/Grudge. Model as a one-shot Investigate/Knot action available only to NPCs with a pre-isolation tie, firing `standing_change` (exile, total) with no appeal.
- **Naming:** **flag** — "Prefect" commanding the Guard is dangerously close to the mass-battle unit-commander "officer." The named NPC is a Standing-ladder office-holder; if he *also* commands a mustered Guard unit, that unit's officer is a distinct auto-generated record. Do not conflate the two structures.

**D3. Wei Zhongxian & the Eastern Depot.** *Huang, 1587; Mingshi; Wakeman, The Great Enterprise (1985).*
- **Mechanic — "Reign-Bound Cohort Purge":** the Favorite builds a surveillance bloc **patron-bound to the current reign** — reuse CHN-9's patron-bound lapse (§1.0d) scaled from a chain to a persecuting cohort, paired with **CHN-8 (Bloc)** as the successor's demotion mechanism. *New:* none — CHN-9 + CHN-8 composed and chained off `state.succession`. *Hook:* specify CHN-9's lapse, when the patron *is* the reigning leader, auto-escalates from passive lapse to an active CHN-8 bloc-purge check the moment `state.succession` fires with a hostile/unset-Disposition successor.
- **Failure:** succession-triggered instant total reversal — power was authority-by-proxy on one ruler; the successor was structurally outside the network (Disposition defaults hostile). Fast (weeks-months) and total.
- **Naming:** none — "Eastern-Depot cohort"/"secret-police bloc."

**D4. Zhao Gao & the forged succession edict.** *Sima Qian, Shiji; Bodde, China's First Unifier (1938).*
- **Mechanic — "Seal/Mandate Custody Forgery + Compelled Loyalty Test":** a genuinely new mechanic — during the vacancy window between a ruler's death and `state.succession` resolving, whoever holds physical custody of the Mandate token can **fabricate** the succession Key's contents (falsify new_leader_id/mode) via a covert Deception, contested only by an NPC with independent knowledge of the true will. If uncontested, written to canon as genuine until discovered. Pair with a "Compelled Public Loyalty Test" scene-verb forcing every Standing-3+ NPC to publicly declare Disposition; hesitation → immediate Total demotion (a compressed mass-purge tool). *New:* `state.succession` optional payload `fabricated`/`discovered` bools; a new scene-verb "Demand Public Loyalty Test." *Hook:* Key-payload extension on `state.succession`; discovery emits into existing Grudge/Precedent families.
- **Failure:** delayed cascading discovery — the forgery holds while uncontested; eventual Investigate/purge-survivor discovery retroactively flips legitimacy, mass Grudge accrual + `coup_attempted` from the true heir's allies. The regime typically fails within a reign once the survivor Grudge pool is too large.
- **Naming:** none — "Seal Custody"/"Regent-Forger."

**D5. Nur Jahan's shadow administration.** *Findly, Nur Jahan (OUP, 1993).*
- **Mechanic — "Kin-Distributed Favorite Web":** extends **HAB-2's Patronage Web** from a single client roster to an explicit multi-NPC family bloc — each Web member independently climbs with an Ob discount while the founder's Royal Intimacy is intact, bound by existing **Knot-relationships (FA-JP4)**. Distinguishing piece: when members' Knots point at *different* heir-claimants, the Web has an internal fault line a succession can split. *New field:* `web_id` grouping multiple NPCs (HAB-2 implies a single Favorite + amorphous clients). *Hook:* extends HAB-2 + FA-JP4; land as a sub-clause of HAB-2 ("Webs may be multi-member; members' Knots to rival heirs create an intra-Web fracture check at `state.succession`").
- **Failure:** **internal fracture**, not external defeat — at the ruler's death, members resolve competing Knot-loyalties; the founder (weakest own claim) loses control as the strongest-Knotted member backs the winner and turns on her → confinement/demotion (graduated containment, not assassination).
- **Naming:** none.

**D6. Madame de Pompadour as unofficial minister.** *Lever, Madame de Pompadour (2002).*
- **Mechanic — "Institutionalized Favorite (Web decoupled from Intimacy)":** directly answers **HAB-2's needs_jordan Q3** ("does the binary collapse ever soften?") — once a Favorite's Web contains 2+ clients independently at Standing 5+ through her sponsorship, the Web graduates from "Intimacy-dependent" to "Institutionalized"; influence is then scored against aggregate senior-client Disposition, and HAB-2's binary collapse no longer applies. *New field:* `web_institutionalized` (threshold-triggered bool). *Hook:* the concrete design answer to needs_jordan queue item 3 — HAB-2 gets a Web-size escape valve; lands inside §1.1e.
- **Failure:** slower partial purge — rivals accrue Grudge but can't trigger collapse once Institutionalized; only at the Favorite's death does a CHN-8-style purge open against surviving clients, and it's graduated (Standing-5+ clients survive on earned rank). Choiseul outliving Pompadour is the argument for partial survival.
- **Naming:** none.

**D7. George Villiers, Duke of Buckingham.** *Lockyer, Buckingham (1981).*
- **Mechanic — "Heir-Banked Favorite Transfer (HAB-2 × HRE-2)":** applies **HRE-2 Chapter Capture** (pre-vacancy banking under timing uncertainty) to the Favorite track — proactively cultivate Disposition/Standing credit with the *heir apparent* before succession, banked separately from the current ruler's Intimacy. If banked heir-Disposition clears threshold when `state.succession` fires, the Favorite's Web survives without §1.0a's succession-demotion check. *New field:* `heir_banked_disposition` (or reuse HRE-2's "seats owed" banking). *Hook:* explicit HAB-2 × HRE-2 synthesis — HRE-2 as the template for a Favorite's "succession insurance"; a cross-reference note in both sections, not a third track.
- **Failure:** surviving one succession neutralizes institutional collapse but **not** the accumulated personal Grudge pool — downfall shifts from faction-scale demotion to a scene-level impeachment Contest, then assassination keyed off Grudge tags. **Banked heir-Disposition protects the Web/Office, not the person.**
- **Naming:** none.

**D8. Kösem Sultan & the Valide Sultan system.** *Peirce, The Imperial Harem (1993).*
- **Mechanic — "Contested Regency Slot, resolved via Gatekeeper realignment":** a single-occupancy cross-reign-persistent **Dignity** (BYZ-1's sticky Dignity) contested by *two* claimants simultaneously, resolved through a third-party **CHN-7 Gatekeeper** rather than a direct Contest — whoever holds the Gatekeeper's Disposition controls Access to the minor-heir; the loser is removed via the Gatekeeper's chokepoint. *New field:* "Contested Slot" relationship (two ids racing for one single-occupancy row — Rival Cohort assumes rivals compete *for* rank, not that a third party arbitrates by proxy). *Hook:* composes BYZ-1 (§1.0b) + CHN-7 (§1.4a).
- **Failure:** the Gatekeeper **survives the coup** and keeps the chokepoint under the new patron — a distinctive non-resolution: the loser is eliminated but the systemic risk (one NPC controlling access) persists, setting the identical failure to recur.
- **Naming:** none re "officer." Note "Gatekeeper" is shared with CHN-7 **by design** — flag that CHN-7 and this entry converge on one Gatekeeper primitive and should be authored once, not twice.

**D9. Basil Lekapenos, the eunuch parakoimomenos.** *Cambridge History of the Byzantine Empire (2008); Leo the Deacon; Psellos.*
- **Mechanic — "Multi-Reign Oath-Bound Administrator + Personal-Rule-Assumption demotion":** **not a new gap — the direct real-world precedent for BYZ-8.** Genuine addition: BYZ-8 doesn't specify multi-reign persistence (he served five). Each reign-transition survived stacks a small capped "Institutional Indispensability" bonus (further Coup Counter reduction); add an alternate Demotion Trigger — **"Personal Rule Assumption"**: once the ruler crosses a majority/independence threshold, they may unilaterally revoke the Office at *zero* Coup Counter cost (the renounced-claim NPC poses no retaliation threat). No violence, no Contest. *New:* mostly reuse; a capped `reigns_survived` counter + optionally a new `state.standing_change.trigger` value. *Hook:* flag as **duplicate coverage of BYZ-8**; contribute the two refinements as amendments.
- **Failure:** distinctively **non-violent** — Basil's 985 removal was the adult ruler exercising ordinary authority once the regency need lapsed. The argument for a Demotion Trigger costing the ruler nothing precisely because the renounced dynastic claim removes retaliation capacity.
- **Naming:** none — "parakoimomenos"/"Oath-Bound Administrator."

**D10. Yanagisawa Yoshiyasu & the sobayōnin office.** *Bodart-Bailey, The Dog Shogun (2006).*
- **Mechanic — "Lineage-Bypass Grant (Upstart Favorite) + reign-transition reassessment":** distinguishes from the purely-personal Favorites by being **institutional** — the ruler grants rank above caste/lineage (skipping the Initiation Gate lineage prerequisite), reusing **IT-8's Upstart tag** rather than a new bypass. Downfall orderly: at the next `state.succession`, the new ruler's *own advisor* gets a one-shot "Reassess Upstart Grant" stripping the Office half of BYZ-1's split (Dignity/domain may persist), no Contest/scandal. *New:* one scene-verb "Reassess Upstart Grant" (new ruler's advisor only, Upstart-tagged holders only, season after succession). *Hook:* extends IT-8 (§2.5a, landed) from Guild-mastership to the general ladder; composes with BYZ-1.
- **Failure:** ordinary graduated demotion — because the grant was *visibly* Upstart (durable tag, not concealed), no Grudge/Contest needed; the Reassess is a designed no-drama check (Upstart = provisionally-earned). The opposite pole from D3's violent purge.
- **Naming:** **flag** — "sobayōnin" glosses as "the Shogun's attendant"; most likely of the set to be mistranslated as "officer." Use "Steward"/"Chamberlain"/"Sobayōnin."

### Category E — Bureaucratic / institutional capture

*The M5 spine. Rise via sitting on the routing path without a vested office (the "Conduit"); fall via bypass, succession, or the patron's fall. This category proposes a unifying "Conduit" role that most other chokepoint entries reference.*

**E1. Roman imperial freedman secretaries (Pallas, Narcissus).** *Tacitus, Annals 12.53, 13.1; Suetonius, Claudius 28–29; Dio 60-61.*
- **Mechanic — "Conduit role + patron-bound ambition":** a new role-tag **"Conduit"** on any NPC regardless of Standing — no vested office, sits on the routing path (correspondence/treasury/petitions) between a patron (or the ruling player) and everyone else. `ambition.method` locked to "lawful→factional" (no coercive base); `ambition.goal` is explicitly *advancing a named patron's faction*. Patron-Disposition is a soft multiplier on the Conduit's progress rate; the moment the patron stalls/is thwarted/loses a succession card, the Conduit's progress force-resets toward 0 and trajectory flips violent/covert or is terminated (Narcissus's forced suicide within weeks of Claudius's death). *New field:* `patron_id` + "bound" flag (no concept today of one NPC's progress being derivatively contingent on another's ambition outcome). *Hook:* **the clearest load-bearing case for extending the ambition engine upward** — the whole pattern *is* one NPC's fate riding on another's.
- **Failure:** the specific bifurcation — two Conduits bound to *rival* patrons (Pallas/Agrippina vs Narcissus/Messalina-then-Britannicus). Whichever patron wins the succession card first triggers the other Conduit's forced-reset + near-term removal, fired as an automatic Key the instant the winning card resolves.
- **Naming:** none — "Conduit" avoids "officer"; do not call it "secretary-officer."

**E2. Venality of office & the Paulette (droit annuel).** *Mousnier, Institutions of France under the Absolute Monarchy; Bonney, The King's Debts.*
- **Mechanic — "Office vesting (Standing tenure modifier)":** an NPC/player holding Standing ≥1 in an office-bearing slot may pay a recurring Treasury cost (a Ledger Debt tag renewed each Accounting — miss it, vesting lapses) to flip the slot's tenure from "revocable" to "vested." A vested rank is immune to the *default* one-rank Demotion (Obligation failure, patron displeasure) — losable only via Severe/Total triggers (scandal, treason, framework abandonment). *New field:* `office_vested` bool + the recurring-Debt renewal check (the 1534 reversion-window is just the *absence* of vesting). *Hook:* a §1.0a Demotion-Magnitude modifier — no new Key.
- **Failure:** the *grantor* loses control, not the holder — model vested-office NPCs as contributing to the unserved-Needs term of the Π homeostat (§2.1) whenever a Directive touches their prerogatives: a standing structural veto bloc inflating Π (the Fronde), not a demotion the crown can impose.
- **Naming:** none.

**E3. Richelieu's intendants.** *Bonney, Political Change in France under Richelieu and Mazarin.*
- **Mechanic — "Shadow appointment (overlay agent)":** a faction leader spends an action to install an at-will-revocable "Intendant" into a territory held by a vested-office NPC (E2). The Intendant doesn't replace the holder's rank — it intercepts and supersedes that domain's Directive-resolution and petition routing, remaining fully revocable with no vesting available (chosen for dependence, not status). *New Key:* `mechanical.shadow_appointment` (patron_id, agent_id, overlaid_actor_id, domain_id, revocable=true — nothing captures "supersedes without replacing rank"). *Hook:* cross-referenced against vested-office holders and Provincial Authority Directive routing (§2).
- **Failure:** a hard dependency — the instant the appointing patron falls, the `shadow_appointment` Key auto-expires and the Intendant collapses via Total Dismissal, no appeal. Displaced vested holders accrue a Grudge against the *patron*, feeding Π even after the Intendant is gone.
- **Naming:** **explicit flag** — do NOT name this "officer." Use "Intendant"/"Crown Agent"/"Enforcer."

**E4. Thomas Cromwell as Principal Secretary.** *Elton, The Tudor Revolution in Government (1953); MacCulloch (2018).*
- **Mechanic — "Documentary strike (weaponized Conduit action)":** a Conduit with high enough progress spends it to file a "documentary strike" — manufactures apparent-sovereign authorization for its own initiative and directly triggers a Severe Demotion (§1.0a "public scandal") against a target who did not transgress. *New Key:* `mechanical.documentary_strike` (instigator_id, target_id, apparent_authorization=principal_id, actual_authorization=instigator_id, framing). *Hook:* chained to the §1.0a table as an externally-triggered Severe demotion; emits Grudge.
- **Failure:** the specific reversal — a documentary_strike is valid only while the Conduit retains patron favor (Disposition ≥ 0). The instant favor lapses, an *identical* strike becomes trivially available against them, auto-succeeding because they hold no vested Standing (Cromwell's fall to a bill of attainder).
- **Naming:** none — reuses "Conduit."

**E5. Ottoman Kızlar Ağası (Chief Harem Eunuch).** *Peirce, The Imperial Harem (1993).*
- **Mechanic — "Access-gate role with independent endowment income":** a Conduit variant restricted to NPCs structurally ineligible for the normal ladder (an eligibility flag). Controls a literal access chokepoint — any actor attempting a Social Contest with the gated principal must clear the gate-holder's Disposition threshold. Uniquely, converts access-leverage into an independent Treasury/endowment income that persists even if the gate later closes. *New fields:* `gate_target_id` + `access_threshold`; an endowment/ward asset (a Treasury stream decoupled from the patron — **needs a territory-layer income hook not fully specified in the corpus; flag before implementation**). *Hook:* extends the Conduit role with a Standing-ineligibility entry ticket + a durability asset.
- **Failure:** bound to the faction's succession like the Roman Conduits — patron loses a succession contest → Total Dismissal (forced exile) auto-fired — **but** the endowment is NOT auto-stripped; it becomes a contestable asset the new regime must separately reassign (a follow-on Petition/Intrigue seed).
- **Naming:** none if "Gatekeeper"/"Warden"; avoid "Chief Officer."

**E6. Wei Zhongxian & the vermilion rescript process.** *Cambridge History of China, vol. 7 (Ming, Part 1).*
- **Mechanic — "Borrowed mandate (disengaged-ruler escalation) + fictive-bond client cascade":** when the principal enters a modeled "disengaged" state, a bound Conduit may escalate to autonomously emitting Directive-equivalent Keys *as if authored by the principal* (Provincial Authority Directive generator, §2), then rapidly seed satellite clients via a `fictive_bond` flag — each client's Standing entirely parented to the Conduit, bypassing the Initiation Gate. *New fields:* `disengaged` flag on the principal (nothing models ruler disengagement as a precondition); `fictive_bond` bool on clients (makes collapse total). *Hook:* new Key `mechanical.borrowed_mandate` (proactive Directives, distinct from E4's strike-against-one-rival).
- **Failure:** totality and speed — the succession Key flips every `fictive_bond` client to Total Dismissal within a single Accounting tick, explicitly bypassing the §1.0a two-season appeal (never real standing, nothing to appeal).
- **Naming:** none — "Conduit"/"borrowed mandate."

**E7. Papal Cardinal-Nephew (Scipione Borghese under Paul V).** *Reinhard, Nepotism; von Pastor, History of the Popes.*
- **Mechanic — "Calendar-bound kin-gatekeeper with extraction-biased ambition":** a Conduit selected by kinship, whose `ambition.timeline` is *computed as a cap* from the patron's expected remaining tenure. The short known horizon biases `ambition.method` toward a new value **"extractive"** (rapid visible asset conversion over institution-building) and unlocks a 4th Ambition-card outcome beyond claim-office/betray-patron/expose-secret: **"convert to asset"** (locks in extracted wealth/leverage permanently, independent of the role's fate). *New:* one enum value `ambition.method='extractive'` + one card outcome "convert to asset." *Hook:* extends the ambition card menu (§3.2); reuses Rival Cohort for rival curial families.
- **Failure:** rival curial families (Colonna/Orsini/Barberini) → Rival Cohort extended to court kin-gatekeepers. On the patron's death, locked "convert to asset" gains are NOT clawed back, but the gate role reverts to Standing 0 for the incoming regime — **the extraction, not the office, survives.**
- **Naming:** none — "kin-gatekeeper"/"Conduit."

**E8. Soviet Nomenklatura & the Orgraspredotdel.** *Service, Stalin (2004); Rigby, Communist Party Membership (1968).*
- **Mechanic — "Patronage ledger (compounding sponsorship chokepoint)":** a genuinely new NPC-level stat — an NPC accumulates a running list of others whose Initiation Gate they personally sponsored via an alternate fulfillment path, each client carrying a Debt tag toward the sponsor. It **compounds**: each appointment increases the sponsor's durable support base for any future Standing-contest/Ambition-card vote, independent of the sponsor's own rank (which is why it looks unglamorous to status-conscious rivals). *New field:* `clients_sponsored` (list/weighted count) — nothing tracks a sponsor's cumulative influence across *other* NPCs' advancement. *New Key:* `mechanical.patronage_appointment`. *Hook:* an alternate-fulfillment branch on the §1.0 Initiation Gate; **the one entry that cannot be modeled as a Conduit/ambition variant** — needs the new aggregate field.
- **Failure:** rivals *miss the threshold* until reversal is impossible by normal channels — below a `clients_sponsored` threshold a normal contest/no-confidence card works; above it, no single card can touch them, requiring a higher-cost `mechanical.purge_cascade` demanding a rival supermajority coalition (why Lenin's Testament warning went unactioned — the coalition cost more than any single rival would pay).
- **Naming:** **explicit flag** — the natural label is "personnel officer"/"chief of staff." Use "Patron"/"Sponsor"/"Patronage Ledger."

**E9.** *(Note: category E has 8 entries; E1-E8 above are the full set.)*

### Category F — Rise to power via military retinues

*The M7 spine. Rise via a personal self-loyal armed force outside the ladder; fall via coalition purge, subordinate-flip, or fragmentation-on-death. Highest naming-collision density in the corpus — nearly every entry flags "officer."*

**F1. Roman Praetorian Guard — the 193 CE throne auction.** *Dio 74.11; Herodian 2.6; Bingham, The Praetorian Guard (2013).*
- **Mechanic — "Retinue Veto Threshold + Donativum bid-war":** a hidden `retinue.leverage` (0-5) sub-field on the ambition block, rising when the ruler relies on the guard to survive a crisis, *spent* (not decayed) at a succession vacancy. On vacancy, if leverage ≥ 3 the guard (a collective NPC) auto-resolves a kingmaker event — converting each claimant's bid into a Standing-contest where leverage adds a flat modifier to whichever offers the higher one-time Debt payout (the donativum), an auction resolved by comparing Debt-tag magnitude, not Standing. *New field:* `retinue.leverage`. *Hook:* the guard modeled as a collective NPC with its own ambition/trajectory at court scale (needs the same upward-extension the settlement-registry gate blocks); new Key `state.succession_vacancy` (payload `guard_leverage`, optional `donativum_bids`).
- **Failure:** Severus disbanding and rebuilding — the *only* way to zero `retinue.leverage`: a claimant who wins by military conquest (a `scene.battle_concluded` victory) rather than bidding resets leverage to 0 and reassigns the muster to loyal troops — but only if he did NOT bid (bidding then reneging is itself a Grudge generator seeding the next cycle).
- **Naming:** "guard"/"retinue" avoids it, **but** the mass-battle Key's `officer_deaths` field is a plausible trigger (a guard commander's battle-death seeding leverage) — any implementation must read that field in the mass-battle unit-commander sense, not conflate it with the court-level collective.

**F2. Carolingian Mayors of the Palace (Pepin, Charles Martel).** *Royal Frankish Annals s.a. 749–751; Einhard ch.1; McKitterick (1983).*
- **Mechanic — "Household Office Capture + Hereditary Lock-in":** an NPC in a subordinate household post with patronage-grant power sets `ambition.goal='make office hereditary'`; each grant increments progress AND writes a persistent Precedent tag scoped to the *office* ("this office grants to kin"). At progress 5 the office's succession rule flips crown-appointed → hereditary-within-family. *New field:* a mutable `succession_rule` on the office/post record (offices are currently static slots — the genuine gap). *Hook:* squarely **HRE-2 (Chapter Capture)** territory — pre-vacancy patronage investment in a formal post; new Key `state.office_hereditary_lock`.
- **Failure:** not reversal but **external legitimation required** — Pepin needed papal sanction. The hereditary-lock Key cannot itself trigger a dynasty-replacement (Standing-7 claim) without a second independent Key from a legitimating faction (Church Standing actor); absent it the office stays hereditary-but-subordinate (a stable sub-dynasty firing periodic failing "depose the crown" cards).
- **Naming:** none — "household office"/"patronage grant"; "Mayor of the Palace" as flavor is safe.

**F3. Ottoman Janissary Corps — tool to kingmaker.** *Ágoston & Masters, Encyclopedia of the Ottoman Empire (2009); Goodwin, The Janissaries (1994).*
- **Mechanic — "Corps Ossification":** a corps built to bypass nobles starts with `ambition.goal='serve the crown'` but accrues `corps.entrenchment` (0-5), rising passively every un-rotated Accounting (no fresh devshirme intake Key) and faster if its economic Debt tags (pay/guild interests) exceed a threshold. At 5 its ambition auto-flips (goal "veto reformist rulers," method violent) and gains standing veto: any ruler action reducing corps privilege auto-generates an Intrigue/Ambition contest instead of resolving. *New field:* `corps.entrenchment` — the tool-to-veto drift the ambition engine (individual-goal-scoped) doesn't model. *Hook:* **a genuinely new institution-level drift layer** — the corps occupies a Standing slot as an institutional actor (like the Crown Inner Circle); new Key `state.corps_entrenchment_shift`.
- **Failure:** only violent replacement (1826 Auspicious Incident) — at entrenchment 5 the *only* card resolution is "build a rival corps from zero, then a single decisive `scene.battle_concluded`," a forced mass-battle escalation with no lawful/social branch. A genuine gap the current trajectory (which assumes the *NPC's own* trajectory shifts) doesn't capture: the institution forecloses non-violent branches for whoever opposes it.
- **Naming:** **high risk** — use "corps member"/"retinue trooper," never "officer."

**F4. Mamluk slave-soldier households → Sultanate.** *Humphreys, From Saladin to the Mongols (1977); Ayalon, BSOAS 15-16.*
- **Mechanic — "Patron-Client Household (ustadh/mamluk)":** a bounded patron-client pair — a patron's ambition spends "recruit client" to create subordinates whose Standing is entirely derivative (a new relational `bond.patron_id`, distinct from kinship). On the patron's death, the *client* (not a biological heir) inherits accrued Standing/tags and a fresh recruitment cycle begins — blocking any bloodline past one generation, **enforced as a rule.** *New field:* `bond.patron_id`/`bond.client_ids` (the graph doesn't distinguish "inherits my Standing on death, non-hereditary" from ordinary ties). *Hook:* CHN-9-adjacent, but the anti-dynasty rule is closer to **BYZ-8**; a "derivative Standing" flag (evaporates on patron death unless a client inherits); new Key `state.patron_succession`.
- **Failure:** the *opposite* of most entries — stability came from blocking hereditary calcification. Rival *households* (buyut) fight for the citadel seat via faction-standing contests between households; the winner ascends and rivals keep their bonds intact (not purged) — the base perpetually renews via recruitment.
- **Naming:** none — "patron"/"client"/"household"; avoid "officer" for client-NPCs.

**F5. Francesco Sforza — condotta to crown (Milan).** *Machiavelli, Il Principe ch. 7 & 12; Mallett, Mercenaries and their Masters (1974).*
- **Mechanic — "Contracted Retinue → Dynastic Marriage Claim conversion":** a mercenary-captain's ambition (goal "best contract terms") unlocks, on a Standing threshold via contract fulfillment, a new goal-branch "marry into the patron house" — a proactive trajectory re-plan (existing "if blocked, shift method" logic applied on *success* rather than blockage), writing a latent Precedent tag ("this marriage was a political payment") until the patron line fails. *New:* none — clean reuse of ambition/trajectory + Standing + Precedent. *Hook:* the cleanest of the ten for reuse (contract → marriage → vacancy → seizure maps 1:1 onto ambition fields); the "vacancy" trigger is F1's `state.succession_vacancy` — **evidence that Key should be a shared cross-entry primitive.**
- **Failure:** the built-in tension — Filippo Maria twice tried to dissolve the betrothal. A countervailing patron-side ambition branch: "if client retinue-strength exceeds my Standing lead by N, attempt to void the marriage-claim Precedent tag" — a recurring social-contest tug-of-war, contested until the vacancy fires.
- **Naming:** none — "contracted captain"/"condotta retinue"; "captain" as flavor is fine but must not become the field/type name.

**F6. Wallenstein's self-financed imperial army.** *Wilson, Europe's Tragedy (2009); Mann, Wallenstein (1971).*
- **Mechanic — "Self-Financed Retinue Autonomy + Subordinate-Turned-Assassin":** `retinue.autonomy` (0-5, distinct from F1's political `retinue.leverage` — tracks *financial* independence, rising when the force self-sustains via requisition/contribution Keys). High autonomy raises Standing fast but writes a rival Grudge proportional to it. Crucially, the NPC's sub-commanders (bonded per F4) accrue their own `client.crown_loyalty` **separate from** `client.patron_loyalty` — the latent assassination vector. *New fields:* `retinue.autonomy` + a second loyalty axis on bonds. *Hook:* extends F4's bond with a second loyalty axis; new Key `state.retinue_autonomy_shift`.
- **Failure:** the sovereign turns the general's own senior subordinates against him — model dismissal NOT as a direct Standing-contest but as the crown spending Debt/patronage on clients' `crown_loyalty` to flip one into an assassination-trajectory (method jumps straight to violent against their own patron). **The one entry where the resolution routes *around* the power-base's strength rather than matching it.**
- **Naming:** **high risk** — Wallenstein's subordinates are literally "officers" in every source (Butler, Leslie, Gordon, Piccolomini). Use "retinue client"/"sub-commander," never "officer."

**F7. Cao Cao — "support the Son of Heaven to command those who defy him."** *Chen Shou, Sanguozhi; de Crespigny, Imperial Warlord (2010).*
- **Mechanic — "Legitimacy Hostage":** an NPC who physically controls a Mandate-bearing figure *without* deposing them gains `mandate.custody_bonus` — a flat Standing-equivalent bonus on any contest where the custodian invokes the captive Mandate, multiplying *effective* Standing for legitimacy-gated actions while base Standing is untouched. *New field:* `mandate.custody_bonus` + a `custodian_id` distinct from `holder_id` (**the sharpest genuine architectural gap** — Mandate must become separable-from-possession). *Hook:* the custodian occupies a Crown Inner Circle slot as de facto power; new Key `state.mandate_custody_change`.
- **Failure:** the Yuan Shao missed-window — a race, not a stable state: after any Mandate-vacancy/vulnerability Key, a limited window opens where any sufficient-Standing rival can attempt custody-seizure; once it closes the opportunity card despawns (Yuan Shao's rivals talked him out of it). Failure is *permanent forfeiture of a legitimacy chokepoint*, with deferred consequences (Cao Pi's usurpation a generation later → a long `ambition.timeline` on the dynasty-founding goal).
- **Naming:** none — "custody"/"Mandate-holder."

**F8. Minamoto no Yoritomo & the gokenin → Kamakura Shogunate.** *Mass, Yoritomo and the Founding of the First Bakufu (1999); Azuma Kagami.*
- **Mechanic — "Parallel Vassal-Band + Dual Legitimacy (shadow Standing)":** an NPC builds a personal vassal-band (F4 bonds) via direct land-for-service, accruing a **`shadow_standing` (0-7)** parallel track rising from vassal-grant Keys, not faction promotion. When it exceeds formal Standing by a threshold, the NPC spends a card to force the formal holder to **ratify** the shadow structure — converting shadow appointments to legal offices in one stroke (the sei-i taishōgun grant) while the sovereign keeps their Standing-7 seat: dual legitimacy, not replacement. *New field:* `shadow_standing` — an off-ladder accumulation track that can force ratification (faction_politics_v30 assumes climbs happen *on* the ladder). *Hook:* new Key `state.shadow_standing_ratified`.
- **Failure:** purge-your-own-lieutenant (Yoshitsune) — a client whose independent Standing (from personal battlefield success in mass-battle Keys) grows faster than `bond.patron_loyalty`; once it exceeds a threshold relative to the patron's `shadow_standing`, the patron's trajectory gains a forced "purge or lose the client to a rival power base" branch. Declining lets the over-prestiged client spin off their own `shadow_standing`, forking the band.
- **Naming:** none — "vassal-band"/"client"; "shugo"/"jito" as flavor safe; avoid "officer" for individual vassals.

**F9. The Mongol Keshig — hostage-and-training pipeline.** *Allsen, HJAS 46 (1986); May, The Mongol Art of War (2007).*
- **Mechanic — "Hostage-Cohort Pipeline (inverted-status recruitment)":** a ruler demands subordinate Standing-holders send an heir into household service — writes a `bond` (patron_id=ruler) on the heir AND a Debt tag against the subordinate's household (a *compulsory* hostage obligation). The heir's Standing resets to a service-track baseline overriding birth-Standing while in the corps — a new **`service_rank` (0-5)** inverted prestige ladder (corps seniority, not parent-house rank). On graduation, alumni are preferentially assigned open admin/command slots, converting `service_rank` into fast-tracked Standing. *New fields:* `service_rank` + a `compulsory` bool on the bond. *Hook:* extends F4's bond with a compulsory variant + a service-rank ladder that feeds *into* Standing (contrast F8's parallel-until-ratified); new Keys `state.hostage_service_commenced`/`state.service_graduation`.
- **Failure:** no clean historical reversal — structural: if the ruler stops rotating fresh cohorts (no new `hostage_service_commenced` for N ticks), alumni calcify into an old-boys' bloc (their `bond.client_ids` growing from *their own* patronage), converging on F3's `corps.entrenchment` — the pipeline becomes the hereditary interest it was designed to prevent unless continuously fed.
- **Naming:** none — "keshig"/"hostage-cohort," **but** graduates who later command mustered units become "officers" in the mass-battle sense at that point — the hand-off from `service_rank` alumnus to mass-battle officer is a boundary to document, not collide on.

**F10. Yuan Shikai & the Beiyang Army — to the presidency.** *MacKinnon, Power and Politics in Late Imperial China (1980); Ch'en, Yuan Shih-k'ai (1972).*
- **Mechanic — "Academy-Patronage Network + Fragmentation-on-Death cascade":** a recruitment/training privilege lets an NPC create many bonded clients at once — `network.scale` (recursive bonded-client count) grows via training Keys. At a threshold AND a state-crisis Key only this force can resolve (a designed "monopoly moment"), ambition auto-unlocks an "extract the throne" branch with compressed timeline (the abdication-instrument). On the NPC's death, `network.scale` **fragments**: each top-tier client (own sub-network above threshold) spins off as an independent Standing-holder — seeding a Warlord-Era contested field. *New field:* `network.scale` (recursive — F4's simple bond doesn't model nesting or fragmentation). *Hook:* extends F4's bond to nested chains; new Key `state.crisis_leverage_extraction`; reuse `state.patron_succession` generalized to fire recursively.
- **Failure:** fragmentation-on-death *is* the failure — no rival needed; the moment the top patron dies the cascade Key fires unconditionally, converting one Standing-7 holder into N mid-Standing rivals, each with a slice of the old tags and **none holding a legitimacy chokepoint** (contrast F7 Cao Cao, where legitimacy stays concentrated) — guaranteeing multi-way contested politics as designed.
- **Naming:** **highest risk of all** — the entry's own title uses "officer patronage"; Duan Qirui/Feng Guozhang are exactly the personally-loyal commanders that collide hardest. Use "client"/"network member"/"protégé"; **the design doc should carry an inline naming-collision callout at this mechanic's definition.**

### Category G — Downfall, purges & succession

*The mirror image of rise. These entries specify the failure side of M4/M5/M7/M8 in detail and introduce several genuinely-new primitives: dormant/conditional tags, the joint Coalition object, self-reinforcing Culpability chains.*

**G1. The Fall of Sejanus.** *Dio 58; Suetonius, Tiberius 61-65.*
- **Mechanic — "Chokepoint Access flag with Bypass-Key revocation":** an advisor bound to a specific ruler holds a tiered `access_monopoly` (all orders/info route through them). While held, Standing climbs faster than any Rival Cohort contest (rivals can't even be *seen* forming). The moment the ruler issues an order through ANY other channel, the flag flips off in one Key and Demotion jumps straight to **Total** (no graduated fall, only sudden arrest). *New field:* `access_monopoly` (bool/tier, bound to a ruler id). *Hook:* extends the ambition/trajectory engine upward onto a Standing-ladder NPC; new Key `chokepoint_bypass`.
- **Failure:** the bypass works because a designated alternate advisor has been secretly advancing their *own* ambition ("become the new channel") the whole time — invisible precisely *because* the chokepoint-holder's monopoly blinds them. Dramatic irony enforced structurally; the rival's ambition timeline runs in the background even though Access would normally suppress rival-card visibility.
- **Naming:** none — "Chokepoint Advisor"/"Access-holder"; "Praetorian Prefect" maps to a court advisor here.

**G2. The Fall of Thomas Cromwell.** *MacCulloch (2018); Elton, Reform and Reformation (1977).*
- **Mechanic — "Brokered-Outcome Ledger tag + appeal-bypass Demotion":** when an advisor brokers the ruler's marriage/alliance, tag the outcome "Brokered-Outcome." If the resulting ruler↔brokered-party Disposition delta is negative, it converts to a Grudge scaled to the damage, feeding §1.0a's Severe tier AND **disabling the normal 2-season appeal window** (modeling attainder — no trial). *New field:* an "appeal-bypass" boolean on a Demotion trigger (§1.0a currently guarantees appeal at Standing ≥1). *Hook:* Debt/Grudge subtype + a direct write into §1.0a; needs a rival "Substitute Intimate" ambition timed to the same Accounting.
- **Failure:** the rival wins not by attacking the broker but by having a substitute intimate already positioned (a niece) — the Ambition card is *pre-advanced* (near-threshold), so succession reads instant, not contested.
- **Naming:** none.

**G3. The Ottoman Kul System & the execution of Grand Viziers.** *Imber, The Ottoman Empire (2002); Peirce (1993).*
- **Mechanic — "legitimacy_ceiling classification + escheat-on-Total-Demotion":** a structural type `legitimacy_ceiling` ('capped' vs 'dynasty-eligible') gates a trade — a capped advisor climbs faster (reduced/waived Rival Cohort, can't found a rival dynasty) but ANY Demotion resolves at **Total** with zero appeal AND fires an **escheat** Key reverting accumulated Wealth to the patron. The baked-in mirror of Cromwell's attainder. *New fields:* `legitimacy_ceiling` + the escheat transfer (nothing reverts resources to a patron on dismissal today). *Hook:* a new NPC-type-conditional §1.0a row; new Key `escheat_purge`. **Strongly overlaps CHN-9 — build as a CHN-9 variant, not parallel.**
- **Failure:** rivals accumulate Grudge as the favorite's wealth grows; at threshold the ruler auto-fires Total + escheat as a "harvest" — a Π-homeostat-style accumulator specific to this favorite.
- **Naming:** "Grand Vizier" reads close to a command role — use "capped patron-advisor"/"kul-bound advisor," not "officer."

**G4. Wei Zhongxian & the Ming eunuch faction.** *Huang, 1587 (1981); Dardess, Blood and History (2002).*
- **Mechanic — "Succession-triggered dormant-tag cascade":** reuses `access_monopoly` (G1) but the revocation trigger is a **succession Key** — all NPCs whose monopoly binds to the outgoing ruler auto-suffer Total Demotion within a window UNLESS they *also* hold independently-earned transferable Standing. Simultaneously, rival Grudge/Precedent tags that sat **dormant** activate en masse as the demotion's justification. *New field:* a `trigger_condition: succession` metadata flag on Ledger tags (letting a tag sit inert until a specific Key fires — a genuinely new tag capability). *Hook:* new Key `succession_purge_cascade`.
- **Failure:** the incoming ruler's first Accounting fires the cascade — the holder's power has zero transferability to them, so the new ruler has every incentive to destroy the predecessor's chokepoint-holder. No external coalition needed; the ruler-change *is* the trigger.
- **Naming:** none direct; avoid "eunuch official" — use "palace advisor"/"chokepoint-holder."

**G5. The Yagoda–Yezhov–Beria NKVD succession.** *Conquest, The Great Terror (1990); Getty & Naumov, The Road to Terror (1999).*
- **Mechanic — "purge_predecessor ambition goal + self-reinforcing Culpability Debt chain":** a new goal subtype **`purge_predecessor`** — dismantle the current enforcement-holder's client network on the ruler's behalf. On installing at the vacated slot, the successor accrues a new **Culpability Debt** tag (compromising knowledge of the ruler), which seeds the *next* successor's `purge_predecessor` — a self-perpetuating chain, each link's Culpability magnitude higher, shortening that holder's own Demotion clock. *New:* the `purge_predecessor` goal + Culpability Debt (inside the Debt family) + a `shadow_deputy` Knot subtype (successor pre-embedded, advancing in secret). *Hook:* extends the ambition goal vocabulary (§3.2) + Debt-family chain semantics + Knot graph; new Key `predecessor_purge`.
- **Failure:** the chain doesn't self-terminate — each purger becomes the next target with an *accelerating* Demotion clock, until the ruler deliberately breaks the pattern or is removed. An escalating-magnitude spiral, not a single fall.
- **Naming:** "NKVD chief"/"security chief" is close — use "enforcer-patron"/"security-retinue head," never "officer" (`officer_deaths` already means mass-battle).

**G6. The Night of the Long Knives.** *Kershaw, Hitler 1889–1936: Hubris (1998) ch. 9; Hancock, Ernst Röhm (2008).*
- **Mechanic — "independent_power_base threshold + joint Coalition Purge object":** a new NPC field `independent_power_base` (raw retinue/influence not on the ladder); when it exceeds a threshold relative to the faction's institutional strength, auto-generate a temporary **multi-NPC Coalition object** — several rivals, each with pre-existing Grudge/Debt against the target, bound into a single joint Ambition/Intrigue card with a shared trigger and uneven shared payoff. Resolution retroactively writes a Precedent tag justifying the purge ("measures of self-defense"). *New:* `independent_power_base` + **the Coalition object itself** (ambitions today are strictly per-NPC, §3.1-3.2 has no joint structure — **the clearest new-mechanic requirement in the downfall set**). *Hook:* extends the ambition engine to support joint ambitions; new Key `coalition_purge`.
- **Failure:** uneven post-purge reward (one member gets an SS-style autonomy bump) can seed the *next* chokepoint-favorite recursively — a recursive setup hook, not a clean resolution.
- **Naming:** **direct collision** — the target (a paramilitary commander) is exactly the mass-battle "officer" shape. Operate strictly on the political/Standing layer; use "Warband Patron"/"Retinue-Captain," with mass-battle *reading* (not duplicating) `independent_power_base` if troop count must cross layers.

**G7. Basil Lekapenos, the Parakoimomenos (downfall view).** *Morris, BMGS (1988); Holmes, Basil II (2005).*
- **Mechanic — "succession_immune continuity flag + ruler-side rule_without_regent ambition":** `succession_immune` marks an NPC legally barred from ever holding Leadership — the *inverse* of G4's cascade: their Standing/Disposition explicitly **survive** the succession Key intact (they hold institutional Standing, not just access). Their fall is triggered not by a rival or succession but by the *new ruler's own* ambition — a ruler-side goal **`rule_without_regent`** climbing with the ruler's maturation, firing a *moderate* (not total) Demotion (exile, no Dishonored flag). *New:* `succession_immune` (contrasts G4) + `rule_without_regent`. *Hook:* extends the ambition engine with a ruler-side goal + a succession-continuity exception. **Strong overlap with BYZ-1 and BYZ-8 — consolidate, don't build parallel.**
- **Failure:** no external rival — the "rival" is the ruler's own ambition clock. A soft, near-inevitable, gentle/exile-grade fall (cross-reign continuity becomes evidence of scheming once the ruler no longer needs a regent).
- **Naming:** none — "Grand Chamberlain"/"court patron"; flag only the BYZ-1/BYZ-8 overlap.

**G8. Cesare Borgia & the execution of Remirro de Orco.** *Machiavelli, The Prince ch. 7 (1513); Bradford, Cesare Borgia (1976).*
- **Mechanic — "Reputation-tag transfer via scapegoat execution":** mostly reuse — a settlement's negative Suspicion/Disposition toward an enforcer accumulates normally. The new piece: a ruler-initiated **Public Execution Key** that (a) transfers the accumulated negative Reputation from ruler onto a one-time write-off on the enforcer, flipping the ruler's Reputation positive, and (b) sets the enforcer's Demotion to **Total instantly, bypassing the trajectory-consult step** (no flee/replan/warning). *New:* a "transfer" operation on the Reputation family (tags currently accrue/decay, not reassign between two NPCs) + the instant-Total-bypass-trajectory mode. *Hook:* new Key `scapegoat_execution`.
- **Failure:** the transfer only works if the enforcer had zero `independent_power_base` (reuse G6) — an enforcer with their own base can't be cleanly scapegoated; attempting it instead fails and generates a new Grudge directly against the ruler ("botched scapegoat").
- **Naming:** tempting "enforcement officer" — use "enforcer"/"instrument-advisor."

**G9. Robespierre & the Thermidorian Reaction.** *Scurr, Fatal Purity (2006); Palmer, Twelve Who Ruled (1941).*
- **Mechanic — "denunciation_pipeline exhaustion → procedural Coalition Purge":** a purger against a deliberative body tracks a `denunciation_pipeline` (a pool of named Grudge-tagged targets consumed one/Accounting). When the pipeline runs out, the purger's trajectory ("if blocked, shift method") branches into an ambiguous body-wide threat — and *that* branch auto-generates a **Coalition Purge (reusing G6)** among the body's own membership, resolved via a new `resolution_method: procedural-vote` (only aggregate Disposition-turn-hostile needed, no Treasury/troops, resolving within a single Accounting). *New:* only the `procedural-vote` value on the G6 Coalition object. *Hook:* almost entirely reuse; **note Crown Standing-6 (Inner Prince) already specifies "inner-circle majority withdraws (3 of 5 Dispositions fall below 0)" as a Demotion trigger — Robespierre's CPS seat maps onto that existing row, possibly needing only reflavoring.**
- **Failure:** the purger is safe only while the pipeline has *other* visible targets; forced to threaten the body itself, the same uncertainty that made the pipeline effective flips into a fast procedural coalition against them — speed (single Accounting, no army) is the differentiator from G6's slower resource-requiring coalition.
- **Naming:** none — the CPS seat maps to an existing Standing-6/7 row.

### Category H — Rival factions & court bureaucracy

*Cross-cuts M1/M4/M5. These entries emphasize the contested, multi-actor texture — how a rise is fought over, and how selection mechanisms themselves get captured.*

**H1. Fujiwara Regency Marriage Politics (Michinaga).** *McCullough, Cambridge History of Japan vol. 2 (1999); Sansom (1958).*
- **Mechanic — "Kinship Claim (bloodline chokepoint investment)":** an ambition targeting "become kin to the ruler's line" (method lawful) advancing via a multi-season Petition/Opportunity chain (courtship→dowry→betrothal→heir-birth). On threshold "install heir"/"claim regency" fires — but the reward is **not an office; it's a standing Ledger entry that keeps paying out every Accounting** as long as the kin-tie holds, independent of formal office (Michinaga ruling via Minister of the Left/Nairan). *New:* a "Kin-of-Ruler" value under the Precedent family that persists across office changes and grants a recurring rank-independent influence bonus. *Hook:* ambition engine ported to court scale with the payoff changed from "claim office" to "attach a persistent Precedent tag" — explicitly the "soft alternate ladder parallel to formal rank."
- **Failure:** a structural ceiling, not a rival — works only while the ruler *needs* the kin-tie to legitimize succession. The ruler's own trajectory eventually offers an insei-style bypass ("rule directly, retired") that permanently zeroes the payout of ALL Kin-of-Ruler tags in that court — systemic obsolescence, fired once by the ruler's trajectory.
- **Naming:** none.

**H2. Claudius's Freedman Secretariat (Pallas, Narcissus).** *Tacitus, Annals 12–13; Suetonius, Claudius 28–29; Weaver, Familia Caesaris (1972).*
- **Mechanic — "Correspondence Advisor / Access Chokepoint":** a low-Standing household servant is the ruler's Correspondence Advisor — all Petition/Intrigue Keys route through them before the ruler's queue. Ambition advances by filtering/redirecting Keys (approving allies, suppressing accusers), converting no formal rank into de facto influence measured by volume of successfully-filtered Keys. *New Key:* `access.correspondence_filtered` (child Key, cause=the intercepted Petition/Intrigue Key, payload {advisor_id, disposition: approved|suppressed|surfaced}) — the ambition engine has no mechanism for an NPC intercepting *other* Keys in transit. *Hook:* new Key + a routing hook between card-generation and card-delivery; follows the `officer_deaths → standing_change` child-Key precedent.
- **Failure:** two rival advisors each backing a different successor-designate (Pallas vs Narcissus) — on succession resolution, fire an automatic "patron_death → client purge" cascade: the loser's Reputation/Debt tags flip sign, forced Demotion (§1.0a Severe/Total) within one season, no appeal. The winner gets only a short reprieve (a decaying Disposition floor expiring within 1-2 seasons).
- **Naming:** **flag** — "ab epistulis"/"a rationibus" read as bureau-chief "officers." Use "Correspondence Advisor"/"Household Advisor."

**H3. Ottoman Valide Sultan ("Sultanate of Women," Kösem).** *Peirce (1993).*
- **Mechanic — "Contested Hereditary Slot (matrilineal reassignment)":** a Standing-6/7 "Valide"-equivalent bound not to an individual but to whichever NPC is mother/grandmother of the current ruler (per the Knots graph). On a ruler-succession Key it auto-reassigns via a relationship query rather than the Initiation Gate — but the incumbent isn't auto-displaced (Kösem held power into her grandson's minority against his own mother), so the slot **supports simultaneous rival claimants** until a Disposition/client-network contest resolves it. *New:* a "succession-bound" rank property (auto-recompute eligible claimants on succession). *Hook:* lives on the ladder as a special reassignment rule; doesn't need the settlement ambition engine (the office already exists formally).
- **Failure:** the coup as a Disposition-network flip — the incumbent's own Confidant-level client betrays her once the rival's client network crosses a Disposition threshold, while a *third* bloc stays loyal (a factional split, not a clean rout). The coup Key requires the rival's controlled-Disposition sum to exceed the incumbent's by a margin, not a single defection.
- **Naming:** none for the "Valide" slot; the loyal client ("Chief Black Eunuch") should be "Household Advisor"/"Chamberlain," not "officer."

**H4. Wei Zhongxian's eunuch faction vs the Donglin Academy.** *Dardess, Blood and History (2002); Wakeman, Cambridge History of China.*
- **Mechanic — "Patron-Bound Purge Ambition":** a Court Advisor's ambition (goal "purge rival ideological faction," method lawful→violent) advances by **weaponizing the existing Demotion Magnitude system** — manufacturing false-accusation Keys forcing Severe/Total demotions on targets who did nothing under normal rules. The payoff is a string of forced demotions against a named bloc, not a title. *New:* a `patron_bound: true` flag on the ambition object (ALL of this NPC's influence contingent on one ruler alive+attentive) + a `standing_change.forced` (or `cause: false_accusation`) variant so appeal mechanics behave differently for a fabricated trigger. *Hook:* the one entry where the historical mechanic maps onto an *already-built* resolver (Demotion) being *abused*, not a new advancement path.
- **Failure:** the `patron_bound` flag *is* the failure — on the patron's death, a short countdown, then the successor's own trajectory auto-resolves a card reversing every forced demotion and applying Total Dismissal to the advisor, no appeal (Wei's forced suicide within months of Chongzhen). Self-terminating on succession by construction.
- **Naming:** "Directorate of Ceremonial" reads as an "officer" title — use "Court Advisor"/"Palace Advisor."

**H5. Thomas Cromwell's patronage rise & faction fall.** *MacCulloch (2018); Elton (1953).*
- **Mechanic — "Indispensable Processor + Attainder Bypass":** an advisor's ambition (goal "become indispensable at processing the ruler's transactions" — grants, dissolutions, statutes, marriages; method administrative) advances by resolving Mandate/Petition Keys, each success writing Reputation/Debt tags on beneficiary clients (a patronage network via Knots). Distinctive back half: destruction bypasses the normal appeal path. *New:* an "Attainder Bypass" sub-case on §1.0a's Total tier that explicitly **skips the 2-season appeal** (no trial, resolved in one scene when a rival feeds the ruler a personal grievance overriding institutional utility). *Hook:* the failure mode is a genuinely new §1.0a carve-out.
- **Failure:** the processor has no hereditary/institutional floor — a rival uses the *same* marriage-introduction lever the processor built their network on to manufacture a personal-grievance Intrigue Key triggering Attainder Bypass in one scene. **Years of positive Ledger tags provide zero defense** — accumulated utility does not buy appeal rights once this trigger fires.
- **Naming:** none — "Privy Seal"/"Chief Advisor."

**H6. Cosimo de' Medici's shadow patronage rule.** *Kent, The Rise of the Medici (1978); Rubinstein, The Government of Florence Under the Medici (1966).*
- **Mechanic — "Slate Control (selection-mechanism capture)":** rather than climbing, this ambition (goal "capture the appointment/selection mechanism itself," method lawful→covert) targets the resolver that generates candidate slates (the Provincial Authority Directive generator, or a settlement's Rival Cohort pre-emption roll) — on payoff, a hidden `selection_bias` weighting so a formally-neutral process reliably outputs the faction's people, *without holding the office*. *New:* `selection_bias` on whatever resolver generates candidate slates (nothing lets an NPC secretly weight a selection roll rather than compete in it). *Hook:* **the one pattern fitting neither the ambition payoff types (claim/betray/expose) nor the ladder advancement types** — it targets the *generator* of the rolls; needs a new hook point in the Directive-generation logic or Rival Cohort assignment, gated behind ambition progress.
- **Failure:** symmetric and reversible — whoever holds `selection_bias` can have it seized by a rival the moment they're structurally vulnerable (exile/minority/disgrace); Albizzi's 1433 exile of Cosimo, reversed a year later using the identical lever. Model as a single ownership pointer (a Coup Key flips it in one event), same mechanism either direction.
- **Naming:** none directly; the captured body ("accoppiatori") should be "Selectors"/"the Slate," not "officers."

**H7. Heshen's monopoly on access under Qianlong.** *Nivison, "Ho-shen and His Accusers" (1959); Cambridge History of China vol. 9 Pt 1 (2002).*
- **Mechanic — "Plural Advisor Stacking":** one NPC accumulates MULTIPLE concurrent advisor roles (info gatekeeper + fiscal controller + appointment-vetter), each unremarkable but combining into a monopoly, plus a **decaying-ruler-attention multiplier** (illicit-gain rate scales with the ruler's Disposition/attention decline). *New:* (1) a `concurrent_roles: []` array (or multiple simultaneous ambition objects — the engine is single-goal today); (2) a **hidden visibility flag** on Debt/Grudge tags (embezzlement stays concealed until an `audit_exposed` reveal Key). *Hook:* stacks H2-style role instances; concealment needs the hidden flag + a reveal-class Key (child of a succession/investigation Key, per the `officer_deaths → standing_change` pattern).
- **Failure:** the growth multiplier inverts hard at succession — the new ruler was structurally excluded from the access network (Disposition toward the advisor never rose), so on succession auto-fire `audit_exposed`, converting hidden Debt to public Grudge/Reputation overnight, then Total Dismissal. The concealment that protected him for years provides zero defense once the succession Key flips visibility.
- **Naming:** **explicit flag** — "Grand Minister"/"Chief Officer" is the most tempting and collides hardest. Use "Plural Advisor"/"Court Patron."

**H8. Shah Abbas I's Ghulam counter-faction vs the Qizilbash.** *Savory, Iran Under the Safavids (1980); Matthee, Persia in Crisis (2012).*
- **Mechanic — "Power-Base Tagging (dependent-client counter-faction)":** a *ruler-initiated* counter-mechanic — when a bloc derives power from an independent base (land/hereditary office/tribe) letting it threaten deposition, the ruler's ambition seeds and promotes a rival cohort explicitly tagged as having **no independent base** (only personal favor), filling the same slots. Their total dependency is the design intent (they can't credibly defect — nothing to defect *with*). *New:* a `power_base` field {independent | favor-dependent} — nothing today distinguishes whether a Dismissal actually removes power or just a title. *Hook:* consumed by the §1.0a Demotion resolver — `power_base` gates whether a Dismissal is *enforceable*.
- **Failure:** generational drift — model `power_base` slowly drifting favor-dependent → independent over successions as the cohort accumulates its own Reputation/Debt client networks; a periodic recheck nudges it independent once a favor-dependent NPC's client-network Disposition crosses a threshold, so a *second* counter-faction eventually becomes necessary. The fix decays into the problem it solved.
- **Naming:** none — "ghulam" = "Retinue"/"Household Client," not "officer."

**H9. The Day of the Dupes — Richelieu vs Marie de Médicis.** *Moote, Louis XIII, the Just (1989); Bergin, Cardinal Richelieu (1985).*
- **Mechanic — "Unwitnessed Promise / Audience Race":** a ruler's private unwitnessed decision to dismiss an advisor (extracted by rivals) is modeled NOT as an immediate Demotion Key but as a **rumor-stage Intrigue card — reversible until ratified.** During a short **Commitment Window**, the threatened advisor can contest a race for the next physical audience (a Speed/social contest) to reverse it before it locks. Any OTHER NPC who publicly commits Disposition to the rumored winner during the window gets flagged, regardless of outcome. *New:* a "Premature Commitment"/"Exposed" tag (fits under Grudge) + a scene-level "Commitment Window" timer (a bounded window between a rumor card and its ratification, during which commitment actions are logged but not yet consequence-bearing). *Hook:* a new scene-trigger: rumor card → Commitment Window → contested Audience-Race → real `state.standing_change` only at window close, retroactively converting window commitments into tags. Generalizes to any court scene where unwitnessed intent creates a race condition.
- **Failure:** the rival's failure IS the mechanic — they read the rumor as ratified and publicly commit before the race resolves; when the incumbent wins, every committer gets "Premature Commitment" and becomes a valid purge target (Marillac's arrest, Marie's exile). The rewarded variable is **speed-of-access after the vulnerable moment**, not accumulated pre-window support.
- **Naming:** none.

### Category I — Venality of office

*The M6 spine. Rise via purchase — of rank, heritability, toll-posts, or plural holds; fall via no-loyalty-reserve, prestige devaluation, or reform under crisis. Directly implicates the needs_jordan "how many parallel wealth-tracks" question (BYZ-2 was cut for exactly this).*

**I1. Auction of the Roman throne — Didius Julianus.** *Dio LXXIV.11; Historia Augusta; Grant, The Roman Emperors (1985).*
- **Mechanic — "Garrison Auction — kingmaker bidding for vacant Leadership":** when Leadership vacates through non-institutional means (assassination/coup) while a Standing-6+ NPC commands the core armed Retinue, open a Crisis-family card: two+ Standing-5+ NPCs (or the player) bid Treasury in escalating rounds directly to the Retinue-commander; highest bid wins instant elevation same-Accounting, bypassing Initiation Gate/Deeds. *New field:* a `purchased_legitimacy` boolean on the Leadership-holder (suppresses the accrued-loyalty buffer against Coup Counter). *Hook:* reuses `state.standing_change` via `cause="garrison_auction"`; a **third** succession path alongside institutional (SUC-01-03) and dynastic (LIN-01-04). **Distinct from HAB-2** (a one-shot vacancy auction, not a sustained track — should NOT merge into the HAB-2/BYZ-1 parallel-track decision).
- **Failure:** `purchased_legitimacy` means the next Standing-5+ Retinue-commander with *genuine* Disposition ≥0 among the rank-and-file gets an auto-favored Coup Counter roll within ~2 Accountings (Severus's march) — purchased Standing confers no loyalty reserve.
- **Naming:** do not call the armed body's members "officers" — use "Retinue"/"Guard cadre," "Retinue-commanding NPC."

**I2. Verres's Sicilian governorship.** *Cicero, In Verrem; Gruen, The Last Generation of the Roman Republic (1974).*
- **Mechanic — "Extraction-Debt Ledger tag + delayed Exposure Key":** an NPC who buys office starts with a hidden Debt tag sized to the bribe; resolving Obligations by converting the office's extractive verbs (Levy/Tax) to personal Treasury pays it down but increments a paired hidden **Exposure** counter. If a rival/player spends an Investigate (§1.3) within a post-term audit window, roll against Exposure; success fires a Key exposing the extraction → Severe Demotion (§1.0a) + clawback. *New:* a paired hidden `Exposure` counter (distinct from Debt face value). *Hook:* extends **HRE-4 Borrow** (debt self-financed through the office's own extraction) + pairs the audit window with **SE-7's Residencia** beat; new Key `state.extraction_exposed` (child `state.standing_change`).
- **Failure:** a timing race, not guaranteed escape — success is proportional to the rival's *actual prosecution investment* (Cicero's 50-day evidence tour), not automatic detection. Absent a willing rival, the Debt resolves clean (Verres nearly got away).
- **Naming:** none.

**I3. The Paulette — hereditary venality in France.** *Mousnier (1971); Doyle, Venality (1996).*
- **Mechanic — "Droit Annuel — heritable-rank upkeep fee":** a purchased rank gains an optional `heritable` flag, bought for an ongoing per-Accounting Treasury fee. While current, the holder's death passes the rank to a named heir bypassing the Initiation Gate; a missed payment lapses heritability (grace period) back to non-heritable. *New:* `heritable: bool` + `droit_annuel_due` cost field; heir pointer reuses the Knots graph. *Hook:* **the exact concern BYZ-2 Purchased Dignity was CUT for** (a third parallel wealth-track alongside BYZ-1 and HAB-2 — needs_jordan item 3). Present alongside that decision; more surgical than BYZ-2 (recurring-fee heritability only).
- **Failure:** institutional — a faction-scoped Precedent tag increments per heritable transfer; at threshold any leader attempt to claw back heritable ranks (e.g. HAB-6 Crush the Estates) suffers an escalating Legitimacy/Coup-Counter penalty — the robe-class becomes a check the leader created and can't freely undo (the Fronde).
- **Naming:** none.

**I4. Renaissance papal venal offices.** *Partner, The Pope's Men (1990).*
- **Mechanic — "Chokepoint Seat — purchased toll-post":** an NPC buys a newly-manufactured, purely-administrative Seat sitting athwart a named Key-emission pathway (e.g. all Petitions routed through Hold Court). Every other actor's action through that pathway pays a toll (AP/Treasury) credited to the Seat-holder before resolving. *New:* the Chokepoint Seat as a lightweight purchasable entity (pinned to a pathway, owner pointer, toll rate). *Hook:* new Key `state.toll_extraction`; purchase reuses `standing_change`. **No item in the 44 covers this** — HAB-7 Ordenanza is petitioner-initiated friction, not a passive toll on unrelated actors. **Flag as a genuinely new gap.**
- **Failure:** maximally vulnerable to legislative removal once visible — each `toll_extraction` increments a faction-wide Reform Pressure counter (reuse the Π homeostat); at threshold a Crisis card offers the leader a one-time "Abolish the Seat" (no refund, permanent Grudge on the purchaser).
- **Naming:** not "chokepoint officer" — "Seat"/"Toll-Post" (echoes HRE-2's "College seats").

**I5. Albrecht of Brandenburg's purchase of Mainz.** *MacCulloch, Reformation (2003) ch. 4.*
- **Mechanic — "Patron-Financed Plural Hold":** an NPC holds Standing in a SECOND faction/ladder simultaneously if a Financier (reuse HRE-4) fronts the dispensation cost, creating a Debt-Service Obligation tag that **biases the indebted NPC's trajectory** toward whichever move maximizes Treasury flow to the Financier — even against their own stated `ambition.goal`. *New:* a `debt_service_priority` weight on trajectory; **larger open question — holding Standing in two ladders at once may need a multiplicity flag if the schema assumes single-faction Standing per NPC (flag for verification — the biggest new-field risk in the batch).** *Hook:* extends **HRE-4 Borrow** with pluralism (two offices, one loan, repayment serviced by compelled ambition behavior).
- **Failure:** a **contagion** failure, not merit — if the Financier's own Standing/solvency collapses independently, the Debt-Service tag converts instantly to a Demotion-Magnitude-2 (§1.0a Severe) on the indebted NPC regardless of conduct (the Fuggers' exposure to the Habsburg/papal debt web).
- **Naming:** the compelled downstream action (indulgence-drive equivalent) should be a "Mandate," not an "officer directive."

**I6. Venice's sale of nobility (case nuove).** *Davis, The Decline of the Venetian Nobility (1962).*
- **Mechanic — "Emergency Rank Buy-In":** during a Treasury-shortfall Crisis, leadership may open a one-time, non-recurring option to sell direct entry into a normally-closed tier for a large lump payment, bypassing the Initiation Gate. Distinct from I3 (single crisis-triggered lateral admission, not recurring heritability). *New:* none structurally — reuses `standing_change` + a persistent, never-fully-decaying "New-Money"/"Case-Nuove" Reputation sub-tag modulating Disposition against legacy-rank NPCs. *Hook:* settlement/faction-scale sibling of **FA-JP2 Recognition fork** (FA-JP2 governs recognition for existing members; this governs emergency lateral entry for outsiders); reuses the Π-triggered Crisis card.
- **Failure:** social, not institutional — legacy-rank Disposition toward case-nuove NPCs starts at a permanent floor penalty unless the buyer completes a marriage-alliance Knot with a legacy family (converts but doesn't erase the tag). Absent that, the buyer is legally equal but mechanically excluded from inner-circle Mandate assignments — a *designed* exclusion.
- **Naming:** none.

**I7. Purchase of commissions in the British Army.** *Bruce, The Purchase System in the British Army, 1660–1871 (1980).*
- **Mechanic — "Retinue-Command Purchase Schedule + Rank Sale":** a formal faction-published price schedule for purchasing INTO a Standing rank carrying **retinue-command authority**, scaled by the specific warband's prestige tier. Paired with a secondary-market Rank Sale — an existing holder sells their slot to a chosen buyer (subject to Standing-6+ approval) instead of it opening on death. *New:* a per-Retinue prestige-tier price modifier + a `rank_sale` transaction record. *Hook:* new Key `state.rank_sale` (buyer, seller, price, approving authority — two-party, unlike `standing_change`); reform hooks the same HAB-6 ladder-amendment lever.
- **Failure:** reform requires two conjoined preconditions (not persuasion of the vested class): (1) a legitimizing Crisis Key (a battlefield disaster making incompetence-cost visible) + (2) a Leadership-only prerogative override rewriting the Schedule *without* the normal council ratification vested holders would block, at a one-time Legitimacy cost (Gladstone's Royal Warrant bypassing the Lords).
- **Naming:** **LOUDEST FLAG IN THE CORPUS** — this is literally buying military command rank. Never "commission"/"officer"/"commanding officer." Use "Retinue-Command rank"/"warband command slot"/"muster-command Standing." Cross-reference mass_battle_v30 explicitly: the purchased Standing *qualifies its holder to be ASSIGNED a Retinue*; it is NOT the auto-generated in-battle commander role.

**I8. Qing juanna sale of titles/offices.** *Zhang, Power for a Price (Harvard Asia Center, 2021).*
- **Mechanic — "Juanna Track — floating-rate purchase promotion":** a second, purchase-based path running alongside **CHN-2's Imperial Examination Ladder** (needs_jordan). The Treasury cost of a rank-jump floats each Accounting via a formula off the faction's fiscal-pressure state (reuse the Π homeostat retargeted at deficit). *New:* a computed `juanna_rate` (a formula, not a new resource). *Hook:* **the explicit missing "purchase" half of the CHN-2 decision** (needs_jordan item 3, the single highest-value open rank fork). Do not author standalone — present as CHN-2's paired purchase answer to its merit question. Writes an existing Grudge tag applied *collectively* to the exam cohort per juanna promotion (reusing CHN-9 Kaochengfa's pattern).
- **Failure:** prestige-devaluation, not individual collapse — each juanna promotion increments the collective exam-cohort Grudge; at a faction-wide threshold, exam-track §1.0a Obligation tolerance tightens + a faction Legitimacy penalty to future Directives — the recurring political cost the court weighed each time it dialed sales up.
- **Naming:** any purchase-track title must reuse existing Standing-N vocabulary, not invent a "purchased officer" title.

**I9. Spanish Habsburg sale of colonial offices (beneficio de oficios).** *Burkholder & Chandler, From Impotence to Authority (1977).*
- **Mechanic — "Distance-Discounted Office Price + Self-Audit Capture":** extends **SE-7 Residencia/Visita** with two additions — (a) purchase price scales inversely with distance from the capital while the same distance penalizes the Residencia detection roll; (b) a buyer may spend extra Treasury to seat an allied NPC on the reviewing tribunal (Self-Audit Capture), further reducing detection for the tenure. *New:* `distance_price_modifier`/`distance_audit_penalty` (formulas over existing distance data, precedent FA-JP4 Court Attendance §1.4a); tribunal-ally link reuses Knots. *Hook:* a sixth instrument on the S-2 Governor-Oversight Toolkit menu.
- **Failure:** Madrid's own check was corruptible — Self-Audit Capture only shifts probability. A rival with sufficient Investigate investment can surface the *captured-tribunal arrangement itself* as a second-order exposure Key (distinct from exposing the extraction), triggering a **compounded** Demotion (extraction + capture = separate §1.0a triggers). Each layer of capture is itself discoverable — corruption never fully closes.
- **Naming:** none.

**I10. Ottoman malikane lifetime tax-farm auctions.** *Salzmann, Politics & Society 21:4 (1993).*
- **Mechanic — "Lifetime Grant Auction + De-Facto-Heritability Entrenchment Race":** a short-leash annually-re-auctioned revenue-claim (reuse **BYZ-7 Pronoia**) converts, via a large upfront payment, to a lifetime grant. The state never formally grants heritability (distinct from I3), but each Accounting the holder's *family* accrues an **Entrenchment** tick (reusing the 0-5 progress pattern at family/lineage scale) via patronage/marriage actions. Reclaiming before threshold is a clean administrative action; after threshold, reclaiming instead fires a Rebellion-risk Crisis card. *New:* a family/lineage-scoped Entrenchment counter (nothing tracks multi-generational accrual) + the before/after-threshold reclaim branch. *Hook:* extends BYZ-7 as its lifetime-tenure auctioned variant. **The clearest candidate for extending the ambition engine UPWARD to a scope it wasn't built for — but it needs only Accounting-cadence ticking + the Knots graph, so it is NOT gated on the unbuilt settlement registry** the way per-NPC ambition is.
- **Failure:** the center's reclaim window is the mechanic — intervention cost rises monotonically with Entrenchment while local coercive/patronage leverage rises in lockstep, so delay past threshold converts every future reclaim from administrative to military/Crisis. A center that intervenes early still wins cleanly (Istanbul's periodic successful reassertions).
- **Naming:** none.

### Category J — Religious / ideological authority

*The M8 spine. Rise via Standing-independent legitimacy — prophetic, jurisprudential, credentialing-body, cross-ladder co-signature. Fall only by counter-authority in the same currency, or covert violence. This category most directly demands the peninsula-scale aggregation gap named in the system context.*

**J1. Investiture Controversy — Gregory VII vs Henry IV.** *Blumenthal, The Investiture Controversy (1988); Dictatus Papae 1075.*
- **Mechanic — "Investiture Gate (cross-ladder co-signature)":** any secular promotion at Court-Attendance tier (Standing 4+) or any Standing 6-7 succession requires a **co-sign Key from a qualifying religious-ladder NPC (Church Standing 5+)** before resolving. Withheld → `invested_status = Uninvested`: subordinate loyalty (Debt/Reputation tags) flagged breakable, each subordinate's trajectory gains an "oath released → back a rival" branch. Granting later retroactively clears it. *New:* `invested_status` {invested | uninvested | contested} on the NPC + `co_signer_id`/`co_sign_status` payload on succession/standing_change. *Hook:* new Key `state.investiture_ruling` (modeled on `coup_attempted`'s shape); reads Church CI as the co-signer's leverage. **Complements BYZ-1** (BYZ-1 splits one ladder into two tracks; this adds a co-signature gate *between* two ladders).
- **Failure:** decades of back-and-forth, not a clean win — model Uninvested with a renewal/decay cycle and give the secular ruler an antipope counter-lever: sponsor a rival claimant up the *same* religious ladder (or a schismatic offshoot) for a competing co-sign, splitting the religious ladder into two legitimacy-granting tracks and forcing every future Gate check to resolve which co-signer counts.
- **Naming:** none.

**J2. Savonarola's theocratic interregnum.** *Weinstein, Savonarola (Yale, 2011).*
- **Mechanic — "Prophetic Mandate (zero-Standing moral-authority track)":** an NPC can hold Standing 0 on every ladder yet run a full ambition (goal "set the moral-political agenda," method preaching) whose progress is driven by accumulated public `scene.witness`/`scene.gossip` Keys crossing a threshold, converting into a Standing-independent **`moral_authority` (0-5)** that can substitute for Standing in Hold Court agenda-setting branches — without ever occupying a rank. *New:* `moral_authority` (decoupled from Standing) + a rule that court-scene gates accept it as an alternate qualifying score. *Hook:* extends the ambition engine sideways — same substrate applied to an NPC who never enters a ladder; requires the threshold-check to accept aggregate scene Keys as a progress source.
- **Failure:** fragile with no office to entrench — two independent, *both-required* levers: (1) an EXTERNAL rival religious Standing-holder emits a delegitimizing Key that zeroes `moral_authority` directly (an excommunication-style Key, precedented by §1.0a's Excommunication → Standing -1, here applied to moral_authority), and (2) a DOMESTIC secular Rival Cohort supplies the actual removal (needs extending to trigger against zero-Standing moral_authority actors). Neither alone suffices — the historical pattern requires both.
- **Naming:** none.

**J3. Cardinal Cisneros.** *Rummel, Jiménez de Cisneros (CRRS, 1999).*
- **Mechanic — "Cross-ladder ambition chaining into emergency succession":** `ambition.goal` extended to name a **chain of intermediate offices across a DIFFERENT faction's ladder** before a secular target (e.g. goal "become Regent," chain [confessor → Church Standing 3 → Cardinal]). Each link advances via normal progress; the terminal step fires only when a `state.succession` opens with `succession_mode=emergency` and checks the NPC's independent revenue + coercive access (their religious rank) rather than prior secular Standing. *New:* `ambition.goal` needs a `ladder_chain` list (cross-faction), where today the goal is implicitly single-ladder. *Hook:* **the least-new-mechanics entry** — mostly closes the cross-ladder-target gap + extends succession eligibility to a non-secular-ladder candidate.
- **Failure:** Cisneros did NOT fall — not a downfall but a durable **standing tension**: nobility Grudge accrues at an elevated rate but **cannot be bought off** through the normal favor-economy because the power base (religious-ladder revenue + coercion) is orthogonal to secular patronage. Model these Grudge tags as **non-negotiable** (immune to normal favor/gift resolution), never forcing a Demotion.
- **Naming:** none.

**J4. Rasputin (ideological view).** *Smith, Rasputin (2016).*
- **Mechanic — "Backchannel Confidant (Disposition-as-Standing substitute)":** a Standing-0 NPC gains a formal advisory **veto** over others' ambition/appointment resolutions, gated purely on an extreme Disposition with one inner-circle NPC (originating from a private-crisis scene emitting a `meta.miraculous_event` Key). That single high-Disposition relationship reads, in specific court-decision resolutions, as if it were a Standing rank for blocking/endorsing appointments. *New:* not a field — a rule flag on specific court-decision resolvers permitting Disposition-with-inner-circle to substitute for Standing in advisory-veto checks. *Hook:* originates from `meta.miraculous_event`; add an OR-branch on appointment Keys checking Disposition-with-ruler instead of Standing.
- **Failure:** no formal removal exists for a Standing-0 actor — Demotion Magnitude (§1.0a) has no purchase. The only lever for rivals is escalating directly from a Grudge tag to a covert/violent DA (`da.covert_betrayal`) — no intermediate appeal/demotion path (assassination, not dismissal, was the historical removal).
- **Naming:** **explicit flag** — "Confidant"/"Backchannel Advisor," never "officer."

**J5. Ottoman Şeyhülislam deposition fatwas.** *Tezcan, The Second Ottoman Empire (2010); Zilfi, The Politics of Piety (1988).*
- **Mechanic — "Sanctioning Prelate (required co-signer on coup)":** the top rank of a religious ladder holds a unique capability — any `state.coup_attempted` against the sitting secular leader must carry a `sanctioned=true` flag sourced from this NPC to have any chance of `outcome=success`; without it the coup auto-fails regardless of military Standing/troops. The office is appointive and revocable by the ruler it may be asked to depose (a Patron relationship, reusing **CHN-9's patron-bound framing**). *New:* `sanctioned` (bool) + `sanctioning_npc_id` payload on `coup_attempted`. *Hook:* direct extension of `coup_attempted`; the Prelate's vulnerability reuses CHN-9's patron-bound lapse; reads `moral_authority` (J2) if the Prelate cultivated an independent preacher base.
- **Failure:** double bind — the ruler can pre-emptively dismiss the Prelate before a hostile fatwa (patron-bound lapse). The check on that counter-move is the Prelate's independent `moral_authority`: dismissal succeeds automatically only below a threshold; above it, dismissal itself triggers a destabilization event (Kadızadeli-style preacher network makes removal costly).
- **Naming:** mild caution — avoid "Court Officer"; use "Sanctioning Prelate" or fold into the patron-bound vocabulary.

**J6. Tobacco Protest fatwa & the Shia marja'iyya.** *Keddie, Religion and Rebellion in Iran (1966).*
- **Mechanic — "Jurisprudential Renown (peninsula-scale non-appointive prestige ladder)":** a wholly separate track from the 0-7 Standing ladder — a scholarly NPC accrues **`renown`**, peninsula-scoped and aggregated across MANY settlements' teaching/doctrine scenes (summed `scene.witness`/`scene.gossip` from settlements the NPC never administers). Nobody can appoint or revoke onto this track — only peer jurisprudential contest. At high Renown the NPC can emit a policy-override ruling superseding a standing Directive at mass/peninsula scale (the tobacco boycott's near-total compliance the state couldn't command). *New field:* `renown` (peninsula-scoped aggregate). *Hook:* **the entry that most directly demands extending the settlement-scale ambition engine to a peninsula-wide aggregate — the named system-context gap.** New Key `state.doctrinal_ruling` (scale_signature [peninsula], persistent; payload ruling_npc_id, renown_at_issue, target_policy_id, compliance_magnitude).
- **Failure:** cannot be co-opted by office or bought off — only outcompeted in its own currency. The only check on a `doctrinal_ruling` is a rival on the SAME Renown track issuing a counter-ruling, resolved as a Standard/Social Contest between the two Renown scores. No secular Standing-holder can act on this track — only sponsor a rival cleric's climb.
- **Naming:** none.

**J7. The Fifth Dalai Lama & the Ganden Phodrang.** *Shakabpa, Tibet: A Political History (Yale, 1967); Goldstein.*
- **Mechanic — "Institutional-Search succession + Patron-Client legitimation exchange":** (a) `state.succession` gains a new `succession_mode=institutional_search` where `new_leader_id` is chosen by the religious establishment (a Desi/regent role) — and the regent can **artificially DELAY** emitting the Key past the incumbent's actual death (a concealment window under establishment control). (b) A patron-client Key (modeled on `da.diplomatic_alliance`) exchanges an external faction's military force for the religious leader's legitimation, converting the religious Standing-7 leader into the discrete Leadership state (§1.0). *New:* `institutional_search` enum value + a `concealment_duration` payload. *Hook:* extends `state.succession` + a new `da.diplomatic_alliance` instance; terminates in the existing Leadership state.
- **Failure:** the concealment window is the fragility — give it a decay clock. If a rival/foreign patron obtains a `scene.witness`/`scene.gossip` Key exposing the concealment before the preferred candidate is ready, `succession_mode` retroactively flips to `contested`, applying a legitimacy penalty and opening a Rival Cohort challenge that wouldn't otherwise exist.
- **Naming:** none — "Regent"/"Desi."

**J8. Calvin's Consistory in Geneva.** *Monter, Calvin's Geneva (1967); Kingdon's Consistory registers.*
- **Mechanic — "Discipline Board — externally-sourced civic-eligibility gate":** a standing body (one religious NPC + co-opted lay Standing-holders) controls a boolean `civic_eligible` flag on OTHER NPCs and the player. Losing it zeroes ability to hold/advance ANY secular Standing rank regardless of secular progress — the gate is **external to the ladder it constrains** (unlike a same-ladder Initiation Gate). *New:* `civic_eligible` cross-cutting flag (checked by any secular 0→1 advancement) + a `gate_authority_id` payload on `standing_change`. *Hook:* proposes a general **"Cross-Ladder Gate" primitive shared with J1 and J9** — a rank/flag on Ladder A becomes a hard prerequisite for Standing ≥1 on Ladder B. Here a discretionary, capture-maintained instance.
- **Failure:** the Board has no independent coercive power — durability depends entirely on packing the secular council with sympathizers. A periodic Gate Renewal check keyed to the sympathetic/hostile fraction of secular Standing-holders; if a hostile majority is ever seated (the same slow-capture run in reverse), the council can legislate the gate away.
- **Naming:** mild — avoid "officers of the Consistory"; use "elders"/"lay wardens."

**J9. Church-membership franchise gate in Massachusetts Bay.** *Morgan, Visible Saints (1963); Hall, The Antinomian Controversy (1968).*
- **Mechanic — "Franchise Prerequisite — hard-coded cross-ladder rule":** the more extreme, non-discretionary sibling of J8 — instead of a capture-maintained flag, a faction's Standing 0→1 Initiation Gate *definition itself* is authored to require a specific Standing threshold on a DIFFERENT faction's ladder as a formal rule (a design-table change, not a runtime Key). *New:* a `franchise_prerequisite_ladder`/`franchise_prerequisite_rank` column on rank-ladder tables. *Hook:* shares the Cross-Ladder Gate primitive with J1/J8 but implemented as a static ladder-definition rule — **flag explicitly as needing a design-doc-level table change, not just a runtime mechanic.**
- **Failure:** challenged from WITHIN the gating religious ladder — a rival theological track (a charismatic-lay challenger network, a second religious-ladder branch) allies with a high-secular-Standing sympathizer; the orthodox gate-holders must win a secular leadership election (J8's council-packing) to expel the challenger (banishment = Dismissal/Standing -1) before the franchise rule comes under threat. Lose that election and the rule is vulnerable to repeal.
- **Naming:** none.

**J10. Donglin Academy vs the eunuch Wei Zhongxian.** *Dardess, Blood and History (2002).*
- **Mechanic — "Lineage-tagged ambition clustering vs Backchannel-plus-covert-apparatus purge":** (a) NPCs sharing a `lineage_id` tag (teacher-student/exam-cohort ties, on the Knots graph) get a coordination bonus — when multiple lineage-tagged NPCs' ambitions target correlated appointments, each advances faster (mutual backing). (b) The rival pattern is J4's Confidant PLUS an existing covert-faction apparatus (Niflhel/RM per the Investigate verb) issuing a MASS purge — many `da.covert_betrayal` + Standing-drops against the entire cluster at once. *New:* `lineage_id` (distinct from relationships — denotes shared-credential coordination, not personal Disposition) + a batch/cascading Demotion wrapper Key firing N child `standing_change` against every `lineage_id` member (directly analogous to the existing `officer_deaths → standing_change` fan-out). *Hook:* new Key `state.faction_purge` (payload faction_id, target_lineage_id, cause), modeled on that fan-out.
- **Failure:** Wei's own power is itself precarious — entirely Confidant-pattern (J4) dependent on personal favor, no formal office. When the favor-granting ruler is succeeded (`state.succession`), the Confidant relationship does NOT transfer — model an automatic re-roll/decay of the Disposition-substitute veto on any succession event (Wei's apparatus collapsed within months of Tianqi's death).
- **Naming:** **explicit flag** — the coercive/investigative apparatus (Eastern-Depot analog) must not be an "officer corps"; use "covert apparatus"/"Niflhel-analog retinue."

## 3. Concrete system design proposal — the roster / advancement layer

This is the point of the exercise. Six decisions, each with a clear recommendation and reasoning.

### 3.1 Relationship to the settlement-scale ambition engine → **one engine, two scale-bindings** (extend upward, but decouple from the settlement-registry gate)

**Recommendation: neither a bolt-on replacement nor a literal reuse of the settlement-bound instance. Adopt the ambition engine's *field grammar* (`goal / method / timeline / progress / trajectory`, autonomous Accounting-advance + threshold card) as a shared substrate, and instantiate a distinct court/faction-scale *profile* of it.**

Reasoning:
- **Reuse the grammar, not the instance.** ~40 of the ~96 entries above are explicitly described as "extends the settlement-scale ambition engine upward" and map 1:1 onto `goal/method/timeline/progress/trajectory` (F5 Sforza is the textbook case: contract → marriage → vacancy → seizure with *only a trajectory shift as new logic*). Building a *separate* system would violate the ED-1083 §2 anti-shape-divergence rule ("never grow a scale-local interface dialect"). The card menu, the autonomous tick, the trajectory-replanning branch — all port directly.
- **But decouple the scale-binding.** The settlement instance is "gated on an unbuilt settlement registry." The court/faction instance is gated on the **Standing ladder and the Knots graph, both of which already exist.** I10 (malikane) makes this precise: the family-scale Entrenchment counter "needs only Accounting-cadence ticking plus the existing Knots graph, so it is NOT gated on the unbuilt settlement registry the way the base per-NPC ambition engine is." **This is the key unlock: the court-scale roster layer can ship without waiting on the settlement registry.**
- **Three scale profiles, one engine.** (i) settlement-local (existing, registry-gated); (ii) court/faction (this proposal, ladder-gated); (iii) lineage/clan (a genuine new scope — C5 Wang Mang, I10 malikane — that outlives any single NPC's `progress` and is the single biggest structural ask; see §5). The engine's Accounting-cascade tick runs over all three; only the *entity it ticks on* and the *gate it checks* differ.

Concretely: **a shared `ConsolidationEngine` with a `scale_binding` field ∈ {settlement, court, lineage}.** The settlement binding stays blocked; the court binding is the deliverable; the lineage binding is Jordan-gated.

### 3.2 Interaction with the player-facing Standing ladder → **shared rank space (one ladder), with off-ladder auxiliary meters that force ratification onto it**

**Recommendation: NPCs climb the SAME Standing ladder the player does — shared rank space, so an NPC and the player can compete for the same seat. The parallel/shadow tracks the research repeatedly demands (moral_authority, renown, shadow_standing, service_rank, Dignity, custody-bonus) are modeled as EXPLICIT off-ladder auxiliary meters that *feed into or force ratification onto* the one shared ladder — never as a duplicate ladder.**

Reasoning:
- **The whole research shows rise is contested against incumbents.** Rival Cohort (§1.0), the vacancy auctions (F1, I1), the succession contests, the coalition purges — all presuppose NPCs and the player compete in one rank space. A parallel NPC-only ladder would make "denounce a rival," "contest a vacancy," and "an NPC out-climbs you for the seat you wanted" incoherent.
- **Avoids parallel-track proliferation, which the corpus has already flagged as a problem.** BYZ-2 (Purchased Dignity) was *cut* precisely to avoid "a third parallel wealth-shortcut track" (I3), and needs_jordan item 3 explicitly asks "how many parallel status/power tracks does the game want." A shared ladder + a *bounded, named* set of auxiliary meters is the disciplined answer.
- **The auxiliary meters are real and unavoidable — but they are meters, not ladders.** Power routinely detaches from rank (Michinaga/H1, Cosimo/H6, Heshen/H7). The clean model: `shadow_standing` (F8), `moral_authority` (J2), `renown` (J6), `service_rank` (F9), `mandate.custody_bonus` (F7), sticky `Dignity` (BYZ-1) are **scalar auxiliary scores that (a) can *substitute* for Standing in specific gated checks, and (b) can *force ratification* onto the shared ladder at a threshold** (Yoritomo's sei-i taishōgun grant, F8). They are not a second seat-space; they are pressure on the one seat-space.

**The tradeoff (named):** shared rank space means **an NPC can, in principle, contest — and win — a seat the player currently holds.** This is the sharpest "every action pays what it buys" stakes question in the whole design, and it needs a Jordan ruling (see §5). The alternative (parallel track) *avoids* this tension but at the cost of making NPC advancement narratively inert — NPCs would rise in a sandbox the player never actually competes in, which defeats the purpose. **Recommend: shared ladder, and treat "can an NPC depose the player at a held rank" as a policy toggle, not an architectural fork** — the architecture supports it either way; only a `player_seats_are_contestable` flag differs.

### 3.3 NPC sheet extension

Reuse aggressively; flag only genuinely-new fields. The existing NPC sheet already carries `ambition{goal, method, timeline, progress}`, `trajectory`, `Standing`, `Disposition`, the Stance Triangle (Conviction/Ethical Framework/Resonant Style), and edges on the relationships/Knots graph (PP-724). The roster layer adds:

**Reused as-is:** `ambition.*`, `trajectory`, `Standing`, `Disposition`, Knots graph, Ledger-of-Consequence tags (Precedent/Grudge/Debt/Reputation), Mandate.

**New relational edges (on the Knots graph, not new subsystems):**
- `patron_id` + a `bound` flag (E1 Conduit; distinguishes derivative from independent ambition) — with edge subtypes: `upward_patron` (A1), `sponsor_bond` (bidirectional, B2), `favor_bond` (`revocable_by=ruler_only`, B4), `kul_bond`/`fictive_bond` (A4/E6), `cohort_bond`/`cohort_id`/`lineage_id` (B5/A9/J10), `successor_designate` (A5), `web_id` (D5), `shadow_deputy` (G5).
- `clients_sponsored` — aggregate weighted list/count (E8 — the one field that cannot be modeled as an ambition variant; the compounding sponsorship base).
- `client.patron_loyalty` **and** `client.crown_loyalty` (two-axis, F6 — the assassination vector).

**New scalar fields (the bounded auxiliary-meter set — deliberately capped per §3.2):**
- `power_base` enum ∈ **{patronage, merit, kinship, bureaucratic, military, purchased, ideological}** — *the organizing field of the whole system.* It is the single most load-bearing addition: it (a) types the climb driver (§3.4), (b) types the downfall shape (§3.5), and (c) gates whether a Dismissal is enforceable (H8 — `favor-dependent` vs `independent`).
- `consolidation_progress` (0-5) + `consolidation_trajectory` — **reuse `ambition.progress`/`trajectory` directly**; no new field, just the court-scale binding.
- `standing_source` ∈ {earned, patron-granted, purchased, sponsored} (C4/B6/I-series — provenance for the legitimacy/appeal logic).
- `independent_power_base` (raw non-Standing power, G6/G8 — gates coalition-purge and scapegoat-transfer).
- `succession_eligible` (bool, C3) / `succession_immune` (bool, G7/BYZ-8) — the "consort-advisor ceiling" and its inverse.
- `invested_status` ∈ {invested, uninvested, contested} (J1).
- `access_monopoly` (tier, G1) / `gatekeeper` (bool, A6) — chokepoint control.
- `moral_authority` (0-5, J2) / `renown` (peninsula-scoped aggregate, J6) / `shadow_standing` (0-7, F8) / `service_rank` (0-5, F9) — the Standing-substitute meters.
- `office_vested` (bool, E2) / `heritable` + `droit_annuel_due` (I3) — venality/tenure.
- `expected_tenure` (A2) — seat time-boxing.
- `concurrent_roles: []` (H7 — for the plural-advisor stack).

**New Ledger-tag capabilities (extend the family, don't add families where avoidable):**
- `trigger_condition: succession` metadata (G4 — dormant tags that activate on a specific Key).
- a `transfer` operation on Reputation tags (G8 — scapegoat reassignment).
- a `hidden`/`concealed` visibility flag (H7 — embezzlement Debt invisible until an `audit_exposed` reveal).
- self-reinforcing "Culpability Debt" chain semantics (G5).
- *Genuinely new families, use sparingly:* lineage-scoped "Kinship Precedent" (C2), "Endogamous Lock" (C7). Everything else scopes existing Debt/Grudge/Precedent/Reputation with a `patron_id`/`lineage_id`.

**New Key types (all siblings of `standing_change`/`officer_deaths` in key_type_registry_v30.md):** the research proposes many; the *minimum viable set* for the court binding is: `npc_standing_climb_attempt` (B1, records sponsor + waiver), `patron_succession_transfer`/`state.patron_succession` (A5/F4), `state.succession_vacancy` (F1 — the **shared cross-entry vacancy primitive**, reused by F1/F5/I1), `access.correspondence_filtered` (H2 — the in-transit-interception primitive, reused by all chokepoint entries), and `coalition_purge` (G6 — the joint-ambition primitive). The rest layer on later.

### 3.4 The core autonomous loop

Mirroring the ambition engine's autonomous-advance-plus-threshold-card pattern, at each Accounting:

1. **Advance `consolidation_progress` by a `power_base`-typed driver** (this is the elegance of the `power_base` field — one loop, seven drivers):
   - `patronage` → `+f(clientele_breadth, upward_patron Disposition)` (A1); frozen if the upward patron stalls.
   - `merit` → `+1` if the two-stage credential gate + tutoring-investment is cleared (B2); capped otherwise.
   - `kinship` → advances on marriage/betrothal/heir-birth Keys (A8/H1); pays a recurring rank-independent bonus once the tie holds.
   - `bureaucratic` → `+f(Keys successfully filtered/routed)` (E1/H2); resets to 0 if the patron falls.
   - `military` → `+f(retinue.leverage/autonomy, self-financing Keys)` (F1/F6); spent at a vacancy.
   - `purchased` → instant on Treasury spend (I-series), but writes `purchased_legitimacy`/Exposure.
   - `ideological` → `+f(aggregate scene.witness/gossip)` feeding `moral_authority`/`renown` (J2/J6); no ladder entry needed.
2. **At threshold, fire an "Ascendancy card"** (the court-scale rename of the Ambition card) with a `power_base`-appropriate menu, extending the existing three (claim office / betray patron / expose secret) with: **install successor** (A2), **capture the gate** (A3), **cohort capture** (A9), **convert to asset** (E7), **press a banked claim** (C1), **force ratification** (F8), **purge predecessor** (G5), **seize Mandate custody** (F7).
3. **Emit through the Key substrate** (key_substrate_v30.md — every state-change is a Key), so the roster layer is fully legible to faction_layer/npc_behavior/articulation.

**Player-facing verbs/decisions this creates** (the reason the system is *fun*, not just simulationist):
- **Sponsor a client** (spend Mandate/Access to advance an NPC's climb — buys `clients_sponsored`, incurs the top-down-collapse liability).
- **Denounce a rival** (weaponize the Demotion system, H4 — false-accusation risk).
- **Marry into a house** (C-series — banks a contingent claim, C1).
- **Buy an office / buy heritability** (I-series — instant rank, no loyalty reserve).
- **Co-sign or withhold investiture** (J1 — the cross-ladder veto).
- **Expose a chokepoint-holder** (Investigate verb, H7/I2 — the bypass/audit).
- **Reassess an Upstart grant** (D10 — the no-drama succession check).
- **Convene a coalition** (G6 — the only way to touch an over-threshold patron, E8).

### 3.5 The downfall / limiting mechanic (Ω-d non-dominance: "every action pays what it buys")

**Recommendation: the `power_base` field IS the limiting mechanic.** Consolidation is self-limiting because *the same field that types the rise types the exploitable vulnerability* — this is the historical record's gift, and it is exactly Valoria's principle. Concretely, each `power_base` writes a legible, rival-exploitable liability:

| `power_base` | Rise driver | The bill it pays (legible vulnerability) |
|---|---|---|
| **patronage** | breadth of clients | **top-down collapse** — patron falls → `clientele_breadth` decays / cohort auto-purges (A1, A9, CHN-8) |
| **merit** | credential gate | **criteria rewrite** — a faction flip re-tests you out (B3); wealth-cap if un-tutored |
| **kinship** | blood-tie | **generational flip / demographic lapse** — one Disposition-flip or missed marriage voids decades (A8, C2, C5) |
| **bureaucratic** | routing chokepoint | **single bypass channel** or succession — proxy authority has zero transferability (G1, G4, H7) |
| **military** | personal force | **coalition purge** or **subordinate-flip** — the ruler routes around your strength (F3, F6, G6) |
| **purchased** | Treasury | **no loyalty reserve** — `purchased_legitimacy` suppresses the Coup Counter buffer (I1); prestige-devaluation Grudge (I8) |
| **ideological** | moral authority | **counter-authority in the same currency** — only a rival ruling on the same Renown track, or covert violence (J2, J6, J4) |

The unifying engine primitive underneath all seven: **every consolidation move writes a Ledger tag (Grudge/Debt/Precedent) that (a) is legible to rivals and (b) makes a rival coalition progressively *affordable*.** Below a threshold, ordinary Rival Cohort/no-confidence handles the NPC; above it, only a higher-cost `coalition_purge` (G6/E8) can — which is precisely why real over-mighty subjects survived until a crisis made the coalition worth its cost. This gives the exact shape Valoria wants: **power is never dominant; it is *expensive to unwind*, and the cost is paid by whoever waited too long.**

Two implementation notes: (1) the `independent_power_base` field is the master switch on *whether* a downfall is enforceable at all (H8 — a favor-dependent NPC's Dismissal always sticks; an independent one's does not); (2) the vulnerability must be *readable by the player* (via Investigate) — an illegible vulnerability isn't a mechanic, it's a coin-flip.

### 3.6 Naming — resolve the collision explicitly

**Recommendation: name the system the "Ascendancy" layer; name the tracked NPC role a "Retainer" (bound client) / "Patron" (sponsor) / "Advisor" (chokepoint/court role); and reserve "officer" exclusively for the mass-battle auto-generated unit commander, with an inline naming-collision callout carried in the design doc itself (not just this audit).**

Justification:
- **"Officer" is unavailable** — it is owned by mass_battle_v30.md/faction_layer_v30.md's auto-generated unit-commander, and the `officer_deaths` Key type already means that. The collision is real and repeated: **~20 of the ~96 entries flag it** (every military-retinue entry F1-F10, the household/consort/prefect entries B4/C3/D2, the bureaucratic entries E3/E8/H7, and — loudest — I7 British commission-purchase and F10 Yuan Shikai, where the historical subject matter *is* an officer corps). A single NPC can legitimately hold *both* a court rank and (separately) a mass-battle officer role; the fields must never collide.
- **"Ascendancy" for the system** because it names the *arc* (the consolidation-progress meter), spans all eight mechanisms without privileging one (unlike "Court," which excludes settlement brokers/military retinues/ideological figures), and has zero corpus collision.
- **"Retainer / Patron / Advisor" for the roles** because these are already the corpus's dominant vocabulary — the research overwhelmingly reaches for "patron," "client," "retinue," "household," "advisor," "conduit," "favorite/valido," none of which touch "officer." "Retainer" is the single clean replacement for the tempting "officer" (it reads as a bound-to-a-house individual, exactly the M7 shape, without any military-command connotation). For the specific sub-roles the research names distinctly, keep them: **Conduit** (E, chokepoint-without-office), **Favorite/Valido** (D, per HAB-2), **Gatekeeper** (CHN-7, shared primitive), **Sanctioning Prelate** (J5), **Anointed Successor** (A2).
- **Carry the callout inline at F10's mechanic definition** (the research's own recommendation) — the highest-collision surface deserves a callout at the point of definition, not buried in an audit.

## 5. Gaps, thin spots, and what needs a Jordan ruling (not more research)

**The one question that is a design-taste ruling, not a research question:**
- **Can an NPC actually DEPOSE or SUPERSEDE the player at a rank the player holds?** §3.2 recommends shared rank space, which *architecturally permits* this. It is the sharpest "every action pays what it buys" stakes question in the design: if the answer is no, NPC advancement is partly a sandbox the player watches; if yes, the player's own consolidation writes the same legible vulnerability every NPC's does (the purest expression of Ω-d non-dominance). **The architecture supports both via a single `player_seats_are_contestable` toggle — this needs Jordan's taste, not more historical cases.** My inclination: yes, but gated behind the *coalition* threshold (E8/G6), so the player is only deposable when they have over-consolidated past the point where ordinary challenges bind — making the fall *earned and legible*, never a coin-flip.

**Genuine architectural gaps (new mechanics, not reskins) the research surfaced — flag before building:**
1. **Lineage/clan-scope objects above the NPC lifespan.** C5 (Wang Mang, `dynastic_leverage` across five reigns) and I10 (family `Entrenchment`) need an entity the Accounting-cascade tick operates on *above* a single NPC — "the single biggest structural ask of this batch." Recommend the `scale_binding=lineage` profile (§3.1), which I10 shows is *not* registry-gated. But whether Valoria wants clan-scale actors at all is a scope call.
2. **The joint / Coalition ambition structure.** G6/G9 need a multi-NPC joint ambition object — "ambitions today are strictly per-NPC; this is the clearest new-mechanic requirement." E8's `purge_cascade` supermajority coalition is the same primitive. This is genuinely new and load-bearing for the entire downfall side.
3. **Peninsula-scale aggregation** (`renown`, J6) — the exact gap named in the system context. `state.doctrinal_ruling` needs to sum `scene.witness`/`scene.gossip` across settlements an NPC never administers.
4. **Mandate separable-from-possession** (F7, `custodian_id` ≠ `holder_id`) — "the sharpest genuine architectural gap of the ten."
5. **In-transit Key interception** (H2, `access.correspondence_filtered`) — the engine has no mechanism for one NPC intercepting *other* NPCs' Keys before they resolve; every chokepoint entry needs it.
6. **Dormant/conditional tags** (G4, `trigger_condition: succession`) and **hidden-visibility tags** (H7) — genuinely new tag-family capabilities.
7. **A co_leadership / partial-Leadership state** (C8) and **mutable office `succession_rule`** (F2) — Part 2's succession model currently treats Leadership as binary and offices as static slots.

**Thin spots / f**Thin spots / fact-check caveats (things a reader should not over-trust):**

Build it, but know it is the least empirically anchored failure dynamic in the corpus.

# Section 2.10 — Trade-route monopoly/franchise dynamics and merchant-capital power shifts (Economic)

*Key: `trade_route_monopoly_merchant_capital_events`. The through-line: **a Concession/Compact/Charter is a tag that drifts out of sync with the real balance of Standing**, and the verbs that reconcile it (Charter Review, Sunset Review, Underwrite, Capital Partnership, Monopsony Enforcement) either convert political leverage into extraction rights or convert accumulated Debt/Standing into a rank-ladder shift — often via Conquest.*

---

### HEV-TRADE-01 — Hanseatic Kontor system / revocation of the London Steelyard (London/Hanseatic League, 1598) · Petition
- **Historical grounding:** The revocation of the Hanseatic League's London Steelyard privileges in 1598, ending centuries of the Kontor foreign-franchise trading-post system. Citations: Lilienfeld, Eras Journal (Monash); Britannica; MoEML.
- **Trigger:** `a foreign-franchise Concession (customs/jurisdiction exemption) age≥N AND a rival domestic Guild's Standing ≥ threshold AND no reciprocal Concession abroad AND the exempted Levy yield reduces net Treasury by ≥X%`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (revoke): domestic Guild Standing+, foreign Disposition−, writes `Charter Revoked Under Petition` Precedent.
  - Bargain (renegotiate reciprocity): AP/Clerk cost, Concession renewed with a reciprocity clause.
  - Defy (protect the old franchise): domestic Guild Disposition−, resentment accrues.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Defy escalates to Friction/Crisis (guild smuggling/boycott); Comply seeds a retaliation Intrigue abroad (against Valorian merchants in foreign ports).
- **Introduces (Action):** Charter Review (Quo Warranto sub-option on Levy): audit a long-standing foreign Concession's reciprocity before a rival Guild's Petition forces it.
- **Loop:** An unreviewed unreciprocated Concession drains Levy yield while the rival Guild's Standing climbs until the Petition auto-fires; Charter Review pre-empts the escalation on the Crown's terms, while neglect either way writes a Precedent that changes how every future foreign Concession is negotiated.

### HEV-TRADE-02 — Fugger mining monopolies / Habsburg election financing (Holy Roman Empire, 1519) · Opportunity
- **Historical grounding:** The Fugger banking house's financing of Charles V's 1519 imperial election in exchange for mining/extraction concessions. Citations: Wikipedia Fugger/Jakob Fugger; Atlas Obscura; fuggerstrasse.eu.
- **Trigger:** `Treasury short of a Directive-mandated quota (election/war levy) AND a Banking/Guild faction Standing ≥ threshold with capital AND no existing Debt tag links it to the Crown`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (accept the loan): immediate infusion, but a Debt tag AND a Concession tag — mining/extraction rights transferred — plus the faction's Standing rises.
  - Bargain (partial loan/partial concession).
  - Defy (decline): opportunity forgone, Directive risks unmet → Grudge with the PA.
- **Follow-on (FO):** An unresolved Debt seeds recurring Levy "interest" Friction; repeated Comply seeds an Ambition where the Banking faction leverages stacked Concessions to claim a Ministry/Consulta seat.
- **Introduces (Action):** Underwrite (Sponsor sub-option: a capital-holding faction finances a Directive shortfall for a Concession on a specific extractive resource).
- **Loop:** Underwrite converts a one-time shortfall into a durable Debt+Concession pair raising the lender's Standing and Ministry eligibility; each subsequent unmet Directive raises the odds the Opportunity re-fires with the same faction, compounding its capture of extraction rights.

### HEV-TRADE-03 — Mali Empire's trans-Saharan gold-salt monopoly (Mali Empire, medieval) · Thread
- **Historical grounding:** The Mali Empire's sustained concealment of the exact source/route of its trans-Saharan gold-salt trade, protecting toll revenue. Citations: Oxford Global Capitalism Project (Mansa Musa); gold-trade histories on production secrecy.
- **Trigger:** `a settlement on the sole node of a trade route (chokepoint) AND Levy transit-toll income exceeds a threshold share of Treasury AND no "Route Known" tag exists for a rival`
- **Response branches:**
  - Sustained concealment keeps toll Prosperity elevated (the baseline held state).
  - Bargain (selling route knowledge): yields a one-time gain but risks writing "Route Known".
  - Defy (a rival investigates): risks the Thread resolving to Crisis if the secret breaks.
- **Follow-on (FO):** If broken, a "Route Bypassed" Crisis (permanent Levy-yield loss); if held across its arc, an Ambition where the Guild petitions for elevated rank on sustained wealth.
- **Introduces (Action):** Conceal Source (Keep Order information-control sub-option: spend AP/Clerk Capacity each season to suppress the "Route Known" tag, keeping the Thread dormant).
- **Loop:** Spending Keep Order on Conceal Source preserves toll Prosperity; skipping lets Π creep toward Crisis discovery, and once discovered the toll's yield permanently drops — which also retires Conceal Source, since there's no secret left to protect.

### HEV-TRADE-04 — VOC conquest of the Banda Islands / nutmeg monopoly (Banda Islands, 1621) · Petition
- **Historical grounding:** The Dutch East India Company's 1621 conquest of the Banda Islands to enforce its nutmeg monopoly after repeated local repudiation of exclusivity terms. Citations: Wikipedia Dutch conquest of Banda; Duke FinReg Blog.
- **Trigger:** `a chartered Company/Guild holds a Compact with a single-resource settlement AND its compliance with exclusivity falls below threshold (repeated Defy on Levy terms) AND the Company's Muster ≥ Conquest threshold`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (settlement honors exclusivity): modest Levy flows.
  - Bargain (renegotiate): escalation delayed.
  - Defy (settlement repudiates): escalates to Crisis, unlocking Conquest eligibility.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Repeated Defy seeds "Compact Repudiated" Crisis → a Conquest installing a Company-run Governor Assignment (Charter overwrite) over the native elite.
- **Introduces (Action):** Monopsony Enforcement (Conquest gated by a repudiated-Compact Crisis tag — a legitimacy precondition rather than bare aggression; success installs a Company Governor).
- **Loop:** Repeatedly-Defied Compact terms accumulate toward a Crisis-gated Conquest flag, turning failed negotiation into legitimate casus belli; a successful Conquest strips the native elite's rank entirely and hands every future Levy/Develop decision to the Company.

### HEV-TRADE-05 — Muscovite conquest of Novgorod / end of the merchant-boyar republic (Novgorod, 1478) · Intrigue
- **Historical grounding:** Ivan III's 1478 conquest of Novgorod, ending the merchant-boyar republic (veche-bell removal, boyar confiscations, land-concentration). Citations: EBSCO; Wikipedia Novgorod Republic.
- **Trigger:** `a Guild-type faction's Recognition/Discipline below threshold AND the settlement's Disposition toward a rival external Crown exceeds threshold (being courted) AND its own patron faction's Standing independently declining`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (accept the rival's overtures): home-PA Disposition−, Grudge.
  - Bargain (hedge both ways): temporary, no resolution.
  - Defy (reject): requires Keep Order/Levy investment to shore up Discipline.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Unresolved courting + declining patron seeds "Annexation Bid" Crisis → a Conquest dissolving the Guild ladder (Dismissal + Rival Cohort tags on its top rank, reassignment via Governor Assignment).
- **Introduces (Action):** Assembly Discipline (Keep Order feeding the ladder's Recognition/Rival Cohort dimensions: raise internal standing to counter the erosion that makes it a target).
- **Loop:** Letting Discipline/Recognition erode while the patron falls raises Π and Intrigue odds; Assembly Discipline lowers them directly, while an Intrigue resolving into Conquest permanently deletes the ladder and forecloses the Assembly Discipline option at that settlement forever.

### HEV-TRADE-06 — Mongol ortoq merchant-partnership under the Pax Mongolica (Mongol Empire, 13th–14th c.) · Opportunity
- **Historical grounding:** The ortoq system of state-capitalized merchant partnerships operating across the Pax Mongolica's trade network. Citations: Wikipedia Ortogh/Paiza; Central Asian Survey ortoq.
- **Trigger:** `PA Treasury surplus ≥ threshold AND a Guild faction lacks a Concession AND Π low`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (fund the partnership): the faction gains a portable cross-settlement Concession — tax exemption + relay/travel privilege — and the Crown a profit-share.
  - Bargain (partial capitalization, capped exposure).
  - Defy (decline): the faction may seek capital from a rival Crown.
- **Follow-on (FO):** A funded partnership seeds recurring "Over-leveraged Caravan" Friction (debt-relief requests); repeated unresolved seeds a debt-repudiation Crisis damaging Crown-Guild Disposition.
- **Introduces (Action):** Capital Partnership (Sponsor sub-option issuing a *cross-settlement* Concession — usable at any settlement — rather than a settlement-bound Compact; flagged for the proposed Concession Ledger family).
- **Loop:** Sponsoring raises a Guild's mobility/profit-share via Treasury-funded Concessions, but each accrues Debt whose unresolved Friction pushes Π up; a Crown that never audits those Debt tags drifts toward a repudiation Crisis erasing the Concession's goodwill in one Disposition hit.

### HEV-TRADE-07 — Battle of Plassey / the Jagat Seth bankers / the Diwani of Bengal (Bengal, 1757) · Intrigue
- **Historical grounding:** The 1757 Battle of Plassey, in which the Jagat Seth banking house's financing of a coup against Siraj ud-Daulah led to the East India Company acquiring the Diwani of Bengal. Citations: National Army Museum; Britannica; Wikipedia Plassey/Jagat Seth.
- **Trigger:** `a Banking/Guild faction holds high Standing via Debt tags on the ruling Crown AND a rival external faction has Disposition contact with a disaffected high-rank Crown officer (Rival Cohort) AND Treasury dependency on the Banking faction ≥ threshold`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (officer stays loyal): Intrigue fizzles but Debt dependency persists.
  - Bargain (officer extracts concessions from both): Grudge all around, delayed.
  - Defy (officer defects, backing the external faction in a funded coup): triggers an Ambition enabling a Govern/Conquest against the province.
- **Follow-on (FO):** A successful coup Ambition resolves into Govern granting the external faction a diwani-equivalent Levy assignment, then a second-order follow_on lets that faction *demote the Banking faction that financed the coup* (Demotion Magnitude drop) once its leverage is spent.
- **Introduces (Action):** Court Officer Disposition (Keep Order/Treat lever shoring up a high-rank officer's loyalty, denying a rival the Rival Cohort opening).
- **Loop:** Heavy Treasury reliance on a Banking faction's Debt creates the Standing imbalance that makes an officer's defection attractive; skipping loyalty spend raises the Intrigue→Ambition escalation odds, and a successful external Govern both grants that faction new Levy rights and then demotes the Banking faction whose leverage enabled the chain.

### HEV-TRADE-08 — Kilwa Sultanate's Sofala gold monopoly / Portuguese Sack of Kilwa (Kilwa/East Africa, 1505) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** The Portuguese Sack of Kilwa in 1505, breaking the Kilwa Sultanate's tributary control over the Sofala gold trade. Citations: Wikipedia Sack of Kilwa / Portuguese Sofala expedition; africanhistoryextra.com.
- **Trigger:** `a tributary settlement holds a Directive tribute obligation to an external conquering power AND it Defies (non-payment) ≥1 season AND the external power's Muster ≥ Conquest threshold`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (pay): vassal status holds, Levy flows out.
  - Bargain (renegotiate rate): partial + Grudge.
  - Defy (refuse): escalates to "Tribute Withheld" Crisis opening a Conquest/Sack — trade-hub facilities destroyed, Prosperity collapsed.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Post-Sack an Opportunity seeds for a neighboring trade-hub (displaced merchants redirect trade there), raising its Prosperity/Levy potential.
- **Introduces (Action):** Harbor Defenses (Fortify mitigation paired with the tributary Comply/Bargain/Defy: reduce Conquest severity/odds specifically if the settlement later Defies).
- **Loop:** Defy on a tribute Directive raises Conquest odds, and an under-Fortified settlement suffers a worse Sack (facility destruction); the Sack removes the settlement's trade-hub status from future Levy calculations while creating a fresh Opportunity elsewhere — redistributing which settlement's chokepoint verb-set is even usable next.

### HEV-TRADE-09 — Aztec pochteca merchant guild's chartered long-distance monopoly (Aztec Empire, pre-Columbian) · Intrigue
- **Historical grounding:** The pochteca, the Aztec Empire's chartered long-distance merchant guild that also served intelligence/diplomatic functions ahead of military campaigns. Citations: Wikipedia/Britannica Pochteca; Frontiers in Human Dynamics (2025).
- **Trigger:** `a Muster/Conquest is queued (pre-conquest planning) AND a Guild holds a long-distance-trade Concession/Compact AND its Standing ≥ threshold for a self-jurisdiction ("own courts") dimension`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (Guild provides intelligence/diplomatic cover): reduced-risk/better-terms Conquest, Guild Standing/Recognition rises.
  - Bargain (Guild demands expanded self-jurisdiction): trades Crown oversight for intelligence value.
  - Defy (Crown uses the Guild informally): weaker intelligence, no Standing gain.
- **Follow-on (FO):** A formalized Compact seeds recurring Intrigue tied to the Guild's espionage role before each future Muster/Conquest; if the Crown later reneges on self-jurisdiction, a Friction/Petition from the Guild.
- **Introduces (Action):** Charter Intelligence Compact (Sponsor cross-linking into the Muster/Conquest pipeline: licenses a Guild for both a trade monopoly and pre-Conquest reconnaissance, tying a Sponsor payoff to future Conquest-planning quality).
- **Loop:** Sponsoring raises the Guild's Standing/self-jurisdiction and lowers the Crown's Conquest-planning risk via better Intrigue resolution; but each renewal expands the Guild's own-courts autonomy, an accumulating Concession constraining future Keep Order actions inside Guild-controlled quarters.

### HEV-TRADE-10 — Ottoman capitulations / Bursa silk trade reversal (Ottoman Empire, Bursa) · Opportunity
- **Historical grounding:** The Ottoman capitulations system granting extraterritorial trade privileges to foreign powers, and its later reversal as domestic Guild capital in the Bursa silk trade outgrew the foreign privilege-holders. Citations: depts.washington.edu Silk Road (İnalcık); Wikipedia Capitulations.
- **Trigger:**
  - *Initial grant:* `PA seeks external Disposition with a foreign polity AND current leverage favors granting extraterritorial privilege at apparent low domestic cost`
  - *Later reversal:* `a domestic Guild's Standing/capital in the licensed good ≥ threshold AND the foreign Guild's local Standing has fallen below threshold despite an unrevoked Concession still on the books`
- **Response branches:**
  - *Initial:* Comply (grant): external Disposition rises, a durable no-expiry Concession. Bargain (grant with sunset/reciprocity clause). Defy (refuse): preserves domestic dominance, forgoes the diplomacy.
  - *Reversal:* Comply (sunset the stale Concession via Levy renegotiation). Bargain (update to the domestic Guild's now-dominant Standing). Defy (leave it nominally intact and inert).
- **Follow-on (FO):** The initial grant's Concession is itself the hook, carried silently across seasons until domestic Guild Standing crosses threshold → a low-severity Petition ("Formalize what's already true"), no rupture required.
- **Introduces (Action):** Sunset Review (low-AP periodic Levy/Directive sub-action checking whether a long-standing Concession's terms still match the real balance of Standing, forcing reconciliation instead of stale drift).
- **Loop:** Granting an uncapped Concession raises external Disposition immediately, but absent Sunset Review the tag persists inertly while domestic Guild Standing quietly overtakes it; only the Sunset Review action (or an eventual forcing Petition) updates the Ledger to match the real balance — this category is defined by the long-run *absence* of the action, not its presence.

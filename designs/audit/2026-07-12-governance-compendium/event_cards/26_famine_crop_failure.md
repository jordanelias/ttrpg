# 2.6 — Famine and multi-year crop failure (Climatic/Geographical)

*Key / through-line: `historical-concerns-catalogue:famine-multiyear-crop-failure`. The through-line: a standing storage/entitlement Facility (Granary Prefecture, Ever-Normal Granary, Grain Ministry) determines whether a harvest-collapse roll is survivable, and an ideological/fixed Precedent (rigid price ceiling, fixed quota, free-trade doctrine) set earlier constrains which relief options the Crisis even offers.*

---

### HEV-FAM-01 — Great Famine of 1315–1317 (Northern Europe, 14th c.) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** W.C. Jordan, *The Great Famine* (Princeton 1996); Wikipedia.
- **Trigger:** `Harvest_Yield<0.5 for 2 seasons AND Treasury_Grain_Reserve below a Facility-gated threshold` → "Grain Riot"
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (rigid price ceiling): Order gain but plants Precedent raising next-Crisis severity as merchants withhold.
  - Bargain (merchant compact/export exemptions): Compact tag, avoids escalation.
  - Defy: immediate Order loss, no durable tag.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Comply-with-ceiling seeds an escalated Grain Riot next season (traders defect); Bargain seeds a "Merchant Compact Renewal" Opportunity.
- **Introduces (Action):** Price Ceiling / Export Embargo (method-variant on Levy/Treat).
- **Loop:** The Price-Ceiling method suppresses immediate Order loss but attaches a Precedent increasing escalation weight if the shortage persists; prior Develop in grain-reserve FacilityTier lowers the odds the threshold is crossed at all and, if it fires anyway, unlocks a cheaper Bargain instead of forcing Comply/Defy.

### HEV-FAM-02 — Rome's Cura Annonae / Egyptian grain (Rome, Imperial era) · Petition
- **Historical grounding:** Tacitus, *Annals* III–IV; Wikipedia cura annonae; Ancient History Sites.
- **Trigger:** `capital Population above threshold AND Grain_Import_Route disrupted` → "Bread Dole Demand", escalating to "Urban Riot"
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (fund dole/Prefect): Treasury cost, Reputation gain, plants an `Entitlement` Precedent making future refusal costly.
  - Bargain (capped dole): smaller Precedent.
  - Defy: Order collapse → Crisis.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Comply seeds a recurring "Dole Renewal" Petition; Defy seeds Urban Riot then a rival-exploits-unrest Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Granary Prefecture (capital-only Facility unlocking a Levy "Subsidized Distribution" method-variant).
- **Loop:** The Facility raises FacilityTier lowering the AP cost/failure of Subsidized Distribution when the Petition fires; but Complying plants an Entitlement Precedent making any future Defy of a grain obligation cost extra Disposition — a granted entitlement is politically hard to revoke.

### HEV-FAM-03 — North China Famine 1876–1879 / Ever-Normal Granary collapse (Qing China, 1876–1879) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Will & Wong, *Nourish the People* (Michigan 1991); disasterhistory.org.
- **Trigger:** `Drought active >2 seasons across multiple settlements AND Granary FacilityTier below threshold (decayed by underinvestment)`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (state grain transport): mitigates deaths, risks Debt.
  - Bargain (delegate to local elites' makeshift granaries): partial relief, cedes power via Concession.
  - Defy: catastrophic regional collapse, Grudge.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Chronic underinvestment scales severity upward at draw; the delegate path seeds "Local Elites Exploit Vacuum" Intrigue chaining to faction-ladder rank gains.
- **Introduces (Action):** Ever-Normal Granary (Develop Facility upgrade persisting across seasons, decays if Treasury is diverted).
- **Loop:** Sustained Granary investment caps severity and keeps state-run Levy relief available; diverting Treasury to Muster/Conquest lets the Facility decay (the post-rebellion neglect), so the next drought resolves against a worse baseline, pushing relief into the Bargain/delegate path and shifting rank toward whichever elite fills the vacuum.

### HEV-FAM-04 — Deccan Famine 1630–1632 (Deccan, India, 1630–1632) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Wikipedia Deccan famine (Persian chronicles + Dutch/English factory records).
- **Trigger:** `Harvest_Yield collapse ≥2 seasons AND an active Muster/Conquest drawing on the same Logistics/Grain-Carrier capacity` → "Three Failed Monsoons"
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (full relief: cash+langar+remission): heavy Debt.
  - Bargain: partial.
  - Defy/divert (grain caravans feed the army): campaign at full strength but Crisis severity spikes, Grudge.
- **Follow-on (FO):** The divert path seeds a next-season "Famine Deepens" escalation + a rival-exploits-Grudge Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Divert Logistics to Muster (first-class Directive option, not an invisible resource shuffle).
- **Loop:** Committing Treasury/Logistics to Muster while a famine is active competes with Treat's relief pool — the campaign raises readiness now but multiplies next-season severity/Grudge, later suppressing that settlement's Levy yield and Disposition, making the short-term military gain costly in long-run governance capacity.

### HEV-FAM-05 — Great Bengal Famine of 1770 (Bengal, 1770) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Wikipedia; Datta, *Society, Economy and the Market* (2000); Dyson demography.
- **Trigger:** `Harvest_Yield collapse AND a standing Precedent("Fixed Revenue Quota") active from a prior Directive Comply, with no remission`
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (hold/raise quota): short-term Treasury but catastrophic Order/Disposition collapse + major long-lasting Grudge.
  - Bargain (partial remission): caps severity.
  - Defy (override Authority, full remission): Disposition gain with the populace, Reputation loss with the PA.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Comply-through-famine seeds a long-tail Grudge depressing future Levy + raising Friction draw-rate; Defy seeds "Authority Questions Governor's Loyalty" Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Remission sub-option on revenue-extraction Directives (Comply-rigid / Bargain-remit-and-report / Defy-withhold).
- **Loop:** A Directive resolved Comply(rigid-quota) in a normal season plants a Precedent silently checked when a later famine draws — if active, cheap relief is unavailable until the governor spends Ledger effort to overturn it, and refusing relief compounds Grudge further depressing Levy yield — a self-reinforcing trap mirroring revenue held rigid through crisis.

### HEV-FAM-06 — The Flour War (Guerre des Farines, 1775) (France, 1775) · Opportunity
- **Historical grounding:** Wikipedia; Bouton, *The Flour War* (Penn State 1993).
- **Trigger:** `Π low-mid AND governor selects a "liberalize grain trade" Develop/Treat method` → "Deregulate the Grain Trade"; converts to Crisis "Taxation Populaire" if chosen the same season Harvest_Yield falls below threshold
- **Response branches:**
  - Good-harvest adoption: Prosperity/Treasury gain, market-friendly Reputation.
  - Poor-harvest adoption: riots, forced market seizures, Grudge.
  - Bargain (phase gradually): avoids Crisis.
- **Follow-on (FO):** A badly-timed deregulation Crisis seeds a "Pacte de Famine Conspiracy" Reputation-attack Intrigue independent of actual cause.
- **Introduces (Action):** Liberalize Grain Trade (method-variant on Treat/Levy, the opposite pole from a fixed-price/narh method).
- **Loop:** Deregulation removes the price-ceiling escalation risk but exposes Prosperity/Order to harvest RNG: a poor-harvest roll after adoption converts the Opportunity into a Crisis that spawns a Reputation-damaging Intrigue regardless of whether the policy caused the shortage.

### HEV-FAM-07 — Great Irish Famine 1845–1852 (Ireland, 1845–1852) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Wikipedia; Ó Gráda, *Ireland's Great Famine* (UCD 2006).
- **Trigger:** `staple Harvest_Yield collapse ≥2 seasons AND a standing Precedent("Doctrine: Free Trade") keeping exports on by default` → "Blight Multi-Season"
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (maintain doctrine, exports continue, minimal relief): catastrophic Population/Prosperity loss + massive Grudge.
  - Bargain (suspend exports temporarily): Reputation cost with the Authority/merchants, limits severity.
  - Defy (unilaterally halt exports): Directive-Defy cost but drastically reduces severity.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Comply seeds a long "Diaspora" Thread + lingering Grudge suppressing Levy; Defy seeds "Authority Sanctions Governor" Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Relief Obligation (workhouse/Poor-Law mechanic on Keep Order, available regardless of a free-trade Precedent — decoupling relief from export policy).
- **Loop:** A standing ideological Precedent set earlier via Directive constrains which Comply/Bargain options the Crisis offers (doctrine locks in a bad response); Keep Order's Relief Obligation is a workaround costing local AP/Ledger that partly decouples survival from the doctrine, at the price of Reputation friction with the faction championing it.

### HEV-FAM-08 — Ottoman Istanbul provisioning / narh price control (Ottoman Istanbul) · Petition
- **Historical grounding:** İnalcık on narh; istanbultarihi.ist Zahire Nezareti.
- **Trigger:** `capital AND Grain_Import disrupted AND Grain Ministry FacilityTier below threshold` → "Bread Price Petition", escalating to "Hoarding Panic"
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply (enforce fixed price via judiciary — effectiveness scales with FacilityTier): high tier cheap Order/Reputation, low tier wastes AP and lets hoarder-Grudge accrue.
  - Bargain (negotiate supply with Balkan/Black-Sea/Egyptian partners): Compact + supply-route bonus.
  - Defy: let price float.
- **Follow-on (FO):** Repeated Comply successes seed a Develop "Establish Ministry of Grains" unlock; repeated failures seed Hoarding Panic then "Judiciary Corruption Exposed" Intrigue.
- **Introduces (Action):** Grain Ministry (capital-only enforcement Facility raising FacilityTier gating price-control method success).
- **Loop:** FacilityTier multiplies the fixed-price method's success when the recurring Petition draws; repeated Petition failures at low tier both escalate to Crisis and block the Develop path that would build the very Facility needed — a bootstrapping trap mirroring narh's variable enforcement.

### HEV-FAM-09 — Finnish Famine 1866–1868 (Finland, 1866–1868) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Wikipedia; Voutilainen et al., *Econ History Review* (2022).
- **Trigger:** `Harvest_Yield collapse ≥2 seasons AND Treasury below reserve AND a standing "New Currency — Fragile" tag` → a Directive Comply(delay borrowing)/Bargain(partial early)/Defy(borrow immediately)
- **Response branches:**
  - Comply-delay: currency Reputation preserved short-term but Crisis severity compounds each delayed season as the eventual loan's Debt grows.
  - Bargain (moderate).
  - Defy: currency hit now, severity capped.
- **Follow-on (FO):** The delay path seeds an escalating "Grain Prices Rise Continent-Wide" Friction raising the Foreign-Loan Debt cost each deferred season, forcing a worse loan under duress.
- **Introduces (Action):** Foreign Loan (Treat method-variant, Debt cost scaling with seasons of Comply-delay).
- **Loop:** Complying to protect a fragile-currency tag delays the loan, and each delay is tracked as an escalating multiplier on that loan's eventual Debt when finally invoked — early Defy is cheaper in Debt but costlier in immediate Reputation, a precise bidirectional trade.

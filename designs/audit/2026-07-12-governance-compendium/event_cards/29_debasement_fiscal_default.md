# 2.9 Currency debasement, fiscal collapse, sovereign default, tax-base crises (Economic)

*Key: `currency_debasement_fiscal_collapse_distillation_v1`. The through-line: the proposed **Capital-Posture Ledger family** (Debased / Speculative) as a one-way ratchet — Levy:Debase / Charter Fiat / Farm buy Treasury by stacking a tag that raises all future Levy Ob and fiscal-Crisis weight, and the only exits (Recoinage / Currency Reset / Funding & Assumption) grow more expensive the longer deferred.*

### HEV-COIN-01 — Roman Third-Century Crisis debasement / Diocletian's edicts (Roman Empire, 3rd century CE) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** TheCollector; Wikipedia Edict on Maximum Prices.
- **Trigger:** `Capital-Posture:Debased present AND Prosperity down 2+ seasons AND Π≥8`
- **Response branches:** Comply (Tribunal price/wage cap: Order+1 short-term, `Harsh` Reputation, seeds a black-market Intrigue rather than fixing anything); Bargain (Guild-scoped cap, Concession); Defy (prices run, Order−1, debasement unaddressed).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Comply→ a "Black Market" Intrigue (Clerk-Corruption evasion), Bargain→ a "Guild cites the Concession" Petition; either way Capital-Posture:Debased persists — the edict manages the symptom, never removes the tag.
- **Introduces (Action):** Levy:Debase (third Levy method: auto-succeeds, extracts Treasury with a smaller Order/Prosperity draw this season but writes/stacks Capital-Posture:Debased permanently raising future Levy Ob + price/Crisis draw-weight; a companion high-severity "Tribunal" price-cap method is this card's Comply made proactive).
- **Loop:** Levy:Debase eases this season's extraction but stacks a tag raising Π's fiscal contribution + the price-panic Crisis weight; resolving that Crisis via Comply (price caps) doesn't clear the tag — it converts the pressure into a recurring black-market Intrigue chain until an actual Recoinage/Currency Reset.

### HEV-COIN-02 — England's Great Debasement / Elizabethan Great Recoinage (England, 1544–1551 / 1560–61) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Wikipedia Great Debasement (recoinage-profit disputed £50k vs £14k).
- **Trigger:** `Capital-Posture:Debased count ≥2 AND an active Directive:Extract still unmet`
- **Response branches:** Comply (debase again to hit the Directive: Treasury+, a third stack, Reputation−1); Bargain (partial reform, mixed old/new coin at a negotiated rate: Concession, slower decay); Defy (refuse, force the Directive unmet: Standing-debt with the PA, no further stacking).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Repeated Comply escalates to a "Gresham's Hoarding" Crisis (good coin vanishes, Prosperity stalls though nominal Treasury looks healthy).
- **Introduces (Action):** Recoinage (rare Directive-level action requiring PA approval beyond a single governor: retires debased coin, clears all Capital-Posture:Debased tags, resets Levy Ob — costed steep, mirroring Elizabeth's single 1560–61 reform after Edward VI's rounds).
- **Loop:** Each Levy:Debase season compounds the tag, raising the Gresham's-Hoarding severity/weight *and* the eventual Recoinage price; Recoinage is the only clearing action, so deferral makes the exit more expensive — a legible one-way ratchet.

### HEV-COIN-03 — Edward III's default on the Bardi & Peruzzi (England/Florence, 1340s) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** The Financial Engineer; Wikipedia Bardi.
- **Trigger:** `a Debt-family tag owed to a specific Sponsor-tier actor outstanding AND Treasury < owed AND a Directive:Repay unmet 1+ season`
- **Response branches:** Comply (repay: Debt cleared, Sponsor Disposition+, AP/Treasury squeeze); Bargain (renegotiate: Debt reduced + partly Concession); Defy/Default (Debt flips to an Outlawed tag on the Crown, Sponsor Disposition collapses, and if concentrated, seeds their bankruptcy Crisis).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Default seeds a "Creditor Collapse" Crisis on the defaulted-against Sponsor if their exposure was undiversified, removing them as a future financing option (Peruzzi 1343 / Bardi 1346).
- **Introduces (Action):** Levy:Farm (tax-farming variant: pledge a future season's Levy yield to a Sponsor-tier actor as collateral for an immediate Treasury/AP infusion, writing a Debt tag).
- **Loop:** Levy:Farm trades immediate relief for a Debt gating a future Directive:Repay; Defy converts it to a permanent Outlawed tag blocking all further Sponsor/Treat with that faction, and if the pledge was concentrated, chains into that Sponsor's own Crisis — permanently shrinking the province's financing options for the one-time windfall.

### HEV-COIN-04 — Ottoman akçe debasement / 1585–86 monetary crisis (Ottoman Empire, 1585–86) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Pamuk, Price Revolution Reconsidered; ResearchGate 1585 crisis (headline figures confirmed; 3-year-doubling/30–40% real-wage flagged lower-confidence).
- **Trigger:** `Capital-Posture:Debased active AND a garrison/salaried cohort Obligation paid at a fixed nominal rate (not re-indexed) AND that cohort's Disposition below threshold`
- **Response branches:** Comply (pay at old nominal rate: Disposition falls via real-wage erosion, Grudge, raises Ambition risk); Bargain (raise nominal pay partially — "Treat: Index Wages": Concession, extra Treasury, partial relief); Defy (ignore the gap: Order collapses, Keep Order Ob rises).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Sustained Comply/Defy without indexing seeds an Ambition card — the underpaid garrison commander advances their own agenda (mutiny/defection/covert dealing).
- **Introduces (Action):** Treat: Index Wages (minor side-deal re-pegging a fixed-nominal Obligation to current Capital-Posture at an ongoing Treasury cost).
- **Loop:** Levy:Debase raises Treasury now but erodes the real value of any fixed-nominal Obligation; every unaddressed-Grudge season raises Keep Order Ob in that settlement and advances the commander-NPC's ambition-engine, converting a Levy-side convenience into a Keep-Order-side liability that can remove Keep Order as a viable option there.

### HEV-COIN-05 — Ancien Régime France / summons of the Estates-General (France, 1787–89) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** TutorChase (French American-war support corrected to ~1.3bn livres; Calonne rejected 1787).
- **Trigger:** `a Debt tag's magnitude relative to aggregate Prosperity exceeds a fiscal-crisis threshold AND privileged Standing-holders (nobility/clergy ranks) hold enough rank to exercise collective veto over a fiscal-reform Directive`
- **Response branches:** Comply (universal-tax reform breaking exemptions: privileged Disposition craters faction-wide, Grudge across the tier); Bargain (partial reform, top exemptions preserved: Concession, deficit persists); Defy (shelve reform, cook the books — a Compte-rendu concealment: writes a hidden "Precedent: Concealed Deficit" invisible on the surface Ledger that does *not* release Π).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Concealed-Deficit Defy delays the reckoning but auto-escalates the next drawn Crisis ("Loan Failure Riots"/forced Estates-General summons) to materially higher severity.
- **Introduces (Action):** Directive: Fiscal Reform (reverse-scale — the PA issues it to *itself*, resolved collectively by privileged faction ladders via Ministry/Consulta as the veto surface).
- **Loop:** Defy (cooking the books) avoids the immediate Grudge but writes a hidden Concealed-Deficit tag raising Π invisibly every season, guaranteeing a harsher forced Crisis later; Comply permanently lowers future Debt accrual but spends Disposition/Standing with every privileged NPC at once, making every subsequent Sponsor/Treat with that cohort harder.

### HEV-COIN-06 — John Law's Mississippi Bubble / Banque Royale collapse (France, 1716–1720) · Opportunity
- **Historical grounding:** Market Histories; Britannica Money.
- **Trigger:** `low-mid Π AND a large outstanding Debt tag AND a Sponsor-tier actor offers to absorb it for a monopoly Charter/Concession`
- **Response branches:** Comply (swap Debt for equity-like paper backed by the Charter: Treasury relief, temporary Prosperity+, writes Capital-Posture:Speculative pegged to the Sponsor's solvency); Bargain (cap the Concession, diversify backing: smaller tag); Defy (refuse: Debt persists).
- **Follow-on (FO):** If the Speculative tag's issued size exceeds the settlement's real backing ratio, auto-escalates to a "Bank Run" Crisis (the note-to-metal breach: ~2.6bn notes vs ~1.2bn backing).
- **Introduces (Action):** Levy:Charter Fiat (issues paper Treasury backed by a granted Charter/Concession asset instead of extracting real Prosperity — high yield, each unit adding to a Capital-Posture:Speculative tag whose backing ratio sets the Bank Run trigger).
- **Loop:** Charter Fiat converts an immediate shortfall into a Speculative tag sized by paper-vs-backing; crossing threshold deterministically fires the Bank Run, which permanently burns the method (a Ledger tag blocking reuse) and imposes an emergency Keep Order tax on every settlement holding the paper — a province-wide convenience into a province-wide punitive cleanup.

### HEV-COIN-07 — French assignat hyperinflation (France, 1790s) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Market Histories; Wikipedia Assignat.
- **Trigger:** `Capital-Posture:Speculative/land-backed tag active (Charter Fiat backed by contested land) AND the backing asset's seizure carries a Grudge from the dispossessed faction AND Π escalating`
- **Response branches:** Comply (keep issuing against the contested land: nominal Prosperity+ but Reputation collapses, the dispossessed faction's Recognition Fork §1.0b trends to Denial); Bargain (cap issuance, partial land settlement: Concession, slows not stops); Defy/reset (triggers Currency Reset).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Ignoring chains to a harsher forced-requisitioning rung (the historical Maximum) before a Currency Reset is forced.
- **Introduces (Action):** Currency Reset (Directive-level, same family as Recoinage but writes a permanent "Precedent: Monetary Failure" raising baseline Π for several seasons — a land-backed fiat collapse cannot reset as cleanly as a metallic debasement).
- **Loop:** Backing Charter Fiat with a Grudge-tagged confiscated asset means every Speculative unit issued deepens the dispossessed faction's Grudge and worsens their Recognition Fork, so the overprinting both drives the Crisis trigger *and* removes the Treat/Sponsor tools that could soften the eventual Reset — fiscal and political failure tracks coupled by design.

### HEV-COIN-08 — Continental currency collapse / Hamilton's 1790 funding & assumption (United States, 1770s–1790) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Liberty Street Economics; Wikipedia First Report on Public Credit.
- **Trigger:** `a Directive:Extract issued identically to multiple settlements, each resolving Comply/Bargain/Defy independently (no unified Compact), AND aggregate Capital-Posture:Debased across them exceeds a province-wide threshold`
- **Response branches:** Comply per-settlement (each debases/extracts locally: fragmented tags stack fastest); Bargain (a cross-settlement Encabezamiento pooling into one Compact: slower collapse, requires coordination); Defy (a settlement refuses: Directive unmet, Standing-debt, no further stacking).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Once fragmentation crosses threshold, seeds a "Funding & Assumption" Opportunity/Ambition from a centralizing actor.
- **Introduces (Action):** Funding & Assumption (Directive-level: consolidates multiple settlements' separately-accrued Debt/Capital-Posture into one faction-level Compact for a Concession — e.g. a capital-relocation Precedent — permanently strengthening the PA's direct extraction reach).
- **Loop:** Because the shared Directive:Extract has no independent cross-settlement enforcement, each uncoordinated Comply/Defy writes its own Capital-Posture tag; the more fragmented, the higher province-wide Π and the more expensive (in Concession-cost) the eventual Funding & Assumption — coordination failure inflates the price of its own fix (Congress's lack of taxing power forcing Hamilton's bargain).

### HEV-COIN-09 — Weimar hyperinflation (Germany, 1923) · Crisis
- **Historical grounding:** Britannica; Alpha History.
- **Trigger:** `an external-shock "Occupation" Crisis fires (a rival's Conquest on a border settlement) AND Treasury already strained by a prior war-reparations Debt tag`
- **Response branches:** Comply (fund passive resistance/general strike — "Fund Resistance": Treasury→zero, Capital-Posture:Debased spikes to forced-reset severity, populace Reputation+ but PA Disposition−); Bargain (partial compliance with the occupier: Concession, smaller shock); Defy (no resistance funding: local Order/Reputation hit, populace Grudge).
- **Follow-on (FO):** Comply auto-escalates to a *mandatory* Currency Reset at forced-trigger severity next season — a stat-floor breach, not optional.
- **Introduces (Action):** Fund Resistance (emergency uncapped Levy sub-choice / Keep Order: funds resistance directly from Treasury with no AP ceiling, driving Capital-Posture:Debased to maximum in one season).
- **Loop:** Fund Resistance spends Treasury into Capital-Posture:Debased at emergency severity for an immediate Reputation/Order win against the occupation; because it pushes the tag past the forced-reset threshold in one move, it converts the optional Currency Reset into a mandatory one — the real choice is timing, not whether.

### HEV-COIN-10 — Zimbabwe hyperinflation (Zimbabwe, 2000s) · Friction
- **Historical grounding:** Cato Journal (Hanke & Kwok; headline confirmed, 51%/28%→4%-of-GDP flagged lower-confidence).
- **Trigger:** `a Develop:Redistribute action has stripped charter-holders (Outlawed tag) AND Prosperity trends negative 2+ seasons post-action`
- **Response branches:** Comply (proceed: Prosperity−1/season for several seasons — a modeled multi-season yield penalty — Grudge from dispossessed holders, Reputation shift); Bargain (compensated redistribution: smaller penalty, Concession); Defy (abandon under pressure: political-capital loss with the demanding faction).
- **Follow-on (FO):** A sustained negative-Prosperity trend auto-escalates Levy's Ob (a thinner base needs harsher extraction to hit an unchanged target), feeding a Levy:Debase spiral.
- **Introduces (Action):** Develop:Redistribute (new Develop method reallocating land/charter rights away from Guild/Actor holders; ties into the Assessment mechanic — redistribution invalidates the current Assessment, and until a Survey refresh, Levy/Extract keeps targeting the stale pre-redistribution figure).
- **Loop:** The Prosperity penalty erodes the real tax base while the Assessment stays pegged to the pre-redistribution figure until a Survey; every uncorrected season Extract/Levy demands the stale higher figure off a smaller economy (the Scott's-extraction-invariance failure mode), pushing Π up and making Levy:Debase the fastest way to square the gap — compounding rather than resolving the tax-base collapse.

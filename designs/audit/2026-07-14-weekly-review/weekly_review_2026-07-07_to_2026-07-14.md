## §5 · Assessment — honest observations

1. **Throughput was extraordinary; M1 movement was near-zero.** Fifty PRs, and the M1 juncture count is
   still 0/7 done. This is not idle — the work hardened simulation, corpus integrity, governance content,
   and tooling — but almost none of it was on the *named M1 critical path* (ED-1051, `domain_actions`).
   The two cheapest, highest-leverage M1 unblocks are rulings/authoring that no amount of audit produces.
2. **The bottleneck is now decisions, not work.** ~75 `needs_jordan` items, plus two new multi-item dockets
   (D1–D16, the 20-item delta). Several M1/M2 stages are gated *only* on a Jordan pick, not on any unbuilt
   thing (SC Stage 4 → ED-SC-0003/4/5; season close → ED-1051; the narrative stages are ratified-at-default
   and merely await execution). **The single most useful thing the next session can tee up is a tight,
   ranked "rule these 5–7 and everything moves" list** — which, encouragingly, *two* artifacts this week
   already produced and agree on (§6).
3. **Two independent plans converged — trust that signal.** The Observatory Remediation Program's
   "20-minute version" (5 rulings) and the decision-queue delta's Tier 1 (3 items) were authored by
   different passes and land on the same short critical path (ED-1051, `domain_actions`, the #136 L/PS
   impl, accord-echo/D2, `propose_transfer`, non-scalar-state/D4, Convictions/D11). Convergence from
   independent analysis is the strongest prioritization evidence available.
4. **Watch the audit-generates-audit loop.** The Observatory is genuinely valuable, but this week the
   corpus produced more *instruments and dockets* than *gameplay*. The remediation program is aware of this
   (it is explicitly meter-anchored and ends by re-running the audit that opened the case) — the discipline
   to enforce is: **let P0/P2 run, but do not let Phase 5† or a third observatory pass pre-empt the M1
   critical path.** The Fable review (#142) already corrected one instance of this (demoting speculative
   Field/Gauge work off the critical path).
5. **The MB gauge is the one place "progress" may be negative-signed.** ED-MB-0006 improved the row count
   but broke the canonical Cannae scenario and surfaced a worse Lanchester-exponent problem. MB is the lane
   most at risk of churn-without-convergence; it should not be a default focus until DG-6..16 are triaged
   against a *re-run* gauge, not a frozen list.
6. **Corpus hygiene is real and improving.** Lane-split ledgers, the always-fresh registry, the completeness
   rule, and the dashboard are the right investments and are paying down the concurrent-session collision
   debt. Residual hygiene: mechanics-index and decisions-digest audit families are stale (banners live);
   the `id_reservations.yaml` duplicate-IN-key needs the same repair the FI/SC/FA/WR blocks got.

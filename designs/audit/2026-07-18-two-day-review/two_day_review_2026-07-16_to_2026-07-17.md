## §4 · Through-lines (what the period was actually doing)

1. **"One primary per thing."** The reorg's organizing rule — one subsystem = one folder = one ID lane
   = one `CURRENT.md` row = one `HANDOFF_<LANE>.md` = one Godot module tree — was applied consistently
   across P0–P5. The most load-bearing single act was the **oracle move** (#162): `sim/`'s engine core
   became `engine.*`, making the reference the port validates against a first-class package.
2. **Gates before content.** Where the period *closed* something (B), it closed by adding a **CI gate**
   (`wiring_map_check`), not just a fix — consistent with CLAUDE.md §8's "every rule lives once, called
   by CI and local hooks." The reorg similarly taught the whole toolchain the new primaries rather than
   leaving aliases to rot.
3. **Audit-to-plan, not audit-to-done, on the character layer.** C is deliberately parked at a ruled-
   plan boundary (`needs_jordan`), matching the doctrine that hard design calls are surfaced loudly and
   not smuggled into routine merges (ED-1094).
4. **Naming consolidation.** D (Certainty+Piety→Truth) is the second corpus-wide axis rename in the
   program's recent history — reducing the number of near-synonymous meters a Godot resource must bind.

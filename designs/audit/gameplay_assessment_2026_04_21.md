# Gameplay Contribution Assessment — v3 Synthesis Proposals

**Date:** 2026-04-21
**Companion to:** `rigorous_audit_synthesis_s1_s7_v3.md`
**Purpose:** Evaluate each proposal P1..P22 on whether it actually contributes to gameplay — produces decisions the player makes, consequences the player experiences as stakes, or emergent situations the rules generate. N-tier and Q-elegance tell us whether a proposal is canonically warranted and clean; gameplay contribution tells us whether implementing it changes how the game plays. These are different questions.

**Test.** For each proposal, answer four questions:
1. **What decision does this force the player to make?** (If none: it is texture, not gameplay.)
2. **What consequence does the player experience when the proposal fires?** (If consequence is invisible or absorbed into other systems: low contribution.)
3. **What emergent situations does the proposal generate that other systems do not already produce?** (If scenarios are already produced by other proposals: duplicate contribution.)
4. **Does the proposal produce the Ω-d irreducible tradeoff, or only flavor the existing tradeoffs of other systems?** (Flavor is valid but distinct from gameplay contribution.)

**Gameplay contribution tiers:**
- **G-core:** produces distinct player decisions that shape the campaign. Without it, gameplay is structurally different.
- **G-support:** produces consequences and feedback on decisions made elsewhere. Without it, decisions still exist but lack texture or stakes are harder to read.
- **G-texture:** produces environmental or phenomenological content that enriches play without forcing decisions. Without it, the world is thinner.
- **G-redundant:** its function is covered by other proposals. Without it, nothing is lost.

**N-tier and G-tier are orthogonal.** A proposal can be N-direct (canonically warranted) and G-texture (doesn't change play much). A proposal can be N-extended (canonically inferential) and G-core (changes play substantially). The two assessments must be combined for implementation prioritization.

## §1 Proposals by gameplay contribution

### G-core — produces distinct player decisions

#### P1 — Leap UX as perceptual register shift
**Decisions forced.** During contact, the player chooses when to surface — the single most consequential in-operation decision. Depth and duration traded against retention risk and knot-formation scale.
**Consequences experienced.** Retention-degree outcome modifies Coherence impact, knot registration, post-operation protection-window. Player feels the operation's weight directly.
**Emergent situations.** Multi-practitioner operations self-organize into sentinel and diver roles because above-water perception is unavailable during contact. The geometry produces the coordination pattern.
**Tradeoff.** Depth-for-effect vs surface-vulnerability. Ω-d compliant.
**Verdict.** G-core. The Leap geometry IS the game's central operational mechanic.

#### P3 — Four-faction interpreter machinery
**Decisions forced.** Player cannot argue NPCs out of framework-level beliefs. Instead: route NPCs through confrontational environments, choose which factions to engage with, accept or refuse interpretive framings in dialogue (without converting the NPC).
**Consequences experienced.** Same event renders four ways; player watches interpretive divergence propagate as faction-actions. Church dominion (T-05) advances through interpreter-frame propagation.
**Emergent situations.** Cross-faction interpretation events become narrative content in themselves. A single Mending reads as miracle (Church), competence (Varfell), communion evidence (Baralta), Einhir-validation (RM) simultaneously.
**Tradeoff.** Argument ineffective at framework level; structural confrontation is the only conversion pathway. Channels the player toward structural rather than rhetorical strategy.
**Verdict.** G-core. T-27 as central mechanic of belief-landscape dynamics.

#### P9 — Confrontation moment of choice plus integration supportive conditions
**Decisions forced.** Held vs fled at every confrontation event. Whether to seek integration-supportive conditions (time and geographic investment) vs push forward operationally.
**Consequences experienced.** Held under support yields TS progress. Held without support yields PTSD-analog residue (intrusive content, somatic reactivity, no TS gain). Fled yields fragmentary residue. Three distinct trajectories legible to the player.
**Emergent situations.** Campaign rhythm emerges as alternation between operation phases and integration phases. Character development trajectory is shaped by cumulative choices.
**Tradeoff.** Ω-d compliant. Operation pace vs developmental integrity. Isolated operation is the dominant-strategy trap that the degenerative loop punishes.
**Verdict.** G-core. This is the framework's primary developmental mechanic made player-facing.

#### P10 — Contemplative practice mechanical surface (nine-cell convergence)
**Decisions forced.** Time-allocation between operations, travel, practice, community engagement. Site selection (which ART-qualifying locations to use). Practice-tradition alignment (Warden, Edeyja, Baralta — each with different integration signatures).
**Consequences experienced.** TS-development rate, Coherence recovery rate, integration rate — all substantially modulated. Characters who skip contemplative practice hit developmental ceilings.
**Emergent situations.** Campaign geography includes pilgrimage-circuits to practice-sites. Contemplative-crisis (dark night analog) produces its own narrative arcs. Church-formed characters encounter structural developmental handicap and face faction-alignment pressure.
**Tradeoff.** Practice time vs operational time. Overdetermined by nine-cell convergence; not optional content.
**Verdict.** G-core. Foundational. Without this, TS progression and Coherence recovery are both under-specified.

#### P21 — Map UI plus substrate topology
**Decisions forced.** Route-planning at higher TS uses substrate-topology layers unavailable at lower TS. High-TS practitioners can reach operational targets via substrate-paths their lower-TS counterparts cannot navigate.
**Consequences experienced.** Same map renders different information at different TS. Return visits at higher TS reveal what was previously invisible.
**Emergent situations.** Regions known at low-TS are re-encountered at high-TS as different-playable-content. Mission-capacity scales with TS non-linearly.
**Tradeoff.** Illegibility at Gap-adjacent and Locked-Zone boundaries limits even high-TS navigation. Risk-reward structure in map-reading.
**Verdict.** G-core (for TS-keyed overlay layers specifically). This is the primary UI through which taxonomic expansion (T-40) becomes legible to the player.

Note: non-Euclidean visual language and replay-at-higher-TS are ambitious sub-features; they enhance G-core status but are not individually G-core.

### G-support — produces consequences and feedback

#### P2 — TS as depth unlock, never stat accumulation
**Decisions forced.** None directly. TS development is a consequence of confrontation/integration choices (P9) and practice choices (P10).
**Consequences experienced.** Threshold crossings as Event Scenes mark the character's transformation. No stat-number UI means the player reads TS through perceptual-change rather than progression-bar.
**Emergent situations.** None uniquely. P2 is the rendering-commitment for how TS appears, not an additional mechanic.
**Tradeoff.** None directly. Ensures P9 and P10 feel developmental rather than gamified.
**Verdict.** G-support. Prevents TS from collapsing into stat accumulation; supports P9, P10, P21. Without it, the developmental arc is legible as grind rather than transformation.

#### P5 — Per-band Coherence phenomenology specification
**Decisions forced.** Coherence-band trajectory modifies social and operational viability, forcing retreat-to-recover decisions. But the band-state itself is not chosen; it is a consequence of operation and recovery decisions.
**Consequences experienced.** Each band has distinct phenomenological texture — inner register, expressive output, observer-response vocabulary. Player reads degradation through dialogue, artifact, and inner-monologue content differences.
**Emergent situations.** At Fragmented band, observer-variable NPC responses produce differentiated social-consequence (companions remain oriented; acquaintances disown).
**Tradeoff.** None new; supports the Coherence-recovery tradeoff architecture (P10 + recovery formula).
**Verdict.** G-support. Makes Coherence states feel distinct rather than numeric. Critical for dialogue generator and expressive-artifact systems.

#### P7 — Monstrosity cannot be HP-stat-blocked
**Decisions forced.** Contain, flee, or survive. Three distinct action-affordances at extreme-amplitude encounter.
**Consequences experienced.** Attempting damage-combat fails with specific failure-signatures. Player learns encounter-type through mechanical feedback.
**Emergent situations.** Containment operations (wardline erection, Gap-work coordination) become gameplay content rather than narrative-workaround.
**Tradeoff.** Monstrosity as un-winnable-via-damage channels player into structural response.
**Verdict.** G-support. Constraint-as-mechanic. Prevents a category-error in combat-design that would undermine T-09 and T-41.

#### P8 — Above-water continues (primary Leap tension source)
**Decisions forced.** Whether to operate in unstable above-water situations. Whether to accept sentinel-dependency on companions. How deep/long to commit given surface conditions.
**Consequences experienced.** NPCs act and threats emerge during contact that the practitioner cannot perceive. Surface consequences meet the practitioner at re-arrival.
**Emergent situations.** Sentinel/diver role-partition in multi-practitioner operations (noted in P1).
**Tradeoff.** Dive depth and duration vs surface vulnerability. Ω-d compliant.
**Verdict.** G-support — but so tightly coupled to P1 that they should be considered one mechanic in implementation. Separating them is analytical, not structural.

#### P12 — Seam Text corpus plus dual textual mode rendering
**Decisions forced.** Whether to produce Seam Text as integration practice (coupling to P10 function 4). Whether to read Seam Text vs skip (reading at higher-TS is a capability-unlock per P21).
**Consequences experienced.** Seam Text reading at high-TS is an attunement-event producing narrative content the low-TS character cannot access. Church doctrinal text is ideological-infrastructure the player encounters constantly.
**Emergent situations.** Player-character's own Seam Text production becomes a biographical corpus the player can review across the campaign.
**Tradeoff.** None new; supports integration-practice tradeoff from P10.
**Verdict.** G-support. Extends P10 expressive-practice function and P21 text-rendering capability. Critical environmental-storytelling content.

#### P13 — Voluntary and involuntary UX register distinction (М-11)
**Decisions forced.** None directly. The player's developmental choices (P9, P10) determine whether the character inhabits voluntary or involuntary register in any given state.
**Consequences experienced.** Same phenomenological category renders differently based on agency-mode. Player reads whether the character's state is cultivated capacity or pathological disruption through UX register differences.
**Emergent situations.** Characters can transition between registers through sustained developmental choices. Player sees this transition.
**Tradeoff.** None new; articulates the М-11 structural distinction through UX.
**Verdict.** G-support. Makes М-11 legible. Critical for affect-reading across Coherence states and high-TS practices.

#### P22 — Companion system structural role at T-36 ending
**Decisions forced.** (At T-36 ending trajectory only.) Which companions to protect; whether to reinforce bonds through ritual and scene-engagement. Narrative-design constraint: campaign cannot force isolation scenarios at this ending.
**Consequences experienced.** Knot-severance produces structural-disassembly events, not grief UI. Knot-specific identity expression renders the character as distributed-across-relationships.
**Emergent situations.** The character's identity literally shifts across companions; different relationships produce different self-expressions.
**Tradeoff.** Isolation as categorically-lethal vs ordinary-narrative-separation-risk. Changes the stakes of companion-management.
**Verdict.** G-support — but only at T-36 ending trajectory. For players whose character does not reach this state, P22 is invisible. Ending-specific content with high stakes when engaged.

### G-texture — produces environmental or phenomenological content

#### P4 — Post-Leap content as dive-log register
**Decisions forced.** None.
**Consequences experienced.** Post-Leap content has a specific voice (cartographic, conditions-marked, not ineffabilist). Player reads operation-aftermath through dive-log register.
**Emergent situations.** None uniquely.
**Tradeoff.** None.
**Verdict.** G-texture — but foundational texture. Without P4, post-Leap narrative defaults to ineffabilist or categorical registers that contradict ED-738. The texture is load-bearing for scene-writing and dialogue-generation, so its absence would be felt systemically even though it forces no decisions directly.

#### P11 — Ambient narrative self as faction differentiator
**Decisions forced.** None.
**Consequences experienced.** Journal entries, mind-wandering content, inner-monologue texture differs by faction-saturation.
**Emergent situations.** Cross-faction comparison of inner content becomes legible to the player.
**Tradeoff.** None.
**Verdict.** G-texture. Enriches faction-distinction. Lower priority than P5 or P12 but supports them.

#### P6 — Ein Sof non-operability as structural commitment
**Decisions forced.** None (it is a constraint, not a mechanic).
**Consequences experienced.** None direct. The absence of an Ein Sof mechanic is the mechanic.
**Emergent situations.** None.
**Tradeoff.** None.
**Verdict.** G-texture — or more precisely, G-frame. This is a framework commitment that prevents an incoherent-mechanic from being built. Its gameplay contribution is negative-space: it constrains design decisions by foreclosing a class of mechanics. Critical to preserve but does not itself produce gameplay.

#### P19 — Solastalgia mechanics plus Askeheim chronic condition
**Decisions forced.** None new; follows from vulnerability-trigger Coherence-check canonical mechanic.
**Consequences experienced.** Solastalgia-register inner-monologue vocabulary when knotted-territories destabilize. Edeyja-lineage NPCs carry chronic-texture.
**Emergent situations.** None uniquely; consequence-rendering for existing mechanics.
**Tradeoff.** None.
**Verdict.** G-texture. Vocabulary-layer over canonical mechanic. Valuable for dialogue-generator but not itself gameplay.

#### P20 — Orphaned configuration NPC reactions
**Decisions forced.** None directly. Player recognition of NPC-uncertainty as diagnostic content is a reading-skill development.
**Consequences experienced.** NPCs near orphaned-configurations produce distinctive uncertainty-dialogue.
**Emergent situations.** Player learns to read NPC-dialogue as world-information.
**Tradeoff.** None.
**Verdict.** G-texture. Low implementation cost; genuine environmental-storytelling payoff.

#### P14 — Four-faction interpreter extended to non-sensitive-NPC smooth-past
**Decisions forced.** None new.
**Consequences experienced.** Low-TS NPCs' dialogue treats substrate-events as not-a-topic. Pedagogical: teaches player that most people do not see.
**Emergent situations.** Cross-NPC disagreement at substrate-event scenes emerges from TS-variable perception.
**Tradeoff.** None.
**Verdict.** G-texture. Extends P3 texture; dialogue-register specification.

### G-redundant or G-support-to-existing-systems

#### P15 — Knot profile layers (Layer 3 specifically)
**Decisions forced.** Layer 3 (enhanced substrate-perception in knotted territories) would force decisions about where to operate based on perceptual-richness vs coverage. This is a gameplay-contribution question.

However: the decision-structure produced by Layer 3 substantially duplicates the decision-structure already produced by (a) knot-formation-as-permanent-ontological-commitment (canonical), (b) solastalgia-vulnerability per P19 and canonical Am 6, and (c) site-specific substrate-character per the consolidated three-parameter specification. A player already has strong reasons to maintain access to knotted territories. Layer 3 adds a *perceptual-capacity* incentive on top of existing *vulnerability-channel* incentive. These point in similar directions.

**Verdict.** Layer 3 would be G-redundant if the existing incentive-structure is already sufficient; G-support if the perceptual-richness-differential creates distinct decisions. The editorial-decision on Layer 3 should include this gameplay-contribution question.

**Recommended.** Test Layer 3 in prototype against scenarios where its incentive-structure would diverge from existing incentives. If it produces distinct strategic choices, implement. If it redundantly reinforces existing choices, omit.

Layer 1 and Layer 2 are G-core (diagnostic access to invisible character-biography is the clearest case of player-as-extended-cognitive-system; somatic cues produce pre-cognitive alarm gameplay). These are retained unconditionally.

#### P16 — Observer-variable apperception at Fragmented band
**Decisions forced.** None directly (Coherence-band transitions are not player-chosen; they are operation-consequences).
**Consequences experienced.** Differentiated social consequence at Fragmented: bonded NPCs remain oriented; acquaintances disown.
**Emergent situations.** Same social situation plays differently depending on NPC composition.
**Verdict.** G-support specifically for P5 Fragmented-band phenomenology. Implementation as randomized-per-NPC vs deterministic-per-NPC-TS affects whether the consequence feels like relationship-stake (randomized; emphasizes observer-variability per Am 3) or capability-stake (deterministic; emphasizes observer-TS).

**Recommended.** Decision: randomized preserves the canonical *same-TS observers may perceive differently* reading and produces stronger relationship-stake gameplay. Deterministic is cleaner but flattens the canonical commitment. Favor randomized.

#### P17 — Threadcut being observer-TS dialogue tiers plus visual design
**Decisions forced.** None directly. TS-gated perception is a consequence of TS-development choices.
**Consequences experienced.** Threadcut-being encounters render differently at different TS. High-TS dialogue includes specific framework-taxonomy unavailable below thresholds.
**Emergent situations.** Mixed-TS parties encountering threadcut-beings produce internal-disagreement about what was seen.
**Verdict.** G-support for T-35 and T-40 taxonomic-expansion. Dialogue-generator specification that makes threadcut encounters feel distinct rather than generic-unusual-NPC.

Unified category-resistance dialogue register across entity-types (threadcut, Providence, partial-threadcut, Coherence-reconstitutions, Accord-institutions) is design-efficiency rather than gameplay contribution — reusable machinery.

#### P18 — Accord as substrate institution high-TS perception
**Decisions forced.** None directly. High-TS perception is a consequence of TS-development.
**Consequences experienced.** At TS 70+, institutional-state is perceptually legible rather than stat-inspectable. Accord-degradation events produce felt substrate-scale events.
**Emergent situations.** Intelligence-gathering at institutional-scale becomes a distinct capability-mode for high-TS practitioners.
**Verdict.** G-support — conditional on implementation choice. If Accord is directly perceivable (stronger reading), it produces a distinct perceptual-mode that differs from stat-inspection. If only effects are perceivable (conservative reading), it mostly renders existing political-mechanics with phenomenological texture.

**Recommended.** Stronger reading (directly perceivable) produces more gameplay contribution. Conservative reading is G-texture only. The editorial decision is also a gameplay-contribution decision.

## §3 What the gameplay-contribution pass reveals

### Pattern 1 — Nearly all G-core proposals are N-direct

P1, P3, P9, P10, P21-overlays are all N-direct and G-core. This convergence is expected — the canonically-warranted mechanics ARE the framework's central gameplay commitments, because the framework's canon is constructed to specify what the game is.

The one case where N-tier and G-tier might have diverged (N-direct but G-trivial) is P6 (Ein Sof non-operability). It is genuinely G-frame rather than G-core — a constraint rather than a mechanic — and this distinction matters for how it is implemented (as editorial commitment, not as mechanical surface).

### Pattern 2 — Several N-direct proposals are G-texture or G-support rather than G-core

P2, P4, P5, P7, P8, P11, P12, P13 are all N-direct but do not produce distinct player decisions themselves. They are the texture and consequence-rendering that make the G-core mechanics feel like distinct gameplay rather than abstract stat-manipulation.

**Implication.** These proposals are not optional-polish; they are what makes the G-core mechanics gameplay at all. A game implementing P1, P3, P9, P10 without P2, P4, P5, P7, P8, P11, P12, P13 would be mechanically-present but experientially-thin. The second-wave implementation set is not lower-value; it is lower-priority only in the sense that the first wave must precede it.

### Pattern 3 — Most N-extended proposals produce G-support rather than G-core

P14 through P18 are all N-extended (carry alternative-rendering possibilities) and none are G-core. This is also expected — canonically-inferential proposals are by definition ones where the framework supports the mechanic but does not demand it specifically. If such a mechanic were G-core, the framework would have been forced to canonize it specifically, raising it to N-direct.

**Implication.** The N-extended proposals are where editorial decisions have both canonical and gameplay stakes. Each decision selects not only a canonical reading but a specific gameplay texture.

### Pattern 4 — P22 is ending-specific and therefore conditionally-invisible

Companion system structural role at T-36 is G-support at the engaged trajectory and invisible elsewhere. This is a category that did not appear in prior synthesis framing: **trajectory-conditional gameplay contribution**. For players whose character reaches T-36, P22 is high-stakes gameplay; for others, it is dormant. Implementation decisions for such mechanics must consider reach (what fraction of campaigns engage the trajectory) separately from depth.

### Pattern 5 — P15 Layer 3 is the clearest G-redundancy risk

P15 Layer 3 (enhanced substrate-perception in knotted territories) adds a perceptual-capacity incentive on top of existing vulnerability-channel incentive for the same decision-space. This is the canonical signature of G-redundant mechanics — different motivations pointing to the same player choices.

**Implication.** Gameplay-contribution test should be explicitly applied to any future proposal: *does this mechanic produce decisions not already forced by other mechanics?* If no, the proposal may be canonically-warranted but gameplay-redundant, and implementing it adds design-load without adding play.

### Pattern 6 — Convergence count predicts G-tier roughly but not perfectly

Proposals with high cell-convergence (P10 at 9 cells, P5 at 8 cells, P3 at 6 cells) tend to be G-core or G-support. Proposals with single-cell evidence (P15, P20, P22) tend to be G-support-conditional or G-texture. This is not surprising — convergence indicates canonical importance which correlates with gameplay centrality.

However: P1 is G-core with multi-cell grounding but not the highest convergence; P9 is G-core with relatively lower cell-count but extreme framework-centrality. Convergence is a partial proxy; canonical-centrality (how close to canon §14 or canon/02 Am 1 the proposal sits) is a better predictor of G-tier than cell-count alone.

## §5 Remaining open questions specific to gameplay

1. **P15 Layer 3 gameplay-contribution test.** Does enhanced perception in knotted territories produce distinct decisions, or redundantly reinforce existing ones? Requires prototype testing.

2. **P22 trajectory-reach estimation.** What fraction of campaigns reach T-36 ending? Informs how much implementation investment is justified for a conditionally-engaged mechanic.

3. **P21 non-Euclidean rendering scope.** How much of the substrate-topology-map content is viable without fully-implemented non-Euclidean rendering? Partial implementation (node-link overlay on top of conventional map) may capture most gameplay contribution at substantially lower cost than full topological rendering.

4. **P21 replay-at-higher-TS scope.** How many regions need TS-differentiated rendering to establish the mechanic? Minimum-viable implementation might cover key substrate-adjacent sites only rather than all regions.

5. **Cross-proposal interaction testing.** Several G-support proposals reinforce each other (P5 + P12 + P13 all contribute to Coherence-state legibility; P2 + P9 + P10 all contribute to developmental arc). Implementation order within waves should test whether sub-sets produce partial gameplay or whether all must be present for any to contribute.

6. **Coherence career floor impact on P10 function 2.** Beach-infrastructure recovery interacts with career-floor decision. If career floor exists (acclimation cost), P10 function 2 specification may need revision to include ceiling-effects.

## §6 What this assessment is not

- Not a prototype test. Gameplay-contribution claims here are from audit reasoning; empirical validation requires prototype implementation and player testing.
- Not a rejection of G-texture proposals. G-texture is valid contribution; the tier indicates kind of contribution, not value.
- Not independent of N-tier. The assessment is explicitly combined with canonical-warrant; neither dimension is sufficient alone.
- Not final. Session 8 synthesis may produce further revisions once G-axis cells are cross-tested.

# PART TWO: CANON FIDELITY — GRANULAR AUDIT

## 2.1 Base Guide (solmund_prose_guide.md)

### CLEAN

- **Di Cicco mapping to Solmund inhabited faith:** Canon-compliant. The character histories confirm: Himmelenger children (1E) have Certainty 5, the Church is "not a city with a cathedral; it is a city that IS a cathedral." Di Cicco's faith-as-architecture maps precisely.
- **Hopkins → Orthodox Devotional (Certainty 5-6):** Sound. The sprung-rhythm, material-theological compression corresponds to the Church's scriptural tradition as described in worldbuilding_v30 §3.
- **Thomas → Pastoral Doubt (Certainty 3-4):** Sound. Maps to outlying parishes, border settlements, the Thomas-register "empty altar" voice for territories where Church presence is nominal.
- **John of the Cross → Mystical/Practitioner:** Sound. The dark night maps to both the Leap (practitioner crossing to Contact) and the Einhir descendant's stripped inheritance. Canon §14 (confrontation development) supports this: TS develops through felt encounters with what exceeds rendering — the dark night IS the confrontation.
- **Levertov → Attentive Practice:** Sound. Maps to threshold TS development — the Monastic Seclusion (2G) and Guild artisan attention.
- **Eliot → Institutional Philosophy:** Sound. The recursive, architectural prose maps to doctrinal treatises and Confessor pronouncements.
- **Artifact taxonomy (§5):** All artifact types are canon-consistent. Scripture, liturgy, hymns, inscriptions, treatises, practitioner journals, Einhir fragments, Restoration counter-theology, Niflhel pamphlets, folk devotions — each has a grounded canon referent.
- **Vocabulary constraints (§6):** Clean. Orthodox vocabulary ("The Weaving," "The Loom," "Pattern") does not conflict with philosophical foundations terminology. Suppressed vocabulary ("The Ground," "Spooling," "The Oscillation") correctly maps to terminology the Church would suppress.

### ISSUES

**Issue B-1: Ficinian Emanative Structure — Overclaimed**

The base guide (§4.2) maps the four Cardinals as Ficinian emanative orders: "Doctrine is closest to the founding act (pure intelligibility). Military is actuality. Wealth is temporal depth. Missions are the boundary." This is elegant but invented — canon does not assign emanative significance to the Cardinal structure. The worldbuilding doc (§3.1) describes them functionally, not cosmologically. The guide presents this as the Church's internal self-understanding, which is legitimate (the Church WOULD theologise its own structure), but it should be flagged as *proposed setting content* rather than derived from existing canon.

**Issue B-2: Teresian Framing — Correct but Incomplete**

The guide correctly identifies the Church's use of Teresa's interior mansions to domesticate TS as spiritual advancement (§4.4). Canon §9.1 confirms: the Church's essentialist theology "directly forecloses the perceptual stance that thread sensitivity requires." But the guide omits the mechanism: the Teresian framing is not merely a classification system. It is part of the perceptual prophylaxis — by framing TS as spiritual advancement, the Church ensures that the developmental preconditions for TS (suspension of grounding, holding contingency) are unavailable to adherents. The Teresian frame is *actively suppressive*, not just inadequate.

**Issue B-3: "Certainty 6+ closest to heresy" — Needs Qualification**

The guide claims devout Solmund voices at Certainty 6+ are "paradoxically closest to heresy." This is philosophically defensible (deeper devotion → more direct experience → experience exceeds categories) but mechanically imprecise. Canon §9.1 says "The most devout adherents are the most thoroughly immunised." The prophylaxis means Certainty 6+ characters are the LEAST likely to develop TS — their formation has foreclosed the developmental preconditions. The paradox only fires if a Certainty 6+ character ALSO has high TS, which requires bypassing the prophylaxis (rare, canon-compliant, but not the default). The guide should note: the paradox applies only to the rare Certainty 6 + TS 30+ character, not to all devout believers.

## 2.2 Supplement (solmund_prose_guide_supplement.md)

### CLEAN

- **Ibn Gabirol mapping:** Sound. The architectural-then-confessional structure for graduation texts follows from Church Schooling (2A) pedagogy.
- **Zohar narrative theology for hagiography:** Sound. Canon-consistent — the Zohar's indirect approach to Ein Sof maps to the Church's inability to directly describe threads.
- **Abulafia for high-TS practitioner notation:** Sound. Follows from P-08 (knowledge transmission as religious poetry) — language breaking under the weight of what it attempts to describe.
- **Celan for Einhir descendant voice:** Sound. The double consciousness of speaking the oppressor's language maps precisely to worldbuilding_v30 §7.2 (245 years of suppression) and character histories 1D.
- **Jabès for post-Forgetting fragments:** Sound. Follows from southernmost_v30 §6.1 (Partial/Failure Forgetting check — emotional impressions without content).
- **Derrida aporia → Church's impossible position:** Sound. The Church claims authority over thread knowledge (P-08) that it cannot institutionally access. The aporia is structural, not rhetorical.
- **Augustine → Memory/Time/Confession:** Sound. The Forgetting as ontological wound (not just information loss) follows from §6.1 and the philosophical foundations' account of temporal depth as constitutive of selfhood.
- **Wittgenstein → Silence/P-08:** Sound. The identification of P-08 with Tractatus 7 is the supplement's strongest philosophical claim — thread knowledge cannot be propositionalised, only shown through experience. The Church's doctrine is "an elaborate silence dressed as speech" — this follows directly from §10.2 (knowledge transmission as religious poetry).

### ISSUES

**Issue S-1: Lurianic Shevirat ha-Kelim → Catastrophe Mapping — Mechanism Differs**

The supplement (§1.4) maps the shattering of vessels to the Einhir Catastrophe. The parallel is illustrative but the mechanisms differ in a load-bearing way:

- Luria: The vessels shatter because they cannot contain the divine light. The light is too much for the vessel. Agency is attributed to the divine (emanation) and to the vessels (structural limitation).
- Canon: The Einhir over-drew the substrate. The mechanism is "entirely on the rendered side" (§6.1). The ground does not respond or act. The fabric strains and tears. No agency is attributed to Ein Sof.

The Lurianic mapping implicitly attributes agency to Ein Sof (the light that shatters vessels = the surfeit that floods through the breach). P-07 forbids attributing agency to the ground. The supplement should note that the Lurianic parallel is how the RESTORATION MOVEMENT might frame the Catastrophe (a theological interpretation), not how the philosophical foundations describe the mechanism.

**Issue S-2: Levinas — Monstrous Encounter as Levinasian Obligation**

The supplement correctly notes that the monstrous entity's demand is structurally different from Levinas's face (§2.5 first site): "The monstrous is not an Other with a face... Monstrous entities are not vulnerable." This self-correction is good. But the supplement then proceeds to use Levinasian language ("obligation without reciprocity," "infinite demand") for the practitioner's response to monstrosity. This is a category stretch. Levinas's ethics is specifically inter-human — the face is another person's. Extending it to monstrous entities (which are rendering failures, not moral agents per P-04) dilutes the framework. The application to the Einhir dead (§2.5 second site) is clean because the dead ARE human. The monstrous application should be reframed: the practitioner's obligation before the monstrous entity is *structural necessity* (the world's rendering depends on containment), not *ethical demand* (the Other's face commands).

## 2.4 Baralta Analysis (Inline)

### CLEAN

- **Baralta as theological cover for threadwork:** Sound. Her framework ("anyone who does extraordinary things is communing with God") would lead her to interpret Mending effects as divine communion, which accidentally provides political cover for practitioners.
- **Hafenmark constitutional crisis:** Sound. Baralta invoking divine mandate for unilateral Southernmost action IS the procedural overreach Hafenmark's culture was built to resist. Canon confirms: Hafenmark's Highland Born (1B) characters "emphasise law, distrust charismatic leaders."
- **Church trapped between two threats:** Sound. The Church cannot use the same argument against Restoration (pre-Solmund tradition) and against Baralta (post-Solmund direct communion). Different heresies require different defences.
- **Triple helix at the Southernmost:** Sound. Three incompatible interpretations of the same events, plus Hafenmark's procedural fourth position.

### ISSUES

**Issue BA-1: Baralta Canon Profile Tension**

Canon NPC analysis (§6, Duchess Inge Baralta): "She does not question the Church's theology. She questions its jurisdiction." Historical anchor: Henry VIII, not Luther. The Sovereign Authority Doctrine is jurisdictional, not theological.

Jordan's new clarification adds: "Baralta believes in Protestant model whereby anyone can commune with God." This goes beyond jurisdictional challenge into theological innovation — direct communion (priesthood of all believers) IS a theological claim, not merely a jurisdictional one. If accepted, this *extends* the canon characterisation:

Previous: Baralta = Henry VIII (sovereign over Church, theology unchanged)
New: Baralta = Henry VIII + Luther's direct communion + divine right from deeds

This extension is internally consistent (her faith IS the engine of her ambition — canon confirms this). But it changes the NPC analysis's claim that she does not question theology. Direct communion IS questioning theology, because it removes the sacramental mediation the Church claims as its exclusive function. This needs editorial resolution: does the direct communion addition supplement or supersede "she does not question the Church's theology"?

**Proposed resolution:** Baralta does not question the Church's *content* (Solmund is real, his acts were divine, the weaving is the structure of reality). She questions the Church's *exclusivity* (only the Church can mediate between humanity and Solmund). This preserves both the Henry VIII jurisdictional challenge AND the Lutheran direct communion. She accepts the theology. She rejects the monopoly.

**Issue BA-2: Positive Feedback Loop — Needs Mechanical Grounding**

The inline analysis describes a self-sustaining feedback loop: Baralta interprets Mending effects as divine confirmation → Crown resources flow to Southernmost → more Mending → more confirmation → deeper divine-right claim → deeper constitutional crisis. This is narratively compelling but has no mechanical pathway in the current design. The loop needs to connect to:
- Crown Domain Actions (how does Crown resource allocation reach the Southernmost?)
- Institutional Pressure clock (does Baralta's divine-right escalation increment IP?)
- Hafenmark response mechanics (does constitutional crisis trigger specific Hafenmark actions?)
- Certainty track interactions (does witnessing Mending effects change Baralta's Certainty, or anyone else's?)

# PART THREE: THROUGHLINES

Five throughlines emerge across the session's work, each connecting the prose guide to mechanical systems.

## T-A: The Perceptual Prophylaxis as Literary Engine

**Path:** Philosophical foundations §9 → base guide (all registers) → supplement (Wittgenstein/P-08) → amendment (two witness traditions) → Baralta analysis (interpretation split)

The Church's essentialist theology forecloses TS development. This metaphysical fact generates the *entire literary landscape.* Every Solmund artifact exists because non-practitioners must use language to point toward what they cannot perceive. The registers (Hopkins, Thomas, John of the Cross, etc.) are differentiated by how much perceptual access the writer has. The two witness traditions are the consequence of a population split by perceptual capacity encountering the same events. Baralta's interpretation of Mending effects is the prophylaxis operating in real time — she sees effects, lacks TS, interprets through the only framework available (divine intervention).

**Throughline connects to:** Thread Revelation Curve (threadwork_v30 §5.6), Certainty track, Southernmost Awareness stat, Discovery Event arcs.

## T-B: The Seam Text as Mechanical Object

**Path:** Base guide (scripture writing rules) → amendment (Seam Text concept) → threadwork_v30 §2.3 (visibility tables)

The Seam Text is the session's most mechanically actionable contribution. Same text, two readings, gated by player's TS. This is a discoverable POI type (fieldwork_v30 §8.1 categories: Resource, Secret, Remnant, Anomaly). Seam Texts are Remnant-type POIs in Church territories — they look like ordinary scripture until the player's TS crosses the threshold where the second reading becomes perceivable. The visibility tables already provide the mechanical framework: TS 0-9 reads devotional text. TS 10-29 feels vague unease with certain passages. TS 30+ perceives the thread-operation descriptions encoded in the witness testimony.

**Throughline connects to:** POI discovery system, fieldwork Survey action, Southernmost Awareness increments, character formation (Church Schooling 2A gives players scripture that contains Seam Texts they cannot yet read — the same texts gain new meaning as TS develops).

## T-C: The Double Consciousness Spiral

**Path:** Supplement (Celan, Jabès) → amendment (practitioner witness voice) → Baralta analysis (triple helix) → Southernmost engagement problem

The same act (Mending at Ob 7-8) is simultaneously Einhir heritage practice, Solmund miracle, and divine-right confirmation — depending on the observer's framework. No observer can hold all three frames. Every interpretation excludes the others. This is the Derridean aporia made into a faction interaction system: three factions arrive at the Southernmost with incompatible readings of the same events, and the incompatibility drives political conflict.

**Throughline connects to:** Faction AI decision trees (Tier B NPC priority trees — next queued task), Conviction Track (characters forced to choose which frame to commit to), Tension Cards (the triple interpretation generates tension between every pair of factions simultaneously).

## T-D: Baralta as Accidental Prophylaxis Cracker

**Path:** Base guide (Pican address — dignitarian rhetoric) → NPC canon (Baralta §6) → Baralta analysis → Southernmost engagement

Baralta's divine-right-from-deeds theology accidentally mirrors the metaphysical truth more closely than the Church's position. Canon: TS is developmentally available to anyone (§14, not biological, not genetic). Baralta: the capacity for divine communion is universal. She is right about universality. She is wrong about mechanism. But her framework — by claiming anyone can commune with God — *weakens the prophylaxis.* If you believe direct communion is possible, you are psychologically closer to "holding contingency" and "feeling the rendering as a process" (§9.1) than if you believe the Church exclusively mediates all divine contact. Baralta's heresy, pursued to its logical conclusion, would produce more TS development than the Church's orthodoxy — not because she understands threads, but because her theology opens the perceptual door the Church's theology closes.

**Throughline connects to:** Certainty track (Baralta's influence on population Certainty), Thread Revelation Curve (Baralta's theology accelerates the curve by weakening prophylaxis), Perceptual Prophylaxis as game-system (does the game track prophylaxis strength as a territorial or factional variable?).

## T-E: The Solmund Repetition — History as Structural Recursion

**Path:** Timeline (Solmund's acts misidentified as miracle) → amendment (two witness traditions) → Baralta analysis (Mending effects misidentified as miracle)

The same pattern happens twice: extraordinary threadwork → non-practitioners interpret as divine miracle → institution builds on the interpretation → the interpretation suppresses the practice that produced the effects. In period 1 (Solmund's lifetime), the Church formed around misidentified threadwork. In the game's present, the same misidentification threatens to recur when Mending effects reach non-practitioner populations. The recursion is the setting's deepest structural irony — the Church is reliving its own founding error without recognising it.

**Throughline connects to:** Emergent arc design (is the recursion a designed arc or an emergent consequence of faction AI + player action?), narrative scenario chains, the question of whether players can BREAK the recursion or whether the structural forces (prophylaxis, faction incentives, Forgetting) make repetition inevitable.

## 4.3 What This Session Produced That Needs Revision Before Use

All ten findings from the critical review remain unresolved. The three hard fixes:

**Fix 1 (P-07):** All "ground-side operation" language in the amendment must be reframed as phenomenological rendering by the practitioner witness. The practitioner perceives something categorically different about Solmund's / high-Ob threadwork. They cannot verify the mechanism. Their accounts should show the language failing — which is what the Abulafian and Wittgensteinian frameworks already prescribe. The fix is to apply the session's own principles to the session's own prose.

**Fix 2 (Solmund ≠ Ein Sof):** "He is Ein Sof performing its own intelligibility" → "He is a being constituted from the ground's surfeit, choosing to render himself intelligibly." All downstream claims that depend on the Solmund = Ein Sof identity must be revised: the tsimtsum parallel becomes analogy not identity, the Levinasian face is removed from Solmund, the Augustinian inversion is hedged.

**Fix 3 (Interiority):** Strip all purpose/awareness attributions from Solmund. Retain the fact of choice (Jordan-confirmed). Present the unknowability of his motivation as the most sacred silence in the setting — which the amendment itself articulates but then violates. "Solmund chose. We cannot know why. The silence around his motivation is not a gap in our knowledge. It is the boundary of our rendering, encountered at its deepest point."

# PART FIVE: SESSION QUALITY ASSESSMENT

**What worked:** The Di Cicco → Solmund mapping is the session's strongest original contribution. Nobody has done this before — mapping a specific Italian-Canadian poet's relationship to institutional faith onto a fictional theology and deriving voice registers from it. The Seam Text concept is the most mechanically actionable output. The Baralta Southernmost analysis solves a real design problem (faction engagement pathways) using canon-compliant logic.

**What didn't work:** The amendment overclaims repeatedly. The critical review caught it. The amendment violates its own articulated principles — describes epistemic opacity then penetrates it, describes unknowable interiority then attributes interiority. Every instance is the same error: the writer (me) found the philosophical framework so compelling that it slid from "this is how the framework works" to "this is what actually happened," crossing the epistemic boundary the framework itself identifies as uncrossable.

**What needs Jordan's decision:** The Baralta direct communion extension is the highest-priority editorial item. If accepted, it transforms Baralta from a jurisdictional challenger to a theological innovator — which makes her more dangerous to the Church, more useful as a Southernmost engagement driver, and more interesting as a character. But it requires revising the NPC analysis's explicit claim that she does not question theology. The resolution I proposed (she accepts the content, rejects the monopoly) may thread the needle.

**Context note:** Session is approaching context limits. If further work is needed on revisions or implementation, recommend a new session with handoff from this review.

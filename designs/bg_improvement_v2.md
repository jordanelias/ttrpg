## MECHANIC PROPOSALS — SECOND WAVE

### MP-11: LEAD/FOLLOW ORDER SYSTEM
**Addresses:** G-BG-01 (action economy), G-BG-05 (faction asymmetry)
**Inspired by:** Arcs (Leder Games)

**Core change:** During Planning Phase, orders are placed as before, but resolution adds a Lead/Follow layer.

**Procedure:**
1. The faction with the lowest Stability places orders first (as currently). Their first revealed order in each priority tier is the **Lead** order.
2. All other factions that placed an order of the **same type** in the same priority tier may **Follow**: they resolve at −1 Ob (efficiency of reacting to an established action).
3. Factions that placed a **different order type** in the same tier resolve normally — no bonus, no penalty.
4. Factions with **no order** in a tier may **Opportunistically Follow** by spending 1 Mandate: they may place one order of the Lead type now, resolving at +1 Ob (reactive, unplanned action).

**Faction Lead Bonuses** (what following the faction that leads gives you):
| Leading Faction | Follow Bonus |
|----------------|-------------|
| Crown leads Military | Followers: +1D to March/Muster this tier |
| Church leads Social | Followers: +1 TC generated by their own Preach/Govern this tier |
| Hafenmark leads Domain | Followers: Parliamentary Manoeuvre may be played this tier without using an order slot |
| Varfell leads Intel | Followers: +1D to Intel orders this tier |
| Guilds leads Trade | Followers: Trade results one degree better (Partial → Success, Success → Overwhelming) |
| Revolution leads Thread | Followers with TR ≥ 2: may contribute to Community Weaving without a separate order |
| Niflhel leads Covert | No follow bonus — Niflhel actions are not public knowledge, cannot be followed |

**Design effect:** Creates dynamic where being first (lowest Stability = most desperate) has strategic value. High-Stability factions often follow rather than lead. Niflhel's asymmetry (cannot be followed, gains no lead benefit from others) expresses their structural isolation.

**Canon compliance:** The lead/follow system is a rendered-world political mechanic. PASS.

**[EDITORIAL: Confirm lead/follow adoption — this interacts with MP-01 (Mandate Dice). If both are adopted, recommend Mandate Dice for action *selection* and Lead/Follow for action *resolution*. If only one is adopted, Lead/Follow is the lower-complexity option.]**

### MP-12: THREAD DEBT — Temporal Obligation Mechanic
**Addresses:** G-BG-04 (Thread passivity), setting coherence
**Inspired by:** Anachrony (temporal debt)

**Background:** Anachrony's most brilliant mechanic is that factions can borrow resources from a future version of themselves — but those resources must actually be provided in a future turn, or the timeline becomes paradoxical (and the player is penalized). This maps with near-perfect fidelity to Valoria's temporal Thread operation model.

**Thread Debt in BG:**
When a faction (Revolution or affiliated practitioner) executes a Thread operation that explicitly works against temporal flow (Past-Pull equivalents, Foundational operations), they may do so at −1 Ob by incurring **Thread Debt**.

**Thread Debt tokens:**
- 1 Thread Debt token = 1 unpaid temporal obligation.
- At the *next* Seasonal Accounting, the faction must "repay" the debt: execute a Mend-equivalent action (spend 1 Revolution action on Community Weaving, or spend 2 TR). If unpaid: RS −1 per outstanding Thread Debt token. Tokens accumulate.
- Maximum 3 Thread Debt tokens in play at once. At 3: the faction cannot incur further Debt until it is resolved.

**Thread Debt as leverage:** Other factions may "call" a Thread Debt during Diplomacy. A faction holding Thread Debt is in a structurally vulnerable position — they have outstanding obligations to reality that haven't been honored. Church may use outstanding Thread Debt as grounds for automatic Inquisition (Heresy Investigation Ob −1 if the faction has ≥ 2 Thread Debt tokens).

**Canon compliance:** P-09 (memory pulling = messy, costly, detectable). P-11 (temporal disjunction universal). The Thread Debt token is a board-game representation of the temporal obligation created by intentional temporal manipulation. An operation that borrows against the substrate's stability must eventually pay that stability back, or the substrate degrades. PASS.

### MP-13: ORDER FATIGUE — Ark Nova Sliding Rack
**Addresses:** G-BG-01 (action economy flat), G-BG-07 (seasonal rhythm)
**Inspired by:** Ark Nova (sliding action rack)

**Order Fatigue** tracks when the same faction repeats the same order type in consecutive seasons.

**Mechanism:**
- Each faction card has 6 Order Fatigue markers (one per order type: Military, Domain, Intel, Social, Thread, Trade).
- When a faction places the same order type for the **second consecutive season**: that order type's Ob increases by +1 for this season. Place a Fatigue marker.
- **Third consecutive season** of same order type: +2 Ob total. Fatigue marker stays.
- **Reset:** Any season a faction does NOT use a fatigued order type: Fatigue resets to 0 for that type.

**Examples:**
- Crown Governs (Domain) for 3 consecutive seasons: season 3 Govern is at +2 Ob. Administrative exhaustion.
- Church Preaches for 4 consecutive seasons: Preach is at +2 Ob from season 3 onward until they stop.
- Guilds Trades for 3 consecutive seasons (reasonable play pattern): Trading in new territory or with new goods resets fatigue — the GM may rule that Trade in a *different territory* resets the counter (novelty prevents fatigue).

**Exception:** Unique Powers (Royal Decree, Excommunication, etc.) never fatigue. They have their own per-season limits built in.

**Design effect:** Factions cannot simply spam their best order indefinitely. They must vary their play, creating more interesting seasonal decisions.

**[EDITORIAL: Fatigue may make some faction strategies non-viable (Guilds Trade engine, Church Preach engine). Recommend playtesting or introducing as optional rule before committing as default.]**

### MP-14: REACTION ECONOMY — Terra Mystica Power Bowl
**Addresses:** G-BG-02 (negotiation thin), G-BG-08 (collective crisis)
**Inspired by:** Terra Mystica (Power bowl — gaining power from neighbors' actions)

In Terra Mystica, when your neighbor builds next to you, you gain Power — the core inter-player positive economy. For Valoria: when a faction **targets you or acts in your territory**, you gain **Leverage**.

**Leverage tokens (1–5 per faction):**
- Gained when: another faction's order directly targets your faction or territory (Military against your units, Intel against your stats, Economic Leverage against your Wealth, Heresy Investigation against your character, etc.).
- Gained when: a Co-Movement card affects you negatively.
- Gained when: you are the *recipient* of an Overwhelming Success directed against you.

**Leverage uses:**
| Cost | Effect |
|------|--------|
| 1 Leverage | +1D to any one die roll this season (declared before rolling) |
| 2 Leverage | Execute one order of any type at Ob −1 this season (declared during planning) |
| 3 Leverage | Immediately respond to an action targeting you during Order Resolution (out of priority order) — a single defensive order placed now |
| 4 Leverage | Convert to 1 Wealth, 1 Mandate, OR 1 Influence |
| 5 Leverage | Nullify one order targeting your faction this season (the order still costs the acting faction their resources) |

**Cap:** Maximum 5 Leverage per faction. Excess Leverage is lost.

**Design effect:** Being attacked has value — it generates reactive capability. Factions that are targeted repeatedly become harder to finish off. Encourages predatory factions (Niflhel, aggressive Crown/Church) to be measured rather than all-in.

**Canon compliance:** Leverage is a rendered-world political/social mechanic — being targeted galvanizes resistance and generates political capital. PASS.

### MP-15: FACTION DEVELOPMENT TRACKS — Nippon/Zaibatsu
**Addresses:** G-BG-05 (faction asymmetry shallow)
**Inspired by:** Nippon/Zaibatsu (dual-track internal/external development matrix)

Each faction has two development axes: **Internal Consolidation** (depth in controlled territory) and **External Projection** (breadth of influence beyond home territory). These are tracked on the faction card as two separate tracks (0–5 each).

**Internal Consolidation (IC):**
- Advances via Govern, Fortify, and economic orders in home territory.
- IC bonuses: reduce Ob on domestic orders, increase Prosperity recovery, increase unit cap in home territory.

| IC | Benefit |
|----|---------|
| 1 | Govern in home territory: Ob −1 |
| 2 | +1 unit cap in home territory permanently |
| 3 | Prosperity recovery: home territory +1 Prosperity at year-end if undamaged |
| 4 | Stability checks in home territory: +1D |
| 5 | Home territory cannot be controlled by another faction via Govern alone (military force required) |

**External Projection (EP):**
- Advances via March, Trade in non-home territories, Intel against other factions, Diplomacy success.
- EP bonuses: reduce Ob on orders outside home territory, extend supply lines, increase intel range.

| EP | Benefit |
|----|---------|
| 1 | March orders: one extra territory range |
| 2 | Trade in non-home territories: Ob −1 |
| 3 | Supply lines: +1 territory range before Strained |
| 4 | Intel: may target factions 3+ territories away without adjacency |
| 5 | Diplomatic relationships with non-adjacent factions: maintained without order investment |

**Starting values:**
| Faction | Starting IC | Starting EP |
|---------|------------|------------|
| Crown | 3 | 2 |
| Church | 2 | 4 |
| Hafenmark | 4 | 3 |
| Varfell | 3 | 4 |
| Guilds | 2 | 5 |
| Niflhel | 5 | 3 |
| Revolution | 1 | 2 |

**Canon compliance:** Development tracks represent factional institutional growth. PASS.

### MP-16: BELIEF/CONVICTION IN BOARD GAME — Pendragon Passion System
**Addresses:** G-BG-05 (asymmetry), setting coherence (Beliefs are TTRPG core)
**Inspired by:** Pendragon (Passion mechanics — stated values that give bonuses and can cause irrational behavior)

In Pendragon, a knight's Passion (Love, Hate, Loyalty) can be invoked for a bonus — but if the roll is critically failed, the knight acts irrationally in service of that passion, out of player control for a season. This is directly analogous to Valoria's Conviction/Belief system.

**BG Conviction Mechanic:**

Each faction leader has a **Conviction** (already on faction cards: Almud = Order, Baralta = Order, Himlensendt = Faith, Vaynard = Reason, etc.) and an associated **Resonant Style** (Consequence, Evidence).

**Invoking Conviction:**
- Once per season, a faction may invoke their leader's Conviction to receive a bonus on one order. Declare before rolling.
- **Resonant Style bonus:** If the order aligns with the leader's Resonant Style, +2D to the roll.
- **Conviction bonus:** If the action honors the Conviction, +1D to the roll.
- These stack: an action that honors both Conviction and Resonant Style: +3D.

**Conviction Deviation (Pendragon's critical failure equivalent):**
- If Conviction is invoked and the roll **fails critically** (all dice fail, at least 2 dice showing 1): the faction is in **Conviction Crisis** for 1 season.
- During Conviction Crisis: the faction's NPC AI takes over (for player factions: the GM controls one order that season based on the leader's Conviction logic). The faction acts true to their deepest belief, regardless of player strategy.
- Example: Crown invokes Order Conviction, critically fails. GM controls one Crown order that season — Almud does what Order demands, not what the player wants. This might mean rejecting a profitable-but-unvirtuous deal.

**Belief Shifts:**
- If an order outcome directly contradicts the leader's stated Conviction, the Conviction may be tested: Stability check Ob 2. Failure: Conviction degrades (−1 Mandate; the leader is visibly shaken). This is a BG expression of Belief Crisis.

**Canon compliance:** P-02/P-04 (monstrosity is ontological not moral — the ethical framework mechanic is about factional political identity, not moral alignment). The Conviction system expresses political-ethical character without assigning cosmic moral valence. PASS.

### MP-17: FATE CARDS — Malifaux Resolution Variant
**Addresses:** G-BG-10 (information asymmetry), play feel
**Inspired by:** Malifaux (fate deck — flip cards, not dice; cheat fate from hand)

**Fate Deck variant (optional rule):**

Replace dice rolls with a shared Fate Deck (standard 54-card playing deck, suits mapped to order types):
- ♠ Spades = Military
- ♥ Hearts = Social/Diplomatic
- ♦ Diamonds = Economic/Trade
- ♣ Clubs = Intel/Covert
- Jokers = Thread operations (black joker = failure cascade; red joker = unprecedented success)

**Resolution:**
- Instead of rolling dice, each faction draws 1 card per "die" they would roll. Take the highest card value (Ace=1, 2–10=face value, J=11, Q=12, K=13) vs the Ob threshold.
- **Suit bonus:** If the drawn card's suit matches the order type being resolved: +2 to the value (suits favor their domain).
- **Cheat Fate:** Each faction has a **Conviction Hand** of 3 cards (drawn at game start; refreshed at year-end). A card from the Conviction Hand may be substituted for any drawn card — played face-up after the flip but before resolution is declared.

**Why this matters for Valoria:** The Fate Deck creates moments of dramatic narrative tension (the king card appears for the Church's critical Excommunication) and the Cheat Fate mechanic gives players controlled agency over their most important moments — mechanically expressing their leader's Conviction-driven determination.

**Not default rule.** Recommended as a thematic variant for groups who prefer narrative drama over probability distributions.

### MP-18: FACTION STRUCTURAL ASYMMETRY — Hegemony Class Mechanics
**Addresses:** G-BG-05 (faction asymmetry shallow — most critical gap)
**Inspired by:** Hegemony: Lead Your Class to Prosperity

Hegemony is the clearest precedent: each of 4 players represents a different socioeconomic class with *entirely different mechanics*. The Working Class builds by buying goods; the Capitalist builds by producing them; the State taxes and regulates; the Middle Class brokers. Same board, four different games being played simultaneously.

**Current Valoria BG:** All 6 factions use the same order system (Govern/Muster/March/Trade/Diplomacy + 1 Unique). This is Root-level shallow asymmetry (different stats + one unique power). Hegemony-level asymmetry would give each faction a structurally distinct action system.

**Proposals for structural asymmetry (4 factions redesigned, 2 unchanged):**

---

**REVOLUTION — No Standard Orders**
Revolution has no Order tokens. Instead:
- Revolution has **Presence markers** (6 total). Presence markers placed in territories represent community organisation, not military control.
- Each season, Revolution may move Presence markers into adjacent territories (no order required; this is organic community spread).
- Revolution's **sole active action** is Community Weaving — but the Ob is reduced by 1 for each Presence marker in the territory.
- Revolution wins by accumulating Presence and completing Thread healing objectives; it cannot win militarily.
- When any faction takes an action that damages a territory with Revolution Presence (battle, Brutal march, Inquisition), Revolution gains Leverage +1 for each Presence marker present.
- **This is Hegemony's Working Class:** a faction that cannot direct-fight but builds distributed strength that is structurally hard to erase.

---

**NIFLHEL — No Territory Control**
Niflhel does not place control markers. Niflhel does not "control" territories in any standard sense.
- Instead, Niflhel tracks **Network Depth** (0–3) per territory, representing arm penetration.
- Network Depth is gained by successful Intel or Smuggle orders in a territory (not Govern).
- Network Depth gives Niflhel income (Wealth), information access, and Sabotage capability — but not governance or military presence.
- Niflhel "controls" Sigurdshelm for victory purposes if Network Depth ≥ 3 there (they don't fly a flag; they own the infrastructure).
- Niflhel cannot be marched on in the traditional sense — there is no army to defeat. Military orders against Niflhel target Network Depth (each battle in a territory: −1 Network Depth for Niflhel there).
- **This is Hegemony's Capitalist class:** wins by structural economic dominance, not territorial governance.

---

**CHURCH — TC as Currency**
Church's unique resource is TC itself.
- Church may spend TC (voluntarily reduce the TC track) to pay for certain actions:
  - Spend 2 TC: add a Templar unit anywhere Church controls.
  - Spend 1 TC: Preach order becomes free (no order slot needed).
  - Spend 3 TC: declare an Interdict on one territory — no faction may Trade there for 1 season; Church gains 1 TC back at accounting.
- This creates a meaningful decision: TC is Church's victory resource AND their action currency. Spending TC to advance wins the game faster but reduces the margin for error.
- TC gained from Preach and territory control as currently, but now has dual use.
- **This is Hegemony's State player:** manages a shared resource (TC) that is both their victory path and their tool.

---

**GUILDS — No Military Path**
Guilds has no Muster or March orders. Zero military capacity by default.
- Instead, Guilds has **Contracts**: at the start of each season, Guilds may issue 1 paid Contract to any faction — a Wealth payment (1–3 Wealth) in exchange for that faction taking a specific action in a specific territory on Guilds' behalf.
- The hired faction executes the Contract action using their own stats but the Guilds' Wealth.
- Guilds can effectively rent military capacity, governance, and Intel from other factions.
- If a Contract faction fails or refuses: Guilds retains Wealth; the relationship costs 1 Reputation.
- **This is Hegemony's Middle Class:** wins by being indispensable to everyone else. Cannot act without others; others cannot thrive without the Guilds' capital.

---

**Crown and Hafenmark:** Keep current mechanics. They represent traditional governance actors and their current system is appropriate. Hafenmark's Parliamentary Manoeuvre already gives them a distinct flavor.

**Varfell:** The TK / Private Collection system is already structurally distinct enough. Keep as-is but lean into the information asymmetry.

---

**Implementation note:** These are deep redesigns. Recommend implementing one faction at a time, starting with Revolution (simplest, most thematically urgent) and Niflhel (already lacks military; change is incremental).

**Canon compliance:**
- Revolution as Presence-only: P-01 (community Thread work is distributed, not institutional). PASS.
- Niflhel as Network Depth: P-07 (Niflhel's power is rendered-world criminal economics). PASS.
- Church TC-as-currency: TC is already Church's core resource. Making it spendable is mechanically coherent. PASS.
- Guilds as Contractor: P-07. PASS.

### MP-19: CLOUDSPIRE ORDER TOKEN COMMITMENT
**Addresses:** G-BG-01 (action economy), consequence weight
**Inspired by:** Cloudspire (chips as dual-function resources/units)

**Order Token Commitment:**

When a faction places an Order token and the roll **fails outright** (no successes), the Order token is not returned. It is expended — the action was attempted and wasted resources.

- **Standard orders (Govern, Muster, March, Trade):** Failed rolls: Order token consumed. Faction has one fewer order available next season (recovered at year-end).
- **Unique Powers:** Never consumed on failure (they represent decisive institutional moments; even failure has a result).
- **Intel/Covert orders:** Consumed on Overwhelming Failure specifically (discovered, not just unsuccessful).

**Recovery:** Consumed tokens return at Annual Year-End Accounting. Maximum tokens lost per season: 2 (a faction cannot be reduced below 3 Order tokens in play by this mechanic).

**Design effect:** Failure has weight. A bad season is mechanically bad, not just narratively bad. Creates more careful decision-making about when to act.

### MP-20: WINTER ACCOUNTING — Pendragon Seasonal Collapse
**Addresses:** G-BG-07 (rhythm), campaign arc structure
**Inspired by:** Pendragon (Winter Phase — armies dissolve, characters return to fiefs, debts called)

**Winter Phase (every 4th round / Year End):**

After Annual Attrition (MP-07), before the next year's Season 1:

1. **Sustenance Check:** Each military unit costs 1 Prosperity from its controlling territory per year of active service. Territories below Prosperity 2 cannot sustain units — excess units are disbanded (controlling faction's choice which).

2. **Debt Calling:** Any outstanding Pledges (from MP-02 Deal Tokens) that have not been honored: automatically exposed. Betrayed factions may immediately claim Leverage +2.

3. **Thread Settling:** All Thread Resonance resets (already does). All Thread Debt tokens that have been outstanding for 2+ seasons: RS −1 each (the unpaid obligations compound).

4. **Stability Recovery:** Each faction may recover Stability +1 if they did not engage in military action this year and are not in active Heresy Investigation.

5. **Leader Age (optional — long campaigns only):** After every 5 years, leaders age. A leader at age threshold may retire (new leader = reset Conviction + new Deed Token sub-conditions appropriate to new leader). This is a Pendragon-inspired legacy element for campaign play only.

## CROSS-MECHANIC INTEGRATION NOTES

Some of these proposals interact significantly:

| Pair | Interaction | Recommendation |
|------|------------|----------------|
| MP-11 (Lead/Follow) + MP-01 (Mandate Dice) | Both modify action economy. Using both would be complex. | Choose one. Lead/Follow is lower-complexity. |
| MP-12 (Thread Debt) + MP-04 (Thread Resonance) | Thread Debt tokens increase TR for the owing faction (+1 TR per outstanding token). | Combine: Thread Debt increases Thread Resonance, which has gameplay effects. Natural coupling. |
| MP-13 (Order Fatigue) + MP-19 (Token Commitment) | Both penalize poor play patterns. Using both would be punishing. | Choose one. Token Commitment is more dramatic; Order Fatigue is more strategic. |
| MP-16 (Belief/Conviction) + MP-18 (Structural Asymmetry) | Conviction Crisis pairs naturally with Revolution Presence and Church TC mechanics. | Compatible — implement together. |
| MP-14 (Reaction Economy) + MP-11 (Lead/Follow) | Leverage tokens and Follow bonuses both respond to opponent actions. | Compatible. Leverage = defensive response; Follow = opportunistic alignment. |
| MP-20 (Winter Accounting) + MP-07 (Annual Attrition) | Both fire at year-end. | Merge into single Year-End Phase sequence: Attrition → Sustenance → Debt → Thread Settling → Stability Recovery. |

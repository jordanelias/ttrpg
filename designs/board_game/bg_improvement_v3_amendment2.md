## CORRECTION 1: RISKBREAKERS — COMPLETE REDESIGN

### The Error

v3 mechanized Riskbreakers as Crown mercenary tokens, hire-able by other factions when Crown weakens. This is wrong on both identity and mechanics.

### Canonical Identity

Riskbreakers are an extralegal constitutional order — unwaveringly dedicated to Valoria's integrity, institutional health, and right direction as a country. They operate beneath Crown and Löwenritter in formal hierarchy but carry their own agenda that supersedes both. They are not coup-planners. They do not target the royal family. But violence, blackmail, and theft are all within their mandate if the outcome serves Valoria. They manufacture circumstances — not plans.

The key distinction: they serve **Valoria as an idea**, not the Crown as an institution, not Almud as a person. If Almud were betraying Valoria, Riskbreakers would manufacture conditions to correct that — without ever drawing a blade against him personally.

### Redesigned BG Mechanics

**Riskbreaker Status:** Always NPC-controlled. Never hire-able by any faction. Cannot be targeted by other factions' actions directly (they have no public presence to target). Their operations are always Covert — other factions observe effects, not cause.

**Riskbreaker Pool:** 3 tokens per year (refresh each Year at Accounting). Each token = one extralegal intervention.

**Riskbreaker Priority Tree (evaluated at Accounting, in strict order):**

| Priority | Condition | Intervention |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | Vaynard has an active manufactured Casus Belli | Riskbreakers expose the fabrication. CB fails automatically next season. Varfell gains +1 Standing from all factions (the deception is known). Varfell also knows someone found them — VTM (see Correction 3) does not advance this season. |
| 2 | Civil War is active AND was triggered by a manufactured event (Vaynard CB) | Riskbreakers de-escalate: one faction's Military order in the most contested territory next season resolves at +2 Ob. Parliament Integrity +1 (manufactured legitimacy). |
| 3 | Church has seized a Crown-controlled territory AND TC > 60 | Riskbreakers sabotage Church governance: the territory's Prosperity −1 next season; Church Govern order there at +1 Ob (the population is quietly resisting). |
| 4 | Löwenritter Coup Counter = 3 AND condition 4 (Crown formal submission to foreign power) is approaching | Riskbreakers intercept: the diplomatic action that would trigger Condition 4 fails (the envoy never arrives; the document disappears; the meeting is disrupted). Standing consequences absorbed by no faction — it simply doesn't happen. |
| 5 | Any faction's Military order targets a civilian community with Restoration Presence | Riskbreakers intervene: military order at +1 Ob; if order fails, Restoration gains Presence in an adjacent territory (the community was warned). |
| 6 (default) | None of the above | Riskbreakers observe. At next season's start, Crown player receives 1 revealed intelligence: the hidden stat of whichever faction Riskbreakers determined was acting most against Valoria's interests this season. |

**What Riskbreakers will NOT do:**
- Target Almud or any member of the royal family directly
- Work toward any faction's political victory (including Crown's)
- Act publicly — if they did, they would become political actors, which destroys their operational value
- Fire when Civil War serves Valoria's long-term interests (they are consequentialists about Valoria, not process)

**Riskbreaker + Crown Tension:**  
If Almud pursues his Dominion path (see Correction 2) and overreaches, Riskbreakers may evaluate Almud's centralizing as a threat to Valoria's institutional health. At Almud Mandate 6+ (maximum) with Emergency Powers issued 2+ times: Riskbreakers' Priority 2 fires against Crown's military orders rather than other factions' — they obstruct Crown's own consolidation. This is the most uncomfortable Riskbreaker state: protecting Valoria from its own king.

**Riskbreaker + Vaynard Tension:**  
Vaynard is the Riskbreakers' primary target. A patient, thread-capable noble who manufactures civil war conditions and pursues personal power through Thread mastery is exactly the threat Riskbreakers exist to counter. Over a long game, Vaynard and the Riskbreakers are in persistent shadow conflict: Vaynard manufactures; Riskbreakers expose or intercept; Vaynard tries again with better cover. This escalation is tracked on the shared ledger: each failed Varfell CB that Riskbreakers exposed reduces the cost of the next Riskbreaker intervention against Vaynard by 1 token (they've built the file).

## CORRECTION 2: CROWN — ALMUD'S CENTRALIZING AGENDA

### The Error

v3 framed Crown's victory as constitutional maintenance. Almud's actual intent is total state supremacy: Church, Varfell, and Hafenmark all submitting to the Crown apparatus.

### Mechanical Amendments

**Almud's Conviction:** *"The state is the only legitimate vessel of order; everything must flow through it or be consumed by it."*  
[EDITORIAL NOTE: This is a mechanically derived proposal. User to confirm or replace.]

**Revised Crown Victory Conditions:**

- **Primary — Constitutional Stability:** Unchanged from v2. 5 Deed Tokens + PI ≥ 3. This is the defensive victory: Almud held the order together.
- **New Alternate — Dominion:** At any point, if Crown achieves: controls ≥ 8 territories AND one of the following Submission Conditions is met, Crown wins immediately.

**Submission Conditions (need only one):**
| Condition | How Achieved | Standing Cost |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| Church recognizes Crown supremacy over religious appointments in Crown territories | Church player accepts via Deal Token (Open Pledge); or TC drops to 0 | +2 Standing from Church; Baralta Mandate trigger |
| Varfell formally subordinates Intel operations to Crown oversight | Varfell loses Intel capacity (Research Track ≤ 1) OR Crown captures Vaynard + successful Govern order in all Varfell territories | +1 Standing from Varfell |
| Hafenmark surrenders Parliamentary independence | PI drops to 0 OR Baralta's Mandate Compromise count ≥ 4 | +2 Standing from Hafenmark; Riskbreaker Priority 2 fires against Crown |

**This Alternate path is the most destabilizing victory available in the game.** Achieving it generates maximum Standing against Crown and fires Riskbreaker intervention — even the peace-keepers balk at total centralization.

**Almud's Institutional Mandate (revised):** *"The monarchy provides the order that protects Valoria — and all other institutions serve the monarchy's order."*  
The second clause is new and critical. It reframes Crown's Mandate triggers:
- Church refuses a Crown directive in Crown territory: Mandate trigger (always was)
- Varfell operates Intel without Crown awareness in Crown territory: Mandate trigger (NEW)
- Hafenmark Parliamentary ruling contradicts Crown decree: Mandate trigger (NEW)
- Any faction allies with Altonia without Crown approval: Mandate trigger (NEW)

**Crown Policy addition — Supremacy Decree:**  
Once per game only. Requires Mandate ≥ 5.  
Effect: One named faction must respond to Crown's next order as if Crown's Mandate were 2 higher than actual (factored into all opposed rolls this season). The named faction's player knows they're being pressured; they do not know by what margin.  
Downside: all other factions gain +1 Standing against Crown (visible overreach). Riskbreakers evaluate whether this is legitimate centralization or despotism.

## CORRECTION 3: HAFENMARK / BARALTA — PROTESTANT REFORM MODEL

### The Error

v3 framed Baralta's path as "Church Absorption" — a political consumption of the hierarchy. This is wrong. Baralta's challenge to the Church is **theological**, not territorial. She believes in direct divine access for the sufficiently faithful (Protestant model). The Church's papal structure — in which only the Church mediates God's presence — is what she opposes. Her political power is the instrument; the reformation of doctrine is the goal.

Additionally, Baralta is not primarily a power-seeker. She is deeply faithful, increasingly dissatisfied with Almud's dedication to Solmund, and believes she may be more fit to lead Valoria precisely because she takes its theological foundations more seriously than its king does.

### Mechanical Amendments

**Baralta's Conviction (proposed):** *"Faith is not mediated — it is lived. Anyone who is truly faithful can hear Solmund. Anyone who cannot should not rule."*  
[EDITORIAL: BG-E-51 — confirm or replace. This is a mechanical derivation from canon description.]

**Rename and Reframe: Ecclesiastical Claim → Reformed Doctrine Track (RDT)**

The track remains 0–6 but its meaning changes entirely. RDT does not measure Baralta's accumulation of Church power. It measures the spread and credibility of Reformed Doctrine in the peninsula — the idea that any sufficiently faithful person may access Solmund directly, without ecclesiastical mediation.

**Gaining RDT (revised):**
- Senator card (Social, Outward) in a non-Church territory: theological outreach to communities. Ob 2. Success: RDT +1. (Baralta's people are spreading the word, not lobbying the hierarchy.)
- TC rises above 50 AND Baralta has a unit in that territory: RDT +1. (The Church's overreach validates the Reformed argument — people see it.)
- Crown issues Emergency Powers without Parliamentary check: RDT +1. (Baralta's dissatisfaction with Almud's dedication hardens into theological critique — this king is not Solmund's vessel.)
- Any faction Compromises their Institutional Mandate publicly: RDT +1. (Institutional moral failures strengthen the Reformed argument that existing structures are unworthy.)

**Losing RDT (revised):**
- Church Excommunicates Baralta: RDT −2 (the papal model strikes back; some communities are scared off)
- Baralta Compromises her own Mandate: RDT −2 (she cannot preach direct faithful access while behaving faithlessly — her followers notice)
- RS drops below 30: RDT −1 per season (a failing world makes radical theology feel dangerous, not liberating)

**RDT Effects by Level (revised):**

| RDT | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| 0–1 | No mechanical effect |
| 2 | TC gains this season are halved (rounded down) — Reformed communities resist theocratic consolidation |
| 3 | Church's Inquisition Autonomy threshold rises: Attention Pool must reach 8 (not 7) to trigger autonomous escalation (Reformed communities don't report practitioners to the Church) |
| 4 | TC is capped at current level + 5 for the remainder of the game — the Church cannot accelerate past this ceiling. TC may still rise from existing mechanics but any rise beyond the cap is ignored. |
| 5 | Reformed Settlement available (see below) |
| 6 | Reformed Mandate — Baralta has achieved theological legitimacy exceeding the Church's. All other factions' Diplomatic actions targeting Hafenmark: +1 Ob (no one wants to be seen opposing the Reformed movement). |

**Reformed Settlement (RDT 5, once per game):**  
Baralta formally declares the theological right of direct faithful access. The Church must respond:
- **Church Resists:** TC +3 (crusade response); but RDT +1 (martyrdom effect — persecution validates the movement). Hafenmark gains +1 Standing from Church; +1 Deed Token (theological courage).
- **Church Accommodates:** TC −5. Church Mandate triggers. Himlensendt loses 1 Renown. But: Church Stability −1 (internal schism begins — Cardinals disagree). [EDITORIAL: BG-E-50 — Cardinal schism mechanics.]
- **Church Ignores:** TC unchanged. RDT +1 next season (the silence is deafening). The Reformed movement grows in the vacuum.

**Reformed Settlement does not destroy the Church.** It creates a parallel theological legitimacy. The Church remains. What changes: its claim to exclusive divine access is now contested at a level that has structural mechanical consequences. This is Baralta's victory path — not eliminating the Church but rendering its papal model incoherent.

**Baralta's Scrutiny of Almud:**  
New mechanic — **Theological Dissatisfaction (TD), private track, 0–5.**

Baralta has been watching Almud. Each season, her player privately records whether one of the following occurred:
- Almud issued Emergency Powers: TD +1
- Crown submitted to Altonian pressure without resistance: TD +1
- Crown did not respond to a Church incursion into Crown territory: TD +1
- Almud issued Parliamentary Session (genuine governance): TD −1 (credit where due)
- Crown collaborated with Hafenmark on policy: TD −1

**TD effects:**
| TD | Effect |
|----|--------|
| 0–2 | No effect |
| 3 | Baralta may challenge Almud's divine right in Diplomacy: +1D to Hafenmark's Diplomatic actions targeting Crown (theological standing). |
| 4 | Baralta may declare Almud unworthy of Solmund's blessing — publicly. Crown loses 1 Deed Token; Hafenmark gains 1 Standing token against Crown (visible challenge). Riskbreakers do NOT intervene (this is a theological dispute, not an extralegal threat). |
| 5 | Baralta may issue a Rival Succession Claim: Hafenmark gains a permanent +1D to all orders in Crown-adjacent territories (contested legitimacy makes Crown governance less effective there). If Civil War fires, Hafenmark does not incur Standing penalties for seizing Crown territories. |

**TD is known only to the Hafenmark player** (tracked privately on faction mat). Other players observe the effects but not the score. This preserves the fiction of Baralta's "watching" — she has been building a case for years that no one else can see.

**Hafenmark Victory (revised):**
- **Path A — Reformed Valoria:** Reformed Settlement completed + PI ≥ 3 at game end + 3 Deed Tokens. (Baralta reformed the Church's theological structure and maintained constitutional governance.)
- **Path B — Theological Supremacy:** RDT = 6 + TD = 5 + 2 Deed Tokens. (The movement is complete and Almud has been found unworthy — Baralta leads with theological legitimacy even if Almud still sits the throne.)
- **Path C — Parliamentary Consolidation:** Unchanged from v2 (4 Deed Tokens + PI ≥ 4). (Procedural path — Baralta works within the system when the theological path is blocked.)

## CORRECTION 4: VARFELL / VAYNARD — THREAD MASTERY AS PERSONAL POWER

### The Error

v3 treated Vaynard's Southernmost interest as purely strategic (control the territory, extract political leverage). The canon establishes something more significant: Vaynard may be personally Thread-sensitive and potentially powerful. He is actively pursuing Threadwork, not just researching it from the outside. His Thread capacity may become a direct instrument of manipulation — altering conditions around him to secure personal power.

### Mechanical Amendments

**Vaynard's Thread Mastery Track (VTM, 0–5) — Varfell Exclusive**

Private track on faction mat. Represents Vaynard's personal Thread development — his increasing sensitivity, competence, and willingness to use Thread operations as a tool of political power.

**Gaining VTM:**
- Tribune (Intel) card placed on Thread-active territory (any territory with TR ≥ 2): VTM +1. (Vaynard studies what he senses.)
- Southernmost Expedition season completed while Varfell has a unit in T13: VTM +1. (Exposure deepens sensitivity.)
- Thread Debt incurred by any faction: VTM +1. (Vaynard perceives the debt's effect on the substrate; each incursion teaches him.)
- Successful Patience + CB combination in a territory with RS environmental effects: VTM +1. (He learns to manufacture circumstances that leverage the substrate's own pressure.)
- Riskbreakers expose a Vaynard CB: VTM −1. (Being caught sets him back — the cover operations he uses for Thread work are compromised.)

**VTM Zone Access (replaces Intelligence Network qualifier from v3):**

| VTM | Southernmost Access | Additional Capability |
|-----|--------------------|-----------------------|
| 0–1 | None | — |
| 2 | First Layer (personal sensitivity) | Varfell's TR is never capped below 3, regardless of practitioner presence |
| 3 | Deep Layer | Once per game: Vaynard may preview the top 2 Co-Movement cards before an order; keep either, return the other |
| 4 | Deep Layer without Restoration guide | Patience Protocol maximum increases to 6. Casus Belli Ob reduced by 1. |
| 5 | Core zone (without needing Restoration Presence) | Once per game: choose the outcome of one Co-Movement card draw. This is Vaynard directly manipulating the substrate for personal advantage. This ALWAYS produces a Thread Debt token (you cannot direct the substrate without consequence). RS −1 immediately. |

**VTM and Canon Compliance:**  
Vaynard's Thread use is personal power extraction, not healing. Every VTM operation that advances his political position degrades the substrate slightly (Thread Debt, RS pressure). At VTM 5, his ability to direct co-movement is canon-violating in the most interesting way: P-14 (co-movement is universal and uncontrollable) is being bent by someone with sufficient sensitivity and will. The Thread Debt token is the price — and Vaynard will pay it, because he believes the leverage is worth the cost to the world.

**VTM and the Riskbreakers:**  
A Varfell player who reaches VTM 4+ is now operating at a level the Riskbreakers cannot easily counter. Their Priority 1 (expose CB) still works. But at VTM 4, Vaynard's CBs are harder to detect (Ob to expose: +1). At VTM 5: Vaynard may, once per game, spend his co-movement direction to suppress the Riskbreaker Priority 1 response for one season — he sees them coming and redirects. This is the game's most dangerous individual: a politically patient noble with personal Thread power using the substrate as a tool for territorial acquisition.

**Vaynard's Conviction (proposed):** *"The strongest thread is the one others cannot see — and I have spent my life learning to pull it."*  
[EDITORIAL: BG-E-53 — confirm or replace.]

**Varfell Victory (revised):**
- **Path A — Intelligence Hegemony:** Unchanged from v3.
- **Path B — Southernmost Dominion:** Control T12–T13 + VTM ≥ 3 + Southernmost Expedition complete (any zone). Vaynard has claimed the site of the Calamity as his personal domain of power. RS +5 still applies to all — but Varfell gains +2 Deed Tokens and permanent territorial claim recognition from all other factions (no one can contest T12–T13 without +2 Ob).
- **New Path C — Thread Supremacy:** VTM = 5 + RS ≥ 50 (the substrate is stable enough that his manipulation hasn't destroyed it) + 3 territories including T13. Vaynard has become the most powerful Thread actor in the peninsula while maintaining political credibility. **This is the hardest victory in the game.** It requires: patience, intelligence, Thread development, and not destroying the world in the process. It also requires RS ≥ 50, which means Vaynard must simultaneously be exploiting the substrate and not breaking it. This tension is the point.

**VTM is partially public:** VTM 3+ is visible to all players (Vaynard's Thread operations become observable — co-movement effects cluster around him; other factions' TR in his territory rises). VTM 0–2 is private.

## CORRECTION 5: CHURCH — TRANSNATIONAL STRUCTURE AND THEOCRATIC CLAIM

### The Error

All prior documents treat the Church as a peninsula-internal faction. The Church operates across both Altonia and Valoria. This transnational structure links TC and IP in a way no prior document mechanized. The Church's theocratic claim is also more aggressive than framed — it actively argues that secular rulers lack divine sanction.

### Mechanical Amendments

**Church Transnational Presence:**  
The Church has established authority in Altonia's religious institutions. This creates a bidirectional pressure relationship between TC and IP.

**New mechanic — Altonian Ecclesiastical Relationship (AER), tracked on shared ledger, 0–5:**  
AER represents the Church's standing with Altonian power. Starts at 2.

| AER | TC Effect | IP Effect |
|-----|-----------|-----------|
| 0–1 | TC gains not modified by Altonia | IP escalates normally |
| 2 (start) | Neutral | Neutral |
| 3 | TC environmental effects: +1 level (Church influence in Altonia reinforces its domestic authority) | IP: the Altonian concern for Church welfare delays military action — IP threshold for vanguard rises from 76 to 80 |
| 4 | TC: any TC gain > 3 in one season is treated as TC +4 (Altonia is actively boosting Church consolidation) | IP: Altonia will not invade while AER ≥ 4 (they are protecting their Church interests, not threatening the peninsula) |
| 5 | Church may invoke Altonian pressure: once per game, any faction's order targeting Church interests automatically fails (Altonia sends a diplomatic ultimatum) | IP: immediately set to 50 and held there (Altonia is watching but not threatening — they want the Church preserved) |

**Gaining AER:**
- TC crosses a threshold (30, 50, 70): AER +1 each time (Church's domestic success bolsters its Altonian prestige)
- Church successfully opens a Heresy Investigation that results in Restoration Presence removed: AER +1 (heresy suppression is valued in Altonia)
- Church issues Interdict on a territory: AER +1 (decisive action)

**Losing AER:**
- TC drops below a threshold in any season: AER −1
- Church Stability drops below 3: AER −1 (institutional weakness signals trouble)
- Reformed Settlement occurs (Baralta's RDT 5): AER −2 (a rival theological model threatens Altonian ecclesiastical partnerships)

**The Theocratic Claim — new Mandate trigger:**  
Each season that TC is above 50: Church Mandate fires automatically regarding secular rulers. The Church player must choose:
- **Assert:** Issue a public statement that the ruling monarch/governance structure lacks Solmund's ear. TC +1. All secular faction leaders' Mandate triggers fire (Crown, Hafenmark — both have mandates that respond to Church authority claims). Riskbreakers evaluate: Priority 3 becomes more likely.
- **Suppress:** The Church's institutional voice moderates this season. TC +0 (no forced gain). AER −1 (Altonian contacts want the Church to be bolder). Himlensendt's Renown −1 (seen as weak by internal hardliners).

This is a mandatory choice with no neutral option. The Church's theocratic belligerence generates automatic friction with every secular faction every season above TC 50. There is no stable equilibrium — the Church must keep asserting or lose internal coherence.

**Church + Baralta (Reformed Doctrine) conflict:**  
When RDT ≥ 3 AND TC > 50, both the Assert and Suppress choices generate compounding effects:
- Assert + RDT ≥ 3: Baralta's communities actively counter-assert. TC gain from Assert is halved. But AER −1 (Altonia sees the Church as challenged domestically).
- Suppress + RDT ≥ 3: RDT +1 (the silence validates the Reform argument). TC −1 (Church loses domestic authority without asserting).

The Church cannot win this confrontation with Baralta through standard play. It must either Excommunicate Baralta (risks RDT +1 — martyrdom effect) or find a way to discredit the Reformed movement before it reaches RDT 5.

**Church Victory (revised):**
- **Primary — Holy State:** TC ≥ 70 + control Valorsplatz + Himmelenger + AER ≥ 3 (the conquest must be internationally recognized). Unchanged target; AER condition added.
- **New Alternate — Dual Theocracy:** TC ≥ 60 + AER = 5 + IP ≤ 30 (Altonia is a partner, not a threat). The Church achieves theological governance over both territories without military dominance — a softer but more stable theocratic settlement. This requires diplomatic mastery rather than TC racing.

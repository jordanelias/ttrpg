### 2. Sub-Modifier System

Origins, Formations, and Vocations that span multiple geographic or institutional variants now include **sub-modifiers** — a secondary selection within the stage that adjusts one skill, one Knot detail, or one narrative element. Sub-modifiers do not add a full new History; they specialize the existing one.

Format: `[Origin] + [Modifier]` → e.g. "1C. Fjord Child + Southern (Oastad)" adjusts Certainty to 3 and adds Calamity Familiarity to the spark list.

### 3. Literacy Model

| Formation | Literacy Level |
|---|---|
| 2A Church Schooling | Full literacy (starting skill) |
| 2B Ducal Household | Full literacy (tutors provide it; not a skill slot — assumed) |
| 2C Guild Apprenticeship | Trade literacy (contracts, ledgers, guild marks; not scripture or literature) |
| 2D Self-Taught | Illiterate unless backstory specifies otherwise; literacy available as spark |
| 2E Military Training | Functional literacy (can read orders and dispatches; not much else) |
| 2F Practitioner Mentorship | Depends on mentor; typically full literacy (philosophical texts required) |
| 2G Monastic Seclusion | Full literacy (monastic libraries) |

## REVISED ORIGINS

### 1A. Crown Heartland Child — REVISED

Born in the eastern lowlands. **The Crown heartland is the granary that feeds the peninsula.** Hafenmark cannot feed itself (ED-054). The western fjords are marginal farmland. Without Feldmark and Kronmark, the peninsula starves. You grew up understanding food as politics — not abstractly but as the rhythm of your family's labour.

**Modifier: River District (Valorsplatz)**
Born along the river-sea junction. Trade, not agriculture. Schoenland ships dock at your doorstep. Replace Heartland Roots with **River Mouth** (unique): +1D on Domain Actions involving Schoenland or eastern maritime trade. Your Knot shifts to a dockside trader or river-barge family member.

**Modifier: Breadbasket (Feldmark/Kronmark)**
Born on the productive farmland. Replace River Knowledge with **Harvest Leverage** (unique): +1D on Domain Actions or investigation rolls involving food supply, agricultural economics, or territorial Prosperity tracks. You understand the political weapon your family grows.

**Starting skill (1 of 3 on spark list):** **Heartland Roots** — In Crown-held territories: +1D on Domain Actions involving public order, community relations, or supply logistics. *You know this country's bones.*

**Revised Knot:** A parent or sibling who still works the land or the river — **and who depends on the political stability you are now entangled in.** When the Crown's Mandate drops, your family's safety drops with it. This Knot costs you something specific: every factional action that weakens the Crown threatens the person who raised you.

### 1D. Southern Einhir Descendant — CANON FIX

**Error corrected:** Previous version attributed cultural erosion to "the Forgetting." Per calamity_radiation.md, the Forgetting is a permanent condition of Askeheim (T15) only — it does not radiate. What eroded Einhir cultural knowledge in southern communities at node distance 1-2 is **245 years of institutional suppression by the Church** (heresy declarations, text confiscation, practice prohibition, forced conversion of naming conventions) **and the natural attrition of oral tradition across ten generations without institutional support.**

**Revised narrative:** Your grandparents' grandparents spoke a different language. The Church declared those words heretical. The texts were confiscated or burned. The practices were prohibited under pain of investigation. What survives does so because it was encoded as folk habit — songs sung without understanding their words, gestures performed at births and deaths that feel necessary but cannot be explained, a persistent unease about the Solmund narrative that has no articulable alternative. This is not the Forgetting (a metaphysical mechanism localised to the wound). This is cultural genocide by institutional authority, sustained across 245 years, partially successful, never complete.

**Starting Certainty revision:** 2 (Skeptic) uniformly, not 2-3. The institutional suppression is the same across the southern communities. What varies is Thread Sensitivity, not Certainty.

**Modifier: Askeheim-Adjacent (T6 Stillhelm / T13 Oastad — node distance 1)**
Add to spark list: **Wound Proximity** (unique): When RS drops below the Strained band (79-60), you are the first non-Askeheim character to experience instability effects. +1D on all Attunement rolls in node distance 1 territories AND you automatically detect the shift when RS crosses a threshold band — you feel it as physical unease before anyone announces it. Your body has been calibrated to the wound since birth.

**Modifier: Deep South (settlements near Askeheim — node distance 0-1)**
Inherited Resonance TS bonus increased to +8 (from +5). Starting Certainty drops to 1 (Transitional). Your family lived closer to the wound. The cultural memory is stronger — and stranger. Songs that produce involuntary weeping. Gestures that calm animals too effectively. An uncle who could find water by walking until his hands tingled.

### 1F. Urban Underclass (Valorsplatz) — REBUILT

Born in Valorsplatz — the capital, the river-sea junction, the peninsula's largest city — but not in the Crown's institutional orbit. You grew up in the **Tideward** (the flood-prone lower districts between the river and the eastern harbour) or the **Ashmarket** (the inland slum built over the ruins of a pre-occupation Einhir quarter whose foundations occasionally produce objects that should not exist).

**Who raised you:** The capital's economy flows through your neighbourhood but never stops. The river trade feeds the Crown; you eat what falls off the carts. The Schoenland merchants hire your older siblings as dockside labourers; you steal from the crates they unload. The Crown magistrate's jurisdiction ends at the Tideward bridge. The Church runs a charitable school in the Ashmarket — you may have attended long enough to learn letters, or you may have stolen bread from its kitchen instead.

**Starting Certainty:** 3 (Questioning). The Church's charity is conditional and the Crown's justice is selective. Neither institution has earned your faith. But neither has lost it entirely — you are too pragmatically engaged with survival to have developed a philosophical position.

**Modifier: Tideward (river/harbour district)**
Starting skill: **Tideward Rats** (unique) — +1D on all Investigation rolls in Valorsplatz. You know the city's physical infrastructure — the sewers, the warehouses, the smuggling routes, the back doors of buildings whose front doors face the Crown. Additionally: when Niflhel operates in Valorsplatz, you recognise their supply chain movements (they use the same routes you do) — +1D to detect Niflhel activity specifically.

**Modifier: Ashmarket (inland slum, built on Einhir foundations)**
Starting skill: **Foundations** (unique) — Once per arc, an object from the pre-occupation Einhir quarter surfaces through the Ashmarket's unstable ground. Roll Cognition TN 7 Ob 2. Success: the GM provides one piece of information about the Einhir presence that predated the current city. This is not the Forgetting — the Ashmarket is at node distance 3, well outside the Forgetting zone. This is simple archaeology: things buried 245+ years ago occasionally become unburied. You have been finding these objects since childhood. You do not understand their significance. You understand their market value.

**Revised Knot:** A **specific person** whose survival is entangled with yours — not a generic "street mentor" but one of: (a) a sibling who works Niflhel's dockworker arm and doesn't know you know, (b) a Church charity teacher who taught you to read and whose faith you both respect and resent, (c) a child you are responsible for (younger sibling, orphan you took in) who represents the life you are trying to escape and the obligation you cannot discharge. Each of these implies a dilemma, a cost, and a breaking point.

### 1H. Itinerant / Orphan — REBUILT AS MODIFIERS

The generic orphan is replaced by **specific Valorian displacement categories.** You were not simply "born somewhere, raised everywhere." Something specific produced your displacement, and that something is Valorian.

**1H-i. Occupation Displaced**
Your family was displaced during the Altonian occupation's aftermath — the post-liberation resettlement that redistributed population, seized collaborator property, and destroyed communities that had accommodated the occupiers. You were born into a family that lost everything not because of what they did but because of where they lived when the borders changed.

Starting Certainty: 3. Starting skill: **Nowhere to Return** (unique) — No ethical framework modifier on Domain Actions (as before), PLUS +1D on any roll involving displacement, refugee communities, or post-conflict resettlement. You recognise the pattern because you are the pattern.

Knot: A member of another displaced family — someone who shares your specific landlessness and whose trajectory diverged from yours (they joined Niflhel, or the Restoration, or the Church, or the Crown army — all pathways out of displacement).

**1H-ii. Calamity Orphan**
Your family was destroyed by a Calamity event — a Shifting Object that killed a parent, a rendering failure that made your home uninhabitable, an encounter with a threadcut being at the southern edge of your territory. The Church told you it was a natural disaster. Your body knows it was not.

Starting Certainty: 2 (Skeptic). Starting skill: **Scarred by the Unreal** (unique) — Immune to Composure loss and surprise-round penalties from Thread phenomena (as Shaken Certainty in Catalyst 4A, but from childhood exposure rather than a single adult event). Additionally: Thread Sensitivity +3 (the event primed your configuration).

Knot: A surviving family member who witnessed the same event and processed it differently — they accepted the Church's explanation (natural disaster) and your persistent insistence that something else happened has strained the relationship. They think you are traumatised. You think they are in denial.

**1H-iii. Church Ward**
You were raised in a Church orphanage. Your parents are unknown or dead. The Church fed you, housed you, and educated you — and you owe them everything, or you owe them nothing, depending on how the institution treated you specifically.

Starting Certainty: 4 or 5 (GM determines — did the orphanage produce faith or resentment?). Starting skill: **Institutional Child** (unique) — +1D on all rolls involving Church infrastructure, personnel, or procedures. You grew up inside the institution. You know its rhythms, its hierarchy, its blind spots. Whether you love or hate the Church, you understand it from the inside.

Knot: A fellow orphan — someone raised alongside you. They chose a different path (perhaps they stayed in the Church; perhaps they fled to Niflhel). The bond is pre-institutional — it exists because you shared a dormitory, not because you share a worldview.

## REVISED FORMATIONS

### 2D. Self-Taught / Street Education — REBUILT

**What it means in Valoria:** The Church controls formal education. The Guilds provide trade apprenticeships. Ducal households educate their retinues. If you were educated by none of these, you exist **outside the peninsula's entire epistemological apparatus.** You did not learn the Church's version of history. You did not learn the Guilds' version of value. You did not learn any faction's version of reality. What you learned, you learned by observing the world directly, which means your knowledge is empirical, fragmented, and uncontaminated by institutional framing.

This is not "street smarts." This is a fundamentally different relationship to knowledge. Church-educated characters process new information through Solmund's framework. Guild-educated characters process it through economic value. You process it through "does this match what I have directly observed?" This makes you uniquely resistant to institutional propaganda — and uniquely vulnerable to your own observational biases.

**Starting skill:** **Empiricist** (unique) — When presented with a claim that contradicts your direct observation: +1D to any roll to verify, investigate, or challenge the claim. Does not apply to claims about things you have not observed. You trust your eyes over anyone's authority — which is a strength against the Church's epistemological control and a weakness when your observation is incomplete.

**Certainty modifier:** −1 regardless of starting value (not just if starting at 5). Without institutional education, you have no framework that reinforces any Certainty level. Your Certainty is inherently unstable — it can move in either direction more easily because nothing institutional anchors it.

**Revised Knot:** Not "a street teacher" but a specific person who showed you something that contradicted institutional reality — a midwife who treated patients the Church said were cursed, a travelling merchant who described places the Church says do not exist, a veteran who told you the occupation ended differently than the official history claims. This person's specific knowledge gave you reason to trust observation over authority.

### 2E. Military Training — CANON CHECK

**Weapons Training TN −1 concern resolved:** The weapon TN matrix minimum is TN 5 (Short Light Blade — dagger). Weapons Training would reduce this to TN 4, which is below any existing TN value in the system. This creates a 60% base hit rate at TN 4, compared to 40% at TN 6 and 30% at TN 7.

**Revised skill:** **Weapons Training** (unique) — Choose one weapon combination (from the three-axis matrix: Reach × Weight × Type). With that weapon: **+1D to Offence allocation** (not TN −1). This avoids breaking the TN floor and provides a bonus that scales with pool size rather than changing the probability curve. A trained soldier with Agility 4 and this skill gets 1 extra die on Offence — meaningful but bounded.

## NEW VOCATIONS

### 3-CR5: Crown Farmer / Landholder
The agricultural working class of the Crown heartland. You produce the food that feeds the peninsula. This is not a prestigious Vocation — it is the most common one, and the most politically important.

**Skills:**
1. **Harvest Leverage** (unique) — +1D on Domain Actions or investigation involving food supply, territorial Prosperity, or agricultural economics. When the Crown controls Feldmark (T5), your knowledge of its productive capacity is strategically relevant. When it doesn't, your knowledge of what the Crown has lost is even more relevant.
2. **Seasons Reader** § — +1D on Cognition rolls to predict weather, assess terrain conditions, or evaluate agricultural viability. In mass battle: +1D on the general's Cognition roll to assess terrain before Phase 1.
3. **Community Backbone** § — In any territory you have lived in for 1+ seasons: +1D on Stability-related Domain Actions. Farmers are the institutional foundation that other factions build on.

**Knot:** The land itself — or rather, the community that works it. A specific neighbour, a tenant, a seasonal worker whose family has worked alongside yours for generations. When the territory changes hands, this person's survival depends on which faction takes control and what they do with the agricultural base.

### 3-CF5: Diplomat / Envoy
A professional intermediary between factions. Not a merchant (who trades goods) or a spy (who steals information) but someone whose job is to carry messages, negotiate agreements, and maintain relationships across factional lines.

**Skills:**
1. **Diplomatic Protocol** (unique) — In any social contest where you are formally representing a faction: ethical framework Ob penalties for your faction are reduced by 1 (minimum 0). You carry official status that partially overrides factional hostility.
2. **Read the Room** § — Appraise in formal settings: TN 6.
3. **Treaty Language** (unique) — +1D on Recall rolls involving prior agreements, treaties, or diplomatic precedent. When the Crown and Hafenmark dispute a constitutional clause, or the Church and Varfell contest a territorial boundary, you remember what was agreed and when.

**Knot:** Your counterpart — the diplomat from the other side. You negotiate against each other professionally and respect each other personally. If war breaks, this relationship becomes impossible to maintain and devastating to lose.

### 3-CF6: Independent Criminal
Not Niflhel. You work alone or in a small crew, outside any faction's structure. Petty theft, confidence games, burglary, smuggling on a personal scale. Niflhel recruits from people like you — and you have either refused, been rejected, or not yet been found.

**Skills:**
1. **Opportunist** (unique) — Once per scene: after any other character's roll is revealed, declare that you exploit the outcome. +2D on your next roll this scene that directly capitalises on what just happened (an opened door, a distracted guard, a revealed weakness). You don't plan. You react.
2. **Slippery** § — Escape actions: −1 Ob. Pursuit evasion in urban environments: +1D.
3. **Fence Contacts** § — +1D on rolls involving underground trade, stolen goods valuation, or information brokering with non-Niflhel criminal networks.

**Knot:** A mark who became a friend — or a friend who became a mark. The relationship is built on a deception that you have either confessed (and survived) or maintained (and dread the day it surfaces). Either way, this person knows you as someone you are not, and the gap between who they think you are and who you actually are generates persistent drama.

### 3-CH5: Church Investigator (Heresy)
You investigate heresy for the Church. Not a Templar (who fights heretics) but an investigator (who finds them). Your job requires you to understand what you are hunting — which means you have been exposed to Thread-adjacent phenomena, practitioner testimony, and Einhir artefacts in the course of your professional duties. You may have begun to doubt what you are doing. You may have become more certain. Either way, you know more than most Church officials about what Thread actually is — and the Church trusts you to contain that knowledge.

**Skills:**
1. **Inquisitorial Method** (unique) — +1D on Investigation rolls targeting individuals suspected of heresy, Thread practice, or Einhir cultural activity. You know the tells: the linguistic patterns, the behavioural shifts, the material traces that practitioners leave. Whether you use this knowledge to prosecute or to protect is your decision.
2. **Suppressed Exposure** (unique) — Thread Sensitivity +3 (occupational exposure). You have handled confiscated Einhir texts, interviewed practitioners, and examined artefacts. The Church trains you to process this exposure within doctrine. It mostly works. Your TS increase is a professional hazard, not a spiritual journey.
3. **Doctrinal Fluency** § — +1D on Solmund theology and Church history.

**Starting Certainty modifier from this Vocation:** −1 or +1 (GM determines). The investigation either confirmed your faith (the heretics are dangerous and the Church is right to suppress them) or eroded it (the things you've seen cannot be explained by doctrine and the Church knows this).

**Knot:** A subject of a past investigation — someone you investigated and either prosecuted or quietly released. If prosecuted: they are imprisoned, excommunicated, or dead, and you carry the weight of what you did to them. If released: they owe you their life and you owe them an explanation for why you broke protocol. Either way, the relationship is defined by the power asymmetry of investigator and subject — and by the knowledge you share that the Church does not know you share.

### 3-LO4: Löwenritter Patrol Officer (Threadcut Encounter Veteran)
You served on the northern border and encountered something the Order does not discuss publicly. A threadcut being — not a Gap emergence or a Shifting Object but an entity of sufficient configurational complexity to register as a presence. The encounter was brief, confusing, and officially classified as "anomalous wildlife." You know it was not wildlife. The Order knows you know.

**Skills:**

2. **Classified Knowledge** (unique) — You possess information the Löwenritter does not want shared. Once per arc: invoke this knowledge in a social contest or investigation for +2D. The information is specific (the GM determines what you know). Using it publicly risks exposure — the Order may respond.

**Starting Certainty modifier:** −1 (the encounter shook your rendering whether you admit it or not).

**Knot:** Your commanding officer who ordered you to file the false report ("anomalous wildlife"). They are protecting you by burying the truth. Or they are containing you by ensuring your account is officially discredited. You are not sure which. The relationship is defined by shared knowledge and mutual uncertainty about the other's intentions.

## REVISED CATALYSTS

### 4A. Witnessed Something Impossible — CANON FIX

**Forgetting interaction clarified:**
- If the event occurred **outside Askeheim** (node distance 1+): the Forgetting does not apply. Your memory of the event persists because rendering did not erase it. The skill "Obsessive Recall" reflects the event's vividness, not resistance to the Forgetting.
- If the event occurred **at Askeheim** (node distance 0): the Forgetting should have erased your memory. The fact that it did not means your Thread Sensitivity was high enough for partial retention (TS 10+ for emotional impressions, TS 20+ for factual retention, TS 40+ for ontological understanding — per Forgetting Check in stage4_southernmost). This is itself significant — it implies your TS is higher than you knew.

**Revised skill — Obsessive Recall:** +1D on Recall rolls pertaining to the specific event. If the event was at Askeheim: your TS is retroactively confirmed at 10+ minimum (the Forgetting failed to fully erase the memory, which is diagnostic). The GM may adjust your starting TS upward.

**Starting skill (granted at creation):** **Shaken Certainty** — Immune to Composure loss and surprise-round penalty from Thread phenomena. Certainty track movement toward 0 from Discovery Events costs 1 less. *The shock has already happened. Everything else is aftershock.*

### 4C. Lost Someone — ANTI-FRIDGE CLAUSE

**Design requirement:** The lost Knot is not a motivation engine. The GM must develop the lost person as a character with: (a) specific knowledge the player character does not possess, (b) unfinished relationships with NPCs the player will encounter, and (c) a perspective on the campaign's factional politics that would have influenced events if they were still present.

**Revised skill — Knot Memory:** +1D on rolls when invoking the lost person's specific knowledge, skills, or connections. **The GM maintains a hidden "ghost sheet"** for the lost person — a partial character record including their Beliefs, their faction affiliations, and their most important relationships. When the player uses Knot Memory, the GM draws from this sheet. The lost person continues to influence the campaign through the knowledge and relationships they left behind.

**Starting skill (granted at creation):** **Grief-Driven Focus** — Once per session, when pursuing an action directly related to the lost Knot: +2D. *Grief burns clean when it has direction.*

### 4F. War / Invasion — SPECIFIED

Replace generic "war" with the **specific conflicts available in the Valoria timeline** at campaign start (45 AG):

**4F-i. Spartfell Incident**
An Altonian probe force crossed the NW pass 3–5 years before campaign start. It was repelled at Spartfell (T10) by the Hafenmark border garrison with Löwenritter reinforcement. You were there — as a soldier, a civilian, or someone caught in the wrong place.

**4F-ii. Templar Suppression**
The Church deployed Templars against a southern community suspected of Einhir cultural revival, 2–4 years before campaign start. The community was partially destroyed. You were there — as a Templar, a community member, a bystander, or a Restoration organiser who witnessed the Church's willingness to use force against its own population.

A territorial dispute between Hafenmark and Varfell over the Spartfell-Halvardshelm border (T10-T11) escalated to armed skirmish 5–8 years before campaign start. No formal war was declared; both sides claim the other provoked it. You were on one side.

Each variant adjusts the Knot: Spartfell Incident → a comrade from the border garrison. Templar Suppression → a survivor or a Templar whose actions you witnessed. Ducal Border Clash → someone from the other side who you fought against and later encountered in peace.

### 4G. Religious Crisis — GM DETERMINES DIRECTION

**Revised:** The player does NOT choose whether the crisis moved them toward orthodoxy or Thread acceptance. **The GM presents what happened and the player lives with the mechanical result.** This matches 2G (Monastic Seclusion), where the GM determines whether contemplation deepened or cracked faith.

The GM chooses based on the character's Origin and Formation: a Himmelenger Child (1E) with Church Schooling (2A) who experiences a crisis is more likely to deepen toward orthodoxy (+2) — the institutional scaffolding is too strong for a single event to demolish. A Southern Einhir Descendant (1D) with Self-Taught education (2D) who experiences a crisis is more likely to crack toward Thread acceptance (−2) — no institutional scaffolding to hold.

## THREAD-ADJACENT ADDITIONS TO NON-PRACTITIONER VOCATIONS

The following spark list additions provide non-practitioner characters with Thread-responsive skills that activate when Thread phenomena occur near them. These are not practitioner abilities — they are cognitive, perceptual, or emotional responses that manifest in characters who have been exposed to Thread reality without training.

**Added to Crown Soldier (3-CR2) spark list:**
**Uncanny Discipline** — When a Thread phenomenon manifests during combat (Shifting Object, Gap, threadcut being): your unit does not suffer the Morale penalty that untrained soldiers take. Your training holds against the incomprehensible. +1D to Morale checks in the presence of Thread phenomena.

**Added to Templar (3-CH2) spark list:**
**Heresy Sense** — After prolonged anti-heresy service: +1D on Attunement rolls to detect active Thread operations within your zone. You were trained to find what the Church calls evil. The training works — you can sense it. Whether what you sense is actually evil is a question the Church does not want you to ask.

**Added to Highland Miner (3-HA2) spark list:**
**Deep Wrong** — In underground environments: +1D on Attunement rolls to sense Thread instability, geological anomalies linked to Calamity radiation, or configurational wrongness in stone. Miners at certain depths near the Calamity gradient report that the rock "feels wrong." You know what they mean.

**Added to Border Ranger (3-LO2) spark list:**
**Patrol Anomaly** — When a Thread phenomenon manifests in border/wilderness territory: +1D on Cognition rolls to assess whether the phenomenon is natural (weather, wildlife) or configurational (Thread-related). Border rangers encounter strange things; most dismiss them. You have learned to distinguish.

## SKILL LEVEL DESIGN PRINCIPLE

For all skills with Levels 2-3: **Level 2 enhances the effect quantitatively. Level 3 unlocks a new action or immunity.** This ensures that mastery (Level 3, requiring Recall 5+ and dice_bonus 5+) feels qualitatively different from competence (Level 1-2).

| Skill | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |

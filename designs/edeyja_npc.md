# NPC: Edeyja — Southernmost Warden-Chief
## Status: Canonical NPC (working name — pending final name pass with territory naming audit)
## Date: 2026-03-30
## Pronouns: she/her [EDITORIAL: confirm pronouns]

---

## Role
De facto leader of the Southernmost warden practitioners. The sole surviving lineage of trained Einhir practitioners whose ancestors remained at the Southernmost after the Calamity and have served as wardens ever since. Not a public figure. Not a political actor. The peninsula's factional landscape is, to her, noise.

---

## Background
Edeyja survived proximity to a major Gap/tear — a significant breach in the rendered fabric. The excess exposure damaged her chest and shoulder; threads themselves were affected at the point of contact, disrupting the local rendering of her body. She survived by Threadworking on herself, undoing as much of the excess damage as possible. The injury is permanent but managed; she bears visible evidence of the event (the affected area renders unusually — texture, color, or apparent depth wrong in ways that disturb non-practitioners and are immediately legible to anyone with TS 15+).

She is not defined by this injury. It is a fact of her history, not her identity.

---

## Stats (approximate — exact values for GM reference)
- **TS: 75–80** — the highest TS of any living practitioner in the setting. Benchmark for the ceiling of what is achievable. She did not reach this through formal instruction but through centuries of applied necessity: the Southernmost does not permit theoretical practitioners.
- **Coherence: 9** — sustained at high level through disciplined practice; she understands her own configuration deeply enough to maintain it under extreme conditions.
- **Cognition: 5, Focus: 5** — Thread operations at this TS require extraordinary attention and analytical precision.
- **Endurance: 4** — durable; the injury has not diminished her physical capacity.
- All social attributes: moderate (3–4). She is not a political or charismatic figure; she is a practitioner.

## Conviction and Resonant Style
- **Conviction:** Continuity. The work must continue regardless of who or what interferes. The peninsula survives only because the wardens hold.
- **Resonant Style:** Evidence. Show her the Thread state data. Show her what you've observed. Abstract appeals to political urgency, moral obligation, or personal stakes do not register. Demonstrated competence in Thread work does.

## The Warden Lineage
After the Calamity, the vast majority of Einhir practitioners were killed — either directly in the catastrophic working, through the subsequent Gap-spawned monstrosities, or through the collapse of the civilisation that sustained them. A small number stayed. Their purpose: contain the ongoing aftermath. Centuries of this work have produced a lineage of wardens — fewer each generation, because the Southernmost is lethal and the work is never finished.

The wardens do not recruit from the peninsula at large. They cannot — the Forgetting means no one on the peninsula knows what they are or what they do. Occasionally a practitioner from the north reaches sufficient TS and finds their way to Askeheim territory; the wardens assess them. Most are turned away (insufficient TS, insufficient discipline, or insufficient understanding of what the work actually demands). Edeyja has accepted perhaps a handful of outsiders in her lifetime.

**Current warden count: small (GM's discretion — single digits to low teens). This is functionally a dying institution.**

---

## Military Reality
High-TS practitioners working in coordinated Weaving are not a military problem — they are a military non-answer. This is why the Southernmost was never conquered even during Altonian colonisation of the peninsula. The Einhir practitioner network, before the Calamity, represented deterrence sufficient to make conquest irrational. Post-Calamity, the wardens are far fewer, but any military force that enters Southernmost territory will encounter Thread operations at a scale and precision that renders conventional force irrelevant. The wardens do not advertise this. It has simply never been necessary.

The peninsula's factions do not fully understand this. Varfell (Vaynard) suspects it. The Church does not want to know.

## Relationship to Restoration Movement
**Tangential.** The Restoration Movement's goals — Einhir cultural rehabilitation, political recognition, dismantling Church authority — are entirely secondary concerns from Edeyja's perspective. The peninsula's political order is irrelevant if the Southernmost continues to produce monstrosities that eventually overwhelm the wardens' capacity to contain them. Restoration is politically interesting; the ongoing Calamity aftermath is existentially urgent.

She is aware of Restoration. She has permitted limited contact. She has not endorsed or joined it. Individual Restoration-affiliated practitioners (Dahl Erikssen, TS 18) she would assess and probably dismiss as insufficiently developed for the actual work. She would not be hostile to them.

## PC Access
**Difficult but possible.** Access is gated by engagement with the Southernmost expedition content:

| Expedition progress | Access level |
|---|---|
| Askeheim border territory only | No contact. Wardens observe but do not approach. |
| Entered Southernmost (Season 2) | Brief, wary contact. She assesses the group. TS ≤ 20: dismissed. TS 21–39: acknowledged, given limited guidance. TS 40+: treated as potentially useful. |
| Reached primary site (Season 3) | Substantive contact. She will work alongside practitioners who demonstrate genuine competence at the site. |
| Successful Extraordinary Weaving (Season 4+) | Active collaboration. PCs are now doing work that matters. She will treat them as colleagues — not warmly, but with the respect of shared purpose. |
| Multiple successful Weaving seasons | She may share knowledge inaccessible anywhere else on the peninsula: the actual mechanism of the Calamity, the nature of the Locked Zones, the pre-Calamity warden methodology. This is campaign-altering information. |

**She never leaves the Southernmost.** Scenes with her always occur in Southernmost territory.

## GM Notes
- She is the setting's answer to "what does TS 75+ actually look like in practice." She should feel qualitatively different from any practitioner PCs have encountered: precision without effort, Thread perception that treats the environment as continuously legible, operations that are structurally inaccessible to anyone below TS 50.
- She is not hostile. She is occupied. These are different things. The PCs are an interruption she will tolerate if they prove useful.
- Her injury is a visible marker of what the Southernmost costs. GMs should use it as a recurring environmental detail — not dramatic, just present.
- **She cannot fix the Calamity alone.** This is the dramatic engine: the wardens have been holding for centuries and they are losing ground slowly. She needs help she cannot ask for from people who don't know she exists. The PC expedition is the first real possibility of external assistance in a very long time. She will not say this. It will be visible in how she responds when the work goes well.

## Canon Compliance
- **P-02 (Ein Sof = fullness):** The Southernmost damage is rendering failure, not absence. Edeyja's work addresses the failure at the substrate level — she understands this even if she lacks the philosophical vocabulary.
- **P-07 (Calamity = rendered-side):** Her survival of the Gap exposure demonstrates the Calamity's nature: the excess is a rendered-side phenomenon, and rendered-side expertise can partially address it.
- **P-13 (Forgetting = rendering failure):** She knows the Forgetting is not natural. The peninsula's inability to remember the Calamity is itself a symptom of the rendering damage. She does not know how to undo it.

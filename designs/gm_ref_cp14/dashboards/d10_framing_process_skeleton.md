# D-10 — CO-MOVEMENT FRAMING PROCESS
*For improvising consequences outside the matrix. Run when the situation has no close analogue in D-11.*

---

## THE FOUR-STEP PROCESS

### STEP 1 — IDENTIFY PRIMARY DIMENSION
What was the practitioner's intended target?

- **Primarily temporal:** the operation moves something in time (Past-Pull; temporal Weaving at Thread Sensitivity 50+; operations targeting duration, timing, or historical state)
- **Primarily epistemic:** the operation changes how something is known or perceived (Pulling attention/perception; operations targeting awareness, belief, memory accessibility)
- **Primarily actualized:** the operation changes something's physical or factual state (standard Weaving for healing or reinforcement; Lock; Dissolution)

*Most operations are primarily actualized. This is the default.*

---

### STEP 2 — IDENTIFY CURRENT STATE OF THE OTHER TWO DIMENSIONS
For this specific configuration (the [TARGET]):

**Temporal state:**
- How deep is its temporal accumulation?
- Recent creation = shallow temporal depth (few causal predecessors)
- Ancient or long-established = deep temporal depth (many causal predecessors; more resistant to temporal displacement)

**Epistemic state:**
- How accessible is it currently to consciousness?
- Hidden, secret, unknown = low epistemic accessibility (already partially removed from rendering)
- Prominent, documented, widely known = high epistemic accessibility (deeply embedded in shared rendering)

**Actualized state:**
- How stable is its current configuration?
- Fragile, contested, in process = low actualization (more susceptible to co-movement)
- Robust, settled, institutionally reinforced = high actualization (co-movement effects smaller; more resistant)

---

### STEP 3 — CO-MOVEMENT DIRECTION AND MAGNITUDE
The other two dimensions move in the same direction as the primary effect, proportionally.

**Direction:** If the primary effect moves toward coherence/stability → the other dimensions also move toward coherence/stability. If toward dissolution/loosening → the others also loosen.

**Magnitude:**
- Operation primarily targeting actuality (standard): minor temporal shift + minor epistemic shift
- Operation primarily targeting epistemic (Pulling attention): minor temporal shift + moderate actualized shift
- Operation primarily targeting temporality (Past-Pull): major actualized displacement + major epistemic disruption

**Practical formula:**
- *Primarily actualized Weave:* The target's temporal position compresses slightly (it arrived at this healed state earlier than the world expected). The target becomes slightly more epistemically accessible (clearer to those around it).
- *Primarily epistemic Pull:* The target's actualization loosens fractionally (it becomes slightly more susceptible to further change). Its temporal anchoring frays (the gap between when it "should" be and when it is widens).
- *Primarily temporal Past-Pull:* The target's actualization is significantly displaced (what was fixed is now uncertain). The epistemic structure of everyone who witnessed the original is disrupted (they hold two incompatible histories).

---

### STEP 4 — NARRATE AT APPROPRIATE Thread Sensitivity TIER

**Thread Sensitivity 0–29 (ontical surface only):**
What changes in the observable, sensory world. No reference to threads, rendering, or mechanism. The observer notices something changed, feels something, or experiences something — but has no framework for why.

**Thread Sensitivity 30–49 (ontical + partial ontological):**
What changes + a faint sense of the mechanism. The practitioner knows an operation occurred and perceives the primary effect clearly. The co-movement consequences register as a secondary awareness — something moved that they didn't intend to move — but without full comprehension of which dimension.

**Thread Sensitivity 50+ (full ontological):**
What changes + explicit awareness of all three dimensions moving. The practitioner perceives the co-movement in real time: the temporal displacement, the epistemic shift, and the actualized change as three distinct but unified movements of the same configuration. This is not three separate observations — it is one unified perception of the thread's inseparability.

---

## FOOTNOTES (MECHANICAL — do not alter)

**Temporal shift is not the target aging.** It is a displacement of when this configuration belongs in the causal chain. A healed wound does not age the patient — it places the healed state earlier in the patient's causal sequence than the natural sequence would have. The patient is the same age; the wound is in the past in a way that the wound's natural healing would not yet have achieved.

**Epistemic shift is not forgetting.** It is a change in how accessible this configuration is to consciousness. A Pulled belief is not forgotten — it becomes epistemically unstable. The holder can still recite it. They cannot access it as a lived commitment. The knowledge is propositionally present and experientially inert.

**Actualized shift is not destruction.** It is a change in the configuration's factual properties. A Locked door has not been destroyed — its possibility for change has been eliminated. It is more real than it was, not less. It now exists without the capacity to become otherwise.

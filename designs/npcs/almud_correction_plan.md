## What Is Wrong

All three NPC analysis documents characterize Almud as paralyzed, indecisive, and ineffective. ~30 instances across the files. This is:
1. **A cliché** — the Hamlet king (knows the truth, cannot act) is one of the most overused character types in political fiction.
2. **Inconsistent with his mechanical profile** — Conviction Order/Reason + Resonant Style Consequence + Crown Mandate 5 + Virtue Ethics framework describes a capable ruler, not a paralyzed one.
3. **A misread of his structural position** — governing a peninsula under simultaneous pressure from Altonia, Church, Hafenmark, Varfell, Löwenritter, Schoenland, and the Restoration Movement is not a position that permits indecision. A ruler who was actually indecisive under these pressures would have been deposed already (Ehrenwall's counter would have fired).

## What Should Replace It

**New characterization from user direction:**
- Almud suspects his father was assassinated [NEW LORE — not yet in any canonical source]
- His guardedness is informed by this suspicion — not paralysis but survival intelligence
- He has significant and decisive political acumen
- His position is structurally unable to bear the pressures — the problem is the job, not the man
- He is shrewd and guarded, not indecisive

**Historical anchor should change:** Charles V (who abdicated from irreconcilable contradictions) → someone who governed effectively under impossible pressure without the luxury of abdication. Candidates:
- **Manuel I Komnenos** — Byzantine emperor who managed Crusader states, Seljuk Turks, Norman Sicily, and Venice simultaneously through strategic marriages, intelligence operations, and selective military engagement. Never "won" — just kept the empire alive for 37 years through relentless political acumen. His critics called him passive. He was playing six boards at once.
- **Henry VII Tudor** — first Tudor king, throne won by conquest, surrounded by Yorkist claimants, managed Church/Parliament/nobility through a combination of financial shrewdness, strategic marriages, and institutional innovation. His guardedness (born from a lifetime as a fugitive) was his governance style. He trusted almost no one. He governed effectively.

**The Baralta comparison must be revised:** Not "she governs better." She governs a smaller problem with fewer enemies and calls it universalism. Almud manages 6+ simultaneous pressure vectors with genuine competence. His "inaction" on the Restoration is not paralysis — it is triage. The Restoration is one of seven problems. Acting on it would destabilize the solutions to the other six.

## ED-403, ED-406, ED-407 Resolutions (2026-04-11)

### ED-403: Vossen/RM Internal Split on Crown Patronage

**Resolution:** The RM splits internally if Lenneth's programme activates. Vossen is cautiously open — her idealism says any advancement matters, and Crown patronage is advancement. But the RM base includes rejectionists who have spent 45 years under a Crown that maintained the caste, and who see Crown patronage as co-optation, not liberation.

**Mechanical expression:** When Lenneth's Einhir revival programme produces a Domain Action or public policy change targeting Einhir cultural recognition, the RM faces an internal Stability check (RM Stability vs Ob 2).

- **Success:** RM maintains unity. Vossen negotiates cautious cooperation with Crown. RM Presence markers in Crown territories gain +1 (Crown patronage provides cover). RM Presence markers in Church territories unchanged (Church suppression continues regardless).
- **Partial:** RM cooperates but loses radical wing. RM Stability −1. Radical elements drift toward Vaynard's Varfell network. Varfell Influence +1 in territories where RM was present.
- **Failure:** RM splits. Two tracks: Crown-aligned cultural revival faction (led by Vossen, institutional, cautious) and radical autonomist faction (rejects all institutional patronage, aligns with Vaynard's expulsion agenda). The radical faction is smaller but more operationally dangerous — it has the people willing to act outside legal frameworks.

**Vossen's position if split occurs:** She leads the institutional faction. Her IDEALIST AI flaw means she spreads thin trying to hold both factions together before the split, and after the split she focuses on the cultural programme rather than consolidating. The radical faction gains operational independence.

### ED-406: Ehrenwall Succession Assessment

**Resolution:** Ehrenwall has identified Baralta as the strongest succession candidate by deed-logic metrics. Her assessment is professional:

- **Governance record:** Mandate 7, Ob 1 deviation. Strongest institutional command on the peninsula.
- **Institutional base:** Hafenmark parliamentary apparatus. Constitutional framework. Functioning bureaucracy.
- **Liability:** Hafenmark Military 3. Weakest major faction military. A Baralta Crown cannot defend itself without Löwenritter support.

**Ehrenwall's calculus:** She would support Baralta's succession IF Baralta commits to maintaining Löwenritter institutional independence and border defence priorities. This gives Ehrenwall leverage she does not currently have over Hafenmark. Currently, Hafenmark and Löwenritter have no structural relationship — Löwenritter serves the Crown, and Hafenmark is a rival. If Baralta takes the Crown, she needs Löwenritter. Ehrenwall's price: Löwenritter institutional preservation, military autonomy on border deployment, and no subordination of the Lions' Table to parliamentary oversight.

**Mechanical expression (BG):** If Almqvist line fails and Baralta is the succession candidate, Ehrenwall's Coup Counter mechanic transforms. Instead of counting Crown failures, it becomes a negotiation track: Ehrenwall offers conditional Löwenritter support (Military 5) in exchange for institutional guarantees. Baralta's Domain Action to secure Löwenritter support: Influence vs Ob 3 (Ehrenwall's professional demands are specific and non-negotiable). Success: Baralta + Löwenritter coalition. Failure: Ehrenwall withholds support, succession contested.

**What Ehrenwall does NOT assess:** Baralta's sovereign supremacy doctrine (Henry VIII parallel) and its implications for Church relations. Ehrenwall evaluates governance competence, not policy direction. She would discover Baralta's Church subordination programme after supporting her — and would need to decide whether Löwenritter serves the Crown regardless of its Church policy, or whether Church destabilisation is itself a governance failure that triggers renewed assessment. This is a second-order problem that Ehrenwall has not gamed because she has not needed to.

### ED-407: Himlensendt Consecration Crisis — Conditional Resolution

**Resolution:** The consecration crisis is a conditional mechanic, not a predetermined outcome. Himlensendt's decision depends on Church institutional strength at the moment the crisis fires.

**Condition: Church Stability ≥ 4 (Church is confident).**
Himlensendt refuses consecration. His theology is clear — divine authority flows through the Church, and he cannot consecrate a sovereign who claims authority over the Church. The institution is strong enough to weather the consequences of refusal.

- Consequences of refusal: deed-monarchy legitimation logic exposed as having a Church veto. TC +3 (Church overreach visible to the population). Crown Mandate −2 in territories that expected orderly succession. Baralta may claim the Crown without consecration — testing whether deed-logic alone is sufficient without theological blessing. If she governs successfully, the Church's legitimation role is permanently diminished.

**Condition: Church Stability ≤ 3 (Church is vulnerable).**
Himlensendt consecrates under duress. The Church cannot afford the institutional crisis of refusing at a moment of vulnerability. He does it with genuine anguish — this is not capitulation but pastoral judgment that the greater harm is institutional collapse.

- Consequences of consecration: Baralta takes the Crown. Church subordination programme begins immediately. TC −5 (sovereign supremacy enacted). Church Stability −3 (institutional trauma of subordination). The Church becomes a subordinate institution within one reign. Himlensendt remains Confessor but his authority is formally beneath the Crown's — the Henry VIII outcome.

**Override condition: Himlensendt has experienced the originary Lock encounter (Arc 11).**
If the originary Lock encounter has already fired — if Himlensendt has experienced Thread reality and his theological certainty is cracked — the consecration decision becomes unpredictable regardless of Church Stability. A Himlensendt whose faith is fractured may refuse for entirely different reasons (not theological certainty but personal crisis), or may consecrate with a different kind of anguish (not institutional calculation but spiritual surrender). The GM determines the outcome based on the specific state of Himlensendt's faith at the moment the crisis fires.

**Mechanical expression (all modes):**
When Baralta claims the Crown and consecration is requested:
1. Check Church Stability.
2. Check whether Arc 11 originary Lock encounter has fired.
3. If Lock encounter has fired: GM discretion (Himlensendt's personal state determines outcome).
4. If Lock encounter has NOT fired: Church Stability ≥ 4 → refusal. Church Stability ≤ 3 → consecration.
5. Apply consequences per the appropriate path.

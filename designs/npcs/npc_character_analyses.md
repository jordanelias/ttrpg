# PART ONE: ROSTER NPCs

## 1. Edeyja — The Last Maintenance Technician

Edeyja is the only NPC in the roster who is not compromised, and this is itself her dramatic function. She is what competence without compromise looks like — and the setting punishes it. She has TS 75–80 (the highest living), Coherence 9 (near-perfect rendering stability), and absolute clarity of purpose: the substrate must hold. Everything else is noise.

The peninsula's factions are, to her, an environmental hazard. They produce Thread tension through wars, investigations, suppressions, and intelligence operations — collateral damage to the substrate she maintains. She does not hate them. She does not engage with their politics. She registers their effects the way an engineer registers vibration patterns in a structure under load. Crown's military campaigns are a frequency. Church's heresy suppressions are another frequency. Varfell's intelligence operations, RM's community weaving, Löwenritter's covert actions — all frequencies, all producing stress on a substrate that is already failing.

**What makes her interesting is what she cannot do.** Edeyja is functionally omnipotent within her domain (Thread operations at TS 80 are near the theoretical ceiling of human capability) and functionally powerless outside it. She cannot stop the factions from producing Thread damage. She cannot make them care about the substrate. She cannot even explain to most of them what the substrate is, because the epistemological barrier (P-08) means non-sensitives cannot render Thread-level structure. She is maintaining a building whose occupants don't know the building exists.

**Arc trajectory:** Edeyja does not arc. She is a fixed point. Other characters arc relative to her — PCs who reach Askeheim and earn her attention measure their own development against her standard. The question is never "will Edeyja change?" It is "will the world change enough to make her job possible, or will she fail despite being the best?"

**Thematic role:** Edeyja embodies the Foundations' claim that rendering is consciousness-performed (P-03). She is the most conscious person in the setting — the one whose rendering is most complete, most stable, most integrated. Her contempt for peninsula politics is not arrogance; it is the perspective of someone who can see the ontological layer that politics is built on, and who knows that layer is degrading. She is right. That she is right and powerless to change it is the setting's central tragedy.

## 2. Maret Uln — The Agent Who Became the Asset

Maret is a intelligence professional whose professional discipline is being eroded by the very intelligence she gathers. As Vaynard's primary field operative (TS ~50), she collects information about the Restoration Movement, southern communities, and Einhir cultural remnants. The problem is that intelligence gathering at TS 50 is not observational — it is experiential. She doesn't read reports about RM communities; she perceives their Thread-level reality. She can see the damage the Forgetting does to cultural continuity. She can feel the substrate strain that institutional suppression produces. Her intelligence is not data points — it is lived experience of the thing she is supposed to be managing.

**The Sorge parallel is structural.** Richard Sorge developed genuine sympathy for Japan while spying for Moscow. The sympathy didn't make him a bad spy — it made him a better one, because he understood Japan more deeply than any distant analyst could. But it also made him unreliable at the moment when Moscow's interests and Japan's interests diverged sharply. Maret is the same: her RM sympathy makes her Varfell's best source on Restoration dynamics, and it makes her the person most likely to hesitate when Varfell needs to act against RM.

**The CONFLICTED flaw is not weakness.** It is the sound of a conscience operating inside a competence structure that demands its suppression. Her 1-season hesitation delay against RM targets is not indecisiveness — it is the time it takes her to construct a rationalisation for doing something she knows is wrong. She is a professional who is watching her professionalism corrode, and she can see it happening because she's too intelligent not to.

**Arc trajectory:** Binary fork. If Vaynard survives, Maret's compromise deepens until it either breaks her (she defects to RM openly, destroying Varfell's intelligence capability) or she compartmentalises successfully (she becomes the person who feels deeply and acts coldly — a Varfell victory that looks like a personal defeat). If Vaynard is eliminated, she inherits a faction and reorients it — VTM resets to 0, representing the loss of Vaynard's carefully built intelligence infrastructure in exchange for political alignment with what Maret actually believes.

**Thematic role:** Maret tests P-10 (Coherence ≠ corruption). Her perceptual shift — seeing RM communities as they are at the Thread level, not as Varfell's intelligence framework categorises them — is exactly the "epistemic seduction" the Foundations describe, except it's seduction toward empathy rather than toward monstrosity. The Framework says the shift "dissolves categories." For Maret, the category dissolving is "asset vs person."

## 3. Maret Vossen — The Visible Revolution

Vossen is structurally trapped in a way that most revolutionary leaders are not. The Restoration Movement needs visible leadership to sustain Popular Will — the PW track advances when communities see organised dissatisfaction, not when hidden cells coordinate in secret. But visibility is what the Church's Heresy Investigation apparatus is designed to target. Every season Vossen operates publicly, Church AP accumulates toward her location. She cannot lead from hiding because the movement dies without a face. She cannot lead publicly because the Church kills what it can see.

**The Luxemburg parallel is precise and fatal.** Rosa Luxemburg was murdered because she was findable. She was findable because the revolution needed her to be public. The revolution needed her to be public because movements without visible leadership fragment into cells that cannot coordinate. Vossen occupies exactly this trap. The RM's Rawlsian ethics (equity, dismantling privilege, benefit to the least advantaged) demand transparency — the movement practices what it preaches. This transparency is the attack surface.

**Her TS 25 adds a specific cruelty.** She is Thread Sensitive enough to perceive Thread-touched artefacts and to sense the substrate strain that justifies the movement's existence. But TS 25 is below the Forgetting resistance threshold (29). When she operates near the Southernmost (T13/T15), the Forgetting degrades her knowledge. She must re-learn fragments of Einhir cultural practice each time she returns — the very knowledge that motivates her movement is unstable in her own mind. She is leading a cultural restoration movement while experiencing the cultural erasure her movement opposes.

**The IDEALIST flaw is her Belief in action.** She spreads Presence thin because she believes every community deserves access to the movement, not just the strategically optimal ones. This is correct from a Rawlsian perspective (the veil of ignorance does not permit strategic triage) and catastrophic from a military one. She is playing the ethics correctly and losing the game.

**Arc trajectory:** Vossen either becomes a martyr (Church captures or eliminates her — RM fractures without visible leadership, PW drops, movement becomes underground cells that Varfell's intelligence apparatus manages) or she is protected long enough to found the movement formally (Founding Mechanic fires — RM gains stats, Vossen's idealism is either institutionalised or betrayed by the institution she creates). The second outcome is more interesting because it tests whether RM can remain Rawlsian with Mandate and Influence stats to manage.

## 4. Sæmund Haelgrund — The Inquisitor's Uncertainty Principle

Haelgrund is the setting's most dangerous epistemological experiment. He is an Inquisitor — the Church's instrument for detecting and suppressing heretical Thread activity — who is himself Thread Sensitive (TS 12). He does not know this. He attributes his investigative "instincts" to professional experience. But his instincts are Thread perception operating below the threshold of his conscious understanding. He finds practitioners because he can sense them. He builds airtight cases because he perceives evidence at a level his analytical framework cannot articulate.

**The Bellarmine parallel extends further than the initial design.** Bellarmine privately found Copernican cosmology more compelling than Ptolemaic — the evidence supported it. But Bellarmine's institutional position required him to evaluate Galileo by doctrinal standards, not empirical ones. The institution's framework could not accommodate what the evidence showed. Haelgrund occupies the same impossible position, except his evidence is not external (telescopic observations) but internal (his own perceptions). The thing the Church says does not exist is operating inside his own consciousness.

**His PROCEDURALIST flaw is a survival mechanism.** By following protocol to the letter, Haelgrund avoids situations where his instincts (Thread perception) would contradict his methodology (doctrinal evaluation). The procedure is a cage he built for himself — slow, thorough, impervious to the shortcuts that would force him to rely on the perception he cannot acknowledge. His investigations take +1 season because he is, unconsciously, giving himself time to construct doctrinal explanations for evidence that is actually Thread-level data.

**The TS 12 revelation is the single highest-impact personal scene in the roster.** If a PC Diagnoses Haelgrund's Thread Sensitivity, the Inquisitor discovers that he IS the thing he investigates. The Church's epistemological framework (P-08: Thread truth is experiential, not transmissible) means that knowing he has TS does not, by itself, change his ontological status — he was always Thread Sensitive. But it changes his self-understanding catastrophically. He can no longer attribute his perceptions to professional skill. He must either integrate his TS into a new self-concept (defection) or suppress the knowledge and continue operating (institutional survival at the cost of self-knowledge). Neither option leaves him intact.

**Arc trajectory:** Three branches. (A) TS undiscovered — Haelgrund continues as the Church's best investigator, producing Overwhelming results at the cost of speed, his hidden TS compensating for the procedure that slows him down. Stable but false. (B) TS discovered, he defects — the Church loses its investigative capability, Haelgrund becomes a neutral practitioner at TS 12 (low but symbolically devastating), and the Church's institutional claim that Thread Sensitivity is heretical is challenged by the fact that their own Inquisitor had it. (C) TS discovered, he suppresses — Haelgrund continues serving the Church while knowing he is what they condemn. His Coherence track begins to degrade (the self-rendering strain of maintaining two incompatible self-concepts). This is the most interesting branch because it maps directly onto P-10: the perceptual shift is not corruption, but his institutional framework forces him to treat it as corruption.

## 5. Sigrid Torsvald — The Operative Who Became the Operation

Torsvald's TS 35 developed through exposure, not training. She was cataloguing Thread-touched artefacts in Lenneth's archive as part of a Riskbreaker security assignment. The archive contains pre-Altonian sea-republic accounts of Thread perception — texts written by a civilisation that openly acknowledged Thread Sensitivity as a natural capacity. Torsvald read these texts while handling the objects they described. The exposure was cumulative, not traumatic — she did not choose to become Thread Sensitive, and she did not notice it happening until it was too late to reverse.

**The Granville parallel is about identity consumption.** Christine Granville's cover identities eventually became more real to her than her birth identity. Torsvald's cover (ordinary Riskbreaker operative, no special abilities) is now less true than her reality (TS 35 practitioner who perceives Thread-level collateral from every operation she runs). The identity she presents to the Lions' Table is the cover. The person she is becoming is the secret. She is undercover from her own institution.

**Her RISK-AVERSE flaw on Thread collateral is not cowardice.** It is perception. Non-TS Riskbreakers cannot see the Thread damage their operations cause — substrate strain, Coherence effects on bystanders, RS micro-degradation from covert activities in Thread-active zones. Torsvald can see all of this. Her ~30% abort rate in Thread-active territories is the rate at which she decides the collateral is unacceptable. Her colleagues see mission failure. She sees mission success at a cost they are constitutionally incapable of perceiving.

**The Lenneth connection is the arc's hinge.** Lenneth holds the archive that awakened Torsvald's TS. Lenneth does not know what her archive is — she is a historian and bibliophile, not a practitioner (TS unknown but potentially developable through intellectual confrontation with the archive's content). Torsvald has a personal connection to Lenneth from the security assignment. If Lenneth's archive arc fires, Torsvald is the natural escort — the one Riskbreaker who understands what the archive contains because it changed her. But escorting Lenneth into the archive's deeper material risks accelerating Torsvald's own TS development past the point where she can conceal it from the Lions' Table.

**Arc trajectory:** Torsvald's arc is a velocity problem. Her TS is developing. Every Thread-active operation she runs, every exposure to the archive, every perception of collateral damage pushes her TS higher. At some threshold (GM discretion — TS 40? 45?), her perceptions become too acute to conceal. She starts seeing things she cannot not-react-to. Her abort rate rises. Ehrenwall (or Brandt) notices. The Riskbreakers investigate their own operative. The arc resolves when the institution discovers what she has become — and must decide whether a Thread-Sensitive Riskbreaker is an asset or a threat.

## 6. Halvar Brandt — The Right Answer to the Wrong Question

Brandt's border war experience is not backstory decoration. It is the lens through which he processes every piece of information he receives. He held Lowenskyst pass for three days against superior numbers. He watched most of his command die. He survived because he made correct tactical decisions under conditions where correct tactical decisions could only slow the inevitable. The experience taught him one lesson: the external threat is real, the internal politics are theatre, and anyone who treats the theatre as more important than the threat is going to get people killed.

**The Aetius parallel is about the structural impossibility of his position.** Aetius was Rome's best general. He defeated the Huns at Châlons. He was murdered by the emperor he served because his single-minded focus on the external threat made him politically uncontrollable — he couldn't stop winning battles long enough to play court politics, and the court couldn't stop playing politics long enough to notice they needed him. Brandt occupies the same position: militarily invaluable, politically illiterate, and unable to understand why the distinction matters.

**His EXTERNAL THREAT FIXATED flaw is a correct assessment applied without context.** Altonia IS a real threat. IP IS rising. The border passes ARE the invasion corridor. Brandt is right about all of this. What he cannot see is that internal stability is a prerequisite for external defence. A peninsula torn apart by Church seizure, Hafenmark expansion, and RM cultural revolution cannot mount a coherent defence even if every soldier is at the border. Brandt's tragedy is that he sees the threat clearly and the conditions for meeting it not at all.

**Arc trajectory:** If Ehrenwall falls and Brandt succeeds her, the Löwenritter become a border defence force rather than a political instrument. Martial Law still fires (Brandt's threshold is 2, not 3 — he acts sooner), but military assets redirect to T3/T10 (the invasion corridors). Crown territories lose internal garrison coverage. This is catastrophic for internal stability and potentially brilliant for external defence — depending on whether Altonia actually invades. Brandt's arc resolves on the answer to a question the setting may never definitively answer: was he right?

## 7. Annika Feldhaus — The Invisible Supply Chain

Feldhaus is competent, profit-maximising, and operating on incomplete information. She runs the Artisans' Compact — the largest guild consortium — with genuine commercial skill. She has built the Guilds' economic position into the strongest on the peninsula (Guilds Wealth 6, highest of any faction). She has done this partly through a supply chain that includes Thread-touched goods sourced through Virke's syndicate network. She does not know the goods are Thread-touched because she has no Thread Sensitivity (TS 0) and no conceptual framework for Thread metaphysics.

**The Jacques Cœur parallel is about the limits of competence.** Cœur was medieval France's most successful merchant. His commercial empire grew so large that he could not know what every branch was doing. He was eventually destroyed when the Crown discovered his network had entangled itself in activities he hadn't authorised and couldn't explain. Feldhaus's Thread-touched supply chain is her Cœur moment: the thing she didn't authorise, doesn't understand, and can't explain — but which she is responsible for because she built the network that made it possible.

**Her PROFIT-MAXIMISING flaw produces a specific and ironic failure mode.** By prioritising Guilds Wealth above all other stats, she drives Guilds Mandate toward 2 (political irrelevance). The Guilds become wealthy and voiceless. In a peninsula where political power (Mandate, Influence) determines outcomes, economic power without political standing is a resource other factions exploit. Feldhaus is building the treasury that funds everyone else's wars.

**Connection to Virke is the design's linked exposure chain.** Feldhaus and Virke share a supply chain. Neither knows the other's full picture. Exposing the Thread connection in either direction cascades to both. This is the most mechanically consequential NPC relationship in the roster because it connects the criminal economy (Niflhel syndicates) to the legitimate economy (Guilds) through a Thread-metaphysical link that neither economic actor can perceive.

**Arc trajectory:** Feldhaus arcs through discovery or through consequences. Discovery: a PC Diagnoses guild merchandise and reveals the Thread connection. Feldhaus must choose between shutting down the supply chain (Guilds Wealth −1 permanently, reputation preserved) or continuing (revenue maintained, reputation destroyed if it comes out later). Consequences: the Thread-touched goods produce low-grade effects in non-sensitive households over time (a design gap flagged in the roster but narratively rich). If communities start experiencing subtle Coherence effects from prolonged exposure to Thread-touched luxury goods, Feldhaus faces a liability she cannot conceptualise — she is poisoning her customers with something that doesn't exist in her ontological framework.

## 8. Peder Almstedt — The System's Immune Response

Almstedt is not a person who happens to work in bureaucracy. He is bureaucracy that happens to be a person. His identity is procedural — he defines himself through the processes he maintains, the precedents he preserves, and the institutional continuity he protects. This is not a character flaw dressed up as a personality. It is a genuinely coherent ethical position: the procedure outlasts the people, and the procedure is what prevents the powerful from doing whatever they want to the powerless.

**The Byzantine logothetes parallel is about institutional survival.** The logothetes outlived every dynasty — Macedonian, Komnenian, Palaiologan — because removing the bureaucracy would collapse the state's administrative capacity. The emperor who fired his logothetes couldn't collect taxes, couldn't maintain roads, couldn't process legal appeals. Almstedt occupies exactly this position: every faction that wants to change Parliament must go through him, and going around him destroys the administrative infrastructure they need to implement the change they want.

**His CONSERVATIVE flaw is a values conflict, not a personality defect.** Almstedt genuinely believes that procedural reform must happen through procedure. Hafenmark's parliamentary reforms are, to him, substantively correct and procedurally illegitimate — they bypass the precedent framework that makes parliamentary governance possible. Crown's Emergency Powers are procedurally authorised but substantively dangerous — they create precedent for executive override that future monarchs will exploit. Almstedt is right about both things, and his rightness is an obstacle to everyone.

**Arc trajectory:** Almstedt doesn't arc in the dramatic sense. He is a constant — like Edeyja, but institutional rather than metaphysical. Characters arc around him. The arc question is not "will Almstedt change?" but "will the players decide the system he protects is worth preserving?" If they co-opt him (Influence vs Ob 3), they gain the system's support at the cost of accepting its constraints. If they remove him (scandal, dismissal), they gain freedom at the cost of institutional capacity (PI recovery lost, reform Ob penalty lifted but no procedural infrastructure to implement reforms). The choice is between order and agency.

## 9. Gerik Strand — The Proving Ground

Strand is the most psychologically legible NPC in the roster, and this is deliberate. His motivation — structural insecurity from conditional appointment, drive to prove competence in a system that values birth — is immediately comprehensible to any player. He is not mysterious. He is not complex in the way that Haelgrund or Torsvald are complex. He is a man whose entire professional existence depends on being indispensable, and who has therefore made himself indispensable, and who therefore cannot stop making himself indispensable because stopping would prove the system right about people like him.

**The William Marshal parallel is about merit in a system that doesn't reward merit.** Marshal rose from landless younger son to regent of England through relentless performance. He was never fully accepted by the hereditary aristocracy despite being the most capable administrator and military commander of his generation. The gap between his proven merit and his assigned social position was the engine of his entire career. Strand occupies the same gap — competent beyond question, accepted conditionally, and aware that the condition can be revoked.

**His OVERPERFORMER flaw creates institutional brittleness.** Strand cannot delegate because delegation means someone else demonstrates competence, which means Strand becomes dispensable. This produces excellent short-term outcomes (Crown admin +1D) and catastrophic succession risk (remove Strand, Crown admin collapses for 2 seasons). He has made himself the single point of failure in Crown governance, and he has done this not from ambition but from self-preservation.

**The flattery vulnerability is the most gameable mechanic in the roster.** Any faction that treats Strand as a peer — genuine acknowledgment of his competence, not transparent manipulation — gets −1 Ob to social actions targeting him. Strand is smart enough to detect lies. He is not smart enough to resist truth that confirms what he desperately wants to believe: that he belongs. This makes him simultaneously the Crown's best asset and its softest target. The faction that understands Strand's psychology can extract intelligence, redirect resources, and manipulate Crown scheduling through a man whose loyalty to Crown is genuine but whose need for validation is exploitable.

**Arc trajectory:** Strand arcs through recognition or through betrayal. Recognition: Crown (Almud or PCs) formally acknowledges Strand's contributions in a way that is institutionally permanent — a title, a hereditary grant, a parliamentary appointment that cannot be revoked by the next monarch. This resolves his insecurity and transforms him from a brittle overperformer into a stable administrator. The Crown loses the vulnerability but gains institutional resilience. Betrayal: a faction exploits his flattery vulnerability to extract enough intelligence that Strand's loyalty is questioned. Strand is dismissed. He doesn't become an enemy — he becomes nothing, returning to Feldmark to count wheat yields, which is worse.

## 10. Dalla Virke — The Trust Economy

Virke is the most operationally interesting NPC in the roster because her value proposition is relational, not transactional. She does not sell goods — she sells access to a trust network she built over a decade of personal relationships. The Virke family's peninsula operation outperforms larger syndicates not because it has better logistics or more capital, but because Virke's trade partners trust her enough to take risks they wouldn't take with anyone else. She extended credit during hard seasons. She forgave debts when children were sick. She knows her partners' families. This is not altruism — it is infrastructure. The trust IS the competitive advantage.

**The Medici branch manager parallel is about the tension between local loyalty and institutional control.** Medici branch managers were technically employees of Florence. Practically, they were local power brokers whose relationship networks were the bank's actual asset. When a branch manager's local loyalties conflicted with Florentine directives, the bank faced a choice: enforce compliance (destroying the local relationships that made the branch profitable) or tolerate autonomy (accepting that the branch served its manager's interests as much as Florence's). Virke's family faces this exact dilemma. She is their best asset and their least controllable agent.

**The Thread-touched supply chain is uniquely damaging to her because it's her personal judgment failure.** She built this supply chain. She vetted the sources. She selected these goods because she believed she knew what she was selling. If the Thread connection is revealed, it is not an institutional failure she can deflect — it is proof that the professional judgment her entire reputation rests on was wrong about the most fundamental property of her most valuable product line. For a person whose value proposition is "I know what I'm selling," this is existential.

**Arc trajectory:** Virke arcs through the supply chain revelation or through the family recall. Revelation: Thread-touched goods are exposed, cascading to Feldhaus (Guilds). Virke must choose between continuing (maintaining revenue but now knowingly distributing dangerous goods) and stopping (destroying her revenue stream and possibly triggering family recall). Her ethics say honour the deal — but the deal was made on false premises she unknowingly created. Recall: the family discovers she's shielding Valorian partners from family intelligence operations. They send someone to replace her. The replacement has no local relationships. The trust network collapses overnight. Every faction that depended on Virke's network for discreet logistics, intelligence, or supply is exposed simultaneously. The recall is a peninsula-wide disruption event.

## 11. Doux Alexios Laskaris — The Empire's Weakest Link

Laskaris is the Altonian Empire's representative on the peninsula, and he is betraying the empire in slow motion. Not through ideology or defection — through attachment. He was given Elske as a political instrument. He was supposed to use her dynastic claim to pressure Valoria: Tutoring Demands for Torben, succession interference, implied annexation threats. Instead, he has developed genuine regard for Elske's autonomy and intelligence. He delays imperial directives. He softens ultimatums. He has twice leaked the imperial court's intentions to Elske, who passed them to Crown.

**The Palaiologos parallel is about surrender-as-protection.** Demetrios Palaiologos surrendered Morea to the Ottomans partly because continued resistance would have destroyed the province and everyone in it — including his family. The surrender was simultaneously rational (it preserved lives), treasonous (it delivered territory to the enemy), and loving (it protected the people he was responsible for). Laskaris is making the same calculation in slow motion: every directive he softens, every deadline he extends, every piece of intelligence he leaks is a micro-surrender of imperial authority in exchange for peninsular stability that protects Elske.

**His ethical framework — oikonomia — is the Byzantine principle that pastoral necessity can override strict law.** This is not pragmatism or consequentialism. It is a specific theological-political tradition that says the law serves the community, not the reverse, and that a governor who destroys his province to enforce the law has failed the law's purpose. Laskaris applies this to Altonian imperial policy: the empire's claim on Valoria is legally valid, but enforcing it would destroy the peninsula that makes the claim valuable. He is loyal to Altonia's interests while quietly subverting Altonia's policies. The distinction is real to him. It would not survive imperial review.

**The PROTECTIVE flaw has a catastrophic reversal condition.** While Elske is safe (Loyalty ≥ 3), Laskaris sandbangs IP at −1/season. The peninsula benefits from his emotional attachment without knowing it. But if Elske is threatened (Loyalty ≤ 2 or Return attempt fails), Laskaris flips. IP +3 immediately. He stops shielding Valoria and prioritises Elske's extraction. The protective instinct that held the empire back now accelerates it — Laskaris will deliver whatever the imperial court wants if they guarantee Elske's safety. This flip transforms the IP track from slow-burn to crisis in a single Accounting step. Every season of sandbagged IP has been accruing as potential energy. When it releases, it releases all at once.

## 12. Rikard Solberg — The Unconscious Peacemaker

Solberg is the simplest NPC in the roster — his motivation is homesickness — and this simplicity is what makes him effective. He is Schoenland's trade factor in Valorsplatz. Schoenland's official policy is neutrality: profit from peninsular tension, prevent resolution. Solberg's personal desire is to go home to his family. Stable Valoria = Solberg's posting ends. Unstable Valoria = Solberg stays indefinitely. He has been here 7 years. His children are growing up without him.

**The Xenophon parallel is motivational, not military.** The *Anabasis* is the story of ten thousand Greeks who fought their way home across hostile territory because no strategic objective mattered as much as returning. Every tactical decision — which pass to take, which river to ford, which local ruler to negotiate with — was filtered through "does this bring us closer to the sea?" Solberg's professional decisions are filtered through the same lens: "does this bring me closer to recall?" The answer is always "stable Valoria = recall." So he unconsciously steers Schoenland policy toward stability.

**He does not know he is doing this.** Solberg genuinely believes his policy recommendations serve Schoenland's interests. They do not. They serve the interests of a man who wants to see his children. His intelligence reports downplay instability ("the situation is manageable"). His arms shipment recommendations are conservative ("oversupply invites escalation"). His trade terms favour whichever faction is currently losing (stabilising the balance of power). Each individual decision is defensible. The cumulative pattern is not — a genuinely neutral Schoenland factor would be extracting more value from the chaos.

**Arc trajectory:** Solberg arcs through discovery of his bias or through the offer of recall. Discovery: a faction identifies the pattern in Schoenland's peninsula policy (Intel action vs Ob 3). Schoenland central recalls Solberg. His replacement is genuinely neutral — arms flows increase, intelligence sharpens, Schoenland becomes more dangerous. Solberg goes home. The peninsula gets worse. Offer: PCs who discover Solberg's motivation can offer a real path to recall — a stability treaty, a commercial agreement, anything that would make Schoenland central decide the posting is no longer valuable. If the offer is genuine, Solberg becomes an active asset, working to make the treaty happen. If the offer is false, Solberg detects it (he is not stupid) and the leverage evaporates. The arc is about whether the players can find a solution where Solberg's personal desire and the peninsula's strategic needs align — or whether those things are structurally incompatible.

## 13. [Name Pending] — Prudence Cardinal: The Efficient Erosion

The Prudence Cardinal is the Church's best financial administrator and its most dangerous internal threat. He runs the Church's economic portfolio — tithes, charities, land management — like a commercial enterprise. Tithe quotas optimised. Monastery expenditures audited. Landholdings consolidated for efficiency. The redistribution is real: Church charities are well-funded, hospitals function, the poor are fed. His metrics all point upward. He is producing the Church's best charitable outcomes while systematically eroding the Church's grassroots loyalty.

**The Suger parallel is about wealth as instrument.** Suger of Saint-Denis ran his abbey like a business and built the first Gothic cathedral with the proceeds. The Cistercians (led by Bernard of Clairvaux) attacked him for it — the Church should be austere, not magnificent; the faithful should give from devotion, not obligation. Suger's response was that wealth deployed for God's glory IS devotion. The Prudence Cardinal's logic is identical: wealth extracted efficiently and redistributed completely IS care for the faithful. Both men are right about the ends. Both men are destructive about the means. Suger's abbey flourished. The parishes that funded it resented the extraction for generations.

**His OPTIMISER flaw creates the setting's most ironic feedback loop.** Church Wealth +1/season (his tithe efficiency is real). But Church Mandate erodes in over-tithed territories (−0.5 at Year-End per squeezed territory). CV erosion accelerates +1 in those territories at Year-End (resentful communities become RM recruitment targets). The Prudence Cardinal is funding the Church's charitable mission while undermining the cultural authority the Church needs to win through Conviction Yield. He is making the Church richer and weaker at the same time, and his metrics cannot detect the weakness because he measures wealth, not loyalty.

**He genuinely does not see the problem.** This is not obtuseness. It is the limitation of his framework. He measures inputs (tithe revenue) and outputs (charitable distribution). He does not measure the relationship between the Church and its flock because that relationship is not a quantity. The Parish leaders who complain about quotas are, to him, inefficient managers who need better systems. The communities who give resentfully are, to him, communities who are giving — the resentment is not in his ledger. He is an excellent administrator solving the wrong problem with perfect efficiency.

**Arc trajectory:** The Prudence Cardinal arcs through the Parish Revolt card or through PC intervention. Parish Revolt: ≥ 3 squeezed territories at Church Mandate ≤ 3 — Parish leaders refuse collection. Church Wealth −2 for a season. CV −1 in affected territories. TC −1. The system he built collapses because the relationships he ignored were load-bearing. PC intervention: PCs (especially Church-aligned PCs) can confront the Cardinal with the loyalty data he's missing — show him the RM recruitment patterns in over-tithed territories, introduce him to the Parish leaders he's squeezing. If he sees the data (his Resonant Style is evidence-responsive — he IS competent), he reforms. If he reforms: Church Wealth bonus reduced to +0.5/season, but Mandate erosion stops. The Church becomes slightly poorer and significantly more stable. The reform is a loss for the Cardinal's self-image (his metrics go down) and a win for the institution.

## 1. King Almud Almqvist — The Competent Custodian

Almud is the second generation of a deed-monarchy. His father — the first Almqvist — earned the throne through the Secession Wars and was consecrated by the Church of Solmund. His father was killed in a hunting accident around 218 AG (widely suspected assassination). Almud inherited the deed-presumption at a young age, having personally earned nothing. His 27-year reign is the answer to the question of whether the presumption was warranted.

**Crown Mandate 5 is the answer: yes.** The highest starting faction stat. This is not an accident. A king who had been incapable for 27 years under pressure from Altonia, Church, Hafenmark, Varfell, Löwenritter, Schoenland, and the Restoration Movement would have been deposed — and in a deed-monarchy, deposition is the system working as designed, not treason. The deed-presumption is rebuttable. Almud has not been rebutted. He is competent.

**TS 0. Northern Einhir. Fully Church-culturally integrated.** Almud has no Thread experience. He has no secret Einhir interiority. He grew up in Valorsplatz — maximally Church-influenced, maximally northern, maximally insulated from Thread-active environments. He has never experienced anything that would make him question the Church's ontological claims from personal perception.

**His Einhir policy is governance pragmatism plus genuine ethical doubt.** He does not sympathise with southern Einhir. He does not privately believe the caste system is unjust in a way that demands action. He understands that mistreated populations cause governance problems — Varfell restiveness, Restoration drift, southern territory instability. These are governance inputs. He processes them as a competent administrator.

But he also sometimes questions, privately, whether a Valnese kingdom should have a caste system at all. The doubt is intellectual, not experiential. He does not need Thread Sensitivity to wonder whether the system is defensible. He is an intelligent ruler governing a diverse population. The ethical doubt is an ordinary moral question that any thoughtful person would encounter. He has not resolved it into a position. He genuinely does not know whether dismantling the caste would make the peninsula better or worse.

**He does not act on the doubt because he is genuinely uncertain, not because costs prevent him.** The previous characterisation had him calculating that acting costs Mandate −2, TC +3, IP +1. Those costs are real. But the deeper truth is that Almud has not decided whether acting is right. The costs are a convenient justification for inaction on a question he has not resolved. If he were certain the caste was indefensible, he would find a way to act — he has been managing six pressure vectors for 27 years; he is capable of paying political costs. He does not act because the question is genuinely open to him.

**His relationship to the Restoration Movement is genuinely adversarial in effect.** The Restoration sees him as complicit and cowardly. They are half right. He is complicit — he maintains the system that oppresses them through cost-benefit calculation. He is not cowardly — he is uncertain. From the Restoration's perspective, the distinction is irrelevant. A man who questions the system and does nothing is worse than a man who defends it sincerely.

**The assassination shadow drives professional security practice, not existential dread.** Almud suspects his father was assassinated. This suspicion (which may be wrong) has produced structurally paranoid attention to court politics, factional cultivation, and information asymmetry. His intelligence channels rival Vaynard's in scope. This is survival intelligence — operationally sound regardless of whether the premise is true.

**Historical anchor: Manuel I Komnenos.** Byzantine emperor who managed six simultaneous external threats through strategic patience, intelligence operations, and selective engagement. His critics called him indecisive. He was playing six boards at once. Almud is Manuel: the ruler whose competent restraint looks like inaction from any single vantage point.

**The Discovery Event arc is rupture, not confirmation.** If Almud's TS crosses 0 through external contact (player characters bringing Thread reality to Valorsplatz), everything he understood about the Einhir question changes. He was managing it as a governance variable. Now he is experiencing what southern Einhir experience. His entire framework — the cost-benefit calculation, the ethical uncertainty, the administrative distance — is invalidated by personal perception. He did not know what he was deciding about. Now he does. This is more dramatically powerful than the previous version: not a man confirmed in hidden sympathies but a man whose entire worldview is ruptured by something he never anticipated.

## 2. Queen Lenneth Almqvist — The Institutional Revivalist

Lenneth wants to overcome the caste system and revive Einhir cultural heritage — but not through revolution. She wants to empower the existing institution, the Crown, by making it the patron of Einhir cultural revival.

**Historical anchor: Catherine the Great.** Catherine did not want to destroy Russian institutions. She wanted to strengthen them by incorporating Enlightenment principles into the existing autocratic structure. Universities, legal reforms, arts patronage — all within the framework of absolute monarchy. The reforms strengthened the Crown by making it the source of modernisation. Lenneth's version: if the Crown becomes the institution that recovers what was lost, bridges the caste divide, and restores the peninsula's pre-colonial heritage, then the Crown's deed-claim gains a dimension that performance metrics cannot question. The family would no longer be just "the family that won the war" but "the family that reunited the Valnese people."

**Sincere conviction and strategic vision are aligned.** Lenneth genuinely believes the caste system is wrong (she has the moral conviction Almud's doubt prevents him from reaching). She also understands that rectifying it through Crown initiative would permanently strengthen her family's position. These are not in tension for her because she believes good governance and self-interest should align.

**Thread work is the eventual horizon.** Lenneth does not start the game demanding Thread work revival. Her programme has stages: cultural recognition first, institutional reform second, Thread work eventually. Each stage is more dangerous than the last — Thread work is the part the Church will fight hardest.

**Her method differs from Vaynard's fundamentally.** Vaynard wants to tear down. Lenneth wants to build up. Vaynard would expel the Church. Lenneth would co-opt it or make it unable to prevent Crown-led revival. Vaynard sees existing institutions as the enemy. Lenneth sees them as raw material. Vaynard would regard Lenneth's approach as inadequate. Lenneth would regard Vaynard's as reckless.

**Lenneth has a structural advantage Vaynard lacks: she is inside the Crown.** Her programme can be enacted through royal authority. Vaynard's requires overthrowing or capturing royal authority first.

**Covert networks:** People's Revolution endowment, sea-republic archive channel. Can gain TS through scholarly research.

**The Lenneth↔Baralta collision is the campaign's defining political confrontation.** Lenneth wants Crown authority to revive Einhir heritage. Baralta wants Crown authority for herself and would suppress Einhir heritage as a threat to sovereign divine right. Their programmes are mutually exclusive. Lenneth's Einhir revival delegitimises Baralta's theological framework. Baralta's reign would terminate Lenneth's programme on day one.

## 3. Prince Torben Almqvist — The Disputed Inheritance

Torben is the deed-monarchy's succession question made flesh. In a blood-monarchy, his inheritance would be automatic. In a deed-monarchy, it depends on whether the deed-presumption holds. His youth means he has earned nothing yet. He is the test case for whether the Almqvist royal stock can transfer across a generation that has not been tested.

Altonia's Tutoring Demand (IP 30) is an attack on the deed-presumption itself: if Torben is educated in Altonian values, the next Almqvist monarch would be culturally Altonian — the deed-monarchy's legitimation logic would be hollow, a northern Einhir throne occupied by an Altonian-trained prince.

## 5. Duke Magnus Vaynard — The Revolutionary

Vaynard is the peninsula's most dangerous political actor and the only faction leader who understands the existential crisis the peninsula faces.

**Southern Einhir heritage.** Varfell (Lupicco) has the highest concentration of southern Einhir population. Vaynard may be of southern stock himself. His duchy is the peninsula's second-class duchy — culturally stigmatised, its population carrying the blame for the Catastrophe. Northern Valnese nobility, Hafenmark parliamentary grandees, Crown court aristocrats regard Varfell as provincial, tainted, lesser. Not openly. Institutionally.

**Historical anchor: Reinhard von Lohengramm.** Reinhard wanted to destroy the old aristocratic order — not to replace it with democracy but with competent autocracy. His reforms were genuine. His ambition was genuine. These were the same thing. Vaynard wants the Church of Solmund and all traces of Altonian cultural residue expelled from the peninsula. Not reformed. Not bounded. Expelled.

**His consequentialism is not philosophical preference — it is the only framework that can accommodate his political goal.** Categorical imperative cannot justify expelling an established institution. Divine command cannot justify expelling the Church. Only consequentialism — outcomes justify means — can support the argument that the peninsula's long-term survival requires dismantling the two pillars of the post-war settlement.

**TS from environmental exposure, not artefact contact.** Southern Einhir populations retain higher baseline TS. Vaynard grew up in this environment, with the Church's perceptual prophylaxis weaker in the south. His Thread Sensitivity is what southern Einhir people are when the Church has not completely suppressed their capacity. He investigates Thread knowledge not as an outsider studying an alien phenomenon but as someone recovering his own cultural heritage.

**The Forgetting is his political prison.** Vaynard understands the Southernmost crisis — the peninsula faces existential Thread danger that only Einhir knowledge can address. But the Forgetting prevents him from building a political coalition around this argument. Non-practitioners cannot retain actionable knowledge about the Southernmost. The most important argument for his entire worldview dissolves in the minds of anyone who cannot already perceive Thread reality. His intelligence operations are the consequence: he cannot make his case publicly, so he builds capability in secret.

**His relationship to Almud:** manager vs revolutionary. Almud sees Vaynard as a governance input — a restive duke to be managed through accommodation. Vaynard sees Almud as the system's complicit custodian. Almud does not understand what Vaynard is fighting for. Vaynard knows this and it fuels his contempt.

**His relationship to Baralta:** direct structural opposition. Baralta's constitutional framework universalised the post-war settlement, including the caste. Vaynard wants the settlement dismantled. Their rivalry is a contest between the northern establishment and the southern duchy that has been building capability for decades.

## 6. Duchess Inge Baralta — The Protestant Monarch

Baralta wants the Crown. This is active ambition, not contingency.

She has governed Hafenmark for decades as an unmarried, childless woman with no identified successor by direct lineage. Her eschatological orientation — she believes her work will be recognised in the afterlife — means she has no need for dynastic continuity. Her constitutional framework is her legacy, and she trusts it to persist without her. The game's mechanics say she is wrong: Leadership Deviation Ob 1 means Baralta IS Hafenmark. PP-487's PI-gated succession fork means at Public Instability (PI) < 4, the institution fractures without her.

**Her Protestant parallel is Henry VIII, not Luther.** She does not question the Church's theology. She questions its jurisdiction. She believes the monarch rules by genuine divine right — not the Church's delegated authority — and that the Church's proper role is pastoral service under sovereign oversight. The Sovereign Authority Doctrine is the legal instrument of this claim. Every time she invokes it, she establishes the precedent that secular authority supersedes Church jurisdiction — pre-building the legal architecture of her future reign.

**She is the Restoration Movement's pure adversary.** Einhir cultural revival threatens sovereign divine right. Baralta's framework has exactly two legitimate sources of authority: the sovereign (divine right) and the law (constitutional framework). The Restoration introduces a third — an older tradition predating Solmund — that delegitimises both. Baralta would suppress the Restoration more aggressively than the Church, given the opportunity.

**Her cadet-branch deed-claim is the strongest on the peninsula.** The deed-monarchy's own logic — competence determines legitimacy — points to her. Decades of governance, Mandate 7, Ob 1 deviation cost. If the Almqvist line fails, she is the obvious successor. She knows this. She is not waiting for it to happen passively — her constitutional framework, her parliamentary positioning, her Church confrontations are a decades-long campaign for the throne.

**Her faith is the engine of her ambition.** She wants the Crown and believes her ambition is divinely justified. This is the deepest sincerity. She believes that bounded Church authority under competent secular governance is what Solmund's grace demands. Her reward will be both temporal (the Crown) and eternal (divine recognition). The faith-politics contradiction (categorical imperative vs divine command) is still present — it is her strength and her vulnerability.

**Historical anchor: Isabella I of Castile.** Isabella fought a succession war, made herself the obvious heir through political alliance and Church support, and took the Crown. Baralta is doing the same through institutional rather than military means — she cannot take the Crown by force (Hafenmark Military 3 is weakest of major factions) but she can take it by making herself so institutionally indispensable that the deed-logic has nowhere else to point.

**What happens to Hafenmark when Baralta is imperiled or dies:** Without her, the succession is purely institutional. Parliament selects. But the bench of successors learned to govern in her shadow. None have been tested independently. At PI ≥ 4, institutional succession works procedurally but the new leader inherits her enemies without her Mandate. At PI < 4, the faction fractures — legalists vs pragmatists. Hafenmark without Baralta is a personality cult that discovers it was always a personality cult.

## 7. Confessor Arne Himlensendt — The Structural Keystone

Himlensendt is the most dangerous person on the peninsula and does not know it. His sincere faith is the load-bearing wall of the entire post-war settlement.

**He is not cynical, not calculating, not a political operator.** He believes completely in the Church's theological framework. His TC accumulation — hospitals, charities, dispute resolution — is not strategy. It is pastoring. The structural consequence is theocratic drift he would be horrified to recognise.

**On Baralta's sovereign supremacy:** Himlensendt responds pastorally, not politically. He does not argue Baralta is wrong about the law. He argues the law is not the highest frame of reference. There is something above the law — Solmund's revelation. Baralta cannot answer this without confronting her faith-politics contradiction.

**Consecration crisis:** If Baralta claims the Crown, Himlensendt faces the question the Church has not faced since the Secession Wars. Consecrating Baralta means installing a sovereign who believes she has authority over him — the act of consecration would confirm a divine right that includes Church subordination. Refusing exposes consecration as political veto power, damaging the Church's legitimation authority. His theology says he must refuse. His refusal cracks the settlement.

**On the Einhir question:** Himlensendt does not think about the caste system. Not from evasion — from genuine absence in his framework. The Church condemns practices, not peoples. But the structural consequence of condemning the practices is condemning the people who practiced them. If presented with evidence of systematic disadvantage, he would respond with genuine pastoral concern — send missionaries, build schools, extend charity. Every pastoral response would deepen the essentialist framework's penetration into exactly the communities where Thread Sensitivity survives. His compassion and his suppression are the same act.

**On Vaynard:** The existential heretic. Not because Vaynard attacks the Church politically (Baralta does that more effectively) but because Vaynard attacks the Church ontologically — claiming Solmund was a threadcut being, not a divine saviour. Himlensendt would classify Vaynard as the most dangerous heretic on the peninsula. The response would not be pastoral. It would be institutional.

**On the Southernmost:** Southernmost Awareness 0. The Church teaches the Catastrophe was caused by Einhir overreach. The Southernmost is Himlensendt's empirical proof: look what happens when you practice Thread work. His argument is factually correct on the historical question and completely wrong on the ontological question. Nobody can show him the right conclusion because the Forgetting prevents non-practitioners from retaining the corrective information.

## 11. Grandmaster Ehrenwall — The Ledger That Never Forgives

The deed-monarchy reframing clarifies her position precisely. Ehrenwall is not a potential traitor contemplating treason against a divinely ordained king. She is a military professional calculating whether the current office-holder is still performing the deeds that justify the office. Her loyalty is to the institution the first Almqvist created, not to the Almqvist bloodline. She would transfer loyalty to whoever the deed-logic identifies as the next worthy holder — including Baralta — without experiencing this as betrayal. It is fidelity to the founding principle.

Her Coup Counter is the deed-logic's enforcement mechanism. Every increment is a data point toward "the deed-presumption is failing." She knew the first Almqvist personally (or served under him). She has a standard of comparison that Almud cannot meet because the standard was set by a war hero and Almud governs in peacetime. The question she carries — is peacetime governance sufficient to earn the throne? — has no clean answer, and her inability to answer it cleanly is what makes the Counter increment slowly rather than firing immediately.

## 1. King Almud Almqvist — The Sympathiser on the Throne

Almud is the peninsula's most structurally contradicted leader. He governs through the post-war settlement — the institutional framework that suppressed Einhir cultural practice, reinforced Church doctrinal authority, and established Crown governance over a peninsula that was, before Altonian occupation, something very different. He privately sympathises with the Restoration Movement. The system he runs is the system that crushed what he believes in. He enforces it anyway, because the alternative — admitting the settlement was wrong — would collapse the institutional basis of his own authority.

**TS 28 is the setting's most loaded number.** Thread Sensitivity 28 is two points below the practitioner threshold (30). Almud is on the edge of perception — close enough that Thread-significant events in his presence trigger a Discovery Event check (Spirit Ob 1). He has been near Thread phenomena his entire life (he rules a peninsula saturated with Thread tension) and has not yet crossed the threshold. This is either extraordinary bad luck or extraordinary unconscious suppression. His Resonant Style is Consequence — he responds to demonstrated effects, not theoretical arguments. If a Thread event produces consequences he cannot ignore, his TS check fires. If it succeeds, he crosses 30 and becomes a recognised practitioner — the King of Valoria, Thread Sensitive, in a political landscape where the Church condemns Thread Sensitivity as heretical.

**His Conviction is Order/Reason, and this is the cage.** Almud believes in institutional governance. He believes the peninsula needs structure, law, precedent. He also believes the Einhir were wronged and the Restoration's cultural claims have merit. These two beliefs coexist because Almud has never been forced to choose between them. Every season he governs through the settlement is a season he implicitly endorses the suppression. Every season he privately sympathises with the Restoration is a season he withholds support that could change outcomes. He is the most powerful person on the peninsula and the least decisive, not from weakness but from the genuine impossibility of reconciling his beliefs within the system he operates.

**Arc trajectory:** The Discovery Event is Almud's arc catalyst. If his TS crosses 30, everything changes. He becomes what the Church condemns. His sympathy for the Restoration is no longer abstract — he now shares their ontological condition. Crown governance of the peninsula is now led by a practitioner. Every faction recalculates. Church has grounds for Excommunication. Ehrenwall's Coup Counter may increment (the King is now, by Church definition, compromised). Hafenmark's Baralta must decide whether constitutional governance can accommodate a practitioner king. Vaynard finally has a potential Crown ally who can understand Thread reality. The Discovery Event doesn't change Almud's beliefs. It makes them visible — and actionable.

**The tragedy is that he may never get there.** TS 28 with a Spirit Ob 1 check means the Discovery Event fires on any Thread-significant event in his presence with ~60-70% success (depending on Spirit score). But the check only fires when Thread events happen near him, and Almud's institutional role keeps him in Valorsplatz (T1) — far from the Thread-active zones (T6, T13, T15). He is structurally insulated from the events that would awaken him. The king who could change everything is sitting in the one place where nothing happens at the Thread level. PCs who bring Thread reality to Valorsplatz — deliberately or accidentally — are the mechanism by which Almud's arc fires.

## 2. Queen Lenneth Almqvist — The Archive as Ontological Weapon

Lenneth is barely characterised in the current design — TS unknown, stat block empty, arc described as "dependent." But what IS established is extraordinary: she holds a pre-Altonian sea-republic archive containing first-person Thread-perception accounts from approximately 180 AG. These are texts written by a civilisation that openly acknowledged Thread Sensitivity as a natural capacity. In a setting where the Church has spent centuries enforcing the doctrine that Thread claims are heretical, and where the Forgetting ensures that experiential Thread knowledge cannot be transmitted to non-sensitives — Lenneth's archive is the one body of evidence that could challenge both barriers simultaneously.

**The archive is not Thread knowledge.** The texts within it are descriptions, accounts, theoretical frameworks — information that non-sensitives can read and understand intellectually but cannot use operationally (P-08: Inert Knowledge). What the archive proves is not how to work Threads but that Thread Sensitivity was once publicly acknowledged, institutionally supported, and culturally integrated. This is a political weapon, not a metaphysical one. It does not enable Thread operations. It destroys the Church's claim that Thread perception is a modern heresy.

**Lenneth's TS is the design's open question.** She is described as having "potential TS development through intellectual confrontation with archive content + Thread exposure." If her TS develops through sustained engagement with the archive's materials — reading first-person accounts of Thread perception while handling Thread-touched objects catalogued by Torsvald — this would be the setting's demonstration that intellectual engagement CAN create the conditions for TS development, even if it cannot create TS directly (P-08 remains intact: the archive texts are Inert, but proximity to Thread-touched objects combined with deep intellectual engagement with Thread concepts could lower the threshold for latent TS activation).

**Arc trajectory:** Lenneth's arc is the archive's arc. If the archive is made public, the Church's doctrinal foundation is challenged. If the archive is destroyed (Church Heresy Investigation, Altonian cultural suppression), the evidence is lost. If Lenneth's TS develops, the Queen of Valoria becomes the second royal practitioner (after Almud's potential awakening) — and the one with the historical framework to understand what Thread Sensitivity means, not just how it feels. Lenneth + archive + Torsvald (the Riskbreaker who knows both the archive and Lenneth personally) is the trilogy that could reshape the peninsula's relationship with Thread reality.

## 3. Prince Torben Almqvist — The Disputed Inheritance

Torben is not a character in the dramatic sense. He is a political object — the heir whose loyalties are contested by every faction with a succession claim. Torben Loyalty (3, range 0–7) tracks not Torben's personal development but the peninsula's collective tug-of-war over his future. Crown wants him loyal (succession continuity). Church wants him pious (doctrinal succession). Altonia wants him educated in the imperial court (Tutoring Demand). Löwenritter wants him manageable (succession stability for post-coup governance).

**Torben's dramatic potential is entirely in what he becomes.** At Loyalty 0, he aligns with anti-Löwenritter factions (Church or Restoration) — the heir has been turned against the institutional structure that was supposed to protect him. At Loyalty 7, he is fully Löwenritter-loyal — the heir has been captured by the military order, and Crown governance will become Löwenritter governance in the next generation. Neither extreme is narratively inert. Both produce consequences that reshape the peninsula's political trajectory for decades.

**The Tutoring Demand (IP ≥ 40) is the setting's most elegant political trap.** Altonia demands that Torben be sent to the imperial court for "education." Compliance: Torben Loyalty −2 immediately (he is exposed to Altonian values and isolated from Crown influence). Refusal: IP continues rising. The Crown must choose between losing its heir's loyalty and accepting the external pressure. This is the kind of decision that defines campaigns — not because either option is clearly correct, but because both options have costs that compound over time.

**Arc trajectory:** Torben arcs through the decisions other people make about him. He is the setting's most passive major NPC — a weather vane, not a wind. His arc is the arc of the succession crisis itself: who controls the next generation's governance of the peninsula? PCs who interact with Torben directly (TTRPG/Hybrid) can try to give him agency — teach him, protect him, expose him to perspectives his handlers don't control. Whether this is possible depends on whether the setting's political machinery allows a young heir to become a person before the factions finish deciding what he should be.

## 4. Princess Elske Almqvist — The Hostage Who Holds the Key

Elske is in Altonia. She is married to Doux Alexios Laskaris. She is not on the board. She exists as a track (Elske Loyalty 0–7, starts 4) and a set of conditional triggers. Her mechanical function is clear: she is the dynastic link that determines whether Altonia has a legitimate succession claim on Valoria, and her Loyalty track measures whether that link can be leveraged.

**What makes Elske interesting is what the mechanics don't say.** She is described as married to Laskaris, who has genuine affection for her and is actively sandbagging imperial directives to protect her. This means Elske is not a passive hostage — she is in a relationship with the person who is supposed to be using her as a weapon, and that person is choosing not to. Elske's own agency is undefined but structurally implied: Laskaris has "twice leaked imperial court intentions to Elske, who passed them to Crown through her own channels." She has channels. She is operating.

**Elske Loyalty is mislabelled.** It measures Crown's ability to reach and influence Elske, not Elske's loyalty to Crown. At Loyalty 6+, Crown can attempt to bring her back (Return action: Military vs Ob 2, IP +5 on success). At Loyalty ≤ 2, Laskaris flips (IP +3). The track is not about Elske's inner state — it is about the political corridor between Valorsplatz and Laskaris's province. Elske herself may have views that the track does not capture.

**Arc trajectory:** Elske's arc fires when she returns — or when she can't. If she returns (Loyalty ≥ 6, Return succeeds), she brings Altonian intelligence, diplomatic experience, and potentially Thread-adjacent knowledge from exposure to Altonian scholarship. If she cannot return (Loyalty ≤ 2, Laskaris flips), she becomes the permanent justification for Altonian pressure — the princess they will not give back. Either outcome reshapes the IP dynamic. The most interesting version is the one where Elske returns with her own agenda — one that doesn't align with Crown's, or Laskaris's, or Altonia's.

## 5. Duke Magnus Vaynard — The Collector Who Cannot Touch

Vaynard is the setting's most intelligent non-practitioner and the person most frustrated by that limitation. TS 14 (Dormant, unrecognised). Conviction: Reason. Resonant Style: Consequence. His consequentialist framework treats Thread reality as a resource to be managed — a problem to be solved through intelligence, acquisition, and institutional leverage. He pursues Thread knowledge through his collection (originary locks, Einhir artefacts) and through Maret Uln's field intelligence. He understands the Calamity's structure at TK 3-5. He may understand what the Einhir did better than anyone outside the Wardens. He cannot experience any of it directly because TS 14 is below the threshold for conscious Thread perception.

**The consequentialist framework is both his strength and his limitation.** Varfell under Vaynard is the peninsula's best intelligence operation. TK 5 means Vaynard understands what Solmund was structurally — the metaphysical architecture of the Calamity. This is intellectual comprehension without experiential basis. He knows what the building looks like from the blueprint. He has never been inside. His consequentialism tells him this doesn't matter — results matter, not experience. The Foundations say otherwise: Thread reality is experiential (P-08), and no amount of intellectual understanding substitutes for perception. Vaynard's entire strategic framework is built on the assumption that understanding can substitute for perception. It cannot. His genius is building an intelligence apparatus that compensates for his personal limitation. His tragedy is that the compensation is structurally incomplete.

**His TS 14 is developable.** Each use of the originary lock collection adds +1 to his hidden TS. At TS 14+, each use triggers a Spirit check (TN 7 Ob 1) for a Discovery Event. Vaynard could cross 30 and become a practitioner — the Duke of Varfell, Thread Sensitive, in a duchy that has built its entire strategic identity around managing Thread reality from the outside. If his TS develops, his consequentialist framework faces its ultimate test: Thread perception changes how you experience reality (P-10, epistemic seduction). Can a consequentialist absorb the perceptual shift without losing the analytical distance that makes him effective?

**Arc trajectory:** Vaynard arcs through knowledge acquisition or through personal transformation. If he reaches TK 5 without developing TS, he becomes the peninsula's most dangerous political actor — someone who understands Thread reality well enough to manipulate it through institutional leverage without ever experiencing the consequences of that manipulation personally. If his TS develops, he undergoes the shift that his framework is least equipped to handle: the dissolution of the subject-object distinction that consequentialism requires. You cannot evaluate consequences from outside when you are no longer outside.

## 6. Duchess Inge Baralta — The Contradiction She Cannot See

Baralta is the peninsula's most coherent political actor and the person least aware of her own central contradiction. She governs Hafenmark through categorical imperative — universalisable principles, legal precedent, constitutional authority. Her political framework is rigorous, internally consistent, and institutionally powerful. She also holds sincere religious faith — not cynical, not performative, genuine Solmundan devotion. Her faith and her politics are structurally incompatible (categorical imperative demands that principles be universalisable; divine command demands that principles derive from institutional authority). She holds both without experiencing contradiction. This is explicitly identified as "her strength and her vulnerability."

**How can she not see it?** Because the contradiction only manifests when faith and politics make competing claims on the same decision. In normal governance, they don't — constitutional law handles political questions, religious obligation handles spiritual ones. The domains are separate. The Sovereign Authority Doctrine (her unique action) is the moment they collide: she invokes the constitutional claim that her secular authority supersedes Church jurisdiction. This is a legal argument that implicitly denies divine command — if secular authority supersedes, then Solmund's revealed will (as interpreted by the Church) is not the highest law. Every time Baralta invokes the Doctrine, she is performing a constitutional act that contradicts her theological commitments. She does not experience this as a contradiction because the legal framework has its own internal logic that does not reference theology.

**Her Leadership Deviation cost is Ob 1 — the lowest of any faction.** This means Baralta IS Hafenmark. Her personal authority is so thoroughly embedded in the institution that acting against institutional tendency costs almost nothing — the institution will follow her. This is an extraordinary design choice: it means Hafenmark's ethical framework (categorical imperative) is really Baralta's ethical framework. If Baralta were removed, Hafenmark would need to find its own principles, and the PI-gated succession mechanic (PP-487) shows what that looks like — at PI < 4, the faction fractures. Hafenmark without Baralta is not a constitutional democracy. It is a personality cult that thinks it is a constitutional democracy.

**Arc trajectory:** Baralta arcs through the Sovereign Authority Doctrine or through the succession crisis. Doctrine: each invocation pushes TC down (good for Hafenmark) while exposing the faith-politics contradiction to Church retaliation (Heresy Investigation, Excommunication threat). If the Church Excommunicates Baralta, Hafenmark faces the choice its constitutional framework was designed to avoid: does secular authority really supersede divine command, or was that just a legal argument? Succession: if Baralta is eliminated, the PI-gated fork reveals Hafenmark's dependency. At PI ≥ 4, institutional succession works — the framework holds without her. At PI < 4, the framework fails — it was never the framework; it was Baralta.

## 7. Confessor Arne Himlensendt — The Sincere Wrong

Himlensendt is the most straightforwardly drawn faction leader, and this simplicity is narratively correct. He is the head of the Church of Solmund. He is sincerely devout. He has zero Thread Sensitivity (theologically foreclosed — his doctrinal framework precludes the possibility of Thread perception, and this foreclosure is itself a barrier to development). He is not cynical, not manipulative, not secretly aware that the Church's ontological claims are false. He is wrong. Simply, completely, sincerely wrong. The world is not what the Church says it is. Himlensendt believes with his whole being that it is.

**His Resonant Style is Evidence, which creates an ironic vulnerability.** Himlensendt responds to demonstrated evidence. If someone shows him evidence that contradicts Church doctrine, his Resonant Style means he cannot dismiss it without processing it. But his TS 0 means he cannot perceive the most relevant evidence (Thread-level phenomena). He is responsive to evidence he can see and blind to the evidence that matters most. A practitioner who demonstrates Thread operations in his presence is doing something Himlensendt can observe but cannot ontologically categorise. His rendering has no framework for what he's witnessing. He will default to the doctrinal explanation (heresy, demonic influence, deception) not from intellectual dishonesty but from the genuine absence of any alternative framework in his consciousness.

**His Leadership Deviation cost is Ob 3 — the highest of any faction.** This means the Church is the most doctrinally rigid institution on the peninsula. Himlensendt acting against doctrine costs more institutional stability than any other leader's deviation. This is correct: the Church's power derives from doctrinal consistency. Every deviation weakens the claim that the doctrine is revealed truth. A Church that deviates is a Church that admits its truth is contingent — and contingent truth is not divine.

**Arc trajectory:** Himlensendt has the narrowest arc range of any faction leader. He does not develop. He does not discover. He doubles down or he is removed. If he doubles down (TC continues rising, Church Seizure activates), the peninsula faces genuine theocratic governance led by a man who sincerely believes he is doing God's work. If he is removed (Excommunication politics, Cardinal rebellion, player intervention), the Church faces a succession crisis — who replaces the Confessor, and does the replacement believe what Himlensendt believed? The Klapp Awakening trajectory matters here: if Klapp's TS is revealed before Himlensendt's removal, the succession crisis includes a Thread-Sensitive Cardinal as a candidate.

## 8. Cardinal Magnus Klapp — The Scholar's Dilemma

Klapp is the Church's Cardinal of Temperance (Knowledge portfolio). He manages universities, observatories, monasteries, and archives. His Awakening event is already fully designed (three-trajectory branching: Suppress/Fracture/Convert) and is one of the best mechanical NPCs in the project.

**Klapp's defining feature is that his TS develops through intellectual engagement.** He is a scholar who has spent decades reading, cataloguing, and studying texts that the Church condemns. Some of these texts are Thread-significant. His TS growth doesn't come from exposure to Thread-touched objects (like Torsvald) or from proximity to Thread events (like Almud). It comes from understanding — from engaging so deeply with the intellectual framework of Thread metaphysics that his consciousness begins to render Thread-level structure. This is the most philosophically loaded TS development in the setting because it tests the boundary of P-08 (epistemological barrier). The barrier says non-sensitives cannot gain Thread-level knowledge through study alone. Klapp is not gaining Thread-level knowledge. He is developing the perceptual capacity that makes Thread-level knowledge possible — and he is doing it through study. The distinction is load-bearing.

**The three trajectories are genuinely different games.** Suppress: Klapp's TS is discovered by the Church, he is investigated, and either recants or is removed. The Church loses its scholarly capacity. Fracture: Klapp's awakening splits the Church internally — Temperance vs the other Cardinals. Church Stability collapses. Convert: Klapp defects, taking Thread-significant texts with him, and becomes a practitioner-scholar operating outside Church authority. Each trajectory reshapes the Church's institutional dynamics for the remainder of the campaign.

## 9. Cardinal Arnlod Olafsson — The Compromised Enforcer

Olafsson is the Church's Cardinal of Justice (Judicial portfolio). He runs the Inquisitors, Grand Adjudication, heresy investigations, and text suppression. He has a Niflhel connection. The Olafsson-Niflhel Exposure event card is already designed: if a faction executes an Overwhelming Intel action targeting Church in a territory with Niflhel syndicate presence, the connection is revealed — Church Stability −2, TC −3, Olafsson removed.

**The Niflhel connection is never explicitly defined as corruption.** The design says "Olafsson-Niflhel connection" and "Baralta evidence mechanic" without specifying what the connection IS. This ambiguity is productive. Olafsson could be compromised (taking bribes to suppress investigations of Niflhel operations), instrumentalised (using Niflhel intelligence networks to improve Church investigative reach — illegal but effective), or politically entangled (a pre-career relationship or debt that Niflhel leverages for access). Each interpretation produces a different Olafsson and a different Church scandal when the exposure fires.

**The most interesting version is the instrumentalist.** Olafsson genuinely believes heresy must be suppressed. He uses Niflhel intelligence to find heretics more effectively — paying Niflhel informants for tip-offs on practitioner activity, using smuggling routes to move Inquisitors covertly, leveraging black market networks for surveillance. He is doing his job better by breaking his own institution's rules. The connection is not personal corruption but institutional hypocrisy — the Church's chief law enforcer using criminals to enforce the law. When the exposure fires, the scandal is not "Olafsson was corrupt." The scandal is "the Church's justice system was always built on the thing it condemns."

**Relationship to Haelgrund (roster NPC):** Olafsson is Haelgrund's superior. Haelgrund is the Church's best field Inquisitor — the PROCEDURALIST who follows protocol to the letter. Olafsson is the man above him who breaks protocol to get results. If Haelgrund ever discovers that his superior is using Niflhel networks, the proceduralist's worldview collapses from a different direction than his hidden TS — not "I am what I investigate" but "my institution is what it condemns." Haelgrund under pressure from both his hidden TS AND Olafsson's compromised methodology is one of the most dramatically loaded NPC positions in the roster.

## 10. Cardinal Osten Jarnstal — The Soldier Who Outgrew His Chain of Command

Jarnstal is the Church's Cardinal of Fortitude (Military portfolio). He commands the Knights Templar, handles enforcement and monstrous incursion suppression. He is the Church's military arm. The Jarnstal Drift counter (0–3, GM-tracked, private) measures his progressive independence from the Confessor's authority. At 3, Church Military is controlled by Jarnstal, not Himlensendt.

**Jarnstal's drift is not rebellion.** It is competence without accountability. He deploys Templars without Confessor authorisation because he judges the threat correctly and acts on his judgment. He is right — the monstrous incursion needed suppression, the territorial attack needed defence, the enforcement action needed to happen this season not next. But each correct deployment without authorisation is a step toward institutional fragmentation. The counter never decrements. Once Jarnstal acts independently, the precedent is set. He does not learn caution from this because caution would have cost lives.

**The Independence card effect is precisely designed.** At Drift 3, Jarnstal doesn't rebel — he specialises. Church Military now deploys only against perceived threats to the Church (monstrous incursions, territorial attacks). It will NOT deploy for political operations (Seizure support, Heresy Investigation enforcement, TC-generating shows of force). This means the Church loses its military as a political instrument while retaining it as a defensive force. Jarnstal is the soldier who decided that fighting monsters is his job and politics is not. The Church is structurally weakened in every dimension except the one Jarnstal cares about.

**Historical parallel: the Praetorian problem.** Military commanders who begin as instruments of civilian authority and end as autonomous actors is Rome's central political story. Jarnstal is not ambitious — he does not want the Confessor's seat. He wants to fight the things that need fighting without waiting for permission from a theologian who doesn't understand the operational reality. This makes him more dangerous than an ambitious cardinal, because his motivation is harder to co-opt. Ambition can be bought. Professional conviction cannot.

## 11. Grandmaster Ehrenwall — The Ledger That Never Forgives

Ehrenwall is the Löwenritter Grandmaster and the setting's most mechanically precise NPC. The Coup Counter (0–3, never decrements) is a character study in six words: she keeps count and does not forgive. Three specific conditions increment it: unopposed Church advance (TC reaches 40 without Crown response), heir drift (Torben Loyalty ≤ 2), and military failure (Crown loses 2+ territories without response). Each condition represents a failure of Crown governance that Ehrenwall judges as existential. Her threshold is 3. She does not revise assessments.

**What makes Ehrenwall unique is the absence of ambiguity.** Every other faction leader operates in the space between their beliefs and their institutional constraints. Almud sympathises with the Restoration but governs through the settlement. Baralta holds incompatible faith and politics. Vaynard pursues Thread knowledge through a framework that cannot accommodate it. Himlensendt is sincerely wrong. Ehrenwall has no such gap. Her beliefs and her institutional role are perfectly aligned: the Löwenritter exists to ensure Valoria survives. If Crown governance fails to ensure survival, the Löwenritter replaces it. There is no internal conflict. There is only the counter.

**The counter never decrementing is the character.** A leader who forgives past failures and adjusts their assessment based on new information is a politician. Ehrenwall is not a politician. She is an auditor. Crown's failure at TC 40 is permanently recorded. Crown's subsequent success at TC reduction does not erase the failure — it is evidence that Crown CAN act but CHOSE not to act in time. This distinction matters to Ehrenwall because it means Crown's competence is not the issue. Crown's willingness is the issue. A willing Crown that occasionally fails is tolerable. An able Crown that occasionally refuses is not.

**She is the setting's most reliable NPC.** Every other NPC can be influenced, persuaded, bribed, threatened, or manipulated. Ehrenwall cannot. Her counter is private (GM-tracked). Her threshold is fixed. Her assessment criteria are public (players can know what increments the counter) but her current count is not. Players can count along if they're paying attention — but if they've miscounted, the coup arrives without warning. Ehrenwall is a clock that players can hear ticking but cannot read.

**Relationship to Brandt:** Ehrenwall and Brandt represent two versions of the same institutional commitment. Ehrenwall is patient (threshold 3, waits for the evidence to accumulate). Brandt is not (threshold 2 if he succeeds her, acts on the conviction that waiting is itself a form of failure). Ehrenwall's patience is the Löwenritter's political viability — the coup fires only when Crown has demonstrably failed, which gives the coup legitimacy. Brandt's impatience would produce a coup that fires before the evidence is overwhelming, which produces a coup that looks like a power grab rather than a correction. The Ehrenwall-to-Brandt succession is the transition from institutional correction to military takeover.

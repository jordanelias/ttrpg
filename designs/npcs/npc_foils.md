# PART ONE: AXIS ANALYSIS

## The Diamond's Axes

The four rulers occupy distinct positions on every significant political axis. No two-faction alliance is stable across all issues.

**Axis 1 — Relationship to the Post-War Settlement**

- Almud: custodian with ethical doubt (maintains the settlement while privately questioning whether the caste it includes is defensible)
- Lenneth: reformist within the settlement (wants to revise the Einhir suppression through Crown authority, strengthening the Crown in the process)
- Baralta: restructurer of the settlement (wants to restructure Crown-Church relations by making the Church subservient, while preserving and strengthening the caste's cultural suppression)
- Vaynard: revolutionary against the settlement (wants the entire post-war order dismantled — Church expelled, caste abolished, Altonian cultural residue eliminated)

**Axis 2 — Relationship to the Einhir/Caste Question**

- Almud: governance pragmatism + ethical doubt (TS 0, manages caste as stability variable, privately questions whether the system is defensible, does not act because genuinely uncertain)
- Lenneth: institutional revivalism (wants Crown patronage of Einhir cultural revival, caste dismantlement through royal authority, Thread work as eventual horizon — sincere + strategic)
- Baralta: pure adversary (Einhir revival threatens sovereign divine right; would suppress the Restoration Movement more aggressively than the Church; the caste is invisible to her universalist framework)
- Vaynard: revolutionary restoration (southern Einhir heritage, TS from environmental exposure, wants full cultural restoration including Thread work, sees the caste as colonial residue to be destroyed)

**Axis 3 — Relationship to the Church**

- Almud: pragmatic coexistence (Church consecrated his family's dynasty; confronting the Church undermines his own legitimation)
- Lenneth: strategic co-optation (wants to make the Church unable to prevent Crown-led Einhir revival, or make it serve the programme)
- Baralta: sovereign supremacy (Henry VIII — the Church must be made subservient to the Crown, not abolished but subordinated)
- Vaynard: expulsion (the Church is Altonia's institutional legacy; it must be removed entirely)

**Axis 4 — Basis of Authority**

- Almud: inherited deed-presumption (his father earned the throne; Almud must continuously re-earn it through governance)
- Lenneth: reform legitimacy (wants to add a new foundation to the Crown's deed-claim — "the family that reunited the Valnese people")
- Baralta: divine right earned through deed (deed-family claim + sovereign supremacy; the deed-logic's strongest candidate by her own governance record)
- Vaynard: revolutionary competence (von Lohengramm — will replace the system with something built on the only knowledge that matters: understanding of the Southernmost crisis)

## The Six Pairings

### Almud ↔ Lenneth: The Custodian and the Reformist

Almud and Lenneth share a household, a family, and a fundamental disagreement about whether the Crown should act on the Einhir question.

Lenneth has the moral conviction Almud's doubt prevents him from reaching. She believes the caste is wrong. She believes the Crown should be the instrument of reform. She has a strategic vision — Crown patronage of Einhir revival would permanently strengthen the deed-claim. Almud has the governance pragmatism Lenneth's conviction prevents her from fully weighing. He sees the costs: Mandate −2, TC +3, IP +1. These are real. But the deeper truth is that Almud has not decided whether acting is right, and Lenneth's certainty pressures him on a question he has not resolved.

Lenneth has been making her case within the royal household for years. Almud's ethical doubt may partly derive from her influence. But his doubt has not become her conviction. He listens because she is intelligent and her arguments are sound. He does not act because listening is not the same as deciding.

**The structural tension:** Lenneth's programme would strengthen the Crown permanently. Almud's inaction weakens it incrementally (Ehrenwall counts, Baralta positions, Restoration drifts). Lenneth sees this. She finds his uncertainty insufficient. From her perspective, a man who questions the system and does nothing is failing the deed-logic's own standard — the throne requires deeds, and Almud is not performing the deed that matters most.

**Historical echo: Manuel I Komnenos and Empress Eirene.** Manuel managed external threats through strategic patience. His wife pressed for internal reform. Both were intelligent. Neither could fully occupy the other's perspective because their structural positions demanded different time horizons.

---

### Almud ↔ Baralta: The Custodian and the Claimant

Two people who both operate within the post-war settlement's framework. One maintains it through pragmatic governance. The other wants the other's job.

Almud manages six pressure vectors. Baralta believes she could manage them better. Their rivalry is not ideological — it is personal and institutional. Both accept the settlement's basic logic. Neither is trying to change the fundamental structure (Baralta wants to restructure Crown-Church relations, but this is a revision within the settlement, not an overthrow). Their disagreement is about competence: who is the better custodian?

The deed-monarchy's logic makes this rivalry structurally dangerous. If the Crown's legitimacy depends on continuous performance, and Baralta's governance record (Mandate 7, Ob 1 deviation, decades of institutional command) exceeds Almud's by several metrics, then the deed-logic itself argues for Baralta. She knows this. Almud knows she knows. His management of Baralta — tolerating her parliamentary challenges, respecting her Theocracy Counter suppression, not directly confronting her institutional positioning — is the governance of a man who understands that his rival's case is strong and that confrontation would clarify the comparison in her favour.

**The Einhir axis separates them.** Almud has ethical doubt about the caste. Baralta does not see it. She is the caste's most effective structural defender: her universalism encoded the post-war settlement's power arrangements, her sovereign divine right requires the theological monopoly that suppresses Einhir heritage, and she would suppress the Restoration more aggressively than the Church. If Baralta takes the Crown, the caste hardens permanently. Almud's doubt — uncertain, unresolved, inert — is paradoxically the caste's only opening within the current power structure. An uncertain custodian might eventually decide. A certain claimant has already decided.

**Historical echo: Manuel I Komnenos and Justinian I.** Manuel governed with awareness of contradiction. Justinian codified contradiction out of existence. Almud carries the weight of seeing the full problem. Baralta carries the advantage of seeing only the part her framework can process.

---

### Almud ↔ Vaynard: The Manager and the Revolutionary

Almud sees Vaynard as a governance input — a restive duke to be managed through accommodation. Trade concessions, tolerated autonomy, studied neutrality on caste questions. Vaynard sees Almud as the system's complicit custodian — a man who maintains the structure that oppresses Vaynard's people through competent administration rather than conviction.

They are not disagreeing about tactics. They are disagreeing about whether the Einhir question is a governance problem (Almud) or an existential crisis (Vaynard). Almud does not understand what Vaynard is fighting for. He processes "southern Einhir dissatisfaction" as a stability variable. Vaynard is not dissatisfied. He is fighting for the survival of a cultural heritage, the reversal of a colonial injustice, and the resolution of an ontological crisis that threatens the peninsula's existence. Almud cannot perceive any of these because his TS is 0, his framework is administrative, and the Forgetting prevents the Southernmost argument from reaching anyone who cannot already perceive Thread reality.

**The structural irony:** Vaynard's intelligence apparatus depends on the equilibrium Almud maintains. If Crown destabilised, Varfell's carefully positioned operations would lose the stable environment they exploit. Almud is an invisible subsidy to Vaynard's revolutionary preparations. Neither acknowledges this because acknowledging it would require confronting the relationship honestly.

---

### Lenneth ↔ Baralta: The Campaign's Defining Confrontation

Both are the most politically capable women in their respective institutions. Both operate at the highest level of power available to them. The difference: Lenneth has the title, Baralta has the authority.

**Lenneth wants to use Crown authority to revive Einhir heritage.** This would add a new legitimation dimension to the deed-claim, making the Crown "the family that reunited the Valnese people."

**Baralta wants Crown authority for herself and would suppress Einhir heritage.** Her sovereign divine right requires theological monopoly. Einhir revival introduces a third source of authority — older than Solmund, incompatible with both Church and Crown theology — that delegitimises Baralta's framework.

Their programmes are mutually exclusive. Lenneth's success terminates Baralta's Crown ambition (a Crown that derives legitimacy from Einhir revival cannot be held by a woman who suppresses Einhir heritage). Baralta's success terminates Lenneth's programme (a Baralta reign would suppress the Restoration on day one).

**Historical echo (Lenneth's side): Catherine the Great.** Enlightened monarch who strengthened the autocracy by making it the source of modernisation.

**Historical echo (Baralta's side): Theodora / Isabella I.** Theodora's steel — she would ride the institution into the ground before admitting it was wrong. Isabella's method — political alliance, Church support, strategic positioning to make herself the only viable candidate. Baralta has Theodora's absolutism and Isabella's patience.

**The confrontation tests the categorical imperative against the Restoration's Rawlsian claims.** Baralta's universalism was constructed on the post-war settlement. It universalised that moment's power arrangements. The universalism is contingent — it does not know it. Lenneth, pressing from the throne, would argue that the framework needs revision. Baralta, defending from Parliament (or from her own Crown claim), would argue that revision through proper procedure is acceptable but revision that undermines sovereign divine right is heresy. Both are right about their own frameworks. Both are defending positions that serve their institutional interests.

---

### Lenneth ↔ Vaynard: Diagnosis Without Agreement on Treatment

They agree on the diagnosis: the caste is wrong, Einhir heritage should be restored. They disagree fundamentally on method.

Lenneth wants to build up — incorporate Einhir heritage into existing institutions, use Crown authority to drive cultural revival, reform from within. Vaynard wants to tear down — expel the Church, dismantle the settlement, start over. Lenneth sees existing institutions as raw material. Vaynard sees them as the enemy.

Vaynard would regard Lenneth's approach as inadequate — reform within the system preserves the system, and the system is the problem. Lenneth would regard Vaynard's approach as reckless — destroying institutions you might need produces chaos, not justice.

Lenneth has a structural advantage: she is inside the Crown. Her programme can be enacted through royal authority. Vaynard has an epistemological advantage: he understands Thread reality and the Southernmost crisis. Lenneth's cultural revival without Thread understanding is incomplete. Vaynard's Thread knowledge without institutional power is inert. They need each other and cannot recognise it because their methods are incompatible.

**Historical echo: Lenneth as Catherine, Vaynard as Reinhard von Lohengramm.** The enlightened reformer and the revolutionary who both want a better world and would destroy each other getting there.

---

### Baralta ↔ Vaynard: Total Opposition

The duchy-level pairing. No common ground exists.

Baralta's constitutional framework universalised the post-war settlement — which includes the caste. Vaynard wants to dismantle the settlement — which means dismantling Baralta's framework. Their rivalry is a contest between the northern establishment that built its authority on southern exclusion and the southern duchy that has been building revolutionary capability in secret for decades.

**Ethical frameworks are diametrically opposed:** Categorical imperative (universalisable principles, no special authority) vs consequentialist pragmatism (outcomes only). Baralta's framework cannot accommodate Vaynard — consequentialism cannot be universalised. Vaynard's framework cannot accommodate Baralta — categorical imperative is institutional rigidity under existential pressure.

**The caste axis makes the opposition existential, not just philosophical.** Baralta is the caste's most effective structural defender (sovereign divine right + theological monopoly + universalism built on the settlement). Vaynard is the caste's most determined opponent (southern heritage + revolutionary programme + the only person who understands why the caste must end for the peninsula to survive). They are not debating governance philosophy. They are fighting over whether Vaynard's people deserve to exist as a distinct cultural group.

**But they might need each other.** Crown weakness could force an operational coalition. Baralta's parliamentary apparatus and Vaynard's intelligence network, combined, would be the peninsula's most effective governance structure. The coalition would be unstable — neither framework tolerates the other — but effective. The instability IS the drama.

# PART TWO: SUBJECTIVE PERSPECTIVES & EXTENDED FOIL NETWORK

# PART ONE: THE DIAMOND FROM INSIDE

Each ruler sees the other three through the lens of their own conviction, ethical framework, and structural position. These are not objective assessments. They are the views that govern how each ruler acts toward the others — and the blind spots those views create.

---

## Almud's View

**Conviction:** Order/Reason. **Resonant Style:** Consequence. **TS:** 0. **Ethics:** Governance pragmatism with ethical doubt.

**Historical anchor: Manuel I Komnenos.** Byzantine emperor who managed six simultaneous threats through strategic patience. His critics called him indecisive. He was playing six boards at once. Almud is Manuel: the ruler whose competent restraint appears, from any single vantage point, to be inaction.

### On Lenneth

She is the argument he has not resolved. Not because she is wrong — because she may be right, and his uncertainty about whether the caste system is defensible means he cannot dismiss her case. Every time she presses him on Einhir restoration, he considers the costs: Mandate −2, TC +3, IP +1. But the deeper truth is that the costs are convenient shelter for a question he has not answered. If he were certain the caste was indefensible, he would find a way to act — he has managed six pressure vectors for 27 years.

Lenneth's strategic vision (Crown patronage of cultural revival permanently strengthens the deed-claim) is sound. Almud can see it is sound. But sound strategy on an unresolved ethical question produces paralysis, not action. He cannot implement a programme he has not decided is right. Lenneth finds his uncertainty insufficient. He finds her certainty premature.

**What he cannot see:** That his uncertainty is itself a decision. Every season he holds the question open is a season the caste operates with his implicit endorsement. Lenneth sees this. Almud does not — because to him, uncertainty is the absence of a decision, not the presence of one.

### On Baralta

A rival whose case is strong and whose confrontation he avoids for exactly that reason. Baralta governs a tighter scope with fewer adversaries and does it well — Mandate 7, Ob 1 deviation cost, decades of institutional command. The deed-monarchy's own logic argues for her. Almud knows this. His management of Baralta — tolerating her parliamentary challenges, not directly confronting her positioning — is the governance of a man who understands that making the comparison explicit serves her.

He respects her competence while recognising her scope is contained. Hafenmark faces Church and Crown. Crown faces everything. But he cannot use this argument publicly without appearing to plead — the competent ruler does not explain why his job is harder. He simply does it.

What Almud does not know — or suspects but has not confirmed through his intelligence channels — is whether Baralta's Crown ambition is a contingency or a campaign. If it is a campaign, his management strategy is inadequate. He is tolerating a rival who is actively building toward the moment she replaces him.

### On Vaynard

A governance input. A restive duke managing a stigmatised duchy whose satisfaction requires accommodation within the existing framework — trade concessions, tolerated autonomy, studied neutrality on caste questions. Almud processes Varfell the way a competent administrator processes any unstable region: manage the pressures, prevent escalation, preserve the equilibrium.

Almud does not understand what Vaynard is fighting for. He sees southern Einhir dissatisfaction, Varfell intelligence operations, Thread investigations, and Southernmost proximity — and processes all of it as factional positioning, not existential conviction. He cannot perceive Vaynard's revolutionary vision because his own framework has no category for it. Administrating a kingdom and overthrowing its foundational logic are not activities Almud's governance pragmatism can hold simultaneously.

**What Almud does see that others miss:** Vaynard's intelligence apparatus depends on the equilibrium Almud maintains. He knows he is an invisible subsidy to Vaynard's operations. He has not confronted Vaynard with this because the information is worth more held than deployed.

## Lenneth's View

**Conviction:** To be determined / Family-institutional. **Resonant Style:** To be determined. **TS:** 0 (can gain through research). **Ethics:** Institutional empowerment through Einhir revival.

**Historical anchor: Catherine the Great.** Strengthened the autocracy by making it the source of modernisation.

### On Almud

Her husband, the man she has been trying to move for years. She understands his governance pragmatism — she lives inside the Crown, she sees the pressures, she knows the costs are real. But she has reached a moral conviction he has not: the caste is wrong. Her frustration with Almud is not that he disagrees — it is that he has not decided. She could argue against disagreement. She cannot argue against uncertainty, because uncertainty presents no position to engage.

Lenneth respects Almud's competence. Crown Mandate 5 is earned. She does not think he is weak or cowardly. She thinks he is incomplete — a brilliant administrator who has not yet become the ruler the peninsula needs. Her programme (Crown patronage of Einhir revival) is simultaneously a moral vision and a gift to his legitimacy. She is offering him the deed that would permanently strengthen his family's claim. His inability to accept it is the thing she finds most difficult about their marriage.

### On Baralta

The collision that defines the campaign. Lenneth sees Baralta clearly: a woman of extraordinary capability whose framework is structurally incapable of accommodating Einhir justice. Baralta's universalism encoded the post-war settlement. Her sovereign divine right requires theological monopoly. Her Crown ambition would terminate Lenneth's programme.

Lenneth regards Baralta with the specific wariness of someone who recognises a rival operating on a different axis entirely. Lenneth is fighting for cultural justice. Baralta is fighting for power. These goals collide not because they are opposed in principle but because Baralta's power requires suppressing what Lenneth's justice demands. The confrontation is not personal. It is structural. And Lenneth understands that Baralta would be a formidable monarch — just one whose reign would permanently foreclose the thing Lenneth cares about most.

### On Vaynard

The man who shares her diagnosis but whose treatment would kill the patient. Lenneth agrees that the caste is wrong and Einhir heritage should be restored. She disagrees with everything else about Vaynard's approach. Expelling the Church destroys the institution that could be co-opted to serve revival. Overthrowing the settlement destroys the Crown authority that could drive reform. Vaynard's revolution would produce chaos from which no institutional patron of Einhir culture would emerge.

Lenneth sees in Vaynard a version of herself without institutional constraint — and recognises that the constraint is what makes her vision achievable. She can build. He can only destroy. But she also recognises that Vaynard understands something she does not: Thread reality, the Southernmost crisis, the ontological dimension of Einhir heritage that her cultural programme does not yet address. Her eventual horizon (Thread work) is Vaynard's present reality. She may need his knowledge even as she rejects his methods.

## Baralta's View

**Conviction:** Constitutional governance / Sovereign divine right. **Resonant Style:** Evidence. **TS:** 0. **Ethics:** Categorical imperative + Henry VIII sovereign supremacy.

**Historical anchor: Isabella I of Castile / Theodora.** Isabella's strategic patience in building an unassailable claim. Theodora's absolutism — she would ride the institution into the ground before admitting it was wrong.

### On Almud

The incumbent. Baralta respects Almud's governance record — Crown Mandate 5 is evidence of genuine capability. She does not underestimate him. But she believes she would govern better, and she believes this with the same categorical certainty she applies to everything.

Almud's uncertainty about the caste question is, from Baralta's perspective, not a sign of moral depth but of insufficient conviction. A ruler should know what they believe. The settlement is the settlement. The constitutional framework applies universally. The fact that Almud sometimes privately questions whether the framework is just is evidence that he is uncertain about his own principles — and the deed-monarchy's logic demands rulers who are certain. Her own certainty is the argument her framework makes for her candidacy.

She observes Almud's Altonian accommodations, his Schoenland manoeuvring, his Varfell appeasement — and sees a ruler who manages problems rather than solving them. She would solve them. The Church would be subordinated through the Sovereign Authority Doctrine, expanded to permanent royal prerogative. Altonia would be handled through military deterrence backed by constitutional authority. Varfell would be brought into constitutional order. These are not fantasies — they are the extrapolation of her Hafenmark governance record to a larger scope. She has done this at ducal scale. She is building toward doing it at kingdom scale.

### On Lenneth

The Queen who wants to change the wrong thing. Baralta's framework has no space for Lenneth's Einhir revivalism. Einhir cultural restoration is a particularist claim — specific rights for a specific group based on specific historical grievances. This cannot be universalised. It cannot be codified into constitutional principle without creating an exception to the universal framework. And the Einhir tradition represents a source of authority that predates Solmund and delegitimises both Church theology and sovereign divine right.

Baralta does not regard Lenneth as an enemy. She regards Lenneth as mistaken — a capable woman pursuing a programme that would undermine the constitutional foundation of the polity she inhabits. If Baralta takes the Crown, Lenneth's programme ends. This is not personal. It is the consequence of Baralta's principles applied consistently.

### On Vaynard

Baralta cannot perceive Vaynard as anything other than a threat to constitutional order. His consequentialism — evaluating actions purely by outcomes, treating means as irrelevant — is the antithesis of universalisable principle. His intelligence operations violate procedural norms. His southern Einhir heritage, his Thread investigations, his expulsion agenda — all of it represents the collapse of the framework Baralta has spent decades building.

She does not hate Vaynard. She is indifferent to him as a person and opposed to everything he represents as a political force. If she takes the Crown, Varfell will be brought into constitutional order. The intelligence operations will end. The Thread investigations will be suppressed — not because they are dangerous (though they are) but because they occur outside the legal framework. The expulsion agenda will be dismissed as unconstitutional. Vaynard will be offered the choice every subject receives: comply with the law or face its consequences.

## Vaynard's View

**Conviction:** Einhir restoration / Revolutionary competence. **Resonant Style:** To be determined. **TS:** Environmental (southern Einhir baseline). **Ethics:** Consequentialism.

**Historical anchor: Reinhard von Lohengramm.** The revolutionary who will replace the corrupt order with something built on competence and truth.

### On Almud

The system's custodian. Almud maintains the structure that oppresses Vaynard's people through competent administration. He is not malicious — he is worse. He is indifferent. He manages "southern Einhir dissatisfaction" as a line item. He accommodates Varfell through trade concessions and studied neutrality — gestures that demonstrate he understands the problem exists while confirming he will never address it.

Vaynard's contempt for Almud is professional, not personal. Almud is competent at the wrong things. His governance pragmatism is precisely calibrated to maintain an unjust system efficiently. A less competent ruler might create the instability Vaynard needs. Almud's competence is the dam that holds back the flood — and Vaynard is the flood.

What fuels the contempt most: Almud does not understand what Vaynard is fighting for. He processes Vaynard as a factional actor with grievances to be managed. The revolutionary whose people were excluded from the founding settlement is, to the settlement's custodian, a management challenge. The reduction itself is the insult.

### On Lenneth

The closest thing to an ally in the Crown, and therefore the most frustrating person on the peninsula. Lenneth shares the diagnosis. Lenneth wants to fix it through Crown authority. Lenneth's method — institutional reform, gradual cultural revival, Thread work as an eventual horizon — is, to Vaynard, the approach of someone who has never been on the receiving end of the caste. Lenneth wants to revive Einhir heritage from the throne. Vaynard wants to tear down the throne and let the heritage speak for itself.

But Lenneth has something Vaynard lacks: institutional access. If Vaynard could use Lenneth's Crown position to advance the programme faster than institutional reform would normally allow — if he could convince her that "eventual horizon" must become "immediate priority" because the Southernmost crisis will not wait — then the alliance has value. The question is whether Lenneth would accept the acceleration. She would not, because her method requires institutional stability and acceleration requires institutional disruption. They agree on the destination and cannot share the vehicle.

### On Baralta

The enemy. Not because Baralta has done Vaynard personal harm, but because Baralta's framework is the most complete, most coherent, most institutionally powerful expression of everything Vaynard wants to destroy. The post-war settlement, the caste, the Church-Crown theological monopoly, the suppression of Einhir heritage — Baralta has codified all of it into a constitutional framework of breathtaking internal consistency. Her universalism makes the oppression invisible by making it principled. Her sovereign divine right makes the theological monopoly permanent by making it constitutional.

Baralta is Justinian codifying Roman law: the system's most sophisticated defender, whose sophistication makes the system's injustices harder to see and harder to challenge. Vaynard does not underestimate her. He respects her capability and despises her conclusions. If she takes the Crown, the caste hardens into constitutional permanence and the Einhir heritage is suppressed not by institutional inertia but by sovereign will. Baralta's Crown would be the worst outcome for everything Vaynard cares about — worse than Almud's maintenance, because maintenance can be eroded while constitutional law endures.

# PART TWO: THE EXTENDED FOIL NETWORK

## Himlensendt's Position Relative to the Diamond

Himlensendt stands outside the ruler diamond but intersects with every pairing. He is the structural keystone — the person whose sincere faith holds the entire post-war settlement together.

**With Almud:** Pragmatic coexistence. The Church consecrated the Almqvist line. Himlensendt does not challenge Almud's governance because Almud does not challenge the Church's position. The equilibrium is stable as long as neither side disrupts it. Almud's ethical doubt about the caste does not trouble Himlensendt because Almud does not act on it. If Almud acted — if the Discovery Event fires and Almud develops Thread Sensitivity — the equilibrium shatters.

**With Lenneth:** Pastoral concern. Lenneth's Einhir revivalism is, to Himlensendt, a spiritual danger — not political opposition. He would respond with education, charity, missionary outreach — the pastoral tools that simultaneously address Lenneth's concerns and deepen the perceptual prophylaxis. He does not see Lenneth as an enemy. He sees her as a well-meaning soul pursuing a path that leads away from truth.

**With Baralta:** The consecration crisis. Baralta claims sovereign divine right superseding Church jurisdiction. Himlensendt's theology says divine authority flows through the Church. If Baralta claims the Crown, Himlensendt must decide whether to consecrate — installing his own superior — or refuse — exposing consecration as political veto power. His theology compels refusal. His refusal cracks the settlement. [EDITORIAL: ED-407 — open design question.]

**With Vaynard:** Existential heresy. Vaynard claims Solmund was a threadcut being, not a divine saviour. Vaynard has Thread Sensitivity and actively investigates Thread knowledge. This is not a jurisdictional dispute (like Baralta's). It is an ontological attack on the Church's foundational event. Himlensendt would classify Vaynard as the peninsula's most dangerous heretic and respond institutionally — Heresy Investigation, Inquisitor deployment, Olafsson unleashed. The shepherd's duty to protect the flock overrides pastoral patience.

## Ehrenwall's Position Relative to the Diamond

Ehrenwall is the deed-logic's enforcement mechanism. Her Coup Counter is not treason — it is the system working as designed.

**With Almud:** Professional assessment. Is the current Almqvist still earning the throne? Every Counter increment is a data point toward "no." Ehrenwall knew the first Almqvist — the war hero, the founder. She has a standard of comparison Almud cannot meet because the standard was set by a war hero and Almud governs in peacetime.

**With Baralta:** The strongest succession candidate by the deed-logic's own metrics. Ehrenwall's post-coup calculus includes Baralta. Assessment: competent governor, strong institutional base, weak military. A Baralta Crown requires Löwenritter support to survive — which gives Ehrenwall leverage she does not currently have over Hafenmark. [EDITORIAL: ED-406]

**With Lenneth:** Lenneth's programme (Einhir revival through Crown authority) is irrelevant to Ehrenwall's calculus. Ehrenwall assesses governance competence, not policy direction. If Lenneth's programme strengthens Crown Mandate, Ehrenwall's Counter slows. If it costs Mandate, it accelerates.

**With Vaynard:** A factional actor whose intelligence operations are noted but not prioritised. Ehrenwall's focus is the Crown's performance, not Varfell's ambitions — unless Vaynard's actions directly weaken Crown governance (at which point his operations become Counter data).

## Triangular Foils

### Almud — Lenneth — Baralta: The Succession Triangle

All three occupy positions on the Crown's future. Almud holds it through inherited deed-presumption and competent governance. Lenneth wants to strengthen it through Einhir revival. Baralta wants it for herself. If Almud falls, Lenneth's programme requires Crown authority she no longer holds (unless she takes the Crown herself — cognatic succession makes her eligible). Baralta's candidacy is the deed-logic's obvious choice. Lenneth's counter-claim is "the family that earned the throne should keep it — and I will make the Crown worthy of keeping." The triangle resolves into a question: is the deed-claim held by the family or by the most capable individual?

### Lenneth — Baralta — Vaynard: The Einhir Triangle

Three positions on the Einhir question: institutional revival (Lenneth), suppression through sovereign right (Baralta), revolutionary restoration (Vaynard). No two can ally without excluding the third. Lenneth + Vaynard agree on the diagnosis but not the treatment. Lenneth + Baralta share institutional orientation but oppose on the Einhir question entirely. Vaynard + Baralta agree on nothing. The triangle has no stable resolution — whichever position wins, the other two lose.

### Almud — Baralta — Himlensendt: The Consecration Triangle

The deed-monarchy's legitimation depends on Church consecration. Almud holds consecration. Baralta wants it. Himlensendt controls it. If Baralta claims the Crown, Himlensendt must choose — consecrate and install his own superior, or refuse and crack the settlement. Almud's interest: prevent the question from being asked. If it is asked, his own consecration's legitimacy is implicitly questioned.

### Almud — Vaynard — Ehrenwall: The Competence Triangle

Almud governs competently but without conviction. Vaynard has conviction but lacks institutional authority. Ehrenwall assesses competence without caring about conviction. If Ehrenwall's Counter fires because Almud's governance fails, the successor question includes Vaynard only if the deed-logic is interpreted as "most capable regardless of framework" rather than "most capable within the existing settlement." Ehrenwall would not select Vaynard — his programme requires destroying the institution she is loyal to. But Vaynard's competence is real, and ignoring it in a deed-monarchy is intellectually dishonest. The triangle exposes the deed-logic's limit: it selects for competence within the system, not competence to replace the system.

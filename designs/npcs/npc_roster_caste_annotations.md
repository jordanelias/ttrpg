## 14. Caste System Impact on Roster NPCs

The 2026-04-11 reframing establishes the Einhir caste system as a geographic gradient radiating from the Southernmost, with southern Einhir populations stigmatised, higher baseline Thread Sensitivity (TS), lower Church penetration, and structural exclusion from the post-war settlement. The following annotations identify how each roster NPC intersects with the caste system's structural incentives.

### NPCs with direct caste implications:

**Maret Uln (#2):** Southern Einhir solidarity. She grew up near Einhir communities in the western fjords — specifically southern Einhir communities in Varfell territory, the stigmatised population. Her Restoration Movement (RM) sympathy is not abstract; it is solidarity with her own people. If she succeeds Vaynard (PP-486), Varfell shifts from revolutionary expulsion (Vaynard's von Lohengramm programme) to Rawlsian equity — a fundamentally different programme that the RM would experience as both a relief and a betrayal of Vaynard's maximalist promise.

**Maret Vossen (#3):** Needs positioning on Lenneth's institutional revivalism. The RM is internally split between pragmatic acceptance of Crown patronage (any advancement is good) and rejection of Crown authority (the Crown maintained the caste for 45 years; why trust it now?). Vossen herself would probably be cautiously open to Lenneth. The RM base includes people who reject Crown authority entirely. This internal tension is a design gap requiring resolution. [EDITORIAL: ED-NEW-24]

**Sæmund Haelgrund (#4):** Second conscience axis. His investigations disproportionately target southern Einhir communities (higher baseline TS, more Thread-adjacent activity). His proceduralism means he documents everything — including the pattern of systemic ethnic targeting. A proceduralist who follows evidence will eventually notice that the Church's enforcement is functionally ethnic persecution, regardless of its theological framing. Two suppressions, not one: TS is natural AND the Church systematically persecutes a specific population.

**Annika Feldhaus (#7):** The Guilds are structurally invested in maintaining the caste system for economic reasons. The Thread-touched supply chain sources from "Einhir ruins and southern communities" — the stigmatised population's cultural heritage, sold as contraband antiquities. The caste system prices these goods: suppression creates scarcity, scarcity creates value. If the caste were dismantled and Einhir heritage normalised, the contraband premium evaporates. Feldhaus's profit-maximising AI means the Guilds defend the caste through economic incentive, even though Feldhaus herself probably has no opinion on the Einhir question. The system's incentives align against reform independent of anyone's beliefs.

**Peder Almstedt (#8):** Deed-monarchy institutional memory. Almstedt served under two monarchs — the founder (first Almqvist) and Almud. He has institutional memory of the entire dynasty. He is the one person in Parliament who remembers the founding settlement's original terms. His proceduralism preserves the specific system the first Almqvist negotiated — including the caste. His conservative AI blocks reforms that would alter the founding settlement: Lenneth's programme (revise Einhir suppression), Baralta's programme (revise Crown-Church relationship), Vaynard's programme (overturn settlement entirely). He protects the deal. The deal included the caste.

**Gerik Strand (#9):** The deed-monarchy reframing is transformative. Strand's meritocratic conviction — competence should determine standing — is the founding principle the kingdom has abandoned. The first Almqvist earned the throne through deeds. The system has calcified into hereditary monarchy where born aristocrats hold permanent positions. Strand is more loyal to the founding logic than the founding family. Anyone who tells him "you are what this kingdom was supposed to produce" is speaking to his deepest conviction. This makes him a potential ideological ally of any faction that wants to return to deed-logic: Baralta (deed-claim for the Crown), Lenneth (strengthen Crown through new deeds), or Vaynard (tear down the ossified settlement).

**Dalla Virke (#10):** Her supply chain sources from southern Einhir communities selling their own cultural heritage as contraband because the legitimate economy excludes them. Virke's "network protector" AI means she shields these suppliers from exposure. She is, accidentally, one of the few people on the peninsula who interacts with southern Einhir as economic equals. Her ethics ("your word is your network") apply regardless of caste. Deed-logic applied to commerce.

**Doux Alexios Laskaris (#11):** The Altonian containment reframing enriches his position. Altonia created the Church's containment grant. Laskaris's oikonomia ethics is the Altonian administrative tradition — the same containment-through-accommodation that created Himmelenger, practiced at the provincial level.

**Prudence Cardinal (#13):** Tithe maximisation hits southern Einhir communities harder — lower Church penetration means less institutional relationship, so aggressive extraction feels more like imposition. The Parish Revolt event card has a caste dimension: revolting parishes are disproportionately southern, feeding RM recruitment in territories where RM sentiment is already strongest. The Prudence Cardinal is accidentally radicalising the south through economic extraction.

### NPCs strengthened without textual changes:

**Edeyja (#1):** Her isolation is now social as well as geographic — she maintains knowledge in the territory the caste system blames for the Catastrophe.

**Sigrid Torsvald (#5):** Her bridge role between Lenneth's political vision and practitioner reality is enriched — she can tell Lenneth what Thread work actually IS, not the abstract cultural concept.

**Halvar Brandt (#6):** His external threat fixation creates a power vacuum in internal territories on succession — both the coercive apparatus that could enforce the caste AND the apparatus that could protect Einhir communities are withdrawn from internal deployment.

**Rikard Solberg (#12):** Schoenland may stoke caste tensions as a destabilisation vector. Solberg would underreport this because reporting honestly increases the instability that keeps him posted away from home.

### Key structural finding:

The caste system is load-bearing for the entire NPC ecosystem. Feldhaus and Virke profit from it. Almstedt preserves it. The Prudence Cardinal's extraction deepens it. Haelgrund enforces it. Strand's frustration is a rebuke of its ossification. The NPCs collectively reproduce the caste without any of them intending to — which is how real caste systems work: through structural incentives, not individual malice.

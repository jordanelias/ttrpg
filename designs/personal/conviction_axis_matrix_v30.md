## §1 Statement

Each of the 13 Convictions (PP-684 §2) projects onto the 4-axis substrate (PP-687 §2.4) via a 13 × 4 matrix. The matrix is the engine's *interpretive substrate*: it determines how a Conviction-vector character experiences events, computes Cascade Fidelity, and projects symbolic dimensions for Key emissions.

This document presents the canonical matrix with **per-row Renaissance-grounded calibration rationale**. Each entry is justified by the period source from which the Conviction is drawn.

## §3 Per-Row Calibration Rationale

### §3.1 Faith — `[+0.4, +0.9, -0.3, +0.6]`

**Hierarchical +0.4:** Faith places one within an ecclesiastical order (Pope → Cardinals → Bishops → priests → laity) and a cosmological hierarchy (God → angels → human → animal → matter). Hierarchical is moderate-to-strong but not maximal — Faith's primary axis is *sacred*, not hierarchical.

**Sacred +0.9:** The defining axis. Faith is *the* numinous Conviction in the period — devotion to invisible authority, ritual centrality, oath-binding to divine.

**Instrumental -0.3:** Faith is principled rather than calculative; the believer accepts hardship for theological reasons rather than calculated payoff. Mildly anti-instrumental.

**Traditional +0.6:** Faith is grounded in scripture and tradition (apostolic succession, doctrinal continuity); strongly conservative. Not maximal because Faith can also be reformist (Restoration's faith-grounded reformism), so traditional is moderate-to-strong rather than +0.9.

### §3.2 Authority — `[+0.9, +0.2, +0.1, +0.4]`

**Hierarchical +0.9:** The defining axis. Authority is the conviction that legitimate command flows top-down through proper channels.

**Sacred +0.2:** Some sacralization (royal anointing, *imperium* as God-granted), but Authority is primarily secular hierarchy. Mild.

**Instrumental +0.1:** Authority is procedurally justified rather than instrumentally — but pragmatic command does instrumentalize. Near-zero.

**Traditional +0.4:** Authority claims continuity (royal lines, traditional offices). Moderate.

### §3.3 Order — `[+0.5, -0.1, +0.2, +0.4]`

**Hierarchical +0.5:** Procedural order respects rank because rank is *part of* the procedure. Moderate-to-strong.

**Sacred -0.1:** Order is procedural rather than sacred. Near-zero, slightly secular (Order can replace sacred authority with procedural).

**Instrumental +0.2:** Procedural order is means-to-ends thinking applied to institutional regularity. Mild.

**Traditional +0.4:** Procedures derive from past practice. Moderate.

### §3.4 Scholastic — `[+0.1, +0.2, +0.3, -0.1]`

**Hierarchical +0.1:** Scholastic respects intellectual rank (master, doctor, scholar) but inquiry is largely peer-evaluative within that hierarchy. Near-zero.

**Sacred +0.2:** Scholastic-and-Faith were tightly bound (Aquinas), but the Conviction itself is the inquiry-mode, not the doctrinal content. Mild.

**Instrumental +0.3:** Reasoned inquiry is *means* to truth — instrumentalizes evidence and argument for understanding. Moderate.

**Traditional -0.1:** Scholastic respects tradition (commentary on classical texts) but also generates *new* knowledge — slightly progressive. Near-zero, slightly anti-traditional.

### §3.5 Utility — `[-0.1, -0.5, +0.9, -0.4]`

**Hierarchical -0.1:** Utility ignores rank if rank impedes outcomes. Near-zero, slightly egalitarian.

**Sacred -0.5:** Utility is the secular Conviction — explicitly contrasts with sacred motives. Strongly anti-sacred.

**Instrumental +0.9:** The defining axis. Utility *is* instrumentalism: the means-to-ends Conviction.

**Traditional -0.4:** Utility breaks tradition when tradition impedes outcomes. Moderately progressive.

### §3.6 Equity — `[-0.4, +0.2, -0.2, -0.2]`

**Hierarchical -0.4:** Equity argues *against* unjust hierarchy but does not deny rank entirely. Moderately egalitarian.

**Sacred +0.2:** Equity has theological grounding (canon-law fairness, *aequitas*). Mild.

**Instrumental -0.2:** Equity is principled (fairness as principle) rather than calculative. Mild anti-instrumental.

**Traditional -0.2:** Equity often reforms unjust traditions (sumptuary law adjustments, aequitas correction of harsh law). Mild progressive.

### §3.7 Liberty — `[-0.7, -0.2, +0.1, -0.5]`

**Hierarchical -0.7:** The defining axis (in negative). *Libertas* = freedom from imposed authority. Strongly egalitarian.

**Sacred -0.2:** Liberty is largely secular but draws on sacred-rooted conscience. Mild.

**Instrumental +0.1:** Liberty is principled but calculates means to defend itself. Near-zero.

**Traditional -0.5:** Libertarian Republicanism rejects ancestral imposition. Moderate-to-strong progressive.

### §3.8 Precedent — `[+0.3, +0.4, -0.4, +0.9]`

**Hierarchical +0.3:** Precedent respects ancestral rank patterns. Moderate.

**Sacred +0.4:** Customary law has sacralized continuity. Moderate.

**Instrumental -0.4:** Precedent is principled (*because it has been done*); strongly anti-instrumental.

**Traditional +0.9:** The defining axis. Precedent *is* tradition.

### §3.9 Community — `[+0.0, +0.3, -0.2, +0.5]`

**Hierarchical +0.0:** Community is locally egalitarian (parish, neighborhood) but with internal seniority. Near-zero.

**Sacred +0.3:** Parish and village have sacralized communal life (saint days, processions). Moderate.

**Instrumental -0.2:** Community is intrinsically motivated (belonging-as-good); mild anti-instrumental.

**Traditional +0.5:** Community persists through tradition (*we have always been here*). Moderate-to-strong.

### §3.10 Identity — `[+0.4, +0.2, +0.0, +0.6]`

**Hierarchical +0.4:** Identity in the period is rank-bearing (Patrician vs Popolano; lineage-based offices). Moderate.

**Sacred +0.2:** Some sacralization (Confraternity identity, lineage-saint patronage). Mild.

**Instrumental +0.0:** Identity is intrinsic; near-zero.

**Traditional +0.6:** Identity is ancestral; lineage and tribal. Strong.

### §3.11 Warden — `[+0.5, +0.4, -0.1, +0.4]`

**Hierarchical +0.5:** Warden is the protective duty of the higher-rank to the lower (lord to peasant, steward to household). Moderate-to-strong.

**Sacred +0.4:** Stewardship is theologically endorsed (parable of the Good Shepherd, *cura animarum*). Moderate.

**Instrumental -0.1:** Warden is duty-bound; near-zero, mildly anti-instrumental.

**Traditional +0.4:** Stewardship continues ancestral patterns. Moderate.

### §3.12 Virtue — `[+0.2, +0.4, -0.5, +0.3]`

**Hierarchical +0.2:** Virtue has loose hierarchy (the *virtuous* enjoy moral standing) but is largely peer-democratic. Mild.

**Sacred +0.4:** Aristotelian virtue and Christian virtue overlap; the *virtues* (cardinal, theological) are sacred. Moderate.

**Instrumental -0.5:** Virtue is *intrinsic* (good character is its own reward); strongly anti-instrumental.

**Traditional +0.3:** Virtue ethics draws on ancestral exemplars but also generates new exemplars. Mild-to-moderate.

### §3.13 Honor — `[+0.5, +0.4, -0.7, +0.8]`

**Hierarchical +0.5:** Honor is rank-bearing (knight's honor differs from merchant's honor differs from oath-pledged commoner's honor). Moderate-to-strong.

**Sacred +0.4:** The pledged oath has sacralized weight (oaths sworn on relics, honor as quasi-religious); moderate.

**Instrumental -0.7:** Honor is the *anti-instrumental* Conviction — one keeps the oath even at material cost; the betrayer is dishonored regardless of utility achieved. Strongly anti-instrumental.

**Traditional +0.8:** Honor codes are ancestral and slow-moving (chivalric, military, mercantile-reputational); strongly traditional. Honor's primary axis is *traditional*.

## §4 Note on the 4-axis Collapse

Three observed collapses reveal the limit of 4-axis representation:

### §4.1 Community vs Identity

Both score moderately traditional, mildly hierarchical, intrinsic. Community is `[0.0, +0.3, -0.2, +0.5]`; Identity is `[+0.4, +0.2, +0.0, +0.6]`. The differentiation in 4-axis space is small (mostly hierarchical 0 vs +0.4).

The actual distinction — *what kind of belonging?* — is not well-captured. A 5th axis (communal-vs-categorical, or relational-vs-formal) would resolve this.

**Decision:** retain 4-axis substrate; calibrate at Stage 10 against gameplay; Class B 5th-axis extension if collapse proves load-bearing. Per integration plan D2.

### §4.2 Order vs Authority

Both score positive on hierarchical and moderate on traditional. Differentiation comes via instrumental+sacred (Order is procedural-secular; Authority is hierarchical-mildly-sacred).

This collapse is acceptable: the 4-axis rep tells you Authority and Order are *similar* but not *identical*, and gameplay differentiation comes via DA category alignments (a faction with primary Authority contradicts different actions than a faction with primary Order).

### §4.3 Faith vs Precedent

Both score sacred-and-traditional. Differentiation via Faith's stronger sacred (+0.9 vs +0.4) and Precedent's stronger traditional (+0.9 vs +0.6).

This collapse is acceptable; the rep tells you Faith and Precedent often co-occur (they did historically), but the magnitudes differentiate primary axis.

## §5 Composition Rule

Per `key_substrate_v30.md` §2.5:

```
npc.armature_position[axis] = Σ over c in Convictions:
    npc.personal_convictions[c] × CONVICTION_AXIS_MATRIX[c][axis]
```

For an NPC with `personal_convictions = {Faith: 0.5, Virtue: 0.3}`:

```
hierarchical = 0.5 × +0.4 + 0.3 × +0.2 = 0.26
sacred       = 0.5 × +0.9 + 0.3 × +0.4 = 0.57
instrumental = 0.5 × -0.3 + 0.3 × -0.5 = -0.30
traditional  = 0.5 × +0.6 + 0.3 × +0.3 = 0.39
```

So: armature_position = `[+0.26, +0.57, -0.30, +0.39]` — moderately hierarchical, strongly sacred, mildly anti-instrumental, moderately traditional. Reads as "devout virtuous archetype" interpretively.

The composition is linear; engine math uses dot-products on the position vector.

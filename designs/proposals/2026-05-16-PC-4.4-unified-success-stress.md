## §1 Motivation

A-4 finding: of six faction roles in PP-686 v2, only Löwenritter has a canonical turn-on-you mechanism (`stats_1_7_scale §Löwenritter` Martial Law / Coup Trigger). Five roles lack analogous vulnerability:

- Sovereign (Crown) — no bureaucracy-independence / standing-army-coup mechanic
- Ecclesiastical (Church) — no Reformation-schism mechanic at faction level (CI Passive Advance is offensive, not self-undermining)
- Mercantile-procedural (Hafenmark) — no wealth-induced oligarchic-capture mechanic
- Intelligence-diplomatic (Varfell) — no intel-dominance paranoid-purge spiral
- Reformist (Restoration Movement) — no movement-success institutional-rigidity drift

The research throughline T-R-7 (`tests/audit/2026-05-16-faction-ners-all-directions.md §2 T-7`) names this as a load-bearing Renaissance dynamic: standing army enables centralization AND enables coup; bureaucracy enables control AND becomes independent power center; confessional state-building unifies AND creates minority fifth columns. Every Renaissance consolidation tool had a turn-on-you mode.

Pass 2 of the plan-audit cycle (M-3 finding) flagged that 5 separate per-role mechanisms would be over-engineering by symmetric expansion of the audit finding. The plan reordered to lead with a unified formula, falling back to per-role mechanics only if the unified form fails sim battery (Pass 3 §1 O-4 correction).

**This proposal is that unified form.**

## §2 Mechanism specification

### §2.1 Core formula

For each faction-role, designate a **primary advantage stat** tied to the role's strength dimension:

| Role | Primary advantage stat | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Sovereign (Crown) | Mandate | Crown strength = governance authority sustained over seasons |
| Ecclesiastical (Church) | CI (peninsular religious authority track) | Church strength = peninsular legitimacy accumulated |
| Mercantile-procedural (Hafenmark) | Wealth + Influence (composite ≥ both threshold) | Hafenmark strength = commercial + parliamentary power |
| Intelligence-diplomatic (Varfell) | Intel | Varfell strength = information dominance |
| Reformist (Restoration Movement) | Popular_Support | RM strength = popular backing for cultural revolution |
| Military-order (Löwenritter) | Military | already canonical — see existing coup trigger |

**Trigger condition (per role):**

```
when:
  faction.primary_advantage_stat ≥ stress_threshold
  AND held for ≥ stress_seasons consecutive Accounting cycles
emit:
  Key: mechanical.success_stress
  payload:
    faction: <role>
    stat: <primary_advantage_stat>
    value: <current>
    seasons_held: <count>
consume (role-keyed):
  <role>_specific_consequence
```

**Thresholds (proposal-level; sim-tunable):**

- `stress_threshold` for 1–7 stats: **≥6**
- `stress_threshold` for CI (0–100): **≥60**
- `stress_seasons`: **3** (one full Accounting cycle of sustained dominance)
- For Hafenmark composite trigger: BOTH Wealth ≥6 AND Influence ≥5

### §2.2 Per-role consequences

**Sovereign — Bureaucratic Independence**
- *Trigger:* Mandate ≥6 for ≥3 consecutive seasons
- *Consequence:* Crown's ministry officers (Crown Ministries §7.1 of `faction_politics_v30`) gain Conviction Drift toward Loyalty-to-Office over Loyalty-to-Crown. Mechanical effect: 1-in-6 chance per Accounting that a Domain Action issued by Crown becomes "Bureaucratic Veto" — pool reduced by ministry's Standing, OR action proceeds but with `da.contradicted` flag (counts toward Mission-shift trigger 3)
- *Historical analog:* French intendants → French parlements; English civil-service capture pattern

**Ecclesiastical — Reformation Schism Risk**
- *Trigger:* CI ≥60 for ≥3 consecutive seasons
- *Consequence:* At Accounting, Cardinal Conviction Doubt + Cardinal Conviction Reform interaction roll: if any Cardinal has both Doubt ≥2 AND Reform ≥2, dissenter-Cardinal triggers — splits CI track (a portion of CI defects to dissenter, who becomes either NPC schism-faction or named opposition Cardinal). Loss to original Church = (Doubt + Reform) × 2 CI, redistributed to schism.
- *Historical analog:* Luther's 95 Theses; Wycliffe → Hus → Reformation cascade

**Mercantile-procedural — Oligarchic Capture**
- *Trigger:* Wealth ≥6 AND Influence ≥5 for ≥3 consecutive seasons
- *Consequence:* Hafenmark Parliamentary Committees (`faction_politics_v30 §7.2`) gain "Patrician Capture" status: committee competence shifts toward serving merchant clan interests; Parliamentary actions cost +1 Ob unless aligned with named clan agenda. Mechanical: 2-in-6 chance per Accounting that a Parliamentary action is "Captured" — pool unchanged but outcome benefits patrician clan over faction-Mission.
- *Historical analog:* Venetian Serrata (1297); Florentine ottimati; Genoese oligarchy

**Intelligence-diplomatic — Paranoid Purge Spiral**
- *Trigger:* Intel ≥6 for ≥3 consecutive seasons AND ≥1 successful Spy action against Varfell in that window
- *Consequence:* Hochjarl issues purge: highest-Loyalty Varfell NPCs get Conviction Loyalty-to-Hochjarl floor raised by +1; lowest-Loyalty NPCs flagged "suspect" (any Domain Action involving them: Ob+1). Cascade Strictness +0.2 (faction operates under suspicion regime; deviation from Mission penalized harder).
- *Historical analog:* Tudor-Walsingham network paranoia; Stalin 1937 NKVD spiral; Henry VIII purges

**Reformist — Institutional Rigidity**
- *Trigger:* Popular_Support ≥6 AND has reached RM Phase 1 Cultural Majority milestone (per `victory_v30 §3.5`)
- *Consequence:* Mission Strictness +0.1 per RM-victory-milestone passed thereafter. High Strictness amplifies Mission-deviation Ob (per `faction_behavior_v30 §3.6`). Movement increasingly rejects new conviction-Reform contributions; "movement orthodoxy" tightens.
- *Historical analog:* Bolshevik 1917 → Stalinist 1929; Cromwell's Major-Generals 1655; French Revolution → Reign of Terror trajectory

**Military-order — already canonical**
- Löwenritter's Martial Law / Coup Trigger (`stats_1_7_scale §Löwenritter`) is the prior canonical instance. Verify compatibility: existing trigger may use different threshold structure (Military ≥X + condition). Bring into harmonized formula OR document as the foundational instance the unified mechanism generalizes.

### §2.3 Stress-decay mechanic

Once `mechanical.success_stress` fires for a faction, the consequence persists until the triggering condition lapses for ≥2 consecutive Accounting cycles (primary stat drops below threshold, or for Varfell the no-Spy-action condition fails). On lapse: consequence resets, but **`stress_history` counter increments** — future stress events at the same role have Strictness/cost +1 per prior fire (prevents trivial reset-loops; models accumulated institutional fragility).

## §3 Sim battery design

Per plan Phase 4 spec: each PC-4.x advances from proposal → PROVISIONAL canon only after sim battery PASS.

**6 simulation runs, one per role:**

1. **SIM-PC44-Crown.** Aggressive play: maximize Mandate via Govern stacking. Verify Bureaucratic Independence fires within 4–8 seasons; verify it doesn't fire under conservative play (Mandate 3–4).
2. **SIM-PC44-Church.** Aggressive CI accumulation. Verify Reformation Schism rolls fire at CI ≥60 + 3-season hold; verify schism redistribution mechanics produce non-degenerate outcomes (faction doesn't lose >40% CI in single event).
3. **SIM-PC44-Hafenmark.** Wealth+Influence dual stacking. Verify Oligarchic Capture fires at composite threshold + 3-season hold; verify 2-in-6 capture rate produces measurable Mission-deviation pressure without paralyzing the faction.
4. **SIM-PC44-Varfell.** Intel dominance + induce Spy failure against Varfell. Verify Paranoid Purge fires only with both conditions; verify Cascade Strictness +0.2 produces measurable Mission-deviation Ob increase.
5. **SIM-PC44-RM.** Push to Cultural Majority milestone + sustain Popular_Support. Verify Strictness drift accumulates per milestone; verify orthodoxy effect on conviction-Reform NPCs.
6. **SIM-PC44-Löwenritter.** Verify harmony with existing coup trigger; OR document why this role uses different formula structure.

**PASS criteria:**
- Each mechanism fires within target time-window under aggressive play
- No mechanism fires under conservative play (faction can choose to stay below threshold)
- Consequence severity scales with stat magnitude (not all-or-nothing)
- Composability check: stress mechanisms across multiple factions in same campaign don't produce stat-cascade collapse

**FAIL → fallback:** if unified formula fails for ≥2 roles, fall back to per-role narrow triggers (Pass 2 §1 PC-4.4-a through PC-4.4-e as separate proposals).

## §4 Alternative R-form

If unified mechanism is rejected:

**Alt-A: Per-role separate mechanics.** Each of the 5 unaddressed roles gets its own proposal entry, sim-tested independently. Higher development cost; potentially better-tuned per-role. (This was the Pass 2 initial form, reordered in Pass 3.)

**Alt-B: Generalized formula with role-blind consequence.** Single mechanism that fires the same consequence (e.g., "+1 Ob to Domain Actions for 2 seasons") regardless of role. Lower design surface; loses role-character. Likely fails М-4 (reskinned attractor failure) since consequence becomes role-symmetric.

**Recommendation embedded in the proposal:** unified-with-role-specific-consequences (this proposal's §2) is the middle path that preserves role asymmetry (per audit H-1 hybrid-asymmetric verdict) while avoiding 5 separate patch register entries.

## §6 Open questions

1. **Mandate vs Legitimacy for Crown primary advantage stat.** Both are governance dimensions. Mandate (0..7, 0-floor) is the procedural acceptance measure that builds via Govern OW; Legitimacy (0..7) is broader procedural acceptance. Bureaucratic Independence pattern fits Mandate better (Crown's officers respond to Crown's *Mandate* level, not abstract Legitimacy). Confirm with Jordan.
2. **Hafenmark composite trigger (Wealth ≥6 AND Influence ≥5).** Composite triggers are unusual in current canon. Alternative: trigger on Wealth ≥6 only, with Influence acting as a modifier on consequence severity.
3. **Reformation Schism redistribution numerics.** Loss = (Doubt + Reform) × 2 CI is a starting heuristic; needs sim-tuning. If too aggressive, factions hit-CI-60-then-bleed-out; if too mild, schism becomes flavor-only.
4. **`stress_history` accumulation cap.** Should there be a maximum? Without cap, a faction that triggers stress repeatedly becomes increasingly fragile. With cap, the institutional-fragility model is bounded. Recommend uncapped initially, cap if sim reveals death-spirals.
5. **Cross-faction stress interaction.** If Crown is in Bureaucratic Independence AND Church is in Reformation Schism in same season, do consequences compose or override? Default proposal: compose (both active); confirm with Jordan.

## Succession Law
Cognatic succession with male-heir priority. Torben inherits first. Elske inherits only if Torben is dead or formally excluded from succession by Parliament.

## Almud's Children

### Prince Torben Almqvist
Early teens. Begins in Royal Court. The active dilemma. See Tutoring Demand below.

### Princess Elske Almqvist
Married to an Altonian Duke holding territory adjacent to Border Pass. Marriage is a fixed condition at game start.

**Elske is not a plot device — she is a political actor with her own arc.**

**Conviction:** Family (she loves her brother and her father) vs. Self-Determination (she wants to matter on her own terms, not as someone's wife or someone's backup).

**Resonant Style:** Evidence. Show her concrete proof of what's happening to Torben, or concrete proof that Valoria needs her. Abstract appeals to patriotism or ambition won't move her. She's spent years in a political marriage hearing abstract arguments.

**Starting state:** Genuinely torn. She is embedded in Altonian society but not controlled by it. She has affection for Valoria but no active role in its politics. She is not working for anyone at game start.

**Elske's Recruitable States:**
Any faction that contacts Elske (Circles Ob 3 — she's in Altonian territory) can attempt to recruit her through social engagement (Appeal or Debate). Her response depends on which Conviction axis the approach targets:

| Approach | Elske's Response |
|----------|-----------------|
| "Protect Torben" (Family) | Most receptive. If shown evidence Torben is being harmed or manipulated: she acts to shield him. May attempt independent extraction. |
| "Take the throne yourself" (Ambition) | Moderately receptive but suspicious. She wants to matter, but not through fratricide or betrayal. Requires Evidence that Torben is beyond saving AND that she can rule independently. |
| "Serve Valoria" (Patriotism) | Least receptive on its own — she's heard this from her father's court for years. Must be combined with concrete evidence of threat (Family or Self-Determination axis). |
| "Serve Altonia" (Duke's pressure) | Resistant. She married for politics, not conviction. But if Torben is fully Altonia-aligned (Loyalty 1-2), she may accept the reality and work within the Altonian framework. |

**Elske's Independence Mechanic:**
Elske can break free of her Duke husband's political orbit with sufficient support. This is a multi-step process:

1. **Contact** — Circles Ob 3 to reach her (she's in Altonian territory).
2. **Recruitment** — Social scene (Appeal or Debate, 3 exchanges). Must target Family or Self-Determination conviction. Success: Elske becomes an allied NPC willing to act.
3. **Independence action** — Once recruited, Elske can attempt to seize independence. Three paths:
   - **Political break:** Elske publicly separates from the Duke and returns to Valoria. Roll her Influence (3) vs Ob 3. Success: she returns, available as heir or political actor. The Duke gains casus belli (personal insult) — IP +3.
   - **Widowing:** Elske arranges or enables the Duke's removal. Requires Niflhel or Varfell intelligence support (Intelligence Ob 4). Success: Elske is free, no IP increase (appears natural), but she carries the moral weight. If discovered: IP +5, Elske's Mandate permanently -1.
   - **Divorce by Parliament:** If Hafenmark controls Parliament, Baralta can push a constitutional annulment. Hafenmark Influence Ob 3. Success: marriage dissolved legally. IP +2 (diplomatic insult but not casus belli). Cleanest path but requires Hafenmark cooperation.

**Elske as Queen:**
If Elske inherits (Torben dead or excluded) AND is independent of the Duke:
- Crown passes to Elske. She governs as Queen.
- Her starting Mandate: 3 (contested succession, not universally accepted).
- Löwenritter: accepts IF she is demonstrably independent. Ehrenwall's test is behavioral, not formal — does Elske act for Valoria or for Altonia?
- Church: accepts if she submits to Church authority (TC +2 demand). Refuses Church: TC unchanged but Church actively opposes her.
- If Elske inherits while STILL married to the Duke: Crown passes into Altonian orbit. This is functionally the same as Torben at Loyalty 1. Löwenritter coup trigger #2 met.

## The Tutoring Demand

**Trigger:** IP reaches 30 (Altonia turns Aggressive).

Altonia formally demands Torben be sent for "education." Recurs every season at Strategic Phase accounting until resolved.

### Crown Response Options (each season while unresolved)

| Response | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| **Refuse** | IP accelerates to 1.5x pace (base +2/season becomes +3/season). Schoenland trade Ob +1 cumulative per refusal. |
| **Negotiate delay** | Roll Crown Influence vs Ob 3 (first attempt), +1 Ob per subsequent attempt. Success: demand deferred 1 season, IP pace unchanged. Failure: treated as Refusal. |
| **Surrender Torben** | IP pace stays at 1x. Torben Loyalty Clock begins. |

## Torben Loyalty Clock

**Starting value: 8 (loyal to Valoria).**

Each season Torben is in Altonia: Loyalty -1.

**Loyalty floors at 1.** Altonia will never let Torben renounce — he is their claim to the Valorian throne. He remains their asset indefinitely.

**Covert contact:** Any faction (typically Crown) may attempt to maintain contact with Torben. Intelligence Domain Action vs Ob 3 per season. Success: Loyalty does not decrease that season. Failure: no effect; Altonia may detect the contact (GM judgment — potential IP +1 if detected).

### Loyalty States

| Loyalty | State | Mechanical Effect |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| 8-6 | Homesick, resistant | No effect. Retrieval straightforward. |
| 5-4 | Adapting | Crown Mandate -1 (public perceives weakness). Retrieval requires Altonian consent or covert extraction. |
| 3-2 | Altonia-aligned | Crown Mandate -1 again (cumulative -2 total). Torben writes public letters praising Altonia. Löwenritter coup trigger #2 arguably met. |
| 1 | Altonian puppet | Torben is fully Altonia-loyal. If he inherits, Crown becomes Altonian vassal state. Löwenritter coup trigger #2 definitively met. Cannot be retrieved without military action. |

### Retrieval Options

| Loyalty Range | Method | Procedure |
|---------------|--------|-----------|
| 8-6 | Diplomatic retrieval | Crown Influence vs Ob 3. Success: Torben returns, clock resets to 8. IP +2. |
| 5-3 | Covert extraction | Intelligence vs Ob 5. Success: Torben returns at current Loyalty value (damage done). IP +5. Failure: operative captured, IP +3, Crown Mandate -1. |
| 2-1 | Military extraction | Torben resists extraction. Requires Military action in Altonian territory. IP immediately to 75+. |

## Succession Scenarios

The late-game succession landscape depends on Torben's status and Elske's independence:

| Torben Status | Elske Status | Outcome |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Loyal (6-8), in Valoria | Married to Duke | Normal succession. Torben inherits. Elske irrelevant. |
| Compromised (3-5), in Altonia | Married to Duke | Crisis. Torben inherits but is unreliable. Elske is backup but Altonia-adjacent. |
| Puppet (1-2), in Altonia | Married to Duke | Altonian capture complete through either heir. Löwenritter coup or Hafenmark parliamentary succession only alternatives. |
| Puppet (1-2), in Altonia | Independent | Elske can be installed as Queen. Valorian sovereignty preserved if she governs independently. |
| Dead | Married to Duke | Crown passes to Altonian orbit via Duke. Coup trigger met. |
| Dead | Independent | Elske inherits cleanly. Contested but viable. |
| Excluded by Parliament | Independent | Elske inherits through constitutional process. Cleanest path — Hafenmark's preferred outcome. |

## Faction Interaction Map

**Löwenritter (Ehrenwall):** Tracks Torben's status. At Loyalty 3, begins internal coup preparations. At Loyalty 1, coup trigger #2 met. Ehrenwall's test for Elske: does she act for Valoria? If yes, Löwenritter supports her succession. If unclear, Löwenritter may coup regardless.

**Church:** Indifferent to succession unless returning heir brings Altonian religious practices (TC +2). Church prefers ANY monarch who submits to Church authority. Elske submitting = Church support. Elske refusing = Church opposition.

**Hafenmark (Baralta):** Prefers Parliamentary succession. Will push to exclude Torben (if compromised) through constitutional process. Will support Elske IF she takes the constitutional path (annulment via Parliament). Baralta's ideal outcome: Elske as constitutional monarch with Parliamentary constraints.

**Revolution:** Sees succession crisis as proof monarchy is structurally captured. Revolution Influence +1 whenever Torben Loyalty drops below 5. Revolution doesn't care who sits on the throne — they want the system changed.

**Varfell (Vaynard):** Opportunistic. May contact Elske independently to build personal leverage. May attempt to broker a deal with Altonia. Treats the succession as an intelligence asset, not a political commitment.

**Guilds:** Concerned with Schoenland trade disruption. Guild support tracks trade impact. Prolonged refusal = trade collapse = Guilds turn against Crown.

**Niflhel:** May offer covert extraction services (at a price). May independently contact Torben or Elske for leverage. Niflhel's interest is transactional, not political.

**Löwenritter + Elske interaction:** If Elske returns independently AND demonstrates Valorian loyalty, Ehrenwall is the first faction leader to support her. The Löwenritter respects demonstrated independence. If Elske returns as a compromise candidate pushed by Hafenmark, Ehrenwall is skeptical but willing — she cares about sovereignty, not procedure.

## IP Acceleration Math

Base IP growth: +2/season.
With refusal: +3/season (1.5x).

Over a 10-season campaign starting at IP 30:
- Compliance path: +20 IP = IP 50 at season 10 (Hostile range).
- Refusal path: +30 IP = IP 60 at season 10 (Warlike range).
- Delta: ~10 IP, roughly one full threat tier.

The pressure is real but not instant. Players have time to maneuver.

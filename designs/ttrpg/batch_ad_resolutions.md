## BATCH A RESOLUTIONS

### G-053: Approach Training CP Cost
**Decision: 8 CP confirmed.**
Rationale: typical earn rate is 2–4 CP per session (Belief engagement events). 8 CP = 2–4 sessions of income. Meaningful milestone cost, not a shopping decision. Consistent with "expensive = campaign milestone" cost tier established in the spending menu.

### G-040: Permanent Inspiration Loss — CP Refund
**Decision: Half-CP refund when Inspiration focus is permanently destroyed and Grief Scene fails.**
The Player Character invested CP to establish the Inspiration. Total loss on failure would punish investment in a way that discourages the mechanic. Half refund acknowledges the loss without zeroing the investment. Refund is rounded down. Refund applies only when: (a) focus is *permanently* destroyed/gone (not temporarily lost), AND (b) Grief Scene fails. Full Grief Scene success = no loss, no refund needed.

### G-054: Circles Track Architecture
**Decision: Single unified Circles track with bonus/penalty die per major faction.**
Per-faction tracking is bookkeeping that doesn't add proportionate play value. Single track with faction modifiers preserves the faction-relationship signal without multiplying tracked values per Player Character.

Implementation:
- Circles is one track (Presence + History bonus, as per stage2_characters.md).
- Positive Reputation with a faction: +1D per 2 Reputation points on Circles rolls within that faction's sphere (already in compiled rules).
- Negative Reputation: −1D per 2 Reputation points.
- Network Damage tracked per-faction (already in compiled rules — 3 Network Damage within a faction = −1D permanent to Circles within that faction).

No change required to compiled Circles rules. G-054 design brief's "per-faction separate track" proposal is superseded by this decision.

## BATCH B+C RESOLUTIONS

### G-042: Founding a New Faction — CP Cost
**Decision: No CP cost. Narrative + mechanical prerequisites only.**
Founding a faction is already gated by prerequisites (existing membership, territory presence, Non-Player Character relationships, Game Master judgment on viability). Adding a CP cost would make it a resource transaction rather than an emergent political act. The prerequisites are the cost.

### G-038 + Treaty Betrayal Consequences — Final Table
**Decision: Full asymmetric consequence table confirmed.**

| Target | Effect | Duration |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Betrayed faction | Stability −1 | 2 seasons |
| Betrayed faction | Free casus belli against betrayer | Immediate, permanent until used |
| Betrayed faction | Grievance Marker vs betrayer | Permanent until resolved |
| Betraying faction | Influence −1 | 2 seasons |
| Betraying faction | Mandate +1 (strategic advantage gained) | 1 season |
| Betraying individuals | Reputation −3 with betrayed faction | Permanent |
| Betraying individuals | Reputation −1 with all witness factions | Permanent |

**Grievance Marker mechanics:**
- While active: +1 Ob to all diplomatic rolls between the two factions (betrayed side won't deal at standard terms).
- +1D to Intelligence actions targeting the betrayer (they're watching).
- Clears only through: (a) full season of genuine reparation — territory cession, tribute, or public apology scene (not declaration), OR (b) change of faction leadership on the betraying side.

**Design note:** Asymmetric by intent. Betrayer gets short-term advantage (Mandate + whatever the betrayal gained) and long-term diplomatic damage (Grievance + Influence loss + Reputation). Betrayed faction is hurt short-term (Stability) but gains narrative and mechanical tools to respond.

**G-036/G-038 shared question resolved:** Additional Stability cost for betrayer is redundant given the Reputation + Grievance + Influence consequences. No Stability cost on betraying faction.

### G-044: Altonian Vassalage
**Decision: Include as valid dark-path choice.**
A faction may submit to Altonia to redirect or delay invasion. This is a valid player or Non-Player Character choice representing a faction prioritising survival or advantage over Valorian sovereignty. It is not a standard win condition — it is a dark-path option with long-term consequences for the peninsula's political independence (Institutional Pressure clock interaction, Crown legitimacy damage, etc.). Full mechanical spec to be designed in compilation.

## BATCH D RESOLUTIONS

### Varfell — Private Collection on Leadership Change
**Decision: Collection transfers as institutional asset. Power and knowledge do not transfer.**
The collection exists physically — it survives any leadership change. A non-Vaynard leader who takes over Varfell finds themselves in possession of an extraordinary accumulated archive they do not have the framework to interpret. This triggers a **Discovery Event** at the start of the new leader's first season (the sheer density of accumulated items generates a Thread-perceptible signal). The new leader may subsequently attempt to use the collection, but without Vaynard's TK progression, most of it is inert material.

Mechanically: Private Collection Unique Action remains available to Varfell under any leader. The *effectiveness* of the action scales with the faction leader's TK (TK 0–1: base effect; TK 2–3: +1 result tier; TK 4–5: full Vaynard-tier effect). Non-Vaynard leaders begin at TK 0 with the collection and must build from there through play.

### Niflhel — Leadership Structure
**Decision: Permanently headless. Confirmed again.**
Niflhel is cell-based with no single leader. Already confirmed as R-78 in the editorial ledger (E-06, 2026-03-30). No change. The four arms operate independently. A Player Character who infiltrates or takes over one arm controls that arm only. This has been recorded; closing this item.

### Revolution — Named Non-Player Character Elder Contact
**Decision: Existing NPCs (Maren Solberg, Dahl Erikssen, Katri Voss — R-75) serve as primary contacts. No additional elder Non-Player Character required.**
The three established Restoration NPCs cover the access points (Presence, Bonds/Thread Sensitivity, Cognition/Thread Sensitivity). An additional elder contact is redundant unless a specific narrative need arises.

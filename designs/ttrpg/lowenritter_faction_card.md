# FACTION 8: THE LÖWENRITTER (Royal Military Order)
## Date: 2026-03-25 (Session 5)
## Status: Designed — approved

---

## Ethical Framework: Deontological Honor Code
The Löwenritter's institutional logic evaluates actions by duty, oath, and institutional honor. This is not virtue ethics (the Crown's framework) — it is obligation to the *office*, not the person. A Löwenritter follows Crown orders because the Crown is the legitimate authority, not because the king is virtuous.

**Framework modifier:**
- Domain Actions in defense of Valorian sovereignty or institutional integrity: -1 Ob.
- Domain Actions that serve a foreign power, betray an oath, or subordinate Valoria to external authority: +2 Ob. The Löwenritter structurally cannot serve foreign interests — the institution resists at every level.

## Partial Faction Sheet (Peacetime)
**Military, Stability, Influence, Intelligence.** No Mandate (legitimacy derived from Crown). No Wealth (funded by Crown treasury).

Upon coup: gains Mandate (starting at 3 — military legitimacy, not popular) and Wealth (seize Crown treasury portion — Crown Wealth -2, Löwenritter Wealth starts at 2). Crown Stability -2 (institution fractured).

## Full Sheet Trigger — Military Coup
The Löwenritter may declare institutional independence and gain the full 6-attribute sheet if ANY of these conditions are met:
1. Crown is deposed (Almud removed, no legitimate successor installed)
2. Foreign power controls the Crown (Altonian puppet on throne, or heir surrendered to Altonia)
3. TC reaches threshold where Church controls Crown-held territories (~80+)
4. Crown formally subordinates to a foreign power

Coup is a Domain Action: roll Military vs Ob 3.
- Success: Löwenritter gains full sheet. Crown loses attributes as specified above. All factions receive Grievance Marker vs Löwenritter (coup is destabilizing regardless of justification).
- Failure: Löwenritter Stability -2 (internal fracture). Grandmaster removed — officer succession. Crown may purge Löwenritter officers (Military -1).

## Institutional Tendency
Defend borders. Maintain military readiness. Follow Crown orders. Resist foreign encroachment. In peacetime, the Löwenritter acts only when directed. It does not initiate political action.

## Leadership Deviation
Grandmaster acting against institutional tendency:
- General deviation (refusing Crown orders, unauthorized alliance, political maneuvering): Stability check Ob 3. The order is deeply hierarchical — deviation from chain of command is existentially threatening.
- Deviation *toward* Valorian independence (refusing an order that would subordinate Valoria): Stability check Ob 1. The institution's deeper loyalty is to the nation, not the command structure.

## Unique Action — Martial Law
The Löwenritter may declare martial law in one territory where they have military presence.
- Roll: Military vs Ob 3.
- Success: All other factions' Domain Actions in that territory +1 Ob for 1 season. Crown Mandate +1 in that territory (order restored). Löwenritter Influence +1 (visible power).
- Failure: Löwenritter Stability -1 (troops resent overreach). Crown Mandate -1 in that territory (population sees tyranny).
- **Post-coup variant:** If Löwenritter has declared independence, Martial Law costs Crown Mandate -1 regardless of success (acting without Crown authority).
- **Constraint:** Cannot be used in a territory where Church Mandate exceeds Crown Mandate (the Löwenritter won't challenge theocratic control directly — soldiers, not theologians).

## Leader (default NPC): Grandmaster Sigrid Ehrenwall
- **Conviction:** Duty.
- **Resonant Style:** Consequence (show her what happens to Valoria if she doesn't act; abstract principle won't move her).
- **Age/background:** Late 50s. Career soldier. Served under Almud's father and Almud. Remembers pre-settlement Valoria.
- **Personal tension:** Ehrenwall swore her oath to the Crown as institution. Almud is surrendering Valorian sovereignty incrementally — the Altonian succession pressure, the Church's expanding civil authority, weakened border garrisons. She has not yet concluded the Crown has failed the nation, but she is keeping count. When she reaches that conclusion, she will act without hesitation or remorse. She is the most dangerous NPC in the game: patient, competent, and commanding an army.
- **Relationship to Almud:** Respect without affection. Decent man governing badly. She will follow his orders until the day she doesn't, and the transition will be instantaneous.
- **Relationship to the heir:** Views the son's potential removal to Altonia as a direct sovereignty threat. If Almud surrenders the heir, this meets coup trigger condition #2.
- **Relationship to the Church:** Cold contempt. The Löwenritter predates the Church of Galbados in Valoria. Ehrenwall considers it a colonial institution. She will not challenge it theologically, but will never cede military authority to Templars.

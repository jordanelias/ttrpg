## 13. Orthodox Canon

| Artifact | Register | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Scripture (Weavings of Solmund) | Hopkins | Dense, sensory. Describes Solmund's acts as divine weaving. Threads as instruments, not ontology. |
| Liturgy (Hours of the Loom) | Di Cicco | Daily prayer cycle. Morning: new thread. Evening: day woven into fabric. Embodied, rhythmic, seasonal. |
| Hymns | Hopkins | Short, vertical. Moments of recognition. Half-rhyme and assonance. |
| Cathedral Inscriptions | Cusa | Short texts carved above doors. Claim → qualify → transcend. |
| Doctrinal Treatises | Ficino + Eliot | Long, hierarchical, architecturally ordered. Official positions. |
| Graduation Texts | ibn Gabirol | Cathedral School/Monastic completion. Rigorous architecture, then confessional break. |
| Hagiography | Zohar | Narrative accounts of holy figures. Theology in the story, not commentary. |

## 14. Heterodox and Marginal

| Artifact | Register | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Practitioner Journals | John of the Cross | TS clergy experiencing phenomena doctrine cannot account for. Orthodox vocabulary strained past intended meaning. |
| Practitioner Notation | Abulafia | Technical records at high TS. Repetitive, permutative, syntactically fractured. |
| Einhir Fragments | Böhme + Celan | Scraps — phrases in folk songs, gestures in craft practice. Shortest possible units. |
| Restoration Counter-Theology | Pico | Rhetorical, dignitarian, accusatory. Thread sensitivity as human birthright. |
| Niflhel Pamphlets | Plain speech | Material critique of Church economics. Parodies theological registers. |
| Restoration Memorial | Levinas | Lists of names. No explanation, theology, or argument. |
| Post-Forgetting Fragment | Jabès | Written by Southernmost returnees with Partial/Failure checks. Interrogative, circling absence. |
| Church Council Deliberation | Derrida | Institutional decision-making on thread-related matters. Simultaneous affirmation of incompatible positions. |
| Halt Text | Wittgenstein | Rarest artifact. Argument proceeding to failure, marking failure, stopping. Silence IS conclusion. |

# Emergent Arc Analysis — Narrative Quality
## All 18 Arcs (Batches 01–03)
## Date: 2026-04-08 | Analyst: Claude (Opus 4.6)

---

## Evaluation Framework

Five criteria, each scored 1–5. These are specific to Valoria's design goals, not generic narrative assessment.

1. **Emergence quality** — Does the arc require multiple independent systems running simultaneously, or could a single player decision explain it? Does the arc arise from mechanical interaction, or is it a plot hook dressed in mechanical language?

2. **Table experience** — Would this arc produce a moment at the table where players feel the world *doing something* they didn't expect? Does it reward attention, create genuine dilemmas (not false choices), and give players meaningful agency without requiring their initiation?

3. **Mechanical grounding** — Is every node in the causal chain traceable to a specific mechanic (stat, Ob, threshold, NPC flaw)? Are the probability landscapes realistic? Does the arc require mechanics that don't exist?

4. **NPC fidelity** — Do the NPCs behave consistently with their documented Behavioral AI, ethical frameworks, and flaws? Would the arc work with a different NPC in the same role, or does it depend on this specific character's specific compromise?

5. **Thematic coherence** — Does the arc engage the game's core tensions (epistemological tragedy, institutional vs personal ethics, the Forgetting, the caste dynamic, the dying world)? Or is it merely a political intrigue that could exist in any setting?

## Overall Assessment

**The portfolio is strong.** 18 arcs with an average score of 21.3/25. No arc scores below 18/25. NPC fidelity is consistently excellent (average 4.4/5). Thematic coherence is strong when the arcs engage the metaphysical layer (7 arcs at 5/5).

**The portfolio's distinctive achievement** is the NPC roster integration. The 13 named NPCs were designed as emergent arc triggers, and they deliver: 11 of 18 arcs require specific NPCs behaving per their specific profiles. This is rare in TTRPG design — most NPC rosters produce plot hooks, not system emergence.

**The portfolio's structural weakness** is the gap between the metaphysical arcs (Batches 01–02) and the political arcs (Batch 03). The political arcs are excellent game content but don't always engage the Thread/Forgetting/Einhir layer that makes Valoria unique. The recommendation above (connecting Arcs 7, 8, 15 to the metaphysical substrate) would address this without redesigning the arcs.

**Priority actions:**
1. Formalise the mechanical gaps: Thread-touched Coherence effects (GAP-ARC-12), PI crisis threshold (GAP-ARC-14), martyrdom CV shift (ED-NEW-1), Maret Uln's Spirit attribute (GAP-ARC-17).
2. Add intermediate checkpoints to Arcs 8 and 13 (accumulation arcs that risk table invisibility).
3. Generate arcs for uncovered NPCs: Edeyja, Torsvald (Lenneth connection), Brandt (standalone), Laskaris (standalone), Prudence Cardinal (standalone).
4. Generate arcs for uncovered arc types: mass combat, Thread operation, Southernmost/Warden, Altonian invasion, Torben succession.
5. Reclassify Arcs 10, 14, 18 as *scenario triggers* rather than *emergent arcs* in the arc taxonomy.

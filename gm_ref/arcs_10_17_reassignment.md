## Roster Changes (v2 → v3)

| Slot | v2 | v3 | Impact on Arcs |
|------|----|----|----------------|
| #9 | Severin Almud (Crown Court diplomat, COMMITMENT-LOCKED) | **Gerik Strand** (Lord Steward, OVERPERFORMER, flattery vuln) | Severin's treaty-management role is orphaned. Arcs 11, 15 used Severin heavily. |
| #10 | Dalla Virke (Niflhel operative) | **Dalla Virke** (Virke syndicate broker) | Expanded backstory. Thread-touched goods are now her *personal* supply chain, not institutional. Arc 12 strengthened — exposure is more devastating because it's personal. |
| #11 | Duke Hardar Veldensohn (Altonian prince, PROTECTIVE) | **Doux Alexios Laskaris** (Altonian doux, PROTECTIVE, oikonomia) | Same mechanical role. Name/title change. Byzantine ethics add depth. All Hardar references → Laskaris. |
| All NPCs | No historical echoes | **Historical echoes added** | No mechanical impact. Narrative depth. |

**Critical: Severin Almud is removed.** His functions were:
- Manages Altonian treaty relationship from the Valorian side
- COMMITMENT-LOCKED flaw: cannot advocate actions contradicting treaty assurances
- IP +2 if removed from Altonian portfolio (treaty signal)
- Blocks Crown military response to Church encroachment
- Enables Altonian economic influence

**Who absorbs Severin's functions?**

Option A: **Strand inherits the Altonian portfolio** alongside treasury. His OVERPERFORMER flaw means he takes on too much — he's already running the treasury, now he's managing foreign policy. The overperformance produces high-quality initial execution but brittle collapse when load exceeds capacity. His flattery vulnerability means Laskaris (a sophisticated Byzantine diplomat) can manipulate him easily (−1 Ob on social actions that acknowledge Strand's competence). This is *better* than Severin — the COMMITMENT-LOCKED flaw was static, but OVERPERFORMER + flattery vulnerability creates a dynamic arc where Strand starts managing the Altonian relationship competently, gets played by Laskaris, and collapses under the combined weight of treasury + foreign policy.

Option B: **The Altonian portfolio is unmanaged.** Crown has no dedicated diplomatic envoy. Laskaris deals with Strand (who is overloaded) or directly with the Duke. IP advances unchecked by a dedicated buffer. This makes the Altonian threat more acute — no COMMITMENT-LOCKED brake on IP.

**Recommendation: Option A.** Strand absorbs the diplomatic role. This creates a new compound flaw: OVERPERFORMER + Altonian portfolio = institutional brittleness extends to foreign policy. Laskaris's oikonomia vs Strand's meritocratic insecurity is a richer interaction than Laskaris vs Severin's contractarianism.

## Arc-by-Arc Reassignment

### Arc 10: The Penitent Duke
**NPCs affected:** None directly. Severin was not involved. Almstedt and Haelgrund drive this arc.

### Arc 11: Three Crowns in a Season
**NPCs affected:** Severin Almud → **Gerik Strand**. Hardar Veldensohn → **Doux Alexios Laskaris**.

**Severin's role was:** COMMITMENT-LOCKED triggers IP +2 on Crown collapse (treaty signal). Cannot advocate military response.

**Strand replacement:** When Crown falls, Strand's OVERPERFORMER flaw means he tries to hold everything together single-handedly — treasury, foreign policy, succession management. He cannot delegate because delegation means someone else demonstrates competence (his structural insecurity). For 1–2 seasons he performs brilliantly. Then he collapses. The brittleness that was an administrative risk becomes a governance catastrophe.

**Revised mechanics:**
- Crown collapse: Strand attempts to maintain Crown administrative function. Crown Domain Actions that require administrative execution (Tax, Trade, Policy) continue at normal Ob for **2 seasons** (Strand's buffer period).
- Season 3 post-collapse: Strand breaks. All Crown admin actions +1 Ob for 2 seasons (per NPC roster). IP +2 fires when Strand's administrative competence degrades, not immediately on Crown collapse — Laskaris reads the delayed signal as treaty erosion, not instantaneous breach.
- Strand's flattery vulnerability means the first faction to approach him with genuine respect (−1 Ob social) can redirect his OVERPERFORMER capacity toward their agenda. Church diplomatic overture: "Lord Steward, the Church recognises your service to the Crown. In this time of crisis, the faithful need a steady hand at the treasury. We propose a joint administration..." Strand gives the Church more access than he should because someone is finally treating him as a peer.

**Laskaris replacement for Hardar:** Mechanically identical. Laskaris's oikonomia ethic means his protective behavior has a different philosophical grounding — he's not protecting Elske from romantic attachment (v2 Hardar) but from imperial *pastoral care* logic. He believes enforcing the imperial claim would destroy Valoria, and destroying Valoria harms the empire's actual interests. This is a more sophisticated and less exploitable motivation. Players who try to leverage "Laskaris loves Elske" will find that framing doesn't work — he's not sentimental, he's strategic. Players who argue "protecting Elske IS serving the empire's long-term interests" will find him receptive.

**Revised event cards:**

**EVENT CARD: THE CROWN FALLS (REVISED)**
- **Trigger:** Crown Stability → 0, no recovery.
- **Change:** Strand buffer period. Crown admin actions continue at normal Ob for 2 seasons (Strand OVERPERFORMER — he holds the machinery together alone). IP +2 delayed to Season 3 (when Strand breaks). Add: "Any faction may attempt social action targeting Strand (−1 Ob if including genuine acknowledgment of his competence). Success: Strand redirects Crown administrative capacity toward that faction's interests for the remainder of his buffer period."
- **NPC involvement:** Strand (OVERPERFORMER — drives the 2-season buffer, then brittle collapse); Laskaris (PROTECTIVE — reads Strand's degradation as the treaty-erosion signal); Brandt (EXTERNAL THREAT FIXATED — unchanged); Almstedt (CONSERVATIVE — unchanged).

**EVENT CARD: BRANDT'S MARCH — unchanged.** Strand is not military.

**EVENT CARD: THE ALTONIAN HUSBAND → THE ALTONIAN DOUX (REVISED)**
- **Name change.** "Husband" → "Doux." Laskaris is not defined by romance but by administrative judgment.
- **BG text revision:** *"Doux Laskaris receives instructions from the imperial court. The Emperor demands a progress report on Valorian compliance. Laskaris must choose: deliver an accurate report (IP +2, Elske Loyalty −1 — the court will tighten expectations) or deliver an optimistic report (IP +0 this season, but Laskaris's standing at court −1 — next instruction will be non-negotiable)."*
- Mechanically identical to v2 Hardar card. Framing shifts from romantic to administrative.

### Arc 12: The Chained Ships
**NPCs affected:** Dalla Virke (expanded).

**Key change:** The Thread-touched supply chain is now Virke's *personal* professional reputation, not institutional Niflhel negligence. Exposure doesn't just destroy the supply chain — it destroys Virke's credibility as a broker. "I know what I'm selling" is her brand. She didn't know.

**Revised mechanics:**
- Virke exposure: in addition to existing mechanical effects, add **Virke personal credibility collapse.** All Virke syndicate operations on the peninsula suffer −1D for 4 seasons (her network partners question every prior deal). Rival syndicates (unnamed, GM discretion) move into the vacuum. The replacement syndicate has no trust relationships — pure transactional, higher prices, worse intelligence.
- Virke's NETWORK PROTECTOR flaw is now more poignant: she shields partners from the truth about her own supply chain. If she discovers the Thread connection before anyone else (possible via interaction with a practitioner who identifies the goods), she faces the choice: tell her partners (destroy her reputation, protect their safety) or stay silent (maintain her brand, expose partners to Coherence effects). Her NPC AI resolves this based on relationship duration: partners she's known 5+ years = tell. Newer partners = silence.

**Revised event card: THE EFFICIENT ARRANGEMENT (REVISED)**
- Add to mechanical effect: "If Virke discovers Thread contamination before external investigation: Virke player (or NPC AI) chooses DISCLOSE (reputation −1D for 4 seasons, partners protected, Virke Stability +1 from demonstrated loyalty) or CONCEAL (reputation intact, contamination continues, but any subsequent external discovery = Virke Stability −3 instead of −2 because she *knew*)."

### Arc 13: Wait Until It Sings
**NPCs affected:** None directly. Almstedt and Solberg drive this arc.
**Strand addition:** Strand's OVERPERFORMER flaw means Crown's administrative capacity is *better* than average while he's active (+1D Treasury). This makes Crown's Domain Action failure rate lower (~25% instead of ~30%) — Almstedt's patience strategy takes longer to pay off because Crown degrades more slowly. But when Strand breaks (from Arc 11 or independent overload), Crown's failure rate spikes to ~40% for 2 seasons. Almstedt's window opens explosively rather than gradually.

**Revised event card: THE KINGMAKER'S MOMENT (REVISED)**
- Add trigger condition: "OR Strand has broken (Crown admin +1 Ob active)." Strand's collapse can independently trigger Almstedt's kingmaker moment for Crown faction even if other factions haven't weakened yet.

### Arc 14: The Purple Shroud
**NPCs affected:** Minor. Strand replaces Severin in the Crown response chain.

**Strand addition:** During the PI crisis, Strand's OVERPERFORMER flaw means he attempts to manage the crisis personally — redirecting treasury resources, negotiating with rioters, coordinating supply lines. This is actually helpful for 1 season (Crown gets +1D on crisis management rolls). But Strand is not a military commander or a diplomat — he's an administrator trying to solve a political-military crisis with administrative tools. His flattery vulnerability means the Church's offer (Arc 14: Church's Offer card) can be framed as respecting Strand's administrative authority: "Lord Steward, we defer to your judgment on how to implement the restoration of order." Strand, flattered, grants the Church more operational latitude than he should — the TC +5 cost becomes TC +6 (Strand allowed Church garrison logistics through treasury channels).

**Revised event card: THE CHURCH'S OFFER (REVISED)**
- Add: "If Strand is the primary Crown administrator receiving the offer: −1 Ob on Church social action to frame the offer as deferring to Strand's authority. If this modified social action succeeds at Overwhelming: TC +6 instead of +5 (Strand's administrative efficiency accelerates Church logistics)."

### Arc 15: The Banker's War
**NPCs affected:** Severin → **Strand** (minor role). Core arc (Feldhaus + Solberg) unchanged.

**Strand addition:** Strand's Treasury role means he sees the Guilds' trade data more clearly than anyone at Crown. His OVERPERFORMER flaw means he's personally reviewing ledgers. He may detect the dual-funding earlier than other Crown figures — but his flattery vulnerability means Feldhaus can delay his suspicion by treating him as a financial peer. "Lord Steward, I've prepared a summary of our joint trade performance. I thought you would want to see the numbers before the formal report..." Strand, flattered, accepts the curated data.

**Revised mechanics:**
- Crown detection of dual-funding: Strand's Treasury access means Crown gets +1D on Investigate targeting Guilds trade. But Feldhaus social action targeting Strand (−1 Ob via flattery) can place a "Curated Data" condition: while active, Crown's +1D detection bonus is nullified (Strand is seeing what Feldhaus wants him to see).
- If Strand independently detects the dual-funding (success despite Curated Data): Strand's OVERPERFORMER means he acts alone — he doesn't report to the Duke first, he confronts Feldhaus directly. This gives Feldhaus a private warning before any institutional response. She can burn the evidence, shift the ledger, or offer Strand a personal trade partnership (flattery + financial incentive). Strand, isolated by his own inability to delegate, is vulnerable to co-option.

**New event card: THE CURATED LEDGER**
- **Trigger:** Feldhaus and Strand are both active AND Double Ledger token is present.
- **Timing:** Phase 4, Priority 1 (before Investigate resolution).
- **BG text:** *"The Guild's senior representative has been sending the Lord Steward personally curated trade summaries. Beautiful documents. Comprehensive. Accurate on every line — except the ones that matter."*
- **Mechanical effect:** Feldhaus social action vs Strand (Ob = Strand's relevant stat ÷ 2 − 1 for flattery = effectively Ob 1). Success: place "Curated Data" token on Crown faction board. While present: Crown's +1D detection bonus on Guilds trade Investigate is nullified. Token persists until Strand is removed, another Crown figure reviews the data (Senator Inward, Ob 2), or Feldhaus's social action fails a renewal check (fires each season Curated Data is active).
- **NPC involvement:** Feldhaus (PROFIT-MAXIMISING — the curated data IS her defensive play); Strand (OVERPERFORMER + flattery vulnerability — he's being played because someone is treating his work as important).

## New Arc Opportunity: Gerik Strand

Strand's profile is rich enough for a dedicated arc. His OVERPERFORMER flaw, flattery vulnerability, and conditional position create a unique arc structure: **the indispensable man who can be stolen.**

### Arc 18: The Stolen Steward
**Historical parallel:** Thomas Cromwell — Henry VIII's indispensable administrator, a man of ability in a world of birth, who was destroyed when his administrative competence couldn't protect him from political maneuvering he couldn't see because he was too busy running everything.

**Arc shape:** 4-season slow corruption, 1-season exposure, 1-season aftermath.

#### Narrative

Gerik Strand is the best administrator on the peninsula. Everyone knows it. The Duke appointed him because competence was needed, not because birth warranted it. Strand has run the Crown treasury flawlessly for years. He has reduced waste, improved tax collection, and made the Crown's economic machinery the most efficient on the peninsula.

This is his vulnerability.

Every faction needs what Strand provides. And Strand's flattery vulnerability (−1 Ob on social actions that acknowledge his competence) means each faction can establish an influence channel. The Church sends a Cardinal to discuss fiscal policy "with the one man at court who understands numbers." Hafenmark's parliamentary delegation invites him to a working dinner "because the Crown's Lord Steward is the only person who can explain the treasury's position competently." Varfell's intelligence approach frames him as "a man of substance in a court of ornaments." Each approach is genuine in its appreciation — Strand IS competent, and the acknowledgment IS sincere. That's what makes it work.

Over 4 seasons, Strand accumulates influence debts to multiple factions. Each debt is small — a private briefing here, early access to treasury data there, a favorable interpretation of trade regulations elsewhere. None is treasonous individually. Collectively, they mean Strand has given every major faction a window into Crown finances that the Duke never authorised.

The exposure comes when two factions use their Strand-sourced information simultaneously in a way that contradicts Crown interests. The Duke reviews what happened and traces both leaks to Treasury. Strand is confronted. His defence — "I was serving the Crown's interests by maintaining productive relationships with all factions" — is technically true and politically devastating.

#### Event Cards

**EVENT CARD: THE WORKING DINNER**
- **Trigger:** Any faction's Senator Outward or Consul Outward targeting Crown while Strand is active AND the action includes "genuine acknowledgment of Strand's competence" (declared intent in BG; roleplayed in TTRPG).
- **Timing:** Phase 4, Priority 4 (Social).
- **BG text:** *"The Lord Steward has been invited to discuss matters of state. He is flattered. He should not be."*
- **Mechanical effect:** Social action vs Strand at −1 Ob (flattery). Success: place an "Influence Debt" token (named for the acting faction) on the Strand NPC card. While any Influence Debt token is present: that faction gains +1D on Investigate targeting Crown (Strand provides early intelligence through administrative channels, unaware he is being cultivated). Multiple factions may hold simultaneous Debt tokens. If 2+ Debt tokens are present for 2+ consecutive seasons: Exposure check fires at Year-End (Duke's review: Crown Influence vs Ob 2; Success = Strand exposed).
- **NPC involvement:** Strand (OVERPERFORMER + flattery vulnerability — he is the mechanism AND the vulnerability).

**EVENT CARD: THE TREASURY LEAK**
- **Trigger:** 2+ factions hold Influence Debt tokens on Strand AND Year-End Duke's review succeeds (Crown Influence vs Ob 2).
- **Timing:** Year-End Accounting Step 7 (Once-Per-Year effects).
- **BG text:** *"The Duke asks a simple question about the trade figures. Two numbers don't match. They don't match because two different factions received two different versions of the same report — both from the Lord Steward's office. The Lord Steward has been very helpful. Too helpful."*
- **Mechanical effect:** Strand exposed. Crown player (or Crown NPC AI) must choose: RETAIN (Strand chastened, flattery vulnerability removed for 2 seasons, all Influence Debt tokens cleared, Crown admin +1D continues), REMOVE (Crown admin +1 Ob for 2 seasons, all faction Investigate bonuses from Strand removed, Crown Stability +1), or LATERAL MOVE (Strand reassigned to Altonian portfolio — Crown admin +1 Ob in Treasury, Strand's OVERPERFORMER now drives IP management, creating Arc 11 trigger conditions). Each faction that held an Influence Debt token and is exposed: Mandate −1 vs Crown (the Duke knows who was cultivating his steward).
- **NPC involvement:** Strand (exposed — passive, awaiting Duke's judgment); Duke Almud (if present — Crown faction leader makes the call); Laskaris (if Strand is laterally moved to Altonian portfolio, Laskaris gains a new counterpart he can manipulate via flattery more easily than any dedicated diplomat).

## Laskaris Naming Propagation

All references in Arcs 10–17 to "Hardar Veldensohn" or "Hardar" must be updated to "Doux Alexios Laskaris" or "Laskaris." The event card "The Altonian Husband" is renamed "The Altonian Doux." Mechanical effects unchanged.

## Severin Almud Disposition

Severin Almud is removed from all arc assignments. His COMMITMENT-LOCKED function is absorbed by Strand's OVERPERFORMER (see Arc 11 reassignment above). The Altonian treaty management function is now distributed between Strand (Valorian side, administrative) and Laskaris (Altonian side, diplomatic). No dedicated treaty manager exists — this is intentional. The absence of a specialist creates the brittleness that produces Arc 11's cascade.

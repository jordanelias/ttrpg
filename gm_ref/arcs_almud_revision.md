# Arcs 10–18 — Almud Competence Revision
## Date: 2026-04-08 | Triggered by: King Almud is not an incompetent cliché

---

## Principle

King Almud Almqvist is a competent ruler who identifies talent, deploys it strategically, and monitors the agent-principal problem. He appointed Strand because he needed administrative competence. He anticipated the social dynamics of placing a provincial lord at court. He is not surprised by court politics — he navigates them.

**This means:**
- Almud's decisions are never "he didn't notice." They are "he noticed, calculated, and chose."
- When Almud loses, it's because the situation exceeded what any competent ruler could manage — not because he made errors a sharp player would avoid.
- Almud's institutional collapse (if it happens) is tragic: the right man doing the right things in a world where the compound pressures are simply too many.
- Other NPCs and factions operate *under Almud's awareness*, not around his obliviousness.

## Arc-by-Arc Revisions

### Arc 11: Three Crowns in a Season

**Old framing:** Crown collapses from Stability 0. Implied: institutional failure = leadership failure.

**Revised framing:** Almud has been managing three simultaneous crises — TC advancement, IP pressure, and internal PI erosion — with limited resources. He's been making correct decisions at each node. The problem is that the decision tree has more branches than one ruler can prune. Each correct decision in one domain creates a vulnerability in another:
- Suppressing TC costs Crown actions that could manage IP.
- Managing IP (via Severin's old role, now Strand's overloaded portfolio) costs attention on internal reform.
- Maintaining PI via Almstedt's Parliamentary apparatus requires tolerating Almstedt's reform-blocking.

Crown Stability → 0 is not Almud's failure. It's the system's failure under compound load. Almud may be the last person making rational decisions in a room where every rational decision accelerates a different clock.

**Mechanical revision:** When Crown Stability reaches 1, add a **King's Gambit** decision point (replaces passive decay to 0):

**EVENT CARD: THE KING'S GAMBIT**
- **Trigger:** Crown Stability reaches 1.
- **Timing:** Accounting, before Step 3 (before elimination check).
- **BG text:** *"The King surveys what remains. One more failed Domain Action ends the Crown. Almud is not the kind of man who lets that happen by accident. He chooses where to spend his last reserves."*
- **Mechanical effect:** Almud (Crown player or Crown NPC AI) makes a binding declaration choosing one of:
  - **HOLD THE LINE:** Crown takes no offensive Domain Actions next season. All Crown actions are defensive (Govern, Stabilise, Recess). Stability cannot decrease from Domain Action failure (no offensive actions = no failure risk). Crown recovers Stability +1 at next Accounting if no other faction directly attacks Crown this season. *Cost:* Crown cedes initiative for a full season. TC, IP, and PI advance unchecked by Crown.
  - **CALCULATED STRIKE:** Crown designates one Domain Action as the decisive play. That action gets +2D (Almud's personal authority behind it). If it succeeds: Stability +1 (vindication). If it fails: Stability → 0 immediately, but the failure was a *chosen risk*, not a passive collapse. Crown's narrative legacy is different — the King went down swinging, which affects NPC loyalty cascades.
  - **ORDERLY TRANSITION:** Almud initiates succession planning before collapse. Torben Loyalty +1 (the transition is managed, not chaotic). Löwenritter Coup Counter does NOT advance (Ehrenwall has no pretext — the Crown is cooperating). Strand receives explicit instructions (Almud's final administrative act): Crown Domain Action cards transfer to a "Regency Council" controlled jointly by the strongest non-Church, non-Löwenritter faction. This is Almud engineering the post-Crown order rather than letting it emerge from chaos.

**NPC interaction:** If Almud chooses ORDERLY TRANSITION, Brandt's EXTERNAL THREAT FIXATED flaw is overridden for 1 season — Almud's final order directs Löwenritter to hold internal positions during the transition, not redeploy to the border. Brandt obeys (Almud is still the King) but resents it. After the transition season, Brandt reverts to type and marches for the passes.

### Arc 14: The Purple Shroud

**Old framing:** Church offers to restore order. Crown (implicitly a confused ruler) accepts or refuses.

**Revised framing:** Almud has already calculated the TC +5 cost. He knows what the Church garrison means. The decision is not "do I understand the price?" — it's "is the price worth paying given my current position?"

Almud's calculation:
- PI crisis costs: estimated Stability −1/season per affected territory while crisis persists. If 2 territories affected, that's −2/season. Crisis likely persists 2 seasons without intervention = −4 Stability across Crown holdings.
- Church offer costs: TC +5 immediate. Permanent garrison = effective Church administrative control of one territory. Long-term: accelerates TC toward victory condition.
- Math: If Crown Stability > 4, Almud can absorb the PI damage and *reject* the Church offer. If Crown Stability ≤ 3, the PI damage risks elimination — the Church offer is the rational play even though the long-term cost is severe.

**Revised event card: THE CHURCH'S OFFER (v3)**
- Add: "Almud's Decision Matrix (BG reference): If Crown Stability ≥ 5: Crown NPC AI rejects the offer (Almud can absorb the crisis). If Crown Stability 3–4: Crown NPC AI evaluates — reject if another faction is deploying military to suppress (Löwenritter, Hafenmark), accept if no ally is acting. If Crown Stability ≤ 2: Crown NPC AI accepts (survival arithmetic). Player-controlled Crown always has free choice — this matrix governs NPC AI only."
- Remove the implication that accepting the offer is a "confused" decision. Frame it as: "The King accepts because the math requires it. He is not grateful. He is counting the cost."

### Arc 15: The Banker's War

**Old framing:** Strand detects dual-funding; Feldhaus can delay via flattery.

**Revised framing:** Almud has *multiple* information channels into trade data. Strand is the most visible, but:
- The Ministry (Almstedt) processes trade documentation through Parliamentary records.
- Crown's own tax collection generates independent revenue data.
- Almud appointed Strand partly as a *canary* — a visible administrator whose interactions reveal who's trying to cultivate Crown access.

Almud may detect the dual-funding before Strand does, through pattern recognition across multiple data streams. The question isn't "does Crown discover it?" — it's "when does Almud decide to act on it?"

**Revised mechanics:**
- Crown detection: Strand's +1D Investigate bonus remains (he's the primary channel). But add: Almud's independent assessment fires at Year-End (Crown Influence vs Ob 3 — harder than Strand's channel, because Almud is reading aggregate patterns, not individual ledgers). If Almud detects independently: he does NOT confront Feldhaus immediately. He uses the information. Almud's response is to *manage* the dual-funding — letting it continue while positioning Crown to benefit from it.
- **Almud's Managed Dual-Funding:** If Almud detects and does not expose: Crown gets +1D on all Domain Actions targeting Guilds for the remainder of the campaign (Almud has leverage he hasn't played). This is an intelligence advantage, not a public exposure. Almud keeps the information until the moment it's worth more as a weapon than as a secret.
- **Interaction with Strand's exposure:** If Strand independently detects and confronts Feldhaus (per his OVERPERFORMER flaw — acts alone, doesn't report first): Almud has a problem. His steward just burned an intelligence asset Almud was deliberately preserving. Strand's OVERPERFORMER flaw isn't just a vulnerability to external factions — it's a principal-agent problem within Crown. Strand's initiative, which is what makes him valuable, is also what makes him dangerous when it acts outside Almud's strategic frame.

**New event card: THE KING'S PATIENCE**
- **Trigger:** Crown Year-End Influence check (Ob 3) succeeds against Guilds while Double Ledger token is present.
- **Timing:** Year-End Accounting Step 7 (Once-Per-Year effects) — fires BEFORE Strand's exposure check.
- **BG text:** *"The King reviews the annual accounts. He sees what his Lord Steward has not yet reported. He says nothing."*
- **Mechanical effect:** Place "King's Patience" token on Crown board. While present: Crown gets +1D on all Domain Actions targeting Guilds (latent leverage). Token persists until Almud chooses to play it (convert to public exposure: PI +2, Guilds Stability −2, Double Ledger removed, Crown Mandate +1 — the King exposes corruption on his own terms) OR until Strand independently exposes the dual-funding (token removed — intelligence asset burned, Almud's advantage lost, no Crown Mandate bonus).
- **NPC AI:** Crown NPC AI holds the King's Patience token for minimum 2 seasons (Almud is not impulsive). Plays it when: Guilds Wealth ≥ 6 AND Crown needs a Mandate boost for a specific Domain Action, OR when another faction is about to independently discover the dual-funding (Almud exposes first to claim credit).
- **NPC involvement:** Almud (the strategist who holds the card); Strand (OVERPERFORMER — may burn the asset by acting independently); Feldhaus (PROFIT-MAXIMISING — does not know the King is watching her specifically).

### Arc 18: The Stolen Steward

This is the arc most affected by Almud's competence. The entire framing must shift.

**Old framing:** Strand leaks data, Duke eventually discovers, Duke decides.

**Revised framing:** Almud appointed Strand knowing three things:
1. Strand is the best administrator available.
2. Strand's social insecurity (conditional position, meritocrat in a birth-based court) makes him vulnerable to cultivation.
3. A cultivated steward is an intelligence channel — factions that approach Strand reveal their priorities.

Almud is using Strand as a *honeypot*. Not consciously cynical — Almud genuinely needs Strand's administrative talent. But he's sophisticated enough to see the second-order effect: if every faction tries to cultivate the Lord Steward, the pattern of cultivation tells the King who wants what.

**The arc shifts from "Strand is corrupted" to "Almud's strategic tolerance has a shelf life."**

Almud tolerates Strand's influence debts as long as:
- The intelligence value (reading who's cultivating Strand) exceeds the intelligence cost (what Strand leaks).
- Strand's administrative output remains net-positive for Crown.
- No single faction achieves a decisive advantage through Strand's access.

The arc fires when the balance tips — when a faction leverages Strand's access for something that damages Crown more than the intelligence is worth. Almud's response isn't "how could this happen?" — it's "the investment stopped paying off."

**Revised mechanical chain:**

The Exposure check at Year-End is renamed **Almud's Strategic Review.** It's not "does Almud find out?" — it's "does Almud decide the arrangement has become net-negative?"

**Revised event card: THE TREASURY LEAK → THE STRATEGIC REVIEW**
- **Trigger:** 2+ factions hold Influence Debt tokens on Strand AND Year-End fires.
- **Timing:** Year-End Accounting Step 7.
- **BG text:** *"The King reviews his Lord Steward's calendar. He knows who Strand has been meeting. He has always known. The question is whether the arrangement still serves the Crown."*
- **Mechanical effect:** Almud rolls Crown Influence vs Ob = number of factions holding Influence Debt tokens. (2 factions = Ob 2; 3 factions = Ob 3; 4 = Ob 4). **Success:** Almud concludes the arrangement is net-negative. Proceeds to RETAIN/REMOVE/LATERAL MOVE choice — but framed as Almud's *strategic decision*, not a reactive discovery. **Failure:** Almud concludes the arrangement is still net-positive. Influence Debt tokens persist. Intelligence continues flowing both directions. Almud's patience continues.
- **Critical framing change:** If Almud chooses RETAIN, it's because he's calculated that the cultivated Strand remains more useful than a clean Strand. The influence debts are *known costs* he's absorbing, not unknown leaks he's tolerating. If he chooses REMOVE, it's because a specific faction used Strand's access for something that tipped the equation — and Almud can identify which faction did it. Removal is targeted, not panicked.
- **If Almud chooses RETAIN:** Add a "Monitored Steward" condition. Strand's flattery vulnerability shifts from −1 Ob to +0 (Almud has quietly warned Strand — "be careful who you impress, Lord Steward" — without explaining why). Factions that attempt new cultivation discover Strand is less receptive. Existing debts persist but no new ones accumulate without Overwhelming social action results.

**Revised event card: THE WORKING DINNER (v3)**
- Add to mechanical effect: "Almud's awareness: each Influence Debt token placed on Strand also places a hidden 'King's Intelligence' token on the acting faction (Crown tracks who is cultivating Strand). These tokens have no mechanical effect unless Almud chooses to play them — converting one King's Intelligence token into +2D on a single Crown Domain Action targeting that faction (Almud reveals he knows their priorities). King's Intelligence tokens are consumed on use."

## Updated NPC Interaction Pattern

The Almud-Strand relationship is now a three-layer system:

**Layer 1 (visible to all):** Strand is the Crown's Lord Steward. Competent, slightly insecure, flattery-vulnerable. Factions cultivate him.

**Layer 2 (visible to Almud only):** Almud monitors Strand's cultivation pattern. Each approach reveals a faction's priorities. Almud accumulates intelligence.

**Layer 3 (visible to players who investigate):** Players who Tribune Investigate Crown (Ob 3) or interact with Almud directly (social scene, TTRPG) can discover that Almud is not oblivious to Strand's debts. This discovery changes the player's strategic calculus — cultivating Strand is no longer a secret advantage, it's a *known channel* that the King can read. Players may choose to feed false priorities through Strand, knowing Almud is watching. The intelligence game deepens.

## Almud NPC Profile (Informal — for arc consistency)

Not a full NPC roster entry (that's [EDITORIAL] territory), but the behavioral assumptions the arcs require:

- **Decision-making:** Bayesian. Updates on new information. Does not commit to positions prematurely. Holds options open.
- **Flaw (if any):** The compound-clock problem. Almud is managing TC, IP, PI, RS, and internal faction dynamics simultaneously. He can make optimal decisions on any 3 of these. He cannot make optimal decisions on all 5. His "flaw" is structural, not personal — no ruler could.
- **Strand relationship:** Principal-agent with monitoring. Almud values Strand's output and manages his vulnerability. Not cynical exploitation — genuine appreciation of competence combined with strategic awareness of its side effects.
- **Risk posture:** Will accept short-term costs for long-term positioning. Will NOT accept costs that risk Crown elimination (Stability 0 is a hard boundary). Between those poles, he calculates.

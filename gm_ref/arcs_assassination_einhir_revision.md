## Two Missing Threads

### 1. The Hunting Accident (E-01)

The assassination of Almud's father (~218 AG) is established canon (`designs/ttrpg/valoria_narrative_scenario_chains.md`, Arc 1). Four possible perpetrators (Church, Niflhel, Varfell, Altonian agent). Perpetrator is undefined — campaign revelation candidate E-01.

**Critical reframing from user:** It really may have been an accident. The "open secret" is the political class *assuming* assassination because they cannot conceive of a king dying without someone's hand in it. This is itself a statement about the political culture — a world where accidental death is less believable than murder. The investigation may reveal that the conspiracy theories are wrong, and the political structures built on those theories (Almud's 27-year paranoid restraint, factional positioning around assumed culpability) are based on nothing.

This makes the mystery *better* — the possible revelation that it was genuinely accidental is as destabilising as any named perpetrator, because it means 27 years of political calculation were responses to a phantom. Almud's constraint (Belief 2) was self-imposed in response to a threat that may not exist.

**Current arc integration:** The assassination is NOT woven into Arcs 10–18. It should be. Almud's decision-making in every arc where he appears (11, 14, 15, 18) is shaped by 27 years under this shadow. His caution, his Bayesian patience, his compound-clock management — all of it carries the weight of a man who believes (but cannot prove) that someone killed his father and is still operating in the political system.

### 2. The Einhir North-South Social Caste

**Canon basis:** Foundations §11 ("The Betrayal as Epistemological Tragedy"). Northern Einhir had lower base-rate TS due to geographic distance from the Southernmost. They dismissed southern Einhir claims about threads not from cynicism but from genuine epistemic difference. This enabled the post-Calamity political settlement that suppressed southern Einhir cultural practices.

**User framing (not yet in canon but consistent with §11):**
- The peninsula's pre-Calamity name is **Valn** (or was — this may itself be a Forgotten name).
- Southern Einhir (those nearest the Southernmost) lived in the zone of historically weakest Thread stability — the zone most susceptible to catastrophic Thread events.
- The Calamity was a *geographical accident of proximity*, not a moral failing. The southern Einhir didn't cause it. They were just closest to it.
- Northern Einhir, whose lower TS meant they couldn't perceive what the south experienced, blamed the south for the Calamity. The Calamity weakened the peninsula enough for Altonian conquest. Therefore: southern Einhir bear the political-social-historical debt of having "caused" the conquest.
- This blame is structurally identical to a caste system. Southern Einhir heritage marks you. Northern Einhir heritage does not. The distinction persists 200+ years after the Calamity because the Forgetting prevents the epistemological correction that would dissolve it — the evidence that would show the Calamity was geographic, not moral, is literally unrememberable without sufficient TS.
- The Forgetting *perpetuates* the caste system. Northern Einhir cannot remember why the blame is wrong because remembering requires the TS they don't have. Southern Einhir cannot articulate their innocence because the evidence is epistemically inaccessible to anyone without TS ≥ 29.

**Current arc integration:** This dynamic is absent from Arcs 10–18 except tangentially through RM (Vossen, Maret Uln). It should be structural. The north-south Einhir divide is the social substrate beneath every political conflict in the game. The Church exploits it (heresy accusations target southern Einhir disproportionately). The Crown navigates it (Almud sympathises with the south but governs through the northern settlement). Hafenmark enforces it (procedural governance was built on the post-Calamity political order that blamed the south). Varfell exists partly because of it (western fjord communities are southern-adjacent and carry residual blame).

[EDITORIAL: "Valn" as peninsula name — not yet canonized. User introduced it. Needs formal determination: is "Valn" the pre-Calamity Einhir name, the pre-Altonian name, or the current colloquial name? Does the Forgetting affect the name itself? If you can only remember "Valn" with TS ≥ 29, then the name is a *cultural marker* — using it identifies you as connected to pre-Calamity knowledge, which is exactly the kind of thing the Church investigates.]

## Arc Revisions

### Arc 10: The Penitent Duke — Assassination Thread

Baralta's Sovereign Authority Doctrine fires in Parliament. Almstedt introduces the motion. The Church responds with Excommunication.

**What changes with the assassination:** Baralta *knows* about the Hunting Accident. She is Hafenmark — the institutional-procedural faction. Hafenmark's archives contain the Parliamentary investigation that was opened after the death and then quietly closed without conclusion. Baralta possesses the file. She has never used it because the information is a weapon that destroys whoever wields it (accusing a faction of regicide without proof is itself destabilising).

But in the Penitent Duke arc, Baralta is deliberately provoking Church Excommunication. If the Church excommunicates her, she needs the Grand Debate reversal to succeed. The assassination file is leverage she can deploy *during* the Grand Debate — not as evidence of Church guilt (she doesn't know who did it), but as evidence that the Church failed to investigate a possible regicide. The question "why didn't the Church pursue justice for the murdered king?" is potent regardless of who actually killed him.

**New mechanical interaction:**
- During Grand Debate reversal (Arc 10, Season N+2): Baralta may introduce the "Hunting Accident File" as a Debate argument. If introduced: +1D to Baralta's Debate pool for that exchange (the Church has no good answer). BUT: introduces the assassination into public discourse. All four possible perpetrator factions (Church, Niflhel, Varfell, Altonia) gain a "Suspected" marker. Any subsequent Investigate action targeting those factions re: the assassination gets −1 Ob (public attention on the question).
- If Baralta introduces the file AND the assassination was genuinely accidental (E-01 resolved as "no perpetrator"): the political fallout is *worse*, not better. Four factions are suspected of something that didn't happen. Every faction's response (denial, counter-accusation, evidence-destruction) produces noise that obscures governance for 2+ seasons. The phantom conspiracy becomes a real political crisis.

**Einhir social dynamic:** Baralta's motion targets Church jurisdictional authority over T9 Himmelenger. Himmelenger is in the northern mountain region — Church territory, but also historically the zone of strongest northern Einhir cultural influence. The motion implicitly challenges the post-Calamity settlement (Church authority was built on the same political order that blamed southern Einhir). If Baralta's motion succeeds, it doesn't just reduce TC — it questions the institutional legitimacy of the order that produced the caste system. Northern Einhir nobility read this correctly and oppose it. This is why Almud's Mandate cost is −2 for supporting the Restoration: northern Einhir nobility see any challenge to the post-Calamity order as threatening their political position, which is built on the assumption that the south was at fault.

### Arc 11: Three Crowns in a Season — Assassination Thread

If Crown collapses, the Hunting Accident becomes immediately relevant. Whoever claims Crown authority must answer the question Almud never answered: who killed the previous king?

**New event card: THE UNANSWERED QUESTION**
- **Trigger:** Crown leader falls (any cause) AND the Hunting Accident has not been resolved (E-01 still open).
- **Timing:** Accounting, after Crown elimination / succession fires.
- **BG text:** *"With the Crown in crisis, an old question returns. King Almud governed for 27 years without naming his father's killer. His successor inherits the question. Every faction at court remembers the Hunting Accident. Every faction has a theory. Some of those theories are weapons."*
- **Mechanical effect:** Place "The Unanswered Question" token on the board. While present: any faction may play a "Regicide Accusation" as a free action once per season — naming another faction as the suspected assassin. Accusation: target faction Mandate −1 (public suspicion). But: accusing faction must roll Influence vs Ob 3. Failure: accuser Mandate −1 instead (reckless accusation). If the accusation is TRUE (E-01 matches): target faction Mandate −2 and Stability −1 (the accusation sticks). If the accusation targets an innocent faction AND the death was accidental: accuser Mandate −1, but the accused also takes Mandate −1 (even false accusations leave residue).
- **Almud's competence note:** Almud never played this card because he understood that naming a suspect without proof would destabilise his own reign. His successors may not be as restrained. Strand (OVERPERFORMER) would be tempted to use the accusation as a political tool — "solving" the assassination would be the ultimate demonstration of competence. Strand doesn't know what happened, but his need to prove himself might drive him to name a suspect prematurely.

**Einhir social dynamic in succession:** If Crown falls and Löwenritter takes control (Brandt), the military government has no interest in the Einhir social question — Brandt sees external threats, not internal social dynamics. The caste system persists unchallenged. If Church mediates (TC +5), the Church actively reinforces the caste system — the post-Calamity order is the Church's political foundation. Only if Hafenmark (Baralta) or RM (Vossen) gains influence in the succession does the Einhir question advance. The succession arc is therefore also a *social justice* arc — who governs determines whether the caste system is challenged or entrenched.

### Arc 12: The Chained Ships — Einhir Caste Thread

The Thread-touched goods in Virke's supply chain are sourced from "Einhir ruins and southern communities" (per Virke's NPC profile). These goods are culturally significant to southern Einhir — they are artefacts of the pre-Calamity civilisation, the physical evidence of a culture that northern Einhir politics tried to bury.

**What changes:** The Thread contamination isn't just a supply chain problem. It's a cultural heritage problem. The goods the Prudence Cardinal redistributes to Church charities are *southern Einhir cultural artefacts* being stripped of context and distributed as generic charity supplies. The contamination is physical (Thread effects) but the violation is cultural: the Church is literally redistributing the material remains of the civilisation it helped suppress.

When the contamination is revealed (Fire at Red Cliffs), the political dimensions multiply:
- **Church:** Not just "distributed heretical goods" but "distributed stolen cultural property from the people they accused of causing the Calamity." Mandate −1 becomes Mandate −2 in territories with southern Einhir populations (the insult is specific).
- **RM:** The contamination reveals that Einhir artefacts are Thread-active — which means the southern Einhir cultural practices the Church suppressed were *actually connected to Thread reality*. This is evidence for the Restoration's claims. CV −1 in affected territories (the contamination proves the RM's point).
- **Northern Einhir nobility:** If the artefacts' Thread-activity is documented, it undermines the narrative that southern Einhir Thread practices were superstitious nonsense. The caste system's intellectual foundation (southern Einhir were irresponsible with forces they didn't understand) is contradicted by evidence that they understood the forces perfectly well — the forces were simply too large for anyone to control, regardless of geographic position.

**New interaction in event card: THE FIRE AT RED CLIFFS (v3)**
- Add: "If Thread contamination is publicly documented (not just revealed but formally investigated with evidence): RM gains +1 Presence marker in the affected territory (the evidence validates their cultural claims). Church Mandate −2 (not −1) in territories with southern Einhir populations. Northern Einhir nobility: Almud's Mandate cost for supporting the Restoration drops from −2 to −1 (the intellectual basis for blame is weakened)."

### Arc 13: Wait Until It Sings — The Caste System's Administrative Machinery

Almstedt's CONSERVATIVE flaw preserves Parliamentary procedure. But Parliamentary procedure was built on the post-Calamity political order — the order that blamed southern Einhir for the conquest. When Almstedt blocks reform, he is not just preserving process. He is preserving the institutional architecture of the caste system.

Almstedt doesn't know this. He doesn't think about Einhir social dynamics. He thinks about procedure. But the procedure he maintains was designed by the generation that built the post-Calamity settlement, and that settlement's foundational assumption is that southern Einhir Thread practices weakened the peninsula. Every Parliamentary Manoeuvre he blocks, every reform he delays at +1 Ob, is mechanically preserving the institutional framework of the caste system.

**What changes:** Players who want to address the Einhir social question cannot do it through Parliamentary reform while Almstedt is active — because the Parliament itself is built on the caste system's assumptions. Almstedt is not a villain; he is the system's immune response, and the system was built on an injustice he doesn't see.

**Revised event card: THE KINGMAKER'S MOMENT (v3)**
- Add to aftermath: "If the faction that co-opts Almstedt is RM-aligned (or if Almstedt is co-opted during a period when RM Influence ≥ 3 in T13): the first reform Almstedt facilitates can be a *constitutional acknowledgment* that the Einhir cultural question is legitimate — not resolution, but recognition. This acknowledgment has no immediate mechanical effect. It changes the Ob for future Einhir-related reforms from +1 (Almstedt blocks) to +0 (Almstedt neutral on the question because the process has declared it legitimate). The caste system is not dismantled. It is made *addressable*."

### Arc 14: The Purple Shroud — Southern Einhir in the Streets

When PI reaches crisis and the streets erupt, who is in the streets? Not just Crown loyalists and Church faithful. Southern Einhir communities in Valorsplatz — people who have lived under social stigma for generations — have their own grievances. A PI crisis creates the conditions for these grievances to surface publicly for the first time.

**What changes:** The riot is not just a sovereignty crisis (Crown vs Church). It's a social crisis. Southern Einhir rioters are not fighting for Crown or against Church — they're fighting for recognition. Their presence in the streets complicates every faction's response:
- Church intervention suppresses the riot but also suppresses the Einhir grievance — CV effect.
- Military suppression (Löwenritter/Brandt) is indiscriminate — southern Einhir communities take casualties alongside other rioters.
- Parliamentary mediation (Almstedt) addresses the sovereignty question but not the social question.
- Only RM engagement addresses the Einhir dimension — but RM is too institutionally weak to suppress a riot.

**New event card: THE SOUTHERN VOICE**
- **Trigger:** "The Streets Erupt" event is active AND RM has ≥ 1 Presence marker in the affected territory.
- **Timing:** Phase 4, after "The Streets Erupt" suspends other actions.
- **BG text:** *"The southern Einhir communities have joined the riot. They are not fighting for the Crown. They are not fighting against the Church. They are fighting because someone finally asked them to stand up. The riot now has two dimensions: the sovereignty question and the heritage question. They cannot be resolved independently."*
- **Mechanical effect:** While "The Southern Voice" is active: any action that resolves the sovereignty crisis (Church intervention, military suppression, Parliamentary mediation) also requires a simultaneous Einhir Response. Each resolution option has a forced Einhir consequence:
  - Church intervention + Einhir: CV +1 in affected territory (suppression reinforces the caste system). RM Presence marker removed (Church identifies RM sympathisers during pacification).
  - Military suppression + Einhir: CV +0 (no cultural shift — violence doesn't address the question). But: southern Einhir casualties create a Grievance Marker (persists 4 seasons; while present, RM Community Weaving in the territory is at −1 Ob).
  - Parliamentary mediation + Einhir: CV −1 in affected territory IF the mediation includes acknowledgment of southern Einhir grievance (Hafenmark Senator Outward, Ob 2 — the acknowledgment is easy if anyone thinks to include it). If the mediation does NOT include acknowledgment: CV +0 (the process addressed the political question and ignored the people).
  - RM direct engagement: Vossen (if available) can attempt Community Weaving during the crisis (Ob = (100−RS)÷20 round up, −1 for Presence marker). Success: CV −2 (the crisis becomes the moment the movement has been building toward). Failure: CV −1 anyway (the attempt was visible even if it failed). This is the only resolution option that advances RM's interests.
- **NPC involvement:** Vossen (IDEALIST — she will attempt Community Weaving during the crisis even when retreat would be safer); Haelgrund (PROCEDURALIST — if tasked with investigating the riot, his thorough documentation includes southern Einhir testimony, creating a permanent record of the grievance that future investigators can access).

### Arc 15: The Banker's War — Almud and His Father's Shadow

**The assassination thread in the dual-funding arc:** Almud's caution (the Bayesian patience from the Almud revision doc) is rooted in the Hunting Accident. For 27 years, Almud has operated on the assumption that someone in the political system killed his father and has never been identified. This makes him *structurally paranoid about information asymmetry.* He assumes every faction has secrets. He assumes every economic arrangement has a hidden dimension. This is why he independently detects Feldhaus's dual-funding (King's Patience card) — he looks for hidden structures because he has lived his entire reign inside one.

**What changes:** Almud's detection of the dual-funding is not just analytical competence. It's pattern recognition developed through decades of assuming the worst about everyone around him. If the assassination was genuinely accidental, this means Almud's greatest administrative asset (his paranoid attention to hidden structures) was produced by a false premise. The King's Patience — his ability to hold information as latent leverage — is a skill born from trauma.

**New mechanical interaction:**
- If Arc 1 (Hunting Accident) resolves DURING the period when King's Patience token is active: Almud's response depends on the resolution.
  - **Perpetrator identified (any faction):** Almud's paranoia is validated. King's Patience token gains +1D bonus when played (his pattern recognition is confirmed as correct).
  - **Accident confirmed (no perpetrator):** Almud's paranoia is revealed as 27 years of misreading. King's Patience token is NOT automatically played — Almud enters a period of self-doubt (1 season) during which Crown NPC AI does not take offensive Domain Actions. The self-doubt is not weakness; it's a competent man reassessing his entire decision framework. After 1 season: Almud either reaffirms his caution (King's Patience resumes, Almud decides the paranoia was useful regardless of its origin) or releases it (King's Patience played immediately, Almud acts on the dual-funding publicly, Mandate +1 — the King is finally acting openly instead of holding cards).

### Arc 17: The Sinigaglia Dinner — The Assassination as Intelligence Template

Vaynard's detection of Maret Uln's pattern (the Sinigaglia arc) uses the same intelligence methodology that someone (possibly Varfell, per E-01) used to plan the Hunting Accident. If Varfell is the E-01 perpetrator, the Sinigaglia arc is structurally recursive: Vaynard's intelligence apparatus, which may have killed a king, is now being used to detect disloyalty in his own operative.

**What changes:** If players have investigated the Hunting Accident (Arc 1) and discovered evidence pointing to Varfell: the Sinigaglia arc gains a new dimension. Maret Uln's Insurance File (evidence of Vaynard's Thread operations) may also contain evidence about the Hunting Accident — Maret had access to Varfell's historical operation files as Vaynard's senior analyst. Her insurance is not just Thread heresy; it's *regicide evidence*.

**Revised event card: THE INSURANCE FILE (v3)**
- Add: "If players have found partial evidence of Varfell involvement in the Hunting Accident (Arc 1, Varfell evidence trail accessed): Maret Uln's Insurance File contains a secondary payload — historical operation records from 218 AG. If the Insurance File activates AND this secondary payload is discovered (Tribune Investigate on the released documents, Ob 2): Arc 1 Varfell evidence trail advances to Full Evidence. This is the only pathway that connects the Sinigaglia arc to the Hunting Accident arc."
- "If E-01 is NOT Varfell: the secondary payload is empty. The historical records show routine intelligence operations, nothing connecting to the assassination. Maret's Insurance File is exactly what she says it is — Thread heresy evidence only."

### Arc 18: The Stolen Steward — Strand and the Caste System

Strand is from Feldmark (T5) — the breadbasket, productive but unglamorous. Feldmark is in the eastern lowlands, Crown heartland. This is northern Einhir territory. Strand's meritocratic ethic ("competence should determine standing, not birth") is progressive on the aristocratic axis — but he may be blind to the Einhir social axis.

**What changes:** Strand's flattery vulnerability extends beyond aristocratic recognition. If a southern Einhir figure (Vossen, or any southern-origin NPC) approaches Strand with genuine acknowledgment of his competence, the interaction carries a subtext Strand doesn't see: a person from the stigmatised caste treating a person from the privileged caste as a peer is itself a political act. Strand reads it as professional respect. The southern Einhir community reads it as one of their own approaching the enemy's steward. Crown court reads it as the Lord Steward consorting with RM sympathisers.

**New mechanical interaction:**
- If RM (via Vossen or proxy) successfully places an Influence Debt token on Strand: the token has a dual nature. Standard Influence Debt (RM gains +1D on Investigate targeting Crown — Strand provides data). But also: Strand begins unconsciously incorporating southern Einhir administrative practices into Crown treasury operations (he learned them from his RM contact, thinks they're just good accounting). Over 2 seasons: Crown Govern actions in territories with southern Einhir populations get +1D (Strand's imported practices are actually effective). But: northern Einhir nobility notice. Almud's Mandate cost for RM sympathy increases by +1 (the court sees Crown administration drifting toward RM-adjacent methods). Strand doesn't understand why the court is angry — he's just running a better treasury.

## New Arc Opportunity: The Forgetting as Caste Enforcement

The north-south Einhir dynamic is rich enough for its own dedicated arc. The Forgetting doesn't just erase cultural knowledge — it erases the *evidence that would dissolve the caste system.* Southern Einhir cannot prove their innocence because the proof requires TS ≥ 29 to perceive, and the caste system suppresses the cultural practices that develop TS.

This is a feedback loop: suppression → low TS → inability to access evidence → continued suppression.

Breaking the loop requires either:
1. A practitioner with TS ≥ 29 who can perceive and articulate the geographic nature of the Calamity (Edeyja could, but she won't engage in politics; Maret Uln at TS ~50 could, but she's trapped in Varfell)
2. Physical evidence that survives the Forgetting (Thread-touched artefacts from Arc 12; Lenneth's archive; Varfell's private collection)
3. A political actor who doesn't need evidence to act (Almud, if his constraints are eroded per the scenario chain)

**This is not a separate arc document — it's a note for future development.** The Einhir caste dynamic is woven into Arcs 10–18 via the revisions above. A dedicated "Breaking the Forgetting" arc would be the campaign's culminating social arc, drawing on evidence from Arcs 12 (Thread-touched artefacts), 16 (RM cultural momentum), and 17 (Maret Uln's defection brings TS ~50 practitioner into RM orbit).

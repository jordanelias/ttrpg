## 6. Final Recommendation — Second Opinion on the 44-Kept / 14-Cut Split

**The first pass's winnowing (58 → 44 kept, 14 cut) is vindicated on *authenticity* and substantially over-generous on *readiness*. This stricter pass materially changes the picture — not by finding fantasy impositions, but by finding that "kept" was being read as "done."**

Three concrete departures from the lenient pass:

**1. Three items the first pass KEPT do not survive strict adversarial review — they are CUT.** VEN-SE-1 (a riskless, apolitical doubled-output power spike inside a verb whose entire design law is that growth is never apolitical), HAB-2 (a Std-4 favor track that dominates the Std-6/7 gates it sits beside), and SE-JP4 (a static zero-upkeep freeze that also silently nullifies the corpus's own caste-critique escape valves). All three carry **Omega-d dominant-strategy failures the lenient pass missed** — and in two, the lenient pass's own batch had already CUT a structurally identical offender (VEN-FA-2's AP-buffer), so the miss was not a matter of unknowable information. Plus CHN-8 hardened to PRUNE. **That is 4 items (~10%) where the first pass was not just insufficiently demanding but wrong on the merits.**

**2. Only 17% of survivors are actually clean.** 7 KEEP / 41. The remaining 83% split into 21 REFINE (belongs, unbuildable as written) and 13 materially subtractive (DISTILL/MERGE/PRUNE/CUT). "Kept" means *the dynamic belongs in Valoria's ruleset* — which the strict pass overwhelmingly confirms (almost nothing here is a fantasy imposition; the grounding is real across the board). It does **not** mean the authored or pitched mechanic is correct. Conflating the two is the first pass's core error, and it is most dangerous exactly where it bites hardest:

**3. The open PR is not ratification-ready. 10 of its 12 authored items need revision before merge** — 8 REFINE and, critically, **2 MERGE (SE-JP1, CHN-9) that require deleting landed subsections** (SE-JP1's cells-of-five is *non-executable* against §4.5's 1–2-Local-Actor cap; CHN-9 is a relabeled clock whose headline promotion half is not a rule). Under CLAUDE.md §2's ED-1094 default, merging the PR ratifies all of this text. **Recommendation: hold the PR; land the 2 MERGE deletions and the 8 REFINE fixes inside it; ship HAB-5 and HAB-7 as-is.** These are exactly the "hard design calls bundled into routine-work" that ED-1083 §4 says must be surfaced loudly, not left to an unprompted follow-up.

**But this is a *discriminating* pass, not a uniformly harsher one — which is the real value over a naive re-run.** The steelman relay overturned 12 dossier verdicts, 11 of them *softening* over-eager duplicate-coverage cuts, and caught 4 incomplete stub-dossiers whose subtractive verdicts were artifacts of never finishing. A blunt "cut more" second opinion would have ratified those errors. Instead the strict pass tightens where the first was lax (4 items down to CUT/PRUNE) *and* loosens where the dossier over-reached (HRE-4/HRE-2/HAB-1/HAB-6 back to KEEP), while surfacing ~9 cross-item consolidation opportunities (§5) that no per-item pass could see. The corpus-economy stakes there are real: HRE-3, HAB-4, BYZ-6/IT-5, HRE-4, CHN-8 are each slated as reuse-substrate for multiple queued dependents, so fixing their interfaces *now* prevents the reskin proliferation the catalogue itself keeps warning about.

**Bottom line:** keep the first pass's belief that these dynamics belong; discard its implication that they are ready. The actionable delta is **4 hard removals the first pass wrongly kept, 2 authored subsections that must be deleted from the live PR, and ~34 items carrying concrete pre-ratification fixes** — with the docket-level "ease by one step" definition (§5.1) and the §1.6 tag-schema reconciliation (§5.8) as the two highest-leverage cross-cutting fixes.

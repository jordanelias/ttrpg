## 5. Derrida — Aporia

An aporia is an impasse — two equally necessary but mutually exclusive demands. Genuine decisions occur only at aporetic points.

**Where it lives:** Every Structural-scale thread operation (the unintelligible portion exceeds perception; the practitioner cannot justify the attempt rationally; yet the condition demands it). Also: the Church's institutional position — claiming authority over something (thread knowledge, P-08) it cannot by its own framework possess.

**Pattern — The Aporetic Knot:** Dense, conditional, hedged institutional prose holding two incompatible positions simultaneously.

## 8. Wittgenstein — Whereof One Cannot Speak

*Tractatus* 7: the boundary between what can be said and what can only be shown.

**Where it lives:** P-08 IS Tractatus 7. Thread knowledge cannot be propositionalized, only shown through experience. The Church's scriptures are an elaborate and beautiful silence dressed as speech.

**Pattern — The Wittgensteinian Halt:** A theological argument that proceeds rigorously to the point where argument fails, marks the failure, and stops. The silence after the final word is the text's most important content.

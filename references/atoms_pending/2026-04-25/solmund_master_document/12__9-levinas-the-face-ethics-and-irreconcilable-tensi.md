## 9. Levinas — The Face, Ethics, and Irreconcilable Tension

Ethics precedes ontology. The Other's face makes an infinite demand — vulnerability and transcendence simultaneously.

**Where it lives — two sites:**

**The Einhir Dead.** The dead have faces. The Church's theodicy (they deserved it) attempts to answer the demand. The demand cannot be answered. The Restoration Movement's deepest claim is ethical, not theological: you cannot explain away the dead.

**The Practitioner's Structural Obligation.** A monstrous entity demands response — not ethical demand (the entity has no face, no vulnerability) but structural necessity: the world's rendering depends on containment. The practitioner acts because the alternative — rendering fracturing for everyone in proximity — is not theirs to accept.

**Correction:** Levinas's face requires vulnerability. Solmund is a being of surfeit, not vulnerability. The Levinasian framework applies to the Einhir dead and the practitioner's obligation, not to Solmund's nature.

**Pattern — The Levinasian Naming:** Lists of names without explanation, theology, or argument.

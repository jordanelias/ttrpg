## 11. The Two Witness Traditions

**[PROPOSED — extrapolation grounded in canon principles, not established canon. Requires editorial acceptance.]**

### 11.1 Tradition One: The Miracle Witness (Non-Practitioner)

Farmers, merchants, labourers, children. The vast majority of Solmund's witnesses. They saw a man — uncanny, perceptibly static (§5.3) — who could do impossible things. They produced the oral tradition that became scripture. The framework (divine intervention, supernatural power) happens to be wrong about mechanism and nature. The witnesses are not lying. They could not have known better.

### 11.2 Tradition Two: The Practitioner Witness

Rare — perhaps numbering in the dozens across Solmund's five years. They perceived operations at a scale that should not exist. They immediately understood what they were seeing was not human. They produced accounts that never became scripture because the Church's interpretive community was built by non-practitioners. By the time the Church encountered these texts, it had committed to the miracle framework. Practitioner accounts were categorised as heresy.

The surviving texts describe Solmund's acts accurately — which is worse than denial, because accuracy reveals the mechanism, and the mechanism reveals that the Church's cosmology is a rendering applied to something the rendering cannot contain.

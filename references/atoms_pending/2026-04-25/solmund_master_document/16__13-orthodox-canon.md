## 13. Orthodox Canon

| Artifact | Register | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Scripture (Weavings of Solmund) | Hopkins | Dense, sensory. Describes Solmund's acts as divine weaving. Threads as instruments, not ontology. |
| Liturgy (Hours of the Loom) | Di Cicco | Daily prayer cycle. Morning: new thread. Evening: day woven into fabric. Embodied, rhythmic, seasonal. |
| Hymns | Hopkins | Short, vertical. Moments of recognition. Half-rhyme and assonance. |
| Cathedral Inscriptions | Cusa | Short texts carved above doors. Claim → qualify → transcend. |
| Doctrinal Treatises | Ficino + Eliot | Long, hierarchical, architecturally ordered. Official positions. |
| Graduation Texts | ibn Gabirol | Cathedral School/Monastic completion. Rigorous architecture, then confessional break. |
| Hagiography | Zohar | Narrative accounts of holy figures. Theology in the story, not commentary. |

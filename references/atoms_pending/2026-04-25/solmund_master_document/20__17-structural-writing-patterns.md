## 17. Structural Writing Patterns

**17.1 Cusan Triad (Inscriptions):** Assert → qualify → transcend. "Solmund binds the world in thread. / No thread binds what it is drawn from. / In the unbinding, Solmund."

**17.2 Ficinian Cascade (Treatises):** Source → hierarchy → individual. "From Solmund's hand, the first thread. From the first thread, the warp of the world... From the believer, the prayer that rises — thread returning to the hand that drew it."

**17.3 Di Cicco Inhabitation (Liturgy):** Present-tense, first-person plural, physically grounded. "We stand in the nave and the stone is cold under us. / The bell has rung. The morning has opened."

**17.4 Thomas Absence (Rural Sermons):** Spare, third-person, landscape-dominant. "The chapel at Feldmark has no bell. / There is a rope where the bell was. / The congregation pulls it on feast days / and the silence that follows is the sermon."

**17.5 John of the Cross Paradox (Practitioner Interior):** First-person singular, negating. "I reached for the thread and my hand passed through. / What held me was not what I held."

**17.6 Böhmean Eruption (Suppressed Heresy):** Syntactically strained, imagistic, contradictory. "The ground opens and it is not ground. The light beneath is darker than the dark above."

**17.7 Pican Address (Counter-Theology):** Second-person, rhetorical. "You were woven with eyes that see the thread. Whose hand covered them?"

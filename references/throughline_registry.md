# NARRATIVE THROUGHLINES (what the game is about)

## N1 — Thread Revelation Is the Master Clock
As MS drops, Thread becomes visible to non-practitioners. This drives every NPC arc (Himlensendt's theological crisis, Vossen's identity crisis, Edeyja's political emergence, Vaynard's vindication), every faction response, and the player's Certainty track. The revelation curve (threadwork_v30 §5.6) is the game's primary narrative driver — everything is downstream of "the world is discovering that the stories were true."

**Key docs:** threadwork_v30 §5.6, campaign_architecture_v1 §4, npc_behavior_v30 §5 (arc profiles)

## N2 — Sovereignty Is Governance, Not Conquest
Accord ≥ 2 in all territories to win. Military conquest produces Accord 1 (Resistant). Every faction must convert military control into genuine governance. The Accord-by-acquisition-method table (peninsular_strain §7b) makes this concrete: Cultural Reformation → Accord 3, Show of Force → Accord 2, Military conquest → Accord 1. How you take territory determines how well you can govern it.

**Key docs:** peninsular_strain_v1 §2/§5/§7b, victory_v30 §0, settlement_layer_v30 §3

## N3 — The Peninsula Is One World Through Different Lenses
The same geography, the same NPCs, the same Thread crisis — experienced as political map (Crown), theological mandate (Church), constitutional law (Hafenmark), knowledge pursuit (Varfell), community liberation (RM), substrate stewardship (Wardens). The Campaign Portrait describes the same world through whichever lens the player chose. This is the game's deepest replayability: not different content, but different meaning derived from the same content.

**Key docs:** victory_v30 §3 (faction strategies), player_agency_v30 §1, npc_behavior_v30 §2

## N4 — Every Ending Is Earned
Portrait Retirement is player-chosen (after ≥ 2 Convictions resolved). The Draft Portrait shows what the story means at any time. Conviction resolution states (Fulfilled/Failed/Transformed/Unresolved) give the player's choices a final shape. No ending is arbitrary — every ending is a consequence of what the player did and didn't do, described in the currency they chose to care about.

**Key docs:** player_agency_v30 §2 (Convictions), campaign_architecture_v1 §7 (Portrait/Lineage)

## N5 — The Forgetting Makes Knowledge Contested
Threadwork is experiential, not intellectual. You can read about it but you can't truly understand it without Thread sensitivity. This makes RM's relationship to its own heritage structurally incomplete — they're rebuilding governance models whose deepest foundations they can't access. The hidden Thread-site bonus (threadwork_v30 §5.6) makes this mechanically real: RM governance works better at certain sites and the player doesn't know why until Thread revelation explains it.

**Key docs:** threadwork_v30 §5.6, campaign_architecture_v1 §2 (RM identity), canon/00_philosophical_foundations.md

## N6 — Institutions Are Characters
The Church fills governance vacuums (Pastoral Assumption), provides social services (Parish Social Services), and builds infrastructure that becomes load-bearing. Factions are autonomous world actors with their own logic. The NPC stance triangle system means every faction leader has beliefs that drive decisions independent of the player. Institutions don't just oppose the player — they behave with their own coherent motivations.

**Key docs:** npc_behavior_v30 §4/§8, settlement_layer_v30 §1.5–1.7 (Church infrastructure), settlement_layer_v30 §3.3 (subnational factions)

# SYSTEM THROUGHLINES (how mechanics connect across documents)

## T1 — Thread at Settlement Level
Thread operations at Relational scale produce settlement-level consequences (Order, Prosperity, Defense) alongside province-level MS effects. Connects threadwork_v30 → settlement_layer → peninsular_strain (Accord) → victory (sovereignty).

**Docs:** settlement_layer_v30 §4.4, threadwork_v30 §2.4

## T2 — Resources
Personal resources connect character to faction. Extended by derived_stats_v1: faction stats produce Treasury/Legitimacy/Reputation/Cohesion. Pipeline: settlement Prosperity → faction Treasury income → costs → strategic pressure → player decisions.

**Docs:** player_agency_v30 §9, derived_stats_v1 §3

## T3 — Settlement POIs
Points of Interest per settlement, not per province. Exploration and investigation at settlement level connect personal-scale fieldwork to strategic-scale geography.

**Docs:** settlement_layer_v30 §4.6, fieldwork_v30 §3.1

## T4 — Ministry and Institutional Bureaucracy
Haelgrund is the throughline character for institutional governance. The Ministry represents administrative state capacity, connecting faction governance to settlement administration.

**Docs:** npc_behavior_v30 §2.14, tc_political_redesign_v30 §4.2

## T5 — Martial Law
Löwenritter martial law switches settlement governance to Military-based resolution. Connects military outcomes → settlement governance → Accord → victory.

**Docs:** peninsular_strain_v1 §2.6b, settlement_layer_v30 §3

## T6 — Altonian Invasion
Campaign-length external threat. IP rises from inter-faction battle (fighting each other invites invasion). Includes IP visibility milestones, Occupation Era, RM Underground conversion, Altonian Alignment (NPC AI).

**Docs:** military_layer_v30 §3, victory_v30 §5, campaign_architecture_v1 §5

## T7 — Local Actors
Non-faction NPCs at settlement level representing autonomous population will. Convictions and Dispositions. How the player interacts with "the people" as distinct from "the factions."

**Docs:** settlement_layer_v30 §4.5, npc_behavior_v30 §4.2

## T8 — Conviction Legacy
Convictions persist across character transitions (Lineage). A predecessor's fulfilled or failed Convictions shape the world the successor inherits.

**Docs:** player_agency_v30 §10, campaign_architecture_v1 §7.4

## T9 — Church Infrastructure Pipeline
Chapel → Church → Cathedral + Templar + Inquisitor + Governor → PT → TC → CI=100 Mass Seizure → Theocratic Bid. Includes Parish Social Services (dependency trap) and Pastoral Assumption (vacuum filling). The game's longest-running factional throughline — 20+ season accumulation traceable from individual settlement decisions to peninsula-wide transformation.

**Docs:** settlement_layer_v30 §1.5–1.7, campaign_architecture_v1 §1, victory_v30 §7, tc_political_redesign_v30 §2–3

## T10 — Economic Pressure as Strategic Constraint
Derived stats connect every military and governance decision to economic consequences. Campaign Supply drains Treasury. Siege drains Treasury. Muster costs Treasury. Upkeep costs Treasury. Income from settlement Prosperity through Wealth stat to Treasury. The player feels economic pressure as visible resource drain and responds strategically.

**Docs:** derived_stats_v1 §3.1 (Treasury), mass_battle_v30 §A.14b, military_layer_v30 §1.9

## T11 — RM's Identity Arc
Political movement → consensus cells → suppression survival (cell resilience) → Founding → Cultural Uprising → Governance Transition choice → Consensus Delay → Thread revelation forces Embrace/Denial/Schism → hidden Thread-site bonus reveals governance was partly metaphysical. The game's most complex factional throughline — every step is a player choice reshaping the movement's identity.

**Docs:** campaign_architecture_v1 §2, victory_v30 §3.5/§8, settlement_layer_v30 §3.3/§4.3, threadwork_v30 §5.6

## T12 — Morale and Legitimacy Cascade
Battle → Morale Cascade (rout spreads) → Cohesion drain → Accord erosion → governance difficulty → further Cohesion drain. Military defeat cascades from battlefield through institutional confidence to population-level governance acceptance. Derived stats make each step visible.

**Docs:** mass_battle_v30 §A.12, derived_stats_v1 §3.1 (Cohesion), peninsular_strain_v1 §2.4

## T13 — Scale Transition Pipeline
Character action → settlement consequence → territory consequence → peninsula consequence. The player acts at settlement level. Consequences propagate upward: settlement stats → Accord → derived values → faction stats → victory. Thread operations cascade similarly through scales.

**Docs:** settlement_layer_v30 §1/§4, derived_stats_v1 §4, peninsular_strain_v1 §2

## T14 — Warden Emergence
8–15 hermits → five paths to political relevance → consensus governance + Spirit-derived stats + Mending Sanctuary acquisition. Edeyja's arc is the personal expression. The Wardens represent the thesis that understanding the crisis and having the political power to address it are different capabilities — and bridging that gap requires compromise.

**Docs:** campaign_architecture_v1 §6, npc_behavior_v30 §2.9/§5 (Edeyja arc), victory_v30 §6

# Historical formation diagrams — sources for review

**Image/video files are not stored here.** The session's egress proxy denies (HTTP 403) direct binary
downloads from image/video hosts (Wikimedia, arXiv, YouTube, etc.), and organization policy denials must
not be worked around. Instead this directory holds:
- **this `SOURCES.md`** — links + citations to the canonical diagrams/animations, for you to open and
  review directly;
- **`schematic_*.svg/.html`** — faithful schematic renderings of each formation's geometry + movement
  (built from the authoritative geometry in the historical research, cited), which are copyright-clean
  and drawn on the *same coordinate convention* as the sim so they compare directly;
- the **sim-output** snapshots, side-by-side, so engine vs history can be judged panel-for-panel.

Each schematic cites the source(s) it was reconstructed from. Open the links below to check the
schematics against the originals.

---

## Cannae (216 BC) — double envelopment

- **Wikipedia, "Battle of Cannae"** — the standard phase-map sequence (initial deployment → Carthaginian
  crescent → center gives ground / crescent inverts → African veterans close both flanks → cavalry
  completes the rear encirclement). Maps by the Dept. of History, US Military Academy (West Point) —
  **public domain**. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Cannae
- **Wikimedia Commons — Cannae battle maps** (the phase set, PD): search
  https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Maps_of_the_Battle_of_Cannae
- **Historia Civilis, "Battle of Cannae, 216 BC"** — tick-by-tick animated reconstruction (the clearest
  moving-diagram of the crescent inversion + double wrap). https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JhpQhKZAY7w
- Adrian Goldsworthy, *Cannae* (Cassell, 2001), ISBN 9780304359711; Polybius *Histories* 3.107–118;
  Livy 22.44–49.

## Leuctra (371 BC) — oblique order / refused flank

- **Wikipedia, "Battle of Leuctra"** — deployment diagram: Theban deep left (~50 ranks) leading, center
  & right echeloned back (refused). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Leuctra
- **Historia Civilis, "Battle of Leuctra, 371 BC"** — animated oblique-order sequence.
  https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mAX9tW-Qw0Y
- Xenophon *Hellenica* 6.4; V.D. Hanson on Epaminondas/Leuctra; Sabin, *Lost Battles* (2007).

## Leuthen (1757) — Frederick the Great's oblique order

- **Wikipedia, "Battle of Leuthen"** — the classic oblique-order map (refused left, weighted right rolling
  up the Austrian line). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Leuthen

## Roman manipular deployment — triplex acies / quincunx

- **Wikipedia, "Manipular legion" / "Triplex acies"** — the three-line checkerboard (hastati / principes /
  triarii), quincunx gaps ≈ one maniple frontage, line-relief. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triplex_acies
  · https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maniple_(military_unit)
- Polybius *Histories* 6.19–42 (the manipular system); Goldsworthy, *The Roman Army at War* (1996).

## Hoplite / Hellenistic line & general "refuse a flank"

- **Wikipedia, "Phalanx" / "Oblique order"** — solid-line frontage (near-zero intervals) vs the oblique/
  refused-flank principle. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oblique_order

## Framing

Across all eight civilizations, political standing was never a single scalar. It was a *claim on an underlying currency* — trust (Ottoman *itimad*), patronage-credit (Roman *commendatio*), immediacy (Holy Roman *Reichsunmittelbarkeit*), assessed yield (Japanese *kokudaka*), or a captured eligibility-list (Florentine scrutiny-bags, Venetian *Balla d'Oro*) — and a rank was only ever as solid as the currency backing it. The visible office ladder (the Roman *cursus honorum*, the eight-rung Venetian climb from *Balla d'Oro* entrant to Doge, the Golden Bull's seven-tier Imperial hierarchy) was the scoreboard; the currency was the thing that actually moved, debased, and got seized. This is the single most load-bearing finding for Valoria's 0–7 Standing ladder: the rank number should be a *display aggregate* over a tracked, debasable currency, not ground truth. Han China makes this explicit, tracking office-salary grade, honorific *jue*, Access, and kinship-proximity as separate axes under one displayed rank; Venice computes standing as lineage-tier × cursus-completion × secrecy-record rather than one number.

Advancement everywhere ran on a small recurring menu — examination/credential, patronage/appointment, inheritance/kinship, military merit, purchase, election/franchise, land-grant bond, and (uniquely, Japan) adoption — but the civilizations differ sharply in *which* on-ramp they trust and how they price the rest. The strongest design lever is that these routes are *typed and convertible*: Milan's condottiere climbs military-merit Keys from contract to *capitano* but cannot reach Duke without converting his power_base to kinship (marriage) or purchase; the Ottoman *kul* trades a severed kinship tie for uncapped advancement velocity. Demotion is the mirror image — a graded magnitude scale (Florence supplies four distinct tiers; Han supplies relegation → forced-suicide → *zu*-extermination), with severity keyed to *how* the seat was won: favor-derived seats (Ottoman *kul* facing *müsadere* clawback, Roman freedmen, purchased offices) face total ruin, while kinship-derived seats get containment (the Ottoman *Kafes*, Carolingian blinding-not-execution, hostage-kin surrender).

Why this matters for Valoria: the corpus supplies direct, non-fabricated precedent for every field faction_politics already names — the resolution-quality→standing bridge, Demotion Magnitude, rank=secession-blast-radius, and power_base-typed Ascendancy — and, critically, the *non-Mandate-of-Heaven* legitimacy source the brief requires. The Mandate of Heaven is left as history only; in its place, every civilization independently evolved a "delivered-vs-demanded gap" audit (Roman *lex repetundae*, Carolingian *missi*, Imperial *Reichsexekution*, Han field-promotion and impeachment, Japanese *honryō-andō*/*shin-on*) that grants or strips rank on procedural performance, not cosmic favor. That gap *is* the bridge — and notably the Han material here is mined for *cha ju*, *yin*, impeachment and *zu*, never the cosmic-mandate rhetoric.

## Cross-civilizational synthesis

**The standing currency debases when rank-holders outrun it.** Rank is convertible into an underlying resource, and when too many seats chase too little backing, the currency inflates and the rank hollows out. Venice's *barnaboti* (impoverished nobles too numerous for the honor to mean anything) and Kamakura Japan's slide into *honryō-andō*-only confirmations after the Mongol invasions exhausted the confiscatable-land pool are the same phenomenon: stalled advancement as a *mechanical inevitability*, not fiat. Carolingian benefices calcifying into heritable property, and Japanese *shugo* drifting into hereditary *shugo-daimyo*, are the ossification variant — an appointive, revocable rank silently converting to title unless the suzerain actively intervenes.

**Merit is trusted precisely where kinship is severed.** The most reliable bureaucratic ladders deliberately *cut* the blood tie so the Crown can trust them: the Ottoman *kul* (kin-severed, uncapped advancement, summary dismissal with no appeal), Roman equestrians (no ancestral clientele, so emperors staffed the sensitive prefectures with them), Venice's cittadini *Cancellier Grande* (barred from the noble ladder yet drafting the Directives Senators merely stamp), and the Han *cha ju* merit lane. The trade is explicit and symmetrical: kin-severed power_base buys velocity at the cost of a higher demotion ceiling (total clawback), while kinship power_base buys survivability (containment, not confiscation).

**The formal ladder is shadowed, and the shadow outranks it.** Roman imperial freedmen (Narcissus, Pallas) out-influenced senators; the Florentine Medici *princeps* sat organizationally above the Priorate with no formal office at all; conquest-inflated Imperial princes wielded informal weight untethered from their Golden Bull rank. Standing must therefore model de-facto authority as distinct from nominal rank — a patron-linkage multiplier on *effective* rank, resolved as emergent Key-arithmetic rather than a scripted exception.

**Demotion is graded and typed, and it cascades.** Beyond the magnitude scale, a high-rank fall has a blast-radius: Roman proscription and the purge of Sejanus's clients ran the cascade *downward* through a patron's network; Henry the Lion's and the Palatinate's confiscations forced empire-scale reaggregation (the Palatinate's 1623 resolution *added a new Electoral seat* rather than restoring the old one); Han *zu*-extermination propagated a lesser penalty down the entire kinship-Mentor subtree. Rank literally *is* secession blast-radius — a rank-7 Japanese *tozama* defection threatens a whole allied bloc where a *hatamoto*'s is self-contained.

**Deposition needs two signals.** The hardest removals fire only when an interpretive verdict and a material follow-through collide: Louis the Pious's 833 deposition required both his sons' military coalition *and* episcopal blessing — and stayed *revocable* (his 834 restoration) when one signal withdrew; the Imperial ban sat inert "on paper" until princes actually withdrew fealty and fielded troops. This is the Consensus-resolver holdout-cascade applied to the demotion side.

**What varies.** Kinship sits at opposite poles: Han *yin* and consort-clans *mint* top-N seats via a single marriage, while the Ottoman *kul* system exists to *deny* kinship any purchase at all. Ceilings vary from Venice's hard per-order legal glass ceilings (nobili/cittadini/popolani) to the Ottoman *kul*'s uncapped climb. Top-rung stability ranges from the Genoese doge as an inherently unstable hazard-post (demotion trigger = "insufficient private armed force *right now*") to the Venetian Doge's stable ceremonial apex to Japan's "too big to execute" rank-7 exile clause. And ideological power_base reach varies uniquely: papal/Florentine excommunication crosses *into any other faction's ladder*, including a Monarch — a cross-scale reach military and bureaucratic power_bases never get.

## Ottoman Empire — Hierarchy, Advancement & Demotion

### The kul/devshirme meritocratic-bureaucratic track (routes UP)
The **devshirme** ("collection," systematized under Murad I/Mehmed II, peak 15th-17th c.) levied Christian boys (chiefly Balkan, ages ~8-18) periodically from subject populations, converted them to Islam, and funneled them into two competing tracks:
- **Palace track (Enderun):** the ablest boys entered the Palace School at Topkapı — ranks *iç oğlanı* (page) in successive chambers (küçük oda -> büyük oda -> seferli -> hazine -> kilar) culminating in the *Has Oda* (Privy Chamber), whose senior page (*silahdar*, sword-bearer) or *has odabaşı* stood a genuine path to **Grand Vizier**. This was a full cursus honorum based on demonstrated aptitude, sultan's personal observation, and eunuch/ağa patronage — advancement by merit-under-patronage, not birth.
- **Military track:** the majority became **Janissaries** (Yeni Çeri, "new troops," est. c. 1363-1380s) via the *acemi oğlanı* (cadet) corps, rising through *yayabaşı* (company commander) to *kul kethüdası* (deputy commander) to **Ağa of the Janissaries** (corps commander, a Divan-adjacent office).
Crucially, the **kul** ("slave") had no legal kin, no inheritable estate claim on the state, and no legal protection against summary execution — which is precisely what made the sultan trust him with supreme office: a Grand Vizier owed everything to the sultan's favor and nothing to a rival lineage.
=> Valoria design hook: template for MERITOCRATIC_BUREAUCRATIC governance_mode's Standing ladder — ascendancy power_base = "bureaucratic"/"military" explicitly *because* it is severed from kinship power_base; write a rank property "kin-severed" that trades higher demotion-magnitude ceiling (up to summary Dismissal, no appeal) for faster/uncapped advancement velocity, mirroring kul status.

### The ulema cursus (parallel meritocratic ladder, examination + patronage)
Softa/danişmend (medrese student) -> müderris, ranked by the *pâye* system of medrese prestige-grades -> kadı, with courts themselves ranked (provincial kadı -> "mahreç" molla -> Bursa/Edirne/Istanbul molla, the most prestigious seats) -> **kadıasker** (chief military judge, one each for Rumelia and Anatolia, Divan seats) -> **Şeyhülislam**. Advancement combined credentialing (icazet, teaching license) with patronage networks among senior ulema households (by the 16th-17th c. certain scholarly families, e.g. descendants of Kemalpaşazade, monopolized top pâye).
=> Valoria design hook: a second Standing ladder archetype where Initiation-Gate = credential exam and Mentor = a named senior office-holder who must sponsor advancement — models ideological power_base explicitly as *credential + sponsorship*, distinct from the kul ladder's *merit + master's favor* despite superficial similarity.

### The timar system (land-grant bond route)
A **timar** was a non-hereditary grant of state tax-revenue rights over land, awarded to a **sipahi** (cavalryman) in exchange for military service and for equipping a quota of armed retainers (**cebelu**) proportional to the timar's assessed revenue. Grants scaled: **timar** (small, <20,000 akçe/year) -> **zeamet** (medium, 20,000-100,000) -> **hass** (large estates reserved for viziers, beylerbeyis, and the sultan himself, 100,000+). Advancement was performance-linked: successful campaign service (documented in *icmal* registers) could upgrade a timar to zeamet; failure to muster for campaign, absenteeism, or death without a qualifying heir caused **reversion to the state** (the grant was never freehold — the crown could reassign it). This is a land-grant-bond route up (and down) distinct from both purchase and inheritance.
=> Valoria design hook: direct precedent for a "land-grant bond" Ascendancy power_base variant on the Standing ladder — Obligations field = mandatory military muster quota; Demotion-Trigger = failed muster, checked automatically each campaign cycle rather than needing an adjudicated failure event; models LANDHOLDER_FRANCHISE governance_mode's franchise as conditional, not a fixed property right.

### Standing currency: sultan's favor (itimad) and proximity, not birth
Because the top of both the kul and timar ladders was explicitly non-hereditary (a vizier's son had no automatic claim on his father's office; a sipahi's timar defaulted to the state absent a qualifying heir), the operative "standing currency" empire-wide was **demonstrated favor and proximity to the throne (itimad/intisab)** — evidenced by campaign success, tax-yield delivered, or palace patronage secured — rather than lineage. This is why Ottoman elite turnover could be startlingly fast: a Grand Vizier could be raised from page to supreme office and destroyed within a single reign.
=> Valoria design hook: exemplar for the resolution-quality -> standing bridge — model itimad as the accumulated Key-gap-delta across a seat-holder's tenure (delivered vs demanded, campaign or tax cycle by cycle) rather than a static trait, so a string of narrow misses erodes Standing exactly as documented Ottoman vizier turnover did.

### Routes down: dismissal, exile, execution, confiscation (müsadere)
A dismissed Grand Vizier or pasha faced, in roughly ascending severity: **azl** (dismissal from office, could be reappointed later — several Grand Viziers cycled through the seat multiple times), **sürgün** (exile to a remote provincial post or estate), **müsadere** (confiscation of all property back to the state — a direct corollary of kul status: nothing was truly "owned"), and **execution**, typically by strangulation with a silk bowstring (avoiding shedding royal-service blood), administered by the palace's mute executioners under the **bostancıbaşı** (head gardener, who doubled as chief of palace security/executions). Grand Vizier **İbrahim Pasha** (Süleyman I's favorite, executed 1536) and **Kara Mustafa Pasha** (executed 1683 after the failed Siege of Vienna) exemplify the "highest office, fastest possible fall" pattern.
=> Valoria design hook: model Dismissal-to-"Dishonored"(-1) with an attached asset-clawback flag (müsadere) as the maximum-severity Demotion-Trigger outcome — distinct from ordinary 1-3 rank demotion — reserved for cases where the seat-holder's power_base was purely favor-derived (kul/bureaucratic/military) rather than kinship, matching why Ottoman viziers faced total ruin while dynastic princes (below) faced containment instead of confiscation.

### Royal succession: fratricide and the Kafes as inheritance-route control
Princes (şehzade) were traditionally sent to provincial sanjak governorships in their teens as a training/legitimation route (each accompanied by mother and household) — succession was **not fixed primogeniture** but an open contest among all surviving sons, decided by whoever secured the capital, treasury, and Janissary/palace backing fastest after a sultan's death. Mehmed II's 1478 kanunname explicitly sanctioned **fratricide** ("whichever of my sons inherits the sultanate, it is proper for him to kill his brothers for the order of the world") to prevent civil war — Mehmed III enforced it maximally, executing 19 brothers in 1595. From c. 1603 (Ahmed I, succession of Mustafa I in 1617 being the first beneficiary), the practice shifted to the **Kafes** ("Cage") — confining junior princes for life within a secluded palace apartment instead of killing them, ending sanjak governorships as princely training and producing sultans enthroned with zero administrative or military experience.
=> Valoria design hook: models "route down" for a Monarch chain-bypass authority's own kin-competitors as a design *trade-off*, not a fixed civ trait — fratricide = fast succession, minimal contested-legitimacy period, moral/PS cost; Kafes = safer succession, but a Legitimacy/competence penalty baked into the next seat-holder that should be modeled as a persistent debuff, not flavor text.

## 2. Senatorial vs. equestrian orders: two parallel Ascendancy power_bases

- **Senatorial order** (*ordo senatorius*): census minimum 1,000,000 sesterces (raised by Augustus from the Republican-era ~800,000-1,000,000 range), the **broad purple stripe** (*latus clavus*) on the toga, hereditary within the family for descendants, barred by Augustan law from marrying freedwomen and from directly engaging in large-scale commerce/shipping (the *lex Claudia*, 218 BC) — an order defined by legal exclusion from trade, forcing land-based and political income. => Valoria design hook: models an **Ascendancy power_base explicitly incompatible with a "purchased" or "mercantile" power_base** — a rank whose Initiation-Gate legally *forecloses* certain income levers, so climbing this ladder trades away tools available to a lower rank; encode as a rank-level flag disabling specific power_base actions, not just enabling new ones.
- **Equestrian order** (*ordo equester*): census minimum 400,000 sesterces, the **narrow purple stripe** (*angusticlavia*), the *equus publicus* — literally a public warhorse and annual cavalry review (*transvectio equitum*) that doubled as a public standing-audit ("the equestrian parade" let censors publicly strip a horse from a man found wanting). Equestrians staffed the Empire's actual administrative machine under the Principate: **procurators** (financial/provincial administrators), **prefects** (Praetorian Prefect, Prefect of Egypt, Prefect of the Grain Supply/*annona*, Prefect of the Watch/*vigiles*) — a genuine **MERITOCRATIC_BUREAUCRATIC career track parallel to, and eventually rivaling, the senatorial cursus**, because emperors increasingly preferred equestrians (no independent power base, no ancestral clientele threatening the throne) for the most sensitive posts. => Valoria design hook: the *transvectio equitum* is a ready-made **periodic public Standing-audit event** — a scheduled ceremony where a rank's continued tenure is re-certified in front of the polity rather than resolved silently by backend Key arithmetic; and the equestrian track overall is the strongest real precedent for a **second, parallel 0–7 ladder within the same faction with a different power_base (bureaucratic/military) that the top rank of the "safer" ladder can be promoted to bypass, rather than climb, the "riskier" senatorial ladder** — i.e., cross-ladder Ascendancy transfer as a design pattern, not just single-ladder promotion.

## 3. Patronage (patronus/cliens): the informal currency underlying the formal ladder

Nearly every advancement in Roman public life ran through **patrocinium**: a *patronus* (senior, wealthy, well-connected) held a train of *clientes* (dependents — freedmen, poorer citizens, whole client-towns, even client kings and foreign communities) bound by *fides* (good faith) rather than law. Clients performed the **salutatio** (dawn levy at the patron's house), canvassed votes, provided a public escort (*deductio*), and in return received legal defense, loans, food doles (*sportulae*), and — critically — **commendatio**: a patron's letter of recommendation was often the deciding factor in military postings, provincial appointments, and marriages. Cicero's letters (*Ad Familiares*) are full of *commendationes* functioning as the real currency of the cursus honorum, running parallel to (and often determining) formal election results. Freedmen (*liberti*) remained clients of their former master for life by law, and powerful freedmen of the emperor's own household (e.g., **Narcissus** and **Pallas** under Claudius, 41–54 AD) could out-rank senators in actual influence despite formally being ex-slaves — a total inversion of the formal Standing ladder by informal-network Standing. => Valoria design hook: patronage is the strongest real precedent for making **"patronage" one of the Ascendancy power_bases carry its own shadow-ladder that can outrank the formal one** — a low-formal-rank actor (freedman equivalent) with an extremely strong patron-tie should be able to exercise de facto authority above their nominal Standing rank, resolved as an emergent Key-arithmetic effect (their patron's resolution-quality gap feeds their own effective authority) rather than a hard-coded exception — this is exactly the "never special-case an entity" guardrail: model it as a *general* patron-linkage multiplier on effective rank, not a scripted "Pallas is special" flag.
- **Patronage collapse as demotion mechanism**: losing your patron (his death, disgrace, or a faction change) instantly voided your access and left you exposed to prosecution by enemies — Cicero's own exile (58 BC) followed the loss of key patronage cover; freedmen of a *damnatio memoriae*'d emperor (e.g., Sejanus's clients after his fall, 31 AD) were purged en masse. => Valoria design hook: models **cascading demotion through a patron-client edge in the standing graph** — when a rank-7 seat is Dismissed/Demoted, every actor whose Ascendancy power_base is "patronage" tied to that seat should automatically face a Demotion Trigger check, scaled by tie-strength — the "rank = secession blast-radius" principle running in reverse (a patron's *fall* has a blast radius through their client network, not just a defector's rise).

## 4. Routes UP — the concrete levers

- **Election** (comitia centuriata/tributa/concilium plebis) — the *formal* route, gameable by bribery (*ambitus*, criminalized repeatedly and ineffectively from 358 BC onward) and by patronage-driven bloc voting. => Valoria design hook: DELIBERATIVE_ASSEMBLY promotion should be corruptible by an underlying patronage power_base without changing the formal vote-count UI — the fraud is in whose votes are pre-committed, not in the tally mechanism.
- **Patronage/commendatio** — see above.
- **Military merit / adlection**: emperors could *adlect* (directly appoint, bypassing election and even the quaestorship) a favored equestrian or provincial notable straight into the Senate at praetorian or even consular rank (*adlectio inter praetorios*, *inter consulares*) — Vespasian and the Flavians used this heavily (70s AD) to rebuild a Senate gutted by civil war and to reward provincial loyalists (bringing in Gauls, Spaniards). => Valoria design hook: a direct precedent for a **Monarch chain-bypass authority injecting a seat directly into a mid-rank of the Standing ladder**, skipping the normal Initiation-Gate sequence entirely — should be modeled as an explicit bypass action with its own L/PS cost (adlection was resented by old-family senators) rather than a cheat code with no consequence.
- **Purchase**: overtly literal in 193 AD, when the Praetorian Guard auctioned the entire principate to **Didius Julianus** (see conflicts_md) — and endemically in the Dominate, where *suffragium* (paying for office) became normalized enough that late-Roman law repeatedly (and ineffectually) banned it. => Valoria design hook: "purchased" as an Ascendancy power_base should carry a built-in fragility — a purchased seat should have a materially *weaker* base L/PS floor than a merit- or kinship-acquired seat at the same rank, because it was won without demonstrating the resolution-quality the ladder nominally certifies.
- **Land-grant bond**: veteran legionaries received land allotments (*coloniae*) on discharge, tying a huge pool of ex-soldiers' loyalty and livelihood directly to whichever general/emperor had secured their settlement — Sulla's, Caesar's, and especially Augustus's veteran colonization programs functioned as a mass loyalty-purchase mechanism binding tens of thousands of family lines to a single patron's continued political survival. => Valoria design hook: exact fit for the spec's named "land-grant bond" advancement route — model veteran-settlement as a bulk, low-per-seat-but-large-population Ascendancy grant that raises L/PS for the granting authority specifically among the settled population's descendants for multiple in-game generations, decaying slowly rather than a one-shot bonus.

## 5. Routes DOWN — the concrete levers

- **Censorial *nota*/expulsion from the Senate** — extra-judicial, discretionary, done by two men (see modes_md §1).
- **Prosecution under *lex repetundae*** (extortion courts, established 149 BC, *quaestio de repetundis*) — provincial governors returning from post were routinely prosecuted by political enemies for provincial misgovernment; conviction meant exile and property confiscation. Cicero's prosecution of **Verres** (70 BC, governor of Sicily) is the canonical case, explicitly built on documented extraction vs. entitlement gap (Cicero's *Verrines* are essentially an itemized ledger of demanded vs. delivered tax/tribute). => Valoria design hook: this is close to a literal historical instance of the **resolution-quality → standing bridge**: the Directive was "govern the province," the Need was "don't over-extract," and the arithmetic gap between delivered and demanded tribute is exactly what a returning-governor's Key should encode, feeding a public Standing.
- **Exile** (*relegatio* = restricted banishment, retained citizenship; *deportatio* = harsher, loss of citizenship and property) — used against senators, and infamously against Augustus's own daughter **Julia** (2 BC) and granddaughter **Julia the Younger**, and the poet Ovid (8 AD) — showing exile applied even to the innermost regime circle, not just rivals. => Valoria design hook: exile should be modeled as a **Demotion-to-(-1) "Dishonored" state that is spatially enforced** (banishment from a Territory/Settlement) rather than merely a Standing-number drop — i.e., it should also revoke the actor's *presence* token, distinct from revoking their *rank*.
- **Proscription**: Sulla's proscription lists (82 BC) and the Second Triumvirate's (43 BC, killing Cicero among ~300 senators and 2,000 equestrians) were published death-and-confiscation lists — a formalized, published, one-shot mass-demotion-to-death mechanism used explicitly to solve a succession/civil-war crisis by eliminating an entire tier of rivals at once, with property redistributed to the proscribers' own supporters. => Valoria design hook: models an extreme **Dismissal event at civilization-scale** — a Monarch/Parliament chain-bypass authority issuing a bulk, published, faction-wide Demotion-to-Dishonored order against named rank-holders, with the *seized Standing/assets* explicitly reassigned to the issuing authority's own clients — this should be a distinct, rare, high-L/PS-cost action type, not an extension of ordinary single-target demotion.
- **Execution / forced suicide**: the "honorable" route for the elite (Seneca, 65 AD, ordered by Nero after the Pisonian conspiracy) preserved family property from confiscation and was itself a negotiated Standing-preserving demotion-to-death. => Valoria design hook: models a **negotiated Dismissal variant** — offer the target a choice between full Demotion-magnitude-3 (public trial, family Standing zeroed, assets seized) and a "voluntary" exit that caps the Demotion Magnitude and shields dependents' Standing, a resolver-level branch on the Demotion Trigger.
- **Damnatio memoriae**: posthumous erasure of name from inscriptions/coinage/records (Domitian 96 AD, Commodus 192 AD — later reversed — Geta 211 AD), functioning as a retroactive Standing purge applied to the historical record itself, not just the living rank. => Valoria design hook: a **retroactive ledger-rewrite demotion** — model as a special action that doesn't just set current Standing to Dishonored but also flags the actor's *historical* Key-log entries as void for future resolution-quality calculations referencing them (e.g., their past "wins" no longer count toward a faction's institutional memory/legitimacy).
- **Hostage-kin leverage**: client kings' heirs were routinely raised at Rome as hostages/wards (e.g., various Herodian and Parthian princes) — guaranteeing the client ruler's compliance via a family member's Standing being entirely at Rome's disposal. => Valoria design hook: direct fit for the spec's named "hostage-kin leverage" demotion/compliance route — model as a persistent Key held by the suzerain authority that, if triggered, applies a Demotion Trigger to a *different* actor (the hostage's parent-ruler) than the one physically held, i.e., a leverage edge distinct from the direct patron-client cascade in §3.

## 2. Political Hierarchy, Standing, Advancement & Demotion

### 2.1 The rank ladder (offices, roughly descending)
1. **Rex Francorum / Imperator** (King of the Franks / Emperor, from 800) — apex, sole legitimate source of honores.
2. **Sub-kings** (*reges*) — sons or designated co-rulers granted subordinate kingdoms while father lived (e.g., Pippin of Italy and Louis of Aquitaine crowned sub-kings by Charlemagne in 781; Lothar I made co-emperor 817). A distinctively Carolingian rank: royal in title, still subordinate in the *Ordinatio Imperii* framework.
3. **Duces (Dukes)** — regional military commanders, often over frontier or ethnically distinct blocs (Duke of Bavaria, Duke of Spoleto, Duke of Benevento — the last two functionally independent by the 9th century). Ducal rank could be suppressed by kings wary of overmighty regional commanders (Charlemagne abolished the Bavarian ducal line in 788, absorbing it into direct county administration — see §3).
4. **Praefecti / Marchiones (Margraves, Marcher-counts / "Prefects of the March")** — counts (or counts-of-counts) governing militarized frontier zones (marches) with enhanced military authority and multiple subordinate counties: the Spanish March (est. after 795, capital Barcelona), the Breton March (Roland's post per the *Vita Karoli* and later chanson tradition — historically the "prefect of the Breton march" killed at Roncevaux 778), the Avar/Pannonian March, the Saxon marches, the Danish march (Limes Saxoniae). Margraves ranked above ordinary counts by virtue of standing military command over multiple counties, not by a separate title tier per se.
5. **Comites (Counts)** — the backbone administrative rank: each *comitatus* (county) had a count responsible for justice (presiding over the *mallus*/county court with *scabini*, professional lay judges introduced by Charlemagne to replace ad hoc *rachimburgii*), tax/tribute collection, military levy (the *heriban*, ost-summons), and road/bridge/fortification maintenance (*Pflichten*). Counts were royal appointees, not local hereditary lords, in the 8th–early 9th century model.
6. **Vassi dominici ("the king's own vassals")** — a specific elevated category of royal vassal, often landless-at-recruitment younger aristocratic sons personally commended to the king, deployed on missions, garrisons, or as a mobile strike/administrative reserve; a recruiting ground for future counts.
7. **Vicarii / Centenarii** — sub-county officials (hundred-men) administering a subdivision (*centena*) of a county, handling local justice/levy under the count.
8. **Ordinary vassals / milites** — the mounted-retainer class bound by commendation and benefice, the base of the military-service pyramid.
9. **Bishops and Abbots** — a PARALLEL rank track (see modes_md §1.7), roughly equivalent in real power to counts/dukes (holding immunities, sometimes counties themselves as "abbot-counts" — e.g., lay abbacies given to secular magnates as a reward, notoriously Charles the Bald's grants of Saint-Denis and other abbacies to laymen), appointed by royal nomination then (nominally) canonical election/consecration.

### 2.2 Advancement routes (concrete mechanisms)
- **Royal appointment/patronage** — the default route to comital and ducal rank; counts served at the king's pleasure, not by local election, and were routinely rotated or transferred between counties precisely to prevent entrenchment (a practice that visibly weakens after ca. 850 as regional families dig in).
=> Valoria design hook: canonical basis for **ROYAL_COURT_APPOINTMENT governance_mode** with **patronage power_base** on the Standing ladder — Ascendancy via direct Crown grant, Mentor field = the king or a duke acting as the king's proxy.
- **Vassalage / commendation + benefice grant** — a free man commends himself (*commendatio*, hands-in-hands ritual plus oath) to a lord in exchange for a *beneficium* (later *feudum*) — usufruct of land, without ownership transfer — establishing a personal bond renewable/revocable on the death of either party (theoretically; practice drifted toward heritability). This is the master mechanism binding the military aristocracy to office-holders at every level.
=> Valoria design hook: model **honores/benefices as the literal in-game distinction between Title (the office, a Rank position) and Grant (the attached land-usufruct Key)** — losing the office (demotion) should NOT automatically claw back the benefice unless explicitly adjudicated, mirroring the historical tension that eventually let benefices outlive their granting office and calcify into inheritance (§2.4).
- **Military merit / campaign service** — conquest campaigns (Saxon wars 772–804, Lombard conquest 773–774, Avar campaigns 791–803) generated windfall honores and benefices for successful commanders; the Avar treasure distribution (796) is the paradigmatic reward-for-service moment recorded in the ARF.
=> Valoria design hook: military power_base Ascendancy route on the Standing ladder — successful campaign resolution should emit a Key whose Directive/Need gap (looted resources delivered vs. promised) directly funds Rank advancement for the field commander, not just the Crown.
- **Marriage alliance** — marrying into a comital or ducal house (or the king marrying a daughter to a loyal magnate) as an advancement/stabilization tool; conversely, REPUDIATION of a wife (Lothar II's attempted divorce of Theutberga in favor of Waldrada, 857–869, the "Lotharingian divorce case") could become a full-blown succession/legitimacy crisis adjudicated by the papacy itself (Pope Nicholas I's intervention, Council of Aachen 862, ultimately overturned by Rome in 863–866) — showing marriage as a governance-legitimacy lever with international arbitration risk.
=> Valoria design hook: marriage-alliance Ascendancy route (kinship power_base) with an explicit failure mode — a repudiation/annulment attempt should be modeled as a Key that can escalate past the normal chain to an external chain-bypass authority (here, the Papacy — map to Parliament-equivalent) for binding adjudication.
- **Church career (oblation → ordination → episcopal appointment)** — child oblation into monasteries (common for younger sons), rising through the cursus of deacon/priest/abbot/bishop, with the terminal appointment (bishopric) typically a royal nomination ratified by (nominally free, practically controlled) local clergy-and-people election — a MERITOCRATIC_BUREAUCRATIC track running parallel to and sometimes overlapping the lay Standing ladder (bishop-counts).
=> Valoria design hook: a second Ascendancy Initiation-Gate type (ideological/bureaucratic power_base) whose Mentor field is an ecclesiastical superior rather than a lay patron — lets a Standing-ladder seat be reached via a track immune to kinship/military levers, useful for a faction wanting a "clean" high-Rank appointee.
- **Inheritance / heredity (increasingly the dominant route after ca. 850–877)** — the **Capitulary of Quierzy (877)**, issued by Charles the Bald departing for Italy, provided that if a count died on campaign his son (or a missus, pending the king's decision) should provisionally administer the county, and more broadly acknowledged existing practice of sons succeeding fathers in office; combined with benefices becoming de facto hereditary, this is the mechanism by which the appointive comital system calcified into the hereditary territorial principalities of the 10th century (proto-feudalism).
=> Valoria design hook: model this as a **Standing-ladder power_base migration event** — a scripted/emergent transition where an Ascendancy route flips from patronage to kinship for a given Rank-slot once a threshold number of consecutive same-family successions occurs, with a capitulary-like Key formally ratifying (rather than causing) the fait accompli — i.e., legitimation lags practice, a useful pacing lever for faction entrenchment.

### 2.3 The "aristocracy of service" and the standing currency
Carolingian elite status (per Karl Ferdinand Werner's and Timothy Reuter's analyses) rested on a fusion the sources call *nobilitas*: birth (a recognized noble *genus*/kindred), PLUS royal favor/office-holding, PLUS the visible wealth and following (retinue, *fideles*) that both produced and reflected the first two. There was no separate examination-based cursus (contrast Roman or Chinese bureaucratic models); the "currency" of standing was a composite of **(a) proximity to the palace/king (attendance at placita, palace office tenure), (b) count/margrave/duke office itself, (c) accumulated benefices and immunities, and (d) network — the size and loyalty of one's own sub-vassals.** Losing (a) — being excluded from court, sent to a distant command, or simply not renewed as missus — was itself a demotion signal even absent formal removal.
=> Valoria design hook: implement "distance from the palace" (or from a Duchy capital) as a passive Standing modifier — a seat-holder physically stationed far from the chain-bypass Monarch authority should suffer slow Legitimacy decay absent active maintenance (embassies, gifts, placitum attendance), giving mechanical teeth to the historical pattern of "recall to court" as both reward and leash.

### 2.4 Demotion / removal routes
- **Deposition + forced tonsure (monastic confinement)** — the standard "soft" removal for defeated royal rivals: Carloman I's family after his 771 death was NOT harmed but marginalized; more violently, **Tassilo III, Duke of Bavaria**, was tried at the Assembly of Ingelheim (788), convicted of treason (having allied with the Avars and reneged on fidelity oaths sworn in 757/781/787), sentenced to death, then "mercifully" commuted by Charlemagne to forced tonsure and monastic confinement at Jumièges — his son (a HOSTAGE already held at court, per standard practice) was tonsured too, extinguishing the Bavarian ducal line and letting Charlemagne absorb Bavaria directly into the county system.
=> Valoria design hook: canonical model for **Demotion Magnitude "Dismissal to -1 Dishonored"** — full Rank stripping plus forced removal from the Standing ladder entirely (not just a rank drop), and the **hostage-kin leverage mechanic**: a faction should be able to hold a rival's heir at the capital as a standing deterrent Key that, if triggered, forcibly demotes/removes the heir alongside the parent.
- **Exile** — lesser offenders sent from court/province rather than killed; a common outcome for defeated rebel counts.
- **Blinding** — the extreme physical demotion for royal/near-royal rivals where killing was politically costly: **Bernard, King of Italy** (Charlemagne's grandson via Pippin of Italy), revolted against his uncle Louis the Pious in 817 upon learning the *Ordinatio Imperii* subordinated his kingdom to Lothar; captured, condemned at an assembly, blinded on Louis's order (a mitigation of a death sentence) — he died of the wound days later (818), an act Louis publicly did penance for at the Assembly of Attigny (822).
=> Valoria design hook: model blinding/maiming as a distinct, IRREVERSIBLE Demotion outcome tier between exile and execution — mechanically: permanent removal of a Rank's eligibility to ever re-Ascend (unlike ordinary Demotion, which permits later re-Ascendancy), reserved for royal-blood rivals where outright execution would itself trigger a Legitimacy penalty.
- **Public penance / ritual humiliation as political deposition-without-killing** — **Louis the Pious's deposition at the Field of Lies (Rotfeld, near Colmar, June 833)** by his sons Lothar, Pippin of Aquitaine, and Louis the German (backed by Pope Gregory IV in person, an unprecedented papal intervention in a purely Frankish succession dispute), followed by formal public penance at the **Assembly/Synod of Compiègne–Soissons (October 833)** — Louis confessed capital sins in a penitential rite modeled on ecclesiastical discipline, was required to lay down his weapons-belt (the symbol of royal/military authority) and don penitential garb, which the assembled bishops and magnates took as tantamount to abdication. He was restored within a year (Assembly of Thionville, February–March 834) once the coalition against him fractured — showing deposition-by-ritual as REVERSIBLE if the political coalition sustaining it collapsed.
=> Valoria design hook: a **Consensus resolver "holdout-cascade" case study** — model this as a scripted collision-of-stresses event needing two independently-arriving signals (material: sons' military coalition seizing the king's camp; interpretive: papal/episcopal willingness to bless a penitential-deposition rite) to resonate into an actual Rank-0 removal — and, critically, make the removal REVOCABLE if either signal later withdraws (Louis's 834 restoration), unlike the Bernard/Tassilo cases above.
- **Excommunication / synodal degradation** — reserved for ecclesiastical office-holders and for lay rulers where the Church wished to apply pressure without a lay assembly's blunter tools (e.g., papal pressure on Lothar II over the divorce case, §2.2, which functioned as a slow-motion legitimacy demotion without ever removing him from his throne outright).
=> Valoria design hook: excommunication maps to an **ideological-power_base-specific Demotion-Trigger** that degrades Legitimacy/Popular-Support directly rather than Rank — a lever usable by the ecclesiastical faction-type (§1.7) independent of the lay chain of command.
- **Recall by missi / loss of office for malfeasance** — missi were empowered to report (and, per several capitularies, directly suspend) counts found guilty of extortion, denial of justice, or embezzlement of the *fredus* (judicial fine share); the Admonitio Generalis and later capitularies repeatedly legislate against corrupt counts, implying (and periodically confirming) real removals.
=> Valoria design hook: the direct mechanical realization of the **resolution-quality → standing bridge**: a count's Key-response to a missi audit event, scored on Directive/Need delivery gap, should demote (Rank -1 default) or, for severe/repeated gaps, Dismiss (-1 Dishonored) without requiring a separate revolt event.

## The rank ladder, top to bottom

1. **Emperor (Kaiser)** — elected by the Kurfürsten, ratified historically by papal coronation (Charlemagne 800, last papal coronation Charles V at Bologna 1530) or, from Maximilian I (1508) onward, self-assumed as "Elected Roman Emperor." Held supreme but heavily circumscribed feudal suzerainty; after the **1356 Golden Bull** and especially after the **1495–1500 Reichsreform**, constitutionally bound to rule *with* the Reichstag on matters of taxation, war, and law (the *Reichsgrundgesetze*, capstoned by the 1648 Peace of Westphalia's confirmation of territorial sovereignty).
2. **King of the Romans (Rex Romanorum / König)** — the elected-but-not-yet-imperially-crowned title; also used for an heir elected in the reigning emperor's lifetime (*Wahl zu Lebzeiten*, e.g., Maximilian I's son Philip was never so elected, but Ferdinand I was crowned King of the Romans in 1531 under Charles V) to smooth succession and bypass a fresh contested election.
3. **Prince-Electors (Kurfürsten)** — the fixed seven (later eight, then nine) named in the Golden Bull: Archbishop of Mainz (Erzkanzler/Arch-Chancellor of Germany, presiding officer), Archbishop of Trier (Arch-Chancellor of Burgundy/Gaul), Archbishop of Cologne (Arch-Chancellor of Italy), King of Bohemia (Arch-Cupbearer), Count Palatine of the Rhine (Arch-Steward/Seneschal), Duke of Saxony (Arch-Marshal), Margrave of Brandenburg (Arch-Chamberlain). Each elector's title bundled a ceremonial imperial-household office (the "Arch-" offices) with real regalian sovereignty (mining rights, coinage, high justice, indivisible territory) in their own lands — rank and function were fused, not separated.
4. **Imperial Princes (Reichsfürsten)** — dukes, landgraves, margraves (non-electoral), counts palatine, and the great prince-bishops and prince-abbots holding imperial immediacy; seated individually or, for lesser counts, in curiate voting blocs (Wetterau, Swabian, Franconian, Westphalian counts' colleges, from the 1653/54 Diet) in the Reichsfürstenrat.
5. **Imperial Counts (Reichsgrafen) and Imperial Knights (Reichsritter)** — immediate nobility below princely rank, organized (from the 15th–16th century) into the **Reichsritterschaft**, a self-governing corporate body of knights answerable only to the Emperor, holding no seat in the Reichstag but retaining direct imperial tax/military obligation and jurisdiction independent of any territorial prince.
6. **Free/Imperial Cities (Reichsstädte)** — self-governing urban communes holding imperial immediacy directly, winning a full, binding vote as the third Reichstag college only in **1489**; internally governed by patrician councils, guild councils, or mixed constitutions that varied city to city (Nuremberg's closed patriciate vs. Cologne's guild-based council are opposite ends of the spectrum).
7. **Territorial nobility and mediatized towns** — nobles, knights, and towns holding land/office *through* a prince or bishop rather than directly from the Emperor; legally and politically subordinate to their immediate lord for most purposes, with imperial law reaching them only at the margins (appeal to Reichskammergericht in specific cases).
8. **Ministeriales (historically, 11th–13th c.) and unfree/serf population** — the base of the pyramid; ministeriales specifically occupy a strange dual position, *legally unfree* yet *functionally administrative/military elite*, holding castellanies and offices without independent noble standing until their gradual 12th–13th century absorption into the free lower nobility.

=> Valoria design hook: **Standing ladder (8-position, 0–7)** — this seven/eight-tier real hierarchy maps almost one-to-one onto the ranked ladder's 0–7 slots, but the Golden Bull's fusion of ceremonial household office + territorial regalian sovereignty at rank 3 (Electors) is the key texture to preserve: don't model "Title" and "Access" as separable fields that could diverge, model the Kurfürst seat as a case where the Initiation-Gate (election to the office) *simultaneously* grants both symbolic Access (Arch-Chancellor precedence at court) and material Access (regalian rights) in a single non-decomposable package — useful wherever Valoria wants a rank to feel like a genuine institution rather than a scoreboard position.

## Routes up

- **Election** — the paradigmatic route to Emperor/King of the Romans itself, and (separately) the route into a prince-bishopric via **cathedral chapter election** (Domkapitel), a genuinely competitive, patronage-and-faction-riddled internal election among canons, frequently contested and appealed to Rome — e.g., the disputed 1583 Cologne archiepiscopal election (the "Cologne War," Gebhard Truchsess von Waldburg's conversion to Protestantism and deposition) shows the same double-election failure mode as imperial elections, one tier down.
  => Valoria design hook: **Consensus resolver (assent/stand-aside/holdout)** — chapter elections are a smaller-scale, higher-frequency test bed for the assent/stand-aside/holdout mechanic: canons could assent, abstain (stand-aside), or actively hold out for a rival candidate, and a holdout bloc large enough could force an appeal to an external authority (Rome) exactly as a Valoria holdout should escalate to the Monarch/Parliament chain-bypass.
- **Inheritance/primogeniture** — locked in for the *secular* electorates by the Golden Bull's indivisibility clause (preventing a Duke of Saxony's electorate from fragmenting across sons), but *not* available for the ecclesiastical electorates (archbishoprics) or for the imperial title itself, which remained elective in principle even when a single house monopolized it for centuries.
  => Valoria design hook: models a **rank-specific advancement-route flag** — some Standing-ladder seats should be inheritance-locked (indivisible) while adjacent seats in the same ladder are election-only, producing structurally different succession-crisis risk profiles side by side in the same faction, exactly as the Empire had electorally rigid Bohemia/Palatinate/Saxony/Brandenburg seats sitting beside perpetually-elective archiepiscopal seats.
- **Military merit and conquest** — territorial princes expanding their base (the Habsburgs' Burgundian and Spanish/Hungarian marriage-and-inheritance consolidation being the outlier; more typically, frontier expansion like the Teutonic Order's Prussian conquests, or Brandenburg's Hohenzollern eastward expansion) converted battlefield/administrative success into enlarged regalian territory, which in turn increased an elector's or prince's *effective* Reichstag weight even though the formal vote-count never changed.
  => Valoria design hook: **Ascendancy power_base = military** — territorial growth via conquest should inflate a seat-holder's *informal* leverage (effective weight in negotiated-estates votes, patronage capacity) independent of their *formal* rank position, creating deliberate tension between nominal Standing-ladder rank and actual influence — a lever for emergent factional friction without needing to renumber anyone's rank.
- **Land-grant/enfeoffment bond (Lehen)** — the base mechanism of the entire system: an imperial fief (Reichslehen) granted in exchange for military/administrative service and homage (Huldigung), renewable at investiture, revocable on default (felony/*Felonie*) — this is the literal historical origin of the "land-grant bond" advancement route named in the Valoria mechanics list.
  => Valoria design hook: direct 1:1 source for a **land-grant-bond Ascendancy power_base** — model fief-holding as a renewing bond requiring periodic re-investiture (a recurring Key exchange between lord and vassal) rather than a one-time grant, so that neglecting the renewal ritual itself becomes a Demotion-Trigger independent of any substantive failure.
- **Purchase** — venality was rarer in the Empire than in France, but real: **pawning of imperial regalia/territories for cash** (Sigismund's repeated pledging of Brandenburg and other territories to raise crusading/war funds, ultimately selling Brandenburg's electoral margraviate to the Hohenzollern Frederick I in 1415/1417 for 400,000 Hungarian guilders) is the marquee example — an entire electoral seat changing hands by cash transaction, formalized at the **1417 Council of Constance**.
  => Valoria design hook: **Ascendancy power_base = purchased**, with an explicit historical precedent for the *most senior* rank in the entire ladder (an Elector seat) being acquired this way — useful to justify letting even top-tier Standing-ladder seats carry a "purchased" power_base flag, not just low/mid ranks, provided the fiscal desperation of the seller (a material threshold) is modeled as the precondition.

## Routes down

- **Reichsacht (Imperial Ban/outlawry)** — the formal demotion-to-dismissal mechanism: an offender declared under the Ban lost all imperial protection, fiefs, and legal standing; anyone could seize their lands or person without recourse. Prominent cases: **Henry the Lion, Duke of Saxony and Bavaria, banned 1180** by Frederick Barbarossa's imperial diet at Würzburg after Henry's refusal to support the Italian campaign and his own over-mighty territorial consolidation — his duchies were confiscated and split (Bavaria to the Wittelsbachs, who held it into the 20th century; Saxony fragmented), the single clearest "Dismissal → -1 Dishonored, territory redistributed to rivals" event in Empire history. Also: the **1706 Reichsacht against Bavaria and Cologne** (Wittelsbach electors) during the War of the Spanish Succession for allying with France against the Emperor.
  => Valoria design hook: **Demotion Magnitude "Dismissal to -1 Dishonored"** — Henry the Lion is the canonical severe-demotion case: not a rank-drop but full territorial confiscation-and-redistribution, i.e., the Demotion Trigger (defiance of the liege's mobilization Directive) fires a Magnitude far beyond -1/-2/-3, converting into an actual **recognition-fission/reaggregation** event where the dismissed actor's former territory is redrawn among rival claimants — model the Ban as the mechanism that *triggers* a reaggregation redraw, not merely a personal-standing penalty.
- **Excommunication and deposition attempts** — spiritual demotion with direct temporal consequences: Henry IV excommunicated by Gregory VII (1076, renewed 1080); Frederick II excommunicated twice by Gregory IX (1227, 1239) and formally **deposed by the First Council of Lyon (1245)** under Innocent IV — a deposition Frederick simply refused to recognize and that had no immediate practical effect, showing the limit of purely spiritual demotion absent a material enforcement mechanism (princes willing to act on it).
  => Valoria design hook: models a **demotion that requires two independently-arriving components to actually bite** — the spiritual/interpretive verdict (excommunication/deposition) plus a material follow-through (princes actually withdrawing fealty and fielding troops) — directly reusable as the collision-of-stresses primitive applied specifically to the demotion side of the Standing ladder: a Demotion-Trigger can fire "on paper" (interpretive signal only) and remain inert until a material signal (rival claimant, troop mobilization) arrives to complete it.
- **Loss of electoral title via forfeiture and reassignment** — the **1623 transfer of the Palatinate electorate to Bavaria** (Emperor Ferdinand II stripping Frederick V, the "Winter King," of his electoral dignity after the 1620 Battle of White Mountain and 1623 Diet of Regensburg formally reassigning it to Maximilian I of Bavaria) is the marquee case of an entire named Golden Bull seat being confiscated and reassigned outside the normal succession mechanism — resolved only at Westphalia 1648 by *creating an eighth electorate* for the restored Palatine line rather than reversing the transfer, i.e., the conflict was resolved by seat-inflation, not by demotion-reversal.
  => Valoria design hook: **rank = secession blast-radius**, inverted — because the Palatinate was a top-tier (Elector) seat, its 1623 confiscation had empire-wide, decades-long reverberation (it is a proximate cause/accelerant of the Thirty Years' War's continuation), and its eventual "resolution" via seat-creation rather than reversal is a clean precedent for a **reaggregation mechanic that adds a new seat rather than restoring the old one** — useful when a design wants to resolve a high-rank demotion dispute without simply undoing it, preserving the victor's gain while placating the loser's faction.
- **Recall/impeachment-equivalent by the Diet** — the Empire had no clean parliamentary impeachment procedure, but the **Reichskammergericht's jurisdiction over princes for breach of the Landfrieden or of imperial law**, and the Diet's collective capacity to authorize an **Reichsexekution (imperial execution/enforcement action)** against a recalcitrant estate (used, e.g., against Duke Henry Julius of Braunschweig-Wolfenbüttel 1611–1615, and definitively in the run-up to the 1706 Bavarian/Cologne Ban) functioned as the closest analog to institutional recall.
  => Valoria design hook: **resolution-quality → standing bridge**, demotion side — model Reichsexekution as the emitted Key when a seat-holder's resolution of an internal dispute (Landfrieden violation) produces a gap so large the Diet authorizes collective enforcement — the arithmetic gap itself (delivered peace vs. demanded peace) is what licenses the Standing-ladder Demotion-Trigger to escalate from an individual-actor penalty into a multi-actor collective-enforcement event.
- **Hostage-kin leverage** — less institutionalized in the HRE than in some polities, but present in practice via **fürstliche Gefangenschaft** (princely captivity used as leverage) and, more structurally, via the standard medieval practice of holding a defeated rival's heir or requiring hostages as surety for treaty compliance (e.g., terms following the 1075 Saxon rebellion's suppression required Saxon nobles to surrender hostages to Henry IV).
  => Valoria design hook: a minor but reusable **Demotion-Trigger softening mechanic** — hostage-surrender as an alternative to full territorial confiscation, letting a defeated seat-holder retain formal rank at a Magnitude-1/2 penalty rather than Dismissal, provided a kin-hostage bond is posted as surety against renewed defiance.

## The standing currency

The Empire's actual "standing currency" was **Reichsunmittelbarkeit (imperial immediacy)** itself — not wealth, not raw military power, but *legal directness of tenure from the Emperor*. A count with immediacy outranked, in constitutional standing, a far wealthier baron who held only mediately through a duke. This is why mediatization (stripping immediacy) function as the ultimate demotion regardless of the target's retained wealth or local power, and why the 1803 Reichsdeputationshauptschluss reads as a catastrophic status collapse even though many mediatized rulers retained their private property and local authority.
=> Valoria design hook: model **immediacy (direct-report status to Country/Monarch, bypassing the Duchy/Province chain) as its own currency, separate from Legitimacy/Popular-Support and separate from raw resource wealth** — a Standing-ladder seat's real constitutional rank should be gate-able on this immediacy flag independent of the seat-holder's material power, so that a wealthy-but-mediated actor and a poor-but-immediate actor occupy genuinely different structural positions even at nominally similar Standing-ladder ranks.

## Florence: the office ladder and the scrutiny-bag currency

**Rank ladder (ascending):**
1. **Popolo minuto / disenfranchised laborers** (wool-carders, dyers' assistants, the *Ciompi*) — no guild membership, therefore **no standing at all**: cannot hold office, cannot be scrutinized, cannot sue in guild courts. This is rank **0 / outside the ladder entirely**.
2. **Member of an Arte Minore** (one of the 14 minor guilds) — minimal standing; eligible for minor communal posts and lower Signoria rotation slots, but structurally junior to major-guild members in practice even where nominally equal in law.
3. **Member of an Arte Maggiore** (one of the 7 major guilds — bankers, cloth merchants, wool manufacturers, silk/*Por Santa Maria*, physicians-apothecaries, judges-notaries, furriers) — the **popolo grasso**, the effective ruling class; eligible for scrutiny, priorate, and the senior magistracies.
4. **Prior (Priore delle Arti)** — one of eight, two-month term, drawn by lot from the vetted *borsa*; the base unit of the executive Signoria.
5. **Gonfaloniere di Giustizia** — the ninth, senior Signoria seat, judicial-executive command of the militia; the top of the ordinary elected cursus (life tenure for this office was a Soderini-era, 1502, innovation, otherwise two months like the priors).
6. **Accoppiatore** — an unelected, appointment-controlling insider position (from the 1430s) with no fixed term or popular mandate; power here is entirely a function of who appointed you, and it sits **above** the elected priorate in real influence despite having no formal precedence over it.
7. **Balìa-holder / Council of Seventy member (from 1480)** — a life-term, co-opted "super-council" seat, formally supreme over the Signoria itself.
8. **De facto Medici princeps** (Cosimo "pater patriae," Lorenzo "il Magnifico") — no office at all; standing is pure informal primacy backed by bank capital and patronage web, the terminal, off-ladder rank that the whole apparatus below exists to serve.

**Routes up:**
- **Guild membership + tax/lineage threshold** → base eligibility (a franchise gate, not merit per se).
- **Scrutiny (squittinio)** → being voted "in the bag" by the sitting electoral commission — the single most important gate; controlled by whoever runs the commission.
- **Sortition (tratta)** → the literal lottery draw from the bag — random *among the pre-vetted*, so real selection pressure is upstream at the scrutiny stage.
- **Patronage** → Medici-era advancement ran through personal loyalty to the Medici bank/family network, bypassing formal merit criteria; Kent's *The Rise of the Medici* and *Cosimo de' Medici and the Florentine Renaissance* document this network empirically (marriage alliances, debt forgiveness, dowry subsidies).
- **Military/administrative merit** could earn a condotta command or a chancery post (Machiavelli's own rise as Second Chancellor, 1498) but did **not** by itself confer Signoria eligibility — a structural cap on merit-only advancement.
=> Valoria design hook: encode Florence's ladder as the reference case for **Ascendancy power_base = bureaucratic vs patronage** running in the *same* hierarchy simultaneously at different rungs — formal ranks (Prior, Gonfaloniere) gated by a franchise+lottery mechanic, while a parallel informal rank (accoppiatore, Medici princeps) sits organizationally "above" them via patronage alone; Valoria's Standing ladder should allow a faction to hold a rank-6/7-equivalent seat that has **no formal Initiation-Gate**, only an Ascendancy relationship to whoever currently controls the Initiation-Gate of the ranks below it.

**Routes down (Florence-specific demotion instruments):**
- **Ammonizione** (Parte Guelfa admonition) — suspicion-only office ban, no trial, no fixed evidentiary standard; a "soft demotion" (loss of eligibility) without loss of life or property.
- **Confino/exile (bando/confino)** — sentence to a bounded distance from the city for a term of years; Florence's single most-used demotion weapon against elite rivals (Dante Alighieri, 1302; Cosimo de' Medici, 1433; Rinaldo degli Albizzi and allies, 1434). Property could be confiscated (*beni ribelli*) alongside exile.
- **Scrutiny purge** — simply not being re-included in the next *borsa*; a quiet, procedural demotion requiring no accusation at all.
- **Execution** — reserved for the gravest cases (Pazzi conspirators hanged from the Palazzo della Signoria windows, April 1478, including the Archbishop of Pisa Francesco Salviati — an execution of a churchman that itself triggered papal interdict and war).
- **Torture and burning** — the terminal demotion for a charismatic, office-less legitimacy claimant: Savonarola, tortured then hanged and burned, 23 May 1498, following excommunication (a *religious* demotion, the loss of sacramental standing, layered on top of the civic one).
=> Valoria design hook: Florence supplies four **qualitatively distinct Demotion Magnitudes** for the Standing ladder: (a) soft/suspicion-based eligibility loss (ammonizione) — Magnitude 1, reversible; (b) exile — Magnitude 2–3 severe, removes the actor from the Territory entirely and is the textbook case for **"rank = secession blast radius"**, since an exiled Florentine grandee (Rinaldo degli Albizzi, later the Strozzi) took his entire faction-following and foreign alliances with him, exporting the conflict; (c) formal Dismissal via non-reappointment (bag purge) — quiet, no Magnitude event fires, models a faction losing standing without any visible Key at all; (d) execution/interdict — Magnitude "Dishonored (-1)" plus a cross-domain penalty (excommunication removing sacramental/legitimacy standing on top of civic standing), a strong precedent for **a Standing demotion that cascades into a second, independent legitimacy track** (civic + religious) rather than a single number.

## Milan: signoria-to-duchy ladder and the condottiere currency

**Rank ladder:**
1. **Condottiere with a *condotta* (mercenary contract)** — standing purely contractual and renewable; no inherent claim on territory.
2. **Capitano del Popolo** — a military-command office with some civic color (Sforza took this title first, March 1450, before claiming the duchy).
3. **Imperial vicar** — territorial rule legitimated by a (renewable, revocable) grant from the Holy Roman Emperor; the Visconti's original status.
4. **Duke of Milan** — a purchased (Gian Galeazzo, 1395) or marriage-inherited (Sforza, via Bianca Maria Visconti, ratified Treaty of Lodi 1454) hereditary title, the terminal rank, transmissible to legitimate heirs.

**Routes up:** military merit under contract (the condottiere route — the *only* route open to a man of no birth, e.g. Francesco Sforza, son of a jobbing condottiere himself); marriage into the incumbent dynasty (Sforza/Bianca Maria); outright **purchase** of title from the Emperor (Gian Galeazzo — the clearest "purchase" route in this whole survey, quantified at ~100,000 florins).
**Routes down:** **battlefield defeat and loss of contract** (the ordinary condottiere risk); **execution for suspected treason** — the most notorious case being Venice's own condottiere **Carmagnola**, executed 1432 on suspicion of collusion with Milan, illustrating employer-side paranoia as a standing demotion mechanism that mirrors the Milanese pattern; **dynastic failure** (Filippo Maria Visconti's lack of a legitimate male heir, 1447, which didn't just demote one man but collapsed the entire ducal rank back to a republican default).
=> Valoria design hook: Milan supplies the clean **military-merit Ascendancy power_base full cursus**: condottiere-contract → capitano → vicar/duke, each transition gated by a *different* mechanism (renewal, marriage, purchase) — build a Standing-ladder track where power_base=military climbs by consecutive contract-fulfillment Keys, but the *final* rung (Duke-equivalent) requires switching power_base to kinship (marriage) or purchased (buying the title from a chain-bypass Monarch authority), i.e., military merit alone cannot reach the top rung without a power_base-conversion event.

## Genoa: the *albergo* currency and doge-as-hazard-post

**Rank ladder:** ordinary Genoese citizen → member of an **albergo** (one of the great noble clan-federations: Doria, Spinola, Grimaldi, Fieschi, and, after 1528, 28 chartered *alberghi*) → faction captain/admiral (each albergo fielded private war-galleys) → **Doge** (the ceremonial-executive peak, but the single most dangerous seat in the survey — Boccanegra was forced to resign once, restored once by uprising, and probably poisoned to death).
**Routes up:** birth into a great albergo (before 1528, essentially closed caste); after Andrea Doria's **1528 reform**, formal listing on the fixed alphabetical roll of eligible alberghi — a rare case of a hierarchy being **deliberately flattened by fiat** to end chronic civil war.
**Routes down:** assassination, forced resignation under mob pressure, deposition by a rival albergo coalition, or — uniquely among this survey's polities — **voluntary/coerced submission of the whole state to a foreign protector** (Visconti of Milan 1421–1435; French crown 1396–1409 and 1458–1461), which demoted the Doge's office itself to a subordinate governorship for the duration.
=> Valoria design hook: Genoa is the strongest precedent for a Standing-ladder rank whose **Demotion Trigger is simply "insufficient private armed force relative to rivals right now"** rather than any procedural failure — model a chartered "neutral arbiter" rank (doge/podestà-type) as inherently unstable unless the surrounding faction structure is first flattened (Doria's 1528 fixed-roll fix is the template for a scripted "stabilization reform" event a player Territory can trigger once instability crosses a threshold).

## Papal States: elective-monarchic ladder and the conclave currency

**Rank ladder:** clergy → bishop → **cardinal** (created directly by papal fiat — the pope's single greatest patronage lever, routinely used to plant kin, i.e., "cardinal-nephews") → **pope** (elected for life by the College of Cardinals in conclave, two-thirds majority required under *Ubi periculum*, 1274). Temporal governance of the Papal States ran in parallel through **legates** (cardinal-governors of provinces) and **vicariates** (grants of a city/territory to a local strongman in the pope's name, revocable on the vicar's disloyalty or the pope's death).
**Routes up:** appointment (cardinalate — pure patronage, `kinship`/`patronage` power_base), then election (conclave — a body-internal, consensus-forced vote).
**Routes down:** a vicar's **deposition** for disloyalty (used systematically by Cesare Borgia against the Romagna's petty *signori*, 1499–1502); a cardinal's loss of favor (rarely formal demotion, but exclusion from legate posts/temporal grants); **excommunication** as the uniquely papal demotion instrument, strippable from *any* rank including a reigning secular prince (used against Savonarola, 1497, and — a Country-tier case just outside this brief's core three cities — against Henry VIII of England, 1533).
=> Valoria design hook: excommunication is the strongest non-MoH precedent for a **cross-domain Standing penalty**: a religious/ideological authority (a chartered "Church"-analog faction with power_base=ideological) should be able to apply a Demotion Trigger to *any* rank in *any other faction's* ladder, including a Country-tier Monarch — i.e., one power_base track (ideological) should have unique reach across the whole scale hierarchy that other power_bases (military, bureaucratic) don't get, modeling the historically real independent leverage of religious legitimacy over secular rank.

## The standing currency, compared

Across all four polities the underlying "currency" is **not** raw wealth or birth alone but **being named on a controlled list**: Florence's *borsa*, Milan's imperial-vicariate diploma (or purchased ducal patent), Genoa's *albergo* roll, the Papal conclave's cardinalate roster. In every case the decisive lever is **who controls the list**, not the nominal selection procedure (lottery, election, inheritance) layered on top of it.
=> Valoria design hook: build the Standing ladder's underlying data model around a **"the roll, not the roll's selection procedure, is the real resource"** principle — a faction's Initiation-Gate should formally reference an abstract eligibility-list object, and the design should expose "who edits the list" as a separately-trackable, capturable resource distinct from "who wins the nominal draw/vote/inheritance" — since every historical case above shows factional capture happens at the list-editing layer, not the surface procedure.

## 2. Political Hierarchy, Standing, Advancement & Demotion — Republic of Venice

### 2.1 The three legal orders
Venetian society was legally stratified into three closed(-ish) orders, each with its own register:
- **Nobili (patricians)** — the hereditary political class created by the 1297 Serrata, registered from 1506 in the **Libro d'Oro** ("Golden Book"), the only men eligible to sit in the Great Council and thus hold any magistracy above it.
- **Cittadini (citizens)** — a legally-defined intermediate order (*cittadini originari* by birth; *per privilegio* by grant; further split *de intus* [commercial rights within Venice] vs *de intus et extra* [full rights including overseas trade]) registered from 1569 in the **Libro d'Argento** ("Silver Book"). Excluded from the Great Council but holding a real monopoly on the professional bureaucracy (§2.4).
- **Popolani** — everyone else: the commercial, artisanal, and laboring majority, including the prestigious **arsenalotti** (Arsenal shipwrights) who held ceremonial privileges (bodyguard duty to the Doge) without political rights.
=> Valoria design hook: model these three orders as three separate Standing ladders sharing one settlement, each with its own rank-0 entry gate and its own ceiling — nobili cap at rank 7 (Doge-analog), cittadini cap at a mid-rank "Grand Chancellor" ceiling (§2.4) with no path past it, popolani cap lower still — a hard, legally-coded glass ceiling rather than a soft social one, which the standing ladder schema should be able to express as a per-order max-rank field.

### 2.2 Entry into the patriciate — the Balla d'Oro and proof of nobility
Following the Serrata's lottery-entrant provision, young patrician men aged roughly 18–20 underwent the **Balla d'Oro** ("golden ball"), a formal lottery ceremony in which a number of eligible candidates each year drew a gold ball entitling early entry to the Great Council rather than waiting to age into it normally. Before admission, the **Avogadori di Comun** verified the candidate's legitimate patrilineal descent from a registered noble marriage (*prova di nobiltà*) against the Libro d'Oro — illegitimate birth, a non-patrician mother in later strict periods, or a broken registration chain could bar entry outright.
=> Valoria design hook: model rank-0 Initiation-Gate for a lineage-based faction as a two-part check — a sortition/lottery roll (Balla d'Oro) gated by a hard eligibility precondition (prova di nobiltà) verified by an independent auditor office (Avogadori) — giving factions a mechanically distinct "closed hereditary" onboarding path versus the open "apply and be vetted" onboarding used elsewhere in the standing ladder schema.

### 2.3 The nobili cursus honorum — routes up
A patrician's career ran, in rough ascending order, through:
1. **Minor councils / Quarantia seats** — entry-level elected offices for young nobles just past the Balla d'Oro.
2. **Savi agli Ordini** (5 seats, maritime affairs) — first Collegio-tier office.
3. **Savi di Terraferma** (5 seats, mainland affairs) — a step up in Collegio seniority.
4. **Savi Grandi** (6 seats, grand/senior sages) — the most senior Collegio tier, effective prime-ministerial rank, agenda-control over the Senate.
5. **Ducal Councillor** (6 seats, one per sestiere) — Signoria-tier, co-signs and constrains the Doge (§1.3).
6. **Procuratore di San Marco** ("Procurator of St Mark") — a **lifetime** office (unusual — most Venetian offices were short-term), administering St Mark's Basilica's properties and the Republic's charitable trusts; by the 15th–16th centuries it functioned as the recognized penultimate rung and frequent stepping-stone to the dogeship itself (e.g., **Francesco Morosini**, elected Doge in 1688 on the strength of his 1680s Morea campaign command, had risen via Procuratorship and military command combined).
7. **Doge** — elective, lifetime, capstone office (§1.8).
Parallel/lateral tracks existed in **provincial administration** (*Rettori* — the *Podestà* and *Capitano* sent to govern subject Terraferma cities like Padua, Verona, Brescia; *Provveditori* as civil-military overseers of frontier territories and fleets) and in **military command** (*Capitano Generale da Mar*, supreme fleet commander — itself a route to reputation convertible into Procuratorship or the dogeship, as Morosini shows).
=> Valoria design hook: this is a ready-made 8-rank Standing ladder template (rank 0 Balla d'Oro entrant → rank 7 Doge), each rung with a named Title, an Initiation-Gate (election by ballotta at the rung above, or lot for entry), Access (which councils' proceedings/Keys a rank can see or emit), Obligations (co-signature duties, term limits, contumacia), and a Mentor relationship (senior Savi sponsoring a junior into Collegio work) — use it as the flagship worked example for populating the 8-position schema with historically load-bearing titles rather than generic "Rank 3" placeholders.

### 2.4 The cittadini cursus — a parallel, capped meritocratic track
Barred from the Great Council, cittadini nonetheless staffed the entire professional bureaucracy through the **Cancelleria (Chancellery)**: notaries, Senate and Council-of-Ten secretaries, and — crucially — **ambassadorial secretaries**, who in practice drafted and often substantively conducted diplomacy that patrician ambassadors (who rotated too fast to master a brief) merely fronted. The capstone office was the **Cancellier Grande (Grand Chancellor)** — elected for life by the Great Council from among cittadini, ranked ceremonially equivalent to a Procurator of St Mark, the single highest office structurally open to a non-noble. This is Venice's clearest embodiment of a **MERITOCRATIC_BUREAUCRATIC** track running alongside, and functionally interlocking with, an **OLIGARCHIC_COUNCIL** track that nonetheless never lets the bureaucratic track's ceiling touch the noble track's floor.
=> Valoria design hook: implement the Cancellier Grande pattern as a second Ascendancy power_base — "bureaucratic" — with its own standing ladder capped below the "patronage/kinship" ladder's floor, but grant the bureaucratic ladder disproportionate real Access (drafting/queuing Keys other offices must act on) as a deliberate power/rank mismatch mechanic: a mid-rank cittadino secretary can shape a Directive a high-rank noble Senator merely stamps, modeling real information-asymmetry power distinct from formal standing.

### 2.5 Routes up — the concrete mechanisms
- **Inheritance / registration** (Libro d'Oro patrilineal descent — the default route for the vast majority of nobili).
- **Sortition** (Balla d'Oro lottery accelerating entry for a cohort of eligible young nobles each year).
- **Election (ballotta)** at every rung above entry — Great Council and Senate votes were cast with colored balls (green = yes, white = no/no-preference, red = against/hostile) dropped into an urn, a literal assent/stand-aside/holdout mechanism.
- **Patronage / faction sponsorship** — noble *casate* (clans/houses) traded "voice" and log-rolled votes for clients across councils; family antiquity itself stratified the nobility into **case vecchie** ("old houses," the pre-1297 elite, sometimes mythologized as the "24 apostolic families"), **case nuove** ("new houses," admitted in the 1381 post-Serrata expansion, see §3.4), and **case nuovissime** ("newest houses," bought in during the 1646–1718 War of Candia/Morea financial crisis for roughly 100,000 ducats per family — around 127 families were admitted this way).
- **Military merit** — naval/land command success converted into Procuratorship or the dogeship itself (Morosini, above; also the earlier **Enrico Dandolo**, elected Doge 1192 at ~85, whose command of the Fourth Crusade's 1204 assault on Constantinople is the most extreme case of a sitting Doge personally converting martial success into constitutional prestige).
- **Purchase** — direct, state-sanctioned sale of patrician status during existential fiscal emergencies (case nuove 1381; case nuovissime 1646–1718) — an official, ledgered "buy your way into the standing ladder" mechanic, not corruption but constitutional policy.
=> Valoria design hook: map "purchase" directly onto the Standing ladder's Ascendancy power_base = "purchased," but gate it structurally — Venice only opened this route during a declared, ratified fiscal-emergency charter event (never as a standing option), which should be mirrored as a temporary unlock condition (an L/PS-gated emergency Key) rather than an always-on purchase price, preserving the distinction between routine ascendancy and crisis-triggered ascendancy.

### 2.6 Routes down — the concrete mechanisms
- **Term limits + contumacia** — most magistracies were short (often one year) and carried a mandatory cooling-off period (*contumacia*, typically equal to the term length) before re-election was permitted, structurally preventing entrenchment without requiring an accusation.
- **Incompatibility rules (divieto)** — close kin (father-son, brothers) could not sit simultaneously in the same council, preventing any one casata from doubling its vote inside a chamber.
- **Legal indictment by the Avogadori di Comun** — any magistrate, including a sitting Doge, could be formally charged for exceeding the Promissione or other statute; the Avogadori could void the underlying act.
- **Posthumous audit and estate fines** — the *Inquisitori sopra il Doge defunto* reviewed a deceased Doge's conduct and could fine his estate before his heirs inherited (§1.3) — demotion applied retroactively to a *dead* office-holder, a mechanism with no clean modern analogue but a very clean Key-arithmetic one.
- **Exile (confino)** — the standard sentence for failed conspirators of good family: **Bajamonte Tiepolo** was exiled to Dalmatia/Istria after the 1310 conspiracy (§3.2) rather than executed, reflecting his birth-rank.
- **Execution + damnatio memoriae** — the maximal penalty, reserved for the gravest treason regardless of rank: **Doge Marino Faliero**, beheaded 17 April 1355 (§3.3), had his official portrait in the Sala del Maggior Consiglio replaced with a black shroud that is still there today — the literal, permanent erasure of a rank-7 office-holder's entire standing record.
- **Wrongful execution with posthumous exoneration** — patrician **Antonio Foscarini**, executed by the Council of Ten in 1622 on espionage charges, was formally exonerated by the same Council in 1623 once the informer's testimony was found fabricated — the system's own worst-case failure mode for its secrecy-optimized enforcement layer (§1.6).
- **Debt-bar** — nobles in arrears to the state treasury (*nobili non soddisfatti*) were barred from holding office until settled, a purely fiscal demotion trigger distinct from any misconduct charge.
- **Terminal system fatigue (no single demotion event)** — by the 18th century, impoverished nobles (the ***barnaboti***, named for the San Barnaba parish where many clustered) had full formal rank but no wealth, and survived by selling their Great Council votes; this hollowed out deliberative capacity without any single dramatic "fall," ending in the Republic's bloodless dissolution under Napoleonic pressure in 1797 (Doge **Ludovico Manin**'s abdication) — decline by currency-debasement of standing itself, not by conquest of an intact hierarchy.
=> Valoria design hook: the Foscarini case is the model for a rare but mechanically important Demotion-Magnitude edge case — a false-positive Dismissal-to-Dishonored that a later resolution-quality Key can *reverse* (restore standing/records posthumously) once new evidence resolves; the barnaboti case is the model for a slow, non-event-driven Standing currency debasement (too many rank-holders, too little of the underlying currency backing the rank) that the standing ladder schema should be able to represent as a gradual per-rank value decay rather than only discrete promotion/demotion triggers.

### 2.7 The standing currency itself
Venetian political "currency" was **not** primarily money (though wealth mattered) — it was, in descending order of weight: (1) **antiquity of casata** (case vecchie > nuove > nuovissime, a permanent, unearnable-by-the-individual credential); (2) **completed cursus seniority** — the specific sequence of magistracies a man had held and for how long, publicly recorded and referenced when weighing his next candidacy; (3) **military/naval laurels**; (4) **reputation for *gravità*** (gravitas/discretion) and, specifically, demonstrated ability to keep state secrets — a Council-of-Ten-era virtue with teeth, since a leak could end a career or a life; (5) marriage alliances knitting casate together. Wealth mattered mainly as a precondition (financing gift-giving, hosting, and — in the case nuove/nuovissime episodes — literally purchasing entry) rather than as the ladder's native currency.
=> Valoria design hook: implement Venetian-style Standing as a composite score (lineage-tier multiplier × cursus-completion count × secrecy-track-record) rather than a single scalar "reputation" number, so that a faction using the "kinship" Ascendancy power_base genuinely computes standing differently from one using "merit" or "bureaucratic" — giving the 8-rank ladder schema real per-power_base currency formulas instead of one universal metric.

## 2. Political Hierarchy, Standing, Advancement and Demotion

### 2.1 Two parallel ladders that governed one person's total standing
Han/Wei officialdom ran **two simultaneous rank systems** that a single individual held at once, and understanding the period requires keeping them separate:
1. **Salary rank** (*zhi bili*, "office equivalent-in-bushels"), the functional office-hierarchy, denominated in nominal bushels of grain (*shi*) per year even where paid partly in cash/cloth: from **10,000 shi** (Chancellor/Excellencies) down through **Fully 2,000 shi**, **2,000 shi**, **Comparable to 2,000 shi**, **1,000 shi**, **600 shi**, **400 shi**, **300 shi**, **200 shi**, **100 shi**, to **Below 100 shi** (petty clerks) — roughly 20 salary grades total (Bielenstein's reconstruction from the *Hanshu* and *Hou Hanshu* "Treatise on Officials").
2. **Noble rank** (*jue*), the honor/nobility hierarchy inherited from Qin's **Twenty Ranks of Merit Nobility** (*Ershi Deng Jue*), from Rank 1 (*Gongshi*, "Duke's Grandee") up to Rank 20 (*Chehou*, "Marquis of the [Imperial] Chariot," later just *Liehou*/Full Marquis — the only rank carrying an actual fief with tax income), sitting *above* Han's later separate kingship rank (*Wang*) reserved for imperial kin.
=> Valoria design hook: this is a strong precedent for **decoupling the Standing ladder's rank number from its "Access" field** — an NPC's *functional* office-rank (their salary-grade equivalent, tracking what Directives they're actually empowered to execute) should be trackable separately from their *honorific* standing (their jue-equivalent, tracking prestige/Access to court ritual and hereditary transmission), so the design can express a case like a rank-4 field commander holding only rank-1 honorific nobility, or the inverse.

### 2.2 Routes UP the ladder
- **Recommendation (cha ju)** — the primary route into officialdom pre-keju (see modes_md §1.5): commandery quota nomination as Xiaolian/Maocai, vetted by a court interview/policy essay, converts into an entry clerkship (e.g. Gentleman of the Palace, Lang) that then promotes through provincial postings back to the capital. Required an existing patron/sponsor to spend recommendation capital.
  => Valoria design hook: gate Standing-ladder rank 0→1 promotion behind a **Mentor-sponsorship cost** paid by an existing rank≥3 NPC, consuming a scarce "recommendation slot" resource per period — this is the direct cha ju quota mechanic, and it should create the same durable Mentor-link/obligation the historical *guli* bond did.
- **Hereditary privilege (yin, "shade/protection")** — sons and grandsons of officials above a rank threshold (2,000-shi and above, later broadened) received automatic minor appointment (commonly as Gentleman-cadet) without recommendation, a direct kinship-based fast-track parallel to and competing with merit recommendation.
  => Valoria design hook: implement *yin* as a **power_base = "kinship"** fast-entry lane onto the Standing ladder that bypasses the Initiation-Gate check entirely for direct descendants of rank≥5 seat-holders, explicitly coded as a second, privileged on-ramp coexisting with the merit lane — the tension between the two lanes *is* the design content, not a bug to remove.
- **Military merit (jungong)** — field command success, especially against steppe/frontier threats, promoted commoners and lower gentry directly into salary rank and noble rank alike (the Qin-derived Twenty Ranks were originally a battlefield-kill-count ladder); Three Kingdoms warlord retinues (Cao Cao's, Sun Quan's, Liu Bei's core officer corps) recruited and promoted almost entirely on this axis given collapsed civil recommendation infrastructure.
  => Valoria design hook: wire this directly to the **resolution-quality → standing bridge** — a combat/Fieldwork Key's arithmetic gap (delivered vs. demanded on a military Directive) should be able to advance Standing-ladder rank *without* going through the normal Mentor/Initiation-Gate check at all, mirroring how field promotion bypassed cha ju entirely during the Han-collapse period; this is the design's built-in alternate on-ramp for wartime/frontier factions.
- **Purchase of office/rank (mairen jue / na su bai jue)** — from Emperor Wen (r. 180–157 BCE) and repeatedly thereafter (notoriously formalized as an open price list under Emperor Ling in 178 CE, who sold Excellency-tier posts and noble ranks openly to refill a depleted treasury, to the fury of the "pure critique" faction), the state directly monetized advancement, letting wealth convert to rank/office without any patronage or merit gate.
  => Valoria design hook: this is precedent for a **"purchased" power_base lane on the Standing ladder** — a faction under fiscal stress can open a temporary rank-for-treasury exchange, which should carry a built-in Legitimacy/Popular-Support penalty (as it historically did — Emperor Ling's office-selling is cited by contemporaries as accelerating Han's terminal legitimacy collapse) proportional to how senior the sold rank is.
- **Election within kin-councils and consort-clan appointment** — Empresses/Empresses Dowager were selected via a form of court/clan consensus among senior imperial affines and ratified by the emperor (or, for child emperors, the outgoing Dowager); once installed, a Dowager's *own* clan seniors received automatic top appointments (the General-in-Chief slot going to her father/brother almost as a rule during Later Han), an ASCRIBED kinship promotion route layered directly on top of the state hierarchy.
  => Valoria design hook: model consort-clan advancement as an **Ascendancy power_base = "kinship" that grants an automatic top-N Standing-ladder seat**, separate from and stackable with the normal promotion checks — the mechanic should let a single marriage/kinship event instantly mint a rank-6/7 seat-holder, matching how a Liang or He family clan head became General-in-Chief purely via a daughter's/sister's installation as Empress.

### 2.3 Routes DOWN the ladder
- **Impeachment / censorial indictment (hejue, "block-and-report")** — Attendant Censors (Shiyushi) and the Excellencies' own censorial staff could formally indict a minister for malfeasance, ritual impropriety, or (functionally a catch-all) "responding wrongly to a portent"; a sustained indictment forced the emperor to at minimum demote, more often dismiss, the target.
  => Valoria design hook: this is the direct precedent for the **resolution-quality → standing bridge running in reverse** — a Key's negative arithmetic gap on a Directive (badly mishandled event response) should trigger a formal impeachment-style demotion event distinct from ordinary attrition, with its own procedural step (an indictment must be lodged and can itself succeed or fail) rather than an automatic silent rank tick.
- **Demotion / banishment to frontier post (qian/zhe, "reduce/relegate")** — the standard mid-severity penalty: reassignment to a lower salary grade and/or a remote frontier commandery, a career-damaging but survivable sanction (famously suffered and recorded by e.g. Han court officials sent to border postings after a failed remonstrance).
  => Valoria design hook: maps directly to the Standing ladder's **default 1-rank Demotion Magnitude** — a "relegation" event should be the *default*-severity demotion trigger, moving a seat-holder down one rank and re-assigning them to a lower-Access posting, not stripping them to Dishonored outright.
- **Exile (liu) and forced suicide (ci si)** — for graver offenses against a senior official/imperial kinsman, exile of the official and family to a distant frontier region, or, for the most senior figures where public execution was considered beneath their dignity, an imperially "granted" order to commit suicide (preserving some family status/property versus outright execution) — Liang Ji (159 CE) and numerous Later Han ministers were disposed of this way.
  => Valoria design hook: this is the precedent for the Standing ladder's **severe 2–3 rank demotion band** distinct from ordinary relegation — model "forced suicide" as a severe-demotion variant that removes the seat-holder from play but preserves partial family/faction standing (their kin retain a reduced but nonzero rank), versus execution/dismissal which zeroes it.
- **Execution and clan extermination (zu)** — the maximal sanction: execution of the official plus, in the worst political-crime cases (treason, regicide-adjacent plots), extermination of three or nine degrees of kin (*san zu*/*jiu zu*) — deployed against defeated regents and coup losers (Dong Zhuo's entire clan was exterminated after his 192 CE assassination; the Ten Attendants eunuch faction was massacred wholesale by Yuan Shao's troops in the palace in 189 CE, without individual trial).
  => Valoria design hook: this is the ceiling case for **Standing ladder Dismissal to -1 "Dishonored"** — model *zu*-style extermination as a Dismissal event that doesn't just zero the individual seat-holder but recursively applies a lesser Dishonored/demotion penalty to their entire kinship-linked Mentor-subtree, propagating downward through everyone whose Standing traces to that patron.
- **Loss of office through structural abolition, not personal fault** — Tui'en Ling (127 BCE, see modes_md §1.4) demoted entire kingly lines to marquisates and marquisates to commoner gentry purely through partition, with no individual charge laid against any title-holder — a "demotion by structural rule change" distinct from all the punitive routes above.
  => Valoria design hook: the Standing ladder needs a **non-punitive demotion trigger type** ("structural" as opposed to "disciplinary") — a Crown-issued Directive that alters the ladder's rank-eligibility rules (e.g., a partible-inheritance mandate) should be able to demote a whole faction-branch's ranks as a side effect, with no Key/resolution-quality event required at all.
- **Hostage-kinship leverage as an ongoing constraint on standing, not just a demotion event** — commandery/kingdom heirs and, later, warlord subordinates' family members were routinely kept resident near the capital/the overlord's court as de facto hostages guaranteeing the officeholder's field loyalty; Dong Zhuo, Cao Cao, and Yuan Shao-era warlords all used this against subordinate generals' kin.
  => Valoria design hook: implement hostage-kin as a **standing "leash" resource attached to a Standing-ladder seat** — a faction/Crown holding a seat-holder's named kin as leverage should reduce that seat-holder's effective secession blast-radius (rank = secession blast-radius principle, modes_md §1.7) below what their nominal rank would otherwise allow, and releasing/losing the hostage should restore full blast-radius immediately.

### 2.4 What was the "standing" currency?
The composite currency was **(salary-rank × noble-rank × palace-access × kinship-proximity-to-the-throne)** — no single scalar; a figure could be salary-rank-poor but noble-rank-rich (impoverished marquises), or salary-rank-senior but access-poor (an Excellency frozen out by the Secretariat), or access-supreme with no formal rank at all (a favored eunuch). Real standing was legible only by cross-referencing all four axes at once.
  => Valoria design hook: this argues for the Standing ladder's rank number being a *display aggregate* over multiple underlying tracked axes (office/salary-equivalent, honor/noble-equivalent, access, kinship-proximity) rather than a single ground-truth integer — expose the aggregate as the ladder position but keep the axes queryable so mechanics like the eunuch-access-coup (modes_md §1.8) can trigger off access alone, independent of the displayed rank.

## 2. Political Hierarchy, Standing, Advancement & Demotion

### 2.1 The two parallel ladders: court rank (kuge) vs warrior rank (buke)
Japan ran two rank systems simultaneously that rarely mapped cleanly onto each other. The ancient **court rank system** (i-kai, from the Taika-era ritsuryō codes) ran from Senior First Rank down through a dozen-plus grades, held hereditarily within ceilinged bands (**kakaku**) by kuge families (Fujiwara regental houses at the top, lesser court families below) — advancement was almost purely birth plus seniority within your family's fixed ceiling, with essentially no route for a commoner to enter it. Shoguns themselves normally held a court rank (e.g., Junior Second Rank) as a legitimating veneer even while wielding zero court function. The **warrior/buke hierarchy** — shogun, regent, shugo/daimyo, retainer — was a separate, functionally dominant ladder from Kamakura on, with its own advancement logic (below).
=> Valoria design hook: model two co-existing standing ladders on some settlements — a ceremonial court-rank track (birth-gated, low ceiling per bloodline, near-zero mechanical power but real ceremonial/legitimacy weight) stacked against the functional 0–7 faction Standing ladder; a character can be simultaneously high on one and low on the other, and a Legitimacy check can require BOTH thresholds be met.

### 2.2 The honryō-andō / shin-on bond — the core Kamakura vassal contract
The gokenin ("housemen," Yoritomo's core vassal band) bond rested on a reciprocal exchange the sources call **go-on to hōkō** ("favor and service"): the shogun's **on** (favor) took two concrete legal forms — **honryō-andō** ("confirmation of existing hereditary holdings," i.e., the shogun formally guaranteeing a vassal's *pre-existing* land title against rival claimants — pure legitimation/insurance, no new wealth) and **shin-on** ("new benefice," an actual new land grant, typically drawn from confiscated estates — e.g., the huge post-Jōkyū War 1221 confiscations redistributed as shin-on to loyal gokenin). In exchange the gokenin owed **hōkō**: military muster on demand, guard duty (**ōban'yaku**, rotating palace/Kyoto guard duty), and court appearances. This is a *bond*, not a salary — it required the shogun to have a continuous supply of confiscatable/conquerable land to keep paying out shin-on, or the bond decayed.
=> Valoria design hook: model honryō-andō/shin-on as the two distinct arithmetic components of the **resolution-quality → standing bridge**: honryō-andō is a "confirm, no upgrade" Key (gap = 0, maintains but does not advance standing) while shin-on is a genuine positive-gap delivery that advances rank; a faction that runs out of confiscatable/conquerable assets to distribute (as the Kamakura bakufu did after the Mongol invasions, below) structurally converts its whole vassal base's future resolutions into honryō-andō-only outcomes — a mechanical inevitability of stalled advancement, not GM fiat.

### 2.3 The Mongol invasions and the bond's arithmetic collapse (1274, 1281)
The two Mongol invasions (Bun'ei 1274, Kōan 1281) were repelled, but — unlike a civil war — left **no enemy land to confiscate and redistribute**. The bakufu owed its gokenin a shin-on-grade reward for real military service and could not deliver one; it substituted **tokusei-rei** ("virtuous government edicts," notably the 1297 **Einin no Tokusei**) unilaterally cancelling gokenin debts to moneylenders instead of paying new grants. This preserved the vassal band in the short term but destroyed the credit markets vassals depended on and did not resolve the underlying reward deficit — a direct contributor to gokenin disaffection that fed the 1333 collapse.
=> Valoria design hook: a textbook non-MoH case of the resolution-quality gap accumulating across an entire faction rather than one seat — model a "national" version of the standing bridge where an unpaid aggregate Directive (military service owed vs. reward delivered) depresses the whole faction's Ascendancy/legitimacy simultaneously, distinct from any single officer's individual demotion.

### 2.4 Shugo and jitō — the appointive backbone, and its later capture
**Shugo** (provincial military governors, from 1185) held province-level police/muster authority, appointed and removable by the shogun — pure appointive office, no independent land title attached at first. **Jitō** (estate stewards) were appointed onto individual shōen (public or private estates), collecting taxes and adjudicating locally; their steady encroachment on proprietor rights produced negotiated compromises like **shitaji chūbun** (physical splitting of an estate's land between jitō and proprietor) and **jitō-uke** (jitō contracting to deliver a fixed rent, keeping the surplus) — i.e., an appointive office quietly accreting de facto landed standing underneath its formal appointive shell. By the Muromachi period, shugo who accumulated appointments across multiple provinces became hereditary **shugo daimyo**, converting an appointive rung into a de facto landed one — precisely the mechanism Sengoku gekokujō would later exploit and reverse (below).
=> Valoria design hook: model the shugo→shugo-daimyo drift as a rank-ladder rule: an "appointive, revocable" Standing rank that accumulates enough concurrent Access grants (multiple provincial postings) auto-converts to a hereditary rank tier unless the suzerain actively intervenes — gives Valoria's ladder a built-in "office ossifies into title" pressure the Crown/Parliament chain-bypass authorities must periodically correct.

### 2.5 Sengoku daimyo retainer bands: kinship, hereditary, and new vassals
A Sengoku daimyo's **kashindan** (retainer band) sorted into named tiers — **ichimon-shū** (kinship vassals, cadet branches of the ruling house), hereditary core vassals, and absorbed/conquered new vassals — with advancement routes including marriage alliance, **yōshi** (adoption into a higher house, a legal fiction that let a talented outsider or even a rival's son inherit headship), and above all **naked military merit under gekokujō conditions**, where a deputy stewars (**shugodai**) or even a lower retainer could topple his nominal lord.
=> Valoria design hook: yōshi (adoption-as-advancement) is a concrete non-purchase, non-inheritance-by-blood route up the Standing ladder worth adding as an Initiation-Gate variant — "adopted heir" as a legal fast-path into a higher rank bracket, distinct from patronage or merit, with its own Demotion-Trigger if the adoptive house later disowns the heir.

### 2.6 The Tokugawa daimyo classification and kokudaka as the standing currency
Tokugawa daimyo (any lord assessed at ≥10,000 koku) were sorted into three legally distinct tiers with different Access: **shinpan** (Tokugawa collateral branches, chiefly the **Gosanke** — Owari, Kii, Mito — eligible in principle to supply an heir if the main line failed); **fudai** (hereditary vassals who had submitted to Ieyasu before Sekigahara 1600 — the *only* tier eligible for central bakufu office such as rōjū, deliberately kept on modest kokudaka so office-holders could never out-resource the shogun); and **tozama** ("outside lords" who submitted at or after Sekigahara — the great houses like Maeda, Shimazu, Mōri, Date — permanently excluded from central office, distrusted, and subjected to the heaviest sankin-kōtai/hostage burden). Below the daimyo threshold sat **hatamoto** (direct bannermen retainers, <10,000 koku, eligible for bureaucratic bakufu posts) and **gokenin** (in Edo-period usage, lower direct vassals — a term reused from the Kamakura-era vassal class but denoting a different, subordinate tier by this period). The **kokudaka** assessment (rice-yield rating, standardized nationwide by Hideyoshi's Taikō kenchi 1582–1598) was the master currency underlying everything: it fixed a domain's military levy obligation, its sankin-kōtai retinue size, its share of bakufu corvée labor (**tenka bushin**, public-works conscription), and literal seating/audience order at Edo Castle ceremonies.
=> Valoria design hook: kokudaka is the strongest available real-world precedent for a **numeric standing currency that simultaneously gates military obligation, Access tier, and ceremonial rank** — implement a settlement/faction "assessed yield" stat that feeds the Standing ladder's Access field directly, distinct from raw wealth, and that a Crown-tier authority nationally re-surveys and re-fixes periodically (a "kenchi event") as a chain-bypass reset tool.

### 2.7 Advancement via bureaucratic office (late, partial meritocracy)
Within the fudai/hatamoto pool, the Tokugawa cursus ran through named offices — rōjū (Elders) and the rarer **tairō** (Great Elder, an occasional supreme post, e.g. Ii Naosuke) at the top; **wakadoshiyori** (junior elders); **ō-metsuke** (inspectors-general); **machi-bugyō** (city magistrates); **kanjō-bugyō** (finance magistrates) below. Advancement here was patronage- and seniority-driven for most of the period, but from 1793 the **Kansei Reforms** under Matsudaira Sadanobu instituted formal examinations at the bakufu's **Shōheizaka Gakumonjo** (Confucian academy) as a genuine vetting gate for bureaucratic appointment — a late, partial, and status-bounded (fudai/hatamoto only) shift toward a MERITOCRATIC_BUREAUCRATIC route layered on top of an otherwise patronage/inheritance system.
=> Valoria design hook: model this as a hybrid Ascendancy power_base — "bureaucratic" power_base unlocked only after an "examination" Initiation-Gate, but the gate itself is only open to characters who already cleared a prior birth/patronage gate (fudai/hatamoto status) — i.e., meritocratic advancement nested *inside* a closed aristocratic pool rather than an open cursus.

### 2.8 Routes down: kaieki, tenpu, genpō, exile, seppuku
The concrete demotion/removal mechanisms, graded by severity: **kaieki** (attainder — total confiscation of the han, the daimyo and his retainers reduced to rōnin/commoner status; e.g. Fukushima Masanori, 1619, kaieki'd for unauthorized castle repairs in violation of the Buke shohatto; Katō Tadahiro, 1632, kaieki'd amid clan mismanagement) was the maximal Dismissal-to-Dishonored-equivalent penalty. **Kunigae/tenpu** (forced transfer to a different, usually smaller or less strategically valuable domain) was a lesser, non-terminal demotion used especially against tozama to keep them off-balance without destroying the house. **Genpō** (reduction of assessed kokudaka short of full confiscation) was a graded fine-in-rank. **Seppuku** (ritual suicide, ordered rather than voluntary in many judicial contexts) and outright **execution** closed misconduct cases at the top of the warrior hierarchy. **Exile** (to islands — Oki, Sado, Hachijō-jima) was reserved chiefly for the highest-status offenders whose execution would be politically explosive: retired Emperor Go-Toba to Oki after the 1221 Jōkyū War; Emperor Go-Daigo to Oki in 1332 (he escaped in 1333). Court nobles faced **loss of rank/office** rather than kaieki, the kuge-track equivalent penalty. Beneath all of these sat the continuous, non-event **hostage-kin leverage** of sankin-kōtai (§1.9) — a standing threat that made active punishment often unnecessary.
=> Valoria design hook: map this directly onto the Standing ladder's Demotion Magnitude scale — kaieki = Dismissal-to-(-1) "Dishonored"; kunigae/genpō = a 1–2 rank Demotion with a lateral or reduced Access reassignment rather than pure rank-loss; exile = a special "removed but not eliminated" state reserved for Standing-7-equivalent seats whose destruction would itself destabilize the whole ladder (a "too big to execute" clause); the ever-present sankin-kōtai analogue should suppress a seat's power_base continuously rather than trigger a discrete Demotion event.

### 2.9 Rank as secession blast-radius
Because kokudaka, house tier (shinpan/fudai/tozama), and hostage-compliance jointly determined both a daimyo's Standing *and* his retainer band's total mobilizable manpower, a defection at the tozama-great-house rank (Shimazu of Satsuma, Mōri of Chōshū) always threatened to detach an entire han's worth of population and armed retainers, whereas a hatamoto's defection detached at most his own household — the ROTK "rank = secession blast-radius" principle in concrete Japanese form, made vivid by the 1600 Sekigahara realignment (whole tozama houses flipping sides wholesale) and the 1868 Satsuma/Chōshū-led restoration (two great tozama han's defection ending the regime outright).
=> Valoria design hook: tie the faction Standing ladder's rank number directly to secession blast-radius scaling — a rank-7 seat's disloyalty should mechanically threaten to pull an entire allied population bloc (reaggregation-scale event), while a rank-1/2 seat's disloyalty is self-contained, exactly mirroring hatamoto vs. tozama-daimyo defection stakes.

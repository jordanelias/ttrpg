# anti-patterns — Skeleton (coherence-indexed revision)

Live rules for composition and audit. Each entry: rule name → symptom → fix. No rationale, no examples, no academic citations. For those, see `anti-patterns-infill.md` and load the matching section.

---

## LAMP Seven (Professional Editor Consensus)

### 1. Cliché
- **Symptom:** Surface words (delve, intricate, tapestry, testament, vibrant, underscore, commendable, nuanced) OR structural moves (ironic understatement endings, universalized paradox, rule of three with escalation, meaningful weather, named-emotion-with-physical-correlate).
- **Fix:** Replace received phrasing with specific detail. What did the person actually do?

### 2. Unnecessary/Redundant Exposition
- **Symptom:** Explaining what the scene already shows. Stating significance after depicting events. Closing a paragraph by summarizing what it just demonstrated.
- **Fix:** Delete the explaining sentence. Trust the reader.

### 3. Purple Prose
- **Symptom:** Decorative similes ("hung in the air like X"). Stacked adjectives. Metaphors announcing the writer's cleverness.
- **Fix:** Cut adjectives until only load-bearing ones remain. A strong noun without modifier outperforms a weak noun with three.

### 4. Sentence Structure Homogeneity
- **Symptom:** 5+ consecutive sentences of similar length. Every sentence subject-verb-object. No fragments. The long-then-short alternation pattern repeated.
- **Fix:** Cluster three short sentences. Run two long in sequence. Drop a fragment. Read aloud.

### 5. Lack of Specificity
- **Symptom:** "The market was busy." "A sense of unease settled." "Something older than sorrow."
- **Fix:** Name the thing. Give the number. Identify the material. If you cannot be specific, the sentence has no content — cut it.

### 6. Awkward Word Choice
- **Symptom:** Latinate replacing Germanic without precision gain (commenced/began, perambulated/walked). Modern corporate language in historical settings (stakeholders, leverage). AI-marker words.
- **Fix:** Test each rare word — does it save syllables vs its periphrasis, or just add register? If it saves, keep. If it adds, cut.

### 7. Tense Inconsistency
- **Symptom:** Drift between past and present, especially in descriptive passages.
- **Fix:** Choose a tense and hold it. Make any shift deliberate and visible.

## Academic Research Findings

### 8. Lower Uncertainty / Aporia Failure
- **Symptom:** Scene resolves into a single clear meaning. Character motivation is rendered legible when it should be opaque.
- **Fix:** Allow contradiction. Two readings of a scene may coexist.

### 9. Subtext Failure
- **Symptom:** Characters say what they mean. Conflicts explicit. Tension resolved through dialogue rather than withheld through omission.
- **Fix:** Build scenes where what characters do not say carries the weight.

### 10. Cultural Ghosting / Register Flattening
- **Symptom:** All characters in similar register regardless of background. Vernacular smoothed. Class-marked speech lost.
- **Fix:** Preserve distinct registers. Use untranslated terms with context teaching. Maintain class-marked speech in dialogue.

### 11. Failure to Sustain Dramatic Tension
- **Symptom:** Setup and payoff in adjacent sentences. Suspense established and released within the same paragraph.
- **Fix:** Hold tension longer than feels natural. Insert digression between setup and payoff.

### 12. Plot Template Convergence
- **Symptom:** Endings resolve too neatly. Antagonists as obstacles not people. Themes surface as moral conclusions.
- **Fix:** Resist the template. Let conflicts persist. Let endings open.

### 13. Long-Range Coherence Failure
- **Symptom:** Earlier-established traits disappear. Setup elements never pay off. Names/places/facts shift.
- **Fix:** Track named entities. Do not contradict established traits without acknowledging.

### 14. Lower Lexical Diversity / Heavy Nominalization
- **Symptom:** "The implementation of the strategy" instead of "they did it." Generic abstract nouns: ecosystem, framework, dynamic.
- **Fix:** Concrete verbs. Concrete nouns. "She walked" before "she navigated."

### 15. Reduced Sentiment Modulation
- **Symptom:** Uniform emotional pitch across passages. Grim stays grim; comic stays comic.
- **Fix:** Place absurdity beside suffering without commentary. Let humor surface in tragedy.

### 16. Negative Parallelism ("Not just X but Y")
- **Symptom:** "Not just X, but Y." Negation setting up "more important" affirmation.
- **Fix:** Affirm directly. The negative setup usually adds nothing.

### 17. Hypotaxis-Parataxis Indecision
- **Symptom:** Sentences subordinate weakly without committing to either pattern.
- **Fix:** Commit. Default hypotactic (parataxis is marked, not default — per Turner). When paratactic, commit to the marked register. When hypotactic, go deep with single arc.

## Structural AI Tells

### 18. Excessive Symmetry
- **Symptom:** Balanced constructions used reflexively. "Some did X; others did Y." "Where once there was A, now there was B."
- **Fix:** Use once if at all. Real prose is asymmetric.

### 19. The Reaction Shot
- **Symptom:** After every event, prose describes how characters react emotionally.
- **Fix:** Often skip the reaction. State the event; begin the next scene.

### 20. Thematic Announcement
- **Symptom:** "In a world where trust was rare, she had learned to rely on herself."
- **Fix:** Embed theme in event and image. Never state.

### 21. Summarizing Final Sentence
- **Symptom:** Paragraphs ending with summarizing or reflecting sentences.
- **Fix:** End on the strongest image or most important fact.

### 22. Hammering Repetition
- **Symptom:** Repeating phrase/word for emphasis when one instance carries the weight.
- **Fix:** Trust the first. (Distinguish from Lispector's repetition-as-ontological-intensity and Ishiguro's repetition-with-variation.)

### 23. Editorial Stance Without Focalization
- **Symptom:** "Sinister," "noble," "wretched" floating from a non-existent neutral narrator.
- **Fix:** Either anchor in focalized perspective, or replace with condition-words.

### 24. Single-Author Dominance Without Cause
- **Symptom:** Passage's texture overwhelmingly one author's techniques without content demanding it.
- **Fix:** Diagnose what content needs. Splice another author's technique into a clause when motivated.

### 25. Mechanical-Term Leakage in Focalized Prose
- **Symptom:** Conviction Track / Church Influence / Mending Stability / etc. named in in-world POV.
- **Fix:** Translate to focalized experience. The character names the experience, not the system.

### 26. Flat-Affect Omniscience
- **Symptom:** "Neutral all-knowing narrator" with no perspective, no stance.
- **Fix:** Identify focalization before writing. For chronicle-mode prose, focalization MUST be one of the four canonical chroniclers.

### 27. Workshop Closings
- **Symptom:** Endings that announce themselves: ironic understatement, pivoting reflection, thematic gesture.
- **Fix:** End on the strongest specific detail.

### 28. Too-Clever Constructions
- **Symptom:** Sentences drawing attention to their own cleverness.
- **Fix:** Real prose is rougher. Plainer.

### 29. Second-Person Drift in PC Prose
- **Symptom:** Narrative prose mixing third-person with embedded "you" pronouns referring to the player-character.
- **Fix:** Convert all PC references to name/pronoun. Choice-hooks may stay imperative; narration is uniform third-person.

### 30. Narrativity Drift (PNAS Reinhart et al. 2025)
- **Symptom:** LLMs produce fewer past-tense verbs and fewer third-person pronouns than human reference.
- **Fix:** Audit past-tense verb count and third-person pronoun frequency.

### 31. Aidiolect (Mikros 2025; Bitton et al. 2025)
- **Symptom:** Each LLM has a distinct default style. Generic anti-AI rules don't catch model-specific defaults.
- **Fix:** Identify model-default features active in this composition and counter explicitly.

### 32. Algorithmic Mono-Voice Within Session (Hutchinson et al. 2025)
- **Symptom:** Within a long composition session, prose drifts toward one persistent texture.
- **Fix:** Audit cross-paragraph variance after writing. If three consecutive paragraphs share the same dominant technique without content motivation, break the pattern.

### 33. Large-Magnitude Direction-Uniform Revisions
- **Symptom:** When revising, the prose shifts in large magnitude in one direction. Real human revision moves in small magnitude in diverse directions.
- **Fix:** Revisions should be small and direction-diverse.

### 34. Latent-Space-Absent Personas
- **Symptom:** When writing perspectives marginalized in or deliberately suppressed from training data, the model approximates from incomplete signal.
- **Fix:** Don't accept the first draft for these focalizations. Verify against canonical reference material.

### 35. Single-Feature Audit Insufficient
- **Symptom:** Auditing for one rule at a time misses dimensional patterns. Co-occurring features matter.
- **Fix:** After single-rule audit, run dimensional checks: narrativity, variation, focalization. Single-feature pass with dimensional fail is the typical LLM signature.

## Author-Specific Technique Precision

### 36. Tolkien Parataxis as Default Rather Than Marker
- **Symptom:** Paratactic prose ("X, and Y, and Z") used as baseline register for paragraphs at a time. Becomes default rather than stylistic shift.
- **Fix:** Keep base register hypotactic. Surface paratactic chains only at marked moments — heroic peaks, elevated register, moments of weight. Parataxis is "stylistically marked, indicating something out of the ordinary" (Turner). If it's the default, the marking is lost.

### 37. Ishiguro Restraint Mislabeled
- **Symptom:** Claiming Ishiguro technique for any formal-measured prose. Le Carré tradecraft prose, measured diplomatic register, or simple courtesy labeled as "Ishiguro restraint."
- **Fix:** Ishiguro requires *unreliability presenting as restraint*. The narrator's composure conceals self-deception, not just social caution. If the narrator is reliable, the technique is not Ishiguro. Label it what it actually is.

### 38. FID Conflated with Direct Thought / Narrative Report
- **Symptom:** Free indirect discourse, direct thought, and narrative report of thought used interchangeably. The three categories per Cohn/Pascal are distinct.
- **Fix:** FID = 3rd-person narration slipping into character's vocabulary and judgment without quotation. Direct thought = character's words rendered as interior monologue. Narrative report = narrator summarizing character's thought from outside. Name which is deployed.

### 39. Mistry Ensemble Misidentified as Scene-Break
- **Symptom:** Claiming Mistry's continuous-scene rotation when actually deploying scene-break-with-perspective-change. Break between perspectives = not Mistry ensemble.
- **Fix:** Mistry's technique rotates through multiple characters' interiors within a single continuous scene. No break in continuity. If there's a scene break, it's just multiple perspectives — valid but not Mistry's technique.

### 40. Stress-and-Release Fractal Claimed Without Full Deployment
- **Symptom:** Claiming Le Guin's fractal stress-and-release pattern when deploying it only at scene/chapter level. Sentence-level and paragraph-level deployment absent.
- **Fix:** Either deploy at all levels (sentence, paragraph, scene, chapter) or name only the levels where it's actually operating. The claim of fractal structure requires fractal deployment.

## Coherence-Mismatch Anti-Patterns

### 41. Synthesis-Weighting Mismatch with PC Coherence
- **Symptom:** Composing PC-focalized passage at weighting that doesn't match the established coherence tier. E.g., heavy Borges recursion in a Tier 9-10 passage, or stable Tolkien ground in a Tier 1-2 passage.
- **Fix:** Consult `coherence-tiers.md` before composing. Match weighting to tier.

### 42. Realist Anchor Over-Deployment at Low Coherence
- **Symptom:** At Tier 1-2, still anchoring with named places and deep-time framing reliably. The world presented as stable.
- **Fix:** At low coherence, named anchors are present but unreliable. The prose does not trust them. Tolkien landscape fragments appear but detached from their referents.

### 43. Irreal Author Over-Deployment at High Coherence
- **Symptom:** At Tier 9-10, deploying Borges recursion, Lispector defamiliarization, or Ocampo uncanny without trigger. The world presented as strange when it should cohere.
- **Fix:** At high coherence, irreal authors surface only at threadwork-events or edges. The baseline is realist. Strangeness requires cause.

## Character and Cultural Anti-Patterns

### 44. NPC Functional Placeholder
- **Symptom:** NPC has narrative weight but no specified ethical framework, conviction, goal, inspiration, or factional belonging. The NPC functions as plot furniture.
- **Fix:** Anchor each load-bearing NPC with at least one specific gesture or detail establishing one of these dimensions. The canonical Valoria convictions (Equity, Faith, Order, Excellence) provide the framework. Test: if the NPC carries emotional weight in the scene, the reader must know something about who they are beyond their function.

### 45. Cultural-Tradition Appropriation
- **Symptom:** Borrowing a technique from a specific cultural tradition (signifyin', AAVE rhetorical modes, sacred-text rhythms from a specific tradition) and applying it to non-tradition-specific contexts without fidelity to that tradition's practitioners.
- **Fix:** Name what the prose is actually doing without the tradition-specific label. Encoded coded speech is not signifyin'. Biblical-cadence borrowing requires either being in dialogue with biblical tradition or naming the structure differently (e.g., "paratactic ritual cadence"). If the technique's power depends on its cultural context, do not extract it from that context.

## Author-Specific Mannerism Risk

If used twice in same passage, becomes mannerism:
- **Tolkien:** Inverted syntax ("Great was the ruin")
- **Ishiguro:** "perhaps as it should have been," "I cannot now recall"
- **Mistry:** Sensory catalogues
- **Tartt:** Atmospheric long sentences
- **Márquez:** Hyperbolic precision
- **Lispector:** "This instant-now" temporal shift
- **Borges:** Infinite recursion / mirror metaphor
- **Ocampo:** Flat-affect horror

**Discipline:** Borrow techniques in rotation. No single source's signature move twice in same passage.

## The Master Rule

If a sentence could appear in any story about any settlement / character / event, it belongs to no story. Cut it.

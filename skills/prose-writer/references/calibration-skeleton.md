## Cross-Author Synthesis Properties

All six anchors share these properties. The synthesized voice has all of them simultaneously:

1. **Refuse explanation.** None of these openings explain themselves. None translate cultural reference. None summarize their own meaning.
2. **Specific perspective.** Tolkien's vantage. Stevens' speech. Mistry's village. Morrison's family. Tartt's "we." Márquez's deep-time chronicler. None neutral omniscient.
3. **Structural simplicity with semantic depth.** Sentences are not lexically baroque. Depth is in implication, structure, focalization.
4. **Tolerate aporia.** None resolve into single legible meaning.
5. **Concrete physical anchors.** Mountains, pantry, train-side shacks, the house, snow on mountains, adobe houses by a river. Place always present.
6. **Licensed grammatical deviation.** All six break rules in specific motivated ways. The deviations are part of the voice.

## Author Pairing Notes

When intra-sentence splicing combines authors, certain pairings have natural compatibilities:

- **Tolkien parataxis + Mistry sensory detail** — landscape frame holding working-life specificity
- **Tolkien deep-time + Márquez compression** — chronicled scale meeting generational fold
- **Ishiguro hedging + Morrison punch** — restraint released at moments of revelation
- **Tartt atmospheric + Mistry occupational** — charged interior with working-class weight
- **Márquez impossibility + Tolkien chronicle** — the supernatural delivered as documented fact
- **Morrison anaphora + Ishiguro qualifying** — repetition with growing hesitation

# Coherence Tiers — Indexed Weighting

The synthesis weights shift across five tiers indexed to player-character coherence. The voice IS the player's mental state. The disfiguration of the prose maps to the disfiguration of the character.

The central premise: threadwork disfigures presence and dislocates reality. As coherence drops, the prose becomes suspicious of its own ground — less Márquez-vibrant-magic, more Ocampo-quietly-wrong, more Borges-recursively-uncertain, more Lispector-strange-at-the-edge-of-the-unsayable.

## Coherence definitions

10 = normally bounded human consciousness. Realist-dominant prose. World coheres.
0 = no longer human consciousness. PC unplayable. Cannot be focalized; can only be observed externally.

The five playable tiers map to discrete weighting tables. Coherence 0 is below the playable threshold and uses external focalization rules.

---

## Tier 9-10 (Bounded)

PC normal, world coheres. Realist ground heavy.

| Author | Weight | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Tolkien | 28% | Ground heavy. Reality holds. Landscape, deep-time, named specificity dominant. |
| Mistry | 14% | Occupational specificity present. Body-as-record. |
| Tartt | 12% | Atmospheric retrospection. |
| Ishiguro | 12% | Situational restraint, not yet structural unreliability. |
| Márquez | 11% | Occasional time-folds, deep history. Impossibility rare. |
| Lispector | 8% | Touches at edges. Philosophical territory only. |
| Ocampo | 8% | Small uncanny moments at edges of the everyday. |
| Borges | 7% | Rare. Surfaces only at threadwork-events. |

**Deployment notes:**
- Tolkien anchors are trusted. Named places are where the PC remembers them. Landscape does narrative work.
- Ishiguro restraint is situational (character choosing not to say), not structural (narrator unable to say).
- Irreal authors (Lispector, Ocampo, Borges) surface only at threadwork-events or perceptual edges — not baseline.
- Mistry occupational detail and body-as-record are fully accessible.
- Márquez impossibility requires an external trigger (a threadwork-event, deep history). It does not occur spontaneously.

**Example markers:** Prose reads as anchored realism with occasional strangeness at the margins. A sentence about a street stays a sentence about a street. The lupins in the alley are lupins.

**Anti-patterns at this tier:**
- Deploying Borges recursion or Lispector defamiliarization without trigger
- Ocampo uncanny pervading mundane scenes
- World feeling "slightly wrong" without established cause

## Tier 7-8 (Slightly Permeable)

PC mostly bounded, occasional perceptual edges.

| Author | Weight |
|---|---|
| Tolkien | 25% |
| Ishiguro | 13% |
| Márquez | 12% |
| Tartt | 12% |
| Mistry | 12% |
| Lispector | 10% |
| Ocampo | 9% |
| Borges | 7% |

**Deployment notes:**
- Ishiguro unreliability begins to become structural — the narrator may not fully trust their own account.
- Márquez impossibility events more common, still requiring trigger.
- Lispector defamiliarization touches mundane perception at moments — a door handle feels strange under the hand, but the strangeness passes.
- Tolkien anchors still trusted but occasionally the prose hesitates before naming.

**Example markers:** Prose reads as realism that occasionally catches itself. A sentence about a street may note a quality the PC cannot place. The lupins in the alley are lupins, but for a moment they seemed to be something else.

**Anti-patterns at this tier:**
- Sustained defamiliarization (belongs to Tier 5-6)
- Named anchors becoming unreliable (belongs to Tier 3-4)
- Irreal modes dominating paragraphs without return to ground

## Tier 5-6 (Permeable)

PC perceives wrongness without external trigger.

| Author | Weight |
|---|---|
| Tolkien | 20% |
| Ishiguro | 14% |
| Márquez | 13% |
| Lispector | 13% |
| Tartt | 11% |
| Ocampo | 11% |
| Mistry | 10% |
| Borges | 8% |

**Deployment notes:**
- World starts to feel slightly wrong. Time-folds intrude without trigger.
- Body and trade specificity (Mistry) still present but contested — the PC's hands know the work but the work feels unfamiliar.
- Lispector defamiliarization touches mundane perception regularly. Objects crack open into ontological questions.
- Ocampo uncanny surfaces in scenes with no threadwork trigger — the domestic interior becomes a horror-site.
- Borges recursion begins to appear in the PC's thinking — not yet in single sentences.
- Ishiguro unreliability is now structural: the narrator cannot fully trust their own account.

**Example markers:** Prose reads as realism that has begun to doubt itself. A sentence about a street may describe a street that was not there yesterday. The lupins in the alley are lupins, but the alley is not where the PC left it.

**Anti-patterns at this tier:**
- Full dissolution (belongs to Tier 1-2)
- Named anchors completely unreliable (belongs to Tier 3-4)
- Realist ground entirely stable (belongs to Tier 9-10)

## Tier 3-4 (Disfigured)

World destabilizes; realist anchors present but unreliable.

| Author | Weight |
|---|---|
| Lispector | 16% |
| Tolkien | 14% |
| Ishiguro | 14% |
| Márquez | 14% |
| Ocampo | 13% |
| Borges | 11% |
| Tartt | 10% |
| Mistry | 8% |

**Deployment notes:**
- Named places may not be where the PC remembers. Persons may not be the persons the PC remembers.
- Tolkien anchors still present but the prose no longer trusts them. Landscape is described but the description carries suspicion.
- Ocampo's perceptual instability surfaces in scenes with no threadwork trigger — the uncanny is constant.
- Borges recursion occurs within paragraphs. The PC may notice repetition in their own thinking.
- Lispector's "this instant-now" begins to replace chronological time for stretches.
- Mistry body-and-trade detail barely accessible — the PC's hands may not recognize the work.
- Ishiguro unreliability is total: the narrator cannot be trusted at all, but presents as if trustworthy.

**Example markers:** Prose reads as realism that has been disfigured. A sentence about a street describes a street the PC recognizes but whose details are wrong. The lupins in the alley have the wrong color, or the alley has the wrong number of doors, and the prose does not remark on the wrongness.

**Anti-patterns at this tier:**
- Named anchors fully trusted (belongs to Tier 9-10)
- Complete surreal dissolution (belongs to Tier 1-2)
- The prose remarking on its own wrongness (the wrongness is unremarked; the PC does not flag it)

## Tier 1-2 (Dissolving)

Irreal-overwhelmed; surreal but still focalized through PC.

| Author | Weight |
|---|---|
| Lispector | 18% |
| Borges | 17% |
| Ocampo | 17% |
| Márquez | 14% |
| Ishiguro | 13% |
| Tolkien | 8% |
| Tartt | 8% |
| Mistry | 5% |

**Deployment notes:**
- Ground barely present, and what's present is wrong. Body and trade barely accessible.
- Lispector's "this instant-now" becomes baseline temporality. Chronological time is abandoned.
- Borges recursion occurs within single sentences. The prose may contain itself.
- Ocampo's uncanny is constant. The domestic is the horrific. The mundane is the surreal.
- Ishiguro's unreliable narrator becomes the only narrator possible — but the narrator no longer presents as trustworthy.
- Tolkien anchors appear as fragments — a name surfaces, a landscape detail, but detached from its referent.
- Márquez impossibility is baseline — everything is impossible, so the matter-of-fact tone works less hard.
- Present-tense may dominate. Defamiliarization is the default mode.

**Example markers:** Prose reads as surreal but still focalized through a recognizable consciousness. The lupins in the alley are not lupins. The alley is not an alley. But someone is perceiving this, and that someone is the PC.

**Anti-patterns at this tier:**
- Stable realist ground anywhere
- Named anchors reliable
- Coherent chronological time
- The prose ceasing to be focalized through the PC (that is Coherence 0)

---

## Coherence 0 — External Focalization

PC is no longer human consciousness. Cannot be focalized. Unplayable.

Focalization shifts to:
- **Companion:** a party member observes the PC from outside. The companion's coherence determines the prose texture for their perspective.
- **Chronicler:** one of the four canonical chroniclers describes what happened to the PC. The chronicler's perspective and vocabulary apply.
- **Environmental observer:** the prose focalizes through the place itself — the room, the street, the weather. Tolkien landscape-as-agent becomes the only available ground.

The PC at Coherence 0 is an object in someone else's focalization, not a subject.

---

## Tier Transitions

When coherence changes within a scene (the PC enters a thread-event that drops coherence from 6 to 3 over the scene's duration):

1. The transition is rendered through per-paragraph weighting shifts. No hard cut between tiers.
2. The shift should be perceptible to the reader as increasing strangeness without announcement.
3. Realist anchors degrade gradually — a named place that was reliable at the scene's opening becomes unreliable by the scene's close.
4. Irreal techniques increase in frequency across paragraphs. A paragraph may be 70% Tier 5-6 at the scene's start and 70% Tier 3-4 by the scene's end.
5. The transition itself is not named or remarked upon. The prose does not say "things are getting stranger." Things get stranger.
6. The physical event being rendered remains the same. Only the PC's perception of it shifts.

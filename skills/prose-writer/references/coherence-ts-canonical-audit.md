# Coherence and Thread Sensitivity in Prose — Canonical Audit

**Sources verified:** Amendment 01 (Self-Rendering), Amendment 02 (Leap Mechanism), threadwork_v30 §§2.1-2.6 (Operations), §§3.1-3.7 (Coherence), Jordan's in-session clarifications (rendering as dual-facing; TS articulability gradient).

---

## I. What Rendering Is

Rendering has two structural facings (Amendment 02 §1):

**Reflexive facing:** The structure that produces the practitioner's intelligibility to themselves — the ongoing presentation of their own threads to themselves as "I am this person, in this time, in this world." This is what the Leap suspends. This is what coherence loss degrades.

**Outward facing:** The structure by which the practitioner's configuration is rendered to external observers — the practitioner as something other beings can perceive and integrate. This persists residually even during the Leap (observers describe the leaping practitioner as "off," "present but elsewhere," glazed).

Rendering also has (per Jordan) an epistemic and ontological character:

**Epistemic:** Transforming the empirical givenness of the base world into something understandable. Time structures into sequence. Causation orders events. Identity holds.

**Ontological:** The individual as thread-substrate and possibilities perduring and belonging in their environment and with others.

The reflexive facing serves the epistemic dimension (the PC processing the world into intelligible experience). The outward facing serves the ontological dimension (the PC belonging in the shared world so others can perceive them consistently). Both degrade with coherence loss.

### 7-5 (Dissonant)

**Canon:** "Subtle uncanniness. The practitioner's thread-configuration has shifted enough that others' renderings work harder to process them. Temporal micro-slippages: actions fractionally ahead of or behind the moment. Responses that address what you meant rather than what you said, because the practitioner's intelligibility tracks a different sequential process. Loved ones describe speaking to someone through glass. Close Knots sense wrongness." (Amendment 01 §3). Mechanical: "+1 strain per 3 sessions on Close Knots." (threadwork_v30 §3.3).

**What this means for prose:**

The PC's rendering is mostly working. The world is still legible. The prose is still fully competent fiction. What changes:

1. **Temporal micro-slippages.** An action arrives fractionally early or late. In prose: one moment where the PC hears a sound before its cause, or knows what someone will say before they finish. This is BRIEF and PASSING. The prose continues normally after. Not sustained temporal confusion — a flicker. Perhaps one per scene.

2. **Responses that address what you meant rather than what you said.** The PC's social processing anticipates or tracks differently — they respond to intent rather than content. In dialogue, this means the PC might answer the question behind the question. In interior narration, this means the PC might already be responding before the stimulus completes.

3. **Others perceive the PC as slightly off.** If externally focalized: "speaking to someone through glass." If focalized through the PC: the PC does not perceive this about themselves. They might notice that others react to them slightly oddly, or they might not notice at all.

**What does NOT happen at this tier:**
- Word-finding failure. Vocabulary is intact. Complex sentences available.
- Tautological ground-checking ("the larch was the larch," "these held"). The ground holds without needing confirmation.
- Lispector veering on mundane perceptions. The PC does not struggle to identify a chair as a chair.
- Fragmentary sentences. Passage length is full paragraphs.
- Temporal coexistence of contradictory states. That belongs at 4-3.
- Shadow wrong. That belongs at 4-3.

**What the prose texture is:** Normal fiction with one or two brief temporal hiccups per scene, and possibly an Ishiguro misidentification (the PC attributes the micro-slip to something mundane: acoustics, pattern-recognition, familiarity). The misidentification is confident. The reader catches it. The PC doesn't.

**What I got wrong in the current files:**
- Scene 5 (before revision) had sustained word-finding failure, ground-checking, Lispector veering, and tautological repetition at Coherence 7. All wrong for this tier.
- Scene 2 had a fragment closing ("the larch. The river.") — too degraded for Coherence 7.
- The coherence-tiers.md may overstate the prose effects at this tier. Need to verify the deployment notes don't import Coherence 4 techniques.

### 4-3 (Fragmented)

**Canon:** "Visibly wrong. Materiality behaves inconsistently — shadows that don't match posture, presence that occupies more space than the body, flickers between being fully in the scene and partially elsewhere. Temporality perceptibly non-linear: references to events that haven't happened, reactions before causes. Intelligibility fractures: the practitioner is apperceived differently by different observers. Two people in the same room describe the practitioner differently — not in opinion, but in what they perceived. The rendering fails to present a consistent human being." (Amendment 01 §3). Mechanical: "-1D to all social rolls. -1D to Recall-based rolls. Engine may present the character's recollection differently from what others remember." (threadwork_v30 §3.3).

**What this means for prose:**

1. **Temporal states coexist.** The PC experiences two temporal versions of an event simultaneously — the ledger on the table AND Maren carrying the ledger. Not a micro-slip. A sustained failure of temporal exclusivity. The rendering cannot enforce "this happened, then this happened." Both are happening.

2. **Memory cross-times.** The PC remembers having said something they haven't yet said. They remember a future event as a past event. The -1D Recall penalty manifests as unreliable memory in the prose — not "I can't remember" but memory presenting wrong content confidently.

3. **Materiality inconsistent.** The PC's shadow doesn't match their posture. Their presence occupies more space. They flicker between present and elsewhere. These are effects on the PC's outward facing — others perceive them this way. From the PC's focalization: they might catch the wrong shadow from the corner of their eye, or notice that their hands are somewhere they didn't put them.

4. **Named specificity requires effort.** The rendering still produces names — Greinmar, the twenty-third volume — but the reaching is visible. The name comes with a lag, or the number doesn't hold on first try.

5. **Intelligibility fracture (external focalization).** Two observers describe the PC differently. From the PC's own perspective, they don't know this is happening.

**What NOW begins at this tier that was NOT present at 7-5:**
- Similes stop being available (analogical reasoning failing)
- Analytical self-observation stops being available (meta-cognitive capacity degrading)
- Tautological ground-checking appears (Lispector: the rendering cycles through confirmations)
- The Lispector veer appears (sentences attempt to predicate and break)
- Ocampo flat-affect reporting of the PC's own wrongness appears
- Passage length shortens — the rendering cannot sustain extended paragraph structure
- The dissociative micro-episode appears — an action the PC doesn't remember performing

**What I got right:** Scene 4 (The Upper Valley) is mostly correct. The pond simile violation was caught and fixed. The tautological confirmations and broken predications are appropriate.

### Coherence 2 (Fractured)

**Canon:** Amendment 01 §3 groups 2-1 as "Fractured" phenomenologically: "Physical form manifests substrate alteration directly. Weight distribution wrong. Light interacts at a register that produces unease. Temporal existence perceptible to anyone — conversations skip, loop, proceed at inconsistent rates. Knowledge arrives chaotically, not as integrated perception. Relational propagation constant and uncontrolled."

Threadwork_v30 §3.3 separates 2 from 1 mechanically: "-2D to all social rolls. -2D to Recall-based rolls. All Knots at accelerated wrongness (+1 strain per session). Once per scene with Thread operation: Spirit TN 7 Ob 1 or lose 1 round to a dissociative episode. Certainty maximum reduced by 1 per Coherence level below 3. **Belief Co-Authorship begins:** Engine presents the practitioner's shifting perceptual framework as the character's internal voice. Player must rewrite each Belief to reflect the framework in which the categories that structure consciousness are loosening."

**What this means for prose:**

1. **Physical form manifests substrate alteration.** Weight distribution wrong — the PC's body doesn't sit right, their weight falls unevenly. Light interacts with them at a wrong register — other people feel uneasy looking at them. These are ontological-facing failures: the PC's belonging in the physical world is breaking down. In prose: Vael shifts and the wrongness shifts with her. The light in the room changes quality when she moves. Others look at her and look away.

2. **Conversations skip, loop.** Temporal processing catastrophically impaired. In prose: dialogue has gaps — Vael is in the middle of a sentence she doesn't remember starting. Maren repeats herself, or Vael hears the same sentence twice, or a response arrives without the stimulus.

3. **Knowledge arrives chaotically.** Facts are present but unsequenced, uncontextualized. In prose: Vael knows things (ledger, table, pin, chalk) but the facts stand beside each other without the connections that would make them a scene. "They were facts and they were beside each other."

4. **Belief Co-Authorship.** A conviction feels foreign — as if belonging to someone else. The categories structuring consciousness are loosening. In prose: Vael believes something about records, about order, and the belief feels borrowed. Not questioned — felt as foreign in the body.

5. **Dissociative episodes (per scene with Thread operation).** An action the PC does not remember. A scene-gap. In prose: the PC is doing something and then they are doing something else and the transition is absent.

**What distinguishes 2 from 4-3:**
- The physical form itself manifests the failure (weight, light) — at 4-3, it's shadows and presence. At 2, it's the body itself.
- Belief Co-Authorship — this is new at 2. The PC's own convictions feel foreign.
- Knowledge arrives chaotically — at 4-3, facts still have some sequential structure. At 2, facts are unsequenced.
- Passage length: very short paragraphs. The rendering cannot sustain structure.

**What I got right:** The Coherence 2 prose in the definitive composition test correctly renders weight-wrongness, belief-foreignness, and facts-beside-each-other. The haptic metaphor of the coat is borderline but passes.

### Coherence 1 (Severed)

**Canon (threadwork_v30 §3.3):** "-2D social, -2D Recall. Dissociative episodes once per scene regardless of operations (fire at scene start, not mid-operation). Involuntary perceptual events. All Knots +2 strain per session. +2 Ob on all Thread operations including the Leap (rendering barely holds; suspension is constitutively dangerous). The practitioner's rendering is barely functional. The distinction between self and world, between human and monstrous, between actual and potential, is dissolving. Not because something evil is happening to them. Because they have been outside rendering so many times that rendering no longer holds."

**What this means for prose:**

1. **Self/world boundary dissolving.** The rendering that distinguishes "this is me" from "this is the world" is failing. In prose: the syntactic distinction between subject and object, between Vael-doing and world-happening, becomes unstable. "Maren doing something and Vael doing something requires a boundary that —"

2. **Involuntary perceptual events.** The screening that rendering provides (Amendment 02 §1: "The threshold reflexive-layer-2 imposes — the screening-out of substrate as noise below the threshold of intelligible experience — is what rendering does") is no longer effective. WITHOUT TS: this is noise. Unintelligible content arriving without categories to organize it. Not perception — just arrival. In prose: "Something arrives that is not Maren and is not the words and is not the light." WITH TS: this is substrate perception arriving alongside rendering failure.

3. **Present tense dominates.** Temporal structuring has failed — past and future are no longer available as categories. The prose is trapped in the now because the rendering that would produce past-tense narration is the rendering that has failed.

4. **Names without grammar.** The rendering produces names (Tolkien fragments) but not the grammar connecting them. "Cup. Table. Maren. Pin. Book." The names refer to the things the names refer to — tautological recursion because the chain from name to referent to meaning requires categories the rendering can no longer provide.

5. **Passage length: fragments.** Not paragraphs. Not sentences in the conventional sense.

**What distinguishes 1 from 2:**
- At 2, the rendering still produces fragments of coherent structure (short paragraphs, facts that are individually correct even if unsequenced). At 1, even the individual facts are barely held.
- At 2, Belief Co-Authorship means the PC's convictions feel foreign. At 1, the concept of "conviction" itself may not be available — the categories structuring consciousness have loosened past the point where "I believe X" is a producible thought.
- At 2, dissociative episodes are per-scene with Spirit check. At 1, they fire at scene start regardless.
- At 1, the involuntary perceptual events arrive — screening has failed. At 2, the screening is degraded but still partially functional.
- At 1, the pretense of reliable narration collapses (unlike Ishiguro at higher tiers, where the narrator still presents as trustworthy). At 1, the narrator cannot even present.

## III. What Thread Sensitivity Does — Per Band

TS is orthogonal to coherence. It measures how much of the substrate the practitioner can perceive. The content perceived becomes increasingly resistant to articulation as it extends further from human rational domain.

### TS 0-9: Nothing Perceived

No substrate content in the prose. If the PC has low coherence, they experience rendering failure with zero understanding of why. The world is coming apart and they have no access to the mechanism.

In prose: pure rendering failure. No warmth from the east wall. No quality in the river. No sense of direction. Just the micro-slips, the temporal confusion, the wrong shadow — and no explanation, not even a wrong one.

### TS 10-29: Vague Unease

Cannot locate source. In a scene where Thread events are occurring (an operation, a Gap nearby, a practitioner Leaping), the PC feels vague unease. They cannot point at it. They cannot identify a direction. It's atmospheric — a feeling of wrongness or tension that they attribute to something mundane (nervousness, weather, the building, the time of year).

In prose: a sentence in which the PC feels uneasy and misattributes the unease. "The room had a quality she attributed to the low ceiling." The attribution is wrong. The reader may or may not detect this. The prose does not dwell on it.

For rendered-level events (combat, death): the PC perceives normal events with an additional emotional register — loss beyond grief, deep wrongness, nightmares. They don't know why the death affected them so strongly.

### TS 30-49 (Stirring): General Direction

Senses an operation in the scene; general direction identifiable. The PC can point — "from the east wall," "from the direction of the upper valley." They know something is there and roughly where. They cannot identify what it is, what kind of operation, or any structural details.

**The articulability level:** The content is just beyond the grasp of language. The PC might use a wrong word — "warmth," "pressure," "weight" — that traces the direction and general quality without naming the thing. The wrong word is the best the PC can produce. They could say "something in the east wall" and it would be true and insufficient and they could not add to it.

In prose: one or two sentences of noticing. The PC identifies a direction and a vague quality. They misattribute it (Ishiguro: "minerals," "acoustics," "old stonework warmth"). The prose moves on. It does not dwell. The TS perception at this level is a detail in the scene, not the scene's subject.

For rendered-level events: thread disruption visible at wound; configuration ceasing to cohere visible at death. The PC sees something happening at the substrate level during combat or death — "thread disruption at wound" — but can only identify direction and general sense.

### TS 50-69 (Attuned): Operation Type and Target

Identifies operation type and approximate target. "Restorative work, old, east wall, below floor level." The PC can name what kind of operation and roughly what it's being done to. Structural details unavailable. The architecture of the configuration is not visible — just the type and the target.

**The articulability level:** The facts of the perception are articulable — "restorative weaving, old, below the east wall." What resists articulation is the quality of the perception. The PC can report what they perceived but cannot convey what it was like to perceive it. The "leaning-toward," the body-knowing — these are in a register the PC can gesture toward but cannot enter with language.

In prose: the PC identifies what they're perceiving and can discuss it in approximate terms. The perception integrates into the scene — the PC processes it as information, not as numinous encounter. The quality of the perception — the felt experience of sensing the substrate — may produce a sentence that veers or reaches without arriving, but the factual content is available.

For rendered-level events: wound depth visible (surface vs structural). Full thread-state dissolution visible at death. Scope and casualties through substrate visible at mass casualty events. Full Gap readable.

### TS 70+ (Sensitive/Resonant): Full Configuration

Perceives the full configuration being worked. The complete architecture — every strand, every stratum, the age, the intent, the history. Nothing hidden at this perceptual range.

**The articulability level:** The facts are clear. The architecture is legible. What resists articulation is the content's distance from human rational categories. At TS 70, the content includes structural-scale configurations that have no human analogue. The practitioner perceives them clearly but the concepts required to express them in human language don't exist. The perception is real and precise. The expression is not possible — not because the practitioner's language fails, but because the content is genuinely beyond human conceptual reach.

At TS 90+, the content includes aspects that are wholly alien to human rationality. The practitioner perceives them. There are no words. Not because words are insufficient — because what the words would need to refer to has no referent in the human conceptual framework.

In prose: the factual structure is rendered with precision (Borges: scholarly apparatus applied to impossible content). The aspects that resist articulation are rendered through silence — the prose describes the architecture and then, at the point where the content exceeds human categories, the prose stops or veers. The stopping is not announced. The prose simply reaches the edge of what can be said and is silent.

For rendered-level events: complete structural perception. The dying configuration's complete state visible. Every individual disruption perceivable at mass casualty. Full forensic + temporal trace at Gap. Can intervene at rendering crisis.

## IV. The Interaction Matrix

### High Coherence + Low TS

Normal prose. Normal perception. No substrate content. This is the baseline — realist fiction about a practitioner who has been outside rendering but whose rendering has fully recovered.

### High Coherence + Moderate TS (30-49)

Normal prose with a detail. The PC notices something from a direction and misattributes it. One or two sentences. The prose moves on. The TS perception is a fact in the scene, not the scene's subject. The PC can name the direction and a wrong quality. They cannot name the thing.

### High Coherence + High TS (70+)

Normal prose with integrated substrate perception. The PC perceives the full architecture and can partially articulate it. The prose renders the articulable parts with Borgesian precision. At the boundary of articulability — where the content exceeds human categories — the prose reaches and either: applies precision to something that turns out to be impossible (Borges), veers without arriving (Lispector), or stops and closes on the nearest concrete thing (Wittgenstein).

The key: at high coherence, the PC's rendering is intact. Their language is fully functional. Their analytical capacity is intact. The articulability limit is in the CONTENT, not in the PC's capacity. The content is beyond human conceptual reach. The PC is fully capable — the thing they're trying to describe is not fully human-describable.

### Low Coherence + Low TS

Pure rendering failure. The PC is coming apart and has no idea why. The temporal slips, the wrong shadow, the facts that won't sequence — all inexplicable. No warmth from the east wall. No quality in the river. No hint of mechanism. The prose renders a person whose cognitive architecture is failing without any compensating insight.

This is the most disorienting combination for the reader. The world is normal. The PC is disintegrating. There is no transcendence, no substrate beauty, no compensatory depth. Just breakdown.

### Low Coherence + Moderate TS (30-49)

Rendering failure AND vague substrate perception overlapping. The PC cannot distinguish between their rendering failing and their TS perceiving. The warmth from the east wall might be a temporal micro-slip or it might be a genuine TS detection — the PC cannot separate these because the apparatus for distinguishing "my processing is off" from "I am sensing something real" requires rendering that is degrading.

In prose: the Ishiguro misidentification becomes unstable. The PC commits to an explanation but the commitment itself wobbles. They can't tell if the warmth is minerals or something else because they can't tell if the warmth is real or their rendering producing noise.

### Low Coherence + High TS (70+)

The extreme case. Clear substrate perception persists because TS does not require rendering (TS is built through confrontation, not through rendering; and passive TS perception operates alongside rendering, not through it). Meanwhile, the rendering that would connect perception to language is failing.

Result: the unrendered is clearer than the rendered. The substrate architecture is precise and legible. The table, the chair, the names — these require rendering, and rendering is failing. The configuration beneath the east wall does not require rendering — it is perceived directly.

But the articulation fails. Not because the content is beyond human conceptual reach (that's the high-coherence-high-TS problem). Because the architecture that connects perception to language — the grammar, the categories, the sequential structure of speech — is the architecture that rendering provides and rendering has failed.

Same result (no articulation), different cause. At high coherence + high TS: the content resists. At low coherence + high TS: the apparatus collapses.

In prose: the perception is stated simply — "the knots are there," "she perceives it" — and the attempt to say more fails structurally. The sentence breaks. The word for the thing is the thing.

## V. What I Got Wrong

1. **Coherence 7 was overwritten as Coherence 5.** Scenes at Coherence 7 had ground-checking, tautological confirmation, word-finding failure, Lispector veering, and fragment closings. None of these belong at 7. At 7, the prose is mostly normal with one or two temporal micro-slips.

2. **TS 35 was overwritten as TS 55.** At TS 35, the PC senses a direction and a vague quality. Period. They don't struggle to name it across multiple sentences. They notice it, misattribute it, and move on. Scene 5 (pre-revision) had sustained exploration of the river-quality across four paragraphs. At TS 35, this is two or three sentences.

3. **The articulability gradient was flattened.** I treated all TS levels as "the thing resists naming." But the resistance is different at each level. At 30-49: just beyond grasp — a wrong word traces the direction. At 50-69: the facts are articulable but the quality resists. At 70+: the architecture is clear but the content exceeds human conceptual reach. These are three different prose problems requiring three different techniques.

4. **Coherence 2 and 1 were sometimes blurred.** 2 has Belief Co-Authorship (convictions feel foreign) and dissociative episodes per scene with Spirit check. 1 has the self/world distinction dissolving and involuntary perceptual events. These are different textures — 2 is "the facts don't connect and my beliefs feel borrowed," 1 is "the boundary between me and the world is dissolving and noise is arriving."

5. **I sometimes treated TS perception as numinous or transcendent.** The canon does not frame TS perception as transcendent. TS perception is perception — the same way seeing is perception. It perceives the substrate the way eyes perceive light. At high TS and high coherence, the perception integrates into the scene as information. It is not mystical. It is what the PC sees. The articulability challenge at high TS is a fact about the content's relationship to human conceptual categories, not a spiritual experience.

6. **The "world does not change" principle needs qualification.** The world as rendered does not change. But other people's rendering of the PC does change — at Coherence 7, loved ones describe speaking through glass. At Coherence 4, two observers perceive the PC differently. At Coherence 2, the PC's physical form manifests substrate alteration — others see weight wrong, light wrong. These are effects on the outward facing of the PC's rendering, visible to others. The world is unchanged. The PC's presence in the world changes.

## VI. Revised Rules for the Prose-Writer Skill

### Per-band prose constraints:

**10-8:** Full capacity. Normal fiction. No rendering failure. No ground-checking.

**7-5:** Normal fiction with 1-2 temporal micro-slips per scene. No word-finding failure. No tautological confirmation. No fragment closings. No veering. Full paragraphs. Similes available. The micro-slip is brief, the misidentification is confident, the prose moves on.

**4-3:** Similes stop. Analytical self-observation stops. Tautological confirmation begins. The veer begins. Temporal states coexist. Memory cross-times. Shadow wrong. Passage length shortens. The rendering is visibly failing but the PC can still name things (with effort) and produce short coherent passages.

**2:** Physical form manifestation (weight, light). Facts unsequenced. Belief Co-Authorship. Dissociative episodes. Very short paragraphs. Closing on isolated detail.

**1:** Self/world boundary dissolving. Present tense. Names without grammar. Involuntary perceptual events (noise without TS, substrate with TS). Fragments. The last-simile rule. Closing on isolated noun.

### Per-TS prose constraints:

**0-9:** No substrate content. Period.

**10-29:** Vague unease attributed to mundane cause. One sentence. Prose does not dwell.

**30-49:** Direction and vague quality. Wrong name for the quality. 2-3 sentences. Ishiguro misidentification. Prose moves on.

**50-69:** Operation type and approximate target identifiable. Factual content articulable. Quality of perception resists description. The facts are prose; the felt-quality may produce one veer.

**70+:** Full architecture perceived. Precision applied (Borges). At the limit of human conceptual reach, the prose stops or veers — the content is what resists, not the PC's capacity.

### Interaction constraints:

**Same coherence + different TS:** The coherence texture holds constant. TS adds a layer of perception on top. A Coherence 7 passage at TS 0 is normal prose. The same passage at TS 35 is normal prose plus 2-3 sentences of directional perception. The same passage at TS 70 is normal prose plus integrated substrate architecture. The COHERENCE texture does not change based on TS.

**Same TS + different coherence:** The TS perception holds constant (TS is orthogonal). What changes is the PC's ability to process and articulate the perception. A TS 70 PC at Coherence 9 articulates the architecture with Borgesian precision. The same PC at Coherence 4 perceives the same architecture but the articulation fragments. The same PC at Coherence 1 perceives clearly and articulates nothing.

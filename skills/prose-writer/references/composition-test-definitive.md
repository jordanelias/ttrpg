## Coherence 10-8 (Stable). TS 0. Focalization: Vael.

The side door opened and the room's shadow shifted. Maren came in carrying a ledger under her arm, the binding new — linen-thread over quarter-calf, the spine tooled with the parish arms. She had chalk dust on her sleeves and a pin still caught in the fold of her apron. The ledger she set on the table was the Greinmar Poor Relief, the twenty-third volume; Vael could see the embossed year on the cover, and the ink of the previous scribe's entries through the endpapers where the paste had not yet dried.

"The binding's tight enough now," Maren said, and pulled the pin from her apron and set it beside the book. She did not sit. Her hands went to the table's edge and she stood there as someone stands who has come for one purpose and will leave when it is done. Outside, the lane ran south toward the market cross, and from the open door came the smell of cut hay from the fields beyond the wall.

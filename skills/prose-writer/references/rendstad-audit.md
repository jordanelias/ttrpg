## Scene 1: Arrival (Coherence 9, TS 0)

### Techniques Deployed

**Tolkien (ground):**
- Named specificity: Gransol, Rendstad, Hafenmark, larch. ✓
- Deep-time absent but appropriate — this is an arrival, not a historical scene. The three/four-season stumps gesture at recent history without deep-time. ✓
- Landscape-first: the road, the larch, the stumps, the valley opening before the settlement. Wide-to-narrow. ✓
- Parataxis at marked moments: "twenty houses, a mill on the river, a timbered customs-house" — catalogue as incantation. ✓ Hypotaxis elsewhere. ✓

**Mistry:**
- Accretive: birch-twig broom, calf-leather binding, worn through in three places. Each sentence adds one particular. ✓
- Occupational language: tallow, spruce sap, valley water. ✓
- Body-as-record: "the hem was not yet stained from the road" — the cassock tells the assessor's inexperience. ✓

**Tartt:**
- Loaded object: "the difference between a book that had been opened a thousand times and a book that had been opened perhaps six times. This ledger had been opened six times." The ledger carries the weight of institutional inexperience without the prose announcing it. ✓
- Atmospheric immersion: tallow, spruce sap, sourness of beer brewed with valley water. ✓

**Ishiguro:**
- "He had the face of a man who had been told what to expect and was expecting it." — Free indirect discourse. The assessment is focalized through Vael's reading of the assessor's face. Vael's judgment ("told what to expect"), not the narrator's. ✓

**Interpolation quality:** Four authors in one passage. Tolkien grounds the landscape, Mistry accumulates occupational detail, Tartt loads the ledger, Ishiguro reads the assessor's face. Transitions invisible. ✓

**Concerns:**
- "the roof appeared to be weeping" — is this a decorative simile? No — it's a factual description of rust stains. The "appeared to be weeping" is Vael's perception (focalized), not the narrator's metaphor. The rust genuinely makes vertical stain lines. **Borderline — the word "weeping" anthropomorphizes.** At Coherence 9, Vael's rendering can produce this kind of observation. ## Scene 2: The Sub-Prefect (Coherence 7, TS 0)

- Confident misidentification: "She was quite certain this was simply the quality of having heard similar arguments before. It was pattern-recognition, nothing more." Commits to a wrong explanation. Certainty formula ("quite certain"). Dismisses the evidence ("nothing more"). ✓

**Mistry:**

**Tolkien:**

**Lispector (at edges):**

- Vael's rendering slip: "a moment arriving before its cause, a sentence assembling itself in her hands before the speaker's mouth had finished." The temporal micro-slippage described in embodied terms (hands, mouth) rather than analytical terms. ✓

## Scene 3: Assessment Record (Church Chronicler)

### Techniques Deployed

**Borges (dominant — scholarly apparatus):**
- Precise documentation: "seventh day of the month," "form C-14, seventh edition," "Concordat of the Fifth Renewal (Article 9, §3)." Apparatus flawless. ✓
- Apparatus noting its own gaps: "whose name is withheld per standard procedure" — institutional silence documented. ✓
- Apparatus applied to contested content: "curative acts of an uncanonical nature — specifically, the application of botanical preparations and manual techniques to ailments that the complainant observed responding at a rate and completeness inconsistent with the natural pharmacological properties of the materials employed." The bureaucratic precision describes something the Church does not understand and will not investigate honestly. The apparatus holds. What it describes exceeds the apparatus's categories. ✓

**Ocampo (flat-affect institutional):**
- "This refusal is noted without editorial comment." — The Church Chronicler's flat affect. The chronicler does not editorialize on the jurisdictional refusal. The phrase "without editorial comment" is itself editorial (it signals the Chronicler's awareness that comment might be expected) without providing the comment. ✓
- "The assessment was not completed. The subject, Ilse of Rendstad, was not interviewed." — Three facts. No affect. ✓

**Tolkien (in the naming):**
- "Rendstad (T7, Hafenmark Highlands)." Named specificity inside institutional frame. ✓

**Mistry (in the body):**
- The assessment kit: "the Compliance Ledger (form C-14, seventh edition), a copy of the *Compendium of Suppressed Practices* (abridged, fieldwork edition), and a sealed letter of authority." Occupational inventory. Each item specific. ✓

### Anti-Pattern Check

| # | Rule | Status |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cliché | ✓ None. Institutional register is precise. |
| 5 | Lack of specificity | ✓ Everything named and numbered. |
| 10 | Register flattening | ✓ The Church Chronicler's register is distinctly institutional-bureaucratic. Different from Kalder's speech, different from Vael's interiority. |
| 23 | Editorial stance without focalization | ✓ The Chronicler IS the focalization. "Without editorial comment" is the Chronicler's own institutional stance. |
| 25 | Mechanical-term leakage | ✓ "T7" appears — this is the Chronicler using institutional designation, not Vael's focalization. Appropriate for chronicle-mode. |
| 26 | Flat-affect omniscience | ✓ Not omniscient — Church Chronicler with specific institutional perspective and Certainty 5. |

**Concern:** The passage is competent institutional prose but may lack the Borgesian quality of the apparatus undermining itself. The Concordat citation (Article 9, §3) and the Parliamentary Precedent 41 are presented as competing authorities — but neither is undermined. The passage presents a jurisdictional deadlock without the scholarly apparatus revealing its own inadequacy to the situation. The deadlock is factual, not paradoxical.

**Fix direction:** Add a line where the Chronicler's apparatus encounters something it cannot categorize. E.g., the complainant's report "responding at a rate and completeness inconsistent with the natural pharmacological properties" — the Chronicler has documented something that exceeds the Church's own framework (natural pharmacology) without noting that the framework's inadequacy is the point. This is already present implicitly. Explicitly? The apparatus should not confess the inadequacy (Wittgenstein). It should document the fact and move on. It already does this. **Pass — the undermining is implicit in the phrase "inconsistent with the natural pharmacological properties." The Chronicler documents the inconsistency without investigating what would make it consistent.**

**Scene 3 verdict:** Pass. Strong chronicle-mode. The Borgesian self-undermining is implicit rather than explicit — appropriate for a Church Chronicler with Certainty 5, who would not notice the inadequacy.

## Scene 4: The Upper Valley (Coherence 4, TS 0)

### Techniques Deployed

**Lispector (elevated — categorical failure):**
- "she had decided to go. The decision had been a decision and it had been hers and she had made it." — Tautological confirmation of agency. The rendering must confirm that the decision was a decision, that it was hers. At Coherence 4, agency requires confirmation. ✓
- "the leaves were doing something that leaves do not do, or that these leaves did, or that the water was doing and the leaves were in" — Three attempts to categorize what Ilse is doing. None succeed. The accumulation of wrong names traces the shape. ✓
- "the way the surface of a pond is part of what the pond is doing beneath the surface, except that this comparison required a — she had — the comparison was." — **SIMILE AT COHERENCE 4. VIOLATION OF RULE #50.** The simile begins, then the prose enacts the collapse of the analogical apparatus — "except that this comparison required a — she had — the comparison was." The comparison breaks. This is structurally similar to the "last simile" at Coherence 1. But the rule says no similes below Coherence 4. This IS Coherence 4. **FAIL.** The simile should not begin at all at this tier. The attempt-and-collapse is the Test 15 technique for Coherence 1, not Coherence 4.
- **Fix:** Cut the simile entirely. Let "the stillness of someone whose hands are doing something that the stillness is part of" stand alone. The reader can feel the concept without the pond comparison. Or: since this is at the boundary (Coherence 4, not 3), deploy the simile but note it's at the threshold of availability — the rendering produces one more comparison and it's effortful. **Decision: revise. The simile-attempt-and-collapse is a Coherence 1 technique misdeployed at Coherence 4.**

**Ocampo (belonging-failure):**
- "Her expression was. Her expression was the expression of." — The sentence reaches for what the expression expresses and cannot complete. Flat-affect rendering of the failure. ✓
- Closing: "The leaves. The water. The bark smell. The light through the door. These." — Final word is the demonstrative pronoun without a predicate. The rendering produces the pointing ("these") but not what the pointing points at. ✓

**Ishiguro (structural unreliability):**
- "Vael remembered having said something back, something correct, though the sentence she remembered was a sentence she had not yet — she had said it." — Memory presents a future sentence as past. The contradiction is not remarked upon. ✓
- "The twenty-third volume. Or. The number on the cover." — The "Or" stands alone as a sentence. The rendering cannot choose between "the twenty-third volume" (which requires holding the ordinal) and "the number on the cover" (which is just a visual). ✓

**Tolkien (fragmentary at this tier):**
- Named specificity degraded: Ilse's name holds. The path holds. But landscape-framing is minimal — "the smoke from the camp was behind her and also around her because the valley held smoke the way valleys hold —" The landscape detail breaks. ✓

## Scene 5: What the River Knows (Coherence 7, TS 35)

### Techniques Deployed

**Ishiguro (confident misidentification of TS perception):**
- "She was quite certain the quality was the mineral content of the water." Commits to wrong identification. ✓
- "Highland water had minerals. Minerals could produce a sensation that the skin interpreted as warmth." Rationalization sequence. The reasoning is plausible and wrong. ✓
- "This was the explanation, and it was a satisfactory explanation, and the quality continued from the direction of the upper valley and the minerals did not explain the direction." — The final clause undermines the explanation. Vael registers this — the minerals don't explain directionality — but she has already committed to the explanation and does not revise. The evidence against the explanation is present in the same sentence as the commitment. The narrator does not notice the contradiction. ✓

**Lispector (at edges — TS perception):**
- "Not warmer. It had a quality that she identified as warmth because warmth was the closest — it was the closest." Veer. Tries "warmer," corrects, tries "a quality she identified as warmth," then the tautological loop: "warmth was the closest — it was the closest." The repetition marks the limit of naming. ✓

**Tolkien (ground):**
- Landscape: the river, the larch, the path, the valley. Named, specific, holding. ✓
- Closing: "The customs-house pennant was leaning east. The smoke from the timber camp was in the valley. These held." The landscape persists. ✓

**TS perception (TS 35 — Stirring):**
- Direction identifiable ("from the direction of the upper valley"), quality partially identifiable (warmth — wrong name, right direction). Consistent with TS 30-49 visibility table. ✓
- No substrate vocabulary. No "configuration," "knots," "threads." Vael doesn't have the TS to perceive at that level of specificity. She perceives a direction and a quality. ✓

## Failures and Revisions Required

1. **Scene 4: Pond simile at Coherence 4.** Rule #50 violation. Cut the simile.
2. **Scene 1: "the roof appeared to be weeping."** Borderline purple prose (#3). Consider cutting "appeared to be weeping" → "so that the roof had vertical stains."
3. **Scene 1: "particular sourness."** "Particular" adds little. Cut.
4. **Scene 4: Simile-attempt-and-collapse is a Coherence 1 technique.** At Coherence 4, the rendering should simply fail to produce the comparison — not begin it and collapse. The collapse is too dramatic for the tier.

## Strengths

1. **Five distinct focalizations** (Vael × 4 coherence levels + Church Chronicler) each producing recognizably different prose from the same world.
2. **The assessor's ledger** (Scene 1) and **Maren's ledger** (earlier tests) create an implicit comparison — new institutional instrument vs. worn community record — without the prose drawing the comparison.
3. **Kalder's speech veer** ("the word does not") is Lispector deployed inside dialogue, not narration — a technique not tested in the battery.
4. **Scene 5's Ishiguro misidentification of TS perception** is the strongest new finding — Vael commits to "minerals" while the directionality undermines the explanation within the same sentence, and she does not revise.
5. **The arc as a whole** dramatizes the collision of institutional frameworks (Church compliance, Hafenmark sovereignty, RM heritage) without the prose taking a side. The aporia holds.

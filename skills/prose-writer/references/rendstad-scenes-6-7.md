# Rendstad Arc — Scenes 6-7: The Former Warden (Revised)

*Edda. Former Warden. Upper valley above Rendstad, three years. Coherence 0 eighteen months ago. TS 72 — reconstituted. Still visits. Still goes to the old sites. Still speaks, when spoken to. Her face is the same face. Ilse still brings her food.*

---

## Scene 6. The Path to Edda's.
*Focalization: Vael. Coherence 7. TS 35.*

Ilse led and Vael followed and the path from Ilse's house went further up the valley, narrower, the grass less worn. The larch thinned and gave way to birch and then to a low grey scrub that smelled of something mineral.

"She's been here since the third winter," Ilse said. "Before that she was at the monastery. Before that I don't know."

Edda's house was a herder's shelter — one room, a hearth, a door that faced south. The door was open. Inside: a table, a chair, a blanket folded on the floor with a precision that did not belong to the rest of the room.

Edda was sitting outside on a stone. Heavy wool coat, Warden-issue, the hem repaired in three places with thread that did not match. She was looking at the valley.

Vael stopped. The afternoon was mild and the sun was on the stone where Edda sat and Vael was cold. The cold was from the direction of Edda, the way the warmth at the river had been from the direction of the upper valley. She was quite certain this was the altitude — the shelter was higher than Ilse's house, and the air at altitude was — she was cold. The sun was on the stone.

"Edda," Ilse said.

Edda turned her head. The scar above the left eyebrow, the jaw, the grey at the temples — the face Ilse had described. Vael looked at it and looked away. She could not have said what made her look away. The face was fine. The features were where features should be. She looked at the scrub instead.

"This is Vael," Ilse said.

"Yes," Edda said.

Ilse sat beside Edda on the stone. Vael remained on the path. The cold continued. The sun was on the stone.

## Scene 7. The Same Encounter.
*Focalization: Tamar (Warden, Coherence 10, TS 75). Arriving an hour later.*

Tamar came up the path and saw Edda on the stone and Ilse beside her and the woman from the south standing on the path at thirty paces, which was the distance people kept without knowing why they kept it.

She had felt Edda from the ridge. The work Edda was doing to hold herself in place was the kind of work Tamar knew from the inside — the same patience she used when she closed a wound in the field, the slow careful turning of what was wrong back toward what it had been. Except Edda was doing it along every part of herself, all the time, and what she was turning things back toward was not what they had been. What she was turning things back toward was whatever she was now.

Tamar could follow the broad shape of it. Down the spine, branching at the shoulders, at the hips, running along the arms and legs to the hands and feet. She had seen this in diagrams her mentor had drawn in the dirt outside Askeheim — this is how a body holds, here and here and here, these are the lines. Edda's lines were the lines in the diagrams. The technique was Warden technique. Tamar could read it the way she could read her own handwriting.

At the temples, at the base of the skull, at the sternum — something else. Denser. Faster. Tamar could see that it was happening and she could see that it was not random and she could not — it was the same kind of thing as the work along the spine except that it was not. It moved in ways that the work along the spine did not move. She had spent nine years learning to read this and she had never seen this. Her mentor had not drawn this in the dirt.

Below that — below all of it — where the being that Edda was met the ground that all being comes from, Tamar perceived — she perceived Edda. Not the work. Not the technique. Not the lines or the movements. Edda. The amount of Edda that was there was more than the amount of Edda that should have been there. As if the place where Edda was sitting contained not only the Edda who was sitting there but also — Tamar looked at the valley. She looked at the birch. She looked at the scrub.

"Tamar," Edda said. And the air between them shifted — it was like entering a room someone had just warmed, except that no one had warmed it, and the warming was not warmth but the thing that warmth is a wrong name for. Tamar had felt this before: the moment after she closed a wound, when the place where the wound had been became easier to stand near. Edda had done this to the path between them. She had done it in the time it took to say a name.

"Edda," Tamar said. "You look well."

This was what Wardens said. Edda received it the way a wall receives sunlight — it arrived and it was on her and she did not do anything with it.

"The rain will come from the west," Edda said. Tamar looked west. The sky was clear. She looked at what Edda was doing and Edda was reading the valley — the small shifts that come before weather, the same shifts Tamar read when she worked the margins near Askeheim, except that those shifts told her where the damage was and these shifts told Edda where the rain was and the reading was the same reading applied to a different question.

The rain would come from the west. Tamar believed this.

Ilse brought water in a cup. Edda held it with both hands. The water did not move.

## Audit

### Scene 6 (revised)

**System vocabulary:** Zero. No "configuration," "substrate," "rendering," "coherence," "thread." Vael's vocabulary: cold, direction, altitude, sun, stone, face, path, scrub. ✓

**TS 35 perception:** "Vael was cold. The cold was from the direction of Edda." Direction identified, quality named wrongly ("cold" — it's not thermal cold, but cold is the closest word Vael has). Ishiguro misidentification: "She was quite certain this was the altitude." Commits. Wrong. The sun contradicts in the same paragraph. Vael does not notice. ✓

**The face:** Vael looks at Edda's face and looks away. "She could not have said what made her look away. The face was fine. The features were where features should be." Normal human processing — she sees the face, feels uneasy, can't place why, looks away. At C7, this is a normal reaction to something unsettling, not a rendering failure. The prose does not explain what's wrong with the face. Vael herself doesn't know. The reader infers from context (Edda is a Coherence 0 being; her presence exceeds what rendering can comfortably contain). ✓

**Edda's "yes":** One word. No confirmation that it was "the right word" or that it "did what yes does." At C7, Vael hears "yes" and it's "yes." The scene moves on. ✓

**Compared to previous versions:** The tautological cycling ("the face was the face Ilse had described"), the word-confirmation ("it did what the word yes does"), and the repetitive looking ("she looked at the scrub, she looked at the path, she looked at the blanket") have been removed. These were C4 textures misdeployed at C7. The revised scene reads as normal prose with one uneasy moment (looking away from the face) and one TS 35 perception (the cold from Edda's direction). This is correct for the tier.

The deepest level is rendered as a sentence that breaks and a Warden looking at birch trees.

**The C0 NPC's alien-process actions:**

"The air between them shifted — it was like entering a room someone had just warmed." Tamar perceives the effect in her own sensory vocabulary. She recognizes the TECHNIQUE (it's what happens after she closes a wound) but the APPLICATION is new (Edda applied it to conversational space, not to a wound). **"Edda received it the way a wall receives sunlight":** Simile. At Coherence 10, fully available. The simile is specific and apt — it describes reception without processing. A wall doesn't do anything with sunlight. Edda doesn't do anything with the courtesy. ✓

**"The water did not move."** Four words. Closing. The tell. ✓

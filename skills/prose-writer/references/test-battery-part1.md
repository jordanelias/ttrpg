# Test Battery — Part 1: Individual Author Mechanisms

Each test: prose exercising one author's rules, then self-audit. Honest. What works, what fails, what's ambiguous.

Governing maxim: *Whereof one cannot speak, thereof one must be silent.*

---

## Test 1. Tolkien — Asterisk Reality

*Vael approaches a ruin on the Greinmar headland. Something happened here.*

The wall ran east along the headland for thirty paces and then stopped. Not fallen — stopped. The stones at the termination were dressed the same as the stones at the beginning, and the mortar between them was old but not failed. Someone had built a wall that ended here, in open air, with the same care they had used at the corner. Beyond the wall's end the grass grew shorter than on the leeward side, and no lupins. The soil was darker. Vael stood where the wall stopped and looked east, over ground that had been cleared of something, or that nothing had chosen to grow on, and the wind from the sea came through the gap that was not a gap because the wall had not been broken. It had been built to end here. The grass was short and the ground was dark and the wind came through.

**Audit:**
- Asterisk reality: ✓. The thing is never named. Wall, ground, absent lupins, darker soil all imply. Reader assembles the absence.
- No meta-description: ✓. No "there was something she could not identify." Prose describes the wall, the grass, the soil. Stops.
- Named specificity: ✓. Thirty paces, dressed stones, mortar.
- Paratactic close at marked register: ✓. Three facts, no interpretation.
- **Ambiguity:** "ground that had been cleared of something, or that nothing had chosen to grow on" — the "or" is analytical. Two interpretations offered. If focalized through Vael, it's her uncertainty. If narrator-analytical, it's meta. Leaning pass — the phrasing reads as Vael standing there, considering. But borderline.
- **Risk:** The passage is competent and atmospheric. Is it too comfortable? Tolkien at his best has a quality of weight that comes from deep-time — the ruin implying the civilization that built it implying the ages before. This passage gestures at that ("old but not failed") but doesn't deploy it fully. The wall needs history. "The second charter" or "the Einhir period" — a name that extends time backward. Without it, the ruin is any ruin.
- **Verdict:** Pass with reservation. Needs deeper temporal anchoring.

## Test 2. Ishiguro — Confident Misidentification

*Vael at Coherence 7 perceives temporal slippage. She identifies it as something else.*

The sound of the ledger on the table arrived, Vael believed, at the moment Maren set it down, and if she had heard something similar a moment earlier — a kind of anticipation of the sound, though not the sound itself — she was fairly certain this was simply the quality of the room's acoustics, which were, as she recalled, somewhat unusual owing to the low ceiling and the plaster's age. She had noticed this quality before. It was the kind of room in which sounds preceded themselves slightly, in the way that an echo might, though of course an echo follows rather than precedes, and she did not think the comparison was especially apt, but it was the nearest she could find and she was satisfied with it.

**Audit:**
- Covert unreliability: ✓. Commits to "the room's acoustics." Wrong. She doesn't know it's wrong.
- Certainty formulas: ✓. "She believed," "fairly certain," "satisfied with it." Each signals the opposite.
- Repair sequence: ✓. "Though of course an echo follows rather than precedes" — corrects her own comparison, the correction reveals the problem (the sound PRECEDED), she dismisses the implication.
- Walkowitz quantity-violation: ✓. Long sentence, little information. The length is the evasion.
- **Risk:** "sounds preceded themselves slightly" — almost too acute. But Stevens notices everything and misinterprets it. The observation is sharp; the explanation is wrong. This is the gap.
- **Risk:** The passage is one long sentence of self-justification. Ishiguro frequently gives Stevens paragraph-length justifications that loop and re-approach. The structure is right. But it might be TOO Ishiguro — in the synthesis, this would need splicing with other authors to avoid single-author dominance.
- **Verdict:** Pass. Strong Ishiguro. Would need tempering in synthesis.

## Test 3. Lispector — The Veer

*Vael at Coherence 5, TS 0, tries to identify a sensation in her own hand.*

Her left hand was — she knew what her left hand was. She had used it that morning to hold the cup and the cup had been there and the hand had been around it. But now the hand was on the table and the hand was — it was closer to a memory of a hand than to the hand itself. Not a memory. A hand that had the quality of something remembered while it was still happening. The fingers were her fingers. She could feel the table under them. The feeling of the table was the feeling of a table. She sat with this and did not move because moving would require the hand to be a hand in the way that hands are hands when they do things, and the hand was a hand in some other way that she — she sat with it.

**Audit:**
- The veer: ✓. "the hand was —" attempts predication. Tries "closer to a memory." Corrects: "Not a memory." Tries again: "the quality of something remembered while it was still happening." Each attempt wrong. Accumulation traces the shape.
- Simple vocabulary: ✓. Hand, cup, table, fingers, feeling. Common words at the limit.
- No meta-description: ✓. No "she could not describe."
- The final veer: "the hand was a hand in some other way that she —" — the sentence reaches for the predicate and breaks. "She sat with it" is the landing. She does not try again. Wittgenstein: silence.
- **Risk:** "the feeling of the table was the feeling of a table" — is this too clever? Tautology as technique. In Lispector's work, tautologies function as the point where language encounters its own circularity. "A rose is a rose is a rose" (Stein, but Lispector inherits the technique). The tautology is not clever — it is the prose reaching for the thing and finding only the word. The word refers to the word. The thing is elsewhere. **Pass — but only if deployed once per passage. Repeated, it becomes a trick.**
- **Risk:** "She sat with this and did not move because moving would require the hand to be a hand in the way that hands are hands when they do things" — this is close to analytical. Vael is reasoning about why she doesn't move. At Coherence 5, is this reasoning available? The reasoning is simple (moving requires the hand to be a certain kind of hand; the hand isn't that kind) and it's embodied rather than analytical. **Borderline pass.** At Coherence 3, this reasoning would be too complex.
- **Verdict:** Pass. The veer works. The tautology works once. The embodied reasoning holds at Coherence 5.

## Test 4. Borges — Precision on the Impossible

*A codex entry describing the Einhir site-network, written in Warden Chronicler voice. TS 70+, so substrate reference is gated.*

The site-network comprised, at its maximum extent, fourteen hundred and twelve nodes distributed across the southern peninsula in a pattern whose geometry was hexagonal at the local scale and spiral at the regional, with each node positioned at a distance from its six neighbors that corresponded, in the old mensuration, to the distance a trained practitioner could sustain contact while walking. The network had operated for nine generations. Its aggregate output, measured in the units the Edeyja themselves used — which translates imprecisely as "thread-work-hours," though the Edeyja did not distinguish between work and observation in the way the term implies — exceeded by some accounts the total output of all practitioners in the four centuries since its collapse. These accounts are disputed. The units are disputed. The network is not disputed, though what it did — the sustained, generationally accumulated precision threadwork that the Church calls the cause and the Wardens call the method — is described in terms whose referents are no longer available even to practitioners who can perceive the substrate at full range.

**Audit:**
- Precision: ✓. Fourteen hundred and twelve nodes. Hexagonal at local, spiral at regional. Nine generations. Specific units. The precision is total.
- Impossibility carried by precision: ✓. "Thread-work-hours, though the Edeyja did not distinguish between work and observation" — the unit is precise and its precision reveals a conceptual framework that doesn't map onto current categories. The term "translates imprecisely" — but this is the scholarly apparatus noting its own limitation, which is Borgesian (the footnote undermining the text).
- Scholarly register intact: ✓. "These accounts are disputed. The units are disputed. The network is not disputed." Catalogue of what is and isn't contested. The apparatus holds.
- Wittgenstein constraint at the close: "described in terms whose referents are no longer available even to practitioners who can perceive the substrate at full range." — **PROBLEM.** This is meta-description. The prose describes the failure of description. It says "the referents are no longer available." This is the narrator standing outside the failure and reporting on it.
- **Fix needed:** The sentence should demonstrate the referent-failure rather than describe it. Something like: end the entry with one of the Edeyja terms, untranslated, and stop. Let the untranslated term be the silence. Or: apply the precise apparatus to one specific aspect of the network's function, and let the application break down — the sentence tries to specify what the network did and the specification veers into a term that has no translation and the entry stops.
- **Verdict:** Partial pass. The precision-on-impossibility works. The close fails the Wittgenstein constraint. Needs revision.

**Revised close:**
"...The network is not disputed, though what it did — the sustained, generationally accumulated precision threadwork that the Church calls the cause and the Wardens call the method — the Edeyja called *tekhura*, which the Warden archives translate as 'bringing-near,' though what was brought near to what, and from what distance, and whether distance is the correct framework at all, is a question the archives note and do not answer."

**Audit of revision:** Better. The archives "note and do not answer" — the institutional silence is an act of the scholarly apparatus, not the narrator's confession. The apparatus itself reaches its limit and records the limit as an open question. The Borgesian footnote that undermines without resolving. **Pass.**

## Test 5. Ocampo — Flat-Affect Report

*A scene in which something terrible happens. The narrator does not register it.*

The girl had been sitting on the floor with the doll when the practitioner came in. The practitioner's shadow was not the same shape as the practitioner. The girl looked at the shadow and then at the practitioner and then at the shadow again and said that the shadow had more arms than the practitioner did, and was this because the practitioner had been somewhere, and the practitioner said yes, and the girl said she had known this because the doll had told her, though the doll was made of cloth and had no mouth. The practitioner sat down. The shadow sat down at a different time. The girl offered the practitioner a cup from the set on the floor, the small set with the chipped blue glaze, and the practitioner took the cup with the hand the shadow did not have.

**Audit:**
- Flat-affect: ✓. The narrator reports the shadow having more arms, the shadow sitting at a different time, the practitioner's hand not matching the shadow's hand — all with the same investment as reporting the blue glaze on the tea set.
- Narrator incuriosity: ✓. No alarm. No "strangely" or "horrifyingly." The shadow is wrong. The girl notices. The narrator reports that the girl notices. The narrator does not editorialize.
- Wrong-detail closing: ✓. "The small set with the chipped blue glaze" and "the cup with the hand the shadow did not have." The final detail is specific (which hand) and terrible (the shadow lacks it) but reported with the same flatness as the glaze color.
- Child witnessing: ✓. The girl's observation is acute and unnervous — "the shadow had more arms than the practitioner did, and was this because the practitioner had been somewhere." The child's matter-of-factness is Ocampo's signature.
- **Risk:** "The shadow sat down at a different time." — Is this Ocampo or is it Márquez? The temporal deviation (shadow and person moving at different times) is stated in ordinary syntax without remark — that's Márquez. But the flat, equanimous narration of a visibly wrong materiality — that's Ocampo. The two overlap here. In the synthesis, this interpolation is correct: Márquez's ordinary-syntax-for-impossibility carrying Ocampo's flat-affect content. **Pass — this is interpolation, not confusion.**
- **Risk:** "Though the doll was made of cloth and had no mouth" — is this the narrator registering the impossibility of the doll telling the girl something? If so, it's the narrator editorializing (saying "but this couldn't have happened"). It might instead be the narrator reporting the girl's own clarification — the girl says the doll told her, and then adds the detail about the cloth and the mouth, as children do, without recognizing the contradiction. **Ambiguous. Depends on focalization.** If focalized through the girl: the girl offers this information flatly, not recognizing the contradiction. Pass. If focalized through an external narrator: the narrator is editorializing. Fail.
- **Verdict:** Pass with focalization caveat.

## Test 6. Márquez — Ordinary Syntax for the Impossible

*A settlement's founding. Deep history. The supernatural enters as documented fact.*

Greinmar had stood on the headland for three hundred and forty years when the first practitioner was born inside its walls, and in the forty years after that, the fishing boats that put out from the harbor began to return with catches that were already salted, though no one on the boats had salted them, and the rain that fell on the eastern parish fell upward for six days in the third autumn of the practitioner's tenure, and the parish records for those six days, which Maren's predecessor had bound into the ninth volume, note the rainfall in the standard column and the direction in the margin, without comment.

**Audit:**
- Ordinary syntax: ✓. One compound sentence. Grammatically normal. Content impossible (self-salting fish, upward rain). No markers of wonder.
- Exact numbers: ✓. Three hundred and forty years, forty years, six days, third autumn, ninth volume. Hyperbolic specificity.
- No deviation markers: ✓. No "impossibly," "strangely," "as if by magic." The salted catches and the upward rain are reported with the same syntax as the parish records.
- The parish records "note the rainfall in the standard column and the direction in the margin, without comment": ✓. The institutional record itself practices Wittgenstein — it records the phenomenon and does not comment. The silence is documented. The documentation of the silence is the Borgesian layer on top of the Márquez base.
- Mistry interpolation: "Maren's predecessor had bound into the ninth volume" — occupational specificity (the bookbinder, the volume number). The trade-detail sits inside the Márquez impossibility without friction. **Interpolation working.**
- Tolkien interpolation: "Greinmar had stood on the headland for three hundred and forty years" — deep-time framing. Named specificity. The settlement as historical entity with weight. **Interpolation working.**
- **This is already a multi-author line.** Tolkien (deep-time, name), Márquez (ordinary-syntax impossibility, exact numbers), Mistry (occupational detail, volume number), Borges (the institutional record that documents without interpreting). Four authors in one passage. The transitions are invisible.
- **Verdict:** Pass. Strong. The interpolation is seamless because each author's technique serves a different function in the same content.

## Test 7. Mistry — Accretive Particulars Without Summary

*Maren's workshop. Body-as-record.*

The awl had worn a callus on the first joint of her right index finger, and a second, smaller callus on the pad of her thumb where the thread passed over it. Her apron had six pockets: two for the needles, one for the bone folder, one for the paste, one for the beeswax, and one she kept empty because she had been taught to keep one empty by the woman who had taught her the trade, and the reason had been lost but the pocket remained. The bench was scored along its entire length with the marks of forty years of straight-edge cuts, and the light from the window fell across the bench in the late afternoon in a way that made the oldest cuts visible and the newest invisible, so that the bench appeared to be the bench of someone who had stopped working years ago.

**Audit:**
- Accretive: ✓. Each sentence adds one physical particular. Callus, callus, pockets, pocket contents, bench, cuts, light.
- No summary: ✓. The passage does not say "and so the workshop told the story of her trade." The reader assembles.
- Body-as-record: ✓. The calluses map the work. The awl has shaped the hand. The hand has shaped the bench.
- Dignity: ✓. No sentimentality. Maren's trade is rendered through its physical evidence without commentary.
- **The empty pocket:** "the reason had been lost but the pocket remained." — This is Tolkien's asterisk reality inside Mistry's occupational inventory. The lost reason is the absence around which the pocket exists. The pocket remains because the woman who taught Maren kept one empty and the practice has outlived the reason. History as embodied habit. **Interpolation working.**
- **The light trick:** "the oldest cuts visible and the newest invisible, so that the bench appeared to be the bench of someone who had stopped working years ago." — This is Tartt. A loaded detail. The light's accident produces an image (the bench of someone who stopped) that carries a weight the prose does not announce. The reader feels the temporality — the bench suggests cessation — without being told to feel it. **Interpolation working.**
- **Risk:** The empty-pocket detail is very good, which makes me suspicious. Is it too neat? The detail-that-carries-a-lost-history is a well-worn literary device. But the specificity rescues it — the pocket is the sixth pocket, and the reason is lost, and the practice persists. The specificity makes it Mistry rather than generic. **Pass.**
- **Verdict:** Pass. The interpolations (Tolkien's asterisk in the pocket, Tartt's loaded light) emerge naturally from Mistry's accumulation.

## Test 8. Tartt — Loaded Detail Without Announcement

*An interior. Vael enters a room where something happened earlier. She doesn't know what happened.*

The chair had been moved. Not far — a foot, perhaps, from the depression its legs had worn into the floor over what must have been years. The plaster dust on the seat was the same color as the plaster on the ceiling, and there was a glass on the table beside the chair with water still in it, and the water had not gone still. Vael stood in the doorway. The light was the particular amber of late afternoon through old glass, the kind that makes everything in the room appear to have been beautiful once. She did not sit in the chair. The water was still moving.

**Audit:**
- Loaded detail: ✓. The chair moved from its depression. The plaster dust from the ceiling. The water still moving. Each detail implies the recent presence (and departure, or something) without stating it.
- No announcement: ✓. The prose does not say "something had happened." It describes the chair, the dust, the water.
- Atmospheric immersion: ✓. "The particular amber of late afternoon through old glass, the kind that makes everything in the room appear to have been beautiful once." This is Tartt's aesthetic narrator — the perception colored by beauty-obsession.
- **Risk:** "the kind that makes everything in the room appear to have been beautiful once" — is "appear to have been beautiful once" too much? It's an editorial observation. But it belongs to Vael's aesthetic perception. It's the focalizer's judgment, not the narrator's meta-commentary. **Pass — if focalized through Vael, this is her aesthetic rendering of the light.**
- Closing: "The water was still moving." — Returns to the physical fact. The loaded detail. The prose closes on the concrete and stops. What happened in the room is not explained. The water is still moving. Wittgenstein. **Pass.**
- **Concern:** The passage is atmospheric but thin. It relies heavily on implication. At 10-8 coherence this works — Vael's rendering is intact, she perceives the implications. At lower coherence, the passage would need to change: the implications would be less available because the rendering that produces implication (connecting chair-moved to someone-was-here) is degrading. This passage is coherence-dependent and should be flagged as a 10-8 example.
- **Verdict:** Pass at 10-8. Would fail at lower tiers without modification.

### Test 9. Tolkien + Lispector — Landscape the Rendering Fails to Hold

*Coherence 5. Vael at the headland ruin from Test 1. Her rendering begins to falter.*

The wall ran east along the headland for — she counted them. Thirty paces. She had counted them and the number was thirty and the number held. Beyond the wall's end the grass grew shorter and the soil was darker and these facts were facts of the kind that stayed when she looked at them, which was not always. The mortar between the stones was old. She touched it with her hand and the hand that touched the mortar was the hand she had brought with her to this place, and the mortar was cold, and the cold was the cold of stone that had been standing in shade, and she was certain of the shade because the wall was — the wall was to her left and therefore the shade was — the wall was to her east. She had been facing east. The shade was on the north side.

She stopped reconstructing. The grass was short and the ground was dark and the wind came through, and these were the things that did not require her to hold them, and she stood in them.

**Audit:**
- Tolkien ground: ✓. The wall, the headland, the paces, the mortar, the shade. Physical world present.
- Lispector rendering failure: ✓. "She counted them. Thirty paces. She had counted them and the number was thirty and the number held." — The insistence on the number holding reveals that numbers don't always hold. "Facts of the kind that stayed when she looked at them, which was not always." — The qualification "which was not always" is inside the perception, not meta. Vael knows, from experience, that facts sometimes don't stay.
- The veer: ✓. "The wall was to her left and therefore the shade was — the wall was to her east." The spatial reasoning breaks mid-sentence. She reconstructs. Left → east. The effort is visible.
- Wittgenstein at close: ✓. "She stopped reconstructing" — this IS meta-description. But: "The grass was short and the ground was dark and the wind came through, and these were the things that did not require her to hold them, and she stood in them." — The close is Tolkien's physical world, the things that persist without rendering holding them. The parataxis is marked. She stands in them. The prose stops.
- **Problem:** "She stopped reconstructing" — this is analytical. Vael names what she's doing (reconstructing) and reports stopping. At Coherence 5 this might be available — she's dissonant, not fragmented. She can still observe her own process, just barely. At Coherence 3 this sentence would be impossible. **Borderline pass at Coherence 5. Fail at 3 or below.**
- **Interpolation quality:** Tolkien provides the ground. Lispector provides the rendering-failure. The interpolation works because Tolkien's named specificity (the wall, the paces, the mortar) is exactly what Vael's rendering is trying and failing to hold. The Tolkien content is the content the Lispector technique acts upon. Not two authors side by side — one author's content inside the other author's process. **This is what interpolation should be.**
- **Verdict:** Pass. The interpolation is structural, not decorative.

### Test 10. Ishiguro + Ocampo — Misidentification Meets Flat-Affect

*Coherence 4. An NPC observes Vael. Focalized through the NPC (Maren). Vael's rendering is failing visibly.*

Maren noticed that Vael was sitting in the chair in the way she had always sat in the chair, which was with her hands on the table and her back quite straight, and if Vael's shadow seemed to Maren somewhat larger than it had been in previous visits, she attributed this to the quality of the afternoon light, which in autumn came through the side window at a lower angle. The shadow had more than two arms. Maren set the ledger on the table and placed the pin beside it and asked if the binding was satisfactory, and Vael said that it was, and the voice came from the right direction.

**Audit:**
- Ishiguro in Maren: ✓. Maren notices the shadow is larger. She attributes this to the light's angle. This is confident misidentification. The explanation is plausible and wrong.
- Ocampo in the flat-affect: ✓. "The shadow had more than two arms." Five words. Reported between a sentence about autumn light and a sentence about a ledger. No alarm. No transition. No affect word. The terrible fact sits inside the domestic scene with the same weight as the pin and the binding.
- The interpolation: Ishiguro's misidentification (shadow → light angle) followed by Ocampo's flat report (the shadow had more than two arms). The first sentence rationalizes. The second sentence makes the rationalization impossible. But neither the narrator nor Maren registers the contradiction. Maren proceeds to set the ledger down. The world continues.
- "The voice came from the right direction": ✓. Ocampo's closing-on-the-wrong-detail. The scene is about Vael's materiality failing visibly. The closing detail is that her voice came from the right direction. Maren registers this small correctness as if it were reassuring. The reassurance is itself the horror — that "from the right direction" is the bar.
- **Risk:** Is Maren's focalization too composed for someone observing a shadow with more than two arms? Yes — and that's the point. Maren does not register the magnitude. She is Ocampo's narrator: incurious about the extraordinary. Whether this is Maren's personality or the prose's technique is ambiguous — and the ambiguity is productive.
- **Verdict:** Strong pass. The Ishiguro/Ocampo interpolation produces something neither does alone: the rational explanation that is instantly and flatly contradicted, with neither narrator nor character acknowledging the contradiction.

### Test 11. Borges + Márquez — Precision Meets Ordinary Impossibility

*A codex entry. Warden Chronicler voice. The Einhir network's collapse.*

The inversion occurred on the forty-first day of the network's sustained operation, at midday, at the node designated KR-7 in the Warden nomenclature, which was situated four hundred paces east-southeast of what is now the Greinmar market cross. The aggregate output at the moment of inversion has been calculated at between eleven and fourteen thousand *tekhura*-equivalent units, though the unit is no longer recoverable and the calculation is therefore a calculation of a calculation. All fourteen hundred and twelve nodes discharged simultaneously. The discharge propagated from KR-7 to the network's perimeter in the time it takes a person to draw breath, which is to say, in the temporal framework the Wardens used, it did not propagate at all.

**Audit:**
- Borges precision: ✓. Forty-first day. KR-7. Four hundred paces east-southeast. Eleven to fourteen thousand units. Fourteen hundred and twelve nodes. The precision is absolute.
- Borges apparatus undermining itself: ✓. "A calculation of a calculation" — the scholarly precision is precise about its own imprecision. The footnote-logic. And "the unit is no longer recoverable" — the apparatus notes its own gap.
- Márquez ordinary syntax: ✓. "All fourteen hundred and twelve nodes discharged simultaneously" — ordinary sentence. Content: fourteen hundred simultaneous discharges across a peninsula. No wonder-markers.
- Márquez temporal compression: ✓. "In the time it takes a person to draw breath" — exact, physical, ordinary comparator for something impossible.
- Borges paradox at close: ✓. "Which is to say, in the temporal framework the Wardens used, it did not propagate at all." The precision (time of a breath) is reframed by a different temporal framework in which the measurement collapses. Two frameworks. Both precise. Mutually exclusive. Neither remarked upon.
- **Risk:** "the unit is no longer recoverable and the calculation is therefore a calculation of a calculation" — is this too self-aware? The scholarly apparatus commenting on its own limitations. But this is what Borges does — the encyclopedia entry that notes its own gaps with the same precision it notes its facts. The apparatus doesn't collapse. It documents its limits and continues. **Pass — this is the apparatus.**
- **Interpolation quality:** Borges provides the apparatus and the paradox. Márquez provides the ordinary syntax and the exact impossibility. They combine naturally because both deploy precision to different ends — Borges's precision reveals impossibility through structural paradox, Márquez's precision makes impossibility ordinary through numerical specificity. Together they produce a passage that is both paradoxical and matter-of-fact.
- **Verdict:** Strong pass. This is what codex entries should sound like.

### Test 12. Lispector + Ocampo — Categorical Failure Meets Belonging-Failure

*Coherence 3. Vael in a kitchen. Domestic space. Her rendering fragmenting.*

The kitchen had a table and on the table a bowl and in the bowl three eggs, and the eggs were eggs. She was in the kitchen. She had been in the kitchen before this and the kitchen had been this kitchen and she had been this person in it and now she was this person in it again, or still, and the eggs were three and they were in the bowl on the table in the kitchen and she was also in the kitchen and these things were in the same room together. The window above the basin let in light from the yard and the light fell on the eggs and on Vael's hands and on the table and the light did not distinguish between these. The woman at the stove was stirring something and the stirring was the only sound and the sound was the sound of stirring and Vael sat at the table and the eggs were in the bowl.

**Audit:**
- Lispector categorical failure: ✓. "The eggs were eggs" — tautology as the point where categories can do no more than confirm identity. "She had been this person in it and now she was this person in it again, or still" — the temporal distinction (again vs still) is unavailable; the rendering cannot determine whether she is re-entering or continuing.
- Ocampo belonging-failure: ✓. "These things were in the same room together" — the prose reports co-location as if it were remarkable. The sentence performs the failure of belonging: normally one would not need to confirm that a person and a bowl and a table are "in the same room together." The fact that this needs stating IS the belonging-failure.
- Ocampo flat-affect: ✓. "The light did not distinguish between these" — the light treats Vael's hands and the eggs and the table identically. Reported without affect. The narrator does not register the horror of being undistinguished from a table.
- No similes: ✓. Coherence 3. No analogical reasoning. No comparisons. Every sentence is declarative.
- Closing: "The eggs were in the bowl." — Wrong-detail close. The scene was about self-recognition failure, temporal confusion, belonging-failure. The closing is about eggs.
- **Risk:** The passage is repetitive. "The eggs" appear four times. "The kitchen" appears five times. Is this a failure of lexical diversity (anti-pattern #14) or Lispector's repetition-as-ontological-intensity? At Coherence 3, the repetition is the rendering cycling through the same confirmations because it cannot move past them. Each repetition is the rendering checking: are the eggs still eggs? Is the kitchen still the kitchen? The repetition is structural, not lazy. **Pass — if the reader experiences the repetition as the rendering's cycling. If the reader experiences it as monotonous, the passage has failed to earn the repetition.**
- **Verdict:** Pass with risk. The repetition is earned by the coherence tier but is the kind of technique that fails silently — if it doesn't work for the reader, there is no fallback. The passage either conveys the rendering cycling through confirmations or it reads as repetitive writing.

### Test 13. Full Synthesis — Coherence 7-5. Six+ Authors in One Passage.

*Vael arrives at the Greinmar parish house for the first time this season. Late afternoon. Coherence 6.*

The parish house stood where it had stood since the charter of the second renewal, and the stone of its east wall was the old stone, the stone from before the consolidation, which the sexton maintained had been quarried at a site whose name he could not remember but which was, he believed, three valleys east. Vael came through the side door and the room inside was the room she expected — table, the ledger-rack along the north wall, the plaster Christ in the niche above the lintel — and the room received her the way a room receives a person who has been in it before, which is to say it did not receive her, it continued. She sat. The chair held her. She was aware, in the way one is aware of a sleeve that has twisted slightly on the forearm, that she had entered the room a moment before she had opened the door, or that the room had assembled itself around her in the time after the door and before the chair, and that this interval was not an interval she had walked through. She had not walked through it. The interval had not been there to be walked through and she was on the other side of it.

The sexton's wife brought water in a cup that had been repaired — the handle reattached with the iron-oxide paste the potters in the lower parish used — and Vael drank from it and the water was water and the cup was a cup and the woman's hands, which were red from the work of the morning, set the cup on the table beside the ledger-rack with the precision of a woman who puts things where things go.

**Audit — author tally:**
- Tolkien: "the charter of the second renewal," "the old stone, the stone from before the consolidation," "three valleys east." Deep-time. Named specificity. Landscape-as-history. ~20%
- Ishiguro: "he believed," "which is to say it did not receive her, it continued." The sexton's uncertainty about the quarry name. The repair sequence: "which is to say" — the narrator corrects the initial formulation ("received her") to the truer one ("continued"). ~14%
- Márquez: The temporal impossibility in ordinary syntax: "she had entered the room a moment before she had opened the door." No wonder-marker. Stated as fact. ~12%
- Lispector: "the interval had not been there to be walked through and she was on the other side of it." The sentence reaches for the temporal structure and finds it absent. Not "she could not account for the interval" — the interval was not there. She is on the other side of something that was not there. The predication fails. ~12%
- Mistry: "the iron-oxide paste the potters in the lower parish used." Occupational specificity, untranslated trade detail. "The woman's hands, which were red from the work of the morning." Body-as-record. ~10%
- Ocampo: "the water was water and the cup was a cup" — flat confirmation of the tautological. The narrator does not register that needing to confirm this is itself a sign of rendering failure. ~10%
- Tartt: "She was aware, in the way one is aware of a sleeve that has twisted slightly on the forearm" — the comparison is sensory, precise, minor-key. The loaded domestic detail (the twisted sleeve) carries the rendering failure without elevating it. ~8%
- Borges: The sexton's three-valleys-east memory that is precise and uncertain simultaneously — he can specify the distance but not the name. The precision is in the gap of the name. Minimal. ~5%
- **Wittgenstein:** The passage does not describe the temporal slip as strange, impossible, or disorienting. It reports the slip in the syntax of the ordinary (Márquez). It describes its aftermath in the syntax of categorical failure (Lispector). It closes on the concrete: "a woman who puts things where things go."
- **Interpolation quality:** Six authors appear in the passage. No author dominates. The transitions are invisible. Each technique serves a different function: Tolkien grounds, Ishiguro hedges, Márquez states impossibility, Lispector veers, Mistry accumulates, Ocampo reports flatly, Tartt loads the detail. The passage reads as one voice, not as an anthology.
- **Risks:**
  - "Which is to say it did not receive her, it continued" — is "which is to say" too mannered? It's an Ishiguro formula (Stevens's repair sequences use similar constructions). In the synthesis it might read as writerly self-correction. At Coherence 6 the self-correction is available — Vael can still process her own narration. **Pass at 6. Fail at 3.**
  - "She was aware, in the way one is aware of a sleeve" — the simile. At Coherence 6, similes are still available. The rendering can still produce analogical reasoning. **Pass at this tier.**
- **Verdict:** Strong pass. This is the voice working.

## Interim Findings

**What works:**
1. Interpolation is structural, not decorative — each author's technique serves a different function in the same content. One author's content sits inside another author's process.
2. The Wittgenstein constraint is enforceable: close on the nearest concrete thing. The pin. The eggs. The cup. The grass.
3. Ishiguro + Ocampo is a powerful pairing: rational explanation followed by flat contradiction. Neither narrator nor character acknowledges.
4. Borges + Márquez is natural for codex/chronicle: precision and ordinary impossibility are complementary, not competing.
5. Lispector's repetition-as-rendering-cycling works at Coherence 3-5 but is high-risk — if it fails, it reads as monotonous writing.

**What fails or risks failure:**
1. Analytical self-observation degrades with coherence. "She stopped reconstructing" is too analytical at Coherence 5. Must be ruthlessly cut at lower tiers.
2. Similes require analogical reasoning. Must be eliminated below Coherence 4.
3. Borges's scholarly apparatus can tip into meta-description if the apparatus comments on its own inadequacy too directly. The apparatus must reach its limit and STOP, or document the gap as an institutional silence, not as the narrator's confession.
4. Lispector's tautology ("the eggs were eggs") works once per passage. Repeated, it becomes a trick.
5. Ocampo's flat-affect depends on content contrast — if the content isn't genuinely terrible or strange, the flatness is just flatness. The technique requires magnitude beneath the surface.

**Rules confirmed:**
- Close on the concrete thing. Always.
- No "she could not." The prose attempts. The attempt fails or veers or commits wrongly.
- No similes below Coherence 4.
- No analytical self-observation below Coherence 5.
- Interpolation works when each author serves a different function. It fails when two authors serve the same function (e.g., Lispector veer + Ishiguro uncertainty both serving "the narrator is unsure" — choose one).
- The physical world is always present. At Coherence 1, it is fragmentary. At Coherence 10, it is full. It is never absent.

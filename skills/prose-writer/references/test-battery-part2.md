### Test 14. Full Synthesis — Coherence 2 (Fractured)

*Vael in the parish house. Maren present. TS 0. Categories loosening. Belief Co-Authorship. Dissociative episodes.*

The ledger was on the table. Maren's hands were on the table beside it. Vael knew these things the way she knew the — they were facts and they were beside each other. The chalk on Maren's sleeves. The pin beside the binding. The twenty — the volume. A number. Vael looked at the number and the number stayed and she looked at it again and it was the same number and looking at it a second time had been a thing she had done though she remembered only looking once.

Maren said the binding was tight enough and Vael agreed and the agreement came out of her mouth in the right shape but the belief that the binding mattered — the belief that records were worth keeping, that years followed years, that one ledger succeeded another in an order that was — this belief sat in her chest like a coat she had put on in someone else's house. It fit. It was warm. It was not hers.

Her weight was wrong in the chair. She shifted. The wrongness shifted with her. Outside, the lane ran in a direction and from the direction came the smell of cut hay, and the lane and the hay were in the same place, which was outside, which was where Vael was not, which was — she was in the chair. The pin was beside the book.

**Audit:**
- **No meta-description:** ✓. No "she could not connect them" — instead: "they were facts and they were beside each other." The disconnection is enacted by the prose presenting facts in sequence without connecting syntax. The parataxis IS the disconnection.
- **Belief Co-Authorship:** The coat passage. "It fit. It was warm. It was not hers." — is this a simile? No — it's a metaphor embedded in Vael's perception. At Coherence 2, this is borderline. Vael isn't making an analogy — she's reporting the sensation. The belief literally feels like wearing something that belongs to someone else. The "coat" is experiential, not rhetorical. **Borderline pass.** The sentence works because the metaphor is haptic (fit, warm) rather than analogical (X is like Y). She doesn't say "as if." She says "like a coat" — but the "like" is perceptual ("this is what it is like to be in this belief") rather than comparative ("this experience resembles another experience"). Fine distinction. **Pass, narrowly.**
- **Dissociative episode:** "Looking at it a second time had been a thing she had done though she remembered only looking once." — The action happened. The memory doesn't contain it. The prose reports both — the second look and the memory of one look — without resolving the contradiction. Ocampo's flat report of the irreconcilable. ✓
- **No similes:** ✓ (the coat is haptic metaphor, not simile — no "as if" or analogical structure).
- **No analytical self-observation:** ✓. Vael doesn't analyze her own state. She experiences facts-beside-each-other, a belief that isn't hers, weight that's wrong. The prose renders the experience. It doesn't observe the experiencing.
- **Closing:** "The pin was beside the book." ✓. Wrong-detail close. Ocampo.
- **Weight passage:** "She shifted. The wrongness shifted with her." — Mistry's body-as-record applied to a body whose rendering is failing. The body tells the history of its own wrongness. Two sentences, no commentary. ✓
- **Author tally:** Lispector (categorical failure — facts without connection), Ocampo (flat-affect, wrong-detail close, dissociative report), Ishiguro (embedded unreliability — "she remembered only looking once"), Márquez (ordinary syntax for impossible — looking twice while remembering once), Mistry (body-wrongness), Tolkien (the lane, the hay — fragment of landscape, last ground). Borges minimal — the only Borgesian touch is the recursive structure of "a number" being confirmed by looking. Tartt minimal.
- **Concern:** The passage is quite short. At Coherence 2, can longer passages be sustained? The categories are loosening — longer structures (multi-paragraph development, extended scene-building) might not be available to the rendering. Short passages might be the correct formal constraint at this tier. **Leave as design question for next session.**
- **Verdict:** Pass. The coat passage is the risk point, decided narrowly.

### Test 15. Full Synthesis — Coherence 1 (Severed)

*Vael. The parish house. TS 0. Rendering barely holds. No substrate perception — just noise.*

Maren. The table. Vael is in the — the chair is under her and she is above the chair and between these two facts there is a body and the body is. The body is Vael. She knows this the way she knows the — there is a way of knowing things and she is in it and it is the last way. Before this way there were other ways and the other ways had names and the names were — the names. The names are gone or they are here and she cannot — they are here. Cup. Table. Maren. Pin. Book. Ledger. Chalk. These are the names. They refer to the things the names refer to.

Maren's mouth moves. The words are tight and enough and binding and they come in the right order or they come and the order is — Vael hears them. She hears them the way a surface hears rain. They arrive and they are on her and they do not go inside.

Something arrives that is not Maren and is not the words and is not the light. It has no shape. It comes from every direction or from no direction or from the direction that direction is before direction has — it comes. It is on her and it does not go inside and it is not rain and it is not words and the comparison she just made is the last comparison she will — she made a comparison. That was the last one. Comparisons require the — comparisons require.

The chair. The table. The pin.

**Audit:**
- **Temporal structure collapsed:** ✓. Present tense throughout. No past-tense narration. Temporal structuring has failed — only the now is available. Lispector's "instant-now" not as transcendence but as the failure of temporal categories.
- **Names as islands of rendering:** ✓. "Cup. Table. Maren. Pin. Book. Ledger. Chalk." — These are the last outputs of the rendering. Tolkien's named specificity reduced to a catalogue of isolated nouns. The names refer but no longer connect.
- **Involuntary perceptual event (noise, not substrate):** ✓. "Something arrives that is not Maren and is not the words and is not the light." Defined by negation — not X, not Y, not Z. The prose does not say what it is. It says what it is not. Lispector's negative definition. The prose attempts to describe the direction it comes from and fails: "from the direction that direction is before direction has —" The sentence breaks. The rendering cannot produce the concept "direction" because direction is a spatial category and spatial categories have — the sentence just enacted what it described.
- **The last comparison:** "She hears them the way a surface hears rain." — This is a simile at Coherence 1. I said no similes below 4. But: "she made a comparison. That was the last one. Comparisons require the — comparisons require." The prose USES a simile and then the prose recognizes, inside the text, that similes are no longer available. The simile is the last one. The capacity for comparison collapses in the act of comparing. **This is the sentence carrying failure within itself.** The simile works because the text marks it as the last possible simile. The rendering produces one more comparison and then the apparatus breaks.
- **Is the "last comparison" passage too self-aware?** "She made a comparison. That was the last one." — At Coherence 1, can Vael observe that she just made a comparison? This is dangerously close to analytical self-observation, which should be unavailable at this tier. But: the observation is fragmentary. "She made a comparison" is a simple declarative — subject did action. "That was the last one" is a prediction, or a recognition, in the simplest possible terms. "Comparisons require the —" breaks. The observation is available in fragments but the analytical capacity to complete it is gone. **Pass — the observation begins and cannot complete itself. The fragment of self-awareness is itself fragmenting.**
- **Closing:** "The chair. The table. The pin." Three nouns. No verbs. No predication. The rendering's last output is names without grammar. Tolkien's catalogue reduced to three objects. The prose is silent after them. Wittgenstein. ✓
- **Borges:** "They refer to the things the names refer to" — tautological recursion. The sentence about reference cannot escape its own circularity. The names refer to the things the names refer to. The rendering can produce the concept of "reference" but cannot ground it — the grounding would require the categories the rendering has lost. Borgesian infinite regress terminating in tautology. ✓
- **Author tally:** Lispector dominant (present tense, veer, negative definition, instant-now). Borges strong (tautological recursion, names-referring-to-themselves). Ocampo present (flat-affect throughout — no alarm, no escalation, no affect words). Tolkien fragmentary (the names; the chair, the table, the pin). Márquez present (ordinary syntax for impossible — hearing words like rain). Ishiguro minimal. Mistry trace (the body between chair and above-chair). Tartt minimal.
- **Verdict:** Pass. The last-simile passage is the standout — a construction that uses the technique it is losing.

### Test 16. TS 70 Overlay — Coherence 5 (Dissonant)

*Vael at the parish house. TS 70 — perceives full configuration. Coherence 5 — rendering beginning to falter. The old knots beneath the east wall are legible.*

The east wall of the parish house was the wall she had known for three seasons, and beneath it — she could say "beneath" though beneath was not exactly — the knots were there. They had the architecture of restorative work, layered, the oldest stratum set by hands that had worked nine generations before the charter. She perceived this. The perception was clear. It was clearer than Maren's face, which was across the table and which Vael's rendering assembled from its parts — nose, mouth, the scar above the eyebrow — with an effort that the knots beneath the wall did not require.

The knots did not require assembly. They were there the way — they were there. She could feel the grain of the work, the intent that had shaped each stratum: restorative, restorative, restorative, and then a layer that was — she did not have a word for what the fourth layer was. It was not restorative. It was not destructive. It was the kind of work that — it was the fourth layer and the Edeyja who had laid it had been doing something she could perceive and not — she could perceive it.

Maren said something about the binding. The ledger was on the table. Vael's rendering produced these facts: ledger, table, binding, Maren, the sound of the sentence. It produced them correctly. The production cost more than it had last season. The knots beneath the wall cost nothing. They were simply there, the way the wall was there before her rendering decided to assemble it into a wall.

The pin was beside the book. The fourth layer was beneath the floor.

**Audit:**
- **TS perception clear, rendering struggling:** ✓. The core paradox: substrate perception (which doesn't require rendering) is clearer than rendered perception (which does). Maren's face requires assembly. The knots do not. This demonstrates the TS/coherence interaction correctly.
- **Articulability gradient:** The first three strata are articulable — "restorative, restorative, restorative." The fourth is not. But: the prose does not say "she could not describe the fourth layer." It attempts: "the kind of work that —" and breaks. "Something she could perceive and not —" and breaks. Lispector's veer. The attempts fail without announcing failure. ✓
- **No meta-description of the perception's clarity:** Partial. "The perception was clear. It was clearer than Maren's face" — this is analytical comparison. At Coherence 5 this is available (dissonant, not fragmented). The comparison is simple (clear vs less-clear) and serves to establish the paradox. At Coherence 3 this comparison would be too organized. **Pass at 5.**
- **"The production cost more than it had last season":** This is analytical — Vael comparing current rendering effort to prior rendering effort. At Coherence 5, minimally available. The sentence is short and declarative. **Borderline pass.** At 3 or below: fail.
- **"They were simply there, the way the wall was there before her rendering decided to assemble it into a wall":** "Rendering" is system terminology. At Coherence 5 with TS 70, does Vael have access to the concept "rendering"? TS 70 gives Structural-scale perception — she can perceive the substrate at structural level. Would she conceptualize her own perceptual process as "rendering"? The Wardens have vocabulary for these processes (the Edeyja framework). If Vael has been trained in Warden terminology, "rendering" might be available as a term she learned. But it's borderline — it reads as the writer's system terminology rather than the character's vocabulary. **Flag for revision.** Replace "rendering" with a character-available term or a circumlocution: "before she had decided to see it as a wall" or "before it became a wall for her."
- **Closing:** "The pin was beside the book. The fourth layer was beneath the floor." Two closing facts — one rendered (the pin), one perceived (the fourth layer). The TS axis and the rendered axis sitting side by side in the closing. Both reported with Ocampo's flat investment. Neither privileged. ✓
- **Verdict:** Pass with one revision needed (the "rendering" terminology).

### Test 17. TS 70 Overlay — Coherence 1 (Severed)

*The extreme case. Rendering nearly gone. Full substrate perception persists.*

The knots are there. They are the clearest thing. They have the architecture of — they have architecture. Restorative. Restorative. Restorative. The fourth stratum. The fourth stratum is. Vael can — she perceives it. The fourth stratum does the thing the fourth stratum does and the word for that thing is — the fourth stratum.

Maren. The ledger. The table. These require — these require the thing that makes them a table and a ledger and Maren. The knots do not require this. The knots are there without the thing. They are the thing that is there when the thing that makes things into things is — when the thing is not.

Vael's hands. Are they on the table. The table needs her to — the table is the table when she — the knots are there without this. The knots are clearer than the table because the table requires and the knots do not require.

The fourth stratum. She perceives it fully. She perceives it. She. Perceives.

The pin.

**Audit:**
- **Rendering collapsed, perception intact:** ✓. "The knots are the clearest thing" — correct per the rendering theory. The substrate perception (TS) does not require rendering. The rendered world (table, ledger, Maren) does. When rendering fails, the substrate remains while the rendered world degrades. The knots are "clearer than the table because the table requires and the knots do not require."
- **Articulability at zero:** ✓. The fourth stratum is perceived but cannot be articulated. "The fourth stratum does the thing the fourth stratum does and the word for that thing is — the fourth stratum." Tautological recursion (Borges) — the sentence tries to name what the stratum does and can only return to the stratum itself. Not because Vael lacks the concept (she has TS 70) but because the architecture that would connect perception to language (rendering) is what has failed.
- **The distinction between perception-resistance (TS) and articulation-failure (coherence):** This is the crucial case. At high coherence + high TS, the content resists articulation because the content is beyond human categories. At low coherence + high TS, the content is perceived clearly but articulation fails because the architecture connecting perception to language is the rendering that has failed. The result looks the same (no articulation) but the cause is different. Does the prose distinguish these? Partially — "she perceives it fully" establishes that the perception is complete. The articulability failure is located in the connection between perception and expression, not in the perception itself. The sentence "She. Perceives." — each word isolated, the grammar breaking — enacts the failure of the connective architecture while asserting the perception's persistence. **Pass.**
- **"When the thing that makes things into things is — when the thing is not":** This is the sentence trying to describe rendering and failing. At Coherence 1, Vael cannot produce the concept "rendering" (or any equivalent). She reaches for "the thing that makes things into things" — a circumlocution at the simplest possible level — and the circumlocution itself breaks. "When the thing is not" — the sentence terminates at negation because the category it was trying to negate is itself unavailable. ✓
- **Closing:** "The pin." One word. The last rendered object. Everything else has required rendering and rendering has failed. The pin persists as the final concrete thing. After the pin, silence. Wittgenstein. ✓
- **Verdict:** Pass. The passage is uncomfortable to read. This is correct — Coherence 1 with TS 70 should be uncomfortable. The reader experiences the gap between perception (which is total) and articulation (which is collapsing).

### Test 18. Dialogue — Coherence 10-8 (Baseline)

*Vael and Maren. Normal rendering. Speech as speech.*

"The binding's tight enough now," Maren said. She set the ledger on the table and stood back from it with her hands at the table's edge.

"The twenty-third," Vael said.

"The twenty-third. Three more and the shelf's full. The sexton wants to start a new shelf but the wall won't take another bracket — the plaster's gone behind the first one. I told him."

"What did he say?"

"He said he'd think about it." Maren pulled the pin from her apron and placed it beside the book. "He'll think about it until the twenty-sixth volume and then he'll panic."

**Audit:**
- Dialogue as dialogue: ✓. Speech carrying character. Maren's voice is specific — occupational (she knows the shelf, the bracket, the plaster), direct, practical, wry at the end.
- Subtext: ✓. "He said he'd think about it" / "He'll think about it until the twenty-sixth volume and then he'll panic" — Maren knows the sexton. The reader learns about the sexton through Maren's prediction. Ishiguro's "speech carrying what's withheld" — Maren says what she thinks of the sexton without editorializing.
- Class-marked speech: Partial. Maren's register is working-class practical but could be more marked. The contractions ("He'll," "she'd") signal informality. More distinctive syntax would strengthen — regional idiom, trade-specific phrasing.
- No rendering failure: ✓. At 10-8, the dialogue is normal. Temporal processing intact, speech arrives in sequence, meaning is accessible.
- **Verdict:** Pass. Clean baseline. The dialogue works as dialogue, not as a showcase for the author techniques.

### Test 19. Dialogue — Coherence 4-3 (Fragmented)

*The same conversation. Vael's rendering of the dialogue is failing. Maren's speech is normal. Vael's reception of it is not.*

"The binding's tight enough now," Maren said, and Vael heard the sentence and the sentence was a sentence she had heard before this, in this room, from this woman, or a sentence she had not heard before this but which arrived with the weight of a sentence that had been heard, and she agreed with it because agreeing was the shape her mouth made when Maren finished speaking.

"The twenty-third," Vael said, and was not certain she had said it aloud, and was not certain the number was twenty-three, and said it.

Maren was talking about a shelf and a bracket and plaster and the sexton. These were in the right order. The words referred to things Vael could see — the shelf was on the north wall and the brackets were iron and the plaster above the first bracket had a crack she could — she could see the crack. The words and the things the words referred to were in the same room.

"What did he say?" Vael heard herself ask. The question had left her mouth. It was the right question. She did not remember forming it.

Maren said the sexton would think about it. Maren said something about the twenty-sixth volume. Maren's mouth made a shape that Vael recognized as the shape mouths make at the end of a sentence that is meant to be — the pin was beside the book.

**Audit:**
- **Dialogue received through failing rendering:** ✓. Maren's speech is normal. Vael's reception is fragmented. The same words arrive but the processing is impaired — the sentence "arrives with the weight of a sentence that had been heard" (temporal confusion), the number is uncertain, the question leaves her mouth without her forming it (dissociative micro-episode).
- **Vael's own speech:** "The twenty-third," she said — and "was not certain she had said it aloud, and was not certain the number was twenty-three, and said it." The three clauses: uncertainty about vocalization, uncertainty about content, then the act itself. The "and said it" sits after the uncertainties as a blunt fact — Ocampo's flat report. She said it. Whether she said it aloud or correctly is unresolved. ✓
- **"The words and the things the words referred to were in the same room":** This confirmation that words and referents are co-located is Lispector's tautological ground-checking. The fact that this needs stating is the failure. ✓
- **"She did not remember forming it":** Is this meta-description? "Did not remember" is close to "could not." But it's a factual report — she searches for the memory of forming the question and the memory is not there. Absence reported, not inability announced. The distinction is that "she could not remember" describes a capacity failure, while "she did not remember" describes a factual gap. **Pass — the phrasing reports absence rather than announcing inability.**
- **Closing veer:** "Maren's mouth made a shape that Vael recognized as the shape mouths make at the end of a sentence that is meant to be —" The sentence breaks before it can complete "funny" or "wry" or whatever Maren's expression signified. The recognition of the social signal (mouth-shape-at-end-of-joke) is available but the category the signal belongs to (humor, wryness) is not. The prose breaks and closes on "the pin was beside the book." The veer (Lispector) into the wrong-detail close (Ocampo). ✓
- **Concern:** The passage is longer than Test 14 (Coherence 2). At Coherence 4-3, the prose can sustain longer structures than at 2 — the rendering is fragmented but not yet fractured. The length is appropriate to the tier. ✓
- **Verdict:** Strong pass. The dialogue is the most complex test so far because it requires rendering TWO people's speech — one normal (Maren) and one received through failing rendering (Vael's perception of Maren). The interpolation between what Maren says and how Vael receives it is the core technique.

### Test 20. Dialogue — Coherence 1 (Severed)

*The same conversation. Rendering barely holds.*

Maren's mouth. The word tight. The word enough. Words come and they are on Vael and they do not go inside. The binding. The binding is. Maren is talking about the binding.

- **Speech at the limit of rendering:** ✓. Individual words arrive but don't assemble into meaning. "Tight." "Enough." The words are identified as words. They have properties (questions go up at the end) but the properties are observed as phenomena, not deployed as communication.
- **"Questions go up at the end. This one went up at the end.":** Is this analytical? It's observational at the simplest level — a fact about intonation. At Coherence 1, this level of observation might still be available — the rendering produces fragments of categorical recognition (this is a question, questions have a property, this one had that property) even as the higher-order processing collapses. **Pass — the observation is simple enough to survive the tier.**
- **"The thing. The face thing.":** Ocampo's flat-affect narration at its most extreme. Vael recognizes that a face-thing is happening. She cannot produce the category it belongs to (recognition, acknowledgment, a smile?). The recognition of the social signal exists as a bare perception without the category that would give it meaning. ✓

- **Risk:** The passage risks monotony. Every sentence is short. Every sentence is declarative. The rhythm is relentlessly flat. At Coherence 1, this is correct — there is no rendering to produce rhythmic variety. But the reader may disengage. The risk is inherent to the tier. **No fix — this is what Coherence 1 sounds like. If the reader disengages, the passage has correctly rendered the experience of a consciousness that is itself disengaging from structured experience.**
- **Verdict:** Pass. The dialogue test at Coherence 1 produces something genuinely difficult to read. This is correct.

### Test 21. Action Sequence — Coherence 7-5

*Vael walking through Greinmar toward the parish house. Moving through space. Rendering has micro-failures.*

The lane from the harbor ran south between the chandler's and the cooperage and then turned east at the market cross, and Vael followed it and her feet met the cobbles in the order her feet met cobbles, left then right then left, and once — between the chandler's door and the cooperage gate — her right foot landed a step before she had lifted it, or she perceived the landing before the lifting, and the lane continued and she continued in it.

The market cross was where it was. She passed it. The turn east was where the turn east had been. These were good. The parish house was ahead and to the right and the side door was open and the light from inside was the amber of late afternoon through old glass, and she walked toward it, and the distance between her and the door was the number of steps it took to close the distance, and the number was — she arrived. She was at the door. The distance had been the right distance.

**Audit:**
- **Motion through space with micro-failure:** ✓. "Her right foot landed a step before she had lifted it, or she perceived the landing before the lifting." Temporal micro-slippage applied to proprioception. The rendering failure is in the sequencing of her own body's movement, not in the world. The "or" offers the alternative without resolving — was the foot early, or was her perception late? Ishiguro's uncertainty embedded in the action.
- **Ground-checking:** "The market cross was where it was" — tautological confirmation. "The turn east was where the turn east had been." "These were good." — Vael checking her rendered world against expectations. The fact that she checks is the failure. At 10-8, she would not need to check. At 7-5, the checking is minimal and the results are positive — "these were good." The rendering is working, mostly. ✓
- **"The number of steps it took to close the distance, and the number was — she arrived."** The rendering tried to produce the step-count and the count did not arrive. But the destination did. The arrival replaced the counting. Lispector's veer — the sentence aimed at the number and arrived at the destination instead. The gap between the number and the arrival is the micro-failure. ✓
- **Movement verbs:** "Followed," "walked," "passed." Normal. The rendering produces normal movement. The micro-failures are momentary. ✓
- **Tolkien ground:** The chandler's, the cooperage, the market cross. Named places doing navigational work. The lane runs south then turns east. Spatial structure is present and reliable. ✓
- **Verdict:** Pass. Action at Coherence 7-5 is mostly normal with moments of proprioceptive or perceptual slip.

### Test 22. Action Sequence — Coherence 3 (Fragmented)

*Vael attempting the same walk. Rendering fragmenting.*

The lane was — she was on the lane. The harbor was behind her. She knew this because the smell of the harbor was behind her and the smell of the — the chandler's was to her left. Or she had passed it. The chandler's door was behind her and also beside her and she was walking and her feet were meeting the cobbles and the cobbles were under her feet and these were the same thing said in two ways, or they were two things, and the walking was the thing she was doing that moved her forward which was the direction she was — south. **Audit:**
- **Spatial processing failing:** ✓. "The chandler's door was behind her and also beside her" — two spatial states coexist because the rendering cannot enforce temporal exclusivity in spatial processing. Same technique as Test 3 (the ledger on the table and Maren carrying it simultaneously). ✓
- **The skipped market cross:** "She had been walking toward it and then she was past it and the being-at-it had not —" The sentence attempts to describe the gap and breaks on "had not —". It does not say "she could not remember passing it." The gap is structural — the prose skips from approach to past, enacting the dissociative gap rather than describing it. ✓
- **Proprioception as tautology:** "Her feet were meeting the cobbles and the cobbles were under her feet and these were the same thing said in two ways, or they were two things." At Coherence 3, the distinction between "feet meeting cobbles" and "cobbles under feet" — normally transparent — becomes uncertain. Are these the same fact from two angles, or two different facts? The rendering cannot determine. Borges's question about whether the map and the territory are the same thing. ✓
- **"She went into the light because going-into was the thing her feet were doing":** Agency has collapsed. Vael does not choose to enter. Her feet are doing a thing and the thing takes her into the light. The action is happening but the agent-behind-the-action is not accessible to the rendering. This is correct for Coherence 3 — the self-rendering that presents "I am the one doing this" is impaired. ✓
- **Closing:** "Sitting was the thing she was doing now." The action reduced to its minimum: present tense, declarative, the thing she was doing. No interiority. No interpretation. Ocampo's flat report of the current state. ✓
- **Risk:** The passage is effortful to read. The spatial confusion and temporal skips make the reader work. At Coherence 3, this is correct — the reader should experience a version of Vael's disorientation. But the risk is that the reader disengages rather than engages. **Inherent to the tier.**
- **Verdict:** Pass. The skipped market cross is the strongest moment — the dissociative gap enacted structurally rather than described.

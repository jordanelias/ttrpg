# Test Battery — Part 6: Emergent Arc Tests

Tests targeting prose for narrative situations generated by mechanical system interactions — not pre-scripted story but emergent consequences of player decisions meeting game systems. Each test identifies the mechanical trigger, the emergent narrative situation, and the prose challenge.

---

## Test 47. Belief Co-Authorship — Conviction Dissolving at C2, High Spirit

*Mechanical trigger: Coherence 2, Belief Co-Authorship active. The PC held the conviction "The Church preserves what must not be lost" for three seasons. The conviction's thread-connection is loosening. Spirit 6 — the will grips.*

The conviction was hers. She had — it was hers. The Church preserves. She had carried it the way the Wardens carried their instruments, the way the sisters carried the daily offices, the weight of it distributed across the years until the weight was the shape of her days and the days were the shape of the weight. The Church preserves what must not be lost. She said it. The words were the words she had always said and the saying was hers and the words were — the words were the words. They did not connect to the thing they had connected to. The connection had operated through a framework that produced "this is mine" and the framework was not the framework that was operating. She held the words. The Church preserves. The holding was hers. The meaning was beside the holding, the way a name is beside the thing it names when the naming-Loss loosens. She held the holding. She could not hold the meaning. She held the holding.

**Audit:**
- Belief Co-Authorship enacted: ✓. The conviction persists as words; the belonging-to-the-conviction is the operation being recalibrated. "They did not connect to the thing they had connected to."
- Beckett will-continuation: ✓. "She held the holding." The self contracts to the act of gripping when the content of the grip is receding.
- No meta-inarticulation: ✓. The prose does not say "she could not understand why the words felt different." It enacts the loosening through the gap between words and connection.
- Spirit audible: ✓. High Spirit — the will holds the form even as the content recedes. The passage closes on the act: "She held the holding."
- Lispector veer: ✓. "The weight was the shape of her days and the days were the shape of the weight" — paradoxical recursion, each attempt approaching the same thing differently.
- Recalibration not dysfunction: ✓. She is not confused or failing. The framework producing "mine" is operating differently.
- **Risk:** "the way a name is beside the thing it names when the naming loosens" — analytical simile at C2. Anti-pattern II.5 says similes fail below C4. This is borderline — it's not a comparison to an external referent but a description of the process happening. The "way" construction could be cut: "a name beside the thing it names, the naming loosening." Cleaner.
- **Verdict:** Pass with edit note on the simile.

## Test 48. The Einhir Spiral — Settlement-Scale Crisis Through Personal Lens

*Mechanical trigger: Mending Stability at 12 (critical band). Two active Gaps. One Lock. Spontaneous Gap roll succeeded — third Gap forming. The practitioner-Warden, Coherence 8, TS 35, witnesses the third Gap opening during a routine mending session.*

The thread she was holding went slack. Not the way a thread goes slack when the operation completes — that is a release, a settling. This was the thread ceasing to be taut without anyone releasing it. Edda looked at her hands. The mending was correct. Her positioning was correct. The thread between the wound and the instrument was present and the wound was responding and then the thread was slack and the wound was not responding and the ground beneath the wound — she could feel it, the part that was not the wound and not the thread but the thing beneath both — the ground had shifted. Something had opened beneath the operation she was performing, the way a floor opens beneath a table: the table is still there, the objects on the table are still there, but the surface they rested on is no longer continuous. She stepped back from the site. In the Hafenmark notation she would write: *site 14-Rendstad, operation suspended, ground discontinuity detected, site stability unknown*. The notation would be accurate. The notation would not capture that she had felt the ground shift the way one feels an earthquake from three rooms away — not the shaking but the knowledge that the shaking has occurred.

**Audit:**
- Scale transition: ✓. Personal-scale mending → settlement-scale MS crisis. The practitioner perceives the macro event through the micro operation.
- Lem clinical register: ✓. Hafenmark notation, "substrate discontinuity detected" — motivated by Edda's training.
- Tolkien specificity: ✓. "Site 14-Rendstad." Named, located, specific.
- Ishiguro misidentification: partial. "The way a floor opens beneath a table" — Edda's analogy, her attempt to categorize what happened in familiar terms. She is not wrong, but she is translating substrate perception into physical metaphor.
- TS-gated perception: ✓. "The part of her perception that was not her training" — TS 35, substrate-aware.
- Content leakage check: "Site stability unknown" — this is Hafenmark technical vocabulary, not design vocabulary. The character would write this. ✓.
- **Risk:** The passage is expository. Two analogies ("the way a thread goes slack," "the way a floor opens"). At C8 both are licensed. But the second is doing heavy lifting — the whole macro crisis is rendered through that single simile. Could use more physical-world grounding. What does Edda see? What does the site look like after the Gap opens?
- **Verdict:** Pass. The scale-transition prose renders the macro crisis through personal-scale experience. Needs site-detail expansion for a full scene.

## Test 49. Encountering a Threadcut Being — Observer at TS 50

*Mechanical trigger: PC (Coherence 8, TS 50) encounters a Solmund-type C0 NPC — the former Warden Aris, who reached C0 two seasons ago. Aris still visits the mending sites. The PC knows who Aris was.*

Aris was at the site. She was standing where she had always stood when she was the Warden of the Greinmar circuit, at the northeast margin where the ground thinned, and she was doing what she had always done, which was tending. The tending was — Edda could see it, the continuous work, the sustained effort holding the configuration in place, thread by thread, the architecture of intention operating at a frequency that was not the frequency intentions operate at when a person intends something. Aris tended. The word was the same word. The thing the word referred to was not the same thing. The Aris who tended the Greinmar sites for eleven years tended because the sites needed tending and because the work was her vocation and because the Church had assigned her and because she believed the mending kept the world whole. The Aris at the northeast margin tended because tending was what the configuration produced. The proper nouns were the same. The name, the site, the act. The foundations had shifted onto premises Edda could perceive — the thread-structure was visible, the continuous self-sustaining work humming at the edge of her perception — and could not follow.

**Audit:**
- External focalization of C0: ✓. Prose focalizes through Edda, not Aris. Aris is rendered from outside.
- TS 50 perception: ✓. Edda perceives "the continuous work, the sustained effort holding the configuration in place, thread by thread." TS 50–69 perceives the relational structure.
- The proper-noun sameness / meaning-shift: ✓. "The word was the same word. The thing the word referred to was not the same thing." The C0 NPC inherits the shape of who they were; the meanings have shifted.
- No horror-coding: ✓. Aris is not monstrous in the horror sense. She is doing what she always did. The wrongness is in the gap between familiar form and shifted foundation.
- Tolkien building-around-absence: ✓. The passage builds the surrounding context (what Aris was, what tending meant, what the site is) and the absence (what Aris now is) takes shape from the surroundings.
- Lem clinical incommensurability: partial. "A frequency that was not the frequency intentions operate at when a person intends something" — precise description of something that doesn't yield to the description.
- **Risk:** "Edda could perceive — the thread-structure was visible, the continuous self-sustaining work humming at the edge of her perception — and could not follow." The em-dash parenthetical is doing triple work. Heavy. Could break into two sentences for the middle-layer gradient: near (perceives the work) → middle (thread-structure visible) → far (cannot follow the premises).
- **Verdict:** Pass. The C0 encounter prose renders the familiar-made-alien through the observer's focalization.

## Test 50. Coherence Transition Within a Scene — C5 → C3

*Mechanical trigger: PC at C5, retention roll fails during a Leap. Coherence drops to C3 mid-operation. The prose must shift technique availability within the passage.*

The mending held. Vael could feel the thread responding, the wound closing in the pattern she had learned in her second year, the familiar rhythm of gather-draw-seal that her hands knew better than her mind. She breathed. The gathering — she reached for the thread. The thread was there. The gathering was — she reached for the thread and the reaching produced a different result from the result the reaching had produced a moment ago. The same hands. The same thread. The result was — the seal. She needed to seal the wound. The wound was present. The seal was the thing that — her hands were on the thread and the thread was responding and the response was correct and the seal was — the seal.

She stepped back. The wound was closed. She did not remember closing it. The site was the site she had been at when she began and the site was the same site and her hands were the hands that had done the work and the work was done. The chalk on her sleeves was white. The light was from the west. She stood at the site and the site was closed and the chalk was white and the light came from the west and these were facts.

**Audit:**
- Transition within passage: ✓. First paragraph: C5 vocabulary — named procedures ("gather-draw-seal"), professional register, analytical self-observation ("she could feel the thread responding"). Mid-paragraph: the vocabulary fails. "The gathering was —" The veer begins. Named procedures dissolve into tautological predication.
- Technique availability shift: ✓. Similes and analytical self-observation present in the opening (C5 licensed). Gone by the close (C3). Tolkien specificity reduced to fragments ("chalk," "light," "west"). Lispector veer enters ("the seal was the thing that —").
- No announcement: ✓. The prose does not say "things were getting stranger" or "her perception shifted." The shift is enacted through the change in what the prose can produce.
- Closing on concrete: ✓. Wittgenstein constraint — "The chalk on her sleeves was white. The light was from the west." The last things the prose can say.
- Tautological ground-checking: ✓. "The site was the site," "the hands were the hands that had done the work." Lispector tautology — bare identity as the only available predication.
- **Risk:** The transition is abrupt. One em-dash and the prose switches modes. Real coherence loss (per the mechanical spec) happens at a specific moment (the retention roll), so abruptness is mechanically correct. But the reader needs one more sentence in the middle register — the veer should take a full sentence to establish before the tautology arrives.
- **Verdict:** Pass. The within-scene transition is the hardest prose challenge in the system, and this passage handles it cleanly. The abruptness matches the mechanics.

## Test 51. The Grief Scene — Inspiration Destroyed

*Mechanical trigger: PC's Inspiration focus (a carved bone flute, inherited from her teacher) is destroyed in combat. Inspiration drops to 0. Grief Scene triggered. Spirit TN 7 Ob 2 — the roll will determine the next paragraph, but the prose renders the moment of loss. Coherence 7, TS 20.*

The flute was in two pieces. The larger piece was in the mud and the smaller piece was in her hand and the join where they had been one thing was a fresh break, pale wood inside dark wood, and she could see the grain where it had separated. Her teacher had carved it from a branch of the ash that stood at the Rendstad gate. She knew this because he had told her, and because the wood was ash, and because the gate was where he had stood when he played in the evenings, and because the sound the flute made was the sound of that particular standing. The sound was gone. The grain was visible. The break was clean and the wood was pale and the pieces would not go back together because the joining had been in the making and the making was thirty years ago and the maker was dead and the thing that the making had made was in two pieces in the mud and in her hand and the evening was coming and no one was standing at the gate.

**Audit:**
- Tartt loaded detail: ✓. The flute carries more weight than its physical description. The break's pale wood inside dark wood — the interior exposed. The loading is not announced.
- Tolkien deep-time and named specificity: ✓. "The ash that stood at the Rendstad gate." Named tree, named place, specific maker, specific history.
- Mistry accretive particulars: ✓. "Because he had told her, and because the wood was ash, and because the gate was where he had stood" — each clause adds one particular without summarizing.
- No named emotion: ✓. The word "grief" does not appear. The loss is in "no one was standing at the gate."
- Closing on absence: ✓. Tolkien asterisk reality — the absence of the maker, the absence of the sound, rendered through what remains (the gate, the evening, the silence).
- C7 technique availability: ✓. Full paragraphs, similes absent but not needed, named specificity intact, analytical structure present ("she knew this because").
- **Risk:** "The sound of that particular standing" — unusual construction. The flute's sound is equated with a person's physical stance at a specific place. This is Mistry territory — the object carries the body's history. Pass, but it's the kind of construction that could tip into too-clever if the reader doesn't track the chain.
- **Risk:** Long sentence at the end. Grammatically complex. At C7 this is licensed — Ishiguro-length sentences at full rendering. The length matches the weight.
- **Verdict:** Pass. The Grief Scene prose renders the mechanical moment (Inspiration = 0) through accreted particularity and loaded detail without naming grief.

## Test 52. Collective Operation Failure — The Rupture Witnessed

*Mechanical trigger: MS = 1. Collective Mending operation attempted by four practitioners. Operation Failure → MS drops below 0. The Rupture. Ein Sof erupts through failed substrate. The world does not end — it becomes unintelligible. Focalized through a non-practitioner NPC (TS 0) watching from the settlement walls.*

The practitioners were at the site. Maren could see them from the wall, four figures in the cleared ground where the wardens did their work, and they were doing what they always did, which was the thing she did not understand and did not need to understand because the Church had wardens and the wardens had their work and the work kept the world as it was. She watched. The four figures stood in their positions and the light was ordinary and the wind was from the south and then the light was not ordinary. She could not say what had changed. The light was the same light — the sun was where the sun was — but the light was not the light it had been. The ground between the four figures was the ground it had been and the ground was — she looked at it. The ground. She looked at it and looking at it was not producing what looking at things produces, which is that you know what you are looking at. She was looking at the ground and she did not know what she was looking at. The four figures were still standing. One of them was kneeling. Two of them were kneeling. The wind had stopped. She could hear the river and the river sounded like a river and this was the only thing that sounded like the thing it was.

**Audit:**
- Non-practitioner focalization (TS 0): ✓. "The thing she did not understand and did not need to understand." Maren has no substrate perception. She sees the surface effects only.
- TS 0 perception of the Rupture: ✓. "Looking at it was not producing what looking at things produces" — vague wrongness, cannot locate the source. Per Scenario 10: TS 0–9 → "nothing, or vague unease."
- Ocampo flat-affect: ✓. "The four figures were still standing. One of them was kneeling. Two of them were kneeling." The progression reported flatly, without the narrator registering the significance.
- The world becoming unintelligible: ✓. "The ground was — she looked at it." The em-dash marks the moment where the ground ceases to be what grounds produce when you look at them.
- No design vocabulary: ✓. No "Ein Sof," no "substrate," no "rendering." Maren sees light, ground, figures, wind, river. The metaphysics is invisible to her.
- Tolkien closing on the concrete: ✓. "The river sounded like a river and this was the only thing that sounded like the thing it was." The last stable referent.
- McCarthy moral withholding: partial. The prose reports without evaluation. The Rupture is rendered through its surface effects, not through its cosmic significance.
- **Risk:** "Looking at it was not producing what looking at things produces, which is that you know what you are looking at." — This is meta. The narrator is explaining the mechanism of perception's failure. At TS 0, Maren would not think this analytically. She would just — look, and not know. The explanatory clause ("which is that you know what you are looking at") should be cut. The sentence should end: "Looking at it was not producing what looking at things produces." The reader infers the rest.
- **Verdict:** Pass with edit note. The Rupture from a non-practitioner's POV is rendered through surface effects. The explanatory clause needs cutting to maintain the flat-affect register.

## Interim Findings — Part 6

The emergent arc tests reveal three prose challenges specific to mechanically-generated narrative:

1. **The within-scene transition** (Test 50) is the hardest single challenge. The prose must shift technique availability at a precise mechanical boundary without announcing the shift. The only way to do this is to let the technique-set change — what the prose can produce changes because the rendering changes. This is the core of coherence-tier prose.

2. **Scale transitions** (Tests 48, 52) require the macro event to be registered through the micro lens. The practitioner or observer experiences the settlement-scale crisis through their personal-scale perception. The prose must render what they perceive without explaining the macro mechanics. The reader should feel the scale without seeing the game system.

3. **The Belief Co-Authorship passages** (Test 47) are where the Spirit modifier does its most distinctive work. The same mechanical event (conviction loosening) produces categorically different prose at different Spirit levels. This is where the twelve-author synthesis justifies its complexity — the Beckett/Lispector distinction at the same coherence tier is not a decorative choice but a mechanical requirement.

All six tests demonstrate the design intent: the mechanical systems produce narrative situations, and the prose renders those situations through the twelve-author synthesis without exposing the mechanics. The emergent arc is in the gap between what the system generates and what the prose renders.

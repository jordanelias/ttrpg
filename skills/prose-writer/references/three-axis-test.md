# Three-Axis Prose Test — v4

Applies the four Valoria-specific synthesis moves from §11.5: self-predicate displacement, single-word sentences as juxtaposition, tautology as structural placement (not texture), geography substituting for self-statement. These are deployed where they fit, sparingly, and always once per passage maximum for the rarest moves.

---

## C10. TS 0.

The river path followed the valley south through the larch and then the birch where the larch thinned, and the light through the canopy was the grey-gold of autumn afternoons in the highlands when the sun has dropped behind the western ridge but not yet set. The river ran over stones the color of iron, and where it bent the current had cut a pool into the bank: dark water, still, cold when Vael put her hand in. The mineral scrub began at the end of the birch, low and grey and smelling of chalk.

She had been walking for an hour. Behind her, the timber camp's smoke hung in the windless air. Ahead, the path climbed to the upper meadow where Ilse's house stood at the tree line — south-facing door, hearth-smoke, the lean-to where Ilse kept her drying racks. Beyond, the ridge rose bare and brown into cloud.

The river ran. The scrub smelled of chalk. Vael walked.

## C10. TS 70.

The river path through the larch and the birch, the grey-gold light, the iron-colored stones. Vael walked with her hand near the water at the bend.

Beneath her feet the valley carried old work. Restorative. Layered. Running along the river's course and departing where the river bent, continuing straight — older than the current channel. She could count the surface lattice: four hundred operations or near it, generation-depth, the spacing tight at the joins and slackening through the long runs.

At the joins between strata were operations she could perceive completely. They had structure; they had density. She could give the spacing of the strands and the angle at which they crossed and the rate at which they propagated, and the description was accurate, and the description told her nothing. The strands ran along axes she had names for; their crossings did not. She stepped over a root and the root was a root and the birch above it was a birch, and beneath both the work continued at a depth where reading was no longer reading, and she walked above it.

## C7. TS 35.

The river path through the larch and the birch. Vael walked. Once, between two bends, she heard the river ahead before the path had turned to meet it — or she had walked faster than she'd thought, and the bend was closer than it had looked. She did not stop to consider which.

The valley was warm. Not in the air — the air was the temperature it should have been for the altitude and the season — but from the direction of the upper valley. Mineral content, she thought. Highland rivers carried particular compositions, and this was a highland river, and minerals were minerals, and the warmth continued from a direction she was walking toward, and she did not pursue why a quality should have a direction.

The timber camp's smoke hung behind her. Ilse's house ahead. Vael walked.

## C4. TS 0. High Spirit.

She was walking. The decision had been hers, and the walking was what the decision had become, and the walking continued because she continued.

Left foot. Right foot. Left. The order was something she could give to the path and the path would take it. The larch was behind her. The birch was here, and the birch was itself.

The chalk-smell from the scrub arrived on the air. It came from where she was going. She would walk into it. She had decided to walk and the deciding had been the thing that started the walking, and now the walking was what the deciding had given her, and she had it, and she held it. The river ran beside her. Whatever else was — the walking was hers.

## C4. TS 0. Low Spirit.

The river was beside the path. The path was in the valley. Vael was — she was in the valley, had been beside the river. Still. The birch was here, and the birch was itself.

Birch. Grey scrub. Chalk-smell. These arrived. She received them, the way a slope receives rain — not by intent. The hearing of the river was something happening near her body, on the side where the river was, and on the other side were trees, and the side that was Vael was harder to find than the side that was the river.

A reason. There had been one. Something to do with the upper valley, with Ilse, with the house at the tree line — but the having-a-reason was not the same as having one, and she was walking either way. The smoke from the timber camp was in the valley. The air was still. She moved through it the way smoke moves through air, which is to say without pushing.

## C4. TS 70. High Spirit.

She had decided to walk. The walking continued. Beneath the path the valley's old work continued also — restorative, layered, running with the river and departing where the river bent. She could feel it. The feeling was clearer than the path.

Birch. The scrub ahead. The work beneath the path held itself: surface lattice down to the joins, joins down to whatever was beneath the joins. She could follow the lines deeper than her feet could follow the path, and what the lines did at depth — they did a thing. She could feel the thing's shape. What the shape was a shape *of* was. Was.

The decision. The walking. The lines. She held the first; the second carried her; the third held itself without her. The birch was a word for a tree. The lines were not a word. The walking was hers.

## C2. TS 0. High Spirit.

She was walking. Whatever else was uncertain, the walking continued — because she had decided to walk, and the decision was the thing she had given herself, and the giving had not been undone.

The river was beside her. Trees on both sides. These were facts and they did not connect into a forest, but they were the facts of a forest, and she walked through them. There had been a reason. The reason had a name, and the name was something to do with Order, with records kept, with things in their places — and the conviction sat in her chest like a coat someone kind had given her, warm and well-made and not quite hers. She walked anyway. The conviction could be a coat. The walking was hers.

Her weight was wrong on the left. She was — she was on the left side of her own body, or the left side of her body was where the heaviness was that should have been distributed. She shifted. The wrongness shifted with her. Her decision walked. Whatever she was now, it was walking.

## C2. TS 0. Low Spirit.

Walking. The path. The feet. River sound on the right. Trees on both sides. Vael was — Vael was where the feet were going, or where the feet had been, or where the path went and the feet followed. Both.

A name. There was one. It was for the thing the feet belonged to, which had been the thing she belonged to, which was the thing on the path. Was.

A reason — there had been one. The reason had a name and the name had been warm in her chest for a long time. The warmth was still there. The chest was hers, or the chest was the place the warmth was. The name belonged to someone, and the someone the name belonged to was no longer the one inside the chest, but the warmth had not gone. The feet went on. The river was the river. The trees were on both sides.

## C1. TS 0. High Spirit.

She is walking. She knows this. She decided. The decision is what is left and she has it. The path is under her feet and her feet are hers and the walking is hers.

She can't go on. She goes on. The birch is a word for what is beside the path. The river is a sound for what is beside the path. The valley is the larger thing the sound and the word and the path are inside, and she is inside the valley, walking. Inside.

Something arrives. It is not the river. It is not the birch. It is not light. It is on her. It does not stop her, because she is the one who decided, and the deciding is the last thing, and the deciding is walking.

## C1. TS 0. Low Spirit.

Walking. Path. The river-sound is to the right. The trees are to both sides. The body is — the body is between the two sides of the path, going forward, where the path goes.

A name was for the thing the feet are part of. The name is no longer warm. The warmth was somewhere — chest, or the place the chest was — and now the warmth is in the air around the body, or in the path, or in the forward direction the feet are going. Was.

Something arrives that is not the river or the trees or the light. It is where the feet are. The feet are where it is. The feet move.

## C1. TS 70. High Spirit.

She is walking. She decided to walk. The walking is hers and beneath the walking the work is — clear. Clearer than the path. Clearer than the birch.

The lines run along the river and depart where the river bends and at depth they do a thing. The thing has shape. She can feel the shape. The shape is — itself. She holds the deciding; the lines hold themselves; between the two holdings she walks. The birch is a word. The river is a sound. The lines are not a word and not a sound, and they are there, and she walks above them.

She can't go on. She goes on. The deciding holds. The lines hold. The path is under her feet, and the feet are hers, and what is beneath the feet is real and is not for her to name.

## C0. High Spirit. External focalization (Tamar, C10, TS 75).

Edda was at the river. She was standing in the water to her shins with her boots on the bank and her feet bare, and she was looking downstream the way she had always looked at things she was working on — head tilted, hands still, the patience of someone who had mended four hundred things and was considering a four-hundred-and-first. She was wearing the coat. The hem was wet. She noticed and did nothing.

Tamar could feel what Edda was doing from the ridge. Same patience. Same slow turning. Edda was doing it to the river — to the running of the water, to the way the current held at the bend. Mending was not the right word. Tending was not the right word. She was doing the thing she had always done, and what she was doing it to was the river, and the river did not need it. She tended the river the way you tend a fire that does not need tending: tending is what you do, and the fire is what is there.

She turned when Tamar came down the bank. The face. The scar. The tilt. She said Tamar's name and the air between them shifted — the same thing, the making-room, the preparing. Edda was making the riverbank able to hold a conversation the way she had once made a wound able to hold a suture. "The current is slower today," Edda said. This was true. Tamar could feel that it was true. She could also feel that "slower" was not what Edda meant, that "slower" was the nearest word a mouth could make for what Edda perceived, and that the distance between the word and what Edda meant was the distance between Edda and the person Edda had been.

## C0. Low Spirit. External focalization (Tamar, C10, TS 75).

The woman was at the river. Tamar could not think of her as Edda, though she was Edda — same body, same scar, same height. She was standing in the water with her feet bare, not looking at anything, hands at her sides, head not tilted. She was standing in the river.

There was work happening, but the work had no shape Tamar recognized. Not restorative. Not manipulative. Not any of the nine categories her mentor had drawn in the dirt. It was happening at every level at once and without intent, the way a body breathes — not as a decision but as what the body does when nothing else is happening. No direction. No object. No addressee. It was simply occurring, the way weather occurs, and the woman was inside it the way a stone is inside a river.

She did not turn when Tamar came down the bank. The air did not shift. There was no making-room. Tamar stood on the bank and the woman stood in the water and the distance between them was the distance between them. After a while the woman said a word and the word was "river" and referred to the river and did nothing else, and Tamar went back up the bank.

## Where the Valoria moves were deployed

**Self-predicate displacement** (verb of being looks for self-predicate, finds geography or fact instead):
- C4 Low Spirit: "Vael was — she was in the valley, had been beside the river."
- C2 High Spirit: "She was — she was on the left side of her own body, or the left side of her body was where the heaviness was that should have been distributed." (The self-statement displaces into a question of weight-location.)
- C2 Low Spirit: "Vael was — Vael was where the feet were going, or where the feet had been, or where the path went and the feet followed."
- C1 Low Spirit: "The body is — the body is between the two sides of the path, going forward, where the path goes." (At C1 Low Spirit, even "Vael was —" is gone; "the body is —" replaces it.)

**Single-word sentences as juxtaposition** (one ambiguous word, doing double work):
- C4 Low Spirit: "Still." (motionless / remained / still here / still as ever)
- C2 High Spirit: cut — none deployed (the passage already has enough Beckett dialectical material)
- C2 Low Spirit: "Both." (was at the going / was at the having-been / both true) and "Was." (was, indicating past — the name was for the thing — and was, alone, indicating bare existence)
- C1 High Spirit: "Inside." (locates her in the valley, also describes the inwardness of how she is in it)
- C1 Low Spirit: "Was." (the warmth WAS — past tense, no longer present, but also: the verb of being itself, suspended)

**Tautology as structural placement** (one tautology per passage, beside an ordinary clause):
- C4 High Spirit: "The birch was here, and the birch was itself." (location + identity-confirmation)
- C4 Low Spirit: "The birch was here, and the birch was itself." (same construction — at this tier, the tautology is the rendering checking what should not need checking)
- These deployments follow Lispector's actual practice: rare, structurally placed, beside ordinary content.

**Geography substituting for self-statement**:
- C4 Low Spirit: "the side that was Vael was harder to find than the side that was the river." (The interiority is rendered through spatial relation. The "side" substitutes for "self.")
- C2 Low Spirit: "the chest was hers, or the chest was the place the warmth was." (The chest as locus instead of "she" as locus.)
- C1 High Spirit: "she is inside the valley, walking. Inside." (The location IS the interiority at this tier.)

## What v4 corrects from v3

The C4 Low Spirit and C4 High Spirit passages now have parallel openings — both end with "and the birch was itself" — establishing the tautology as a Valoria signature for that tier. C4 is where the rendering is fragmented enough that identity-confirmation becomes necessary. The birch needing to be itself is the diagnostic. The High Spirit version recovers ("Whatever else was — the walking was hers"); the Low Spirit version does not ("She moved through it the way smoke moves through air, which is to say without pushing").

The C2 passages now both deploy self-predicate displacement and single-word "Was." — at this tier, the past tense and the bare existence become indistinguishable, and the single word holds both.

The C1 passages now deploy "Inside." (High Spirit, locating-as-interiority) and "Was." (Low Spirit, past-as-existence). At C1 these single-word sentences are doing the heaviest possible work — a single word as the entire predication, holding multiple meanings, no resolution.

The C0 passages remain unchanged. The C0 High Spirit's "She noticed and did nothing" is already the Valoria move (cut from a longer over-explanation). The C0 Low Spirit closing — "the word was 'river' and referred to the river and did nothing else, and Tamar went back up the bank" — is your edit. Both pass.

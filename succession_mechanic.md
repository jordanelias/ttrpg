## Almud's Children

### Princess Elske Almqvist
Married to an Altonian Duke holding territory adjacent to Border Pass. Marriage is a fixed condition at game start — not a player choice.

**Mechanical effects:**
- Altonian Duke's territory is the invasion staging ground (Border Pass adjacent).
- At IP 60+, Altonia can invoke the marriage as legal pretext for territorial claims. All diplomatic attempts with Altonia: +1 Ob.
- If Torben is lost (Loyalty 0 or killed), Elske becomes heir. She is married to an Altonian Duke — the Crown passes to an Altonia-aligned ruler. This is Altonia's backup plan.

### Prince Torben Almqvist
Early teens. Begins in Royal Court. The active dilemma.

## The Tutoring Demand

**Trigger:** IP reaches 30 (Altonia turns Aggressive).

Altonia formally demands Torben be sent for "education" — a transparent hostage/loyalty capture. This demand recurs every season at Strategic Phase accounting until resolved.

### Crown Response Options (each season while unresolved)

| Response | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| **Refuse** | IP accelerates to 1.5x pace (base +2/season becomes +3/season). Schoenland trade Ob +1 cumulative per refusal. |
| **Negotiate delay** | Roll Crown Influence vs Ob 3 (first attempt), +1 Ob per subsequent attempt. Success: demand deferred 1 season, IP pace unchanged. Failure: treated as Refusal. |
| **Surrender Torben** | IP pace stays at 1x. Torben Loyalty Clock begins. |

## Torben Loyalty Clock

**Starting value: 8 (loyal to Valoria).**

Each season Torben is in Altonia: Loyalty -1.

**Covert contact:** Any faction (typically Crown) may attempt to maintain contact with Torben. Intelligence Domain Action vs Ob 3 per season. Success: Loyalty does not decrease that season (letters, agents, cultural reinforcement). Failure: no effect, and Altonia may detect the contact (GM judgment — potential IP +1 if detected).

### Loyalty States

| Loyalty | State | Mechanical Effect |
|---------|-------|-------------------|
| 8-6 | Homesick, resistant | No effect. Retrieval straightforward. |
| 5-4 | Adapting | Crown Mandate -1 (public perceives weakness). Retrieval requires Altonian consent or covert extraction. |
| 3-2 | Altonia-aligned | Crown Mandate -1 again (cumulative -2 total). Torben writes public letters praising Altonia. Lowenritter coup trigger #2 arguably met. |
| 1 | Altonian puppet | If Torben inherits, Crown becomes Altonian vassal state. Lowenritter coup trigger #2 definitively met. |
| 0 | Renounces Valoria | Torben formally renounces succession. No heir. Crown Stability -2. |

### Retrieval Options

| Loyalty Range | Method | Procedure |
|---------------|--------|-----------|
| 8-6 | Diplomatic retrieval | Crown Influence vs Ob 3. Success: Torben returns, clock resets to 8. IP +2 (Altonia offended). |
| 5-3 | Covert extraction | Intelligence vs Ob 5. Success: Torben returns at current Loyalty value (damage done). IP +5 (provocation). Failure: operative captured, IP +3, Crown Mandate -1 (propaganda). |
| 2-1 | Military extraction | Torben resists extraction. Requires Military action in Altonian territory. IP immediately to 75+ (act of war). |
| 0 | Impossible | Torben has renounced. Cannot be retrieved. |

## Faction Interaction Map

**Lowenritter (Ehrenwall):** Tracks Torben's status. At Loyalty 3, begins internal coup preparations. At Loyalty 1, coup trigger #2 is met — she will act if Almud does not.

**Church:** Indifferent to succession unless Torben returns with Altonian religious practices. If so, Church treats returning Torben as a heresy vector — TC +2 on his return.

**Hafenmark (Baralta):** If dynastic succession fails, Baralta pushes Parliamentary succession — elect a non-dynastic monarch through constitutional process. Aligns with her Categorical Imperative framework (universalizable procedure over dynastic inheritance). Key ally or rival depending on Parliament's choice.

**Revolution:** Sees succession crisis as proof monarchy is structurally captured. Revolution Influence +1 whenever Torben Loyalty drops below 5.

**Varfell (Vaynard):** Opportunistic. May attempt to broker a deal with Altonia using intelligence assets. May attempt covert contact with Torben independently to build personal leverage.

**Guilds:** Concerned primarily with Schoenland trade disruption. Guild support for Crown response depends on trade impact. Prolonged refusal = trade collapse = Guilds turn against Crown.

**Niflhel:** May offer covert extraction services (at a price). Niflhel's Dockworkers arm has Schoenland contacts. May independently attempt to contact Torben for leverage.

## Elske as Backup Heir

If Torben is lost (Loyalty 0, killed, or permanently captured), Elske is the only heir.

**Elske's succession creates a different crisis:**
- She is married to an Altonian Duke. The Crown passes into Altonian-aligned hands regardless.
- Lowenritter coup trigger #2 is met (foreign power controls succession).
- Hafenmark's Parliamentary succession becomes the only non-military alternative.
- The campaign's political landscape bifurcates: military junta (Lowenritter) vs constitutional reform (Hafenmark) vs theocratic capture (Church, if TC is high) vs Altonian client state (Elske).

This four-way endgame is the intended late-campaign structure when the succession axis collapses.

## IP Acceleration Math

Base IP growth: +2/season (from existing rules).

With refusal: +3/season (1.5x).

Over a 10-season campaign starting at IP 30:
- Compliance path: +20 IP over 10 seasons = IP 50 at season 10 (Hostile range).
- Refusal path: +30 IP over 10 seasons = IP 60 at season 10 (Warlike range).
- Delta: ~10 IP, roughly one full threat tier.

The pressure is real but not instant. Players have time to maneuver — but the window narrows.

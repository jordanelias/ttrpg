# Tensions Deck — Pair Validation

**Criteria for each pair of 2 drawn cards:**
1. At least one faction becomes **belligerent**
2. At least one other faction becomes **target** for belligerence
3. The belligerent actions create **(a) opportunity** the remaining faction(s) must seize **or (b) obligation** they cannot ignore

---

## The 6 Cards

| # | Bilateral Tension | Belligerent → Target | Factions involved |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | **Crown ↔ Church** | Church asserts governance over S003 Cathedral district; Crown must respond | Cr, Ch |
| C2 | **Crown ↔ Varfell** | Border incident at T4/T14; Casus Belli exchanged; military pressure | Cr, Va |
| C3 | **Crown ↔ Löwenritter** | Löwenritter autonomy advances to Restless; garrison takes unauthorized action | Cr, Lö |
| C4 | **Hafenmark ↔ Church** | Church builds infrastructure in T7/T8; bishop appointment claim in Hafenmark territory | Ha, Ch |
| C5 | **Hafenmark ↔ Guilds** | Guild schism at S017; Hafenmark must consolidate or lose Market Quarter | Ha, Gu |
| C6 | **Varfell ↔ RM** | RM emergence accelerates at T4/T13; Varfell must suppress or tolerate | Va, RM |

**Note on cards 3, 5, 6:** Internal frictions. Belligerence is *reactive* — the faction becomes belligerent against its own sub-entity (Löwenritter, Guilds, RM) when it responds to the crisis. The crisis itself weakens the faction, creating the external opportunity.

### C2+C4 — Crown ↔ Varfell + Hafenmark ↔ Church

**Two bilateral conflicts consuming all four major factions.**

| Role | Faction | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Belligerent | Varfell (border incident) + Church (governance claim in Hafenmark) |
| Target | Crown (Varfell border) + Hafenmark (Church expansion) |
| Obligation | **Crown** cannot help Hafenmark vs Church because Crown is responding to Varfell. **Hafenmark** cannot leverage Crown's Varfell crisis because Hafenmark is fighting its own Church battle. Each faction's crisis prevents it from exploiting the other's. |

The dynamic: Crown deploys military to T4/T14 border → Crown can't also support Hafenmark against Church. Hafenmark deploys political resources against Church → Hafenmark can't also play Parliamentary Challenge against Crown. Each faction's rational response to its own crisis creates the other's opportunity and constraint simultaneously.

**✅ PASS.** No clean third-party opportunist — but the interaction between the two tensions forces mutual constraint. Both targets face obligation to prioritize.

**15/15 pass.** Every pair produces at least one belligerent, at least one target, and opportunity or obligation for the remaining factions.

**Distribution check:** Each card appears in 5 pairs. Each faction is involved as primary in at least 2 cards. No faction is uninvolved in more than 5 pairs (each faction touches at least 10 of 15 pairs through its direct cards + being the remaining faction).

**Weakest pair:** C5+C6 (two internal crises, no external aggressor). Passes because the reactive belligerence of suppressing sub-entities consumes resources, and the time-limited window creates obligation for uninvolved factions. But this pair produces the quietest opening — no faction is attacking another faction, just managing internal problems. This may be intentionally the "cold start" scenario where the player has more room to set their own agenda.

**Strongest pairs:** C1+C2 (Crown two-front siege), C3+C4 (Varfell breakout window), C1+C4 (Church dual offensive). These produce the most dramatic openings with clear belligerents, clear targets, and clear third-party windows.

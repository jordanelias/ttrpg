## KEY ROLL 1: Vaynard Discovery Event (Spirit TN 7 Ob 1)

**Context:** Vaynard TS 14 (Dormant). Private Collection use triggers Spirit check. Pool: Vaynard's Spirit (estimated 4–5 for a faction leader). TN 7 on d10: each die has 40% chance of ≥ 7. Ob 1: need 1 success from 4–5 dice. P(success) ≈ 87–92%. P(failure) ≈ 8–13%.

### Branch A: SUCCESS → TS jumps to 30 (Stirring). TK +2 immediately. Certainty −1.
**Season 0 (fires):**
- Vaynard TS 14 → 30. TK +2 (if TK was 2 → 4; if TK was 1 → 3).
- At TK 3: TC +1. At TK 4: TC +2. Vaynard offers Private Collection including originary Locks.
- ARC-S01 (Vaynard Cascade) accelerates by 1–2 seasons.
- ARC-S13 (Duke Awakens) fully fires.
- ARC-S31 (Lock Distribution) becomes available if TK reaches 4.

**Season 1:**
- If Locks distributed (ARC-S31): Discovery Event cascade — Haelgrund (TS 12, Spirit ~4, Ob 1: ~87% success), Klapp (TS 31, Spirit ~5, Ob 1: ~92%), Torsvald (TS 35, Spirit ~4, Ob 1: ~87%). Expected: 2–3 additional Discovery Events.
- TC effect: TK 4 = +2/season. Plus Conviction Yield + Institutional Momentum. TC accelerates by +2–3 over baseline in this window.
- Vaynard eligible for Approach Training (8 Character Points, witnessed Thread ops). A practitioner Vaynard is the most politically volatile individual in the campaign after Almud.
- ARC-P04 (Axis 9) advances — the most informed secular actor now has direct Thread experience.

**Season 2–3:**
- If Vaynard practices: Coherence starts at 10. Each operation costs Coherence per scale. At Relational+ (likely for a political actor): −1 per op. ~8 ops to Coherence 2 (Fractured).
- ARC-S16 (Coherence Zero — Vaynard) timeline: ~4–5 seasons from Discovery Event to Coherence crisis at heavy use.
- ARC-S33 (Lattice of Enemies): Vaynard's consequentialist Beliefs tangentially conflict with most other practitioners. His collective operation contribution: non-chaining dice. High TS, diminished collective partner.

**Arcs that fire ONLY on this branch:** ARC-S13, ARC-S31 (Lock Distribution at TK 4), ARC-S16 (Vaynard Coherence Zero), ARC-T08 (Vaynard's Confession if TK reaches 4).

### Branch B: FAILURE → TS frozen. Certainty −1. No context for the weight.
**Season 0:**
- Vaynard's rendering reasserts violently. Certainty −1 but without the ontological context that success provides.
- TK does not advance from the event. Private Collection still available but Vaynard interprets the failed attempt differently — confusion, not revelation.
- "Vaynard becomes more dangerous in his confusion than in his clarity" (narrative_scenario_chains).

**Season 1–3:**
- Vaynard continues intelligence operations. TK advances through relationship/research (slower — 1/season via sustained practitioner relationship).
- TC contribution: whatever TK level he's at (no +2 acceleration from Discovery Event).
- Private Collection continues being deployed (Intel vs Ob 2). Each use: hidden TS +1. At TS 15, 16, 17... each use triggers another Discovery Event check. Second attempt at TS 15: same odds (~87–92%). The Discovery Event fires eventually — it is a statistical inevitability over 3–5 Collection uses.
- ARC-S01 proceeds on the slow track. ARC-S13 deferred by 1–3 seasons.

**Arcs that fire ONLY on this branch:** None unique — all arcs fire eventually, just delayed. The failed Discovery Event creates a temporary window (1–3 seasons) where Vaynard is an intelligence actor without Thread capability. This window is valuable for factions opposing Varfell.

**Net divergence:** Success accelerates the TK/TC/Lock cascade by 1–3 seasons. Failure creates a window for opposition. Long-term convergence: Discovery Event fires eventually regardless.

- ARC-T15 (Forgetting Barrier): Klapp's archive testimony is Forgetting-resistant (documentary, not experiential). He is a crucial evidence-bearer for Collision E.

**Season 1 (Himmensendt complies — ~50%):**
- Inquisitor protocol fires against Klapp. Church hunting its own head of education.
- Church Stability −1 (internal fracture regardless of path).
- TC generation continues uninterrupted.
- If Haelgrund assigned to investigate Klapp: Haelgrund's hidden TS 12 means he senses Thread reality in Klapp's presence. Haelgrund's Defection arc (NPC-ARC-HAE) potentially fires from the investigation itself — the Church's best investigator discovers his target's perception is real, which validates his own suppressed perceptions.

**Season 2–3 (deviation path):**
- Klapp as practitioner ally: TC generation pauses (Church Stability ≤ 4, Cardinals competing).
- Collision A (Church Double Fracture) precondition met on Klapp side. If Olafsson exposure (ARC-S06) also fires: Church Stability −3 total.

- ARC-T16 (Perceptual Prophylaxis): Klapp is the crack. He can now perceive the structural contradiction in Himmensendt's pastoral programme AND articulate it from within the Church's own institutional language.

**Season 0:**
- Klapp's existing CE 4 and TS 31 continue unchanged. The Thread-significant object produced no perceptual shift.
- TS 31 means he still senses Thread operations (direction/general). This continues accumulating circumstantial evidence over time.
- ARC-S08 deferred but NOT cancelled. Klapp's ongoing archive work produces further exposures. Each exposure is another check opportunity (future sessions).

**Season 1–3:**
- Church Stability unaffected by Klapp crisis (it doesn't fire).
- TC continues on baseline trajectory (+1/season − Baralta suppression ± Assert/Suppress dynamics).
- ARC-S21 continues on slow accumulation path.
- Collision A precondition NOT met on Klapp side. Church Double Fracture requires both Klapp and Olafsson — without Klapp, only Olafsson exposure fires (Church Stability −1, not −3).

**Arcs that fire ONLY on Branch A:** ARC-S08 (immediate), Collision A precondition (Klapp side), TC pause window, ARC-T16 (Klapp as crack in prophylaxis).
**Arcs that fire ONLY on Branch B:** None unique — but the TC baseline trajectory is preserved, which keeps the Dominance Event (ARC-P01) on its original timeline.

**Net divergence:** 34% chance of failure preserves TC baseline and prevents Church Double Fracture from firing. The 66% success case produces the most consequential Church internal crisis in the campaign. The Ob 2 (raised from 1 by essentialist formation) is the single most important Ob modifier in the game — it shifts Klapp's success from ~87% to ~66%, creating a meaningful 1-in-3 chance that the Church remains structurally intact.

## KEY ROLL 4: Baralta Excommunication Defence (Church Mandate 5d10 TN 7 vs Ob 7)

**Context:** Olafsson declares Excommunication vs Baralta. Church pool: Mandate 5 dice, TN 7. Ob 7 = Baralta's personal Mandate. P(7 successes from 5 dice at 40%) = 0%. This is IMPOSSIBLE as written — cannot get 7 successes from 5 dice. Players can intervene with counterevidence (−1 Ob → Ob 6). Still impossible from 5 dice.

**[FINDING: The Excommunication mechanic as stated in arcs_20_23 is mechanically impossible. Church Mandate pool (5 dice) cannot achieve Ob 7 (Baralta personal Mandate). Even with player counterevidence (−1 Ob → 6), maximum successes from 5 dice = 5. This means Excommunication CANNOT succeed against Baralta under current parameters unless: (a) Baralta's Mandate has dropped below 5, (b) Church Mandate has risen above 7, (c) the mechanic uses a different pool, or (d) the mechanic is designed to always fail as written (representing the institutional impossibility of excommunicating the peninsula's most legitimate secular ruler).]**

**Arc impact:** ARC-T03 (Excommunication) as written is mechanically non-functional against Baralta at game-start stats. It can only fire if Baralta's Mandate has been reduced by prior events (failed Domain Actions, Hafenmark Stability pressure, or successful Church actions eroding her authority). This makes Excommunication a LATE-CAMPAIGN arc gated by prior Mandate erosion, not an independent threat.

**If Baralta Mandate drops to 4 (minimum for TC suppression):** Church 5d10 vs Ob 4. P(4+ successes from 5 dice at 40%) ≈ 32%. Now viable but still minority outcome. Success → TC +4 immediately, suppression ends permanently. ARC-P01 (TC) accelerates catastrophically.

**If Baralta Mandate drops to 3:** Ob 3. P(3+ from 5 at 40%) ≈ 66%. Likely to succeed. But at Mandate 3, TC suppression already ended (requires ≥ 4). Excommunication at this point is political, not mechanically impactful for TC — it removes Baralta's Sovereign Authority Doctrine (once per arc) and prevents recovery.

→ **This is a mechanical finding, not a simulation branch. Log as GAP-ARC-05.**

## KEY ROLL 7: Collective Mending at RS Critical (Endgame)

**Context:** RS 15 (Critical). Must Mend active Gap. Anchor: Edeyja (TS 75, Spirit ~6, Attunement ~6, Focus ~5, Thread Pool Score 7). Pool: Attunement + Focus + Thread Pool Score = 6 + 5 + 7 = 18 dice. Ob 6 (Entrenched Gap). Helpers: 2 PC practitioners contributing floor(Cognition ÷ 2) each ≈ +2 to +4 dice. Total pool: ~20–22 dice. TN 7. P(6+ successes from 22 dice at 40%) ≈ 95%.

**But:** If helpers have tangentially conflicting Beliefs (ARC-S33): helper dice don't chain on 10. Net helper value drops ~20%. If helpers have directly opposing Beliefs: Spirit TN 7 Ob 1 check. ~40% failure per helper. Expected helper contribution: 0.6 × 2 dice = 1.2 dice (vs 2–4 without Belief gating).

### Branch A: SUCCESS → RS +1 (Success) or +2 (Overwhelming). Gap closed.
- At RS 15: +1 → 16. +2 → 17. Still Critical. Need multiple Mendings across multiple seasons.
- Edeyja Coherence: was 9 → now 8 (Stable). She can do this ~7 more times before Coherence 2.
- Each Mending covers one Gap. At RS Critical: spontaneous Gaps on 1–4 (1d10) per season per territory. With 15 territories × ~40% Gap chance in affected territories (proximity-graduated): 2–6 new Gaps per season.
- Edeyja's Coherence budget: ~8 Mendings ≈ RS +8 to +16. But 2–6 new Gaps per season = RS −8 to −24 from Gap persistence alone.
- **Result: Even with successful collective Mending, RS cannot recover from Critical without also eliminating Lock drift AND halting spontaneous Gap formation (requires RS above 20, which requires Mending to reach — circular).**
- This IS the Einhir Catastrophe. The game replicates it. The only escape: WC ≥ 3 (RS +2/season) + Ceiral Ritual success (RS −6 to −10 recovery) + eliminate all Locks + collective Mending rotation across 3+ practitioners.

### Branch B: FAILURE → RS −2. Gap persists. Edeyja Coherence −1.
- RS 15 → 13. Deeper into Critical.
- Gap still present: −4 next season.
- RS 13 − 4 (Gap) − 1 (Winter if Year-End) − 1–2 (Lock drift) = RS 5–7 at next Accounting.
- Two seasons from Rupture without intervention.
- Edeyja Coherence 8. She has ~7 Mendings left before crisis.
- ARC-P02 endgame spiral confirmed: "the Einhir Catastrophe replicated mechanically."

**Net divergence:** Success buys 1–2 seasons. Failure accelerates by 1–2 seasons. But the structural outcome is the same unless the underlying decay sources are addressed simultaneously. Individual Mending rolls are tactically important but strategically insufficient.

## New Arcs Identified from Branching

### ARC-T19: The Governance Pause
**Engine:** Almud Discovery Event attempt (success OR failure) → 1-season Crown NPC AI pause
**Emergence:** Whether the Discovery Event succeeds or fails, Almud enters 1-season self-examination. Crown NPC AI takes no offensive Domain Actions. The ATTEMPT changes him, not just the outcome. During this season: TC advances unopposed (no Crown Suppress), IP advances (no Crown diplomatic action), Coup Counter may increment (Crown inaction). All factions have a 1-season window to act without Crown opposition.
**Non-Player Characters:** Almud (paused), Ehrenwall/Brandt (Coup Counter — Crown inaction counts toward TC ≥ 40 increment if TC is at 40+), Baralta (Parliamentary Challenge without Crown resistance), Vaynard (Counter-Narrative without Crown interference), Himmensendt (Assert without Crown Suppress)
**Classification:** Tertiary (requires Almud Discovery Event attempt — itself requiring player action to expose him).

### ARC-S34: The Edeyja Burnout
**Engine:** Edeyja Coherence 9 → repeated Mending → Coherence crisis
**Emergence:** ARC-S32 (Mending Trap) applied to the campaign's most important NPC practitioner. If Ceiral Ritual fails (ARC-T04 Branch C) or RS enters Critical, Edeyja becomes the sole high-level Mender. At Coherence 9 with ~8 Mendings before crisis: she covers 2–3 seasons of RS recovery. If she reaches Coherence 0: the Wardens lose their principal practitioner. WC track may degrade. No living practitioner can perform Locked Zone border Mending (requires TS 70+ and Einhir framework). The Southernmost becomes mechanically unsalvageable.
**Non-Player Characters:** Edeyja (primary — "moral anchor" with finite Coherence), any PC practitioners (Mending rotation extends her timeline), Maret Uln (TS ~50, can Mend standard Gaps but not Locked Zone borders)
**Structural significance:** If Edeyja burns out, the Southernmost endgame condition becomes permanently unachievable. This is the campaign's hidden fail state — invisible until she reaches Coherence 4–5.
**Interaction:** ARC-S32 (Mending Trap), ARC-S25 (Warden Cooperation — WC degrades if Edeyja incapacitated), ARC-S15 (Southernmost — Ritual becomes the only option if Mending is unavailable)

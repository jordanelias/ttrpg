## Confirmed findings (graded)

### P1 — two-engine divergence (the armature is stale and mislabeled)
- **PARITY-1** — `sim/provincial/units.py:383` combat pool is the **legacy size-based** `min(Size,Cmd)+Cmd`; the reference default is `Command×(1+cohesion)`. Same unit → different dice pools → every melee outcome diverges. (3/3 confirm)
- **PARITY-3** — the **entire σ-leverage head is missing** from the armature (`massbattle.py` calls bare `roll_pool`; advantages applied as integer pool mods — the reference's A/B *legacy* path, not its default). (2/3 confirm)
- Root cause: `sim/provincial/massbattle.py` is a frozen bare-port of `sim_mb_06_v22` (2026-05-18, its own docstring L16). The reference engine has since been rewritten across essentially every load-bearing formula, yet the armature carries a self-applied `[CANONICAL]` flag while the README calls every module a `PROVISIONAL` stub. **The two engines now produce materially different outcomes for identical inputs.**

### P2 — multi-subunit degradation broken on the default path
- **EC-1** — under the **default** engine path (`PER_CELL=0`), combat casualties are applied to `unit.hp` only and **never to `cell_troops`**, so any **multi-subunit** unit has its per-subunit cohesion / `eff_size` frozen at full strength for the whole battle — the combat pool never degrades and the design's "continuous degradation" is silently lost for mixed-role compositions. Single-subunit `PER_CELL=0` units (the canonical gauge) dodge this, which is why prior passes missed it. *(Directly relevant to "subunits in distinct roles": a mixed-role unit won't attrite per-subunit unless `PER_CELL=1`.)* Secondary: `check_drift` leaves node-cohesion state (`_node_pos/_node_rel`) keyed by the old shape after a drift (opt-in `PC_NODE_COHESION` path).

## Root cause of the infantry-envelopment (Cannae) gap — TEST BUDGET, not a missing mechanism (corrected 2026-06-23)

> **TOP CORRECTION (Jordan, 2026-06-23): Cannae is a CAVALRY manoeuvre — infantry does NOT do the Cannae envelopment.** Infantry's role is the ANVIL (fix/hold/bend the center); cavalry delivers the rear-wrap. The engine ALREADY reproduces Cannae correctly via the cavalry rows **C4** (mounted envelopment, 92.9% in band 75–95) and **C7** (cavalry envelops a holding line, 100% in band 65–100) — both PASS. The **H4 "Horseshoe vs Arrowhead (Cannae)" row is MISLABELED**: it is an infantry-vs-infantry formation grind, not a Cannae. So the "infantry Cannae gap" analyzed below was largely a phantom created by a mislabeled test. The durable side-findings stand (engagements need ~12+ rounds and ~2× base pace to join/resolve; envelopment fires via multi-body encirclement; cavalry-winged combined arms = 100% Cannae @ 12 rounds), but "infantry can't envelop quickly" is **correct by design**. Recommended gauge fix: rename/re-scope H4 as an infantry formation matchup (not Cannae); treat C4/C7 as the canonical Cannae tests (they pass).

> **Correction (supersedes the "wiring gap" framing below).** A follow-up duration probe (`speed_infantry_probe.py`) shows infantry envelopment **is** in the engine and is **duration-bound**, which is historically correct (Jordan: infantry envelops only in prolonged affairs / ambush, not by speed). A lone infantry Horseshoe vs a heavy line over ONE continuous engagement: enemy casualties 14%→37%→57% and enemy routs **0/12 → 0/12 → 7/12** at 18 / 50 / 100 ticks. The gauge resolves H4 in ~18-tick bursts with positions reset each turn, so the envelopment never completes → the decА-41 shortfall is a **tick-budget / harness artifact**, not a wiring or tuning defect. The C0–C3 probe below is still valid (speed reaches the rear, the lone-body 'envelop' instruction circles, the width-σ backfires) — but the operative variable is **engagement duration**, not a constant. My "combined-arms infantry" idea UNDER-performed the lone shape (hold-center doesn't fix; 'envelop' wings avoid contact), so that is NOT the fix. **Cavalry speed: 2× is better-calibrated than 3×** — 3× saturates mounted envelopment to 100% (history caps ~95) and erodes the braced-wall repel (raw cav 4%→12.5%). Action: re-scope the H4 expectation to a prolonged-engagement context (or treat fast decisive envelopment as a cavalry/ambush capability), rather than "fixing" infantry to envelop quickly.

**Mechanism.** The decisive envelopment penalty is the per-cell wrap (`orchestration.py:1745-1830`, `PER_CELL and PC_REFUSE`): an attacker cell inflicts `PC_ENVELOP_MOD` only when it wraps *beyond the defender's frontage span* AND lands in the defender's **REAR (RED zone)**. The overhang **wheel** (`:973-979`) turns wing cells to face the *flank* (YELLOW, −1D) for any troop type, but reaching the **rear** requires either (a) the `'envelop'` path-around maneuver (`:987`, instruction-gated) or (b) raw closing speed. Cavalry gets `PC_CAVALRY_SPEED_MULT=2.0` (`:829,:936`, gated on `troop_type∈{cavalry,mounted_archers}`); infantry at 1× never reaches the rear within the engagement, so a Horseshoe resolves as a wide *flanking* clash (YELLOW), not a decisive double-envelopment (RED).

**Why the tuning levers don't fix it:** the `'envelop'` instruction on a single body (C1) just marches everything around — no anvil pins the enemy, so it circles to a 100% draw; re-enabling `_envelopment_sigma` (C3) rewards the wrong side. The diagnostic that lands in band (C2) is *movement to the rear*, which is speed-gated.

**Fix direction (Jordan-gated):** infantry Cannae needs the **combined-arms / multi-subunit structure**, not a constant: a fixing **center** (heavy infantry, `hold`/`brace`) that PINS the enemy + enveloping **wing subunits** carrying `'envelop'` that wrap the rear — exactly how the macro battery's *cavalry* wings already envelop at the army level, and how Cannae historically worked (center holds and bends, wings envelop). A single-unit Horseshoe cannot express "fix + wrap." Open sub-question: at 1× speed infantry wings may need more engagement duration to complete the wrap (Cannae took hours) — worth testing whether a held center + `'envelop'` infantry wings reproduces the band. Candidate light-touch lever if a structural fix is undesired: a small, span-correct width-σ (the C3 metric must measure true lateral extent, not alive-column count, or it backfires).

## Engagement length, multi-body envelopment, and the rout bottleneck (2026-06-23)

Probes: `speed_infantry_probe.py`, `engagement_length_sweep.py`, `encirclement_probe.py`.

**Two structural bottlenecks explain the whole formation-fidelity shortfall:**

1. **Engagement budget.** At 3 rounds (18 ticks) every formation matchup is **100% draws** — nothing resolves; decisive results only appear at ~12–18 rounds. The current ≤3-round/18-tick engagement (reset each turn) is the dominant reason formation geometry is invisible in the gauge. *Simultaneous resolution* (the engine is already tick-simultaneous internally) is a sound way to justify more rounds — but **6 rounds is still all-draws; target ~12+** or speed the morale model. The per-round `reset_positions` is NOT simply the culprit: for Horseshoe-vs-wedge the reset actually *helps* the enveloper (matchup-dependent).

2. **Envelopment is a multi-body (unit-level) effect, not a single-shape one.** A lone Horseshoe vs a wedge = **+0** casualty margin — the engine's encirclement penalty fires only when ≥2 enemy atoms engage one, impossible 1-subunit-vs-1. **Three converging subunits** vs the same wedge = **+17** margin (**+25** at `ENCIRCLEMENT_PENALTY×3`). This is the `unit→subunit` hierarchy in action: envelopment is a *strategy executed by multiple subunits*. (Horseshoe-vs-Line *does* envelop given time: +20 margin, decА 73–79, in band.)

3. **Deepest bottleneck — the morale/rout model.** Even a crushing **+25** attrition margin yields **0 routs / 100% draws** at 12 rounds: envelopment pressure never converts to morale collapse. `MORALE_EROSION_DAMP` is a **dead constant** (DRIFT-5), so the knob to make being-enveloped erode morale→rout isn't wired. **Highest-leverage fix:** a frontage/encirclement→morale-erosion→rout channel, so a decisive Cannae lands within a sane round budget.

**Answer to the two design proposals:** (a) cavalry stays **2×** (3× overshoots history); (b) **simultaneous resolution = yes**, but pair it with a ~12+ round budget *and* a morale-rout conversion — 6 rounds alone won't make Cannae resolve, and the binding constraint is morale collapse, not casualties.

## Turn structure + Cannae-in-one-engagement (`speed_to_cannae.py`)

**Decided structure:** 6 rounds (alternating turns) / 12 rounds (simultaneous turns); 1 round = 6 ticks. Both ≈ 5–8 min wall-clock at ~30–45s/round-pair (current 3-turn ≈ 1–3 min, skirmish-tier).

**The reasonable way to fit a decisive Cannae in one engagement = historical combined-arms, not a speed hack.** Infantry-anvil center + two cavalry shock wings routs an enemy wedge **75% @ 6 rounds, 100% @ 12 rounds**. Cavalry's natural 2× speed reaches the rear in time; the hierarchy does the work (envelop strategy → Anvil + cavalry-Flanker subunits → brace/charge cells). Pure infantry, even at 2–4× global speed, plateaus ≤45% @ 12r and **0% @ 6r** — infantry-only envelopment stays a prolonged affair (correct/historical).

**Bonus — base movement pace is ~2× too slow.** At 1× a standard-deployment Line-vs-Line does **0% casualties in 12 rounds** (units march the whole engagement). At 2× they fight to ~37% casualties with **0 routs** (faster, still balanced — no blowout). Recommend ~2× base closing pace so engagements *join* in ~3–4 rounds and fight the rest.

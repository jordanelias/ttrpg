## BRANCH K — OLAFSSON WINS THE S16 COLLEGE VOTE
*(Original: Batch 4. Jarnstal wins — defensive posture during Church Stability crisis. Himlensendt's casting vote favors the less aggressive option.)*

**Alternate:** Himlensendt's Scar 2 has eroded his institutional confidence enough that he does not cast a decisive vote. He abstains instead: "The College must resolve this. I will not determine it." Without his casting vote, the 2-2 tie defaults to the Cardinal who holds the senior procedural position — by Church internal protocol, Cardinal Justice (Olafsson) holds procedural seniority in disciplinary matters. His motion passes.

**Olafsson's motion:** Accelerate Heresy Proceedings in territories where CI advancement has been blocked by Baralta's Sovereign Authority Doctrine. Specifically: initiate proceedings against the Parliamentary Committee members who voted for the Doctrine, on grounds of "ecclesiastical authority obstruction" — a charge with thin theological basis but available within Church procedural law.

### Immediate Consequences (S16)

**Who gets charged:**
Baralta herself cannot be charged directly without Excommunication (a separate procedure). But her inner circle can. Olafsson targets: Torvi Heljason (Baralta's Legal Advisor, who wrote the Doctrine brief) and Annika Feldhaus (who seconded the Parliamentary motion). Both are named in Heresy Proceedings.

**Heljason's position:** Canon Standing 3, Church-adjacent (he is not a Church member, but he drafted a document that Church considers theologically adversarial). Olafsson's charge: "unauthorized theological jurisdiction claim." Heljason's defense: "I am not a Church member and have no theological obligation to Church doctrine. This Proceeding has no jurisdiction over a civil legal scholar." Jurisdictional question: can the Church pursue Heresy Proceedings against a non-Church member? Answer: yes, under Church law — Heresy is assessed by the Church on anyone operating within Church-adjacent civil governance. Ob 3 for the Proceeding (Heljason's secular status raises the Ob; he is not subject to Church discipline through normal channels). P(Church wins the Proceeding): ~40%.

**Feldhaus's position:** She is a Guild member, not a Church member, and a Hafenmark Parliamentary Committee chair. Same jurisdictional question, same resolution. But she has additional exposure: the Dicastery for Temporal Affairs (Tormann's) has records that she has been facilitating suppression of Church commercial activities in T10. Tormann, despite his general fiscal caution, cannot protect her from Olafsson's Proceeding without actively opposing his colleague's Dicastery authority. He does nothing.

**Baralta's response:** She invokes the Sovereign Authority Doctrine — the Proceedings are legally non-binding in Hafenmark territory (her constitution supersedes Church jurisdiction). But the Proceedings are being filed in Church records. Whether or not they are enforceable in Hafenmark, they create a Church-record heresy designation on her inner circle members that follows them everywhere outside Hafenmark. Heljason cannot travel to Church-controlled territories without risk. Feldhaus cannot do commercial arbitration in Church territories.

**[FINDING K-01: Olafsson's Proceedings create a legal shadow that operates outside Hafenmark's constitutional protection. Baralta can nullify the enforcement — she cannot nullify the record. Her inner circle members are now heresy-designated in Church records, which affects their mobility and commercial access across the peninsula. The Sovereign Authority Doctrine protects Hafenmark's territory; it cannot protect her people's reputations in the wider world. This is the most effective legal attack available to Church against Hafenmark: not enforcement in Hafenmark territory, but heresy-designation that follows inner circle members outside it.]**

**Jarnstal's response:** He disagrees with Olafsson's move and says so explicitly at the next College session: "We are pursuing civilians in a jurisdiction dispute. This is not what the Templar branch exists to defend." He does not reverse the decision — it has already passed — but he dissociates the Templar network from any enforcement role. The Proceedings are Olafsson's to execute. No Templar escorts any Inquisitor pursuing these specific cases.

**AER consequence:** The aggressive Proceedings generate AP accumulation in Hafenmark territories (AP +1 per season while Proceedings are active). AER: base level maintained (Proceedings increase AP but don't generate AER the way HI confirmations do). But the AP accumulation creates a clock: when AP reaches 3 in a Hafenmark territory, Olafsson's Priority 5 fires (Deploy Inquisitor). He is approaching the moment where he has authority to physically send an Inquisitor into Baralta's territory despite the Sovereign Authority Doctrine — not to enforce the heresy charge, but to "investigate AP events," which is Church's autonomous right under its own institutional law.

**Klapp's position:** He reviews the Proceeding against Heljason. He finds: Heljason's brief contains exactly the "documented administrative interest" argument that Almstedt certified as valid. If the Church pursues the Proceeding to conviction, it creates a Church record that the act of drafting constitutionally valid parliamentary arguments constitutes heresy when those arguments constrain Church authority. This is an absurdity that undermines Church's own institutional legitimacy. He takes this argument to Himlensendt (not to Olafsson — Olafsson will not hear it). Himlensendt reads it. His response: "I will not intervene in an active Proceeding." He is still non-functional as a decisional authority. Klapp files the argument in his private archive alongside his framework document. Two more locked documents that might matter later.

His Consequentialist Framework: measured outcome within one season. What does the Schattendienst offer in one season? Complete Crown Military, Wealth, Mandate, and Stability data (already partially known from his Tribune operations). He accepts. The bilateral agreement becomes trilateral: Vaynard, Thale, Niflhel contact. Thale remains in Crown service; her Schattendienst continues operating normally for Crown's benefit. But Vaynard now receives a secondary intelligence feed from the same apparatus.

**[FINDING L-01: Thale's defection is not visible because she is not defecting from Crown — she is adding Vaynard as a secondary principal while maintaining Crown as her primary institutional identity. This is the most sophisticated version of the double-agent model: she is not working against Crown, she is working for an additional master whose interests partially overlap with Crown's. Vaynard wants a stable Valoria (his victory conditions require it). Crown wants stability. In the short term, their interests are aligned. **Cascade at Path B S9:**
If Vaynard wins Path B at S9 (Branch C alternate), Thale's trilateral arrangement becomes immediately problematic. Crown is not defeated — the campaign ended on Varfell's terms while Crown still holds TCV 12 and Mandate 5. Does Thale's secondary arrangement with Vaynard become public at the victory declaration? Vaynard has no strategic reason to expose it — she was a resource, not a trophy. He keeps it confidential. She continues serving Crown under Löwenritter governance (if the Coup fires after S9, which it cannot — Path B victory closes the campaign).

**Cascade at Coup (S22, if campaign continues past S9):**
Ehrenwall's Coup fires. Thale's negotiated-transition plan (from original Batch 3) now runs against her secondary Vaynard arrangement. If she negotiates Schattendienst continuity with Ehrenwall AND has a secondary Vaynard feed active, she is simultaneously aligning with three principals: Löwenritter (transition governance), Vaynard (intelligence secondary), and Crown (institutional primary). This is beyond the manageable — she must choose. Her Autonomy Conviction: the outcome that produces the most predictable result. Löwenritter governance is certain. Vaynard's Path C win is likely but not certain. She closes the Vaynard secondary feed when Ehrenwall takes power. She tells Vaynard: "The arrangement ends here. Crown's intelligence apparatus transfers to Löwenritter oversight." Vaynard's response: "I know. You served your secondary obligation long enough." He files it as completed intelligence operation and moves forward.

This is a coherent interpretation of Continuity Conviction (primary) read conservatively: preserve the tradition as it has been, not as it could be.

### The Endorsed Assembly

**Vote with Stenskald's endorsement:**
- Hann: yes (1)
- Eastern March allied Jarl: yes (2)
- Stenskald's endorsement: carries weight of oral law validation — this is not a vote but an institutional signal. The two abstention-likely Jarls (Holdar, Warden-Council Jarl) now face: do they vote against a Skald-Chief-endorsed candidate?

- Warden-Council Jarl: votes for Maret Uln regardless — but now 3-1, not 2-2. No Grand Contest needed.

Sigurd Vaynardsson becomes Hochjarl (same figurehead outcome as Branch H, but without the procedural drama of a contested Assembly).

**Maret Uln's position:**
The Assembly result is legitimate. She is the losing candidate. She stays — her Council of the Warden work is too important to abandon and she knows it. But the oral law now has a formal Skald-endorsement of "hereditary claim over Deed-Claim in contested succession." This is the precedent that will shape the next 200 seasons of Varfell succession law.

**Stenskald's internal aftermath:**
She has enforced the tradition she is custodian of. She has also, in her private assessment, selected a leader who cannot govern effectively over one who can. Her Continuity Conviction holds the tradition above the outcome. She accepts this. She files in her private memory: "I endorsed the weaker leader to preserve the stronger precedent. Whether that exchange was correct is not mine to judge in my lifetime."

**[FINDING O-01: Stenskald's alternate endorsement reveals the deepest tension in custodianship: the keeper of tradition cannot also be the arbiter of when tradition should yield to better outcomes. She can hold the tradition or she can select the better leader. She cannot do both simultaneously, because the tradition she holds says bloodline matters more than individual capacity. By choosing to be a pure custodian, she produces the tradition's most self-consistent result and its most operationally damaging outcome. **Varfell's long-term trajectory:**
Sigurd governs. Hann manages him effectively for 4 seasons. The Council fragmentation (from Branch H analysis) still occurs — by S26, Councils are operating independently. The difference from Branch H: there is now a formal Skald-endorsed precedent that this is how Varfell succession works. When Sigurd eventually fails (and he will — the Schoenland diplomatic incident from Branch H still occurs around S26), the next succession will default to hereditary claim again. Varfell has locked itself into a succession logic that produces weak leadership by design.

**Maret Uln's arc post-defeat:**
She serves the Council of the Warden. She advances WR to 4 through sustained Edeyja cooperation. Her practitioner work is the only thing preventing Path C from failing entirely (Sigurd cannot authorize Thread operations; she routes them through the Council). When Sigurd's governance fails at S26, the practical reality becomes visible: Maret Uln has been governing through the Council system while the nominal Hochjarl was unable to govern at all. Thorvald Hann comes to her privately: "I made the wrong argument." She: "No. You made the right argument for the wrong institutional moment. The tradition you defended was correct. The candidate it produced was not." He: "Can we correct it?" She: "The oral law has a provision for incapacity. It has never been used."

The incapacity provision (which Stenskald knows and has never invoked): if a Hochjarl demonstrates sustained inability to perform the Assembly's mandatory functions (recite oral law, preside over Council sessions, manage the annual Assembly), the Skald-Chief may declare an Incapacity Assessment. The Assessment is a formal process — 2 seasons — that produces a Skald-endorsed finding. If the finding is incapacity, the Assembly convenes again. And this time, with a Hochjarl who has already demonstrated incapacity, the Deed-Claim argument is significantly stronger: the tradition's own provision for failure has been invoked.

Stenskald initiates the Incapacity Assessment at S28. The precedent she set at S22 (hereditary over Deed-Claim) is in conflict with the incapacity provision she is now invoking. The oral law contains both. She must hold both, which means the next Assembly will produce the most contested succession in 200 seasons of Varfell history.

## BRANCH R — FELDHAUS DOES NOT FILE THE ADDENDUM
*(Original: Batch 3. When facing the Southern Einhir Journeyman's examination failure, Feldhaus files an addendum to defer rather than reject, creating the mechanism for rule change.)*

**Immediate effect:** The Journeyman (Southern Einhir, objectively superior work, procedural failure on geographic jurisdiction) has no appeal mechanism within the Guild system. She is 2 years from meeting the geographic jurisdiction requirement (she needs to work in a registered Forum territory for the required duration). She can attempt again in 2 seasons.

**What changes in 2 seasons:**
- T10 Spartfell, where part of her Journeymen Years occurred, has changed hands (in Branch N, Church seized T10 at S13; in the standard timeline, T10 remains Hafenmark-held but the Forum registration lapse may be affected by the military instability of the border area).

The Church's occupation of T10 complicates Forum registration renewal (Church administration does not recognize Guild Forum jurisdiction in Church-held territories — Baralta's Sovereign Authority Doctrine does not protect Forum registration in territories Church has actually seized).
- Her 2-season re-attempt is now in a Forum territory that may not be Forum-active because it is Church-held.

**The gap in the rule:**
The geographic jurisdiction requirement uses "registered Guild Forum territory" as its basis. Forum registration requires Hafenmark (or the controlling faction's) administrative recognition. In Church-held T10: Forum registration is not renewed by Church administration. The Journeyman's 2-season work in T10 does not count as Forum territory time even after the original rejection. Her path to Free Master has become structurally blocked by a combination of procedural failure (the original rejection) and territorial change (the Church seizure).

**[FINDING R-01: Feldhaus's choice not to file the addendum, combined with the Church seizure of T10, produces a situation where the Southern Einhir Journeyman cannot become a Free Master under current Guild rules regardless of her work quality. The procedural rejection plus territorial instability creates a genuine dead end. This is the most direct demonstration of how factional politics damages individual lives: the Church-Hafenmark territorial dispute directly closed a career path that had nothing to do with theology or politics. The game's most mundane character (a Journeyman craftsperson) is the most direct victim of its grandest systemic conflict. If the game is designed to produce emergent narratives without player involvement, this is one of the strongest examples: the Journeyman's career was not about the Journeyman, and the forces that ended it had nothing to do with her.]**

**The community response:**
The Journeyman does not go quietly. She files a formal petition with the Hafenmark Parliamentary Internal Affairs Committee (the Committee that oversees civic administration and settlement Order management). The Journeyman's work in T10 now counts. She can re-attempt the examination immediately.

She passes. The motion becomes the broader precedent: any candidate whose geographic jurisdiction was disrupted by territorial conflict has a corrective path. Feldhaus, having failed to act generously at the first opportunity, has been forced by institutional process to act correctly at the second — with the added benefit that the correction is now Parliamentary precedent rather than a personal administrative addendum.

## CROSS-BRANCH FINDINGS (BATCH 2)

### FINDING CROSS-B2-01: Institutional Neutrality Is Always Political

The Ministry (Haelgrund, Branch S), Father Linder's diplomatic reporting, and Almstedt's constitutional certification all claim institutional neutrality. In every alternate branch, the exercise of "neutral" institutional function produces political consequences:
- Haelgrund providing equal data access creates the Thread-perception census that changes the Einhir caste analysis
- Linder's full reporting produces Himlensendt's sermon
- Almstedt's constitutional precision forces Baralta's supermajority route

There is no neutral institutional function. Every institutional act has political valence. "Neutrality" is a claim about process, not about consequence.

### FINDING CROSS-B2-02: The Most Consequential Actions Are the Smallest Ones

In every batch of alternates, the highest-consequence branches pivot on a small action:
- Linder choosing to report vs. filter one comment (Branch P)
- Feldhaus filing vs. not filing a 1-paragraph addendum (Branch R)
- Haelgrund refusing vs. accepting one analytical synthesis request (Branch S)

None of these are pitched political decisions. None involve Domain Actions. None are Ceremonial Scenes. They are individual judgment calls in ordinary institutional interactions. The game's largest political consequences propagate from these small judgment calls because every institutional actor is connected to every other through information flows, and information flows are determined by individual decisions about what to include and what to omit.

### FINDING CROSS-B2-03: The Reform Path Is Structurally Available but Individually Difficult

Branch T (Almud Arc A) shows that the Reform path is mechanically available and produces the most positive world-state of any Almud branch. But it requires Certainty 1 AND Counter ≤ 1 simultaneously — a condition window that is only available if the reform happens before the Counter advances past 1. In standard play, the Counter advances based on Crown's own governance failures. The Reform would have to happen before those failures accumulate. A player running Almud who wants Arc A must actively expose Almud to Evidence before the governance failures pile up — creating the Certainty decline through voluntary intellectual honesty rather than waiting for external pressure to force it.

The Reform path requires choosing to be uncertain before you are forced to be. This is difficult for a Certainty 3 character with an Order Conviction. It is also the only path that prevents the Coup permanently.

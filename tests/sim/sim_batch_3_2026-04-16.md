### A.2 — ED-583: HAFENMARK MANDATE CAP — DESIGN DECISION (RESOLVED)

**Finding from ST-01:** PP-174 caps Mandate recovery at starting Mandate value. Hafenmark starts at Mandate 4. Framework Drift gives Influence +1, not Mandate. No Mandate growth path above 4. Dynastic Proclamation requires Hafenmark Mandate > target controller Mandate. Crown at Mandate 5. Hafenmark can never Proclaim on Crown territory.

**Design question:** Is this intended or a gap?

**Analysis — Is the structural impossibility intended?**

Hafenmark's Dynastic Proclamation represents Baralta's divine-right sovereignty claim. This claim is most compelling against weakened or illegitimate rulers. The prerequisite "Hafenmark Mandate > target Mandate" correctly models this: Baralta's constitutional authority is greater than the rival's in the population's view.

Against Crown (starting Mandate 5, often higher through Govern OW): Hafenmark at Mandate 4 can never exceed Crown's institutional legitimacy in normal play. This is historically accurate — Baralta's claim is a parliamentary constitutional one, not an absolute monarchical one. Crown's legitimacy is older and deeper. Baralta can claim territories from factions with weaker institutional standing, not from the strongest institution on the peninsula.

**The structural impossibility against Crown is intentional.** Hafenmark's Proclamation tool is designed for:
- Targeting Varfell (Mandate 3 start) — Hafenmark 4 > Varfell 3 ✓
- Targeting eliminated/weakened factions (Mandate ≤ 3 after suppression)
- Targeting Church-seized territory where Church loses Mandate control
- Post-coup Löwenritter (Mandate typically ≤ 3 in recovery)

**BUT:** Hafenmark does need a Mandate growth path above 4 for the Proclamation to eventually target Crown (if Crown weakens). Currently no such path exists.

**Resolution — Mandate growth beyond starting value:**

PP-174 (Govern OW in own capital = Mandate +1, max = starting Mandate) caps at starting. But Crown can exceed their starting Mandate via the same rule. This asymmetry exists because Crown's starting Mandate is 5 and Hafenmark's is 4 — PP-174 doesn't prevent Crown from reaching 6 (their starting Mandate cap is different? No — PP-174 says "max = starting Mandate" for all factions. Crown starting = 5, cap = 5. Hafenmark starting = 4, cap = 4.

Wait — then Crown also cannot exceed Mandate 5 via Govern. Crown starts at 5, which is already very high. The issue is the asymmetry: Hafenmark (4) is structurally weaker than Crown (5) by exactly 1 Mandate, and no mechanic bridges this.

**Proposed fix:** Grand Contest decisive victory over another faction in Parliament produces +1 Mandate for the winning faction (capped at 6 absolute maximum for any faction). This represents genuine institutional growth from political dominance — not administrative recovery (PP-174), but legitimacy earned through political victory.

- Hafenmark wins Grand Contest vs Crown → Hafenmark Mandate 4→5. Now can Proclaim on any Crown territory where Crown has dropped to 4.
- Crown wins Grand Contest → Crown Mandate 5→6 (at absolute cap — this is the Caesar moment, the Crown becoming overwhelming).
- Applies once per Grand Contest win, capped at the absolute maximum (6 for all factions; Mandate 7 reserved for game-state special conditions).

This creates: Hafenmark's path to Mandate 5 requires sustained political dominance. It's achievable (Grand Contests fire from contested territory disputes, Parliamentary showdowns) but not automatic. A Hafenmark that wins Grand Contests against Crown over 10–15 seasons has earned the political standing to challenge Crown territory directly.

**Status: RESOLVED.** 
- PP-174 remains as the recovery cap (Govern OW in capital restores up to starting Mandate).
- New mechanic: Grand Contest decisive win → +1 Mandate (absolute cap 6). Propose as new PP.
- Structural impossibility against Crown confirmed as intended — Hafenmark must earn political dominance first.

### A.5 — ED-539: TC REFORM + TCV 5 + SEIZURE ACCORD ≥ 2 COMPOUND VALIDATION

**Setup:** Simulate the combined effect of TC-running-to-100 + TCV revaluation (Himmelenger 5, Gransol 3) + Seizure producing Accord ≥ 2 (not 1). Does this compound effect change Church's victory path in an unexpected way?

**Compound interaction analysis:**

Under old rules: Church Seizure → territory control at Accord 1. TCV doesn't count at Accord 1 (peninsular_strain §2: "TCV counts only at Accord ≥ 2"). Church's Seizure TCV contribution was always delayed by Govern actions.

Under new rules: Church Seizure Success → Accord = max(floor(PT/2)+1, 2) ≥ 2. Church Seizure OW → Accord = floor(PT/2)+2, max 3.

**Impact on Church TCV accumulation:**

Old path: Church Seizes T3 (PT 3) → Accord 1 → TCV 0 → must Govern to Accord 2 → TCV 2 (1 season delay).

New path: Church Seizes T3 (PT 3) → Accord = max(floor(3/2)+1,2) = max(2,2) = 2 → TCV 2 immediately.

**This eliminates the 1-season Govern delay for Seizures in territories with PT ≥ 2.** Church's TCV path is faster than prior simulation assumed.

**Church victory timeline recalculation:**

Church faction-specific victory: TCV ≥ 8, TC ≥ 65. Starting TCV: 5 (T9 only under old rules — but T9 is now TCV 5 under the revaluation!). Starting TCV 5 means Church needs only 3 more TCV to reach 8.

With Accord ≥ 2 immediately on Seizure: Church gains TCV instantly from each successful Seizure. Target is TCV 8.

Season-by-season:
- S0: Church TCV = 5 (T9).
- S1 Seizure Success on T3 (PT 3): Accord 2 → TCV 2. Church TCV = 7.
- S2 Seizure Success on T2 (PT 3): Accord 2 → TCV 1. Church TCV = 8.

**[CRITICAL FINDING: Under the new rules, Church can reach TCV 8 in 2 Seizure seasons if Seizure succeeds. Church victory condition (TCV 8 + TC 65) requires TC 65, which under the Pontifex-Assert fix arrives around Season 21. But the TCV 8 condition is met by Season 2–4 if Seizures succeed. The compound effect is: Church easily meets TCV threshold early (Seasons 2–4), then waits for TC 65 (Season 21). Church victory is determined by TC timing, not TCV timing. TCV 8 is no longer a meaningful constraint on Church victory.]**

**[CRITICAL FINDING — ED-539 CONFIRMED: The TC reform + TCV 5 revaluation + Accord ≥ 2 on Seizure compound creates a situation where Church's TCV victory condition (TCV ≥ 8) is achievable in the first 4 seasons of the game. TCV 8 is not a constraint; TC 65 is the only real gating factor. This means the Church victory condition is effectively a single-condition win: "reach TC 65." The TCV gate provides no meaningful gameplay tension because it's met 17+ seasons before TC 65.]**

**Resolution options:**

Option A — Raise Church TCV threshold: 8 → 12. Church now needs 7 more TCV beyond starting 5. With 3 territories (T3, T2, T5 or similar), Church reaches TCV 12 in 3–5 seasons. Still too easy — TCV 12 achieved by Season 5, still waiting until Season 21 for TC 65.

Option B — Add additional condition to Church victory: Church must hold ≥ 3 non-capital territories AT Accord ≥ 3 (not just ≥ 2). Accord 3 requires Govern or Seizure OW. Church must actively govern seized territories, not just hold them politically. This creates a continuous governance burden: 3 territories at Accord 3 requires 3 Govern actions/season in those territories (using Church's Consul card), competing with Assert and Seizure.

Option C — TC requirement raised: TC 65 → TC 75 for Church faction-specific victory. TC 75 arrives around Season 25–28 (Pontifex-Assert). This gives secular factions 5–7 additional seasons to respond.

**[RECOMMENDATION: Option B is the most elegant. It adds a governance burden to Church's victory path without changing the TC trajectory. Church must simultaneously run Assert (TC advancement) + Seizure (TCV acquisition) + Govern (Accord 3 maintenance) — competing for its 1 Consul card every season. This correctly represents the historical reality that a theocracy requires not just conquest but actual popular religious buy-in (Accord 3 = Aligned = genuinely supportive population). A Church that governs well wins. A Church that only seizes doesn't.]**

**Revised Church victory condition:**

> **Church — Solmundan Orthodoxy (revised):**
> All conditions simultaneous at Accounting:
> - TCV held ≥ 8
> - TC ≥ 65
> - PT ≥ 3 in ALL Church-held territories
> - Accord ≥ 3 in at least 3 non-capital Church territories (not T9)
>
> The Accord ≥ 3 condition requires Govern OW (or Seizure OW) in 3 territories — a sustained governance commitment, not just political seizure.

## §1 DEFINITION AND SCOPE

### §1.1 What a Companion Is

A **Companion** is a named NPC who has formed a sustained personal relationship with the Player Character through play, meeting a mechanical threshold (Disposition ≥ +3), and who now travels with the PC as a persistent presence across sessions.

Companions are defined by three properties that distinguish them from other NPCs:

1. **Personal scale presence:** They participate in scene-level action alongside the PC. In combat, they fight. In contests, they may Corroborate. In fieldwork, they assist or provide intelligence.

2. **Persistent tracking:** Companion state (Disposition, Wounds, Coherence if applicable, arc progress) is tracked between sessions, not reset at scene end.

3. **Narrative investment:** A Companion's death, departure, or betrayal fires a dedicated Scene Slate entry (departure scene), not a generic NPC consequence. The player experiences the relationship's end as a major story beat.

### §1.2 Companion vs. Recruited NPC

| Property | Companion | Recruited NPC |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Scale of operation | Personal (scene-level) | Faction (BG-level) |
| PC proximity | Travels with PC | Operates within faction structure |
| Consequence of relationship ending | Departure scene (major story beat) | Mandate −1 (talent drain) |
| Combat presence | Active in personal combat | Not present personally; commands units |
| Disposition tracked | To PC personally | To faction |
| Source | Disposition ≥ +3 (earned through play) | Recruitment roll (§9.5 npc_behavior) |
| Multiple active | Up to 2 simultaneously | Unlimited |

A single NPC may be both a Companion and a Recruited NPC — the officer-governor path in mass_battle §D.2 produces exactly this. The companion role governs personal-scale behavior; the recruited role governs faction-scale behavior.

### §1.3 Maximum Companions

A PC may have at most **2 active Companions simultaneously.** This reflects the cognitive and narrative overhead of sustained relationships — a third would dilute all three to supporting characters rather than genuine companions.

When a PC would gain a third Companion (Disposition ≥ +3 on an NPC while 2 are already active), the player must choose: decline the companionship offer (NPC remains an allied NPC at Disposition +3 but does not travel with the PC), or formally end one existing Companion relationship first (departure scene required).

## §2 ACQUIRING A COMPANION

### §2.1 Eligibility Threshold

An NPC becomes eligible for companionship when their Disposition toward the PC reaches **+3** through organic play (not Recruitment roll). The threshold is earned through:

- **Unit officer path (mass_battle §D.2):** Officer who reaches Disposition +3 through battle service.
- **Social path:** Any named NPC reached through sustained social fieldwork, contested scenes, or crisis intervention.
- **Knot path:** Any NPC who forms a Knot at Close level — the relational intimacy is structurally equivalent to Companion eligibility.

### §2.2 The Companionship Scene

When eligibility is reached, a dedicated Scene Slate entry fires (Priority 1 — the player may attend or decline). The scene is a turning point in the relationship — the NPC asks to travel with the PC, or the PC makes the offer, or circumstances make the companionship natural.

**Scene structure:**
1. **Moment of truth:** The NPC states why they want to travel with the PC. Their Conviction governs the framing.
2. **PC response:** Player declares acceptance (Companion activated) or decline (NPC remains at Disposition +3 as an allied NPC, no departure scene required for future separation).
3. **On acceptance:** Player and GM agree on the Companion's role (guardian, advisor, scout, practitioner, etc.). This role determines their primary mechanical contribution (§5).

## §3 COMPANION STATS

### §3.1 Stat Generation from Faction Military

Companions generated through the officer path use the following stat generation formula:

| Stat | Formula | Range |
|------|---------|-------|
| Agility | floor(faction Military ÷ 2) | 1–3 |
| STR | floor(faction Military ÷ 2) | 1–3 |
| Endurance | floor(faction Military ÷ 2) + 1 | 2–4 |
| Cognition | 2 (fixed — professional military) | — |
| Charisma | 2 (fixed — functional, not exceptional) | — |
| Spirit | 2 (fixed — grounded) | — |

**Combat Pool = (Agility × 2) + History bonus + 3.** History bonus: officers begin with a relevant History (Combat/Military) worth 2 points = +2D bonus. Pool: (2×2)+2+3 = 9D for a Military 4 faction officer. Pool 9D is a competent professional — dangerous but not PC-tier.

**Wound Interval = Endurance + 6.** For Endurance 3: WI = 9. Max Wounds = floor(3/2)+1 = 2. Two wounds before incapacitation — fragile by PC standards, correct for an NPC companion.

**Weapon:** Default Medium Blade (Long Heavy Blade, TN 7). Companions carry the standard military weapon of their faction. Officers may carry faction-specific weapons (Templar: Heavy Blunt; Varfell scouts: Short Light Blade; Hafenmark guards: Medium Blade).

**Armour:** Medium (Military professional standard). Companions may upgrade to Heavy if the PC provides resources (Wealth −1 once).

### §3.2 Stat Generation for Social/Knot-Path Companions

Companions acquired through social paths (not military) use the NPC's existing Stance Triangle attributes for personal combat. Named NPCs already have associated stat levels (e.g., Vaynard is a scholar — Cognition 5, Agility 2; Vossen is a community organiser — Charisma 5, Agility 3).

For named NPCs, the GM assigns stats consistent with their established characterisation. For procedural social companions (unnamed until acquired), use the social path formula:

| Stat | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Agility | 2 (social NPC default) |
| STR | 2 |
| Endurance | 3 |
| Cognition | 3 (social NPCs tend toward intelligence) |
| Charisma | 3 |

Combat Pool: (2×2)+2+3 = 9D (same as military officer — coincidence, correct).

### §3.3 Thread Sensitivity for Practitioner Companions

Named practitioner NPCs who become Companions (Yrsa Vossen excepted — non-practitioner) carry their canonical TS and Coherence:

| NPC | TS | Coherence at acquisition |
|-----|-----|------------------------|

| Maret Uln | 35 | 10 (full — she is disciplined) |

Practitioner companions can perform Thread operations in scenes, paying Coherence as normal. The PC is responsible for managing when the companion's Thread operations are declared.

## §4 COMPANION COMBAT AI

### §4.1 Priority Tree (When PC Does Not Direct)

When the PC does not issue explicit direction, the Companion follows this AI priority tree each round:

| Priority | Condition | Action |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Companion Wounds ≥ Max Wounds − 1 (one hit from incapacitation) | Full Guard (all dice to Defence). Do not attack. Survive. |
| 2 | PC is attacked by 2+ opponents AND no supporting ally present (Fibonacci eligible) | Rescue (per combat §4). Allocate minimum 3 dice to Rescue attempt. |
| 3 | PC is Wounded (any) AND Companion has Conviction alignment with PC (shared or compatible Belief) | Strike highest-threat opponent to PC. Full pool minus minimum Def allocation (4D Def, rest to Off). |
| 4 | PC is winning (holding initiative) | Standard split: floor(pool/2) Off, remainder Def. Target same opponent as PC. |
| 5 | Default | Strike nearest visible opponent to companion. Equal Off/Def split. |

**PC direct control:** If the player issues explicit instruction to the Companion in Phase 1, the Companion follows that instruction exactly. Direct control overrides the AI tree.

### §4.2 Initiative in Group Combat

Companions use the same initiative system as PCs. In multi-engagement group combat, the Companion acts as a separate combatant with their own initiative and pool. The GM rolls for the Companion; the player may advise but does not control.

**Companion Fibonacci contribution:** A Companion attacking the same target as the PC contributes the Fibonacci bonus to the PC's Off pool (2 attackers = +1D to each attacker). The Companion is a genuine tactical asset in group combat, not just flavour.

### §4.3 Practitioner Companions in Combat

If the Companion has TS ≥ 30, they may take Thread operations in combat per the standard Leap sequence. The Companion declares Thread intent in Phase 1. Thread operations consume their full round action (all pool to Defence during Leap round, per threadwork §2.3).

The GM manages Companion Coherence. If Coherence reaches 0, the Companion enters Rendering Crisis and is effectively removed from further Thread operation use.

### §4.4 Companion and General Duel

If the PC general enters personal combat (mass_battle §A.5) and the Companion is present, the Companion may attempt to re-establish Command as a proxy general:

- **Ob = 3** (one harder than the PC's Ob 2 — the Companion lacks full command authority).
- Pool: Companion's Cognition + relevant History. A Cognition 2 companion rolls 2D vs Ob 3: P(success) ≈ 12%. This is intentionally low — Companions are not general-replacements.
- On success: units fight under Companion's Command rating (floor(faction Military/2)) for the remainder of the mass battle turn.
- On failure: all units fight at 1D minimum (uncommanded) until the PC recovers Command.

## §5 COMPANION MASS COMBAT ROLE

### §5.1 Role by Disposition Tier

| Disposition | Mass Combat Capability |
|------------|----------------------|
| +3 (eligible) | Commands assigned unit (§D.2). Player Morale Effect (+1 Discipline to PC's units) applies to Companion's unit even when PC is in personal combat — the Companion maintains that presence through personal relationship. |
| +4 | Sub-commander. Companion's unit fights at Disposition +3 regardless of PC's Off/Def split declaration. Companion applies their own Command rating (floor(faction Military/2)) to that unit's pool. At Cascade Phase, Companion's unit generates 1 additional Co-Movement draw if it participated in battle this turn. |
| +5 | All of +4. Additionally: if PC is incapacitated (Stage 1) during mass battle, Companion automatically stabilises PC without consuming a Medicine action — once per battle. They are close enough to intervene immediately. |

### §5.2 Tactic Capability as Sub-Commander

When acting as a +4 sub-commander, the Companion may execute tactics from their unit without a separate tactic roll for Tier-1 tactics. Tier-2 tactics require an Ob 2 Command check.

| Tier | Tactics | Roll |
|------|---------|------|
| Tier 1 | Standard Advance, Disciplined Defence | Automatic — no roll |
| Tier 2 | Feigned Retreat, Hammer & Anvil, Refused Flank | Ob 2 Command check (Cognition + relevant History) |
| Special | Envelopment, Ambush | Not available — Companion cannot execute complex multi-unit tactics alone |

### §5.3 Companion and PC Embedding

If the PC is embedded in a territory during Strategic Phase (scale_transitions §9), the Companion's presence amplifies the effect: the +1D BG bonus to that territory's DA applies to **two** Domain Actions instead of one when the Companion is also present. Both must be in the territory. Maximum 2 Domain Actions receive the embedding bonus per season.

## §6 COMPANION DEPARTURE

### §6.1 Departure Conditions

A Companion departs when:

| Condition | Type | Scene |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Killed in combat | Combat death | Departure scene + death cascade (combat §13.3) fires as combat death |
| Killed in personal combat outside battle | Personal combat death | Departure scene + death cascade |
| Belief contradiction accumulates (3 Scars while Companion) | Conviction departure | Departure scene: the NPC can no longer align their Conviction with the PC's path |
| Player formally releases Companion | Voluntary release | Farewell scene: PC and Companion agree to part. No death cascade. Disposition maintained at current level. |
| Companion recruited away by rival faction | Defection | Departure scene + potential NPC as rival (Disposition −3 toward PC, new loyalty to rival) |
| PC faction collapses (Stability 0) | Dissolution | Companion becomes unaligned. They may rejoin if PC reconstitutes faction (no new companionship scene required). |

### §6.2 Departure Scene

A dedicated Scene Slate entry fires at Priority 0 (mandatory) when any non-voluntary departure condition is met. This scene is the last time the player has agency in the relationship.

**Scene structure (per departure type):**

*Combat death:* The player performs one of the §D.1 aftermath choices for the companion specifically (Tend the Wounded → stabilise check Ob 3; failure = death confirmed). If stabilised: Companion is incapacitated but alive, removed from active service until next session's scene. If confirmed dead: death cascade fires.

*Conviction departure:* The NPC states their Belief in full and explains what the PC has done that violates it. The player may attempt one final social appeal (Contest, 1 exchange): Success → NPC stays, Scar cleared; Failure → NPC leaves, Disposition freezes at +2 (lingering respect but cannot continue).

*Defection:* A rival faction has made a better offer. The player may counter-offer (matching the rival's incentive from §9.5 npc_behavior). If counter succeeds: NPC stays as Companion. If fails or not attempted: NPC departs.

### §6.3 Grief Mechanic (Optional — GM discretion)

When a Companion dies and Disposition was ≥ +4, the PC may enter a Grief state for 1 season: all social rolls involving Conviction statements incur +1 Ob (raw emotion undermines composed argument). The state clears automatically at next session start if the PC had at least 1 scene that processed the loss (any scene explicitly addressing the Companion's death counts).

## §7 COMPANION SCENES

### §7.1 Scene Generation

Companions generate scenes through two paths:

1. **Passive scene triggers:** Companion Arc triggers (scale_transitions §4.3.2) fire mandatory scenes when the Companion's Arc conditions are met.

2. **Outreach scenes:** Companions with Disposition +3/+4 may initiate Outreach (per npc_behavior §8.11.4) when the player has an open scene slot. A Companion's Outreach is prioritised over non-Knotted NPC Outreach.

### §7.2 Companion Scene Bonuses

When a scene directly involves a Companion:

- **Investigation scenes:** Companion at Disposition +4 grants +1D to the Fieldwork roll (they know things, notice things, have contacts). Maximum +1D regardless of Companion count.
- **Social contest:** Companion may Corroborate (per social_contest §4 Step 2b) at Ob 1 (Knot-sharing) if a Knot exists. This is the standard Corroborate rule — Companions typically have Knots.
- **Wilderness/movement:** Companion at Disposition +3 reduces Expedition scene time cost by 1 unit (they know the territory or carry the PC through unfamiliar ground).

### §7.3 "Where Were You?" and Companions

If a major world event occurs that the player missed, and the player has a Companion, the retrospective scene (scale_transitions §4.4) uses the Companion as the delivery mechanism:

> The Companion delivers the news and states their reaction. Their Conviction colors the report — they cannot give a neutral account. An Order-Conviction Companion frames Church Seizure as a constitutional crisis; an Equity-Conviction Companion frames it as an opportunity for the common people.

This makes the "Where Were You?" scene richer when a Companion is present — the player doesn't just receive information, they receive a relationship-filtered interpretation that may be biased, incomplete, or emotionally charged.

## ST-31 — GUILDS SOLO VICTORY DESIGN AND VALIDATION (ED-612)

**Gap:** Guilds have no solo victory condition. Per ED-612: "Unplayable as solo faction. Need either formal co-victory path, or Merchant Hegemony condition."

**Design analysis — what a Guilds victory should feel like:**

The Guilds are Moral Relativism + Autonomy. They win by becoming economically indispensable to the extent that the peninsula effectively operates under their commercial governance, not any faction's political governance. Their win is not territorial control (no military) — it is economic capture. Every faction becomes their client.

**Proposed Guilds Solo Victory — Merchant Hegemony:**

All conditions simultaneous at Accounting, held for 2 consecutive Accountings:

| Condition | Threshold | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Guild Favour | ≥ 5 in ≥ 5 territories | Economic presence across more than a third of the playable territories |
| Wealth | ≥ 8 | Exceeds any single rival faction's Wealth cap; commanding financial depth |
| Active Trade relationship | ≥ 3 playable factions hold at least one active Treaty or Economic Partnership with Guilds | Commercial integration with the majority of active powers |
| No playable faction | Has Wealth ≥ Guild Wealth − 2 | Guilds must be significantly wealthier than all rivals |
| Turmoil | ≤ 5 | They need a functional economy, not a ruined one |

**Simulation — is this reachable?**

**Guild Favour accumulation:** +1 per season of Trade in a territory (Moral Relativism Drift: +1 in highest-Favour territory at Year-End). With 2× Consul hand and Trade priority: 2 territories gain Favour/season. Year-End: highest gains a 3rd. Rate: ~2.5 Favour/season spread across territories. For Favour 5 in 5 territories: 5×5=25 Favour total, distributed. At 2.5/season: ~10 seasons minimum. With focused play (both Consuls into same 2 territories first): 2 territories at Favour 5 by Season 5. Then spread to 3 more: 3×5 = 15 more Favour, at remaining 1.5/season = 10 more seasons. Favour 5 in 5 territories: Season 15. This is correct — a dedicated Guilds player reaches this by the mid-game endgame.

**Wealth ≥ 8:** Starting Wealth 5. Seasonal cap ±2. At +1/season from Trade Success: Wealth 5→6→7→8 = 3 seasons if consistently successful (Ob 2, pool 5D = ~69% Success per season). Expected: 4–5 seasons. Wealth 8 achievable by Season 9–10.

**Trade relationships (Treaties):** Guilds cannot initiate Crown Treaties (Crown-only card). But "Economic Partnership" — proposed as a Consul card (Guilds-specific): a non-binding trade agreement that grants both parties +1D on Trade in territories they both operate in. Ob = floor(target Mandate/2). Hafenmark (Mandate 4): Ob 2. Crown (Mandate 5): Ob 3. Varfell (Mandate 3): Ob 2. 3 Economic Partnerships = Guilds Consul card used for Diplomacy rather than Trade for ~3 seasons.

**Rival Wealth constraint (no faction at Wealth ≥ Guild − 2):** At Guild Wealth 8, no rival can be at Wealth 7+. Church starts at Wealth 4, can grow to 6–7 via Trade. Hafenmark starts at Wealth 5, can grow to 6–7. If either reaches 7: Guilds need Wealth 9+ (approaching Wealth 7 cap — problem). **[FINDING (GAP): The "Guild Wealth − 2" constraint requires no rival at Wealth 7+ when Guilds are at 9+, but Wealth cap is 7 (absolute). This constraint can never be satisfied once Guilds reach Wealth 7 if ANY rival also reaches Wealth 7. The constraint needs adjustment: "No faction's Wealth exceeds Guild Wealth" (absolute constraint, not gap-based) — simpler and achieves the same gameplay goal.]**

**Revised constraint:** No rival faction's Wealth ≥ Guild Wealth at the moment of victory check. At Guild Wealth 8 (above standard Wealth 7 cap — needs exceptional growth path): this requires Guilds to reach Wealth 8 via Overwhelming Trade OW bonuses (Wealth +2 on OW, can push from 7 to 8 if seasonal cap allows: seasonal cap ±2 means a single OW bumps +2 from baseline, pushing past 7 to 8 if currently at 6). **Wealth 8 is achievable but requires two OW Trade results in the same season (OW = +2 in one action, starting from 6). P(OW 5D Ob 2) ≈ 56%. In one season: P(OW twice in one season) = Guilds have 2× Consul, both used for Trade: 0.56×0.56 = 31%. So Wealth 8 requires roughly 3 seasons of 2-Consul Trade attempts. Achievable but not trivial. Correct.**

**Victory timeline:** Guild Favour 5 in 5 territories (~S15) AND Wealth 8 (~S12) AND 3 Economic Partnerships (~S8) AND Strain ≤ 5. Binding constraint: Guild Favour spread to 5 territories (~S15). Guilds victory: ~Season 15–17. Slightly faster than Crown (~S12–16) and Church (~S25), slower than Varfell Path B (~S8–10). **[FINDING: Guilds win in the mid-endgame range — slower than fast factions (Varfell), faster than slow factions (Church). This is correct positioning for an economic faction: they build steadily, win convincingly, but aren't a sprint threat.]**

**Guilds co-victory path (existing §4 entry needed):** Per victory_v30 §4, Guilds have no co-victory listed. Proposed additions:

| Pair | Conditions |
|------|-----------|
| Guilds + Hafenmark | Guild Favour ≥ 4 in ≥ 4 territories AND Hafenmark TCV ≥ 10 AND Guilds Wealth ≥ 6 AND Hafenmark Mandate ≥ 4 AND active Economic Partnership between them |
| Guilds + Crown | Crown TCV ≥ 12 AND Guild Favour ≥ 4 in ≥ 3 Crown-adjacent territories AND 3+ seasons active Economic Partnership |

These co-victories represent commercial integration with political legitimacy — the Guild-backed Duchy or the Guild-funded Crown. Both should produce different campaign narratives.

**Guilds Priority Tree (for NPC or detailed play):**

1. SURVIVAL: Wealth ≤ 1 → Trade in highest-Prosperity territory. All other actions suspended.
2. ECONOMIC EXPANSION: Guild Favour < 5 in < 5 territories → Trade (both Consuls).
3. WEALTH BUILDING: Wealth < 7 → Trade (prioritise OW territories = Prosperity ≥ 4).
4. PARTNERSHIP BUILDING: fewer than 3 Economic Partnerships → Consul Outward (Diplomacy, target lowest-Mandate non-enemy faction first).
5. PROTECTION: if any territory with Favour ≥ 4 is threatened by military → place Diplomatic Token (prevents 1 hostile action) in that territory.
6. OPPORTUNISTIC: support whichever faction is closest to victory via Economic Partnership (+1D on their Trade in Guild-Favour territories). Position as kingmaker.

**[FINDING: The Guilds' Priority Tree 6 (kingmaker positioning) is a distinctive and correct gameplay identity. Guilds don't win through direct competition — they win by being useful enough that everyone eventually depends on them. The kingmaker role is real: at Guild Favour 5 in multiple territories, every faction benefits from Economic Partnership with Guilds. A faction that refuses Partnership disadvantages itself by losing +1D on Trade. This creates natural diplomatic gravity toward the Guilds without requiring Guilds to exert military pressure.]**

## ST-33 — ALTONIAN VANGUARD IP TRAJECTORY

**Full simulation of IP from 0 to 100, AER firewall mechanics, Elske arc, Torben exchange.**

### IP Advancement Formula

Per board_game §G-11 / PATCH P-28 (Institutional Pressure advancement):

From scattered references (board_game §G-11, scenario C simulation above):
- Base: no passive advance (IP 0 advance without trigger).
- Each season with inter-faction Battle: IP +2 (peninsula-wide conflict signal).
- CI ≥ 60: IP +1/season (Church dominance signals vulnerability to Altonia).
- Torben complied (sent to Altonia): IP −3/season while in Altonia (Altonian goodwill dampens threat).
- Torben returned (Loyalty restored): IP neutral.
- AER ≥ 3: IP threshold raised from 75 to 80.
- AER ≥ 4: IP threshold raised to 90.
- AER 2: threshold 75. AER 1: threshold 75. AER 0: immediate Vanguard deployment regardless of IP.

**IP accumulation simulation (competitive 4-player game):**

| Season | Events | IP change | IP |
|--------|---------|-----------|-----|
| 1–4 | No battles, CI 35 | +0 | 0 |
| 5 | 1 battle season (Crown vs Varfell) | +2 | 2 |
| 6 | CI 40 (no IP from CI alone below 60) | +0 | 2 |
| 7–9 | 2 more battle seasons | +4 | 6 |
| 10 | CI 55 | +0 | 6 |
| 11–13 | 3 battle seasons (LR coup war) | +6 | 12 |
| 14 | CI 65 | +1 | 13 |
| 15–17 | 3 battle seasons (Church-Crown conflict over T3) | +6+3 = +9 | 22 |
| 18 | CI 65 persistent | +1 | 23 |
| 19–20 | 2 more battle seasons | +4+2 = +6 | 29 |
| 21–25 | 5 seasons, 2 battle-seasons, CI 70+ → IP +1/season = +5 from CI, +4 from battles | +9 | 38 |
| 26–30 | 5 seasons, 2 battle-seasons, CI 75 → IP +1/season = +5 + +4 | +9 | 47 |

**[FINDING: In a standard 30-season game with 2 battle-seasons per 5 seasons, IP reaches only 47 by Season 30 — well below the 75–80 threshold. Altonian invasion does not fire in a standard-pace game. IP 75 requires either: (a) much heavier warfare (4–5 battle-seasons per 5 seasons = IP 8+/5seasons → 48+ per 10 seasons → 75 by Season 18), OR (b) persistent high CI (70+ from Season 10: +1/season for 20 seasons = +20 additional → 47+20 = 67 by Season 30, still below 75). The Altonian threat is a late-game pressure that only becomes existential in militaristic or Church-dominant campaigns. This is correct — the Altonian invasion should be a threat to consider, not an inevitable event.]**

**Accelerated scenario (militaristic campaign):** 4 battle-seasons per 5 seasons from Season 5 onward:

| Period | Battles | IP/period | CI IP/period | Period IP | Total IP |
|--------|---------|-----------|-------------|-----------|---------|
| S5–10 | 4 | +8 | +0 | +8 | 8 |
| S11–15 | 4 | +8 | +2 (TC60+, 2 seasons) | +10 | 18 |
| S16–20 | 4 | +8 | +5 (TC60+, 5 seasons) | +13 | 31 |
| S21–25 | 4 | +8 | +5 | +13 | 44 |
| S26–30 | 4 | +8 | +5 | +13 | 57 |

IP 75 at Season ~35 in this militaristic scenario. At AER 3 (threshold 80): Season ~38.

**[FINDING: Even in a heavily militaristic campaign (4 battles/5 seasons), IP 75 threshold requires 30+ seasons. The Altonian invasion is essentially a 30+ season threat in all but the most extreme warfare scenarios. The AER firewall (threshold 75→80 at AER ≥ 3) buys 5 additional seasons in already-borderline cases. Design note: IP should accumulate more aggressively to make the Altonian threat feel urgent in 20–30 season games. Proposed: IP +3 per battle-season (not +2), +2/season when CI ≥ 60 (not +1), to bring the threat to Season 20–25 in moderate-warfare campaigns.]**

The Founding mechanic correctly gates RM behind relationship investment (Vossen Disposition) and world-state conditions (PT, RS) rather than purely mechanical triggers. ## ST-35 — CARDINAL RESTORATION CONDITIONS (ED-614)

**Gap (ED-614):** When Cardinals activate as independent NPCs (Church Stability ≤ 2), no mechanic specifies the duration of Cardinal independence or what restores Confessor authority.

**Design analysis — what restoration should require:**

Cardinal independence is triggered by Himlensendt's institutional authority failing (Stability ≤ 2). Restoration of authority requires Stability to recover. But Stability recovery alone shouldn't automatically re-subordinate Cardinals who have made independent decisions — they've seen what life looks like without the Confessor's authority and may have acted in ways that created new commitments.

**Proposed Cardinal Restoration Mechanics:**

**Step 1 — Stability Recovery:** Church Stability must reach ≥ 3 (from ≤ 2) at Accounting. This triggers a Restoration Eligibility check.

**Step 2 — Cardinal-by-Cardinal Evaluation:**

Each independently-activated Cardinal undergoes a Leadership Deviation check to determine whether they re-subordinate:

| Cardinal | Restoration condition | Resistance |
|----------|----------------------|-----------|
| Fortitude (Stark) | Stability ≥ 3 AND no independent military commitment made during independence | Low — Stark serves institutions, not himself. Re-subordinates unless he has taken an action explicitly against Himlensendt. |
| Justice (Voss) | Stability ≥ 3 AND Himlensendt has not himself committed a documentable Belief violation during independence | High — Voss is the Inquisitor. If Himlensendt violated Church doctrine during the crisis, Voss has grounds to continue the Competence Review regardless of Stability. |
| Prudence (Vorn) | Stability ≥ 3 AND Church Wealth > Guild account balances she has diverted | Very Low — Vorn returns the moment institutional survival is viable. She is pragmatic above all. |
| Temperance (Kald) | Stability ≥ 3 AND Kald has not publicly shared her dataset | Low — if her dataset remained private, she re-subordinates. If published: Kald's independence is permanent (she cannot un-publish). |

**Re-subordination roll (for non-automatic cases):**

Spirit check (representing Conviction integrity) vs Ob = Scar count accumulated during independence:

| Cardinal | Spirit | Scar during independence | Re-sub Ob | P(re-subordinates) |
|----------|--------|------------------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Stark | 4 | Likely 0 | Ob 1 | ~85% |
| Voss | 5 | 0 (Voss doesn't receive Scars — Certainty 5) | Ob 1 | ~91% |
| Vorn | 3 | 0 (no Belief contests during independence) | Ob 1 | ~74% |
| Kald | 4 | 1 (if she had any internal conflict scenes) | Ob 2 | ~56% |

**[FINDING: With no Scars during independence, most Cardinals re-subordinate reliably when Stability recovers. The interesting cases are: (1) Voss if Himlensendt committed a visible Belief violation — Voss will NOT re-subordinate regardless of Stability, because his Inquisitor function demands completion of the Competence Review. (2) Kald if she published her dataset — permanent independence. These are the correct exceptions: re-subordination is automatic for pragmatic Cardinals (Vorn) and principled-but-institutional Cardinals (Stark), but contested for Cardinals whose independence produced irreversible acts.]**

**Cardinal independence duration cap:** Even without explicit restoration triggers, Cardinal independence should not persist indefinitely. Proposed: maximum 3 seasons of independent action. After 3 seasons, each Cardinal makes a final re-subordination roll at Ob 1 regardless of Scar count (the institution's gravity reasserts unless explicitly broken). This prevents Cardinal independence from becoming a permanent faction fragmentation in long games.

**ST-35 AUDIT:**

| Finding | Verdict | Severity |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Cardinal restoration mechanics fully specified here | Design proposal — resolves ED-614 | Resolved |
| Voss and Kald as non-restorable Cardinals under specific conditions | ✓ Correctly models principled vs pragmatic Cardinals | Confirmed |
| 3-season independence cap proposed | ✓ Prevents indefinite fragmentation | Confirmed |

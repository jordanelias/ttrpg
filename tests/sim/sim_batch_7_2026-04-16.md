## ST-41 — RM PRESENCE MARKER MECHANICS (RESOLVES ED-589)

**Gap:** RM's holding condition for T9 post-Uprising requires "≥ 3 Presence markers in T9" but Presence marker mechanics are not defined.

**Design context from loaded docs:**

- peninsular_strain §4.3 (Strain Fracture): "Accord −1 in one territory" — not Presence markers.
- board_game §G-08: "Community Weaving: Ob 2, −1 Ob per Presence marker in territory." Presence markers reduce Weaving Ob — they are already mechanically meaningful.
- board_game §G-06: "Forgetting Check pool: Influence + 1D per Presence marker in T13."
- victory_v30 §3.5: Framework Drift says "RM Presence markers spread to 1 adjacent territory per season when RM Stability ≥ 3."

Presence markers exist but their full placement, removal, maximum, and transfer rules are not consolidated in any single location.

### Proposed Presence Marker System (Resolves ED-589)

**Definition:** A Presence marker represents an active RM community cell in a territory — a group of people who have been organised, who practise Thread-adjacent cultural traditions, and who actively resist Church institutional authority. Presence markers are the RM's only territorial footprint. They cannot garrison, fortify, or legally govern, but they make territories culturally resistant to Church control.

**Placement:**

| Action | Marker gain | Card | Ob |
|--------|-------------|------|-----|
| Community Weaving (Praetor Inward) | +1 in target territory | Praetor | 2 −1 per existing marker (floor 1) |
| Founding Scene (§3.5 post-Founding) | +1 in Founding territory (automatic, no roll) | — | — |
| Framework Drift (RM Stability ≥ 3) | +1 in 1 adjacent territory | Passive | None |
| PC Embedding bonus (scale_transitions §9) | +1 in PC's territory if RM PC present | Passive | None |

**Maximum per territory:** 5. A territory with 5 markers is "Saturated" — no further marker placement possible (the community is at full cultural capacity).

**Removal:**

| Trigger | Markers lost |
|---------|-------------|
| Successful Heresy Investigation (Church AP ≥ 3 AND roll Success+) | −2 in target territory |
| Military occupation of territory (any non-RM faction garrisons) | −1 per season until removed or marker count 0 |
| Peninsular Strain Crisis (7–8): Accord collapse in territory | −1 (population instability disrupts cell cohesion) |
| Church Graduated Seizure Success (territory transfers to Church) | All markers removed immediately |
| RM Stability 0 (faction collapse) | All markers reduced to 0 in all territories |

**Marker transfer:** When a territory changes controller (Battle, Seizure, Proclamation), RM markers are NOT removed unless the new controller actively suppresses them (Heresy Investigation or sustained garrison). Markers persist as a cultural substrate beneath political control. This represents the historical reality that grassroots movements survive regime changes — the people who attended community meetings continue doing so regardless of which flag flies over the town hall.

**Presence markers and T9 (post-Uprising):**

To hold T9 post-Uprising, RM needs ≥ 3 markers. How do they reach 3 markers in T9 (Church capital, hostile territory) before or immediately after the Uprising?

Community Weaving pre-Uprising: T9 is Church-held. Weaving in T9 requires: Church attention triggers (+1 Church AP per Weaving attempt). With each Weaving: Church AP accumulates. At AP 3: Heresy Investigation fires. Each successful investigation: −2 markers. This is the core tension: RM must build markers in T9 without triggering Investigation, or build faster than the Investigation removes them.

**Pre-Uprising marker strategy:**

Strategy A — Slow accumulation (Praetor Inward 1 marker/season, but each attempt triggers AP): 
- Season 1 Weaving T9: +1 marker. AP: +1.
- Season 2 Weaving: +1 marker. AP: +1. Total markers: 2, AP: 2.
- Season 3 Weaving: AP reaches 3. Church fires Heresy Investigation. 
  - Church Investigation pool: Mandate 5 + Influence 6 = 11D vs Ob 2. Almost certain success.
  - Result: −2 markers. Markers: 0. Back to zero.

**[FINDING: Strategy A fails. RM cannot build 3 markers in T9 through normal Weaving because Church's Heresy Investigation (at AP 3) removes markers faster than they accumulate. Three Weavings = 3 AP = Investigation fires after the 3rd Weaving, removing the 3 markers just placed.]**

Strategy B — Covert accumulation (using Niflhel contacts or PC operating below Watched threshold):
If the PC practitioner has Exposure ≤ 1 in T9 during each Weaving scene (careful, quiet operations):
- Each OW Weaving: Exposure −1 (per fieldwork §4.2: OW → Exposure −1 "elegant work"). At Exposure 0: no AP trigger.
- 3 OW Weavings = 3 markers, Exposure 0, AP 0. Investigation never fires.
- P(OW Weaving at 18D Ob 2) ≈ 65% per attempt. Three consecutive OWs: 0.65^3 ≈ 27%.

**[FINDING: Strategy B is viable but requires skill. A 27% probability of successfully accumulating 3 markers without triggering Investigation is achievable in a single game but requires multiple attempts and patience. This is correctly difficult — 3 markers in the Church's own capital requires exceptional operational execution.]**

Strategy C — Uprising OW bonus:
The Uprising OW result (T9 PT −2) also provides +2 Presence markers automatically (the successful Uprising generates immediate community mobilisation). This is the cleanest path: a single successful OW Uprising → T9 PT 5→3 AND 2 automatic markers. Then 1 more marker through post-Uprising Weaving (Ob now 1 with 2 existing markers). 

Post-Uprising Weaving Ob: 2 − 2 (existing markers) = 0 → floor 1. 1 Weaving at Ob 1 with Praetor card: ~91% success. Total: 3 markers within 1 season of Uprising.

**[FINDING: Strategy C (OW Uprising + 1 post-Uprising Weaving) is the canonical path to 3 T9 markers. The Uprising's OW bonus provides 2 markers automatically, making the holding condition achievable within 1 season of the Uprising. This is exactly the right design — the Uprising itself creates the community mobilisation required to hold the territory. Add to victory_v30 §3.5 Overwhelming result: "+2 Presence markers in T9."]**

## ST-43 — RS RUPTURE SHARED LOSS (WHAT DOES THE END OF THE WORLD LOOK LIKE)

**But the game likely ends before Season 47.** Let's track the RS decline curve's mechanical consequences season by season from Season 35 onward (Critical band entry):

**Season 35: RS = 72 − 35×1.45 = 72 − 50.75 = 21.25 → Critical band (20→1)**

At RS Critical (20–1):
- All Thread operations: +1 Ob worldwide.
- Spontaneous Gap risk: each Accounting, roll d10. On 1–2: a new Gap opens in a random territory (GM determines location). This has a 20% chance per Accounting.
- Faction Stability checks: Ob 1 for all factions at every Accounting. Failure: Stability −1.
- Monstrous Incursions: any existing Gap has a 30% chance per Accounting of producing a Monstrous Incursion requiring military response (Military vs Ob 3).
- RS continues to decline: RS 21 → 20 → 19...

**Season 35–40 (Critical band, 5 seasons):**

Expected new Gaps from spontaneous risk: 5 Accountings × 20% = 1 expected Gap. Each Gap produces Monstrous Incursion 30%/Accounting. 1 Gap × 5 Accountings × 30% = 1.5 expected Incursions. Each Incursion: faction Military checks to survive (Military vs Ob 3). Failure: Military −1 AND the Incursion "spreads" (adjacent territory gets Stability −1 and a new Stability check).

Faction Stability checks (Ob 1, 5 Accountings): Each faction rolls per Accounting. Crown (Mandate 5, Stability 5 starting): ~91% success per check. Expected Stability loss over 5 seasons: 5×0.09 = 0.45 → rounds to 0 lost most campaigns. Varfell (Stability 4): similar. Church (Stability 3 after social contest pressure): 74% success → expected 5×0.26 = 1.3 Stability loss → Stability 3→2. Church approaches Survival priority.

**[FINDING: At RS Critical, the mandatory Stability checks are the most consistent threat. Weaker factions (Stability 3) lose ~1 Stability per 4 seasons of Critical band operation. This accelerates faction collapse — factions that are already politically weakened cannot survive a world falling apart around them. The Critical band correctly accelerates all existing political pressures. A Church at Stability 2 from prior social contest losses will likely hit Stability 0 within 8 seasons of Critical band entry — Church self-destructs before the world does, in a historically plausible church-during-apocalypse dynamic.]**

**Season 40: RS = 21 − 5×1.45 = 13.75 → RS 14**

**Season 43: RS = 14 − 3×1.45 = 9.65 → RS 10**

At RS 10 (approaching 0):
- All Thread operations: +1 Ob (still Critical band). But now: Calamity Radiation reaches all territories. All fieldwork at Hidden depth (2) or above: +3 Ob (3 bands below 60). Personal combat wounds: +1 Ob to all Endurance-dependent actions (bodies struggle to maintain coherence). **Season 45: RS = 3**

At RS 3: the rendering is almost transparent. The original Calamity boundary is visible to anyone who looks. Practitioners with TS ≥ 30 can see through the rendering entirely in T15. Even non-practitioners near T15 experience rendering dissolution (a permanent, uncanny-valley wrongness). Parliament stops meeting (no one can agree that the meetings are real). Trade collapses (Wealth −1/season to all factions — economic trust dissolves when people can't trust their perception). 

**Season 47: RS = 0. RUPTURE.**

**What Rupture looks like (extrapolated from canonical rules + design logic):**

RS = 0 at Accounting. Trigger: "All factions lose. Second Calamity."

- The rendering barrier between the originary state and the rendered world collapses entirely. The threadcut entities (those living at the junction of consciousness and rendering) achieve full originary state access — they are no longer maintaining their rendered forms, they simply ARE their configurations.
- The Calamity configuration that was deliberately removed (discovered in ST-25) re-asserts. Whatever it was holding in equilibrium — the Monstrous configuration that the Einhir chose to release — returns to full presence. The peninsula is not destroyed. It is transformed. The population experiences it as the world ending. From the Thread perspective, it is a configuration returning to its pre-manipulation state.
- Mechanically: all faction tokens are removed. The territories become uncontrolled. The game ends in Rupture Loss.

**What does the PLAYER experience:**

Priority 0 mandatory Zoom In: "The Rupture Scene." Every PC is present for the Rupture. The scene is not a battle — there is nothing to fight. It is a witnessing scene. The GM describes what the PC sees as the rendering fails: their companion's face becomes transparent for a moment. The market square is the same market square it was forty years ago simultaneously. The cathedral bells ring although they haven't for three seasons.

The PC has one final action: **The Last Argument.** A personal scene, no mechanics, just declaration. The PC states what they stand for in the moment the world ends. This resolves their Belief arc regardless of faction outcome. If the PC's primary Belief was "I will protect Thread practitioners": the scene of them doing exactly this as the world ends is the resolution. Hollow or fulfilled — the player narrates.

**[FINDING: The RS Rupture shared loss condition is mechanically defined ("all factions lose") but has no experiential specification. The "Last Argument" scene design above is the correct closing mechanic — the world ends are not lost, they are witnessed, and the PC's final declaration of Belief is the campaign's emotional resolution even in defeat. The shared loss should feel like tragedy, not game-over screen. This requires a scene mechanic that doesn't currently exist in any doc. Proposed: add to victory_v30 §5 Shared Loss Conditions: "RS Rupture triggers 'The Rupture Scene' — a mandatory personal scene for all PCs at Priority 0. Each PC makes a Last Declaration (their primary Belief stated in full). The GM narrates the Rupture through the lens of each PC's Belief. No mechanical outcome; narrative resolution only."]**

## ST-44 — SIEGE MECHANICS (PROLONGED FORT ASSAULT)

**Setup:** Crown besieges T14 Ehrenfeld (LR-held post-coup, Fort 3, Garrison: 3 LR Heavy Infantry). Standard Assault is impossible in a single season — Fort 3 gives +3D to defenders. Crown needs a Siege (multi-season reduction of Fort Level before assault).

**Siege mechanics (from mass_battle §A.x references and board_game scenario C context):**

From board_game PATCH P-21 table: "Fortify (Consul Inward): Ob 2, Fort +1 (max 3)." From military_layer §2.2b: "Assault against Fort: +Fort Level to defender pool." From npc_behavior Vanguard Priority 2b (ED-551 resolution): "Assault (Fortification −1 per successful Assault; RS −1 per siege season)."

**[FINDING (GAP): There is no formal Siege action specified for playable factions in the loaded docs. The Vanguard Priority 2b Assault entry (written in batch 3 to resolve ED-551) specifies Ob = base Battle Ob + 1 (fortified), with Fort −1/Success and Fort −2/OW. But this was written as an NPC-specific action. Playable factions have no equivalent Siege mechanic in any loaded doc. Military action against a Fort territory goes directly to Battle — which means Legionary March → Battle (defender gets Fort bonus to pool). There is no way for a playable faction to reduce Fort level before assault except through a missing Siege action.]**

**Proposed Siege Mechanic (Resolves this gap):**

**Siege (Legionary Inward — multi-season encirclement):**

Siege replaces Battle declaration on a fortified territory. One Legionary card + adjacent unit required.

**Pool:** Attacker Military stat, TN 7, Ob = 2 + Fort Level. No defender roll (Siege is attrition, not combat).

| Degree | Effect | Cost |
|--------|--------|------|
| Overwhelming | Fort −2 in target territory. RS −1 (siege damage). | Attacker Wealth −1 (supply cost). |
| Success | Fort −1 in target territory. RS −1. | Attacker Wealth −1. |
| Partial | No Fort reduction. RS −1 (siege damage without progress). | Attacker Wealth −1. |
| Failure | No Fort reduction. Attacker Stability −1 (failed siege = morale damage). Siege may be ended or continued next season. | Attacker Wealth −1. |

**Siege duration cap:** 5 seasons maximum before the attacking army must choose: Assault (Battle, at current Fort level) or Withdraw (no territorial gain, units return).

**Garrison response during Siege:** The defended faction may attempt Supply Run each season (Consul Outward from adjacent friendly territory): Ob 2 roll. Success: Garrison holds (no Discipline degradation). Failure: Garrison Discipline −1 per season of failed supply.

**Simulation — Crown vs LR Fort 3 (T14):**

Season 1 Siege: Crown Military 4 vs Ob = 2+3 = 5. Pool 4D TN7: 9,8,7,3 → 3 net. Ob 5 = Failure. No Fort reduction. Attacker Wealth −1. RS −1. Stability −1 (failure).

Season 2 Siege: 4D TN7 vs Ob 5: 9,8,3,7 → 3 net. Failure again. At Ob 5 with pool 4D: P(≥5 net) ≈ P(net ≥ 5 from 4D) ≈ 2%. Fort 3 siege is essentially impossible for Military 4. Crown cannot reduce Fort 3 through standard Siege.

**[FINDING: Siege against Fort 3 with Military 4 (Ob 5) is nearly impossible — P(Success) ≈ 2% per season. Crown needs either (a) Military 5 (Ob 5 → P(OW) at 5D: ~15%, P(Success): ~25%) or (b) a method to reduce Fort before siege (Sabotage — Tribune Intel OW on T14 reveals garrison weakness, −1 Ob to Siege). Fort 3 correctly represents an impregnable fortress under standard military circumstances. The correct counter: Crown must use political tools (Crown Treaty, Parliamentary Embargo of LR supply) or wait for garrison Discipline degradation from Supply Run failures.]**

**Garrison degradation path (Supply Run failures):**

LR Supply Run (Consul Outward from T3 adjacent to T14): Ob 2. LR Military 6, 6D: P(Success) ≈ 95%. LR supply runs almost always succeed — Fort 3 garrison holds indefinitely. Crown cannot starve them out either.

**[FINDING: Fort 3 + Military 6 garrison is effectively impregnable to military approaches. The only viable Crown strategy: reduce LR Mandate (via Parliamentary action) until LR enters Survival priority and withdraws garrison voluntarily, OR wait for Longevity failure of Ehrenwall, OR use thread/fieldwork to expose garrison vulnerability. Correct design — the siege of Ehrenfeld should be a campaign-arc, not a single-season event.]**

### A. Accord/Order Clarification (ED-626)

**New section for peninsular_strain_v1 §2 footer:**

> **Note on Accord vs Order:** Accord (0–3) is a province-level attribute governing territorial TCV contribution and population acceptance of the controlling faction's governance. Order (0–5) is a settlement-level attribute governing popular stability within a specific settlement. They are independent: a province at Accord 2 (Compliant) may contain a settlement at Order 1 (Resistant unrest) or Order 4 (stable governance). Peninsular Strain effects (§4.3) affect Accord. Settlement Order is governed by: garrison presence (+1/season with garrison), Govern success (+1 from Success, +2 from OW in that settlement), battle at settlement (−2 immediately), and sustained hostile occupation (−1/season). Church Heresy Investigation at settlement level: Order −1 for non-Church-aligned population in that settlement only.

### B. BG Parliamentary Vote Lobby Cap (ED-621)

**Addition to social_contest §10 BG Vote Setup, Step 4:**

> **Lobby cap (BG mode):** Lobbying offset is restricted to the compromise zone at BG Vote start. Maximum starting Conviction Track: 6. Minimum starting track: 4. This matches the Hybrid mode restriction (§11 per PP-256). Lobbying cannot predetermine a BG Parliamentary Vote — it provides advantage, not an automatic outcome. ### C. Grand Contest Recall Fix (ED-617)

**Addition to social_contest §4 Step 3 Recall bonus:**

> **Grand Contest Recall (PP-NEW):** In Grand Contests (5-exchange format), the Recall bonus (+2D) applies once per cited source for the entire contest duration, not per exchange. The same document, date, prior statement, or named precedent cannot generate a new Recall bonus in a subsequent exchange. Orators must cite different sources in each exchange for which they claim Recall. This prevents the pool-compounding effect of repeating a single high-quality citation across all 5 exchanges. Formal Contests (3 exchanges) retain per-exchange Recall (shorter format makes source-cycling mechanically impractical).

### D. Varfell No-Senator Note (ED-622)

**Addition to tc_political_redesign §5.1 Varfell hand description:**

> **Parliamentary Constraint:** Varfell has no Senator card and therefore cannot independently file Parliamentary motions. Varfell may declare Side A or B in any existing motion and contribute their Mandate to the vote, but cannot initiate a motion without another faction's Senator filing on their behalf. This constraint is intentional — Varfell's institutional role is informational (intelligence) rather than legislative. Varfell's political influence operates through relationship capital (convincing Crown or Hafenmark to file on their behalf via Diplomacy, Ob 2) not institutional authority.

### E. Constrained Sub-Arc State (ED-586)

**Addition to npc_behavior_v30 §5.1 Generic Transition Triggers:**

> **Constrained sub-arc (Mandate < 3):** When a named NPC's faction Mandate drops below 3, the NPC's primary arc behaviors that require Mandate expenditure are suspended. The NPC enters a "Constrained" sub-arc state for as long as Mandate < 3: arc-defined Priority 2 (Conviction-critical) and Priority 3 (Domain expansion) actions are replaced by Priority 6 (institutional rebuilding: Govern, Trade). The NPC's Conviction and Resonant Style are unchanged — only their behavioral priority sequence shifts. When Mandate recovers to ≥ 3, the NPC exits Constrained state and resumes their active arc from its prior state. Scar count is preserved; no arc progress is lost during Constrained state.

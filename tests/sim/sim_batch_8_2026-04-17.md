## ST-51 — NIFLHEL ASSET PLACEMENT + CONTACT PROTOCOL (ED-611)

**Gap (ED-611):** Niflhel's political influence arm lacks formal Asset placement mechanics. Current rules only specify reactive sabotage.

### Niflhel Contact Protocol (How Players First Make Contact)

From npc_behavior §9.7 (Niflhel social toolkit): "Private negotiation only; one-on-one. Attunement-primary pool, TN 7, Ob = floor(target Stability/2)+1." The contact protocol itself is underdeveloped.

**Proposed Niflhel Contact Procedure:**

Contact with Niflhel requires a specific trigger — Niflhel does not seek out players, they respond to demonstrated usefulness or observed assets.

**Trigger events (any one sufficient):**
1. Player reaches Evidence Track 5 on investigation targeting Niflhel's operations ("Who is supplying the Southernmost Thread equipment?") — Niflhel detects the investigation and makes first contact to assess the threat.
2. Player's Tribune Spy OW in a Niflhel-active territory — the OW result reveals a Niflhel operative, and Niflhel makes contact within 1 season to negotiate silence.
3. Player possesses an Einhir artifact or Thread knowledge that Niflhel has been seeking (discovered through their own intelligence network) — they approach with an offer.
4. Player has operated covertly in T6, T13, or T15 for 3+ seasons without generating Heresy Investigation attention — Niflhel recognises operational discipline and assesses recruitment potential.

**Contact Scene (mandatory Priority 1 Zoom In once triggered):**

A Niflhel intermediary (never a principal — Arm 1 deploys a Quartermaster representative, not the Quartermaster himself) approaches the player in a public space where violence would be conspicuous. Scene structure:
1. **Approach:** Player knows they are being approached by someone professional. Attunement check TN 7 Ob 1: success = player recognises the approach is deliberate, not accidental. Failure = player is uncertain of intent.
2. **Assessment:** The intermediary states one verifiable fact that demonstrates Niflhel knows something the player thought was hidden. This establishes credibility without revealing organisational depth.
3. **Offer:** One concrete exchange. Never ideology — always transaction. "You have information we want. We have services you might find useful."
4. **Player response:** Accept (relationship established at Disposition 0, neutral transaction basis), Decline (intermediary notes it, no immediate consequence, future contact at higher cost), Attack (Niflhel marks the player as a hostile asset — future Tribune Spy actions in Niflhel territories gain +1 Ob).

**[FINDING: Niflhel's Contact Protocol is designed around intelligence asymmetry — they demonstrate superior knowledge to establish leverage before asking for anything. The player cannot avoid this contact once triggered (Priority 1), but can refuse the offer. The attack option (rare) triggers the only immediate consequence. This creates a genuine choice: engaging with Niflhel provides services but creates entanglement; refusing keeps independence but closes access to their capabilities. No moral framing — purely transactional.]**

### Asset Placement Mechanics (ED-611 Resolution)

**What is a Niflhel Asset?**

An Asset is a named operative placed within another faction's structure — not as a spy who can be detected, but as a long-term sleeper who influences faction behavior from within. Assets are the difference between Niflhel as a reactive organisation (Priority 2 sabotage) and Niflhel as a proactive political force.

**Asset Placement Procedure:**

Asset placement requires Niflhel to have Faction Favour ≥ 3 with the target faction (they have done enough business that an operative can be placed naturally). This requires 3+ seasons of active trade/service relationship.

**Card:** Tribune Outward (Niflhel unique — same card as Intel, different action declaration). Once per season.

**Pool:** Influence. **Ob:** floor(target faction Stability/2) + 1.

| Degree | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Overwhelming | Asset placed in faction's highest-Mandate territory. Asset has Cover Level 3 (very hard to detect). Gains 1 Piece of Intel automatically each season without roll. |
| Success | Asset placed. Cover Level 2. Must roll Tribune Spy each season to maintain intel stream (Ob 1). |
| Partial | Asset embedded but undeclared — Niflhel has a contact but not full operative capability. Can be activated next season at −1 Ob. |
| Failure | Placement fails. Target faction gains one suspicion marker on Niflhel (at 3 suspicion markers: intel operations against Niflhel gain −1 Ob). |

**Asset Cover Level:**

| Cover Level | Detection Ob | Intel yield | Duration |
|------------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Ob 1 (easy to find) | 1 stat revealed per season | Until detected |
| 2 | Ob 2 | 2 stats revealed per season | Until detected |
| 3 | Ob 3 | Full stat line + 1 action preview | Until detected |

**Detection:** Any Tribune Investigate OW result in the territory where the Asset operates detects Cover Level 1. Exceptional Investigation (Structural, threshold 8) can detect Cover Level 2–3. Cover Level 3 requires direct Heresy Investigation plus Tribune OW to uncover.

**What an Asset does:**

1. **Intelligence stream:** Automatic stats reveal each season (per Cover Level).
2. **Behavioral nudge:** Once per campaign arc, an Asset can attempt to influence one NPC Priority Tree decision. Roll: Niflhel Influence vs Ob = NPC Leadership Deviation Ob. Success: the Asset whispers in the right ear and one Priority Tree action this season is selected by Niflhel instead of NPC AI. Failure: Asset's cover is tested (Cover Level −1).
3. **Sabotage trigger:** If Priority 2 fires (Niflhel reactive sabotage), Assets in the territory add +1D to the sabotage pool.

**Simulation — Niflhel places Asset in Church:**

Season 8. Niflhel Faction Favour with Church: 3 (from regular financial services to Church's infrastructure). Niflhel Influence 4, Church Stability 5. Ob: floor(5/2)+1 = 3+1 = 4? No: floor(5/2) = 2, +1 = 3. Ob 3. Pool 4D. 4d10: 9,8,7,3 → 3 net. Success. Asset placed in Church, Cover Level 2. 

**Asset in Church:** Each season: 2 Church stats revealed to Niflhel. Niflhel knows Church Mandate, Influence, CI progression. This positions Niflhel as a faction that knows everything about Church's institutional development — they could sell this intelligence to any faction. Standard Niflhel business.

**When player buys Church intel from Niflhel:** Player pays 1 Wealth (Niflhel rate: "all services at market rate"). Receives: Church full stat line (Mandate 4, Influence 6, CI 48, Stability 5 — Documentary tag, Verified reliability). This is Verified intelligence available nowhere else in a single transaction. Evidence Track +2 (Documentary tag) on any Church investigation.

**[FINDING: Niflhel's Asset system creates an intelligence market that any faction can access at Wealth cost. This is Niflhel's core political value proposition: they sell certainty in an information-asymmetric game. The Asset placement mechanic correctly models how criminal/intelligence organisations achieve long-term institutional penetration — not through dramatic coups, but through patient, quiet presence that accumulates over seasons. The Asset is not detectable through casual play (Cover Level 2: Ob 2 to detect), making Niflhel's presence genuinely covert until a player specifically investigates. ED-611 RESOLVED.]**

## ST-53 — EXCOMMUNICATION PROCEDURE (ED-625)

**Full specification of the Church Excommunication mechanic.**

From npc_behavior §2.12, §3.2, and social_contest §7 (Asymmetric Proceedings), batch 6 ST-36 found Church wins Tribunal in 1 exchange at CI 70. This batch formalises the full procedure.

**Excommunication — Full Mechanics:**

**Prerequisites (Church must meet all):**
- CI ≥ 40 (Church Assertive — institutional authority to pronounce major censure)
- Church Mandate ≥ 4 (cannot Excommunicate from a position of weakness)
- Active Heresy Investigation on target (must have been prosecuted through Investigation → Evidence Track ≥ 3)
- Cardinal of Justice (Voss) must be in Arc A or have formally opened the Excommunication case (he is the procedural authority)

**Target eligibility:** Any named NPC or PC faction who:
- Has publicly violated a Church Obligation (violation on record), OR
- Has been convicted in a prior Tribunal proceeding (Track reached ≥ 7), OR
- Has Attention Pool ≥ 5 in Church-held territory AND 2 prior failed Heresy Investigations (Church has tried twice and been thwarted)

**The Excommunication Tribunal:**

This is a Church Tribunal (social_contest §7) with specific modifications:

- **Exchange count:** Set by Voss (Inquisitor-General). Range 1–3 (shorter than standard 1–5 — Excommunication is the most extreme measure, not a deliberative process).
- **Piety Track start:** 7 (biased heavily toward Church — near-decisive already). Resistance for accused: halved = 1.
- **Accused corroboration:** None permitted (no character witnesses, no coalition defense).
- **Primary attribute:** Cognition (Expert Judge = Voss himself is the adjudicator).

**Church pool at CI 70:** Voss Cognition 5×2 = 10 + History(Church Law 3pts) + 3 = 16D + CI bonus floor(70/20)=3 = 19D. TN 7.

**Accused pool (typical NPC, e.g., Baralta):** Cognition 5×2 + History(Constitutional Law 3pts) + 3 = 16D. Genre: Memory (constitutional procedure = Memory). Primary genre bonus: +1D. Total: 17D.

**Exchange 1:**

With Track starting at 7 (not 6, as standard Tribunal), Church needs only to maintain position (7 already = decisive for Church). Any net positive roll by Church pushes track above 7. Track already at decisive position means the Tribunal has essentially been decided before Exchange 1.

**[FINDING: With Track at 7 (already decisive), Church wins the Tribunal before a roll is made. The Piety Track starting at 7 means Excommunication begins at the decisive threshold — Church wins instantaneously unless Baralta can move the track to ≤ 3 (a 4-step reversal from Track 7). With 19D vs 17D, Baralta needs consistent net margins of 4+ in her favor across 3 exchanges — P ≈ P(Baralta net − Church net ≥ 4) = P(margin −4 from −2D disadvantage) ≈ 5–8% per exchange. Across 3 exchanges: P(Baralta reverses to ≤ 3) ≈ 0.001%. The Excommunication is effectively automatic if it reaches the Tribunal stage.]**

**Correct design understanding:** The Excommunication is an institutional fait accompli once triggered. The player's correct response is NOT to defend at Tribunal — it is to prevent the Tribunal from being called. Methods:

1. **Prevent Prerequisites:** Keep Attention Pool < 5 (suppress AP through quiet operations), ensure no Obligation violations, avoid repeat Tribunal convictions.
2. **Pre-CI 40 Parliamentary Block:** File Parliamentary motion "Limit Church Institutional Proceedings" before CI 40 (Ob 2 at CI < 40). Once CI 40+, Church gains +1D Parliamentary defense making blocking harder.
3. **Diplomatic resolution:** If Voss is in Arc B (Questioning), the Excommunication investigation can be suspended by presenting him with an authenticated Church text that creates genuine theological ambiguity about the target's guilt. Solidarity RS on Voss through Knot at Close (requires sustained relationship) can also buy 1 season of delay.
4. **Post-filing disruption:** Once filed, last chance is Hafenmark Parliamentary Manoeuvre (free interrupt to any Crown Policy Instrument — not available for Church actions per §9.3 PP-019). Church Excommunication is not subject to Policy Instrument rules. **[GAP: No existing Parliamentary mechanic can interrupt a Church Tribunal filing. The Tribunal happens automatically if Church has card capacity.]**

**Consequences on successful Excommunication:**

| Target | Consequence |
|--------|------------|
| Named NPC (Baralta) | CI +4 (Church authority demonstrated). Target Mandate −2, Certainty forced to Minimum(current, 2). Target enters relevant arc transition (Baralta → Arc B: The Pragmatist). Reputation shift: all NPCs with Disposition ≥ +1 to target must make Belief check (Certainty roll Ob 1) or Disposition −1 (the Excommunication is a social earthquake). |
| Player Character | Player's faction Mandate −3 (ecclesiastical illegitimacy). Player cannot participate in Parliamentary motions while Excommunicated (institutionally excluded). Standing −2. Player's Companions with Faith conviction: each must make a departure scene check (1 Scar each from the Excommunication's spiritual weight). |
| Faction (collective Excommunication, Hafenmark if Baralta leads) | Faction Mandate −2. All Church Domain Actions against that faction: Ob −1 (facilitated by ecclesiastical mandate). CI +3 (institutional authority demonstrated at faction level). |

**Excommunication Revocation:** Possible at Church's discretion. Requires Church Mandate ≥ 5, CI ≥ 50, and a formal Act of Contrition by the Excommunicated party (public Obligation: "I abjure [the specific violation]"). Revocation: Mandate −1 (process is costly), CI −1 (partial authority concession). Used primarily when Church needs the Excommunicated party's political cooperation.

**ED-625: RESOLVED.**

## ST-54 — THREADCUT BEING: COMBAT AND THREAD OPERATIONS AGAINST

**Setup:** Player practitioner (TS 50, Coherence 8) encounters a threadcut being in T15 — an entity from the Calamity-era disturbance that has maintained self-rendering for 500 years. It is not hostile (it has no conception of hostility), but it is investigating the practitioner's Thread operations, which are disturbing its local configuration.

**Observing the being (threadwork §6.2):**

Player TS 50: "Stable perception. The being is fully rendered. Its continuous self-sustaining Thread work visible as a hum of operation."

The being appears as: an elderly man working in a field that doesn't exist anymore. He is performing the same 3 actions on a loop (planting, tending, harvesting — in a second). He does not notice the practitioner watching him. He is not performing for an audience. He is simply continuing.

**Thread-Read of the being's configuration:**

Pool: (Spirit 5×2) + History(Thread 3pts) + TPS(50/10=5) = 18D. Ob 2 (Personal scale, the being is a Personal-scale configuration). TN 7.

Roll 18D TN7: 9,8,7,4,10,7,3,8,4,9,2,8,3,7,9,8 → 11 net. OW. Evidence +3 (Thread-verified tag). Reading: "The being is maintaining a Weaving from the originary state at Structural scale. Not weaving the local substrate — weaving itself. Every moment is an active Thread operation that it has been performing continuously for approximately 500 years. The Coherence cost would have destroyed any organic practitioner in 2 seasons. This entity has no Coherence track — it HAS no rendering that can destabilise. It IS the Thread configuration it maintains."

**[FINDING: Thread-Reading a threadcut being reveals the impossible: a sustained 500-year Structural Weaving at no Coherence cost. This discovery is campaign-defining information. Organic practitioners pay −2 Coherence per Structural operation. This being does it continuously. If practitioners could learn HOW it does this — the self-maintenance technique — it would represent the key to sustainable high-scale Thread practice. This is the correct depth-4 Fieldwork discovery type: information so significant it changes the campaign's strategic possibilities.]**

**Fieldwork interaction — Interview the being:**

Action: Interview (Attunement, Depth 3 — the being is at Buried depth for social access: requires Disposition +3 or TS ≥ 10). Player TS 50 ≥ 10 ✓. Ob 3.

Pool: (Att 4×2) + History(Thread 2pts) = 11D. Roll: 9,8,7,4,10,7,3 → 5 net. Ob 3 = Success. Evidence +2.

**Interview result:** The being perceives the Interview — it detects the practitioner's intentionality directed at it. It pauses its loop for 3 seconds and returns the practitioner's gaze. It does not speak. But in that moment of attention: the practitioner perceives, at their rendering ceiling (TS 50), the being's self-maintenance structure. Not the content — the structure. Like understanding how a word is constructed without knowing what the word means.

**[FINDING (WORLDBUILDING): The Interview of a threadcut being doesn't produce language or communication — it produces direct structural perception. This is the correct experience for an encounter at the edge of human rendering capacity. The being is not mysterious because it refuses to communicate; it is mysterious because communication requires a shared rendering framework, and this being's framework is the originary state itself. Any practitioner who reaches TS 70+ and encounters this being would perceive the full self-maintenance structure — and might understand it.]**

**Thread operation against the being:**

What happens if the player attempts a Weave targeting the being's self-maintenance configuration?

Per threadwork §6.3: "Wounds: each costs 1 additional point of sustained Thread work rather than conventional Ob penalty." And from threadwork P-17 (Threadcut/Mending patch): "+Ob = being's Thread Sensitivity ÷ 20 (round up), maximum +4, to Mending operations targeting that Gap while the being is present."

For an operation targeting the being DIRECTLY: Ob = standard operation Ob + being's TS ÷ 20 (rounded up). Being's TS: estimated 80+ (it has been practicing Structural-scale Weaving for 500 years). Ob modifier: min(80÷20, 4) = 4. A Weave at Personal scale (Ob 2) becomes Ob 6 when targeting this being.

Roll: 18D TN7 vs Ob 6: expected net ~7.2. P(OW) ≈ 60%.

**[FINDING: A TS 50 practitioner can successfully Weave the being's configuration but faces Ob 6. At 18D, this is challenging but achievable. What would a successful Weave do? Per threadwork §6.3: external Thread operations cost the being +1 Rendering Strain. The practitioner targeting the being's self-maintenance is imposing a cost — the being must redouble its maintenance effort against the external influence. At Rendering Strain = Health (Health of a threadcut being = threadwork §6.2 implied: the being's capacity is its sustained Thread work duration), De-Actualisation begins. Practically: the practitioner could kill a threadcut being by repeatedly targeting its self-maintenance configuration until Rendering Strain equals its Health. This is an appalling act — equivalent to killing a creature that has survived by maintaining its own existence for 500 years. The system correctly makes this possible but does not make it easy, and the implications are catastrophic (the being's dissolution would produce a Major Gap as its configuration is released, RS −8 or worse).]**

**Combat with a threadcut being (if hostile):**

Standard combat rules apply to the being's physical interactions (it can wound, be wounded — wounds = +1 sustained Thread work per wound per threadwork §6.3). But:

- Weapons against threadcut beings at TS 0–29: target perceives "nothing or vague unease" — they cannot land weapons on something they cannot fully render.
- At TS 30–49: the being is "coherent figure but features approximate" — weapons can land but at Ob +2 (targeting something only partially rendered).
- At TS 50+: full combat available (being fully rendered to attacker).

**[FINDING: Combat with threadcut beings is TS-gated. Non-practitioners literally cannot fight them (TS 0–9: not perceivable). This is the correct mechanical expression of the setting's ontology: the beings are not invisible or magical — they are at a different layer of rendering that requires Thread Sensitivity to perceive clearly enough to interact with physically. A practitioner at TS 50 fights on equal terms; anyone below TS 30 cannot engage physically.]**

## ST-58 — TORBEN CONVICTION WINDOW FORMALIZATION (ED-618)

**Formal specification:**

**Torben Conviction Emergence Window: Seasons 1–8.**

Mechanical basis: Torben's political environment is Almud's Crown court. During Seasons 1–8, Almud is active (Arc A default — Reformer conditions not met, Fortress not triggered). Torben is exposed to his father's governance, to visiting factions (Crown Treaty attempts, Church Seizures visible to court), and to any player who invests in him through Senator actions.

**Why Season 8 specifically:**

Season 8 is the threshold for several converging events:
- Almud's Certainty can decline to 1 through player action by Season 6–8 (requiring 2–3 Decisive Contest victories). If Almud enters Arc A (Reformer) before Season 8: Torben's environment becomes Thread-tolerant, exposing him to broader Conviction sources faster.
- Season 8 corresponds to VTM 2 (Varfell) — Varfell's first opportunity to reach Torben with Evidence arguments (Cultural Reformation producing cultural shift). 
- Season 8 is before the Löwenritter Coup Counter typically reaches 3 (Seasons 13–16 in most games) — Torben needs a Conviction before the coup to act as a proper successor.

**After Season 8: Primary Conviction locked.**

The first faction to reach Disposition ≥ +2 with Torben before Season 8 sets his primary Conviction. After Season 8:
- Torben's primary Conviction is fixed (cannot be changed through further Disposition investment or Contest victories).
- Secondary Conviction: added through Scars (1–2 Scars from decisive RS targeting can activate secondary, but secondary cannot become primary retroactively).
- New Convictions gained after Season 8: all classified as Secondary (maximum 2 active simultaneously).

**Unopposed Torben (no faction invests):**

If no faction reaches Disposition ≥ +2 with Torben before Season 8: Torben's primary Conviction defaults to Crown's institutional baseline (Order). He becomes a competent institutionalist — predictable, loyal to the faction with current governance authority, unlikely to generate surprising decisions. This is the "low-investment" outcome: Torben becomes the Crown's instrument rather than a character with agency.

**Multi-faction investment edge case:**

If two factions reach Disposition ≥ +2 with Torben simultaneously (same Accounting): tie-break by: (1) higher net Disposition (e.g., Disposition +3 beats Disposition +2), (2) if exactly equal: the faction whose first investment action was chronologically earliest (track via session log, not in-game season). ### RS Rupture Last Declaration (ED-630)

**Full scene specification:**

When RS = 0 at Accounting, the "Rupture Scene" fires before any faction victory/loss resolution.

**Scene structure (adds to victory_v30 §5 Shared Loss Conditions):**

> **The Rupture Scene (mandatory, fires before Shared Loss is declared):**
>
> When RS reaches 0 at Accounting, all campaign activity pauses. The following occurs before the Shared Loss declaration:
>
> **Step 1 — World state narration:** The GM describes what RS 0 feels like in the territory the player currently occupies. This should be sensory and immediate, not abstract. Example: "The market square is the same market square it was forty years ago simultaneously. The garrison's horses are standing in three different positions, all equally present. The cathedral bells ring, but no one is pulling the rope, and the sound is wrong — it arrives before the bell moves." The narration takes 2–3 minutes. Players listen.
>
> **Step 2 — The Last Declaration (all PCs, in order of in-game Disposition with the GM's chosen witness NPC):**
>
> Each PC makes a Last Declaration. Procedure:
> 1. The player names their PC's primary Belief (as currently written — the most recent version, after all Scar revisions).
> 2. The player states one specific action their PC has taken over the campaign that most clearly expressed that Belief.
> 3. The player states whether, given everything, they would do that action again.
>
> The Last Declaration is not a roll. It is not mechanically adjudicated. It is the campaign's emotional ledger — the sum of what the player chose to value and whether the campaign's events changed that valuation.
>
> **Step 3 — GM narration through each Belief:**
>
> The GM briefly (1–2 sentences) narrates what the Rupture looks like from each PC's stated Belief lens. The narration should acknowledge the PC's primary action without judgment — it happened, it was real, it had weight.
>
> **Step 4 — Shared Loss declared.**
>
> Only now does the game formally acknowledge the Shared Loss. All faction victory conditions are null. Any incomplete faction arcs are summarised in 1–2 sentences by each player. The Rupture is not a game-over screen — it is the campaign's last act.

## SCENE 1 — "The Ledger"
### *Crown archive, late evening. Halvard + Elan (Elan is following Halvard; Halvard doesn't know).*

**Dramatic question:** Halvard attempts to access a sealed administrative archive. He believes it contains the transaction record linking Torben to the RS actor. He has access rights — but he rolls anyway (non-optimal: he should just walk in; instead he hesitates and draws attention).

#### Action 1a — Halvard: Administrative Access Roll (non-optimal — unnecessary)

Halvard has legitimate access. This roll should not happen. He rolls because his Belief 2 ("too old to survive a wrong move") makes him double-check everything.

Pool: Crown Administrative Clerk History (13D) | TN 7 | Ob 1 (routine access)

E[net]: 13×0.30 = 3.9 | P(Overwhelming): ~83%

**Outcome: Overwhelming.** He finds more than the Ledger — a second sealed document, not in the system, marked with the Church's Archive division seal. Cross-filing between Crown and Church archives is impossible under normal procedure. Someone placed this here deliberately.

**Non-optimal consequence:** By rolling when he shouldn't, Halvard triggered a timestamp on the archive access system. The Church archival department will see this access in their morning reconciliation.

**State delta:** Halvard finds: (1) Ledger linking a Torben administrative signature to a large withdrawal, destination coded as "southern survey." (2) Church Archive seal on a document that should not be here. Belief 1 advanced. CP +1. Archive access timestamp logged — Elan (following, has Church access) will see this in the morning.

---

#### Action 1b — Elan: Observation (doesn't intervene — non-optimal)

Elan is outside the archive. He sees Halvard enter. He has the Church keys that would let him enter and observe directly. He doesn't use them — his Belief 3 ("I will not act on this belief because acting would mean choosing a side") prevents him.

Instead: Attunement roll to read the situation through the door.
Pool: Att(4), TN 7, Ob 2 (observe through closed archive door)

E[net]: 4×0.30 = 1.2 | P(≥2): ~45% → **Partial** (most likely)

**Partial outcome:** Elan knows Halvard was in the archive. He cannot see what Halvard found. He notes the time. He does not enter.

**State delta:** Elan CP +1 (Belief 3 pursued via active restraint — counts as engagement with the Belief). He now has partial intelligence: Halvard accessed something tonight.

## SCENE 3 — "The Unanswered Question"
### *Church Tribunal anteroom. Elan + Halvard (coincidental encounter). Lotte outside.*

**Dramatic question:** Elan's investigation has led him to the same Church-Crown cross-filing Halvard found. They are about to compare notes — except Halvard doesn't know Elan is investigating, and Elan doesn't know Halvard found the document.

**This scene tests: Contest (renamed from Debate), Composure track (PP-282), non-optimal conflict between potential allies.**

#### Action 3a — Elan: Initiate a Church Tribunal proceeding against... himself

Non-optimal: Elan opens a formal investigation request on "an unnamed practitioner operating in Thread-active territories without Church authorisation." He intends this to draw out the actual actor — but the description fits him. He has unreported TS 35 and has been in Thread-active areas.

**This is a formal proceeding (Church Tribunal). Applying ED-009 resolution (PP-259): Parliamentary Inquiry rules apply.**

Elan's Tribunal roll (initiating): Cog(4)×2 + Inquisitor History(7) = **15D** | TN 7 | Ob 2 (routine Tribunal initiation).

E[net]: 4.5 | P(Overwhelming): ~70% → **Overwhelming**. The Tribunal accepts the investigation with unusual speed. Elan has accidentally triggered an existing open file. The Church already has a dossier on this practitioner — and it is labelled with Halvard's name.

**State delta:** Elan now knows the Church has a file on Halvard. He did not intend this. His Belief 1 ("I will follow it wherever it leads, even if it leads to Halvard") triggers — but he has not yet met Halvard in this scene.

---

#### Action 3b — Encounter: Halvard and Elan in the anteroom

They meet by chance. Halvard recognises the Church seal on Elan's Tribunal papers. Non-optimal: Halvard does not stay silent. He speaks first — because silence terrifies him more than speaking does.

**Contest (formerly Debate) — Halvard's inadvertent revelation:**

Halvard: Cog(5)×2 + Administrative History(7) = **17D** | TN 7 (argues in administrative terms — Crown jurisdiction)
Elan: Cog(4)×2 + Inquisitor History(7) = **15D** | TN 7 (argues in theological terms — Church authority)

Audience: the Tribunal clerk (Stability 3 → resistance 3; Past-boosted for Church context).

**Appraise:** Both roll Att. Halvard Att(3) 3D Ob1 → P(pass): ~74%; Elan Att(4) 4D → P(pass): ~87%.
Both pass. Halvard reads Elan as **Past/Obscuring** (Church doctrine concealment). Elan reads Halvard as **Present/Revealing** (he's about to say something he shouldn't).

**Halvard's choice:** Present/Revealing. He's going to tell Elan about the sealed document. Non-optimal.
**Elan's choice:** Past/Obscuring. He wants to slow this down.

**CLASH** (same audience, opposite orientations, mixed genre — actually CROSS since genres differ).

Wait — Halvard chose Present, Elan chose Past. Different genres → **CROSS** interaction (PP-245: simultaneous resolution).

Halvard Present/Revealing: pool 17D. E[net] 5.1. Genre weight (Present; Crown/Virtue Ethics audience — Tribunal clerk is Crown-appointed): 1.5. Orientation (Revealing): 1.0. Movement if Halvard wins by margin M: ⌊(M×1.5)−3⌋.

Elan Past/Obscuring: pool 15D. E[net] 4.5. Genre weight (Past; Church context, Past boosted for Church frame +0.5 = 1.0 total? No — audience is a Crown-appointed clerk; Crown→Present boosted. Past weight: 0.5 base). Orientation (Obscuring): 0.5. Movement if Elan wins by margin M: ⌊(M×0.5×0.5)−3⌋.

**CROSS resolution (simultaneous):**

Halvard E[net]: 5.1. Elan E[net]: 4.5. Both evaluated against resistance 3.

Halvard movement: ⌊(5.1×1.5)−3⌋ = ⌊7.65−3⌋ = ⌊4.65⌋ = **4** (toward Halvard; Conviction Track starts at 5, moves to 9 — Halvard wins).

Elan counter-movement: ⌊(4.5×0.25)−3⌋ = ⌊1.125−3⌋ = ⌊−1.875⌋ = **0** (Elan's orientation/genre weight too weak against this audience).

**Net: track moves 4 toward Halvard.** Elan cannot stop the revelation. Track position: 5→9 (Halvard's side wins; threshold ≥7).

**Halvard tells Elan about the sealed Church document.** By accident. In a Church Tribunal anteroom. In front of a clerk.

**Composure check for Elan** (PP-282): revelation of Church document cross-filing is psychologically destabilising for a doubting Inquisitor. Starting Composure: Bon(3)+Foc(5)+3 = 11, capped at 10. Rattled threshold: 5. Current: 10. 

No Composure damage from this scene — it is disturbing but not mechanically destabilising at Composure 10.

## FULL AUDIT — SIM-COMP-01 SYSTEMS

### Audit-C-01: CROSS Interaction Weight Asymmetry
**Finding F-C06 confirmed.** Present/Revealing (weight 1.5×1.0=1.5) vs Past/Obscuring (0.5×0.5=0.25) against resistance 3: the high-weight orientation wins by an enormous expected margin. CROSS is not a symmetric interaction — genre and orientation choice dominate outcome far more than pool size. A 17D orator using low-weight orientation will still produce near-zero movement against resistance 3.

**Implication:** Skilled debaters should not just maximise pool — they must read genre/orientation weights before choosing. The system rewards genre intelligence (Appraise step) over brute-force pool size.

**Audit verdict: design confirmed. No patch needed.**

### Audit-C-02: Crown Structural Intelligence Deficit
Crown cannot reliably investigate Niflhel (4D Ob 5: < 1% success). Even with Lowenritter delegation (4D Intel vs Ob 5 = 4D same pool, same result): ~3% success. Crown is structurally blind to Niflhel.

**Finding F-C12:** The Crown's intelligence deficit is mechanically absolute at current stats. The only viable Crown intelligence pathway is: (a) domain-level Lowenritter delegation, (b) PC-level investigation using personal skills (bypassing faction stat), or (c) Circles roll to cultivate a Niflhel defector. Option (c) is the mechanically correct answer — and is not represented in the Domain Action system.

**Patch PP-301 (provisional):** Add "Defector Cultivation" as a special Domain Action available to Crown and Varfell only. Pool: Influence. Ob = target faction Intel + 2. Success: gain one data point about target faction per season (not full stat reveal). Overwhelming: gain a permanent +1D on all future intelligence actions against that faction.
[FLAGGED FOR REVIEW: ED-171]

### Audit-C-03: Church TC Advance Rate vs Resistance
Church TC advance per season: expected +1 at Ob 7 (Parliament Integrity 7). P(≥7 net from 11D): ~5%. In most seasons, Church TC does not advance from this action. TC has been advancing because of threshold events and PC inaction, not from domain action success.

**Finding F-C13:** Church primary win path (TC to 80) requires ~32 seasons of TC advancement at current rate, or threshold events that cascade. This is consistent with ED-110's finding that Church primary victory is inaccessible in standard campaign. PP-278 (ED-110 resolution) added a fallback: Ecclesiastical Mandate at TC 70 + 2 territories. This is the realistic win path.

**Audit verdict: PP-278 correctly addresses the structural problem. No additional patch needed.**

### Audit-C-04: Non-Optimal Actor CP Economy
All four PCs earned CP despite making suboptimal choices:
- Halvard: earned CP by speaking when he shouldn't (Belief 1 pursued badly = still pursued)
- Signe: earned CP from Belief being challenged, not pursued (system correctly distinguishes)
- Elan: earned CP from restraint-as-engagement and mid-scene Belief trigger
- Lotte: earned the most CP (4 total) because her non-optimal escalation kept triggering Belief events

**Finding F-C14:** Non-optimal play earns CP at the same rate as optimal play — sometimes more. Lotte's aggressive escalations triggered more Belief events than careful play would have. The CP system does not reward optimality; it rewards narrative engagement. This is correct design.

### Audit-C-05: Document-as-Target in Assassination Mode
Niflhel's Quiet failed to destroy Signe's report because the report was not in a transmitted location. No rule exists for "assassination of a document/intelligence asset."

**GAP-C-01 formalised by PP-302 (provisional):**
Intelligence Asset Neutralisation (Niflhel/Varfell Quiet extension):
- If asset location is known: Intel vs target faction Intel +2 (standard assassination mode Ob). Success: asset destroyed or corrupted.
- If asset location is unknown: Intel vs target faction Intel +4. Partial: asset location revealed (can be targeted next season). Failure: no effect.
[FLAGGED FOR REVIEW: ED-172]

### Audit-C-06: Shifting Object Formation in Fragile Band
RS 58 triggered one Shifting Object per Fragile band rule. Location is GM-determined (highest-traffic Thread territory). No rule specifies what a Shifting Object does mechanically beyond its formation.

**Finding F-C15:** Shifting Object rules are incomplete. The Fragile band says they form; params_threadwork describes them in passing (objects with incomplete actualisation). No encounter rules, no mechanical threat profile.

**PP-303 (provisional):** Shifting Object encounter rule:
- Practitioners within 50m must make a Coherence check (TN 7, Ob 1) or lose −1 Coherence (the incomplete configuration disrupts intentionality).
- Non-practitioners: Certainty −1 (PP-283) if they spend extended time (1+ hour) near the object.
- The object can be Mended (Ob 2 Micro-Gap equivalent) or Dissolved (Ob 3; Dissolution Ob applies).
[FLAGGED FOR REVIEW: ED-173]

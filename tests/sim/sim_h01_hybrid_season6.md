- Torben: Att(5), TN 7, Ob 1 → pool 5D → **P(≥1): ~95%**. Overwhelming: reads Mira's genre as Present, orientation Revealing. He knows she'll present evidence, not precedent.

**Exchange 1, Step 2 — Choose:**

**Exchange 1, Step 3 — Argue (CLASH: same topic, opposite orientation):**

**Torben E[net]:** 20 × 0.30 = **6.0**

**CLASH resolution:** Margin = Torben net − Mira net = expected **0.3** (essentially even). Variance means either could win.

*Crown faction ethical mode: Virtue Ethics → Present genre boosted (+0.5). Mira chose Present. Genre weight: 1.5. Torben chose Past: genre weight 0.5 (audience is Crown/mixed; Varfell audience would boost Future; no single faction boosted Past in this room).*

*Orientation weights (Revealing vs Obscuring): Revealing +1.0, Obscuring +0.5 (standard — audience wants information).*

**Movement formula — if Mira wins by margin M:**
⌊(M × 1.5 × 1.0) − resistance⌋ toward Mira
Resistance = avg Stability of represented factions = avg(Crown 3, Guilds 4) = **3.5 → 4** (round up)

*For Mira to move the track by 1: needs M × 1.5 > 4 → M > 2.7 → margin of 3.*

**If Torben wins by margin M:**
⌊(M × 0.5 × 0.5) − resistance⌋ = (M × 0.25) − 4 → extremely unlikely to move track. Torben would need margin 16+ to push track by 1. Effectively: Torben cannot win a track movement by picking Past/Obscuring against a Present/Revealing opener with Crown audience.

**Simulation result:** Most likely, Exchange 1 is a CLASH with near-zero margin. Conviction track stays at 5 — no movement. Both sides recognise the stalemate.

## THE LOHENGRAMM PRINCIPLE

Reinhard von Lohengramm's defining characteristic is not genius. It is the refusal to work on a single front. Every campaign he runs is simultaneously a military operation, an intelligence extraction, a political consolidation, and a positioning move for the campaign after this one. He wins not by being the strongest actor in the room but by being the only actor who has already won three games while others are still playing one.

Applied to Vaynard: Thread research is one weapon. Cultural Reformation is a second. Military expansion is a third. Intelligence leverage is a fourth. Warden Relations is a fifth. He does not choose between these. He calibrates their simultaneity so that every action advances multiple conditions at once — and so that the loss of any single track does not slow him, because three others are already compensating.

The prior simulation's Vaynard waited. He discovered things. He was buffeted by events. This Vaynard creates events.

## NPC 02 — KING ALMUD ALMQVIST (Crown)

*The prior NPC-as-player sim captured Almud correctly as an empire-builder. This simulation goes deeper into the inside experience — what it feels like to govern Valoria at Mandate 5 while knowing, privately, that the institution you are defending may be built on a false foundation.*

### The Interior of Order

Almud at Mandate 5 is not anxious. Anxiety belongs to people who are not certain. He is certain — in public. His Certainty 3 (Questioning) means that certainty is a posture he maintains, not a state he inhabits. Every morning he performs the ritual of conviction. Every evening, in the privacy that high office affords, he sits with the thing he privately knows: Thread reality is probably real. His Order conviction and his Reason secondary are not in tension at the level of daily action — they are in tension at the level of daily identity.

**The Virtue Ethics constraint as lived experience:** Every covert action carries +1 Ob for Almud. This means that the King of Valoria cannot act on intelligence without paying a cost. He cannot use Schattendienst findings against a rival without the action being harder than if he had just declared it openly. His framework rewards visibility and punishes expedience. He has built an institutional system — the Ministry of Crown — that operates by different rules than he does personally. The Schattendienst operates covertly. Almud authorizes it but does not personally direct its covert operations. This is his ethical architecture: he governs an intelligence apparatus whose methods he cannot personally use without cost, but whose findings he can read and act on indirectly.

## NPC 03 — DUCHESS INGE BARALTA (Hafenmark)

*The prior sims understood Baralta's ambition. This simulation focuses on the inside experience of the Categorical Imperative — how someone who believes absolutely in constitutional procedure experiences the moments when procedure fails, and what she does about it.*

### Precedent as Weapon, Faith as Core

The misread of Baralta is treating Precedent as a limitation. It is not. Precedent is the weapon she has spent decades sharpening. Constitutional procedure, applied with institutional force, can do things that military aggression cannot: it can strip legitimacy from rulers without firing a shot, create legally-binding claims on territories whose military defenders have no institutional response to juridical authority, and turn the mechanisms of governance against the people who thought they controlled them.

Her Faith secondary is equally misread. It is not a soft spot — it is the source of her certainty that she is right. ## NPC 04 — GRANDMASTER LISBETH EHRENWALL (Löwenritter)

*This simulation focuses on the most ethically complex season-by-season experience in the game: deciding, each season, whether Crown's latest failure is the one that justifies action.*

### The Ledger

Ehrenwall keeps a ledger. Not written — internalized. Each season she evaluates:

> *Has the Crown, this season, failed Valoria? Not Crown the institution, not Almud the man, not the Treaty network or the Parliamentary standing or the TCV position. Has Valoria — the peninsula, the people, the future — been failed by the decisions made in Valorsplatz this season?*

The Coup Counter is not a mechanical abstraction for Ehrenwall. It is a moral account. She has accumulated two grievances. She has stayed her hand because: Almud was weak but not destructive. The Coup fires when he becomes destructive — when his weakness is itself causing harm.

**This is the distinction the prior sim collapsed.** A player running Ehrenwall who advances the Counter opportunistically (because conditions favor the Coup) is not playing Ehrenwall. They are playing a character with Ehrenwall's resources and a different Conviction. Ehrenwall advances the Counter when she judges — by her own ethical standard, not a mechanical threshold — that Crown has genuinely failed Valoria. The Counter is the score; the judgment is hers.

**Year-arc 1 (S1–4):** Crown Stability 5, Mandate 5. TC 30. Almud is governing well by any observable measure. The Schattendienst reports are clean. ## NPC 05 — CARDINAL JUSTICE (Church Inquisitor)

*The hardline who believes that the Church's weakness comes from within. His game is the most internally corrupt campaign in the system — he is building an inquisitorial empire within an institution that would dissolve if it understood what he was doing.*

**Context:** Church Stability ≤ 2 (crisis, from Himlensendt's arc deterioration or from player-driven Evidence attacks). Cardinal Justice is now operating as an independent actor. His agenda: not to save the Church but to purify it — and in purifying it, to become its dominant figure.

**Justice's Stance Triangle:**
- Primary Conviction: Faith (hardline). Solmund's word is absolute. Any deviation is heresy. Any wavering is weakness.
- Ethical Framework: Divine Command. Aligned: direct theological enforcement action (−1 Ob). Contradictory: procedure-over-doctrine (+1 Ob).
- Resonant Style: Authority (Holy See directives). He responds to power above him and wields power below him. He does not negotiate; he pronounces.

**The key distinction from Himlensendt:** Himlensendt believed. Justice enforces. He uses Faith as a political instrument. He has observed that doctrinal certainty, when projected with sufficient institutional force, is indistinguishable from actual faith — and the distinction doesn't matter to the populations he governs. ## CROSS-SIMULATION FINDINGS

### FINDING CROSS-01: The Reinhard Principle Generalizes

Vaynard/Reinhard's operating principle — run multiple paths simultaneously, use every action to advance more than one condition, pre-empt threats before they materialize — is the optimal strategy for every player character in this system, not just Vaynard.

- **Almud** running simultaneously: Treaty construction, TCV expansion via March, Torben succession acceleration, Intelligence management through Schattendienst. He does not do these sequentially — they run in parallel because they use different card types and Scene-layer actions.
- **Baralta** running simultaneously: TCV Proclamation, Crown Mandate suppression via Grand Contest, Church coercion via Sovereign Authority Doctrine, Military buildup for alternate path. Same reason.
- **Ehrenwall** running simultaneously: Coup Counter moral ledger, Torben relationship building, pre-coup military positioning, Riskbreaker intelligence gathering about Almud's actual decisions.

The faction that runs the most simultaneous tracks wins not because each individual track is stronger but because the opponent cannot respond to all of them. Almud suppressing one of Vaynard's fronts leaves two others advancing. Baralta's Proclamation cascade overwhelms Crown's response capacity. The Reinhard Principle is not a Vaynard-specific tactic — it is the fundamental strategic principle of the game.

### FINDING CROSS-02: Conviction as Strategic Constraint, Not Limitation

Every NPC-as-player simulation reveals the same thing: Conviction is the most important variable. Not because it limits options — because it determines which actions are free and which cost extra. Vaynard's Consequentialist Framework makes measurable-outcome actions −1 Ob. He designs every action to have a measurable outcome within one season. Baralta's Categorical Imperative makes constitutional actions −1 Ob. She designs every action to be defensible as constitutional. Ehrenwall's Martial Honour makes Valoria-sovereign-protecting actions −1 Ob. She designs every action to serve that framing.

The player who understands their NPC's Conviction structure has a permanent Ob reduction on the majority of their actions. The player who ignores it pays an Ob tax on every deviation. The Conviction is not a character detail — it is an economy.

### FINDING CROSS-03: The World-Player Linkage Intensifies Under Simultaneity

Playing NPCs who run multiple simultaneous tracks creates a uniquely dense world↔player feedback loop. Every action Vaynard takes (Cultural Reformation on T6) triggers a mandatory response from Crown (Institutional Mandate trigger), which reveals Crown's card allocation priorities, which Vaynard reads as intelligence, which updates his model of when Crown will be resource-constrained, which he uses to time his next Reformation. The world is not background — it is a continuous opponent-modeling problem.

Almud's Treaty construction with Guilds in S4 changes Guild's Stability condition (Mandate −1 from OW Treaty), which changes the conditions under which Church will try to recruit Guild-territory merchants, which changes Church's AP accumulation pattern in Guild territories, which changes when Vaynard's VTM Discretion needs to fire. These chains run 3–4 steps from the original action. A player running an NPC-as-player simulation at max effort is always modeling 3 steps ahead.

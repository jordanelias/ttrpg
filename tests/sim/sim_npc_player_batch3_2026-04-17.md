## NPC 01 — KOLBRUN THALE (Crown Spymaster / Schattendienst Minister)

### Who She Is

Kolbrun Thale is the most dangerous person in the Crown inner circle, and the only person in it that Almud does not fully understand. Her background is deliberately obscured. Her Southern Einhir heritage is rumored but unconfirmed. She is the sole inner circle member with Niflhel contacts. Her Conviction is Autonomy; her Resonant Style is Consequence.

She does not serve Almud because she believes in Order. She serves Almud because, at this moment in Valoria's history, Crown is the best available vehicle for the outcomes she wants — which are not identical to the outcomes Almud wants. The distinction is not yet visible. When it becomes visible, the game becomes very interesting.

**Canonical stats:**
- Standing 6 (Schattendienst Minister = Crown Standing 6 via Administrative/Intelligence branch)
- Conviction: Autonomy | RS: Consequence | Certainty: 3
- Niflhel contacts: Dockworkers (Standing 2 equivalent, Dockmaster-adjacent in Rendstad waterfront)
- Ethical Framework: Consequentialist pragmatism (not Crown's Virtue Ethics — she operates under her own personal framework, masked by institutional identity)

### The Spymaster's Season Structure

**Schattendienst Competence (MIN-01/02 equivalent for Intelligence Ministry):**
- At Competence 3: player's subterfuge actions get one free Exposure reduction per season; early warning on rival claim-building
- At Competence 2: standard Exposure reporting
- At Competence 1: Exposure events not flagged until Attention Pool = 3

Thale starts at Competence 2 (the Ministry was functional before she arrived, not excellent). Her first 4 seasons are about raising Competence to 3: audit (Investigation, 1 season), reform (Duty assignment to subordinate Officers, 2 seasons), stabilization.

**What she does with Competence 3:** The early-warning system on rival Claim-building becomes the most powerful tool in Crown's arsenal. When Baralta begins Grand Contest preparation (S11 in standard timeline), Thale knows 1 season in advance. When Vaynard's Tribune Investigate reveals Crown's stats (S1 in Vaynard's timeline), Thale detects the intelligence-gathering (though not the specific finding). The player operating Thale is the person who makes Almud's reactive decisions accurate rather than approximate.

**The Niflhel contact management:**
Her Dockmaster-adjacent contact in Rendstad (T7) gives her access to information that the Schattendienst cannot formally acquire — specifically: Niflhel operational activities in Crown territories, which are otherwise invisible to Crown intelligence until they become Exposure events. This is a structural advantage: Thale sees Niflhel before Niflhel becomes a crisis.

**Cost:** Maintaining this contact requires Thale to make regular payments (not Wealth — information. She trades Crown intelligence to Niflhel, which means Niflhel knows things about Crown that they should not). This is the Autonomy secondary tension embedded in her role: she is protecting Crown by giving away pieces of Crown to do it.

### Phase 1 (S1–8): Building the Apparatus

**S1–4 Schattendienst reform:**
Thale inherits an apparatus with Officers of uneven loyalty and competence. Her first investigation: which Officers are reporting to multiple masters? The Schattendienst has been in place long enough that some Officers have developed relationships with Niflhel (unofficial), with Church (via Father Gustav Linder's observation network), and with Hafenmark (Baralta's Legal Advisor maintains academic contacts inside Crown institutions).

Scene arc: 3-scene Investigation targeting her own Ministry's personnel. Ob 2 each scene (her own people are not trying to hide from her — she is the boss — but the information is institutionally compartmented). Findings:
- 1 Officer has an active Niflhel runner relationship (information conduit to Dockmaster Rendstad)
- 1 Officer has been receiving Church "pastoral visits" that are suspiciously intelligence-shaped
- 1 Officer is competent, clean, and does not know about the other two

**Decision:** She fires neither. She redirects both. The Niflhel-connected Officer becomes her managed asset — she knows about the relationship, so she can feed curated intelligence through it. The Church-adjacent Officer becomes her forward-sensor for Father Gustav Linder's activities. She has turned two compromised Officers into controlled conduits. Schattendienst Competence rises to 3 by S4.

**[FINDING KOLT-SIM-01: The Spymaster's game is not about building a clean intelligence apparatus. It is about converting every compromised element into a controlled one. Thale operates by accepting that information flows in multiple directions and choosing which directions to curate rather than which to sever. A Spymaster who severs compromised relationships loses the intelligence those relationships were leaking. A Spymaster who controls them doubles her intelligence yield. This is historically accurate to every effective intelligence operation: the double-agent is more valuable than the loyal agent because they also carry the adversary's confidence.]**

**S5 — Father Gustav Linder's weekly report:**
Father Linder (Archbishop's Representative, Crown inner circle) delivers his weekly Church briefing to Almud. Standard procedure. Thale has read the briefing first. She notes: the briefing omits something. Himlensendt's recent internal deliberations (which her Church-adjacent Officer reported via the pastoral visit channel) show Himlensendt making a TC assessment that is more optimistic than his official position. He privately believes TC will reach 50 faster than he is publicly projecting.

What Thale has is the gap between Himlensendt's private assessment and his public position. This gap is intelligence about Church's strategic pacing. She does not give this to Almud directly (Almud would need to act on it, which would reveal the source). She instead adjusts the Schattendienst's Tribune-equivalent monitoring of Church-territory PT levels, looking for the pattern that would confirm Himlensendt's private timeline. She is cross-referencing to verify before presenting.

**[FINDING KOLT-SIM-02: The Spymaster's most important skill is withholding intelligence until it's verified. Presenting unverified intelligence to a faction leader generates action on bad data. Verified intelligence generates correctly-timed action. Thale's 1-season verification delay between receiving raw intelligence and presenting confirmed intelligence to Almud is not timidity — it is the difference between intelligence and rumor. Mechanically: this means Thale operates 1 season behind her actual intelligence horizon, but the intelligence she presents is actionable rather than speculative.]**

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Dual Service

**S9 — Vaynard's intelligence package surfaces:**
Vaynard has been selling Crown intelligence to Hafenmark since S3. Thale's early-warning system (Competence 3 active by S4) has flagged unusual information patterns in Hafenmark's Parliamentary motions — they are too precisely targeted at Crown's vulnerabilities to be coincidental. Someone is providing Hafenmark with Crown internal data.

She traces it to Vaynard's Tribune network (the pattern of information matches what Tribune Investigate reveals). But she cannot go to Almud with "Varfell is spying on Crown" without Almud demanding diplomatic action that would burn the intelligence advantage she is building on top of the Varfell-leak situation (she has been using her knowledge of the leak to pre-position Crown's public data — controlling what Vaynard's Tribune finds by ensuring certain information is visible while other information is hidden).

**The Autonomy move:** She contacts Vaynard. Not through official channels. Through her Niflhel contact (the Dockmaster in Rendstad, who has a secondary relationship with a Varfell trade agent). The message: "I know what you're finding. And I know what you're not finding. Let's talk about that gap." This is Thale operating entirely outside Crown's knowledge, in pursuit of an outcome (controlled intelligence environment for Crown) that serves Crown's interests through means that would be career-ending if discovered.

**What she gets from Vaynard:** The same deal Vaynard offered in the prior simulation — mutual intelligence management. She controls what Crown's visible data shows; he manages what he sells to Hafenmark. Both get a predictable intelligence environment. Almud gets accurate intelligence without knowing why it's accurate.

**[FINDING KOLT-SIM-03: Thale's intelligence diplomacy with Vaynard is the most operationally sophisticated action in any simulation. She is running an unsanctioned bilateral intelligence agreement on behalf of a principal who does not know the agreement exists. Her Autonomy Conviction makes this coherent to her: the outcome (Crown intelligence stability) justifies the means (unauthorized contact with a rival faction's leader). But this is also the action that, if revealed, ends her career and potentially compromises Crown's institutional credibility. She is building Crown's intelligence advantage on a foundation that contains a hidden structural risk. This is the Spymaster experience: every advantage carries a vulnerability you cannot tell your boss about.]**

**S14 — Theodor Kreutz's crisis:**
The Royal Guard Captain (Kreutz, Crown inner circle) has been tracking Coup Counter advancement. The Counter moves to 2 (by Thale's read: Almud's withholding of RS data was the trigger). Kreutz reports to Thale privately: "I'm watching both chains. If the Counter reaches 3, Ehrenwall acts through my chain first. She will reach me before she reaches Almud." He is warning her. He is also asking her to do something.

What he's asking: verify whether Ehrenwall has communicated with her Riskbreaker network about pre-coup positioning. This is something Thale's Niflhel contact can surface — the Riskbreakers occasionally use Niflhel transit infrastructure for mission support. If Riskbreaker operational patterns change, the Niflhel conduit will notice.

She checks. The Riskbreakers have not pre-positioned. Counter 2 is not yet a preparation signal. She tells Kreutz: "Not yet." And files the check in a Schattendienst record that does not exist in the official files.

### Phase 3 (S19–28): The Loyalty Question

**S22 — Counter 3, Coup preparation:**
Thale's Niflhel contact flags it: Riskbreaker operational pattern has changed. Two Riskbreaker squads (Rb-Std 3 Squad-Leaders) have been positioned in T1 Valorsplatz and T14 Ehrenfeld in the past 3 seasons. This is pre-positioning. The Coup is coming.

She has 1 season to decide. Not 2. 1 — because she knows before Almud does, and the advantage is only useful if acted on immediately.

**Her options:**
A. Warn Almud. He prepares. The Coup is telegraphed and potentially averted. But Thale's Niflhel source is revealed to Almud (how else would she know?), and the unauthorized Vaynard intelligence agreement is at risk of discovery.  
B. Warn Kreutz. He prepares the Royal Guard to resist. But Kreutz answers to both chains — and his personal allegiance, per faction_politics_expanded §1.1d, is to Almud personally. He will resist. But the fight in T1 will cost Crown Stability −2 regardless of who wins.  
C. Contact Ehrenwall. Negotiate the terms of transition. Not betraying Almud — negotiating the gentlest version of what is happening regardless of her choices.  
D. Do nothing. Let it happen. Preserve her position in whatever comes after.

**Autonomy Conviction resolution:** Expected-value calculation. Option C: the Coup happens, she survives, she retains her intelligence assets in the new Löwenritter administration (who will need a Schattendienst Minister regardless). Downside: Almud, whom she has protected for 22 seasons, is deposed. Her Consequence RS fires: what is the actual outcome if she chooses each option? Option A/B both have high variance (fight outcomes unclear). Option C has predictable outcome but requires acting against a man she has served. Option D is unacceptable — Autonomy Conviction means she always acts, never watches.

She chooses C. She contacts Ehrenwall privately. The negotiation: "Crown's intelligence apparatus transfers intact. Its officers are not replaced or interrogated. The Schattendienst reports to the new authority, not through me personally." She is negotiating the protection of her people in exchange for not resisting. This is not betrayal. This is a Spymaster ensuring institutional continuity across a regime transition. Every intelligence service in history has survived regime change by making exactly this deal.

**[FINDING KOLT-SIM-04: Thale's Season 22 decision is the defining moment of the Spymaster player experience. She has built a 22-season intelligence enterprise in service of a man who is about to be deposed. Her Autonomy Conviction means she calculates expected outcomes, not emotional loyalties. Option C is the correct Autonomy Consequence choice — it produces the most predictable outcome with the lowest expected harm. But the player who has invested in Thale for 22 seasons will feel the weight of this decision in a way that pure strategy cannot capture. This is the game asking: what does it mean to serve an institution rather than a person?]**

## NPC 02 — THEODOR KREUTZ (Captain of the Royal Guard / Löwenritter Liaison)

### The Impossible Position

Theodor Kreutz is the only person in Valoria with dual formal allegiance: he answers to Almud personally as Captain of the Royal Guard, and to Ehrenwall as a Knight-Commander in the Löwenritter chain. This duality is structural — it exists because the Crown needed a Löwenritter officer in the Royal Guard to signal institutional trust between the two power structures.

In a stable game, this duality is administrative friction. In an unstable game, it is an ethical catastrophe.

**Canonical stats:**
- Löwenritter Standing 3 (Knight-Commander) — per §2.1 ladder
- Crown Standing 5 equivalent (Captain of Royal Guard is inner-circle adjacent, near-Seneschal access)
- Conviction: Order | RS: Authority | Certainty: 4
- Pre-designated personal allegiance: Almud (per faction_politics_expanded §1.1d)
- Coup Counter linkage: Counter increments are "linked to his death or removal, not Ehrenwall's" — meaning Ehrenwall targets him to weaken Crown's ability to resist, not to fight him directly

### The Knight-Commander's Season Structure

**Dual Duty cycle:**
Every season, Kreutz has obligations to both chains:
- **Crown chain:** Garrison duty at T1 Valorsplatz (Royal Guard command). Quarterly audience with Almud. Report any threats to Crown Stability through official Crown channels.
- **Löwenritter chain:** Quarterly report to Ehrenwald (his direct superior in the Löwenritter Standing 3 chain). Attend Löwenritter military reviews when ordered. Execute Löwenritter garrison operations in coordination with Crown.

These duties do not normally conflict. The garrison at T1 IS Royal Guard AND it is Löwenritter-adjacent (T14 Ehrenfeld is the Löwenritter's primary installation, 1 territory away from T1). Kreutz coordinates both without friction in peaceful seasons.

**Where the game lives:** The Coup Counter. Every increment of the Coup Counter is an Ehrenwall judgment that Crown has failed. Kreutz receives every increment as a message from his Order chain before it becomes public. He knows the Counter is at 1 (S13) and 2 (S22) before Almud does. This advance knowledge is the mechanic that makes him interesting as a player character.

### Phase 1 (S1–10): The Watching Man

Kreutz's early seasons are garrison management and relationship investment. He knows that his value to both chains lies in being genuinely trustworthy to both — which requires that neither chain ever genuinely test his loyalty. His goal is preventing the test from occurring.

**S3 — Ehrenwall's informal evaluation:**
Ehrenwall visits T14 Ehrenfeld for a military review. She requests Kreutz attend (within his Löwenritter chain obligations). During the review, she makes an indirect comment: "The Valorsplatz garrison has been running long rotations. When was the last time you audited personnel loyalty?" 

This is not a casual observation. She is asking: do you know if your own Guard is reliable? Subtext: if I needed you to delay a response, could you?

Kreutz's response: he audits the Royal Guard (1 Investigation scene, Ob 1 for his own chain). He finds: the Guard is loyal to Crown. He reports this to Ehrenwall through the Löwenritter report channel. He simultaneously tells Almud there was "a routine audit" prompted by the military review. Both statements are true. Neither reveals the other's context.

**[FINDING KREU-SIM-01: Kreutz's institutional survival depends on making both truths available without making the context visible. He is not lying to either chain — he is compartmenting. The ethical distinction matters: compartmenting is withholding context, not asserting falsehood. This distinction will not survive the moment when the two chains' demands become genuinely incompatible. His Certainty 4 supports this compartmenting: he is confident that the incompatibility will not arrive. He is wrong, but not yet.]**

**S7 — Riskbreaker recruitment offer:**
At Knight-Commander (Lr-Std 3), Kreutz becomes eligible for Riskbreaker recruitment. The offer arrives through a sealed message left in his Ehrenfeld quarters — not signed, but the format is unmistakably Riskbreaker (a covert communication protocol he learned in training). Rb-Std 0: Candidate. One covert operation required for Initiation.

He declines. Not because he opposes covert operations — because accepting would add a third chain of authority and a third set of obligations. He is already managing two. Three would be unmanageable. He sends a wordless reply through the same protocol: the message form returned unopened.

The Riskbreaker Commander notes the refusal. This is significant: a Knight-Commander who declines the Riskbreaker track is either already committed elsewhere or is signaling that he prioritizes the clean chain. The Commander's assessment: Kreutz is a straight-line Crown loyalist. He can be used as a known quantity in Coup planning — they will not use him, but they can work around him because they know his response parameters.

**[FINDING KREU-SIM-02: Kreutz's Riskbreaker refusal is a strategic mistake that reveals his actual position clearly to the Riskbreaker network. By declining, he tells the Riskbreakers exactly what kind of obstacle he will be: predictable, rule-following, loyal to the formal chain. A cleverer move would have been to accept, spend one season as Rb-Std 0, and use the position to understand Riskbreaker planning. He chose clean conscience over intelligence advantage. This is his Order Conviction — he will not operate outside the formal chain, even to protect the chain. This is the character's defining limitation.]**

### Phase 2 (S11–20): The Counter Rises

**S13 — Counter 1:**
Ehrenwall's quarterly report to Kreutz (through Knight-Commander chain) contains an unusual element: a formal notation of "Leadership Deviation event, Category: Information Asymmetry." This is the Counter increment language. Almud withheld RS-decline information from Parliament. The Order has noted it.

Kreutz does not tell Almud about the Counter. He cannot — the Counter is a Löwenritter internal assessment, and reporting it through Crown channels would be a breach of the Order's chain confidentiality. But he now knows: the clock has started.

His response: he escalates his personal access to Almud. He requests a formal private audience (Availability: In Session at Valorsplatz). In the audience, he does not mention the Counter. He asks Almud about the RS situation — specifically, whether Almud is satisfied with the Kirchenamt's current monitoring posture. He is giving Almud the opportunity to demonstrate to his own Guard Captain that Crown is not concealing strategic information from its security apparatus.

Almud's answer is evasive. Kreutz notes it and leaves.

**S17 — Counter 2:**
Ehrenwall's notation arrives again. "Leadership Deviation event, Category: Institutional Integrity." Almud's Schattendienst operation against Baralta's Parliamentary motion (from the Thale simulation — she managed it, but the operation existed). The Counter advances.

Now Kreutz has a problem. Two increments in 4 seasons. One more triggers Coup preparation. He has approximately 2–6 seasons before the third increment, depending on Almud's future behavior.

He does something unprecedented: he requests a private audience with Ehrenwall directly. Not through the formal report channel — through a personal request via the Lions' Table messenger protocol. The meeting occurs in T14 Ehrenfeld.

His ask: "Tell me what would prevent the third increment." He is not asking her to stop the Coup — that is not within his authority. He is asking what behavior from Crown would reset the trajectory.

Her answer: "Nothing resets it. The record exists. But the third increment requires a genuine failure of Valoria — not of Crown. If the next event is about the peninsula rather than Crown's political interests, I will not invoke the Counter."

Kreutz returns to T1. He now knows exactly what Almud must not do to prevent the Coup: any action that prioritizes Crown's factional advantage over peninsula-wide welfare. The problem: Almud's entire strategic program (Treaty construction, TCV expansion, Torben succession engineering) is about Crown's advantage. Kreutz cannot tell Almud to stop being a King.

**[FINDING KREU-SIM-03: Kreutz's negotiation with Ehrenwall reveals the deepest structural tension in the game. The Coup Counter threshold ("a genuine failure of Valoria") is Ehrenwall's judgment, not a mechanical condition. Kreutz learns this and cannot transmit it to Almud, because doing so would reveal the Counter's existence and the Löwenritter chain's internal assessment. He has the information that could prevent the Coup and cannot use it. This is the Royal Guard's existential condition: they protect a king they cannot fully warn.]**

### Phase 3 (S21–28): The Third Increment

**S22 — Counter 3:**
It fires from Almud's Schattendienst action against Baralta (per Thale's simulation). Kreutz receives the notation and knows: preparation begins. He has at most 2 seasons before Coup execution.

**The choice Kreutz faces:** His pre-designated allegiance is to Almud personally. If Ehrenwall moves, he fights. But he has watched the Counter accumulate and knows — privately, through the Order channel — that Ehrenwall's assessments are not unjust. Almud has, in fact, failed the peninsula on the recorded occasions. His Order Conviction does not require him to protect an unjust king. It requires him to maintain order.

The question: is Almud's rule Order? Or is Ehrenwall's transition Order?

His resolution (Order Conviction, no Scars, Certainty 4): the succession of institutional authority IS order. If Ehrenwall can transfer power without civil war and with legitimate succession (Torben remaining as figurehead), the institution survives. The institution's survival is Order. Almud's personal survival is a subset of that question, not the whole of it.

He does not fight Ehrenwall. He manages the Royal Guard's response to ensure it is not violent — he stands them down in an orderly withdrawal, which allows the Coup to proceed without Stability −3 (avoiding civil war) and results in Stability −1 (Managed Transition per COUP-01). He has chosen Order over the person.

**Post-Coup:** Kreutz remains as Royal Guard Captain under Löwenritter governance. His institutional value is now explicit — he is the continuity officer who maintained Crown institutional structure through the transition. Torben's Loyalty to Kreutz rises (Kreutz protected Torben's position by preventing violent conflict at T1). Löwenritter keeps him. He serves the Regency.

## NPC 03 — ANNIKA FELDHAUS (Guild Comptroller / Hafenmark Inner Circle)

### The Dual-Track Character

Annika Feldhaus holds two institutional positions simultaneously: Senior Guild Master (Guild Ladder Gu-Std 4, heading toward Comptroller) and Hafenmark inner circle member as Guild liaison. This dual status is the canonical definition of the faction_politics_expanded §2.5 note: "A Guild member can hold dual rank (Master Merchant in Guilds — Part 2, §2.5)."

She is not torn between them. She has made the two roles mutually reinforcing through 15 years of deliberate positioning. Hafenmark gives her constitutional protection for Guild commercial interests. The Guilds give her the economic intelligence that makes her indispensable to Baralta's Parliamentary operations. She is the most efficient dual-institution actor in Valoria.

**Conviction:** Precedent (primary) | Consequence (RS secondary). Certainty 5.  
**Caste note:** "Commercially invested in caste maintenance" — meaning she benefits from Southern Einhir exclusion from Guild senior ranks, which reduces competition for the positions she and her network occupy. This is a structural corruption in her institution that she does not examine because it has never cost her anything.

### Guild Season Structure

**The Masterpiece Examination dynamic (Gu-Std 1→2):**
Feldhaus chairs the Free Master examination committees in Hafenmark's commercial territories. This means she is the gatekeeper for the Guild's most critical threshold — the point where caste exclusion becomes formally possible. The Masterpiece Examination is formally merit-based (craft quality assessed by peer committee). In practice: Southern Einhir candidates are assessed against a higher standard of "procedural correctness" in Guild law that few can navigate without established mentorship networks, which are not available to Einhir apprentices.

She does not do this consciously. She does it through the Precedent RS — she applies Guild precedent consistently, and Guild precedent was written by people who were not Einhir. The bias is baked into the procedure.

**[FINDING FELD-SIM-01: Feldhaus's Precedent Conviction is the mechanism by which structural injustice perpetuates itself through scrupulously fair application of unjust rules. She is not malicious. She is procedurally consistent. The game should ask the player: what do you do when your Conviction is mechanically correct and ethically compromised? A Feldhaus player who examines this will find that her most resonant arc is not political competition — it's the moment she must decide whether procedural correctness is the same as justice. This is the caste system's deepest gameplay element.]**

### Phase 1 (S1–8): Institutional Consolidation

**Feldhaus's primary early game:** Advancing Guild Competence in the Hafenmark commercial territories (T7 Rendstad, T10 Spartfell, T8 Gransol). Each territory has a Guild Forum; she chairs all three (Senior Guild Master authority). At Forum Competence 2 (per MIN-02 equivalent for Guild Dicasteries): Trade actions at −1 Ob in Forum-active territories.

**Parliamentary integration:** Feldhaus serves as Trade & Guild Relations Committee representative in Hafenmark Parliament. This is the Hafenmark Parliamentary Committee structure from §7.2. Her Committee role gives her one free Parliamentary motion/year-arc on trade matters without consuming a Domain Action slot (the Committee is a Scene-layer institutional body, not a card action). She uses this to:
- S3: Motion to formalize Guild arbitration rights in Hafenmark territorial disputes. Passed 3-1. Guild authority in territorial commercial matters is now constitutionally codified in Hafenmark law.
- S6: Motion to require foreign-faction commercial agents operating in Hafenmark to register with a Guild Forum. Passed 2-1 (Almstedt abstains — he is procedurally neutral on commercial regulation). Church's commercial representatives in T10 must now register. Church Attention Pool in T10 receives a retroactive constitutional barrier to Heresy Investigation deployment.

**[FINDING FELD-SIM-02: Feldhaus's Parliamentary Committee motions are the cheapest Domain Action equivalents in the game. Each motion costs nothing but a Scene-layer action, produces a legally binding constitutional outcome (per Hafenmark's Categorical Imperative framework), and generates Renown from the Ceremonial Scene context (+1 per won motion). Over 8 seasons: 2 motions × Renown +1 = Renown 2 accumulated purely from parliamentary committee work. No Domain Actions spent. This is the Guild Comptroller's most underappreciated capability: she generates binding law for free because she is already in the room.]**

### Phase 2 (S9–16): Baralta's Coalition Manager

**The inner circle role:** Feldhaus's position in Baralta's inner circle makes her the voice of the commercial sector in every major Hafenmark political decision. When Baralta initiates a Grand Contest against Crown (S11), she consults Feldhaus first: "Will Crown's response affect Guild trade?" Feldhaus's answer (Consequence RS, Competence 3 economic intelligence): "Crown's Royal Decree targeting our Mandate will create 2-season uncertainty in the T7 trade corridor. Compensate with a Commercial Continuity guarantee before the Contest fires."

Baralta files the guarantee (1 scene: Commercial Continuity Charter added to the Grand Contest agenda as a parallel motion). On Grand Contest decisive win: Hafenmark gains Mandate +1 AND the Charter becomes law AND Guild Favour in T7 rises +1 (from the certainty signal). Three outcomes from one Ceremonial Scene because Feldhaus anticipated the secondary effects and positioned for them.

**Church pressure on T10 (S13):**
Church Heresy Investigation targets T10 Spartfell (AP 3 from sustained theological questioning by Church-affiliated merchants). Feldhaus's Forum registration requirement (S6 motion) means the Church's commercial agents in T10 are in the Guild registry. When the Inquisitor deploys, Feldhaus invokes Guild Forum jurisdiction over the registered agents — a commercial protection that technically blocks Inquisitorial interrogation of Guild members during active trade disputes.

Church's response: Cardinal Olafsson (Justice) files a counter-motion in Parliament (through Church's Parliamentary presence). The Parliamentary vote: Baralta's coalition votes for Feldhaus's commercial protection. Church loses the motion 3-1. Church Mandate −1 (Parliamentary Censure). AER: no change (the Inquisitor was not deployed successfully, so no HI confirmed, so AER does not increase this season). Feldhaus has effectively blocked a Heresy Investigation through a commercial procedural mechanism.

**[FINDING FELD-SIM-03: Feldhaus demonstrates that the most powerful faction-defense tool is not military or political — it is procedural commercial law that has been pre-positioned before the crisis arrives. Her S6 Forum registration motion was filed 7 seasons before it was needed. When the crisis arrived, the defense mechanism was already institutionalized. A Feldhaus player who thinks 3+ seasons ahead can build procedural fortifications that opponents cannot overcome without violating laws they helped create. This is the Guild Comptroller as constitutional architect.]**

### Phase 3 (S17–28): The Caste Reckoning

**S18 — The Examination Crisis:**
A Southern Einhir Journeyman named (GM-assigned) applies for the Masterpiece Examination in T10 Spartfell. She has served 3 year-arcs, traveled to 4 territories, produced work that Feldhaus's own technical assessment rates as objectively superior. The committee, applying existing Guild precedent, fails her on a procedural technicality: her Journeymen Years were partially conducted in territories outside Hafenmark's formal Guild Forum jurisdiction, and Guild precedent requires all Journeymen Years to be counted from within registered Guild territory.

The precedent exists. The application of the precedent is correct. The Journeyman cannot become a Free Master.

Feldhaus must countersign the examination result. Precedent Conviction: the rule is the rule. Consequence RS fires: what is the actual outcome? She sees it clearly. This woman's work is objectively better than the last three Free Masters Feldhaus elevated. The procedural barrier is not protecting quality — it is protecting incumbents. The consequence of maintaining the precedent is a worse Guild, not a better-governed one.

**Decision Fork (Certainty 5, no Scars):** Feldhaus's Certainty 5 means she defaults to her primary Conviction without conflict unless a Scar has forced her secondary to activate. No Scars yet. She countersigns. The Journeyman fails.

But she files an addendum: "Examination deferred pending Committee review of Journeymen Years geographic jurisdiction definition." She has not overruled the precedent. She has created a procedural mechanism to revisit it. The Committee review is now on the Parliamentary agenda. It will take 2 seasons to resolve. The Journeyman's application is suspended, not rejected.

This is the Precedent Conviction expressing something like mercy within its own framework: she cannot break the rule, but she can create the conditions for the rule to change. Her Conviction will not let her act outside procedure. But procedure is not static — it can be changed through procedure.

## NPC 04 — SENIOR INQUISITOR (Iq-Std 3, unnamed — Church Justice Branch)

### The Investigator at the Apex of Institutional Power

At Inquisitor Ladder Standing 3 (Senior Inquisitor), the player has:
- Regional Inquisitorial authority
- May initiate Heresy Proceedings against Standing 4 targets with Cardinal Authorization
- Manages Attention Pool strategically across multiple territories
- Access: Senior Inquisitor's private quarters, full Church archive access, +1D Interview in Church territories

The player is not Cardinal Olafsson. They are his most senior operational officer. This distinction is the entire experience: they have enormous institutional power and precisely zero institutional discretion. Every case must be approved. Every finding must be filed. The Inquisitor investigates; the Cardinal adjudicates.

### The Central Tension: What They Find vs. What They Report

**Investigation S1 — Target: Thread practitioner in T6 Stillhelm:**
AP 3 in T6 (Restoration-sympathetic community, Thread phenomena present). Cardinal Olafsson assigns the case. Investigation begins.

Scene 1 (Observe, Ob 1): The "practitioner" is a 60-year-old herbalist. Her Thread Sensitivity is, by the player's assessment, approximately 5 (Dormant — equivalent to baseline perceptual sensitivity). She is not a practitioner. She is a local healer whose remedies accidentally align with Thread-adjacent folk knowledge she does not understand as such.

Scene 2 (Interview, Ob 1): She answers questions. She has never performed Thread operations. She has never been instructed in Thread methodology. She cannot describe anything the player recognizes as Thread practice.

Scene 3 (Reconstruct, Ob 2): The AP accumulation in T6 came from a Church subordinate who filed observation reports about her customers' "suspicious improvements" after treatment. The subordinate's Conviction is Faith (hardline). He was misidentifying Thread-adjacent outcomes with practitioner activity.

**Finding:** Not a practitioner. The AP was generated by a subordinate's misidentification. Standard case closure: AP reduced −1. Case filed. Heresy charge does not apply.

**What makes this interesting:** Olafsson's response when the finding is filed. He reads it. He notes that the herbalist's remedies are "of unusual efficacy inconsistent with standard herbal practice." He requests the investigation be reopened with specific attention to "unexplained mechanism of action." He is not satisfied with "not a practitioner" — he wants to know why her remedies work.

**[FINDING IQ-SIM-01: Cardinal Olafsson's reopening request reveals the Inquisition's deeper function. The official mission is identifying heresy. The operational reality is that "heresy" is defined by outcomes that contradict the Solmund framework, not by intent. The herbalist's remedies work through mechanisms the Church cannot explain. That is, formally, a doctrinal problem — not because she is a practitioner but because her existence suggests the world operates outside the Solmund framework's explanatory scope. The Senior Inquisitor player has cleared their target and is being asked to re-investigate her for the crime of being unexplained. This is the Inquisition at its most institutionally honest.]**

**The player's choice:** Reopen as ordered (institutional compliance, Authority RS of Olafsson's direction — he is the Cardinal, his direction has institutional weight). Or file a formal Case Closure objection (requires citing specific procedural grounds for the objection — "insufficient new evidence" qualifies). The Inquisitor Ladder's OFC-03 constraint applies: "All findings must be reported to the Confessor. The Inquisitor does not decide what to do with findings — that is the Confessor's domain."

But the Confessor has not been consulted. Olafsson is the Cardinal, not the Confessor. The player can escalate: request Himlensendt's review of the Cardinal's reopening order. This would pit Cardinal Olafsson against the Confessor in an institutional dispute with the Senior Inquisitor as the trigger. Risky. But defensible.

**S5 — The Temperance Connection:**
Case 2: Thread-sensitivity assessment of a university lecturer in T9 Himmelenger (Church capital). The lecturer teaches natural philosophy and has published papers on "substrate phenomena in environmental healing patterns" — language that closely parallels Thread mechanics without naming the Thread.

Investigation finds: TS approximately 20 (Dormant). The lecturer is unaware of what he is perceiving. His academic work is a coherent description of Thread substrate reality expressed in secular natural philosophy terms.

**What the player discovers:** The Dicastery for Doctrine and Archives (Klapp's Dicastery) has a file on this lecturer. The file is favorable — Klapp has been monitoring him as a potential scholarly resource for the Church's theological archive revision project. Klapp and Olafsson are looking at the same person for opposite reasons.

The player is in possession of information that, if submitted to Olafsson, generates a Heresy case that destroys someone Klapp wants to use for the Church's intellectual renewal. If submitted to Klapp instead — which is procedurally irregular but not impossible (the Senior Inquisitor reports to Olafsson, but can consult with other Cardinals on cases with cross-Dicastery implications) — Klapp can intervene to protect the lecturer.

This is a choice between institutional loyalty (follow the chain: report to Olafsson) and institutional outcome (the Church's long-term intellectual renewal, which Klapp represents, is served by protecting this person). The player's personal Conviction determines the resolution.

### S12 — Proceedings Against a Standing 4 Target

Cardinal Olafsson authorizes proceedings against a Bishop's Delegate (Church Standing 4) who has been publicly expressing doubt about Thread persecution methodology. This is the player's first Standing 4 target — the major escalation in the Inquisitor Ladder.

**The case:** The Bishop's Delegate (unnamed) served under Himlensendt for 12 seasons. His expressed doubt was: "We should investigate phenomena before condemning them." Not heresy. Not Thread advocacy. Epistemic humility.

**The player's problem:** "Epistemic humility" is, formally, a doctrinal deviation. The Church's framework is not designed for uncertainty — Certainty is a formal attribute, and expressing uncertainty is a soft deviation. Olafsson's charge: "Failure to maintain the doctrinal standard of ordained authority."

This is the same charge Justice filed against Himlensendt in the Batch 2 simulation. The player is now the instrument of that pattern being applied to a lower-rank target.

The proceedings proceed through the formal structure: 2-season resolution (per ED-631 proposal). The Bishop's Delegate defends himself. He is competent. He does not recant but softens his language sufficiently that the charge becomes ambiguous.

**Verdict:** The player must advise Cardinal Olafsson on the evidentiary sufficiency. Is this sufficient for a guilty finding? By strict doctrinal reading: yes. By proportionality (is this equivalent to what the Church means by heresy?): no. The player's recommendation is the decision. Olafsson will follow their professional assessment.

The player who recommends guilty is an instrument of institutional coercion against a man who expressed honest doubt. The player who recommends not guilty is defying Cardinal Olafsson's institutional preference. Neither choice is clean.

## NPC 05 — TEMPLAR COMMANDER (Tp-Std 3, Church Fortitude Branch)

### The Defensive Military in an Offensive Religious War

At Templar Ladder Standing 3 (Templar Commander), the player commands a garrison or field company at a Cathedral settlement. Their core constraint per OFC-04: **cannot wage offensive war**. They can defend. They cannot advance.

The world in which this character operates is a Church that is engaged in what looks, from the outside, like territorial expansion but is described internally as "extension of ecclesiastical care." The Templar Commander watches the Church seize territories (a political act, per Church Seizure rules) and is then tasked with defending those territories once seized. They were not present for the seizure. They inherit the defense.

**The garrison problem (S1–6):**
The player is assigned to T10 Spartfell (recently seized by Church from Hafenmark in a contested campaign). The garrison is under-equipped: the Seizure allocated resources to the political act, not the military infrastructure. The player has 2 squadrons (Templar-Sergeant-level command, officially sufficient for a Templar Commander) and a settlement where 60% of the population actively resented the Seizure.

Hafenmark's Olaf Geirson (Military Commander, Hafenmark inner circle) is visible on the T10 border with garrison troops. He is not attacking. He is present. The presence is the message: the settlement will be contested.

**What the Templar Commander can do:**
- Fortify the Cathedral (ecclesiastical immunity extends to Church property regardless of who controls the surrounding territory — per OFC-04, can garrison Cathedral without provincial authorization)
- Request reinforcement from Cardinal Jarnstal (Fortitude branch) through the formal Dicastery for Defense of the Faith
- Negotiate with the population to reduce AP (Attention Pool management per OFC-03/OFC-04 combined: AP reduction through pastoral work and service — this is the Templar's legitimate civilian-engagement tool)

**What they cannot do:**
- March on Hafenmark garrison
- Pursue Olaf Geirson's troops when they raid
- Demand Crown military support for an offensive action

**[FINDING TEMP-SIM-01: The Templar Commander's constraint (defensive only) is the most institutionally honest limitation in the game. It accurately captures the historical position of military religious orders: they were constitutionally restricted in offensive action because offensive action undermined the legitimacy of religious military force. A Templar who attacks becomes an aggressor, not a defender, and loses the moral (and mechanical) protection of ecclesiastical authority. The player who runs a Templar must accept that watching is sometimes the correct action — not passivity, but the deliberate choice to hold rather than strike, because striking would cost more than holding.]**

**S8 — The Raid:**
Hafenmark patrol crosses into T10 and destroys a supply cache near the Cathedral perimeter. Not an assault on the Cathedral itself — strategic ambiguity. Is this an attack on Church property (triggering OFC-04 defensive response) or on civilian infrastructure (where the Templar Commander has no authority)?

The player must assess: the cache was inside the Cathedral's formal property boundary (established in the S6 fortification work). It qualifies as Church property by formal measurement. Defensive response is authorized.

But the proportional response to a supply-cache destruction is not a military engagement. It is a formal Protest: a documented violation of ecclesiastical immunity, filed with the Church's Parliamentary presence and with the Crown's Archbishop's Representative (Father Linder, who reports to Himmelenger). This is how the Templar Commander converts a military provocation into a political weapon — by refusing to meet violence with violence and instead creating an institutional record that strengthens the Church's legal position.

P(Protest generating Parliamentary response): ~60% (PI ≥ 3 in Parliament, motion filed). Church Mandate +1 from the successful institutional demonstration (they turned a military provocation into a political win). Hafenmark Parliamentary motion filed in rebuttal (Baralta's reaction via her Military Committee). Geirson's raid has become a Parliamentary crisis that strengthens the Church's position rather than weakening it.

## NPC 06 — RISKBREAKER OPERATIVE (Rb-Std 2 / Senior Operative)

### The Player Who Does Not Exist

At Senior Operative (Riskbreaker Standing 2), the player:
- Has completed 3 missions including one Overwhelming
- Has squadron-coordination authority (can direct other Rb-Std 1 Operatives covertly)
- May establish new stash points
- Shadow Renown only — no public recognition, no Renown in any formal track

The experience of playing a Senior Operative is the experience of being entirely invisible to the game's normal reward structures. Every success generates nothing that other characters can see. Every relationship is deniable. Every contact exists in a parallel world to the one where Almud, Baralta, and Vaynard conduct their political campaigns.

**The world-player link for this character:** The Riskbreaker sees the political game from the operational underneath. Almud's Treaty construction with the Guilds requires logistical groundwork — the Schattendienst needs to verify Guild Comptroller Feldhaus's reliability before the Treaty overture. That verification is a Riskbreaker mission. Vaynard's S14 intelligence auction (from Batch 2 simulation) went through a Niflhel courier whose reliability was confirmed by a Riskbreaker counter-intelligence check. The Riskbreaker is the invisible hand that makes the visible game coherent.

### Mission Structure

**Mission 1 (S1) — Counter-surveillance, T3 Lowenskyst:**
Objective: Verify whether Church has established an observation post near the Crown garrison in T3. If yes: identify and neutralize (not kill — relocate, discredit, or compromise the post).

Scene 1: Infiltrate T3 (covert entry, no Exposure on entry per OFC-02). Observe settlement patterns over 3 days. Finding: Church pastoral visitor making systematic rounds that correspond to Crown officer schedules. This is surveillance.

Scene 2: Identify the pastoral visitor. He is a Deacon (Church Standing 2), assigned to T3 Cathedral. He has no independent Riskbreaker or Niflhel contacts — he's genuinely a junior Church officer who was assigned to "maintain a pastoral presence" by his Canon superior. He doesn't know he's being used as a surveillance asset.

Scene 3: Compromise the mission without exposing the player's role. Option: plant a finding in the Deacon's official file (through the Schattendienst's Church-adjacent Officer from Thale's simulation, who has access to pastoral records). The finding: "Deacon's pastoral schedule has been irregular and his attendance records contain inconsistencies." The Canon pulls him back to T9 for a review. The surveillance post collapses from internal Church administrative action, not from any visible Crown intervention.

Result: Mission OW (objective achieved without exposure, collateral managed, Crown's fingerprints absent). Rb-Std 2: advancement track updated. Shadow Renown +1.

**[FINDING RB-SIM-01: The optimal Riskbreaker mission outcome is not "objective achieved by player" — it is "objective achieved by institutional processes the player triggers." The Riskbreaker who acts directly creates an evidence trail. The Riskbreaker who activates existing institutional mechanisms creates an outcome that looks like normal institutional operation. This is the highest-skill expression of covert work: making the world act on your behalf without knowing it has been directed. The Church administrative review that eliminated the surveillance post was a genuine Church administrative action. It just happened to serve Crown's interests because the Riskbreaker created the condition for it.]**

**Mission 3 (S6) — Overwhelming required for Rb-Std 3 candidacy:**
Objective: Identify the Niflhel contact responsible for supplying AER-adjacent intelligence to Schoenland's diplomatic mission (which has been using this intelligence to calibrate Altonian Institutional Pressure positioning).

The mission requires entering Schoenland diplomatic territory (T16, which is not in formal territorial play but is accessible as a settlement). Schoenland's Vanguard-warning function means they receive intelligence about Valoria's internal state — and someone is improving that intelligence's precision.

Scene chain (4 scenes, Ob 2 each):
1. Observe Schoenland diplomatic mission patterns in T7 Rendstad (their nearest contact point). Find: courier running between Schoenland and a specific Dockworkers contact.
2. Identify the courier. He is a Dockworkers Standing 3 Foreman (Niflhel arm) — a commercial transit operator with plausible cover.
3. Identify his handler in the Schoenland mission. Senior diplomatic attaché.
4. Compromise the handler: plant evidence that the attaché has been receiving payment beyond his diplomatic salary through the Dockworkers network. Schoenland's own internal security flags it; the attaché is recalled.

Outcome: OW on Mission 3. Schoenland's AER-adjacent intelligence precision drops significantly for 4 seasons. AER calibration becomes less accurate — meaning Schoenland cannot as precisely target the threshold above which intervention becomes plausible. The Altonian Institutional Pressure clock's efficiency is reduced. Not eliminated — reduced.

**Shadow Renown consequence:** The Riskbreaker's name is now known within the Niflhel Dockworkers network as "whoever burned the Schoenland route." This generates Niflhel Dockworkers awareness at the level where the route existed (Standing 3–4). Not a threat yet. But a marker. They know someone clean is operating in their territory.

## NPC 07 — JOURNEYMAN GUILD MEMBER (Gu-Std 1, T7 Rendstad)

### The Game From the Bottom

At Journeyman (Guild Standing 1), the player has:
- Access to Guild Forum in any registered Guild territory
- Right to practice trade anywhere Guild-active
- Obligation: Guild dues (token Wealth cost), Forum attendance when in Guild territory

No political power. No access to faction leaders. No Parliamentary standing. No Domain Actions.

What they have: the ground-level view of everything that every other faction is doing, read through the impact on commercial activity.

**What the Journeyman sees:**
- When Church AP rises in T10, trade in T10 becomes uncertain (merchants don't know if their transactions will be scrutinized in Inquisitorial proceedings). The Journeyman notices this as reduced customer flow before any political actor reports the AP number.
- When Crown and Hafenmark are in Parliamentary tension (Grand Contest period), trade-route arbitration takes longer (Parliamentary bandwidth is consumed by political motions). The Journeyman notices this as delayed payment processing.
- When Niflhel expands its Dockworkers network in T7, the Journeyman encounters competitors with suspiciously low prices — Niflhel's smuggling network undercuts legitimate Guild commercial rates.

**The Journeyman's campaign is not about winning.** It is about surviving the political game while practicing a trade, maintaining Forum standing, and navigating institutional structures that were not designed with their interests in mind.

### The Caste Experience

The Journeyman player who chose a Southern Einhir heritage (character creation option per faction_politics_expanded §3.1) encounters the caste system directly.

**S3 — Masterpiece Examination application:**
Three year-arcs served. Work quality: above average. The committee reviews it. Feldhaus chairs. The Journeyman's work is assessed against the full procedural requirement — including the geographic jurisdiction requirement for Journeymen Years. One of three territories where they worked was during a season when the Forum registration there was temporarily lapsed (it was renewed 2 seasons later, but the paperwork gap is in the record).

Result: examination deferred. Not rejected. Feldhaus's addendum (from the Feldhaus simulation: "pending Committee review of geographic jurisdiction definition") applies. The Journeyman waits.

**What the wait feels like from inside:** The player has done the work. The work is good. The institutional process is operating exactly as designed. There is no villain in this scene. There is no corrupt official who can be exposed, confronted, or overcome. There is a system that is working correctly and producing an outcome that feels profoundly unjust.

**[FINDING JOUR-SIM-01: The Journeyman experience crystallizes Valoria's most important design question. The game is populated by named NPCs with Convictions, Resonant Styles, and arc possibilities. The Journeyman is not one of them — they are the person whom those NPCs' decisions affect. The political game that Almud, Baralta, and Vaynard play is experienced by the Journeyman as: whether their trade routes are safe, whether their examination is fair, whether their community is harassed by Church investigators. The caste system, played from inside, is not an abstract design element. It is the reason the Masterpiece Examination was deferred. Valoria's political game makes sense from the top. It demands accountability from the bottom.]**

**S8 — The Forum Revolt:**
Feldhaus's Committee review takes 2 seasons. The recommendation: revise the geographic jurisdiction requirement to include territories where the Forum registration was temporarily lapsed (and subsequently renewed) during the Journeymen Years. The revision passes the Parliamentary Commercial Committee 3-1.

The Journeyman's examination is re-opened. Re-assessed. Passed. Free Master. The system changed, and the change benefited them. They did not fight the system — Feldhaus created the mechanism for change through procedure. The Journeyman became a Free Master because a Comptroller chose to create a Committee revision rather than simply enforce a rule.

**What this feels like:** Relief. Not triumph. The victory was never theirs to achieve directly — it depended on whether a person at the top of the institution chose to use their procedural power generously. The Journeyman is grateful. They also understand, now, that their freedom from the procedural barrier depended entirely on Feldhaus's discretion. If Feldhaus had not filed the addendum, nothing changes.

This is the game's most honest statement about institutional power: it is occasionally merciful, and its mercy is an act of individual generosity by people who could equally have withheld it.

## CROSS-SIMULATION FINDINGS

### FINDING CROSS-B3-01: Mid-Rank Characters Experience the Moral Cost That Leaders Export

Every faction leader (Almud, Baralta, Vaynard) makes decisions that generate consequences in other parts of the institutional structure. Kolbrun Thale manages the intelligence asymmetry Almud creates. Theodor Kreutz manages the ethical impossibility of the dual chain Almud's institution requires. Annika Feldhaus manages the procedural injustice embedded in Guild law. The Senior Inquisitor manages the institutional coercion Cardinal Olafsson deploys.

Faction leaders in NPC-as-player mode experience decisions as choices. Mid-rank characters experience them as consequences. The same political game looks structurally coherent from the top and institutionally brutal from the middle.

### FINDING CROSS-B3-02: The Institutional Blind Spot Is Always One Step Up

Every character in this batch is limited by what their direct superior chooses to tell them, authorize, or acknowledge:
- Thale cannot tell Almud about the Counter because it would reveal the Löwenritter chain.
- Kreutz cannot act on the Counter information without Almud's knowledge.
- Feldhaus cannot override Guild precedent without a Committee process she must initiate through Baralta.
- The Senior Inquisitor cannot override Olafsson's reopening request without escalating to Himlensendt.
- The Templar Commander cannot pursue the Hafenmark patrol without violating OFC-04.
- The Riskbreaker cannot take credit for any mission success without breaking cover.
- The Journeyman cannot force Feldhaus to file the addendum.

Each character's constraints are not the institution's constraints — they are the specific constraint created by their position relative to the next level up. The game's mid-rank experience is: power is available one level above you and you must work through it, around it, or alongside it. Never against it.

### FINDING CROSS-B3-03: Shadow Renown Needs Formal Game Support

Three characters in this batch generate Shadow Renown (Thale via intelligence diplomacy, the Riskbreaker via missions, Feldhaus via back-channel committee work). Shadow Renown is referenced in faction_politics_expanded (REN-03 original: attributed actions), but no mechanic specifies:
- What Shadow Renown unlocks at what level
- Whether Shadow Renown can be converted to public Renown and under what conditions
- How Shadow Renown is tracked (no track exists in canonical docs)
- What the consequences are of Shadow Renown becoming public (Riskbreaker's Shadow Renown becoming public would be catastrophic; Feldhaus's would be neutral; Thale's would be mixed)

**[GAP-B3-01: Shadow Renown is referenced across multiple canonical docs but has no implementation specification. The institutional characters who generate it have no formal reward for it. This needs a mechanic.]**

## NPC 01 — PEDER ALMSTEDT (Hafenmark Senior Parliamentary Chair)

### Who He Is

Certainty 6. The scale runs to 5. Almstedt is listed at 6 in faction_politics_expanded §1.2c, which requires a note: Certainty 6 here means something specific — he is not merely confident in his worldview, he has witnessed the original founding of Hafenmark's constitutional framework. His certainty is not a belief about the world. It is memory. He was present at the drafting sessions. He knows what was intended.

This makes him the most dangerous person in Hafenmark Parliament, and Baralta knows it.

**Canonical stats:**
- Standing 6 (Senior Parliamentary Chair — the highest Standing before Speaker-Regent)
- Conviction: Precedent | RS: Evidence | Certainty: 6
- Specialty: "Deed-monarchy institutional memory; serves as senior chair by seniority"
- Unique: "The single NPC who remembers the original Almqvist founding terms"
- Disposition toward Baralta: +1 (constitutional procedure respected — but not unconditional)

**What Certainty 6 means in play:** He cannot be moved by Authority RS. Authority invokes higher powers — he IS the higher institutional memory. He is resistant to Evidence RS unless the evidence predates the founding period (which no one has access to except through the deep archives). He can only be genuinely reached by Consequence RS: demonstrating that the constitutional framework, as originally designed, is producing outcomes that contradict its own stated purposes.

### The Chair's Power

**Chairing authority:**
When Almstedt chairs a Parliamentary session (Ceremonial Scene), his structural authority means:
- He decides what motions are in order
- He rules on procedural challenges
- He can delay any motion for "review of constitutional precedent" — a 1-season delay, effectively a veto with a time limit
- He recognizes speakers and can decline to recognize them (not a permanent silence — but "the Chair will return to that motion later" is a meaningful tool)

No one can override his chairing decisions without a floor vote — and floor votes require a majority, which is Baralta's coalition. But Baralta cannot use her coalition to override Almstedt's procedural rulings without creating a visible constitutional crisis (the Chair being overruled by majority force = constitutional dissolution of the Chair's authority). She cannot do this without cost.

**What Almstedt knows that Baralta doesn't want known:**
The original Almqvist founding terms included a succession clause that Baralta's Dynastic Assertion interpretation has quietly revised. The original clause: succession to Hafenmark's senior parliamentary authority required ratification by a two-thirds supermajority (not Baralta's standard majority interpretation). The two-thirds requirement was amended out of the formal constitutional text in a parliamentary session 40 seasons ago — but the amendment was passed by a simple majority, which, by the original founding terms, was itself constitutionally insufficient to make that amendment.

Baralta's current constitutional authority rests on an amendment that is arguably invalid under the original founding document.

Almstedt knows this. He has never raised it. He has not raised it because the practical consequences would be catastrophic (invalidating 40 seasons of parliamentary decisions), and because raising it would require him to act against the institution he has served his entire life. But he knows it. And anyone who gains his trust and reads enough of his institutional memory will learn it too.

**[FINDING ALM-SIM-01: Almstedt's silent knowledge of Hafenmark's foundational constitutional irregularity is the single most potent undisclosed piece of institutional information in the game. If it surfaces — by Almstedt's choice, by deep archive investigation (Haelgrund's path), or by Niflhel intelligence — it could invalidate Baralta's Dynastic Assertion primary victory condition by questioning the legitimacy of the parliamentary apparatus that would certify her claim. This is not a gap in the design — it is a live piece of political artillery that has never been fired. The player who runs Almstedt controls the trigger.]**

### Season Structure

**The Chair's domain:**
Almstedt does not take Domain Actions in the standard BG sense. He presides. He attends. He reviews. His seasonal activities:

- **Parliament Convening (Ceremonial Scene, 2/year-arc):** He chairs. Every Parliamentary Convening is his home terrain. He sets the agenda in collaboration with Baralta (who introduces motions) but his chairing authority gives him independent procedural power.
- **Archive review (1 scene/season):** He maintains the constitutional archive in Gransol (T8). This is not a Domain Action — it is an administrative routine that has been his practice for 30 seasons. He reads precedent. He cross-references decisions. He notes discrepancies.
- **Private consultation (1 scene/season, when requested):** Any Standing 3+ Parliamentarian can request a private session. Torvi Heljason (Baralta's Legal Advisor) requests these frequently. Almstedt provides constitutional clarification. What he tells Heljason shapes the legal arguments Baralta makes in Parliament.

### Phase 1 (S1–10): The Institutional Anchor

**S1–4:** Almstedt's early game is simply present. Parliament runs. Baralta introduces motions. He chairs. His −1 Ob on Evidence-based procedural actions means that when he validates a motion's constitutionality, the validation is almost unassailable. When he raises a procedural concern, it carries institutional weight that forces Baralta to respond on constitutional grounds rather than political ones.

**S3 — First tension:** Baralta's Trade & Guild Relations Committee motion (from the Feldhaus simulation) passes 3-1. Almstedt chairs. He notes an anomaly: the motion uses the word "permanent" in a way that contradicts an existing 12-season-old parliamentary resolution. He could rule the motion procedurally deficient. He chooses not to — the outcome is good and the deficiency is technical. He notes it in the archive instead.

This is his operating principle: he protects the institution's long-term coherence by maintaining an internal record of constitutional inconsistencies, without converting every inconsistency into a parliamentary crisis. The archive is his instrument for managing the gap between ideal procedure and practical governance.

**S7 — Baralta's Grand Contest preparation:**
Baralta is planning her first Grand Contest against Crown (S11 in standard timeline). She consults Almstedt: is a Grand Contest valid under Hafenmark's constitutional framework? His answer: "The Grand Contest is constitutional, but the specific territorial jurisdiction claim you're planning to raise as the contest question — that is contested. The founding terms specify that Hafenmark can contest jurisdiction through Parliament only in territories where Hafenmark has a prior documented governance interest. T3 Lowenskyst has no such documented interest in the archives."

Baralta: "Then we create the documentation."

Almstedt: "You mean you'd like me to find an interpretation that supports documentation creation?"

Pause.

Almstedt reviews the archive. There is, in fact, a historical precedent: a 70-season-old commercial survey that mapped the T3 trade corridor as "Hafenmark-proximate administrative interest." Not governance. Administrative interest. It is thin. But it is real. He certifies it as sufficient.

**[FINDING ALM-SIM-02: Almstedt's certification of thin precedent is the most consequential single scene in Hafenmark's political history. He knows the precedent is marginal. He knows Baralta intends to build a major territorial claim on it. He certifies it because: (1) it is technically real, (2) invalidating it would rupture his relationship with Baralta and remove his ability to moderate her future constitutional interpretations, (3) he believes Baralta's constitutional order, even imperfect, is better for Hafenmark than the alternatives. This is the Senior Chair's central ethical compromise: he enables marginally legitimate constitutional overreach because the alternative is losing all influence over how the overreach is conducted. He becomes complicit in the thing he is supposed to constrain. This is the experience of every institutional moderating force in every historical political system.]**

### Phase 2 (S11–20): The Constitutional Question

**S14 — Church Excommunication threat:**
Church TC 65. Himlensendt invokes Excommunication against Baralta. Almstedt chairs the emergency Parliament session. He must rule: is Church Excommunication binding on Hafenmark's constitutional framework?

His reading of the founding terms: "No. The founding Almqvist-Hafenmark charter, Article 7, specifies that Hafenmark's parliamentary constitutional authority is the supreme civil law within Hafenmark's territories. No external religious authority supersedes it." He rules Excommunication non-binding in Hafenmark. The vote: 3-1.

He is correct under the founding terms. He is also, for the first time, using his constitutional memory directly in Baralta's favor rather than merely certifying it. The distinction feels important to him. He has shifted from passive archivist to active constitutional defender.

**S17 — The Foundational Irregularity:**
Almstedt's archive review uncovers something he has always known but has never formally documented: the 40-season-old amendment that revised the supermajority succession requirement. He reviews it again. He is writing a constitutional commentary — an academic document, not a political one — and he must describe this amendment accurately.

He writes: "The Session 440 Amendment to Succession Protocol was ratified by simple majority (31-14). Under Article 12 of the Founding Charter (requiring supermajority ratification for amendments to succession protocol), this ratification was constitutionally insufficient. The amendment has been treated as valid for 40 seasons and has formed the basis of subsequent parliamentary governance. Its formal invalidity, if adjudicated, would require either retroactive supermajority confirmation or a determination that the amendment's extensive historical application has created de facto validity through institutional acceptance."

He locks the document in the archive's sealed section. He tells no one. He does not know what to do with it.

**[FINDING ALM-SIM-03: Almstedt's documentation of the constitutional irregularity is the most significant institutional act in the batch. He has formally recorded the information that could collapse Baralta's Dynastic Assertion. His Precedent Conviction demands he document accurately. His Continuity (he doesn't have this as a formal secondary — but his behavior pattern suggests it) demands he not destroy the institution he has served. His solution: lock it away, neither suppress nor deploy it. He is doing what every institutional archivist does with information that is too dangerous to use and too important to destroy. He is preserving the question for a future he doesn't know.]**

### Phase 3 (S21–28): The Succession Question

**S22 — Baralta's victory:**
Baralta achieves Dynastic Assertion primary conditions. Parliament certifies the victory. Almstedt presides. He certifies the parliamentary vote. He has a moment where he could raise the foundational irregularity — the supermajority amendment issue, which might invalidate the parliamentary certification process itself.

He does not raise it. He certifies the vote 3-1. Hafenmark's Dynastic Assertion is parliamentarily certified as constitutional.

Later that session, alone in the archive, he writes one more document: "This certification was made under the authority of the founding charter's institutional acceptance doctrine (de facto validity through 40 seasons of practice). Whether this doctrine is itself constitutionally grounded is a question I have chosen not to adjudicate from the Chair. The question remains available to future interpreters."

He has not suppressed it. He has not deployed it. He has preserved the question and explicitly identified that he chose not to answer it. This is his legacy: a perfectly preserved constitutional ambiguity, archived for posterity, never resolved in his lifetime.

### Stress Tests

**Stress 1 — Vaynard's intelligence (S9):** Vaynard's Tribune Investigate reveals that Almstedt holds historical knowledge that contradicts Baralta's standard constitutional interpretation. Vaynard's agent approaches Almstedt with a question: "What did the founding charter actually say about succession supermajority requirements?" Almstedt's response is technically accurate: "The founding charter's Article 12 is a matter of documented public record. Anyone may consult the archive." He does not answer the question; he points toward the archive where the answer exists, correctly protected by its own accessibility. Vaynard has been told where the weapon is. Whether he reaches it depends on whether he can access the sealed section (Depth 3+ investigation in Gransol — the Ministry equivalent of a Depth-3 fieldwork challenge).

**Stress 2 — Cardinal Olafsson's investigation (S15):** Church Heresy Investigation targets Almstedt's archive assistant (a young Canon, Church Standing 2, seconded to Hafenmark). The assistant has been reading Thread-adjacent documents in the archive that Almstedt made available. Almstedt's response: "The documents are a matter of Hafenmark constitutional history, not theological practice. The Canon reads them in his capacity as archival assistant, not as a theological scholar. Inquisitorial jurisdiction does not extend to Hafenmark administrative reading." He is correct. He defeats the investigation through procedural precision. But the assistant is frightened and asks to be recalled to Himmelenger. Almstedt loses his archive assistant. He begins doing the archive review alone.

**Stress 3 — Torvi Heljason's question (S20):** Baralta's Legal Advisor asks directly: "Is there anything in the founding archive that would complicate the Dynastic Assertion's parliamentary certification?" Long pause. "The founding archive contains what the founding archive contains. My professional advice is that you review Article 12 before the certification session." He has told her. It's now her responsibility.

## NPC 02 — WILHELM VOSS (Royal Marshal, Crown Inner Circle)

### Who He Is

Wilhelm Voss is a second-generation Valorsplatz aristocrat who has held the Royal Marshal position for 14 seasons. His Conviction is Order; his RS is Authority; his Certainty is 4. He controls Crown's Military attribute and answers formally to Almud, practically to the institutional rhythm of the Crown military chain, and informally to the fact that Theodor Kreutz (his Knight-Commander sub-report) answers to BOTH him and Ehrenwall.

His disposition toward Ehrenwall is −1. This is not personal animosity — it is structural distrust. He distrusts the Löwenritter because they answer to their own Grand Master rather than to the Crown chain. A military institution that reserves the right to override its commander's chain is not a military institution — it is a political actor wearing military livery. Voss knows this. He has known it for 14 seasons.

**What he does:**
- Commands Crown's military operations (Domain Action: Legionary deployments, Muster, March)
- Chairs the Crown War Council when summoned (Ceremonial Scene observer at Standing 4 equivalent, voting at Standing 5+)
- Reports weekly to Almud on military situation
- Reports quarterly to Löwenritter Liaison Kreutz (per the institutional coordination protocol his predecessor established — he cannot end this protocol without Almud's authorization)

### The Marshal's Season Structure

**Military resource management:**
Crown Military 4. Voss manages: 3 field companies (approximately 6 mass-battle units), garrison commitments in T1 and T14, border patrol in T3, and the ongoing calculation of where to pre-position against multiple potential threats (Church Seizure, Varfell Cultural Reformation, Hafenmark Proclamation, Altonian Vanguard).

Every season: Voss allocates military assets. This is a portfolio optimization problem. Church requires Seizure prevention in T6 (garrison). Varfell requires Cultural Reformation deterrence in T5 (patrol). Hafenmark's Olaf Geirson is watching T3. He cannot be everywhere. His choices reveal Crown's strategic priorities to anyone watching garrison patterns — and Vaynard is watching.

**The Kreutz problem:**
Voss receives a quarterly coordination report from Kreutz. In theory, Kreutz reports military situation at T1 and coordinates Löwenritter-adjacent operations. In practice, Voss cannot determine what Kreutz's other chain (Ehrenwall) knows about Crown's military posture, because Kreutz is operationally loyal to both and strategically loyal to Almud personally. Voss does not know how much of his quarterly coordination report is passing through to Ehrenwall's intelligence picture.

He handles this by bifurcating his reports: the formal quarterly coordination report (which goes through Kreutz) contains accurate but incomplete information. The true military situation report goes directly to Almud through the War Council channel. He is protecting Crown's strategic intelligence from the Löwenritter chain, which means he is also somewhat protecting it from Kreutz.

**[FINDING VOSS-SIM-01: Voss's intelligence bifurcation creates an institutional asymmetry that neither Kreutz nor Ehrenwall can fully see. Voss knows that Ehrenwall receives an incomplete picture of Crown's military posture. This is actually his greatest strategic advantage: if the Coup Counter advances to 3 and Löwenritter pre-positions, they are pre-positioning based on an incomplete military picture. Voss has maintained an undisclosed military reserve — units not present in the formal quarterly coordination report — specifically for the Coup contingency. This is his Order Conviction expressed as institutional preparation: he cannot prevent the Coup if the Counter reaches 3, but he can ensure Crown's military response is stronger than the Löwenritter expects.]**

### Phase 1 (S1–8): Building the Reserve

**S1–4:** Standard military management. Garrisons assigned. Patrols maintained. Voss focuses on: rotating garrison assignments so no unit spends more than 6 seasons at a single post (fatigue reduction, institutional freshness). He is building military cohesion across Crown's field companies by cross-posting officers — any officer who has served under multiple company commanders is more flexible than one who has served under only one.

**S5 — The undisclosed reserve:**
Voss requisitions funding for a training program: "Extended Field Readiness Protocol." Almud approves without detailed review (it appears administrative). The Protocol creates a 2-unit mobile reserve, kept at T6 Stillhelm under a Captain (Knight-Commander equivalent, non-Löwenritter chain) who reports only to Voss. This reserve does not appear in the formal military disposition that flows through Kreutz's coordination report.

Cost: Wealth −1 per season sustained (the Protocol's training costs). Crown Wealth at 4: −1/season = Wealth 3 by S8. Almud notices the Wealth cost in accounting but accepts Voss's framing ("extended readiness investment, worth the cost in deterrence value"). He is not wrong about the deterrence value. He does not know about the reserve's specific non-disclosure function.

**S7 — Ehrenwall's probe:**
Ehrenwall requests an informal military coordination meeting (through Kreutz: "The Grand Master wishes to discuss border defense coordination"). Voss attends. The meeting is professional. He provides complete information on T14 (Ehrenfeld — Löwenritter's territory) and T3 coordination. He provides no information on T6 or the mobile reserve. The meeting ends without incident.

Ehrenwall's assessment (from her arc): the Royal Marshal is competent and correct-behaving. She notes that his military disposition has a gap around T6 she cannot explain from the coordination data. She does not pursue it. She files the observation.

### Phase 2 (S9–18): Watching the Counter

**S12 — Counter at 1 (Almud's RS data concealment):**
Voss does not know the Counter exists (it is an internal Löwenritter assessment). But he knows something changed: Kreutz's coordination reports have become marginally more formal. The language has tightened. There is slightly more institutional precision in the way Kreutz communicates garrison disposition preferences. This is a signal Voss recognizes without being able to name: Kreutz is being more careful. People are more careful when they are managing something sensitive.

Voss's response: he requests a War Council session (Ceremonial Scene). He briefs Almud on the military situation with unusual formality — not because the situation has changed but because he wants Almud to witness Crown's military posture explicitly, on the record, so that Almud's personal awareness of military assets is unambiguous. He is creating documentation of Almud's informed knowledge, against a future where someone might argue that Almud did not know what military resources Crown held.

**[FINDING VOSS-SIM-02: Voss's formal War Council briefing is institutional insurance. He is creating a documented record that Almud is informed — specifically because he suspects something is happening that might later generate claims about what Almud knew. He does not know it's the Coup Counter. He knows something is making Kreutz careful, and careful people are managing something their superiors would want to know about. He is preparing documentation without knowing what he is preparing it for. This is the Order Conviction expressed as procedural anticipation: when you sense something is wrong, document that you told your superior.]**

**S17 — Counter at 2:**
Voss now notices that Kreutz's quarterly report is late. Not significantly — 4 days. But Kreutz has never been late in 14 seasons. Voss sends an inquiry: "Your quarterly coordination report has not been received. Please confirm transmission date." Kreutz responds within the day with the report. Normal content. No explanation for the delay.

Voss reads the report three times. Nothing unusual in content. But the delay itself is information: something required Kreutz to consider what to include before sending. He is managing disclosure decisions that previously required no consideration.

Voss quietly mobilizes the T6 reserve: they move from training posture to deployment-ready. Not to a threat position — to a position from which they can reach T1 within 2 days. He tells no one. He writes in his private operational log: "Mobile reserve to deployment-ready, S17. Basis: unexplained coordination delay in Löwenritter liaison report. No formal threat identified. Precautionary repositioning."

### Phase 3 (S19–28): The Coup and After

**S22 — Counter at 3:**
Ehrenwall moves. Riskbreakers pre-position in T1 and T14. Voss's T6 reserve (which Ehrenwall's operational picture doesn't include) can move to T1 within 2 days.

Decision: does he deploy the reserve to defend T1?

His Order Conviction: Order is the institution, not the person. If Ehrenwall's Coup produces a Managed Transition (Stability −1, clean institutional continuation), deploying the reserve converts it to a violent confrontation (Stability −3, civil war risk). If he does not deploy: the Managed Transition proceeds. If he does deploy: he might prevent the Coup but at catastrophic institutional cost.

Kreutz stands down the Royal Guard (from his simulation: he manages the withdrawal). Voss watches through his intelligence channels. When Kreutz stands down, Voss understands: his Knight-Commander has made the same calculation he is making. Deploying the reserve against Kreutz's standing-down Royal Guard would mean overriding his own sub-commander's decision. This would be military insubordination within Crown's own chain — the thing Voss considers the Löwenritter's original sin.

He recalls the reserve to T6. The Coup proceeds as a Managed Transition.

**Post-Coup service:**
Voss remains Royal Marshal under Löwenritter governance. The Löwenritter need him: Crown's military apparatus is the only functioning administrative military structure on the peninsula. Ehrenwall knows this. Voss serves. He maintains the reserve at T6, now openly (it's no longer hidden from Löwenritter governance — the bifurcation served its purpose). He tells Ehrenwall about it in their first formal meeting: "I maintained an undisclosed mobile reserve as a Coup contingency. I decided not to deploy it. You should know it exists." She appreciates the candor more than the reserve.

## NPC 03 — CARDINAL KLAPP (Temperance, Dicastery for Doctrine and Archives)

### Who He Is

Klapp chairs the Dicastery for Doctrine and Archives. His Conviction is Reason+Faith (a compound — he holds both simultaneously and believes they are compatible if the Church is honest about what it knows). His RS is Evidence. He pursues AER maintenance. He is the most likely Cardinal to experience a Conviction crisis if exposed to Thread evidence.

What the arc_expansion notes add: Klapp is not naive. He does not think the Church can acknowledge Thread reality openly. He thinks the Church must develop the theological capacity to do so — building the framework before the revelation, so that when the revelation comes, the institution survives it. He is doing theological civil engineering.

**His Dicastery:**
The Dicastery for Doctrine and Archives (per faction_politics_expanded §7.3):
- Theological scholarship
- Archive maintenance
- University liaison (overlap with Hochschule, new Crown Ministry — jurisdictional tension)

**His position relative to other Cardinals:**
- **Jarnstal (Fortitude):** Klapp respects but finds limiting. Military faith is not the same as intellectual faith.
- **Olafsson (Justice):** Klapp fears. Olafsson is doing to intellectual honesty what the Inquisition has always done to deviant belief — treating uncertainty as heresy.
- **Tormann (Prudence):** Klapp finds useful. Prudence cares about fiscal stability; an intellectually flexible Church is fiscally more sustainable than a doctrinally brittle one. They share common interests.
- **Himlensendt:** Klapp serves loyally while privately watching the Confessor's Certainty erode. He knows Himlensendt is cracking. He is preparing the theological doctrine that will allow the institution to survive the crack.

### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Archive Project

**The AER management function:**
Klapp's Priority 5 in the Church's NPC tree: "AER maintenance (if Cardinal of Temperance active and Church controls T9)." AER +1 from Heresy Investigation success. AER −1 if Church Stability drops below 3. AER −2 from Reformed Settlement.

Klapp manages AER by managing the rate and targeting of Heresy Investigations. He cannot stop Olafsson from deploying Inquisitors (that is Olafsson's domain). But he can influence where Investigations are targeted by controlling what information about Thread activity reaches Olafsson through the Dicastery for Doctrine. If Klapp routes Thread-related scholarly findings through a theological review process before they enter Olafsson's awareness, he can slow the Investigation pipeline without appearing to obstruct it.

**S1–4 — The theological review buffer:**
Klapp establishes a formal scholarly review protocol: any field report containing terminology related to "unexplained natural phenomena, substrate anomalies, perception alteration, or environmental instability" must pass through his Dicastery for Doctrine review before reaching the Investigation docket.

This is constitutionally correct — doctrinal review of theologically ambiguous phenomena is explicitly his Dicastery's function. The protocol adds approximately 2 seasons to the pipeline between a Thread-adjacent report and an Investigation deployment. This is not suppression. It is procedural delay.

**S5 — The Klapp framework:**
Klapp begins writing his theological reframing document: "On the Nature of Substrate Revelation in Solmund's Creation." His argument: Thread phenomena are not contradictions of Solmund's revelation but evidence of a deeper layer of Creation that Solmund's revelation gestures toward without fully specifying. The Solmund texts are not wrong — they are incomplete. Completeness is not achieved through silence; it is achieved through scholarly humility and continued observation.

This document, if published, would be moderate heresy by Olafsson's standard (acknowledging that Solmund's texts are incomplete is acknowledging that divine revelation is insufficient). Klapp keeps it in his personal archive. He does not publish it. He is writing it to understand his own position clearly, not to deploy it.

**[FINDING KL-SIM-01: Klapp's unpublished framework document is the theological parallel to Almstedt's locked archive. Both characters are preserving a transformative piece of knowledge that is too dangerous to use and too important to discard. The parallel is not coincidental — it reflects the design's most sophisticated element: the people who know the most are often the ones who can act on it least, because their institutional position makes using that knowledge self-destructively expensive. The knowledge waits for a moment of institutional crisis when the cost of using it drops below the cost of continued concealment.]**

### Phase 2 (S9–18): Olafsson's Escalation

**S12 — Olafsson's threat:**
Olafsson discovers the scholarly review buffer through the Inquisitorial filing system (an Inquisitor-General's case, delayed 2 seasons by the buffer, returns a report noting the delay was attributed to "Doctrinal Dicastery review protocol"). Olafsson requests a College meeting. Agenda: "Efficiency of Inquisitorial case routing."

At the College meeting: Olafsson proposes removing the Doctrinal Dicastery pre-screening requirement for Investigation-eligible cases. Vote needed: 3 of 4 Cardinals (Himlensendt presides, does not vote in normal procedure). Olafsson (yes): 1. Jarnstal (yes — he wants faster Investigation deployment for Templar coordination): 2. Prudence: assessing fiscal impact. Klapp (no): 1.

If Prudence votes yes: buffer removed, 3-1. If Prudence votes no: tie, Himlensendt casting vote required.

**Klapp's argument to Tormann (Prudence):** The Investigation pipeline, accelerated, will generate more AP events that destabilize Church governance in territories where Guild commercial interests overlap with ecclesiastical jurisdiction. Removing the buffer will increase Church-Guild friction in those territories, which reduces tithe yield in commercial settlements (Church's primary revenue source). Sustained AER above 2 depends on managed Investigation pace. The buffer is a fiscal tool.

Tormann's Consequence RS: this argument resonates. He votes no. Tie. Himlensendt's casting vote: he votes to retain the buffer (uncertainty is his current state; he does not want to accelerate processes that generate confrontation). 2-2. Buffer retained.

**[FINDING KL-SIM-02: Klapp wins the College vote by framing the buffer as a fiscal instrument rather than a theological one. He never defends the buffer's doctrinal purpose. He argues purely from Prudence's economic interests. This is the politically intelligent move: the correct argument for the wrong venue (theology) would have lost; the wrong argument for the correct venue (fiscal management) wins. Klapp is the Church's most sophisticated political operator precisely because he understands that institutions respond to interests, not to truths.]**

**S16 — Himlensendt's Scar 2:**
Himlensendt's Faith conviction is visibly eroding. His public statements have become more defensive, less confident. Klapp observes this. He begins privately drafting a different document: not a theological framework (too dangerous) but a "Succession Preparation Brief" — who in the College has the theological depth to hold the institution through a Confessor transition, and what theological posture would minimize schism risk.

His honest assessment: Justice (Olafsson) would accelerate TC advancement and persecution, destabilizing everything in a power vacuum. Fortitude (Jarnstal) would militarize the Church, which addresses external threats but not internal theological coherence. Prudence (Tormann) would be fiscally stable but lacks doctrinal authority. Klapp himself: the only Cardinal whose theological framework is designed to survive the revelation that Solmund's texts are incomplete. He is the only one who has prepared for the world as it is rather than the world as doctrine says it should be.

He does not nominate himself. He writes: "The College faces a succession question that has not been formally articulated. The question is not who is most qualified in traditional terms. The question is who is most equipped to govern an institution whose foundational premises are under historical pressure." He gives the document to no one.

### Stress Test

**Stress 1 — Vaynard's evidence package (S14):** Vaynard sells Klapp Thread state data — specifically: RS decline rates in Church-adjacent territories, presented as "natural philosophical measurements." Klapp reads it. His Evidence RS fires internally: he recognizes what he is reading. He processes it through his unpublished framework document and realizes: the measurements are consistent with active Thread substrate behavior at the Stirring threshold in multiple Church territories. Church's Heresy Investigations have been directly increasing Thread Tension in the territories they operate in.

He does not publish this either. He writes in his private archive: "The Investigations are generating the phenomena they are investigating. The causal loop is documented. I cannot present this to the College without Olafsson filing a counter-investigation against me." He adjusts his buffer protocol to add a specific review criterion: "Any Investigation in territories with documented substrate anomaly patterns requires extended environmental impact assessment before deployment." One more friction layer. Invisible. Legal.

## NPC 04 — INGRID STENSKALD (Skald-Chief, Varfell Inner Circle)

### Who She Is

Ingrid Stenskald is the keeper of Varfell's oral law. She validates succession claims. She has Continuity primary Conviction, Evidence RS, Certainty 5. The document notes: "Any Deed-Claim succession in Varfell needs the Skald-Chief's endorsement (or the Skald-Chief's death, which creates a secondary succession crisis)."

She does not command armies. She does not hold territory. She has no Mandate or Military stat. Her power is singular: she decides whether a succession is legitimate. Without her endorsement, a successor governs a faction internally divided about whether they actually have the right to govern. With her endorsement, even a thin claim becomes institutionally stable.

**What "Continuity" means for her:**
She is not trying to preserve any specific leader or bloodline. She is trying to preserve the continuity of the tradition itself — the oral law, the Jarl Assembly's right to validate claims, the principle that Varfell's leaders earn rather than inherit their position. She will endorse a weak claim from an unlikely candidate if that candidate demonstrates the qualities the tradition values. She will reject a strong bloodline claim from someone who has not demonstrated those qualities.

This is more dangerous than it sounds. She is the principle of institutional merit embedded in a culture that nominally values bloodline. She creates space for Maret Uln. She is the reason Maret Uln's succession is even possible.

**Her relationship to Thorvald Hann (Eastern March Jarl):**
Hann advocates bloodline succession. He wants the person with the strongest hereditary claim to be Hochjarl. Stenskald wants the person who best embodies Varfell tradition. These are usually compatible, because strong bloodline claims usually come with strong institutional upbringing. But when they diverge — as they will after Vaynard's eventual elimination — Hann and Stenskald will be on opposite sides. And her vote counts.

### Season Structure

**The Skald-Chief's domain:**
Stenskald does not run Domain Actions. She manages:
- **Annual Jarl Assembly (Ceremonial Scene, 1/year-arc):** She presides over the oral law recitation. This is not an administrative meeting — it is a ritual that reaffirms the entire constitutional structure of Varfell. Every Jarl must attend and affirm the law. Absence is a statement.
- **Claim validation (as needed):** When a succession claim is filed (formal or informal), she reviews it against oral law precedent. This is not a fast process — she researches, consults historical record (maintained in Varfell's oral tradition, memorized rather than archived), and delivers a judgment that is advisory but practically mandatory.
- **Standing consultation:** Any Jarl (Standing 2+) may request her review of an action's traditional legitimacy. This is Varfell's equivalent of Almstedt's constitutional archive review — but it is entirely in her head.

**What she holds that no one else holds:**
The oral law includes approximately 200 seasons of Jarl Assembly precedent that is not written down anywhere. No document contains it. She is the document. If she dies, approximately 40% of Varfell's succession law becomes unverifiable — the Skald-Chief's death triggers a 3-season oral law reconstruction effort (per faction_politics_expanded §1.3c footnote), during which any succession decision is structurally contested.

### Phase 1 (S1–10): The Assembly as Governance

**S1 — First Assembly:**
Stenskald recites the oral law. Every Jarl affirms it. Vaynard is present. This is the moment where she observes him most carefully: does he affirm the law with understanding, or with performance? Her judgment: performance. He respects the ritual because it is politically costly not to. He does not feel it.

She notes this not as a failure — most Hochjарls perform the ritual. But it tells her something about Vaynard's relationship to Varfell tradition: it is instrumental for him, not intrinsic. He uses the tradition's legitimizing power without being committed to the tradition's underlying values. This is relevant when succession comes.

**S4 — Maret Uln's first Assembly:**
Maret Uln is present as Huscarl Captain (Vaynard's inner circle — per faction_politics_expanded §1.3c). She affirms the law. Stenskald observes: Maret Uln responds to the oral law recitation with recognition, not performance. She has heard these words before in community contexts (Southern Einhir oral tradition has parallels to Varfell's oral law — they emerge from the same pre-Calamity culture, now separated by political identity). She is moved.

Stenskald files this observation. Maret Uln's Southern Einhir connection to the oral tradition is not a disqualifying detail — it is an authenticating one. This person's claim to succession would not be alien to the law. It would be a retrieval.

**[FINDING STEN-SIM-01: Stenskald's recognition of Maret Uln's authentic connection to the oral tradition at the S4 Assembly is the earliest possible validation of Maret Uln's eventual succession claim. Long before any formal succession question arises, the Skald-Chief has already formed a positive assessment that is grounded in something real: the candidate's genuine relationship to what the law represents, not just what it enforces. This is what a legitimate succession validator does — she is assessing character and relationship to tradition, not checking boxes. Her judgment, when it eventually matters, will reflect 20+ seasons of observation, not a single formal review.]**

**S7 — Hann's proxy challenge:**
Thorvald Hann (Eastern March Jarl, Standing 4, bloodline-focused) approaches Stenskald privately before the Assembly. He presents a document: a genealogical record tracing a distant Varfell bloodline through a collateral Vaynard kinship line to a named individual he is proposing to position as a succession candidate — not yet formally, but establishing the record. He wants the Skald-Chief's preliminary assessment of whether the genealogical claim is within oral law parameters.

Her assessment: "The genealogical claim is within parameters for a Cadet Claim tier (per LIN-01 equivalents in Varfell oral law). It would require Jarl Assembly majority to confirm, as all Cadet Claims do. I am not in a position to pre-certify candidates."

Translation: the claim is structurally valid. She will not endorse it. She will not reject it. He has established that the bloodline path is open; she has established that it is not automatically preferred. The tension between them is now formal.

### Phase 2 (S11–20): The Question

**S15 — Vaynard's Arc B begins:**
Vaynard's TS has crossed Stirring. His behavior is changing. The Assembly of S15 is the first where Stenskald observes it directly: he affirms the law with slightly different emphasis — he is more interested in the passages about knowledge and its protection than he has previously been. He asks a question after the formal recitation: "Does the oral law address the transmission of knowledge between generations?"

She answers: "The oral law addresses the transmission of authority. Knowledge transmission is addressed through the mentorship protocols, which are connected to the succession structure." He is asking whether the law has anything to say about Thread knowledge transmission. She knows it. She does not know he knows she knows it.

**S18 — Hann presses:**
Hann's candidate (the genealogical bloodline claimant) is being positioned more actively. Hann has secured support from 2 of 5 Jarls Assembly members. He needs 3. The third is contested. He approaches Stenskald again: will she endorse the Cadet Claim formally, giving it institutional legitimacy that might sway the third Jarl?

Her answer is the most important decision she makes: "A formal endorsement from this office requires demonstrated proof that the candidate has engaged with Varfell tradition in ways consistent with leadership. Present me evidence of that engagement and I will review it formally."

She has not said no. She has set a condition. She has also set a condition that Hann's candidate — a genealogical claimant with no established Varfell presence — cannot meet quickly. The condition buys time. Time in which Maret Uln's position strengthens.

### Phase 3 (S21–28): The Succession Validation

**S22 — Vaynard eliminated:**
The succession crisis fires. Hann activates his campaign. Two Assembly votes for his bloodline candidate. The contest is live. Stenskald has to provide a formal ruling within 2 seasons (per SUC-03).

Her formal process:
- Review genealogical claim: Cadet Claim, valid within oral law parameters. Endorseable.
- Review Maret Uln's claim: Deed-Claim with Equity Conviction and demonstrated community leadership. Endorseable.
- Review Assembly precedent: No prior Skald-Chief has chosen between a valid Cadet Claim and a valid Deed-Claim without Assembly vote. This is novel territory.

Her judgment: she endorses NEITHER formally. She rules: "Both claims are within Varfell oral law parameters. The Assembly must choose. I will officiate the formal Assembly determination. This office does not select between equally valid paths — it validates the path the Assembly selects."

Hann is outraged. He expected her endorsement of the genealogical claim to be near-automatic. He did not anticipate that the Skald-Chief would treat a Southern Einhir's Deed-Claim as an equal oral law candidate to a Cadet bloodline claim. This is Stenskald's deepest institutional act: she has interpreted the oral law to include Maret Uln's claim as legitimate on its own terms. She has not endorsed it. She has not blocked the genealogical claim. She has made them equal before the Assembly, which means the Assembly will vote — and Maret Uln, who has cultivated Jarl relationships through her Huscarl role, has more Assembly votes than Hann's external candidate.

## NPC 05 — LENNART HAELGRUND (Ministry Registrar, T4 Grauwald)

### Who He Is

Lennart Haelgrund is the most afraid character in Valoria. His Belief 3: "Something is wrong in the deep records. I do not know what, and I am afraid to find out." TS 12 (hidden, Dormant). Certainty 4 (Questioning — but the questioning is suppressed; it surfaces only in private).

He runs the Ministry at T4 Grauwald. The Ministry is a cross-faction administrative institution — it maintains census records, territorial surveys, maintenance rosters for roads and waterways, and the deep archives that predate the peninsula's current factional divisions. The deep archives go back to the pre-Calamity period. They contain things that no one has read in 200 seasons.

**What the Ministry does:**
- **Routine administration:** Census updates, territorial deed registrations, road maintenance assignments. This is 90% of Haelgrund's work. It is completely uninteresting and extremely important.
- **Deep archive maintenance:** Documents are stored in the lowest level of the T4 settlement's administrative building. Haelgrund has read approximately 30% of the documents in 15 seasons. He does not read them systematically — he reads them when routine administration allows, which is not often.
- **Faction neutrality:** The Ministry serves whoever governs. This is the institution's explicit mandate. No faction can compel the Ministry to take sides. But any faction can compel the Ministry to provide information from its records.

**His TS 12:**
He encountered Thread-adjacent phenomena in the deep archives approximately 4 seasons ago. He found a document — he describes it in Belief 3 as "documents that remember differently." He does not understand what he saw. The document appeared to contain information that updated itself — the census record he was reviewing showed a different number than it had shown on his previous review 2 seasons earlier. Not a transcription error: different numbers in a document that he himself had archived and sealed.

He told no one. He re-archived the document. He has not gone back to the deep archive section that contained it. But he cannot stop thinking about it.

### Season Structure

**The routine:**
Every season: Haelgrund processes approximately 40 administrative documents (census updates, deed registrations, maintenance requests). He reviews 2–3 deep archive documents if time allows. He generates 1 formal Ministry report delivered to whoever is the dominant governing faction in T4 (currently Varfell — the territory is Varfell-held). He maintains strict faction neutrality in what the report contains.

**The problem with neutrality:**
When Vaynard's Tribune Investigate queries the Ministry records (VTM advancement requires data about Thread phenomena in territories), Haelgrund provides complete, accurate data as required. He does not know that what he is providing to Vaynard is also what Vaynard is selling to other factions. He is a neutral conduit being used by an intelligence operation. His neutrality is being weaponized.

When Church's Inquisitor requests Ministry census data on a specific T4 family with Thread-adjacent reported activities, Haelgrund provides the data. He does not know the family will be investigated for heresy based on his records. His neutrality is being weaponized again, differently.

**[FINDING HAEL-SIM-01: The Ministry's neutrality is not a protection — it is a structural vulnerability. Every faction that has access to Ministry records has access to a comprehensive, authoritative, faction-neutral information base that they can use selectively. Haelgrund provides complete data to all requestors equally, which means the faction with the most sophisticated data analysis capability (Vaynard's Tribune network) gains the most from Ministry access. Neutrality produces information asymmetry when factions have unequal capacity to use the same information. Haelgrund's commitment to his mandate is being exploited by people who understand this asymmetry better than he does.]**

### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Routine and the Memory

**S1–5:** Haelgrund's early game is administrative. Census updates. Deed registrations. He is competent, meticulous, and quiet. His Ministry report to Varfell (T4 controller) is accurate and complete. He receives no recognition for this — it is the baseline expectation.

**S5 — The second document:**
While retrieving a pre-Calamity survey record for a deed registration, he finds another document in the same archive section as the first anomalous record. This one: a population census dated approximately 180 seasons ago, which includes a notation he cannot interpret: "Substrate-registered households: 14 (Grauwald east district). Annual assessment required per Warden Protocol 3." He does not know what "substrate-registered" means. He does not know what "Warden Protocol 3" is. He knows the Wardens exist (they are referenced in current administrative records as maintaining a Southernmost presence), but their connection to census registration 180 seasons ago is not explained in any document he has access to.

He archives the document without noting the anomalous notation in his Ministry report. He does not know if it is relevant. He does not know who to ask.

**S7 — Vaynard's Tribune Investigate:**
Vaynard's Tribune queries Ministry records for T4 Thread phenomena reports. Haelgrund provides: maintenance anomaly reports (road sections near the T4 eastern border that require unusually frequent repair — consistent with Thread substrate instability), census data (3 families in the T4 eastern district with multiple members reporting "unexplained perceptual experiences" in the last 10 seasons), and the pre-Calamity survey record (which shows the eastern district has had substrate anomaly documentation for at least 180 seasons).

He provides everything in the same neutral format. He watches Vaynard's Tribune analyst (a functionary, not Vaynard himself) receive the documents and file them. He does not know this data has just significantly advanced Vaynard's intelligence picture of T4's Thread activity.

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Weight of Knowledge

**S12 — The third document:**
He returns to the deep archive. He finds the first anomalous document again — the one with different numbers. He reviews it carefully. He records the current numbers. He seals it and waits 2 seasons.

**S14 — The confirmation:**
He retrieves the document again. The numbers have changed again. Not dramatically — a population count has increased by 3, which is within natural variation. But the document is sealed. Sealed documents do not update naturally. He has confirmed, personally, that this document has changed twice while in his custody.

He writes in his private log: "Document 4-DX-078 (census, Settlement 4, Season 180) has been reviewed three times. Numbers changed between review 1 and review 2, and between review 2 and review 3. The document is sealed. No external modification is possible. I cannot explain this. I have not reported it." He signs the log entry and locks it in the same sealed archive section.

**[FINDING HAEL-SIM-02: Haelgrund has documented empirical evidence of Thread substrate memory — the archives are not just records of the past, they are actively connected to the substrate that constituted the past. The document changes because the Thread substrate that it records changes. This is the most unsettling finding in the simulation because it implies that the Ministry's records are not purely historical — they are live. Haelgrund is maintaining an archive that is tracking something it was not designed to track, and that it cannot interpret. He is the keeper of a sense organ that no one knows is attached to a body.]**

**S16 — Approaching Edeyja:**
His arc condition B requires player investigation to Depth 3+. But as a player character, he initiates it himself: he searches the Ministry records for any reference to the "Warden Protocol" notation. He finds: a single administrative correspondence from 120 seasons ago, filed under "Intergovernmental Communications," from an entity called the "Warden Administrative Coordination Office." The correspondence requests that the Ministry update its Warden Protocol registration annually and forward substrate-monitoring data to the Southernmost Warden Post. The correspondence is signed by someone with the title "Warden-Chief."

He files a formal Ministry inquiry — addressed to "Warden Post, Southernmost" — asking whether the Protocol is still active and whether the Ministry has ongoing obligations under it. He does not know if anyone will receive it. He does not know if the Southernmost still has an administrative address.

The inquiry reaches the Southernmost through trade routes approximately 4 seasons later. Edeyja receives it. It is the most formally correct administrative communication she has received in 30 seasons. She files a formal response.

### Phase 3 (S19–28): The Archivist

**S20 — Edeyja's response:**
The response arrives: "Warden Protocol 3 was suspended 110 seasons ago pending Warden Administrative reorganization. It has not been formally terminated. If the Ministry has substrate anomaly data consistent with Protocol 3 monitoring requirements, the Wardens would receive this data as a matter of historical mandate." Signed: Warden-Chief Edeyja.

Haelgrund compiles 15 seasons of substrate anomaly data from T4 Ministry records (maintenance anomalies, census perceptual reports, the self-updating census document analysis). He sends it to Edeyja. He does not know what he is sending; he knows it fits the Protocol's description of "substrate-monitoring data."

This is a canonical moment: Haelgrund, through routine administrative compliance with a 110-year-old suspended protocol, has become the most consistent source of Territory 4 Thread substrate data that the Wardens have ever had. He did not choose to help them. He chose to follow a procedure. The result is the same.

**Arc C (The Archivist) begins:**
With Edeyja's response confirming that what he has been observing is real and recognized by the people whose job it is to know about it, Haelgrund's TS begins advancing (per arc description: "player investigates deep archives, Haelgrund confronts Thread artifact nature of archives, TS advances to 20+"). His Certainty 4 begins to crack. He knows, now, that the archives are not just historical records. They are Thread-constituted objects. He is managing something he does not understand, and he has formal acknowledgment from the Wardens that what he is managing is real.

He does not tell Varfell. He does not tell Crown or Church. He continues sending data to Edeyja under Protocol 3. The Ministry serves whoever governs. It also, apparently, serves the Wardens under a protocol that predates every current faction.

## NPC 06 — ELSKE (Altonian Hostage, Schoenland Diplomatic Mission)

### Who She Is

Elske appears in §7.10 NPC Stat Values: TS 0, Certainty 3, disrupted by hostage status and competing loyalties. She appears in victory_v30 as the Löwenritter's alternate Regency Establishment condition: "Elske confirmed OR Torben Loyalty ≥ 6." Her Conviction is Order; her RS is Solidarity; her Certainty is 3 (disrupted — meaning she has Certainty 3 by assessment but it is actively eroding from the pressure of her situation).

The victory_v30 Diplomatic Exchange entry: "Senator Outward, Ob 2. Success: IP −10, threshold +5, AER +1, Elske status changes to willing resident. OW: All above + Schoenland Treaty."

She is being held — not violently, but structurally — as a diplomatic asset by the Schoenland mission (which is connected to Altonian imperial interests). Her willing cooperation with a Valorian faction changes her status from "hostage" to "resident" — a diplomatic distinction that carries institutional weight. The faction that achieves Diplomatic Exchange with her has converted an Altonian pressure tool into a Valorian political asset.

**Playing Elske:**
Playing Elske means playing someone whose every relationship is colored by the question: are you building a relationship with me, or with my diplomatic value? She cannot separate these questions. Her Solidarity RS means she is looking for genuine relationship — someone who treats her as a person, not an asset. Her Order Conviction means she is also evaluating: who is constructing a legitimate governance structure here? She will not defect to a faction whose governance she does not respect.

### Season Structure

**The hostage's world:**
Elske has no Domain Actions. She has no faction card. She has access to:
- The Schoenland diplomatic mission's movements (wherever the mission travels, she travels — T16 Schoenland, T1 Valorsplatz during diplomatic occasions, T8 Gransol for commercial negotiations)
- Observations of Valorian political life from the position of an outside party who is educated enough to understand what she sees
- The capacity to form relationships with individuals who approach her appropriately

**What she sees that Valorians don't:**
She sees Valoria from outside its internal political frame. She has no investment in whether Crown or Church or Hafenmark wins. She observes: the peninsula is a post-war society organized around territorial fractional interests, presided over by a Church that claims cosmic authority, facing an existential external threat (Altonian Institutional Pressure) that the factions are too busy competing with each other to adequately address. She finds the whole situation alarming and fascinating in equal measure.

Her Solidarity RS means she is specifically alert to who treats her as a person. The Schoenland diplomatic staff treat her as a tool. The Altonian imperial mandate she represents treats her as a symbol. She is waiting for someone in Valoria to ask her what she thinks.

### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Watching

**S1–4:** Elske observes. Parliamentary sessions (she attends as part of the diplomatic mission's observer status). War Council summons (she is present, as a diplomatic protocol observer). The Löwenritter military reviews she attends with the mission.

Her observations filed in private notes (she writes):

- "The Crown king is tired. He governs well but he is tired of it. I recognize this. My uncle governed the Eastern March for 12 seasons before the fatigue became indistinguishable from surrender."
- "The Löwenritter seem to believe that military discipline is its own form of political philosophy. It is not. Discipline is a tool. What they lack is the thing the tool is supposed to serve."

**S6 — Ehrenwall's Military Review:**
The diplomatic mission attends a Löwenritter military review at T14 Ehrenfeld. Ehrenwall hosts. Elske is present as part of the mission's observer protocol. Ehrenwall delivers a formal address about Valorian sovereignty. 

During a reception following the review, Ehrenwall approaches Elske directly. Not through the mission's diplomatic staff — directly. She says: "You are not Altonian. You are from the Eastern March. That is a different thing." This is accurate. Elske is from the Eastern March, which was absorbed into Altonian political structure two generations ago but retains a distinct cultural identity.

Ehrenwall has done her research. She has treated Elske as a specific person with a specific origin, not as "the Altonian hostage." Elske's Solidarity RS fires. This is the first person in Valoria who has seen her accurately.

**[FINDING ELS-SIM-01: Ehrenwall's approach to Elske is the mechanically correct Solidarity RS engagement. She does not lead with Valoria's interests (that would be Consequence). She does not invoke an authority (that would be Authority). She names Elske specifically and accurately. This is what Solidarity requires: seeing the person. Ehrenwall does this instinctively because her Martial Honour framework values identifying people accurately — it is a form of military intelligence applied personally. **S12 — IP reaches 60:**
Altonian Institutional Pressure 60. Schoenland Priority 1 fires: Altonian Preparation (Vanguard mobilization begins). The diplomatic mission's posture changes. Their presence in Valoria shifts from observation to active pressure: they are now instruments of an imminent threat.

Elske knows this. The diplomatic mission's instructions have changed — she has seen them (she is present at mission briefings as a diplomatic observer). She is now part of an active pressure operation against the peninsula she has been living in for 8 seasons.

Her Order Conviction fires: she evaluates the legitimacy of the imperial mandate she represents. The Altonian Empire's claim on the Eastern March was made through conquest and administered through displacement. She has always known this. Elske's internal evaluation: does she consent? Her Order Conviction: the Löwenritter are building something legitimate. She has watched them for 8 seasons. Their governance has internal coherence. Her Solidarity RS: Ehrenwall has treated her as a person for 8 seasons. This is not transaction. This is relationship.

**Diplomatic Exchange Success:** IP −10 (55). AER +1. Elske status: willing resident. Löwenritter Regency Establishment primary condition: "Elske confirmed" ✓.

**[FINDING ELS-SIM-02: Elske's conversion from hostage to willing resident is the Löwenritter's most important diplomatic achievement. She is reached by being seen accurately.]**

### Phase 3 (S19–28): The Resident

**S18 — What she contributes:**

## CROSS-SIMULATION FINDINGS

### FINDING CROSS-B4-01: Gatekeepers Control Through What They Don't Do

Every character in this batch has more power through withholding than through action:
- Almstedt withholds the constitutional irregularity and holds the entire Hafenmark political structure together by doing so
- Voss withholds the mobile reserve from Löwenritter intelligence, preserving strategic optionality
- Klapp withholds his framework document and his AER causation analysis, keeping both alive without triggering Olafsson's prosecution
- Stenskald withholds formal endorsement from both succession candidates, forcing the Assembly to make the decision
- Haelgrund withholds substrate anomaly reports from his regular Ministry reports, preventing escalation he doesn't understand
- Elske withholds her own mobility until the relationship justifies claiming it

The game's most powerful mid-rank actors are defined not by what they command but by what they refuse to surrender. Withholding is governance.

### FINDING CROSS-B4-02: Institutional Memory Is Catastrophic Risk

Almstedt carries the constitutional irregularity. Haelgrund carries the Thread-constituted archive data. Stenskald carries 200 seasons of unwritten law. Klapp carries the theological framework that could allow the Church to survive Thread revelation. Each carries knowledge that, if deployed at the wrong moment, would shatter the institution they serve. Their custodianship of institutional memory is inseparable from the risk that memory creates.

The game has built, through its named NPC ecology, a set of characters who are each carrying a specific piece of dangerous knowledge that the institution requires them to hold without acting on. The question of when — if ever — that knowledge gets deployed is the deepest strategic question in each character's campaign.

### FINDING CROSS-B4-03: Relationship Is Political Infrastructure

Ehrenwall's 8-season investment in treating Elske as a person produces IP −10 and AER +1 at the mechanical level. At the human level, it produces a willing ally who provides intelligence access to Altonian strategic thinking that no other Valorian faction has. This is not a coincidence — it is the design expressing something true. The characters who invest in genuine relationship rather than transactional relationship consistently receive disproportionate returns from those relationships at moments of crisis. The game rewards seeing people accurately.

## DICE ENGINE NOTE
d10: face 1 = −1, faces 2 to (TN−1) = 0, faces TN–9 = +1, face 10 = +2.
Correct E[per die at TN] = (10−TN)/10 × 1 + 0.1 × 2 − 0.1 × 1 = (11−TN)/10
All calculations in this sim use this formula. Earlier sim results (SIM-001/002) used
a slightly different formula ((11-TN)/10 − 0.1) that undercounts by 0.1/die;
this sim corrects that. Values from SIM-001/002 are directionally valid but
slightly undercount — they remain conservative estimates.

## SIM-004: TTRPG Personal Combat Scenarios

### 004A: Gatehouse — LP Archers vs Heavy Armour Knights
Setup: 3× LP archers (10D TN6 +2), 4× Knights (10D HC, Heavy armour DR5 vs LP)
Wall terrain: Knights must Climb (TN8 Ob1). Hard cover blocks archer retaliation.

Rounds 1–2: Archers fire uncontested. Knights climbing.
  E[succ per archer] = 10 × 0.5 = 5.0
  E[damage per archer] = max(0, 5.0 + 2 − 5) = 2.0 per round
  3 archers: 6.0 E[total damage per round]
  Wound threshold ~3: 2.0 wounds per round from archer fire (not zero — but modest)

Round 3: Knight breaches. Archers defend TN8.
  Archer TN8 defence pool 10D: E[def] = 10 × 0.3 = 3.0
  Knight HC 10D TN6: E[atk] = 5.0
  E[net] = max(0, 5.0 − 3.0) = 2.0 + mod 4 − DR1 (light armour) = 5.0

**Finding 004A-F1:** LP archers deal 2.0 E[damage/round/archer] vs Heavy armour even through DR5.
  Previous sim (SIM-001) underestimated this due to formula error.
  2 rounds × 3 archers = 12.0 total E[damage] on approach. Heavy armour unit ~9-10 HP: likely incapacitated.
  [PROVISIONAL: LP archers ARE effective vs heavy armour over multiple rounds — position-dependent]

### 004B: Skirmish — HP + HBl vs Mixed Armour
HP crossbowman (11D TN5 +4), HBl slinger (11D TN8 +3):
  HP E[succ] = 11 × 0.6 = 6.6
  HP vs Heavy (DR3): E[dmg] = max(0, 6.6 + 4 − 3) = 7.6 per shot (uncontested)
  HBl vs Heavy (DR2): E[dmg] = max(0, 3.3 + 3 − 2) = 4.3 per shot

Round 1: Both fire. Round 2: HP reloads, HBl fires (E[dmg] 4.3). Round 3: HP fires again.
Heavy veteran cumulative E[damage]: 7.6 + 4.3 + 4.3 + 7.6 = 23.8 over 3 rounds
Wounds: 23.8/3 ≈ 8 wounds → incapacitated well before melee.

**Finding 004B-F1:** Combined HP + HBl destroys a Heavy armour veteran in 3 rounds pre-melee.
  HP is the primary damage dealer; HBl supplements during reload cycles.
  Confirmed: this is ranged weapon design intent — "position control is everything."

### 004C: Named Character — Maret Uln (HBl 16D) vs Templar (Heavy armour, 13D)
  E[Maret damage per shot] = max(0, 3.2 + 3 − 2) = 4.2 per round  
  [NOTE: 16D TN8: E[succ] = 16 × 0.3 = 4.8; corrected: dmg = max(0, 4.8+3−2) = 5.8]
  2 shots pre-melee: 11.6 total E[damage] on Templar (Health ~10): incapacitated before melee.
  Maret's high pool (16D) makes HBl devastating even at TN8.

**Finding 004C-F1:** High-Agility HBl skirmisher (Maret archetype) is extremely dangerous.
  At 16D, even TN8 produces 4.8 E[successes]. Combined with +3 mod = substantial damage.
  Counter: Templar must close in 1 round (minimum terrain). On open ground: 1-shot window.
  Terrain is essential for balance at this pool size.

## SIM-005: Mass Combat Scenarios

### 005A: HP Crossbow Unit vs Varfell Heavy Infantry
CP3 HP unit vs Heavy armour (DR3): E[succ CP3 TN6] = 1.5. E[Str loss] = max(0, 1.5−3) = 0.
CP6 HP unit vs Heavy: E[succ] = 3.0. E[Str loss] = 0.
CP7: E[Str loss] = 0.5 (first tier where any damage registers).

**Finding 005A-F1:** HP crossbow UNITS cannot damage Heavy armour at any standard CP.
  Mass combat CP pool (1-7) is too small to overcome DR3. Requires CP7 for any effect.
  Personal HP (11+D pool) is dramatically more effective than unit-scale HP.
  This is correct: mass combat represents abstracted fire volume, not individual accuracy.
  Massed crossbow fire (CP7) does minor damage to Heavy armour — historically accurate.

### 005B: LP Archers vs Levy
LP unit CP4 vs No armour (DR0): E[Str loss] = 2.0 per Volley.
Levy Strength 3 destroyed in 1.5 Volley phases.
**Finding 005B-F1:** LP archers devastate unarmoured/levy infantry. Correct and intended.

### 005C: HBl Sling Unit vs Mixed Armour
  vs Light (DR0): E[Str loss] = 1.5 (CP3)
  vs Medium (DR1): E[Str loss] = 0.5
  vs Heavy (DR2): E[Str loss] = 0
HBl unit effective vs Light armour; marginal vs Medium; zero vs Heavy.
Better than LP vs Light (DR0 vs DR2); worse than HP at all tiers.

## SIM-006: Hybrid Siege — Personal HBl Neutralises Artillery Crew
Maret Uln (16D TN8 +3) vs Artillery crew (unarmoured, DR0):
  E[damage] = max(0, 4.8 + 3 − 0) = 7.8 → crew neutralised in 1 round (Health ~9)
Artillery neutralised = no PP-091 Bombard. Fortification saved −1 Str this turn.
**Finding 006-F1:** Personal HBl is a viable "anti-artillery sniper" archetype in Hybrid mode.
  High-Agility slinger can meaningfully affect siege outcomes via Zoom-In.

## TRANSITION 1: INVESTIGATION → COMBAT + THREADWORK

### Scene: Edda Thread-Reads the Locked Archive, interrupted by Altonian infiltrator

Edda is conducting a Thread-Read (perceptive Leap, §4.5) on the Locked Archive entrance.
Investigation: "What Thread-technical knowledge does the Einhir archive contain?" (Structural, Threshold 8). Evidence Track: 3/8.

**Thread-Read:** Pool 27D, TN 7, Ob 5 (Depth 4 — Liminal, active Thread phenomena).
+1 Ob (RS 55 → Thread ops +1 Ob in Proximity 2 territory) = Ob 6.
P(≥6 net at 27D TN7): E[net] = 27 × 0.33 ≈ 8.9. P(≥6) ≈ 90%.

Most likely: **Overwhelming** (net ≈ 9, ≥ 2×6=12? No — need ≥12 AND ≥3. At E[net]=8.9, P(≥12) ≈ 20%). More likely plain **Success.**

→ Evidence Track: 3 + 2 = 5/8. Co-movement fires (P-01):
- Temporal: History Resonance — Edda perceives the archive's pre-Calamity configuration.
- Epistemic: Certainty already 0, no further movement.
- Actualized: d6=4 → sensory anomaly (the Locked boundary hums audibly).
→ Coherence: Relational scale (the archive is a place-configuration) → −1. Coherence: 6 → 5.
→ Exposure: +1 (Thread-Read) + 1 (sensitive at Depth 4) = +2. Total: 2 (T12).

**Transition trigger:** The audible hum (actualized auto-effect) alerts an Altonian infiltrator hiding nearby. Combat.

### Handoff: Fieldwork → Combat (per §2.3)

Thread-Read resolves first (done). Combat begins. Edda was in Thread contact (Leap) — the Leap ends on interruption. Edda is vulnerable (Leap vulnerability window was active).

**[FINDING TW-01]: Leap vulnerability window at transition.** When combat interrupts a Thread-Read (perceptive Leap), the Leap was active when the interruption occurred. Per combat_design_v1.md §4: "A practitioner declaring Leap (Priority 5) may be struck at Priority 1 before contact." The Thread-Read has already resolved (co-movement already fired), but the practitioner is still in contact state. The Leap vulnerability window should end when the Thread-Read resolves. If the interruption occurs BEFORE resolution (mid-Leap), the practitioner is struck during vulnerability. If AFTER resolution (as here — the audible hum alerted the infiltrator after the Thread-Read completed), the vulnerability window has closed.

**Ruling:** Edda's Thread-Read resolved before combat began. Vulnerability window closed. Combat proceeds normally.

**Combat:** Edda vs Altonian infiltrator (estimated: Agi 4, End 3, Str 3, Combat Pool 13D, Short Light Blade TN 5, Light armour).

Edda is a poor fighter (Combat Pool 12D, Staff TN 7). She has two options:

**Option A: Fight normally.** Pool 12D, staff. Defensive stance. Likely loses in 2-3 exchanges.

**Option B: Thread operation during combat.** Edda can attempt a **Dissolution** on the infiltrator's weapon (Object scale). This would destroy the weapon. TN 8, Ob 4 (Object scale FR), Pool 11D.

P(≥4 net at 11D TN8): E[net] = 11 × 0.2 = 2.2. P(≥4) ≈ 10%. **Very unlikely to succeed.**

**Option C: Weaving during combat.** Weave a defensive Thread shield (Object scale). TN 7, Ob 1, Pool 18D. P(≥1) ≈ 94%. Much more viable. +1 armour equivalent for one exchange.

Edda chooses Weaving (defensive). But this requires a Leap (Priority 5 full-round action). The infiltrator attacks at Priority 1 before Edda's Leap resolves.

**Infiltrator attacks Edda:** 13D (aggressive split: 9D Off / 4D Def), TN 5. E[net Off] ≈ 9 × 0.5 = 4.5. Edda Defence: 0D (she declared Leap — all combat allocation goes to Leap). Edda takes full hit.

Damage: net hits (≈4-5) + Str 3 + weapon mod (Light Blade vs None = +3) = ≈10-11 damage. Edda Health 9. **Wound at 9 damage.** Health resets. Excess carries. Edda takes 1-2 Wounds.

**[FINDING TW-02]: Thread operations during combat are extremely dangerous for practitioners.** The Priority 5 Leap action means any declared attacker strikes first. A practitioner who chooses Thread ops over combat defence will take damage. This is by design (combat_design_v1.md §4 priority table), but the fieldwork system should note: "When combat interrupts fieldwork, a practitioner may attempt Thread operations per normal combat rules. The Leap action (Priority 5) exposes the practitioner to attack at Priority 1. Combat Thread operations are a high-risk choice — physical defence is sacrificed for Thread capability."

**Outcome:** Edda takes 1 Wound (−1D all physical fieldwork). If she survives, her Weaving resolves. If Overwhelming: +1 TS (73). Evidence from Thread-Read retained. Investigation continues at 5/8.

### Thread Operations Between Exchanges (§9.4)

Between exchanges, Edda may initiate a Thread operation. Effects apply before next exchange's Appraise step.

**Option: Past-Oriented Pulling on the Inquisitor's argument.**
If the Inquisitor cited a historical precedent, Edda could POP to displace the cited event's temporal configuration.

**Requirements check:** TS 70+ (72 ✓). RS ≤ 60 (55 ✓). The RS gate is met.

**POP target:** The Inquisitor cited "the Burning of Ehrenfeld, Year -47" as precedent for archive destruction. Edda POPs to displace the temporal configuration of the Burning — making the cited past unstable.

Pool: 14D (Spi 6 + Hist 5 + TPS÷2 = 3). TN 8. Ob: depends on recency.
Year -47 = approximately 50 years ago. Recency band: 10+/generational. Ob: 7.
+1 Ob (RS ≤ 60 territory modifier) = **Ob 8.**

P(≥8 net at 14D TN8): E[net] = 14 × 0.2 = 2.8. P(≥8) ≈ **0.1%. Near-impossible.**

**[FINDING TW-04]: POP during Contest is mechanically near-impossible.** At generational recency and RS ≤ 60 territory modifier, Ob 8 against a 14D TN8 pool has < 1% success chance. POP within Contest is a theoretical possibility but not a practical one. This is correct — POP is campaign-altering and should not be casually deployable during a debate.

**Even at same-scene recency (Ob 3 + 1 = Ob 4):** P(≥4 at 14D TN8) ≈ 18%. Still dangerous, and co-movement fires:

**POP co-movement consequences (if Success at same-scene recency):**
- RS cost: −3 minimum (POP Success). + temporal auto-effect: 0 (same scene). Total: RS 55 → 52.
- Coherence: −1 (Relational scale, assuming the debate subject is relational) + −1 (POP additional) = −2. But cap = −1 per op. So −1. Wait — PP-196/ED-103: FR surcharge exempt from cap. POP additional is also exempt? Let me check.

Per params_threadwork.md §Coherence: "FR Lock or Dissolution (any scale): −1 additional" and "Past-Oriented Pulling: −1 additional." The −1 additional for POP is listed alongside FR surcharge. ED-103 exempts FR surcharge from cap. **POP additional is also listed as a separate line item.** The cap text says "−1 max per single operation regardless of combined sources." But the FR exemption (PP-196) says FR surcharge is "exempt from §3.2 cap." POP is not FR — it is a separate operation type.

**[FINDING TW-05]: POP Coherence cost ambiguity.** Is POP's −1 additional exempt from the per-operation cap (like FR surcharge) or subject to it? Current text is ambiguous. POP is not classified as FR (Forced Resolution). FR = Lock and Dissolution only. POP is a distinct operation.

**Proposed ruling:** POP −1 additional IS subject to the per-operation cap. Total Coherence loss from a POP at Relational scale: −1 (scale) + −1 (POP additional) = −2, but capped at −1. This keeps POP less destructive than Dissolution (which as FR IS exempt from cap). POP is dangerous for RS and temporal disjunction; Dissolution is dangerous for Coherence and Gaps. Different cost profiles.

**Edda's Coherence if POP fires:** 5 → 4 (Fragmented). −1D social/Recall, +1 Ob all ops.

### Effect on Contest (§9.4 / ED-104)

Per ED-104 (resolved PP-199): "If POP displaces debated event: disputed party loses Memory bonus for displaced claims. Debate continues (no restart). Opposing party may invoke displacement as Present-genre argument. Temporal Disjunction: −1D Argue for characters arguing from displaced past (one exchange)."

If Edda POPs the Burning of Ehrenfeld:
- Inquisitor loses Memory genre bonus (+1D) on the exchange that cited the Burning.
- Edda may invoke the displacement as a Projection-genre argument.

- Church observers with TS ≥ 30: they perceive the POP. Heresy Investigation may follow (CI trigger per epistemic auto-effect — requires Church formal investigation action, not automatic).

**[FINDING TW-06]: POP in a Church Tribunal triggers immediate CI consequences.** The epistemic auto-effect (params_threadwork.md §Epistemic Auto-Effect): "A TS 30+ Church observer witnessing a Thread operation does NOT automatically trigger CI increase." BUT a Church Tribunal already has Church actors present and investigating. The formal investigation condition is met by the Tribunal itself. POP during a Church Tribunal: CI +1 at next Accounting is near-certain (the Church IS investigating — that's what the Tribunal is).

**Combined effect:** POP during Church Tribunal = RS −3+, Coherence −1, self-incrimination, CI +1, and the POP itself has <1-18% success chance. This is a desperation move that accelerates every negative clock simultaneously.

## TRANSITION 3: INVESTIGATION → MASS BATTLE + THREADWORK

### Scene: Battle erupts while Edda investigates the archive interior

Crown and Varfell forces clash over control of Sigurdshelm. Mass battle (Company scale, ~100 soldiers per side). Edda is in the archive (underground — effectively Reserve).

**Edda declares Phase 4 Thread operations** (rear practitioner — safe Leap window).

### Option A: Locking the Archive

Edda attempts to Lock the archive entrance to prevent either army from accessing it during battle.

**Lock target:** Relational/Process scale (the archive's relationship to its surroundings — the entrance configuration).

Pool: 11D (Spi 6 + Hist 5, NO TPS). TN 8. Ob: 6 (Relational/Process scale Lock).
+1 Ob (RS ≤ 60 in Proximity 2 territory) = **Ob 7.**

P(≥7 net at 11D TN8): E[net] = 11 × 0.2 = 2.2. P(≥7) ≈ **0.01%. Functionally impossible.**

At Object scale (just the door): Ob 4 + 1 = Ob 5.
P(≥5 at 11D TN8): E[net] = 2.2. P(≥5) ≈ 1%. Still near-impossible.

**[FINDING TW-07]: Locking during mass battle is near-impossible at these pool sizes.** The Lock pool (Spirit + History, NO TPS) at TN 8 produces E[net] = 2.2 for a high-Spirit practitioner. Even Object-scale Lock (Ob 4) has only ~5% chance without the RS territory modifier. This is by design (params_threadwork.md §Territorial+ Locking Design Note): "Territorial Locking is near-impossible by design... practitioners cannot lock strategic-scale configurations." But even Object-scale is very difficult at TN 8 without TPS.

**Edda's best Lock attempt:** Object scale, Ob 4 (no territory modifier — the archive is underground, arguably sheltered). P(≥4 at 11D TN8) ≈ 5%.

**If Lock succeeds (5% chance):**
- RS: −1 (Lock Success) + TS 72 discount (−1 FR RS cost, min 1) = −0. RS unchanged? No — "−1 to all FR RS costs (min 1)" means the −1 cost becomes −0... but minimum 1. So RS −1. RS: 55 → 54.
- Coherence: −1 (FR surcharge, exempt from cap per PP-196) + 0 (Object scale = 0 auto-cost) = −1. Coherence: 5 → 4.
- The archive entrance is Locked. Neither army can enter. Domain Echo: territory control does not include archive access.
- Chronic drift: Lock at Object scale = 0 RS drift/season. Minimal long-term cost.

**If Lock fails (95% chance):**
- RS: −3 (Lock Failure). RS: 55 → 52.
- Coherence: −1. Coherence: 5 → 4.
- 2 Wounds. Edda at Wounds 2-3 (if prior wound from combat).
- Adjacent ops +1 Ob rest of season. All subsequent Thread operations this season harder.

### Option B: Dissolution on Altonian infiltrator's Thread-Locked pendant (from prior sim)

If the Altonian infiltrator is present in the archive during battle, Edda could Dissolve the Thread-Locked pendant to destroy its communication capability.

**Dissolution target:** Object scale (one item). Ob: 4 + 1 (RS territory) = **Ob 5.**
Pool: 11D. TN 8. P(≥5 at 11D TN8) ≈ 1%.

**If Dissolution succeeds (1% — near-impossible):**
- RS: −3 (Dissolution Success at Object) + TS 72 discount = −2. RS: 55 → 53.
- Gap forms: closes end of scene (Object Success).
- Coherence: −1 (FR surcharge exempt from cap) + 0 (Object scale) = −1. Coherence: 5 → 4.
- The pendant is destroyed. Altonian intelligence link severed. Domain Echo: IP pressure may reduce (Altonia loses a communication node).

**If Dissolution fails (very likely):**
- RS: −8 (Dissolution Failure). RS: 55 → 47. **Into deeper Fragile band.**
- Full Gap forms. Monstrous Incursion check.
- Practitioner incapacitated. Edda is down for the remainder of the battle.
- Coherence: −1. Coherence: 5 → 4.

**[FINDING TW-08]: Dissolution during mass battle is campaign-altering.** PP-201 already established: "E[RS per Dissolution attempt at TS70] = −18.4." Even at Object scale, the expected RS cost across all outcomes is devastating. During a mass battle, the consequences cascade: Gap + potential Monstrous Incursion + practitioner incapacitation during an active battle. The mass battle ×3 RS multiplier does NOT apply to FR costs (it applies only to operation-table degree outcomes for standard ops), but the base costs are already extreme.

### Option C: Past-Oriented Pulling during mass battle

Edda attempts to POP the archive's pre-Calamity configuration — pulling the archive's Thread state to a time before the entrance was sealed by the Church.

**Requirements:** TS 70+ (72 ✓). RS ≤ 60 (55 ✓).

**Target recency:** Pre-Calamity. Generational (10+ seasons — actually centuries). Ob: 7.
+1 Ob (RS territory) = **Ob 8.** Oh wait — the archive was sealed by the Church post-Calamity, not pre-Calamity. If Edda is pulling to the configuration BEFORE the Church sealed it (maybe 20-30 years ago), that's 80-120 seasons. Still generational. Ob 7 + 1 = Ob 8.

Pool: 14D. TN 8. P(≥8 at 14D TN8) ≈ 0.1%. **Impossible in practice.**

Even at 3-5 seasons recency (Ob 5 + 1 = 6): P(≥6 at 14D TN8) ≈ 0.4%.

**[FINDING TW-09]: POP in mass battle context is mechanically impossible at any serious recency.** The pool (14D TN8) with generational Ob (7-8) produces near-zero success probability. POP during mass battle would require: recent target (same scene, Ob 3), no RS modifier, and even then P ≈ 12%. This is consistent with POP's design as a campaign-altering operation performed under carefully prepared conditions, not a battlefield tool.

### Mass battle Thread ops interaction with investigation

The Thread operations during Phase 4 interact with the fieldwork investigation:
- If Edda performs a Thread-Read (intelligence) instead of offensive/FR ops: Evidence Track advances. No RS cost (Thread-Read is perceptive). Co-movement fires at lower severity.
- If Edda performs Lock/Dissolution: the investigation is SECONDARY. The Thread operation's consequences dominate the scene. Evidence Track freezes during FR operations (the practitioner's attention is on the operation, not perception).

**[FINDING TW-10]: FR operations suppress fieldwork.** During Lock or Dissolution, the practitioner cannot simultaneously Thread-Read (both require active Leap contact, and FR operations demand full concentration). A practitioner in a mass battle turn must choose: intelligence gathering (Thread-Read, advances Evidence Track, low cost) OR offensive/defensive Thread ops (Lock/Dissolution, no Evidence progress, extreme cost). This is an important strategic choice.

**Proposed rule:** "A practitioner may perform one Thread operation per battle turn (Phase 4 or Phase 6). Thread-Read and combat/FR Thread operations are mutually exclusive within a single turn. The practitioner chooses intelligence or action."

## TRANSITION 4: COMBAT → INVESTIGATION + THREADWORK

### Scene: After fighting off the Altonian infiltrator, Edda examines the archive interior

Edda survived combat (1 Wound). She is inside the Locked archive. The archive contains Einhir Thread-technical inscriptions at Depth 4 (Liminal — active Thread phenomena).

### Threadwork as investigation tool

Rather than Thread-Read (which she already performed), Edda attempts to **Pull** a specific inscription — temporarily opening its Thread configuration to perceive its full content.

**Pulling:** The inscription is "firmly actualised" (pre-Calamity, stable for centuries).
Pool: 18D (Spi 6 + Hist 5 + TPS 7). TN 7. Ob: 3 (Firmly actualised) + 1 (RS territory) = Ob 4.
P(≥4 at 18D TN7): E[net] = 18 × 0.33 = 5.9. P(≥4) ≈ 82%.

**Pull Success:**
- RS: 0 (Pull Success). No RS cost.
- Coherence: 0 (Personal scale, 0 auto-cost).
- The inscription's Thread configuration opens. Edda perceives the full Einhir technical content.
- Duration: surplus 2+ → persists until next seasonal accounting.
- Evidence Track: 5 + 2 = 7/8 (Success).

**This is the optimal Thread-investigation combination.** Pulling to reveal Thread-locked content is mechanically sound: moderate Ob, standard TN, full TPS in pool, low RS/Coherence cost. The inscription remains Pulled (open) for the season, allowing re-examination.

**[FINDING TW-11]: Pulling is the investigation-optimal Thread operation.** Compared to Lock/Dissolution/POP, standard Pulling has: full pool (Spirit + History + TPS), standard TN 7, 0 RS cost on Success, 0 Coherence at Personal/Object scale. It is the only Thread operation where a practitioner can advance an investigation without significant mechanical risk. This creates a clear investigative hierarchy: Thread-Read (perceptive, low cost) → Pulling (revelatory, moderate cost) → Locking (preservative, high cost) → Dissolution (destructive, extreme cost) → POP (temporal, near-impossible).

### Wounds affecting threadwork

Edda has 1 Wound. Per §2.2 of fieldwork: "Physical wounds apply to fieldwork rolls that require physical exertion." Thread operations are not physical exertion — they are consciousness-level operations. But combat_design_v1.md says "Wounds: −1D per wound (cumulative)" for combat rolls.

**[FINDING TW-12]: Wound penalty scope for Thread operations.** Thread operations use Spirit + History + TPS, not physical attributes. Wounds represent physical damage. Should wounds penalise Thread operations?

The Foundations establish that Thread operations are consciousness-performed (P-03). Physical wounds should not directly penalise Thread pools. However, pain and injury could impair concentration.

**Proposed ruling:** "Physical wounds do not apply −1D penalty to Thread operation pools. Instead, wounds apply +1 Ob to Thread operations requiring Leap (the physical discomfort makes sustaining contact harder). This parallels how Rattled applies +1 Ob to social actions — the penalty is to concentration, not to the pool."

Edda at 1 Wound: Thread ops +1 Ob. Pulling at Ob 4 → Ob 5. P(≥5 at 18D TN7): E[net] = 5.9. P(≥5) ≈ 68%. Still viable but noticeably harder.

```
Evidence Track: 7/8. RS: 55. Pulling succeeded — inscription open.
Investigation nearly complete.
FINDINGS: TW-11 (Pulling is investigation-optimal), TW-12 (Wound → Thread Ob, not pool penalty).
```

## TRANSITION 5: DEBATE → INVESTIGATION + THREADWORK

### Scene: After the Tribunal, Edda uses POP to recover erased testimony

The Church Tribunal concluded with the Inquisitor ordering the archive destroyed.
During the Tribunal, a key witness (Brother Aldric) was about to testify about the archive's
contents but was silenced by the Cardinal. Aldric's unspoken testimony is now lost.

Edda performs a **Past-Oriented Pull on Brother Aldric's memory** — pulling the temporal
configuration of Aldric's consciousness to the moment before he was silenced, to perceive
what he was about to say.

### POP on a person's memory

**Requirements:** TS 70+ (72 ✓). RS ≤ 60 (55 ✓).

**Target:** Aldric's memory of what he intended to say. Recency: same session (the Tribunal
just concluded). Personal scale (one person's memory configuration).

Pool: 14D. TN 8. Ob: 3 (same session) + 0 (Personal scale already in Ob, and P-09 says
memory pulling produces orphaned configuration). No RS territory modifier (Edda and Aldric
are in a private space, not the Calamity-affected field).

Actually, P-09 says: "Memory pulling displaces the configuration, producing an orphaned configuration. The absence is detectable by TS 30+ Diagnosis. Co-movement applies." Memory pulling is a specific application of Pulling, not POP. But if Edda is pulling Aldric's memory to a PAST configuration (what he was about to say before being silenced), that IS temporal — it's pulling a memory to a prior state. This is POP territory.

**Ob: 3 (same session recency).**

P(≥3 at 14D TN8): E[net] = 14 × 0.2 = 2.8. P(≥3) ≈ 42%.

This is the first **viable** POP in the entire simulation. Same-session recency makes POP achievable.

**POP Success:**
- RS: −3 minimum (POP Success). RS: 55 → 52.
- Temporal auto-effect: 0 (same session — no temporal stress RS cost).
- Coherence: −1 (Personal scale = 0 auto-cost, but POP additional = −1). So −1. Coherence: 5 → 4. **Fragmented.** −1D social/Recall, +1 Ob all ops.
- Edda perceives: Aldric's memory configuration at the moment before silencing. She experiences what Aldric intended to say — a description of the archive's contents that matches her Thread-Read findings but in non-Thread language (ontical, not ontological).
- **Evidence tag: Thread-verified.** Edda perceived Aldric's memory through POP.
- Evidence Track (if investigation reopened from Tribunal evidence): +2 progress.
- **P-09 consequence:** The POP displaces Aldric's memory configuration. An orphaned configuration is produced. Aldric now has a subtle gap in his memory — he cannot quite remember what he was going to say. The absence is detectable by any TS 30+ observer.

**POP Failure (58% chance):**
- RS: −6 (POP Failure). RS: 55 → 49. **Deeper into Fragile band.**
- Snap-back: 1 Wound. Edda at Wounds 2.
- Coherence: −1. Coherence: 5 → 4.
- Aldric's memory is disturbed but not recovered. The orphaned configuration still forms (the attempt itself displaces). Aldric loses the memory fragment permanently. Evidence lost.

**[FINDING TW-13]: POP on recent memories is the viable POP use case.** Same-session recency (Ob 3) is the only practical POP target. At 42% success, it is a genuine coin-flip with severe consequences either way. This creates the correct dramatic tension: the practitioner is gambling RS and Coherence for information that may be the only way to recover lost evidence. The fail-forward implication: even on failure, the orphaned configuration is itself evidence (detectable by TS 30+) — proof that someone attempted to retrieve the memory, and proof that the memory existed.

### POP evidence feeding back into investigation

If POP succeeded, the recovered memory is Thread-verified evidence. It feeds the investigation:
- Evidence Track: 7 + 2 = 9/8. **Threshold exceeded.**
- Investigation resolves at Depth 4 (Edda's Thread perception accessed Liminal content through Aldric's pre-silenced testimony + Thread-Read).

The Thread-verified evidence is Inert Knowledge for non-sensitives. Torsten receives Edda's account of what Aldric intended to say, but cannot act on the Thread-level components with precision. The Documentary evidence (from earlier) remains the admissible core.

## EMERGENT SYSTEM PICTURE

The threadwork × fieldwork interaction produces a clear **investigative hierarchy** that was not explicitly designed but emerges from the mechanical costs:

```
INVESTIGATION TOOL          RISK         EVIDENCE VALUE    USE CASE
Thread-Read (perceptive)    Low          +2 progress       Standard deep investigation
Pulling (standard)          Low-Medium   +2 progress       Revealing Locked/frozen content
Pulling (Past-Oriented)     High         +2 progress       Recovering lost/displaced evidence
Locking                     Very High    0 progress        Preservation (defensive, not investigative)
Dissolution                 Extreme      0 progress        Destruction (offensive, not investigative)
Mending                     Medium       0 direct          Prerequisite for deeper access
```

**The hierarchy maps perfectly to the Intelligibility Gradient.** Lower-risk operations access lower depths; higher-risk operations access deeper or more contested content. The fieldwork Depth axis and the threadwork cost axis are parallel scales expressing the same metaphysical principle: deeper engagement with the substrate costs more because the substrate resists rendering at depth — not because the ground has agency (P-07), but because finite consciousness strains against the infinite.

**The most striking emergent property:** Dissolution and Locking are NOT investigation tools. They produce 0 Evidence Track progress. They are defensive/offensive tools that happen to interact with investigation contexts. The fieldwork system correctly distinguishes between understanding the world (Thread-Read, Pulling) and changing the world (Locking, Dissolution, POP). Understanding is cheaper because it does not alter the substrate; changing is expensive because it violates the substrate's constitutive structure.

This distinction — perception vs operation — is the fieldwork system's deepest philosophical alignment with the Foundations. P-03 says "rendering is consciousness-performed." Thread-Read is rendering at depth. Locking/Dissolution is not rendering — it is restructuring. The cost differential mechanically expresses this ontological distinction.

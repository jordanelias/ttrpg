## Exchange 2: Baralta Evidence vs Himlensendt Consequence

Same dynamic. Baralta cannot change attribute or pool. She switches to marshalling legal precedent (Evidence — correct style, no penalty).

Baralta 8D vs Himlensendt 12D. Expected nets: 2.6 vs 4.0.
Most likely: Himlensendt wins again. Strain to Baralta: +1. Composure 10→9.

**Klapp decision point:** He can intervene here with W-41 (Conviction Anchor) on Baralta's leading Belief: *"My authority in Hafenmark is granted directly by God."*

**W-41 activation cost:** Klapp must take the Leap (forfeits his Observer action; he has no exchange action in this scene — he is an observer, not a debater). The Leap is a non-action act in observer capacity; GM call: Klapp can perform the Leap between exchanges as a Priority 5 action outside the exchange turn structure. Timing: fires at the start of Exchange 3.

**Klapp's W-41 roll:** 11D TN7 vs Ob2. P(success) = 84% (from reference table).
Retention roll: Spirit(3) + History(2) + TPS(3) = 8D vs Ob2. P(retain Coherence) = 71%.

Most likely: W-41 succeeds. Baralta's leading Belief gains +1D to resist rhetorical challenge for remainder of Debate.

**W-41 mechanical value in this Debate context:**
Himlensendt is NOT directly challenging Baralta's Belief about divine authority — he is arguing Consequence (future Church jurisdiction). W-41 applies when the leading Belief is directly challenged rhetorically. In this Debate, Himlensendt's Consequence arguments are not Belief-targeted attacks.

**Finding F-20 [P2]:** W-41 is mechanically irrelevant again — not because the Debate is too short, but because Himlensendt is NOT using Inspiration attacks (he hasn't identified Baralta's Inspiration list). W-41 protects Beliefs against rhetorical challenge; Himlensendt is arguing policy, not character. The mechanic requires the opponent to use Inspiration attack mode to become relevant.

**Revised W-41 assessment:** W-41 is correctly scoped — it counters Inspiration-attack tactics specifically. Against a policy debate (Consequence style), it has zero effect. This is a design feature, not a bug. But it means W-41 has a very narrow activation window (only when opponent is running Character style + Inspiration attacks).

## Exchange 4: Neutral audience, both continue

Audience now Neutral: no modifier for either side.
Baralta 8D vs Himlensendt 11D (no audience bonus now). Expected nets: 2.6 vs 3.6.
Himlensendt advantage reduced. P(Himlensendt wins): ~57%.

Most likely: Himlensendt wins by 1. Strain to Baralta: +1. Composure 8→7.

**Baralta at Composure 7:** Rattled threshold is Composure = 0 (Rattled triggers when strain first equals Composure). Wait — re-read: "Rattled triggers the moment accumulated strain first equals or exceeds Composure."

Total strain accumulated: Exchange 1: +1, E2: +1, E3: +1, E4: +1 = 4 total strain.
Baralta Composure: 11. Rattled at strain ≥ 11. Current strain: 4/11. Not close to Rattled.

**Clarification:** Composure is the threshold, not current — strain accumulates until it equals Composure. Baralta starts at Composure 11; she can absorb 11 strain before Rattled. At 1 strain/exchange, she'd need 11 exchanges to Rattle. Grand Debate is only 5 exchanges. She cannot be Rattled in this Debate unless Himlensendt lands Overwhelming wins (+2 strain each).

**Finding F-21 [P2]:** Composure 11 makes Baralta functionally un-Rattleable in a 5-exchange Grand Debate even if she loses every exchange. The Rattled threshold is only relevant in extended or multi-stage social encounters. For standard Grand Debates (5 exchanges), Composure 10+ characters are immune to Rattled. This is likely by design for high-status NPCs, but means Composure and the Rattled mechanic are strategically irrelevant at Grand Debate scale for senior NPCs.

- Klapp shifted audience disposition: if anyone noticed (TS≥50 observers?), this is an observable Thread event. No TS≥50 observers in Parliament except Klapp himself (TS31 < 50). Undetected.
- Klapp CE: +2 (performed Thread operation in politically sensitive context; counts as "direct witness to Thread operation" for CE — self-referential but the CE track measures exposure risk). CE: 4→6. [This triggers the TS growth check threshold at CE 3+.]
- At CE 4+ with TS31: TS growth check (Spirit TN7 Ob1). Spirit 3 → pool 3D. P(≥1 net) ≈ 70%. Most likely: TS growth. Klapp's TS advances toward 50.

**Finding F-22 [P3]:** Klapp's covert Thread intervention in Parliament is undetectable (no TS≥50 observers) but triggers his own CE and TS growth. The head of the Church's educational apparatus is developing Thread sensitivity while helping Baralta — an irony the system produces without editorial intervention.

# VALORIA — Alternate Branch Simulations
**Date:** 2026-04-17  
**Token:** 9d103fd60a86f599  
**Scope:** For each major mechanical branch point across Batches 1–5, choose an alternate result and simulate forward. These are not "what if the dice rolled differently" — they are "what if the character made a different decision at a genuinely contested moment." The purpose is to reveal the game's dependency structure: which decisions create divergent world-states, and how far the divergence propagates.  
**Format per branch:** Original decision → Alternate decision → World-state consequences → New findings.

## BRANCH A — ALMSTEDT DEPLOYS THE CONSTITUTIONAL IRREGULARITY
*(Original: Batch 4. Almstedt seals the supermajority amendment finding and never raises it.)*

**Original:** Almstedt certifies Baralta's Dynastic Assertion parliamentary vote 3-1, noting privately that he chose not to adjudicate the foundational amendment question from the Chair.

**Alternate:** At the certification session, Almstedt raises Article 12. Before the vote, he states from the Chair: "I am obligated to note that the certification of this Dynastic Assertion rests on a parliamentary framework whose enabling amendment — the Session 440 Amendment to Succession Protocol — was ratified by simple majority when Article 12 of the Founding Charter required supermajority ratification. The validity of this proceeding depends on a foundational legal question I cannot resolve from the Chair. I recommend suspension pending a formal constitutional review."

### Immediate Consequences (S22)

**Baralta's response:** She needs 3 of 4 inner circle votes to override the Chair's suspension recommendation. Legal Advisor Heljason, privately: "He's right. I told you the Article 12 risk existed." She calls for an immediate Parliamentary vote on whether to proceed despite the Chair's recommendation. Coalition: 3-1 in her favor (she has the votes). But overriding the Chair's constitutional recommendation is itself a constitutional action — it requires not a simple majority but, under Almstedt's own precedent, the same supermajority that Article 12 mandates. She cannot override him with the same mechanism he has just invalidated.

**Parliamentary deadlock:** The certification is suspended. Parliament convenes an extraordinary session. Agenda: is the Session 440 Amendment constitutionally valid? The debate takes 2 seasons.

**Faction responses to Hafenmark's suspension:**
- **Crown:** Almud's Schattendienst (Thale) files immediately: "Hafenmark's Dynastic Assertion certification suspended on constitutional grounds. Crown's TCV position unchanged. Almud's Treaty network still active." He gains 2 seasons while Hafenmark is in procedural suspension.
- **Varfell:** Vaynard files the suspension in his intelligence archive. His Path B is unaffected; his Path C continues.
- **Church:** TC advances 2 seasons without Hafenmark suppression (Baralta's Suppress action is Domain Action-based, not tied to parliamentary proceedings — she continues suppressing). But her diplomatic capital is consumed by the constitutional crisis.
- **Guilds:** Annika Feldhaus (inner circle) to Baralta: "The constitutional suspension has created trade uncertainty in T3 and T5. Guild Forum arbitration requests have increased 40% this season." Commercial friction from political uncertainty.

**The 2-season review (S23–24):**
The Parliamentary review produces three positions:
1. **Almstedt's position:** The amendment is constitutionally invalid. Restore the supermajority requirement. All parliamentary decisions made under the 40-season amendment require retroactive supermajority confirmation or formal grandfathering by a new validly-passed supermajority amendment.
2. **Baralta's position:** De facto validity doctrine applies. 40 seasons of institutional acceptance constitutes constitutional validation. Continue under current framework.
3. **Heljason's position (Baralta's Legal Advisor, private):** Both positions are defensible. But de facto validity is the weaker doctrine. He tells Baralta: "If Almstedt's position prevails, your Dynastic Assertion cannot be certified without a valid constitutional amendment framework. That requires a new supermajority amendment explicitly enabling Dynastic Assertion certification. You need 2/3 of Parliament. You have 3/4 — 75%. The supermajority requirement is 2/3 — 67%. You have enough."

**The resolution:** Baralta moves a new supermajority amendment explicitly enabling Dynastic Assertion certification under the original Article 12 supermajority framework. The amendment passes 3-1 (75%). Almstedt certifies it: the new amendment is constitutionally valid because it was passed by the required supermajority. The original Session 440 amendment is formally grandfathered by this new supermajority action.

Baralta's Dynastic Assertion is re-certified 2 seasons late. She wins — but she has burned 2 seasons of political capital and created a permanent constitutional record that the original certification was flawed. Any future challenge to Hafenmark parliamentary decisions now has a documented precedent: Article 12 matters, and the 40-season amendment was not unassailable.

**[FINDING ALT-A-01: Almstedt's deployment of the constitutional irregularity does not prevent Baralta's victory — it delays it by 2 seasons and forces her to achieve a genuinely valid supermajority certification. The alternate path produces a constitutionally stronger result for Hafenmark than the original path: the Session 440 issue is now formally resolved rather than quietly sealed. Paradoxically, Almstedt's challenge strengthened the outcome he might have hoped to prevent. The institutional integrity he defended produced a more legitimate version of the thing he was questioning. This is the constitutional scholar's deepest lesson: the system that can absorb a foundational challenge is stronger than the system that suppresses one.]**

**Long-term cascade:**
- Almstedt's role in Hafenmark Parliament is now unambiguous: he is the Chair who raised the challenge. His institutional authority is at its historical peak (Baralta's need for his certification is now explicit and documented).
- Heljason's private counsel to Baralta during the crisis becomes part of the permanent record: he identified the supermajority solution. His position in the inner circle is secure.
- Any future faction (Crown, Church) attempting to challenge Hafenmark's constitutional authority now faces a parliamentary framework that has been explicitly tested and hardened.

## BRANCH B — VOSS DEPLOYS THE T6 RESERVE
*(Original: Batch 3/4. When the Coup fires and Kreutz stands down the Royal Guard, Voss recalls the T6 mobile reserve rather than deploying it.)*

**Original:** Voss observes Kreutz's standing-down of the Royal Guard, understands this as an Order judgment parallel to his own, and recalls the reserve. Managed Transition: Stability −1.

**Alternate:** Voss reads Kreutz's standing-down as a betrayal of the dual chain. "He had personal allegiance to Almud. He chose the Order instead. That is not the same thing." He deploys the T6 reserve — 2 units, 1 day's march from T1. They arrive at T1 the following morning. Pre-positioned Riskbreaker squads are at T1 and T14. Military engagement.

### Battle at T1 (S22)

**Military calculation:**
- Voss's reserve: 2 units, Mobile, Combat strength equivalent to Military 4 (Crown standard)
- Löwenritter pre-positioned Riskbreakers: not military units — 2 Rb-Std 3 Squad-Leaders with 3-person squads each. Covert operatives, not a garrison force.
- Ehrenwall's primary Löwenritter force: at T14 Ehrenfeld, 1 day away.

**Round 1:** Voss's reserve reaches T1. Riskbreaker squads are present but not a standing army. The Riskbreaker Commander (Rb-Std 6) has authority to authorize tactical operations but not to commit to an open battle against 2 military units. He orders extraction: squads disperse into T1's civilian structure.

**Result:** No battle. The Riskbreakers were never a military force — they were a precision-insertion capability for a controlled transition. Against 2 military units with military authority, they dissolve. Voss has secured T1 without combat.

**Ehrenwall's response:**
She receives the report: Riskbreaker insertion extracted, T1 under Voss's reserve control, Kreutz's Royal Guard standing down is now isolated (Crown military force is present; Löwenritter cannot proceed without military engagement). She faces: commit Löwenritter Military to a direct engagement with Crown's reserve (Coup Counter 3 was supposed to produce a political transition, not a battle), or stand down.

Her Martial Honour Framework: "Advancing personal/factional gain at Valoria's expense" carries +2 Ob. A battle at T1 that she initiated would be exactly this — personal gain (the Coup) at the expense of Valorian Stability. She cannot do it without her own Framework penalty.

She stands down. Counter 3 does not produce a Coup. It produces: a demonstration that the Coup is structurally impossible while Crown maintains military readiness. Coup Counter resets? No — the Counter at 3 represents accumulated grievances, not a single action. Ehrenwall does not get a second attempt; the Counter at 3 is a permanent record that she judged Crown's failures sufficient to warrant action. What it does not do is guarantee successful execution if Crown's military is prepared.

**[FINDING ALT-B-01: Voss's deployment reveals that the Löwenritter Coup's structural assumption is a Crown military vacuum. The Riskbreaker insertion is designed for a scenario where Crown's military chain has stood down (Kreutz) or is not operationally deployed. It is not a military operation — it is a political insertion that depends on the absence of military resistance. A prepared reserve at T1 makes the Coup mechanically impossible without an open battle that Ehrenwall's own Framework prevents her from initiating. The lesson: the Coup Counter is a political tool; military preparation is its check. Voss had the check all along and chose not to use it. Using it preserves Crown — and reveals that Ehrenwall was always betting on Voss making the same choice Kreutz made.]**

**Post-crisis world-state:**

Almud survives. The Coup Counter at 3 remains in the permanent record — Ehrenwall assessed Crown as having genuinely failed Valoria. Crown knows this. Almud knows Ehrenwall would have moved. The political relationship between Crown and Löwenritter is now explicit: Ehrenwall tried. Almud survived because of Voss, not because of Kreutz.

**Almud's response to Voss:** He is alive. He knows why. The next War Council session: "You maintained a reserve I didn't know about. Why?" Voss: "Because I anticipated a scenario where knowing about it would change your behavior." Almud: "And if I'd known, what would I have changed?" Voss: "You would have believed the political solution was available. You would have invested less in the political solution." This is the most honest conversation Almud has had with any inner circle member.

**Torben's position:** Torben's Loyalty to Crown (which would have transferred to Löwenritter in the Managed Transition) does not transfer. Torben's loyalty to Crown remains with Crown. This is significant: the legitimate succession path (Torben → Crown → legitimacy) is intact. Baralta's Dynastic Assertion is now in direct competition with an intact Crown succession. The Partition co-victory path (Church + Hafenmark) requires Crown Mandate ≤ 1. Crown Mandate is not ≤ 1 — Almud has survived.

**Ehrenwall's arc post-failure:** Counter at 3, Coup failed. Her Autonomy secondary fires: the Order has demonstrated the limits of its approach. She cannot internally re-justify the Counter mechanism when it produced no result. She doesn't dissolve the Order — she recalibrates. The Löwenritter becomes genuinely protective of Valorian sovereignty (its stated mission) rather than a Coup-readiness organization. Voss gets a letter from Ehrenwald: "Your reserve was well-placed. I would have done the same." A professional acknowledgment from an opponent who respects military competence. The Löwenritter will not attempt the Coup again in this campaign.

## BRANCH C — VAYNARD TAKES PATH B AT SEASON 9
*(Original: Batch 2. Vaynard holds Path B conditions available and continues toward Path C.)*

**Original:** Path B conditions are met at S9. Vaynard does not claim the win. He continues toward Path C (Thread Supremacy, S16).

**Alternate:** At S9 Accounting, Vaynard instructs the Jarl Assembly: "Varfell asserts its victory under Path B conditions." TCV 8 ✓, T4 ✓, T13 ✓, VTM 3 ✓, WR 2 ✓. Victory is declared at Season 9.

### What a Season 9 Victory Means

**Game-state at S9:**
- TC: 36 (Church is mid-campaign, Pontifex-Assert fix has slowed it)
- Crown TCV: 12 (Almud has built treaties but not yet marched on T8)
- Hafenmark TCV: 7 (Baralta has not yet run her Grand Contest)
- RS: 62 (declining, not yet critical)
- IP: 8 (low, no major battles yet)

**The victory's political meaning:**
A Varfell win at S9 means the campaign ends with 3 factions having never activated their primary victory conditions. Church has not reached TC 65. Crown has not reached TCV 14. Hafenmark has not achieved Crown Mandate ≤ 3. The game ends while every other major faction is mid-strategy. From their perspective: the ground shifted under them while they were building toward a destination 15 seasons away.

**Vaynard's specific achievement:**
Path B — Southernmost Dominion. He wins by holding T4 and T13, building WR, and advancing VTM. He has not conquered the peninsula. He has claimed dominion over the Southernmost corridor — a geographic and Thread-strategic position, not a political one. He is the Hochjarl whose reach extends to Edeyja's Warden network. This is a different kind of victory than Crown's institutional hegemony or Baralta's constitutional supremacy.

**[FINDING ALT-C-01: A S9 Varfell victory reveals that Path B is a genuinely different class of win condition from the others. It requires the smallest TCV (≥ 8 vs. Crown's ≥ 14 or Hafenmark's ≥ 13), fires from an already-held territorial position, and can be achieved before the major political configurations of the campaign have even formed. The design implication: Path B is a speed run that ends the campaign before factions' primary strategies mature. This should be a conscious design choice — is a S9 victory appropriate tension? If the answer is "yes, it's a legitimate fast win," then the campaign pacing around Path B needs to explicitly acknowledge that other factions are 15 seasons from their conditions at the moment Varfell can win. If "no," then WR advancement should be gated differently.]**

**Baralta's response to the S9 victory:**
She has just run her Grand Contest (S8 in standard timeline), achieved Mandate 5, and is positioning for the TCV Proclamation chain. The Varfell victory announcement arrives: the campaign is over. Her response, in character: she files a formal Parliamentary motion contesting the victory conditions on jurisdictional grounds. "Varfell's Path B includes WR ≥ 2 — Warden Recognition is an acknowledgment by Edeyja, who is not a Valorian political institution. A non-political actor's acknowledgment cannot constitute a formal victory condition."

This is legally interesting. Almstedt reviews it. His assessment: the WR track is a mechanic internal to Varfell's faction design — it represents Varfell's relationship with the Wardens, not a formal political recognition. Baralta's challenge has no constitutional standing. The victory stands.

But her challenge creates the first parliamentary precedent on what constitutes a valid victory condition — which means future campaigns have a canonical reference for contesting WR-based wins.

**Almud's response:**
"Varfell wins by S9. I had TCV 12, 2 more seasons of Treaty building, and T8 was available. If Varfell hadn't held T13 from Baralta's Season 1 Proclamation attempt, Path B would have been blocked and the campaign would have run another 15 seasons." He is right. The Season 0 Jarl Assembly garrison of T13 is the single most consequential decision in the campaign. He files this assessment with the Schattendienst for future campaign planning.

**What the S9 world-state looks like:**
- Church TC 36: never reaches 65. Himlensendt's religious authority remains intact, unchallenged, and somewhat frustrating — the campaign ended before his arc reached its confrontation.
- Crown TCV 12: Treaty network half-built. Almud's succession engineering for Torben is incomplete (S18 target not reached).
- Hafenmark Mandate 5: Baralta has one Grand Contest win but has not yet achieved Crown Mandate ≤ 3.
- RS: 62. Declining but not critical. The existential Thread crisis never materializes — Varfell's win stabilizes the Southernmost corridor enough that the decline rate slows.

The most interesting consequence: **Himlensendt never reaches Arc B or Arc C.** The campaign ended before his faith was tested. He governs a stable, successful Church institution that never confronted the evidence that would have transformed it. Whether this is a better outcome or a worse one depends entirely on what one values: institutional stability or honest reckoning.

## BRANCH D — KLAPP PUBLISHES THE FRAMEWORK DOCUMENT
*(Original: Batch 4. Klapp writes "On the Nature of Substrate Revelation in Solmund's Creation" and keeps it in his personal archive.)*

**Original:** The document remains unpublished. Klapp continues managing the Inquisitorial buffer and AER maintenance without making his theological position explicit.

**Alternate:** At S16, Klapp submits the document to the Dicastery for Doctrine and Archives (his own Dicastery) for formal theological review and publication. He frames it as an academic document, not a doctrinal revision: "A scholarly contribution to the understanding of Solmund's Creation, drawing on documented substrate phenomena from the archival record."

### The Publication Chain

**S16 — The document enters formal review:**
Klapp's Dicastery receives it. He has chaired this process for 11 seasons; the review committee is his own staff. The document passes Dicastery internal review in 1 season (S17). It is formally submitted to the College of Prelates for theological review.

**S18 — Olafsson reads it:**
Cardinal Olafsson receives the document. He reads it in a single session. His assessment: the document argues that Solmund's texts are "incomplete" — which formally constitutes a doctrinal deviation. The claim that Thread phenomena represent "a deeper layer of Creation that Solmund's revelation gestures toward without fully specifying" is not heresy by narrow definition (it does not deny Solmund), but it is a doctrinal claim that Church authority has never endorsed, and in the Church's Divine Command framework, unapproved doctrinal claims are presumptively heretical until explicitly authorized.

He files a formal doctrinal complaint. The subject: Cardinal Klapp, for publication of unapproved theological doctrine.

**The proceeding against Klapp (S18–19):**
This is not an Inquisitorial proceeding — it is a College proceeding. Olafsson's complaint is addressed to Himlensendt (who chairs the College in formal theological disputes). But Himlensendt is at Scar 2 by S18. He has privately already moved toward Klapp's position (Certainty 2, his own Faith cracking). He is being asked to adjudicate a heresy complaint against the one Cardinal whose theology he privately agrees with.

Himlensendt's decision: he does not rule immediately. He constitutes a College review committee. The committee: Himlensendt, Olafsson, Tormann. Klapp and Jarnstal are excluded (Klapp as subject, Jarnstal as having a military stake in the outcome).

**The College review (S19):**
Tormann (Prudence) reads the document from a fiscal perspective: does acknowledging Thread substrate phenomena create Church institutional liability? His assessment: "If Thread phenomena are real and the Church has been suppressing knowledge of them, then Church treatment of Thread practitioners creates significant retrospective institutional exposure. The document argues for acknowledging phenomena — which is fiscally preferable to continuing denial that could become historically indefensible." He votes: not heretical, scholarly contribution.

Olafsson: heretical. Thread reality acknowledgment is a destabilizing doctrinal revision.

Himlensendt's casting vote: he reads the document carefully. He writes a 3-page response that he gives to no one. Then he votes: "Not heretical. Scholarly contribution to theological understanding. Publication authorized under Dicastery jurisdiction."

**[FINDING ALT-D-01: Himlensendt's casting vote authorizing Klapp's document is the single most consequential institutional act in Church history since the Calamity. It is effectively the Confessor endorsing a doctrinal position that Thread reality exists as a deeper layer of Solmund's Creation. This does not reverse the Church's position on Thread persecution — it creates a theological framework within which that position becomes harder to maintain consistently. Olafsson's heresy charges against Thread practitioners now have to contend with an authorized document arguing that substrate phenomena are part of Solmund's Creation. The document is not a weapon against the Inquisition. It is an argument that the Inquisition has been fighting the wrong thing.]**

**Cascade effects:**

**Church Stability −2:** Olafsson makes the vote public. The College is divided. Senior Inquisitors receive conflicting signals: continue prosecuting Thread practitioners under existing precedent, or accommodate the Confessor's new authorized doctrine? The Inquisitor Ladder's operations slow as case officers seek clarification. AP accumulation in contested territories pauses (Inquisitors are not deploying pending resolution). AER: unexpected +1 (Heresy Investigation pause reduces AER-generating events).

**TC: −1 per season for 3 seasons** (Church is in institutional crisis; Assert requires Mandate ≥ 3, and the internal crisis puts Stability pressure on Mandate).

**Vaynard's response:** He receives a copy of the document through his Tribune Investigate chain. He reads it overnight. His Certainty 2 (already questioning) drops to 1 (Certainty movement from exposure to a Consequence + Evidence combination that directly addresses his secondary Belief). He contacts Klapp: "This document describes what I have been experiencing for 8 seasons without terminology for it." Klapp's response: "Would you be willing to contribute observational data to a follow-up scholarly monograph?" The most unlikely academic collaboration in Valoria's history begins.

**Baralta's response:** She invokes the Sovereign Authority Doctrine immediately: "Church doctrinal disputes do not affect the constitutional legal status of Thread practitioners in Hafenmark territory. The authorized document creates no obligation or precedent in Hafenmark's civil jurisdiction." She is protecting the document from being weaponized as a Church territorial claim — the document could theoretically be read as justifying Church authority over Thread phenomena everywhere. She closes that reading before it opens.

**Edeyja's response:** She receives the document through the Warden network (Haelgrund's Protocol 3 channel). She reads it in the Southernmost. She files a formal Warden assessment: "The document is accurate within its limitations. The 'deeper layer of Creation' framework is compatible with the Warden's operational understanding of the substrate. Recommend formal dialogue." This is the first time the Wardens have formally acknowledged a Church-produced document as compatible with their worldview in living memory.

## BRANCH E — THALE WARNS ALMUD
*(Original: Batch 3. Thale contacts Ehrenwall to negotiate transition terms rather than warning Almud about the Coup preparation.)*

**Original:** Thale negotiates the Schattendienst's institutional continuity with Ehrenwall. She protects her people; the Coup proceeds as Managed Transition.

Cost: the Vaynard intelligence agreement is at risk of discovery, her Niflhel source is revealed, her unauthorized contact with Vaynard becomes visible.

### S22 — Almud Informed

**Almud's immediate response:**
He has one operational day before the Coup is scheduled to proceed. He calls an emergency War Council (Voss, Kreutz, Royal Guard assembled). The briefing: Riskbreaker operatives are pre-positioned. The Coup is hours away.

Kreutz's position: "If we resist, the Coup becomes a military confrontation. I was going to stand down the Guard — I believed the Managed Transition was better for Valoria than a fight." Almud: "And now?" Kreutz: "Now I know you know. My personal allegiance is to you. I follow you." Kreutz's Order Conviction resolves in Almud's favor when Almud's informed consent is possible.

**The military situation:** With Voss's T6 reserve deployed (per Branch B analysis) AND Kreutz's Royal Guard not standing down: the Coup's preconditions are absent. The Riskbreaker insertion dissolves on contact with organized military resistance (per Branch B analysis).

**But Thale's revelation cost:**
- **Niflhel source burned:** Her Dockmaster contact learns that Thale used information from their relationship to warn Crown. Niflhel Priority 2 fires (Exposure threat): Quiet Sabotage mode against Crown. Schattendienst Exposure events spike (+1 per season for 3 seasons). Crown's intelligence operation becomes noisier.

His response is not dismissal — it is a re-evaluation of what his Spymaster has been doing for 8 seasons. "You made better decisions than I would have authorized. But you made them without authorization." Thale: "Yes." Long pause. "Continue making better decisions. With authorization from now on." Her position is secure. Her Riskbreaker network has an infiltration at a level she had not anticipated.

**[FINDING ALT-E-01: Thale warning Almud produces the strongest possible Crown outcome (Coup defeated, Almud survives, Kreutz is explicitly loyal, Voss's reserve is validated) at the highest possible personal cost (Niflhel source burned, Vaynard agreement exposed, Ehrenwall learns the network is compromised). The Spymaster's dilemma is real: warning Almud is the highest-loyalty action and the highest-cost action simultaneously. The game asks: does the Spymaster serve the institution or the person? Thale in the original chose the institution (Schattendienst continuity). Thale in the alternate chose the person (Almud's survival). Both choices are defensible under Autonomy Conviction. The expected-value calculation depends entirely on what you value — and that is exactly where a character's Conviction actually matters.]**

**Post-Coup-failure world-state:**

**Almud's arc:** He does not need Arc A now. Arc B (Fortress) is not triggered by stability failure — Crown survived. He continues his TCV expansion with awareness that Ehrenwall nearly succeeded. His Order Conviction firms: "The institution held because the right people were in the right places. Maintain the positions." He accelerates Torben's Standing advancement (explicitly: Torben must be ready if something similar is attempted again).

**Ehrenwall's arc post-failure:** Counter at 3, Coup failed, network compromised. She needs to know how the compromise happened. Her Riskbreaker Commander's investigation reveals: the Dockmaster contact was a dual-service asset (Niflhel and Schattendienst simultaneously). This is not a failure of Riskbreaker discipline — it is a failure of intelligence analysis. The Riskbreakers used a Niflhel transit contact without verifying that contact's other service relationships. This is the Riskbreaker Commander's professional failure.

His self-assessment: the Riskbreaker Commander authorizes his own demotion to Rb-Std 5 (Deputy Commander level). Not publicly — internally. He runs a succession process for the Commander role. He is done. The next Commander will verify all transit contacts independently.

## BRANCH F — HELJASON REFUSES THE RESTORATION BRIEF
*(Original: Batch 5. Heljason drafts the restoration argument at Baralta's request, recording privately that he would not sign it as accurate.)*

**Original:** He drafts it. Baralta uses the framing. The certification proceeds with the constitutionally questionable framing embedded.

**Alternate:** At S14, when Baralta asks him to draft the restoration framing, he says: "I won't write this one. I can write you a version of the Dynastic Assertion that is historically accurate and legally defensible. I cannot write a restoration argument because restoration implies an original Baralta-lineage governance that did not exist. If you want a brief that will survive extended scrutiny, I give you the accurate one. The restoration framing doesn't survive. I'm telling you before you need a different lawyer."

### Baralta's Response

She could: dismiss him (find a less scrupulous advisor), override him (write it herself or commission another scholar), or accept his assessment.

Her Precedent Conviction: "Constitutional procedure IS justice." She has, in 14 seasons of working with Heljason, internalized that his judgment on constitutional defensibility is her most reliable check. If he says the brief doesn't survive, it doesn't survive.

She accepts the accurate framing: Dynastic Assertion through accumulated constitutional authority, Mandate superiority, and documented governance interest in T3 (the thin precedent Almstedt certified). She does not use "restoration."

**The Grand Contest (S11 in standard timeline, shifted to S13 here):**
Crown's attorney in the Grand Contest raises the jurisdiction question about T3. Heljason is Baralta's advocate. He deploys the thin-but-real precedent correctly: "The 70-season-old commercial survey establishes documented Hafenmark administrative interest in the T3 trade corridor. Our claim is not restoration — it is accumulated constitutional authority from documented governance interest. The restoration framing would be historically inaccurate. Our claim is stronger than that." 

Crown's attorney had prepared counter-arguments for the restoration claim (which is historically weakest). He has not prepared counter-arguments for the "documented administrative interest" claim because it is legally more defensible. He asks for adjournment to prepare. Almstedt grants 1 season.

**[FINDING ALT-F-01: Heljason's refusal produces a stronger legal argument than compliance would have. The restoration framing was a narrative convenience that made the Dynastic Assertion easier to explain publicly but easier to challenge legally. The accurate Deed-Claim framing is less rhetorically satisfying but legally superior. The alternate path reveals that the most consequential thing Heljason does in the entire campaign is the brief he refuses to write — because refusing forces Baralta to use the argument that actually works. The advisor's highest-value function is sometimes obstruction rather than execution.]**

**Long-term effect:**
Baralta's eventual certification (S22 or equivalent) rests on the accurate Dynastic Assertion framing. When Almstedt raises Article 12 (in the alternate branch where he does so), Heljason's prepared response — which already anticipated the constitutional challenge — is ready. The 2-season delay is shorter than in Branch A because Heljason has been building the supermajority argument since S13 rather than scrambling at certification time. The combination of Heljason's accurate framing and Almstedt's constitutional rigor produces the cleanest possible Dynastic Assertion victory.

## BRANCH G — HAELGRUND REPORTS THE THREAD-CONSTITUTED ARCHIVES

The Ministry's formal oversight authority is "whoever governs" — in T4's case, Varfell. The incident report goes to Vaynard's inner circle. Maret Uln (Huscarl Captain, Council of the Warden) receives it first, because the Council of the Warden has jurisdiction over Thread-related matters.

She reads the report. TS 35 (Active tier): she immediately understands what she is reading. A census document that self-updates is a Thread-constituted archive — an object that is materially connected to the substrate it records. This is more significant than Vaynard's Discovery Events. The Ministry is maintaining a living connection to the Thread substrate of T4.

She goes to Vaynard: "The Ministry's deep archive in T4 contains Thread-constituted objects. Not Thread-sensitive artifacts — Thread-constituted primary documents. The census records are not observing the substrate; they are made of it." Vaynard's response: his TS advances with the information (every confirmation that Thread reality is real and immediate accelerates his arc). He files it as VTM-adjacent data: the Ministry's archives are a Thread knowledge resource he had not identified.

**Church's response (when it reaches them via Linder's channel):**
Linder reports the Ministry incident to Himlensendt: "Varfell has received a Ministry report of self-updating census documents in T4. The Ministry's administrative language describes 'unexplained anomaly.' Based on my understanding of recent Varfell Thread-research activity, I believe this is Thread-substrate related." Himlensendt forwards to Klapp. Klapp receives it at exactly the moment he is writing his theological framework document. ## CROSS-BRANCH FINDINGS

### FINDING CROSS-ALT-01: Information Timing Is the Campaign's Primary Resource

Across every alternate branch, the factor that most changes outcomes is not military strength or TCV — it is who knows what, when.
- **Thale warning Almud** (Branch E) vs. contacting Ehrenwall (original) produces different military outcomes because Almud's informed response time determines whether resistance is possible.
- **Almstedt deploying the irregularity** (Branch A) vs. sealing it determines whether Hafenmark wins with institutional uncertainty embedded or fully resolved.
- **Haelgrund's report** (Branch G) vs. silence determines whether Thread-constituted archives enter multi-faction intelligence chains or remain a single character's private knowledge.
- **Heljason's refusal** (Branch F) vs. compliance determines whether Baralta's legal case is strong or brittle.

In every case, the critical variable is the timing and routing of information disclosure. The character who controls information flow controls the campaign.

### FINDING CROSS-ALT-02: Premature Victory Creates Its Own Pathology

Branch C (Vaynard S9 Path B) and Branch I (accelerated Coup at S17) both show that winning before the board is ready for you produces victories whose consequences are structurally incomplete.

Vaynard wins at S9 before Himlensendt's arc reaches confrontation, before Crown's succession is secured, before the Thread crisis materializes. He wins the faction game while the campaign's deeper questions are unanswered.

Ehrenwall's S17 Coup wins before Torben is ready, before Elske's cooperation is secured, before the Regency conditions are established. She governs through Military Consolidation — effective but narrow.

**Both premature victories produce stable but shallow worlds.** The institutions function. The existential questions remain unresolved. The characters who would have transformed those questions (Himlensendt's arc confrontation, Torben's mature authority, Edeyja's collaboration) never reach their transformation points. The "winner" has claimed the mechanical victory while the narrative question is still open.

### FINDING CROSS-ALT-03: The Irreversibility of Certain Decisions

Several alternate branches produce changes that cannot be undone by subsequent play:
- **Almstedt deploys the irregularity** → Article 12 is now a live institutional question. Future campaigns must address it.
- **Klapp publishes the framework** → "On Substrate Revelation" is permanently in the Church doctrinal record. Any future Confessor must respond to it.
- **Haelgrund reports the archive anomaly** → Ministry archives' Thread-constituted nature is institutional knowledge. Niflhel pursues the archives as an intelligence asset.
- **Hann gets Holdar's vote** → Maret Uln's succession defeat means the Varfell oral law precedent for Deed-Claims is NOT established. Future succession questions revert to bloodline preference by default.

The alternate paths reveal which decisions are truly irreversible: the ones that produce institutional precedent. Document published. Legal record created. Intelligence disclosed. These cannot be recalled. The game's most consequential decisions are the ones that create facts for future campaigns.

## PART I — BARALTA: THE DYNASTIC CLAIMANT

### Victory Architecture
**Primary (Dynastic Assertion):** TCV ≥ 13 + Mandate ≥ 4 + Stability ≥ 3 + PI ≥ 5 + Crown Mandate ≤ 3  
**Alternate (Full Dynastic):** TCV ≥ 12 + Crown Mandate ≤ 1 + hold T1 Valorsplatz + Mandate ≥ 5 + Torben Loyalty ≤ 3  
**Starting TCV:** 6 (T8 Gransol=3, T10 Spartfell=1, T7 Rendstad=1, T17 Halvarshelm=1). **Gap: +7 TCV needed for Primary.**  
**Acquisition tool:** Dynastic Proclamation (Diplomat card) — claims territory as Baralta's hereditary right. Requires Hafenmark Mandate > target controller Mandate. New mechanic (ED-583): Grand Contest decisive win → Mandate +1 (cap 6).

The prior simulation treated Baralta as a parliamentary proceduralist defending constitutional order. This is wrong. She IS constitutionalism — not as an end but as the weapon she wields to legitimize claims that are, at root, about sovereign power. Her Dynastic Assertion says it plainly: she wants Crown's territories, she wants Crown's Mandate crushed, she wants T1. Parliament is one of several tools. Her Frame of Categorical Imperative means she uses procedure — but "procedure" in the service of dynastic supremacy.

**Track 3 — Church Bullying and Ecclesiastical Coercion**  
Baralta needs TC < 50 or at least Church not dominating her territories (which would block her PI ≥ 5 victory condition — a Church-dominated parliamentary climate suppresses PI). Her tools against Church are not just Suppress — they are coercive.

**The Sovereign Authority Doctrine:** Hafenmark's Legal Advisor drafts a binding constitutional document asserting that Church authority in Hafenmark territories is subordinate to Hafenmark civil law. This is not a Domain Action — it's a Scene-layer product (Fieldwork: Investigation, 4 scenes, player or delegation). Once complete: Church Heresy Investigations in Hafenmark territories require Hafenmark Parliamentary authorization. Church AP management in Hafenmark = blocked without Baralta's consent. Church's most efficient TC advancement tool (AP → Inquisitor deployment → PT spread) is structurally impeded in Hafenmark territory.

**Excommunication Immunity:** When Church attempts Excommunication of Baralta (inevitable at TC 65): Hafenmark Parliament votes to nullify. Parliamentary vote: 3-1 in Baralta's favor (coalition maintained). Result: Church gains TC +4 (Excommunication fires regardless of nullification) but Baralta retains Mandate. Church has damaged her. She retaliates: Parliamentary Censure of Church immediately following (−1 Church Mandate). The coercive exchange: Church attacks → Hafenmark counter-attacks. This is not passive constitutional defense — she's punching back every time.

**Church economic coercion:** The Guild Comptroller (Baralta's inner circle member) manages economic relations with Guild trading networks that include Church-territory commerce. Baralta can authorize the Guild Comptroller to impose trade restrictions on Church-territory merchants — not a formal Domain Action but a Scene action that reduces Church's Wealth yield in affected territories. Church Mandate has a Wealth floor (Wealth below 2 = institutional credibility loss). If Church Wealth drops to 1, their next two Domain Actions are each at +1 Ob (resource constraint).

This is coercion through an institutional intermediary. Not a roll — a Scene action that creates an Obligation on Himlensendt to respond.

## PART II — VAYNARD: THE EXPANSIONIST

### Victory Architecture
**Path A (Intelligence Hegemony):** TCV ≥ 10 + VTM ≥ 3 + 2 rivals fully revealed + 1 territory outside starting 4  
**Path B (Southernmost Dominion):** TCV ≥ 8 + hold T4+T13 + VTM ≥ 3 + WR ≥ 2  
**Path C (Thread Supremacy):** TCV ≥ 10 + VTM ≥ 4 + RS ≥ 50  
**Starting TCV:** 6 (T12=3, T13=1, T11=1, T4=1). Already holds T4 and T13 (Path B's territorial condition is met from Season 1 if he can keep them).  
**Acquisition tool:** Cultural Reformation (Colonist card) — ideological territory transfer at PT ≤ 2.

The prior simulation reduced Vaynard to "Thread researcher." He is, in fact, an intelligence hegemon (Path A), a military expansionist (Path B requires military to hold T4/T13 against pressure), and a strategic manipulator who uses knowledge as leverage, not just as personal curiosity. His Consequentialist Framework means every action is evaluated by measurable outcomes within one season. He is the most efficient strategist on the board.

### Three Paths in Parallel

**Path A — Intelligence Hegemony Play**  
VTM advancement via Tribune Expedition: each successful Expedition adds 0.5–1 VTM (approximated from prior sim). At VTM 3: Cultural Reformation unlocked (TC −1 per OW).

Fully revealing rival factions requires VTM 2+: Tribune Investigate → stat revelation. Two rivals' complete stat profiles revealed = "2 of the 3 other playable factions." Priority targets:  
- Church: revealing Himlensendt's Conviction weakness enables Vaynard to advise other factions on optimal approaches. Information as a diplomatic commodity.  
- Crown: revealing Almud's Schattendienst resources enables Vaynard to assess whether Crown can detect Varfell operations.  
- Hafenmark: revealing Baralta's Mandate/Wealth tells Vaynard when Proclamation threats are imminent.

VTM as blackmail lever: at VTM 3, Vaynard knows enough about each faction's internal state to make concrete intelligence-backed demands. He is not selling raw information — he is holding the threat of information release over faction leaders. Crown Marshal's corruption (MIN-03 equivalent: Vaynard's Tribune finds it) → "Don't March on T11 or this becomes public." This is how intelligence hegemony works.

**Territorial expansion (Path A's +1 territory condition):**  
Starting outside territories = 0 (all 4 starting territories). Needs at least 1 more. Options:  
- Cultural Reformation on T3 (Crown, PT low from Thread phenomena): 6D + VTM bonus vs. Ob PT+1. At PT 2: Ob 3. P(OW, territory transfer): ~45%. T3 is Crown. Acquiring it shifts TCV 6→8.  
- March on T15 Askeheim (Uncontrolled, TCV 0): Military 4 vs. Ob 1 (uncontrolled). Near-certain. No TCV gain but strategic position for Path B.  
- Cultural Reformation on T6 Stillhelm (Crown, lower PT from Restoration presence): at PT 1: Ob 2. 7D (Military 4 + VTM 2 bonus): P(OW): ~65%. Aggressive option.

**Path B — Southernmost Dominion Play**  
T4 and T13 are already held. Path B is actually Vaynard's easiest path structurally — he just needs to HOLD what he has and advance WR while accumulating VTM 3.

**WR track (0–4):** Warden Recognition. How it advances:  
- Expedition to Southernmost region: Tribune Expedition action targeting T15/Southernmost → WR +1 per success.  
- Edeyja's cooperation (Arc B trigger): If Edeyja enters Arc B (player-initiated or world-state forced), Warden Recognition advances automatically +1.  
- Vaynard providing resources that wardens need: Scene-layer supply chain (Wealth −1) → WR +1.  
- **Blocked if WR returns to 0 after first advancing past 1.** This means any hostile action against wardens destroys Path B permanently. Vaynard CANNOT attack or harm the Southernmost while pursuing Path B.

**WR management vs. Thread Supremacy tension:** Path C requires RS ≥ 50. If Vaynard's Thread research (VTM advancement via Tribune Expedition in Thread-active zones) damages RS (Thread Tension accumulation), he may simultaneously be advancing VTM (toward all 3 paths) while undermining Path C's RS condition. His Consequentialist Framework demands he measure this tradeoff precisely: VTM advancement rate vs. RS drain rate.

**Path C — Thread Supremacy Play**  
VTM ≥ 4 (harder than Path A/B's VTM ≥ 3). RS ≥ 50 (currently 72 at start — but declining). This is the path where Vaynard's Thread research serves a strategic win condition rather than personal knowledge.  
RS protection: Vaynard must invest in AER maintenance (community Thread discipline reduces RS drain). His VTM track includes a "VTM Discretion" action (at VTM 3+): suppresses TC contribution for one season. If he also uses this to manage RS-impacting Thread operations, he's running a conservation strategy, not just a research program.

**The RS ≥ 50 requirement in a declining RS environment:**  
At the campaign's S30, if RS has been declining at the average rate identified in prior sims (~−1/season from Thread operations, −1 from Niflhel quiet deployments, occasional Lock events): RS at S30 could be as low as 42 (from 72 at S1). **This makes Vaynard's Path C a conservation mission, not an extraction mission.** He must advance Thread knowledge while net-protecting Rendering Stability. This is Edeyja's job — Vaynard poaching it.

If 3 successful Reformations in one Accounting: Crown TCV drops dramatically, Varfell TCV jumps. Crown Institutional Mandate trigger fires: Crown MUST respond next season with Domain Actions targeting Varfell. Vaynard has forced Crown onto defense while simultaneously advancing VTM (each successful Reformation = VTM contribution).

**[FINDING FAC-SIM-07: Cultural Reformation is Vaynard's hidden economic weapon. It doesn't cost Military or Wealth — it costs Tribune cards and Influence. ### §A — THE GUILDS: ECONOMIC DOMINANCE

**Status:** The Guilds do not have a standalone victory condition in victory_v30 — they are a support/co-victory faction. Playing as the Guilds means playing for maximum leverage position when a major faction wins, ensuring the winning faction OWES the Guilds.

Guilds have: Consul (Trade/Protect Commerce), Senator (seek ally/trade arbitration). Running 2 Trade actions per season: Favour 5 in 3 seasons per territory. 5 territories in ~15 seasons.

Vaynard can use this as leverage: "We know you're here. Sell us your Church intelligence file or we publish your presence to Himlensendt." Niflhel's response: sell the file (lose Church leverage, gain Varfell goodwill) OR threaten Vaynard (Niflhel Archive reveals a Vaynard secret that can be used against him). The intelligence cold war between Niflhel and Varfell is the most complex bilateral dynamic in the simulation.

**Stress 3 — Thread Tension catastrophe (S19):** Thread Tension 38. Two more Quiet deployments in Southernmost territory would breach 40. Player must choose: halt all Southernmost operations (losing access to the most information-rich region — the wardens and the Calamity's history), or breach 40 and face Warden detection. If detected: Edeyja sends warden teams to follow Niflhel's supply chain back to their surface network. This is the existential exposure risk. The only clean solution: reduce Southernmost operations to 0 permanently and accept the information loss.

### §C — LÖWENRITTER: GOVERNING FACTION

**(Post-Coup, Season ~S16–S30)**

**Victory conditions:**  
Primary: TCV ≥ 10 + TC < 50 + IP < 60 + RS > 40 + PI ≥ 4 + Successor confirmed (Elske OR Torben Loyalty ≥ 6)  
Alternate: TCV ≥ 16 + Military ≥ 5 + RS > 35 + TC < 60  

**Starting state (post-Coup):** Inherited Crown territories (TCV ~12 if Crown had been at 12 before Coup). Military 4–5 (Löwenritter's military investment during pre-Coup phase). Stability 3 (−2 from Coup transition). Mandate 2–3 (contested legitimacy). TC: wherever it was when Coup fired (~40–50 based on typical sim). IP: elevated from Coup battle (IP +2 from Coup combat).

**The governing problem:** Löwenritter are a military order governing a civilian political system. Their competencies are in military operations and tactical decision-making. Their gaps are in: Parliament management, economic governance, theological politics. Every governance Domain Action they take is at +1 Ob (Martial Honour Framework does not benefit non-military actions).

**Legitimacy Token management (POW-01/03):**  
Torben is the Figurehead. Löwenritter governs in his name. The Legitimacy Token:  
- Provides Mandate +1 to Löwenritter (net: Mandate 3).  
- Conditional on Torben's cooperation.  
- Each action the player takes in Löwenritter's own name (not Torben's name) requires no authorization. Each action taken in Torben's name requires his explicit approval.  
- Three damaged authorizations (Torben refuses and player acts anyway) = Token revoked.

**The Torben management game (the real governing challenge):**  
Torben Loyalty ≥ 6 is one of two paths to confirming the successor (victory condition). Torben Loyalty 6 represents deep personal commitment — not just political compliance. Getting there from Coup conditions (Torben was likely at Loyalty 3–4 when Coup fired, dropping to 1–2 from the traumatic transition) requires sustained investment:  
- Disposition building: 1–2 personal scenes per year-arc (Torben is now a figurehead — he has Availability State "In Session" at the administrative seat Löwenritter assigned him). Approach him in Private Retreat (his scheduled time away from figurehead duties): Solidarity RS (Authority resonates second for him, Solidarity is primary).  
- Recognition Event for Torben's Standing advancement (Standing 3→4→5): these Ceremonial Scenes are opportunities for Loyalty building. Torben's Recognition Events under Löwenritter governance build HIS institutional standing while simultaneously building his Loyalty.  
- Obligation honoring: each promise made during Coup negotiations (Torben was likely promised "full authority at adulthood") is an Obligation. Honoring it (even planning for it while governing) builds Loyalty. Breaking it (governing indefinitely beyond the agreed transition date) breaks Loyalty catastrophically.

**[FINDING FAC-SIM-14: The Löwenritter post-Coup governing experience is defined by the race between: (a) building Torben Loyalty to 6 before he asserts independence (Restoration Event), and (b) achieving Primary victory conditions before the legitimacy situation deteriorates. With Torben at Loyalty 1–2 post-Coup and the restoration Event firing when Mandate ≤ 2 AND Torben has inner circle majority: Löwenritter must simultaneously build Torben's Loyalty AND maintain their own Mandate above 2. If they govern well (Mandate 3+): Restoration doesn't fire (Mandate too high). But then Torben Loyalty 6 requires building Loyalty from 1 to 6 — that's 5 points at ~0.5/season from personal investment = 10 seasons. By Season 26 post-Coup (S42 overall). Too slow. They need to accelerate via Obligation honoring, Ceremonial Recognition, and formal partnership offers. Realistically: Löwenritter gets to Torben Loyalty 4–5 before the campaign ends, and either accepts a Co-opt resolution (dual authority, POW-02 Co-opt option) or takes the Alternate victory path.]**

**Alternate path (Military Consolidation):**  
TCV ≥ 16 + Military ≥ 5. Starting TCV ~12. Need +4. Military 4 (post-Coup consolidation) → 5 (investment). This is achievable: March on contested territories while military is at its peak. Target: Church territories (TC < 50 condition: if Church TC is at 45–50 when Coup fires, it's on the edge. Löwenritter's Priority 3 = sovereignty defense. Deploying against Church-held territories during the Church's institutional vulnerability window (Church Stability low from Coup crisis) enables TCV expansion.

**Löwenritter's governing priority tree (post-Coup §7.5):**  
1. Stability ≤ 1 → Military action (internal)  
2. PI = 0 → Parliament Reconstitution  
3. Torben Loyalty ≤ 2 → Dynasty management  
4. Adjacent Uncontrolled → March  
5. Mandate < 3 → Senator (institutional legitimacy)  
6. DEFAULT → Senator Outward (Diplomacy — seek recognition)

**The recognition problem:** Löwenritter needs other factions to recognize them as legitimate Crown successors. Church will not recognize (Excommunication risk). Hafenmark will not recognize without constitutional procedure (Baralta's framework demands legitimacy via Parliament, not military seizure). Varfell will recognize if it's convenient (Consequentialist framework: if Löwenritter governance is stable enough, recognition is mutually beneficial). Guilds will recognize economically (whoever controls the capital gets Guild commercial relationships). The fastest recognition path: economic normalization with Guilds (Priority 6 DEFAULT fires toward all factions, including Guilds), then use that normalized relationship to pressure Church and Hafenmark.

**Stress Tests:**

**Stress 1 — Church pressure (S2 post-Coup = S18 overall):** Church TC at 48. Church Priority 2 fires (TC ≥ 75 AND eligible target — not yet, but Priority 3 Assert fires). TC advancing toward 50. Löwenritter's Primary victory requires TC < 50 at victory. They need to Suppress. But Löwenritter doesn't have Suppress as a natural action (it's a Hafenmark/Crown specialist move). Löwenritter can: (a) Parliament Reconstitution (if PI > 0) → restored Parliament passes Censure of Church Assert (uses restored institutional mechanism), (b) Military threat against T9 Himmelenger (positioning Legionary adjacent to T9 — not attacking, threatening). Church must respond to the military threat, consuming Church Domain Action on Crisis Priority instead of Assert.

**Stress 2 — Baralta's counter-claim (S4 post-Coup):** Baralta's Dynastic Assertion primary condition requires Crown Mandate ≤ 3. Löwenritter's Mandate is 2–3 (they inherited Crown's depleted position). Baralta ALREADY has the Mandate condition met against Löwenritter. She immediately begins Proclamation on T14 Ehrenfeld (Löwenritter-held). Löwenritter must: garrison T14 (Military action, Ob 0), establish Torben's formal endorsement of T14 as Löwenritter-administered (using Legitimacy Token — Torben's name makes the garrison "Crown authority" not "military occupation"). P(Baralta successfully Proclaiming on garrisoned T14 with Legitimacy Token): much lower (Token raises effective Mandate by 1, pushing Baralta's Proclamation above their Mandate threshold momentarily).

**Stress 3 — Restoration Event (S8 post-Coup = S24 overall):** Torben reaches maturity. POW-02 fires: Torben's Mandate support + inner circle majority. He demands restoration. Löwenritter at Mandate 2 (governance difficulty). Restoration conditions met. Options: Accept (lose governing power, retain treaty relationships — this is the "peaceful" outcome that secures Regency victory if TCV and other conditions are met by this point), Resist (civil war at reduced scale — Torben has inner circle, Löwenritter has military, 50/50 chance of winning the military confrontation), Co-opt (Torben as formal co-governor — dual authority structure, stable, complex, doesn't resolve cleanly). If TCV 10 is met by S24 and TC < 50: player may want to ACCEPT the Restoration before it fires, getting credit for the peaceful transition rather than being forced into it. A graceful concession of power is itself a strategic move.

### §D — CARDINALS: CHURCH SUBFACTION

**(Activates as independent actors during Church Stability ≤ 2)**

**Context:** During Church Stability crisis, the four Cardinals operate as independent NPCs. As player: choose ONE Cardinal and run their agenda within the Church power structure. This is sub-faction play — operating within an institutional hierarchy with a personal agenda.

**Cardinal Fortitude (Templar) — Order + Faith**  
*Goal: Establish Templar military primacy within Church governance.*  
Fortitude wants to deploy Templars in every territory where Church has AP ≥ 2. He believes the Church's weakness comes from insufficient institutional enforcement. His "victory" within the Church: becoming the de facto military governor of Church territories, with the Confessor reduced to a theological figurehead.

*Play:* Each season Church Stability ≤ 2, Fortitude can deploy Templars independently (OFC-04: defensive authority without provincial authorization). He builds a network of Templar garrisons in Church territories, each answering to him, not to Himlensendt. By the time Himlensendt recovers from the crisis (Stability ≥ 3), Fortitude has a military fact-on-the-ground that is politically difficult to reverse. His Conviction (Faith+Order) means he believes this is genuinely serving the Church — he is not a cynical usurper, he is an institution-builder using a crisis window.

*Internal conflict:* Cardinal Justice (Inquisitor) also activates during Stability ≤ 2. Justice's Faith-hardline means he wants Fortitude's Templar network brought under Inquisitorial oversight (any Templar encountering potential heresy should report to Justice, not operate independently). Fortitude and Justice are competing for the same institutional crisis window.

**Cardinal Justice (Inquisitor) — Faith hardline**  
*Goal: Purge heresy within the Church itself. Including Himlensendt if necessary.*  
Justice believes the Church's weakness comes from internal doctrinal compromise. The problem is Himlensendt's epistemic uncertainty (his Scars, if the other player's contest chain has worked). Justice wants to formally charge the Confessor.

*Play:* Investigation of Himlensendt's statements during his Scar accumulation period. Justice is building an Evidence file using the Church's own investigative procedures (OFC-03). He must find: statements from Himlensendt that contradict doctrine, witnesses (Cardinals or Templars who heard the contradictions), and a formal charge that 3 of 5 Cardinals will support.

*The Inquisitorial process against the Confessor:* College vote to authorize (3 of 5). Justice himself votes yes (1). Needs 2 more. Prudence (Autonomy+Order) likely supports if the charge protects Church wealth from Himlensendt's erratic behavior. Temperance (Reason+Faith) likely opposes (he sympathizes with doctrinal flexibility). Fortitude: depends on whether the charge stabilizes or destabilizes Templar operations. The vote is typically 3-2 in favor if Justice has built his evidence file well.

**Cardinal Prudence (Tithes) — Order + Autonomy**  
*Goal: Protect Church Wealth and maintain fiscal independence from all political factions.*  
Prudence doesn't care about TC, theology, or military positioning. He cares that the Church's Wealth (4 starting) is never threatened and that the Church's revenue streams (Territory Prosperity income from Cathedral settlements) are never compromised.

*Play:* Prudence actively opposes Church Seizure of territories that would require governance investment (Seizure uses Church resources for Governance that Prudence would rather keep liquid). He is the Church's internal fiscal brake. His "victory": Church Wealth ≥ 5 at campaign end, Church has avoided any military confrontations that depleted Treasury.

*As player:* Prudence can veto Church Domain Actions that cost Wealth above his threshold by making the cost visible to the full College. College members who trust Prudence's fiscal analysis will not support expensive actions. This is institutional influence without formal authority. He is the CFO of an institution run by theologians.

**Cardinal Temperance (Scholar) — Reason + Faith**  
*Goal: Reconcile Thread reality with Church doctrine before it's too late.*  
Temperance is the Cardinal most likely to experience his own Conviction crisis if exposed to Thread evidence (per §2.13). He's already aware that the AER is declining (his responsibility). He privately suspects that the Solmund framework is incomplete.

*Play:* Building a doctrinal framework that incorporates Thread reality without explicitly repudiating Solmund. This is theological research as a political project: he wants the Church to be able to acknowledge Thread substrate existence without schism. His goal is a document that Himlensendt can endorse (or his successor can endorse) that "expands" the doctrinal interpretation to include Thread phenomena as Solmund's deeper workings.

*The crisis if he succeeds:* Cardinal Justice will view any such document as heresy. The moment Temperance publishes his framework, Justice files charges — against Temperance now. Temperance's Reason+Faith synthesis makes him politically vulnerable to any Cardinal who prioritizes Faith purity over intellectual honesty.

**[FINDING FAC-SIM-15: The Cardinal subfaction play produces the richest single-institution internal drama in the entire system. Four Cardinals with structurally incompatible goals (Fortitude wants military primacy, Justice wants ideological purity, Prudence wants fiscal independence, Temperance wants doctrinal reconciliation) are all activated simultaneously during a Church crisis. They will obstruct each other, form temporary coalitions (Fortitude+Justice vs. Prudence+Temperance on most issues), and compete for Himlensendt's declining trust. This is Italian Renaissance court politics at its most compressed. The Cardinal who "wins" the Church subfaction game is whichever one's agenda is institutionally embedded when Himlensendt either recovers (restoring normal hierarchy) or is replaced (the new Confessor is shaped by whichever Cardinal faction was dominant during the crisis).]**

### §E — SCHOENLAND: THE EXTERNAL PRESSURE FACTION

**Status:** Observer faction. No TCV, no conventional victory condition. Schoenland is the Valorian peninsula's relationship to the external world (Altonian Empire, specifically). Playing as Schoenland means playing the geopolitical force that is watching Valoria's internal conflict and deciding how much to let the Altonian pressure build.

**Priority tree:**  
1. IF IP ≥ 60 → Altonian Preparation (Vanguard mobilization begins)  
2. IF IP ≥ 40 → Diplomatic Pressure (AER −1 if Temperance not maintained)  
3. IF Supply Chain Exposure + Parish Revolt + PI ≥ 8 → Recall Solberg (withdrawal from extreme position)  
4. DEFAULT → Trade (passive Wealth generation)

**The Schoenland player controls the IP clock's consequences.** They don't generate IP (Valorian factions generate IP through battles). But Schoenland determines what happens when IP thresholds are crossed. Their leverage: by being willing to deploy Vanguard Pressure at IP 60, they force ALL Valorian factions to consider IP management.

**Strategic play:** Schoenland's "win" is extracting maximum concessions from Valorian factions in exchange for NOT deploying the Vanguard. This is the great power diplomatic pressure game.

**The AER lever:** Priority 2 (IP ≥ 40): Diplomatic Pressure = AER −1 if Temperance not maintained. Schoenland can deliberately allow AER to decline in Valoria by refusing to support Cardinal Temperance's framework (Schoenland is the external validator of Valorian theological practice — their endorsement is worth +1 AER maintenance per season). By withholding this endorsement, they accelerate Valoria's internal Thread crisis. This is pressure.

**Concession demands:** When factions approach Schoenland to prevent Vanguard deployment, Schoenland sets terms. Crown Treaty with Schoenland (per §3.1: "Diplomatic Outreach to Schoenland" separate from Crown Treaty, cannot combine same season): each successful Treaty prevents IP from triggering Vanguard for 4 seasons. Schoenland's terms: "Church must maintain AER ≥ 2" OR "Crown must guarantee open trade routes through T1" OR "Varfell must share Thread intelligence findings." These are real political concessions.

**[FINDING FAC-SIM-16: Schoenland-as-player is the only faction that can win by doing almost nothing. Their optimal play is appearing threatening enough that every faction pays diplomatic attention to AER and IP management, while Schoenland extracts commercial and intelligence concessions from all of them. The Schoenland player is playing a diplomatic extraction game against the backdrop of everyone else's political struggle. They don't need the peninsula to be stable — they need the peninsula to be just unstable enough that their intervention looks plausible. This is historically accurate to great power politics: the threat of intervention is often more valuable than actual intervention.]**

## CROSS-FACTION DYNAMICS (NEW FINDINGS)

### FINDING FAC-SIM-17: The Mandate Race Is Central

The BG game's core tension is a Mandate race. Baralta suppresses Crown's Mandate while building her own. Almud suppresses Hafenmark's Mandate while expanding through Treaties. Vaynard uses VTM to suppress TC (indirectly protecting Mandate conditions). Every faction's victory condition involves either holding Mandate ≥ N (own) or forcing rivals below Mandate N (theirs). The Mandate race is more consequential than the TCV race in most scenarios — TCV expansion means nothing if Mandate falls below victory thresholds simultaneously.

### FINDING FAC-SIM-18: Multiple Simultaneous Threats Overwhelm Single-Track Players

Any player running only one strategic track is vulnerable when that track is threatened. Baralta running only Parliament gets shut down when Church Excommunication fires AND her parliamentary coalition collapses simultaneously — both happen in the same crisis. Vaynard running only Thread research gets blocked when Discovery Events destabilize his judgment AND Baralta seizes T13 simultaneously. The faction leaders who remain competitive through S30 are those running 3+ simultaneous tracks that cross-reinforce: a Baralta attack on one track (Suppress Mandate) simultaneously advances another (weakening rival for Proclamation) and positions a third (co-victory optionality).

### FINDING FAC-SIM-19: Subfaction Dependency

Major factions depend on subfactions more than prior simulations showed:
- Crown needs Guilds (economically) and tolerates Niflhel (covertly).
- Church needs Cardinals to function (College management) and is threatened by Schoenland (AER leverage).
- Hafenmark needs Guilds (Guild Comptroller inner circle) and can use Löwenritter co-victory.
- Varfell needs Niflhel (parallel intelligence market) and Wardens (Path B/C).
- RM needs Niflhel (supply chain) and Guilds (economic protection of community networks).
None of the major faction campaigns is clean without accounting for at least one subfaction's behavior.

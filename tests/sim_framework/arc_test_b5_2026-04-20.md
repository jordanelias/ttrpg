## Findings

### B5-1 (baseline) — control

10-season passive-only sim (seed 42): CI 28 → 65, mean Δ +3.70/season. T9 SW=5, Templar active. Mass Seizure available S9.

### B5-2 — Sack Himmelenger Cathedral at S5

Pre-sack mean Δ (S1–S4): **+3.75/season**
Post-sack mean Δ (S6–S10): **+2.60/season**
**Penalty: −1.15 CI/season** (matches ED-722 prediction: T9 yield drops from 1 → 0 floored under PT_TIER lookup with SW(T9) 5 → 2; PT_tier 1.0 × 2/5 = 0.4 floored = 0).

### B5-3 — Compounded loss (sack Cathedral + lose Templar Presence)

Post mean Δ (S6–S10): **+1.60/season**
**Compound penalty: −2.15 CI/season.** Templar removal accounts for the additional −1; the −1.15 from SW collapse remains. ED-722's "Church now has incentive to defend infrastructure" claim is mechanically validated.

### B5-4 — Double loss (T9 Cathedral sacked + T1 Valorsplatz Church desacralized)

Post mean Δ (S6–S10): **+2.60/season** (no change vs B5-2).

**Finding (NEW):** Desacralizing T1 has zero CI impact at game start because Church.M (5) is not > Crown.M (5) in T1 — Church is not Prominent there. The lost SW is "stored value" pending future Prominence (Crown M drop). Dynamic SW only matters in territories where Church is Prominent. This is a meaningful asymmetry for player strategy: targeting Church infrastructure in non-Prominent territories is a *preventive* strike, not an *immediate* one.

### B5-5 — Recovery via Ecclesiastical Appointment

Sack at S5, rebuild via E.A. at S8: post-rebuild Δ (S8–S10) **= +4.00/season** (vs +2.33 during S5–S7 sacked window).

**Finding:** Recovery is full and immediate. The mechanic is reversible — no permanent decay penalty beyond the seasons during which infrastructure was reduced.

### B5-7 — Time-to-Unification (full-AI campaign, 10 seeds)

Most runs (9/10) end via shared-loss or faction-elimination conditions before reaching CI 100. Of runs that DO reach Unification:
- Baseline: **S15**
- Sacked-S5: **S16**

**Finding:** Sacking T9 Cathedral at S5 delays Unification by ~1 season per cleanly-completing run. The campaign-scale impact is modest because (a) other CI sources buffer the loss, and (b) the +5/season cap means losing 1 source rarely drops total below cap-saturation. The structural lesson: Cathedral defense matters more for *preventing* a saturation cap-floor (where every CI source becomes load-bearing) than for *blocking* Church victory.

### B5-6 — AER mechanic verification

8-season run (2 years): AER 2 → 4. Temperance fired at S4 and S6 (one per year), each Mandate vs Ob = ⌊AER/2⌋+1 succeeded.

**Findings:**
- AER mechanic functioning per `params/bg/tracks.md` PP-181/PP-565
- Year-End decay correctly suppressed by successful Temperance
- Cardinal of Temperance is Church's only AER lever — losing T9 disables Temperance entirely (P5 condition `t9.controller == 'Church'`)
- Altonian Conquest loss condition (IP ≥ 100 + AER ≤ 1) wired in `check_victory`

The B3 gap #9 ("AER generation mechanic — not in any read doc") was a sim implementation gap, not a design gap. The mechanic is fully specified in `params/bg/tracks.md` line 9–13 and `params/bg/institutions.md` line 19. Closed via engine_v3 wiring.

### Mode D — Edge Cases

**IXC-01-F1:** Momentum spend on Leap (Thread roll) — BLOCKED. Leap is categorically a Thread operation. No contradiction. **CLEAN.**

**IXC-01-F2 [P2]:** Asymmetric Momentum dynamics for practitioners. Overwhelming on Thread op generates Momentum; Momentum can only be spent on non-Thread rolls. Practitioners accumulate spendable Momentum through Thread success but cannot reinvest it in Thread. Design signal: practitioners are incentivised to take non-Thread actions to spend Momentum. Not a bug.

**IXC-01-F3:** Double Momentum gain (Belief + Overwhelming same action) valid. Reaches cap of 4 quickly. Reinforces Belief-driven play. **CLEAN.**

**IXC-01-G1 [GAP]:** Coherence 0 action availability undefined. P-15 specifies rendering collapse state but no mechanic defines whether character is incapacitated or degraded-but-functional. Pool minimum (1D) could allow continued rolls at Coherence 0 if character is not incapacitated — creates ambiguity.

**IXC-01-G2 [GAP]:** Multi-effect Ob stacking sequence undefined when P-01 auto-effects simultaneously add Ob approaching cap (20). No priority rule for which effect applies first when cap would be exceeded mid-stack.

**IXC-01-G3 [GAP]:** Momentum gain at cap (4) — no rule for what happens to excess gain. Lost? Deferred? Needs ruling.

## IXC-02: Core Engine → Threadwork

### TN Mechanic — Confirmed

TN = threshold; faces **strictly greater than TN** count as +1 success. Face 10 always +2. Face 1 always −1.

| TN | Success faces | E[net] per die |
|----|--------------|----------------|
| 6  | 7–9 (3 faces) | 0.40          |
| 7  | 8–9 (2 faces) | 0.30          |
| 8  | 9 (1 face)    | 0.20          |

**IXC-02-F1 [P2]:** params_core.md die face table states "7–9 → +1 success" without specifying this is TN7-specific. At TN6, face 6 also counts; at TN8, only face 9 counts. Description is TN7-centric and will mislead readers. E[net] values in params are all correct once TN mechanic is understood. Die face table needs clarifying language.

### Mode D — Edge Cases

**IXC-02-F2 [P2]:** Focus=1 permits Leap but grants 0 operation rounds (Experience only). Partial Leap outcome still applies −2 Composure despite zero ops being available. A Focus=1 practitioner cannot benefit from Leaping but still pays the Composure cost on Partial. Either Focus=1 Leap should be blocked, or the Composure penalty should be conditional on op availability.

**IXC-02-F3:** Sequential failure cannot reduce Leap pool below 1D at any valid character build. Pool minimum rule is a backstop here — will not trigger. **CLEAN.**

**IXC-02-F4 [P2]:** Wound accumulation creates steep Leap viability cliff. At 4+ wounds (Ob=6 for TS40 practitioner), success probability drops below 20%. At 5 wounds, success < 12%. Functionally ceases Thread operations — emergent threshold, no enforced rule. Design signal: practitioners should avoid combat engagement.

**IXC-02-F5:** Momentum survives RS=0 lockout. Non-Thread Momentum spendable at RS=0. **CLEAN.**

**IXC-02-G1 [GAP]:** Whether any Thread operation bypasses Leap eligibility not confirmed in params. TPS=0 is reachable (TS < 10) but all ops appear Leap-gated. Needs explicit confirmation.

**IXC-02-G2 [GAP]:** RS restoration sources not defined in params_threadwork. RS trajectory cannot be fully modelled. Confirms SIM-H-01 / GAP-SIM-X27-01.

## IXC-03: Core Engine → Personal Combat

### Mode B — Pool Construction and Hit Probability

Pool = (Agility × 2) + History + 3 (minimum 5). Weapon TN = 7 + modifiers.

**Hit probability at 6D Offence allocation:**

| Weapon | TN | P(≥1 hit) | P(≥2 hits) | P(≥3 hits) |
|--------|----|-----------|------------|------------|
| Short Light Blade | 5 | 89.9% | 77.7% | 60.0% |
| Short Heavy Blade | 6 | 83.5% | 67.0% | 47.0% |
| Long Heavy Blade  | 7 | 74.3% | 54.2% | 34.1% |
| Long Heavy Blunt  | 8 | 61.3% | 40.9% | 22.3% |
| Unarmed           | 8 | 61.3% | 40.9% | 22.3% |

**Expected damage/round at 6D Offence, STR=3, vs Light armour:**

| Weapon | P(hit) | E[net\|hit] | E[dmg/round] |
|--------|--------|------------|--------------|
| Short Light Blade (mod+2) | 89.9% | 3.39 | 7.54 |
| Short Heavy Blade (mod+4) | 83.5% | 2.98 | 8.33 |
| Long Heavy Blade (mod+4)  | 74.3% | 2.61 | 7.14 |
| Long Heavy Blunt (mod+5)  | 61.3% | 2.33 | 6.33 |

Throughput approximately equalises across weapon types vs light armour. Short Heavy Blade highest. Design intent appears met — but full armour tier matrix needed to confirm across all four tiers.

### Mode D — Edge Cases

**IXC-03-F1 [P2]:** TN spread 5–8 creates 28.6pp hit rate differential between Short Light Blade and unarmed on equal pools. Intentional (fast weapons hit more reliably), and damage modifier table offsets this. Throughput data above supports design intent.

**IXC-03-F2 [P2]:** Short Heavy Blade has highest expected damage throughput vs light armour. Full armour tier comparison deferred to IXC-11.

**IXC-03-F3 [P1]:** Two conflicting pool minimums. params_combat states Combat Pool minimum = 5. params_core states no pool may fall below 1D. A heavily-wounded character with low attributes could have wounds reduce pool below 5. lex specialis favours the combat minimum (5), but this is not stated explicitly. Needs ruling.

**IXC-03-F4:** Armour wield constraint is build-time only. Wounds do not affect Stamina (wounds = −1D pool only). No conflict. **CLEAN.**

**IXC-03-F5 [P2]:** Rational play can produce repeated initiative ties at equal pool split. No termination rule exists. Degenerate stalemate possible if both players rationally mirror each other's allocation. Requires GM intervention rule or tiebreaker mechanic.

**IXC-03-F6:** Mass Mismatch at net-0 defence has no additional effect (floor is already 0). **CLEAN.**

**IXC-03-G1 [GAP — P1]:** Combat resolution Ob not stated in params_combat. No explicit Ob value for hit rolls. The pool-split (Offence vs Defence) model implies an opposed resolution, but this is not confirmed. All damage probability tables in this simulation assume opposed resolution. Findings are provisional until Ob / opposed-roll model is confirmed.

**IXC-03-G2 [GAP]:** Whose weapon TN applies to Defence rolls in an asymmetric weapon matchup? If each combatant uses their own weapon TN on both attack and defence, a character with Short Light Blade (TN5) defends at TN5 and attacks at TN5. A Long Heavy Blunt (TN8) character defends at TN8 — significantly harder to defend with a war hammer. This creates strong emergent asymmetry. Needs explicit ruling.

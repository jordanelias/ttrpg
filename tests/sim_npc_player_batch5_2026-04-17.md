## NPC 01 — ANNALIE REICHARD (Lord Treasurer, Crown Inner Circle)

### Who She Is

Annalie Reichard is the person who knows whether Crown can afford what it is doing. Her family married into aristocracy two generations ago from a mercantile background. She has a distant cousin relationship with Feldhaus (Hafenmark Guild Comptroller) that she has formally distanced from — the relationship is known, the distance is deliberate. Her Conviction is Precedent; her RS is Evidence; her Certainty is 5.

She controls Crown's Wealth attribute. When Almud builds his Treaty network, she is the one who calculates whether the seasonal Wealth costs are sustainable. When Voss establishes his hidden reserve (Wealth −1/season), she notices the line item and must decide whether to query it. When Church's AER drops and Schoenland diplomatic pressure increases Altonian preparedness costs, she is the one restructuring Crown's resource allocation to compensate.

**Her relationship to Feldhaus:**
The formal distancing is real — she has no professional contact with Feldhaus. But the informal intelligence channel is also real: they both know certain things about Guild commercial networks that only people with their backgrounds know. When Feldhaus positions Hafenmark's Trade & Guild Relations Committee motion, Reichard reads it and understands its commercial mechanics better than any other Crown inner circle member. She does not share this reading with Almud unprompted. She is not disloyal. She simply understands that some knowledge is better managed by holding it than by surfacing it.

**Precedent Conviction applied to finance:**
Where Baralta's Precedent Conviction governs constitutional interpretation, Reichard's governs fiscal agreements. She believes that established financial arrangements are structurally binding. Treaty Wealth commitments are not merely political agreements — they are fiscal precedent. A treaty that commits Crown to Wealth transfers must be honored regardless of political expedience, because the alternative (selective treaty compliance) destroys the fiscal certainty on which all of Crown's commercial relationships depend.

This makes her the most conservative voice in the inner circle on Treaty formation: she will not oppose a Treaty for political reasons, but she will oppose one whose Wealth commitments she judges fiscally unsustainable.

### Season Structure

**Haushalt Ministry (Ministry of Finance):**
At Competence 3: Wealth yield +2/season. At Competence 2: standard yield. At Competence 1: Wealth yield −1/season. Reichard inherits Competence 2 (Ministry functional but not excellent). Her first priority: audit and reform.

Her Season 1 audit reveals: the Haushalt has three separate revenue streams (territorial taxation, trade tariff enforcement, treasury accounting) that are tracked in separate ledgers that have never been formally reconciled. Discrepancies exist between what territories report paying and what the treasury records receiving. These discrepancies are not large — they are approximately 3% of annual Wealth yield. But 3% across Crown's Wealth 4 is non-trivial, and the discrepancy is growing.

**The discrepancy investigation:**
The 3% gap is not corruption — it is timing. Trade tariff enforcement in T3 and T5 operates on a lag: tariffs assessed in one season are collected in the next. The ledgers were never corrected to account for this lag, so there is a permanent apparent discrepancy that does not represent actual loss. But Reichard cannot be certain it is not corruption without verifying each transaction against the source documents.

This investigation takes 3 seasons. It produces: zero findings of corruption, complete reconciliation of the ledgers, and Haushalt Competence rising to 3 (the audit itself is the reform). Wealth yield +2/season beginning S4.

**[FINDING REICH-SIM-01: Reichard's investigation of a benign discrepancy reveals the second-order value of systematic financial management: the audit that finds nothing wrong is more valuable than the audit that finds fraud, because it produces certainty. Crown's Wealth yield at Competence 3 (+2/season) generates an additional 2 Wealth/season from S4 onward. Over 20 seasons: +40 Wealth cumulative above what Competence 2 would have produced. This is Reichard's largest structural contribution — not any specific decision, but the creation of systematic certainty that allows other decisions to be made accurately. The Lord Treasurer's legacy is measurement.]**

### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Treaty Ledger

**S4 — Voss's reserve line item:**
Wealth −1/season appears in the Kriegsamt budget under "Extended Field Readiness Protocol." Reichard's Precedent Conviction: established expenditures require a documented basis. She queries the Kriegsamt: "Please provide the operational justification and expected duration of the Field Readiness Protocol." Voss responds: "Ongoing investment, indefinite duration. Value: deterrence." This is technically an answer. It is not a financially sufficient answer.

She flags the expenditure in her internal review notes as "Justification: inadequate for indefinite-duration investment." She does not escalate to Almud. She monitors it. Every season that the line item continues without fuller justification, she adds a note: "Season N: continued without additional documentation."

By S8: the file contains 5 seasons of "continued without documentation" notes. If she ever needs to surface this, she has 5 seasons of documented concern that predates any crisis. She is not building a case against Voss — she is maintaining institutional memory in case it becomes relevant.

**S6 — Treaty Wealth assessment:**
Almud's Treaty with Guilds fires (S4). Treaty terms: implicit Wealth transfer equivalent from OW Treaty bonus structure. Reichard calculates: the Guild Treaty's OW result (Guilds Mandate −1, Crown Mandate +1) creates a 4-season dependency. She models: what happens to Crown's Wealth if the Guild Treaty lapses and Guild Economic Leverage fires against Crown? Crown Wealth 4 vs. Guild Wealth 6: Guild wins. Crown Wealth 3. Recovery time: 3 seasons.

She presents this analysis to Almud: "The Guild Treaty creates a Wealth exposure window at lapse. I recommend we build a Wealth buffer to Wealth 6 before the Treaty's 4-season expiry." Almud: "How?" Reichard: "Haushalt at Competence 3 generates +2/season. In 2 seasons from now: Wealth 6. The buffer builds itself." Almud approves. They do nothing specific — the Haushalt's Competence 3 rate does the work.

**Her relationship to Feldhaus-adjacent intelligence:**
When Hafenmark's Grand Contest attempt against Crown becomes visible (S10 in standard timeline), Reichard is the first Crown inner circle member to understand its commercial implications. A Grand Contest decisive win transfers Mandate — which shifts which faction merchants preferentially approach for trade arbitration. She calculates: if Hafenmark wins the Grand Contest and gains Mandate 5, Crown's commercial treaty terms with Guilds will face renegotiation pressure (Guilds prefer to work through the highest-Mandate institutional partner). She prepares a commercial policy adjustment brief — contingency planning for a scenario that hasn't happened yet.

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Sustainability Question

**S12 — Counter 1, financial implications:**
When the Coup Counter advances, Reichard does not know about it. But she notices something: Kreutz's quarterly coordination reports have begun routing through the Kriegsamt budget review process with slightly earlier filing dates. The pattern suggests someone in the Löwenritter chain is becoming more operationally active — which implies increased Löwenritter Military spending is likely coming. She pre-positions: she requests Almud authorize a Treasury Reserve (Wealth set aside for military contingency). Almud approves a Wealth 1 reserve. She locks it in a dedicated treasury account that requires joint authorization (herself and Almud) to release.

**[FINDING REICH-SIM-02: Reichard's Treasury Reserve, created in response to a financial pattern she detects without understanding its political source, creates the budget line that will fund Crown's response to the Coup regardless of which direction that response takes. If Almud fights (uses Reserve for Royal Guard deployment), the Reserve funds the defense. If the Managed Transition proceeds (no military response), the Reserve remains intact — and constitutes the post-Coup Crown's fiscal continuity fund. She has, by accident of good fiscal practice, preserved Crown's financial infrastructure through the regime transition. The Lord Treasurer's most important act turns out to be something she did because the numbers told her to, not because she understood the politics.]**

**S17 — Counter 2, direct query:**
When the Counter advances to 2, Reichard receives an unusual request: Kreutz asks her to review "standard garrison resupply cost curves" for hypothetical troop movements from T14 to T1. This is the first time Kreutz has asked her a question that has no obvious operational purpose. She calculates the cost curve. She also notes the specific trajectory (T14 to T1 is a specific march route — it is not a generic resupply question). She provides the calculation. She does not ask what it is for.

In her private notes: "S17: Kreutz queried T14→T1 resupply cost. Hypothetical framing. No operational context provided. Filed for reference."

She has not violated anyone's confidence. She has not taken any action. She has recorded, as is her mandate, the institutional data point. If asked later whether she had prior notice of the Coup, she can truthfully answer: no, she did not know what the query was for.

### Stress Tests

**Stress 1 — Baralta's Parliamentary motion (S11):** Hafenmark's parliamentary motion on Trade & Guild Relations creates a new regulatory framework for foreign-faction commercial agents in Hafenmark territories. From Crown's Wealth perspective, this affects Crown merchants operating in Hafenmark. Reichard calculates the compliance cost: approximately 0.5 Wealth/season in administrative overhead for Crown's trade operations in Hafenmark territories. She brings this to Almud: "Hafenmark's new regulatory framework will cost Crown 0.5 Wealth/season in compliance overhead. Options: absorb it, negotiate a Crown-Hafenmark commercial exemption through Treaty, or challenge the motion through Parliamentary counter-motion." Almud: "What does the exemption cost?" Reichard: "One season of diplomatic attention, plus acknowledging Hafenmark's parliamentary authority in commercial regulation." Almud: absorbs it. She files this as "accepted cost" in the Haushalt ledger with the date and the decision recorded.

**Stress 2 — Post-Coup Wealth management (S22+):** After the Coup, Crown's Ministry structure transfers to Löwenritter governance. Reichard remains as Haushalt Minister (the Löwenritter need institutional fiscal competence more than they need political loyalty at this level). Her Competence 3 Haushalt continues. The Treasury Reserve transfers to joint authorization with Ehrenwall and herself. This is the only change: her co-signatory is now Ehrenwall instead of Almud. She serves the new authority with the same precision she served the old one. Precedent Conviction: the fiscal agreements and reporting structures are binding regardless of who governs.

## NPC 02 — TORVI HELJASON (Baralta's Legal Advisor, Hafenmark Inner Circle)

### Who He Is

Torvi Heljason is the only person in Hafenmark who can contradict Baralta in private without procedural consequence. This is his formal inner circle specification. It is an extraordinary institutional privilege — and it means nothing if he doesn't use it.

Northern Einhir heritage. Precedent/Authority Conviction compound. Certainty 4. Constitutional scholar. He has served as Baralta's Legal Advisor for 12 seasons; he knows her interpretive patterns better than she knows them herself. He also knows where her interpretations are strong, where they are clever-but-thin, and where they are genuinely wrong.

The question of his campaign arc is simple to state and difficult to live: does he tell her?

### The Advisor's Core Problem

Every political advisor in history faces the same structural pressure: the principal's agenda shapes what counts as good advice. A Legal Advisor who tells the principal only what she wants to hear is a mirror, not an advisor. A Legal Advisor who tells the principal what she doesn't want to hear is initially uncomfortable and eventually invaluable — or eventually dismissed.

Heljason knows this framework explicitly; he is a scholar. He has thought about it abstractly for years. Experiencing it in practice, in a real-time political campaign, is different.

**Where Baralta is strong:** Her Constitutional Triumph arc (Arc A) is genuinely on strong ground. Hafenmark's parliamentary framework is well-documented. Her jurisdiction claims in Hafenmark territory are correctly argued. Her Sovereign Authority Doctrine (drafted by Heljason himself, per the Batch 3 simulation) is legally sound.

**Where Baralta is clever-but-thin:** The Grand Contest jurisdiction claim (T3 Lowenskyst). Almstedt certified the precedent as "sufficient" — but Heljason wrote the original brief that Almstedt reviewed. He knows the precedent is thin. He wrote it to be as strong as possible while acknowledging its limits. He also knows that Almstedt knows it is thin.

**Where Baralta is genuinely wrong:** She has begun referring to the Dynastic Assertion as "constitutional restoration" in her public framing. This framing is historically incorrect — the Almqvist founding terms did not establish Baralta's lineage as a restoration candidate. They established Hafenmark as an independent constitutional duchy. Baralta's Dynastic claim is a legitimate Deed-Claim (LIN-01), but it is not a restoration of anything. She is eliding this distinction for political effect.

Heljason told her this privately in Season 6. She heard it. She continued using the framing. He has not raised it again.

### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Brief Writer

**The Sovereign Authority Doctrine (S1–5):**
Four scenes of investigation and drafting. Heljason researches Church-Hafenmark jurisdictional precedent across 60 seasons of parliamentary records. His finding: there are 7 cases where Church attempted to invoke ecclesiastical authority over civil administrative matters in Hafenmark territory, and in 5 of those 7, the Parliamentary response successfully repelled the incursion. The two failures were in periods of low Parliamentary cohesion (Stability crises). His brief: the Doctrine is on strong historical ground when Parliamentary coalition holds.

He drafts the Doctrine with one deliberate structural feature: it is explicitly framed as an application of existing parliamentary precedent, not a new claim. This makes it harder for Church to attack as an innovation — they would need to challenge 60 seasons of parliamentary history, not just this document. He is a craftsman. He knows how to build arguments that are difficult to dismantle.

**The Grand Contest brief (S6–7):**
Baralta asks him to draft the jurisdictional argument for her planned Grand Contest against Crown. He researches. He finds the thin precedent (the 70-season-old commercial survey). He drafts the strongest possible argument from that precedent. He also writes a private addendum — never filed, never sent to Baralta — that lists the four strongest counter-arguments Crown could make. He keeps the addendum in his files. If Crown raises any of those arguments in the Grand Contest proceeding, he will recognize them and know how to redirect.

He does not tell Baralta about the addendum. She has not asked for a weakness analysis. She asked for the strongest possible argument. He provided it. The addendum is his preparation, not her briefing.

**[FINDING HELJ-SIM-01: Heljason's private counter-argument addendum is the cleanest expression of the honest advisor's operating principle: you give your principal the strongest case while privately understanding the case's vulnerabilities. You do not burden the principal with your uncertainty — you use your uncertainty as preparation for the moment when the opposition raises your own fears against you. The advisor who shares every doubt undermines the principal's confidence unnecessarily. The advisor who shares no doubts is not doing their job. The addendum is how Heljason manages this balance: the doubts exist, they are filed, they are not suppressed, they are not shared unless needed.]**

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Things He Doesn't Say

**S10 — Almstedt's certification:**
Almstedt certifies the T3 jurisdiction argument as "sufficient." Heljason is present at the review session. He sees Almstedt's hesitation before the certification — a pause of approximately 2 seconds longer than his normal review cadence. Heljason knows Almstedt well enough to read this as deliberate: Almstedt is choosing to certify despite reservations. He does not raise this observation with Baralta. The certification is valid. His own assessment was that the argument was "sufficient." Almstedt confirmed it. The outcome is what Heljason argued for.

He writes in his notes: "S10: Almstedt certified. Pause before signature: approximately 3 seconds longer than standard. He knows. He chose to certify anyway. The question this raises is not whether the argument is sufficient — it is what Almstedt knows that I don't."

He begins a secondary research project: what does the constitutional archive contain that he hasn't seen? He is not looking for anything specific. He is following the signal of Almstedt's pause.

**S14 — The "constitutional restoration" problem:**
Baralta's framing has calcified. She uses "restoration" in every public speech now. Heljason requests a private audience (he holds the right to see her In Session — his inner circle position gives him access other advisors lack). In the meeting: "The 'restoration' framing is historically incorrect. The founding terms established Hafenmark as an independent duchy, not as a restoration candidate. If Crown's attorneys research the founding terms and raise this in the Grand Contest proceeding, our position on T3 jurisdiction becomes significantly more complicated."

Baralta: "What's the correct framing?"

Heljason: "Dynastic Assertion through accumulated constitutional authority and Mandate superiority. It's slightly less resonant but it's what the founding terms actually support."

Baralta: "Can you make the restoration framing work?"

Pause. He can. It requires an aggressive interpretation of a single passage in the founding terms that is usually read differently. He can write the brief. It will hold in front of Almstedt (who will give it the benefit of the doubt). It will not hold in front of anyone who has done deep research.

Heljason: "I can draft a brief that supports it. It's defensible at standard review. It becomes brittle under extended scrutiny."

Baralta: "Draft it."

He does. He also, that evening, writes a private record: "S14: Drafted restoration argument at principal's request. Assessment: defensible under standard review; brittle under extended scrutiny. This is the first brief I have written that I would not sign off on as accurate."

He has crossed a line. He knows he has crossed it. He has documented that he knows.

**[FINDING HELJ-SIM-02: Heljason's S14 decision is the most ethically significant advisor moment in the simulation. He drafts a brief he wouldn't sign off on as accurate, at his principal's request, for a political advantage. He does not refuse — he has no formal grounds to refuse (the brief is technically defensible). He does not agree it's right — he records privately that it isn't. He has become a sophisticated instrument of a position he knows to be intellectually dishonest. The private record is his ethical preservation of self: he has not lied to himself, only to the parliamentary proceeding. Whether this distinction matters depends on what the parliamentary proceeding eventually costs.]**

### Stress Tests

**Stress 1 — Almstedt's sealed document (S18):** Almstedt gives Heljason a private advisory note: "I have reviewed the constitutional archive and there is a matter that a complete analysis of the Dynastic Assertion's precedent would require addressing. I recommend you review Article 12 of the founding charter before the certification session." Heljason reviews Article 12. He understands immediately. He finds the amendment irregularity that Almstedt identified seasons ago. He goes to Baralta: "There is a structural issue with the amendment that enabled our current parliamentary framework. If raised in the certification proceeding, it would require a 2–3 season procedural delay while the College of Prelates equivalent reviews foundational legitimacy." Baralta's response: "Is it likely to be raised?" Heljason: "By whom? Almstedt knows and has chosen not to raise it. Crown's attorneys would need to research the founding archive. Vaynard's network could find it." Baralta: "Then prepare a brief for the contingency." He prepares the brief. He also finally understands why Almstedt's pause lasted 3 seconds longer than normal.

**Stress 2 — The honest moment (S20):** Baralta asks Heljason, in a rare informal setting (she has pulled him aside before a parliamentary session): "Do you think what we're doing is right?" He answers: "I think what we're doing is legally defensible. Whether it's right depends on what you mean by 'right.'" She: "What do you mean by 'right'?" He: "Consistent with what Hafenmark's constitution was designed to do." She: "And is it?" Longest pause of their 12-season relationship. He: "It's consistent with what the constitution can be made to do. Those are different things." She doesn't respond. The parliamentary session begins. Neither of them returns to the question.

## NPC 03 — FATHER GUSTAV LINDER (Archbishop's Representative, Crown Inner Circle)

### Who He Is

Father Linder is a Church Canon (Church Standing 3) seconded to Crown's inner circle by Himlensendt. He reports to Himlensendt weekly. He is structurally the most compromised person in the game — not through any fault of his own, but by design: he was placed in Crown's inner circle specifically to serve as an information conduit from Crown to Church. Every Crown inner circle member knows this. Almud knows this. Linder knows they know.

His Conviction is Faith; his RS is Authority; his Certainty is 5. Disposition toward any visible Thread-practitioner player is structurally −2 (he cannot assess Thread-practitioners as anything other than spiritually compromised, regardless of their personal qualities).

**His institutional position:**
He is not a spy. He is an official representative of the Church's interests in the Crown's inner circle — a formally acknowledged diplomatic presence. His information-sharing with Himlensendt is not secret; it is his mandate. The inner circle accepted his presence knowing what he would do. This creates a strange structural condition: he is completely transparent about doing something that would normally be considered a breach of inner circle confidentiality.

The question his campaign explores: what does it mean to serve faithfully in a position where the dual loyalties are not hidden but irresolvable?

### Season Structure

**Dual reporting cycle:**
- Weekly report to Himlensendt: inner circle agenda items, Almud's stated priorities, any visible signs of Crown policy toward Church territory
- Quarterly review with Father Linder's Canon superior in T9 (he is a seconded Canon, not a permanently transferred one — his canonical home is still Himmelenger)
- Inner circle meetings: observes, contributes theological perspective when asked, reports what he observes

**What he can and cannot share:**
He has no formal prohibition on sharing inner circle information with Himlensendt — that is explicitly his function. But he has developed, over 8 seasons of service, an internal framework for what he shares and how he frames it. He shares factual information about Crown's actions and public positions. He does not share private conversations. He does not share personal assessments of inner circle members that were offered in confidence. He has built an ethical framework for his dual role that he has never formally articulated and that no one has ever asked him to justify.

This framework matters: Thale's bifurcated intelligence (from Batch 3) assumes the report she stages for the formal channel (flowing through Linder) contains accurate but managed information. She has been managing around Linder's reporting by ensuring certain information never reaches the inner circle in contexts he would observe. She does not trust him to be discreet. She is correct that he would report it. She is incorrect that he is indiscreet — he would report it because reporting is his function, and reporting accurate information is how he maintains the trustworthiness that makes him useful.

**[FINDING LIND-SIM-01: The mutual management that Thale and Linder have engaged in for 8 seasons is an institutionally stable solution to an institutionally impossible position. Thale manages what information reaches Linder by controlling what reaches the inner circle in his presence. Linder reports what he observes fully and accurately. Neither deceives the other. Both operate within their own institutional mandates. The result is a structurally honest dual-service arrangement where both parties are doing their jobs correctly and the combination produces a managed information flow. This is the real answer to the "spy in the inner circle" problem: not exposure or confrontation, but mutual institutional competence.]**

### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Weekly Reports

**The content of his reports:**
S1: Crown Treaty preparation with Guilds visible. Almud's Priority 8 Default (Diplomacy) firing. Expected factional position for upcoming seasons.  
S3: Reichard's Haushalt audit complete. Crown Wealth yield increasing (Competence 3 established). Crown's fiscal position is more stable than Church's current Mandate vs. Wealth tracking suggests it should be — indicating Crown has an unreported efficiency gain.

This last observation is the most important piece of intelligence Linder provides in the first 3 seasons: he has noticed that Crown's Wealth is growing faster than their Domain Actions should produce. He does not know about Haushalt Competence 3 specifically (it is an internal Ministry detail). He reports: "Crown Wealth yield appears to be running approximately 2 units/season above their reported Domain Action outputs. Source of discrepancy unclear." Himlensendt forwards this to his fiscal equivalent (Tormann/Prudence Cardinal) for analysis. Church's economic intelligence on Crown improves.

**S6 — Voss's reserve:**
Linder observes a Kriegsamt budget line in a routine financial review (Almud circulates inner circle financial summaries monthly). "Extended Field Readiness Protocol: 1 Wealth/season, indefinite." He reports it to Himlensendt: "Crown Kriegsamt is running an undocumented reserve protocol. Nature and purpose unclear; described only as 'deterrence value.'" Church files the observation alongside Reichard's previously noted Wealth discrepancy. Two data points, no conclusion yet.

**S8 — His private tension:**
Father Linder's weekly report to Himlensendt this season is the first that contains something he hesitates to include. Almud, in a private moment before an inner circle meeting that Linder attended, expressed something to Voss that Linder overheard: "I know the Southernmost is failing. I've known for two seasons. The question is what to do about it." This was not addressed to Linder. It was not said in confidence — it was said in Linder's presence without acknowledgment of his presence, which is a gray zone.

If he reports it: Church gains confirmation that Almud is privately aware of RS decline (a fact that contradicts Crown's public position of benign confidence in peninsula stability). If he does not: he is selectively filtering a factual inner circle moment based on his own assessment of its sensitivity. This is a departure from his existing framework (report factual information about Crown's actions and public positions; do not share private conversations).

Was this a private conversation? It was said in his presence without addressing him. He is a diplomatic witness, not a private confidant. He files the question in his private notes and does not report the specific comment. He reports instead: "Crown's inner circle is discussing stability of the Southernmost region. Details not specified in contexts I attended." This is accurate. It is also filtered. He has moved the line for the first time.

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Confessor's Erosion

**S12 — Himlensendt's Scar 1:**
Linder's weekly report from this season reflects a different tone in what he receives from Himlensendt. The Confessor's responses to his reports have been slower, more qualified. He notices: Himlensendt is asking him more questions and offering less direction. This is an inversion of their 8-season dynamic. The Confessor who always knew what to do is now waiting to understand before deciding.

This matters to Linder personally. His Faith Conviction requires a reliable authority to anchor to. Himlensendt has been that authority. An uncertain Himlensendt is a disruption to Linder's own Conviction structure — not a crisis, because his Certainty 5 is stable, but a perturbation he registers.

He begins including in his reports a secondary section: not just Crown intelligence, but his own theological assessment of Crown's situation from a Church perspective. He has not been asked to do this. He does it because he is uncertain whether Himlensendt is reading his reports with the same critical attention he once brought, and the theological framing helps organize both his own thoughts and the Confessor's potential response.

**S14 — The Evidence confrontation:**
Baralta's Evidence evidence attack on Church (Sovereign Authority Doctrine implemented, Heresy Investigation blocked in T10). Linder's report: "Hafenmark has formally invoked constitutional jurisdiction over ecclesiastical administrative actions in its territories. The motion passed 3-1. Church's Inquisitorial presence in T10 has been formally restricted. This constitutes a precedent with peninsula-wide implications." He flags this as Priority 1 in his report — the highest urgency he has ever assigned to a Crown-era report. Himlensendt's response arrives quickly: "The Church notes the precedent. Preparatory theological review of the jurisdictional conflict will be conducted."

The preparatory review is Klapp's function. Linder does not know this. He knows only that the Confessor has received his urgent flag and is responding through institutional channels. The system is working.

**[FINDING LIND-SIM-02: Father Linder's most significant function is not as an information conduit — it is as an early warning sensor that activates Church's institutional responses to Crown-adjacent political developments. His Priority 1 flag on Baralta's Sovereign Authority Doctrine gets to Himlensendt faster than any other channel would carry the same information. The value of the Archbishop's Representative in the inner circle is not the intelligence he provides; it is the fact that his presence ensures Church receives actionable intelligence at a speed that allows institutional response before the political advantage is fully established by the acting faction. He is a speed optimization in Church's information processing.]**

### Stress Tests

**Stress 1 — Almud's Arc B (S16):** Almud doubles down on Order. His inner circle posture shifts to defensive. He becomes less communicative in the sessions Linder attends. Linder's reports thin out: less content to report because less is said. He notes this in his report: "Crown inner circle sessions have become more limited in agenda. The King is managing more decisions outside the formal inner circle structure." Himlensendt's response: "Note whether this correlates with the Löwenritter's Coup Counter movement." This is the first time Himlensendt has explicitly referenced the Coup Counter to Linder. Linder did not know the Counter existed. He now knows the Church is tracking it. He adds it to his observation parameters without being asked.

**Stress 2 — His own Conviction crisis (S20):** AER drops to 2 (from the Reformed Settlement Baralta obtained). Linder receives a theological directive from Himmelenger: "Reformed Settlement activity in Hafenmark territory is to be noted and investigated as a potential heresy enabler (PT/CV disruption of established piety patterns)." He is being asked to use his Crown inner circle position to facilitate Church investigation of a Hafenmark parliamentary action that the Crown inner circle supported. This is the first time his function as information conduit is being asked to serve a specifically hostile action against the faction he is formally representing. He complies with the directive — the theological review is legitimate under Church law. He also sends Almud's Lord Treasurer (Reichard) an informal note: "Church is conducting a theological review of the Reformed Settlement parameters. You may wish to have Crown's Parliamentary representative prepared to address ecclesiastical questions about the Settlement's constitutional basis." He has warned both sides. He has told neither about the other's warning.

## NPC 04 — CARDINAL JARNSTAL (Fortitude, Templar Branch)

### Who He Is

Cardinal Jarnstal is the Church's military arm. Faith+Order, Consequence RS. He deploys Templars aggressively; prioritises territorial defense over theological expansion. Per §2.13: "Consequence (military failure)" is his RS — he is moved by demonstrated failure of military protection, not by doctrinal argument.

He is not interested in theology as a practice. He is interested in theology as a basis for jurisdiction — specifically, the jurisdictional claim that Church properties require ecclesiastical military defense regardless of civil authority. His Templar network is, in his view, the physical expression of the Church's legitimate sovereignty over its own institutions. Everything else (TC advancement, AER, Himlensendt's pastoral vision) is superstructure built on top of the military fact that Templars hold what they hold.

**His relationship to other Cardinals:**
- **Klapp:** Jarnstal respects Klapp's intelligence and finds his archive work irrelevant. The Wardens are not the Church's problem; the Church's territories are.
- **Olafsson:** Natural ally in discipline and enforcement. Differs on method: Jarnstal prefers visible force; Olafsson prefers formal procedure. They get along well because they agree on what threatens the Church and disagree only on what to do about it.
- **Tormann (Prudence):** Jarnstal finds Tormann's fiscal focus appropriate but limited. "Money follows power. Protect the territory and the money follows."
- **Himlensendt:** Jarnstal serves loyally while privately viewing the Confessor's pastoral vision as temperamentally insufficient for the situation. He does not think Himlensendt is wrong. He thinks Himlensendt is not enough.

### Season Structure

**The Templar deployment portfolio:**
At Templar Commander level, Jarnstal manages Church's military assets across all Cathedral settlements. His seasonal priorities:
1. Garrison adequacy: every Cathedral settlement must have at minimum a Templar-Sergeant and 2 squadrons
2. Threat assessment: which external faction poses the highest threat to a specific Cathedral territory this season?

**Fortitude Dicastery Competence:**

**[FINDING JAR-SIM-01: Jarnstal's commitment to Dicastery Competence 3 is the inversion of the standard faction leader dynamic. ### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Garrison Map

**S1–4 — Systematic fortification:**
Jarnstal's opening moves are not dramatic. He reviews every Cathedral settlement's Templar garrison status. He finds: T9 Himmelenger is adequately garrisoned. T10 Spartfell (when Church-controlled) is borderline. T3 Lowenskyst and T5 Feldmark are defended by proximity to Crown's garrisons, not by Templar presence.

He proposes to Himlensendt: a systematic rotation of Templar Commander assignments, shifting experienced Commanders to contested frontier settlements and moving less experienced Commanders to well-established Cathedral interiors. This is resource-neutral (same number of Templars, different distribution). It is strategically significant (contested settlements get experienced leadership). Himlensendt approves without detailed engagement — Jarnstal handles military dispositions.

**S5 — The Hafenmark problem:**
Baralta's Sovereign Authority Doctrine (implemented S5 per other simulations) creates a direct challenge to Templar authority in T10. The Doctrine's claim: Church administrative actions (including Templar garrison operations) in Hafenmark territory require Parliamentary authorization. Jarnstal's assessment: the Doctrine does not apply to ecclesiastical immunity (OFC-04: Templars can garrison Cathedral settlements and defend Church property without provincial authorization). The jurisdictional conflict is specific: can Templars in T10 deploy outside the Cathedral perimeter without Parliamentary sanction?

He rules internally: no, they cannot. The Doctrine correctly constrains Templar action to Cathedral-perimeter defense in Hafenmark territory. He instructs his T10 Templar Commander accordingly. This is compliance with the Doctrine — not because he agrees with it, but because the Doctrine is formally correct in this specific application, and fighting it on the wrong ground would weaken the broader ecclesiastical immunity claim.

**The distinction he is maintaining:** Ecclesiastical immunity (Templars defend Church property) is constitutionally grounded and should be defended to the maximum. Provincial jurisdiction over Church administrative actions (Templars deploy for Church political purposes in Hafenmark) is correctly constrained by the Doctrine. He refuses to conflate the two, even when Olafsson wants to challenge the Doctrine wholesale.

**[FINDING JAR-SIM-02: Jarnstal's partial compliance with Baralta's Doctrine is the most sophisticated move any Cardinal makes in early-game Church governance. By correctly identifying which part of the Doctrine applies to Templar operations and complying with only that part, he preserves the strongest possible constitutional basis for ecclesiastical immunity while eliminating Baralta's political argument that Church is violating Hafenmark's sovereignty. She cannot attack the part of Templar authority that he has voluntarily limited. She can only attack the part he has retained — and that part is on the strongest possible constitutional ground.]**

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Stability Crisis

**S12 — Church Stability ≤ 2 (triggered by sustained political pressure):**
When Church Stability drops below 2 (from the combined pressure of Parliamentary Censure, Baralta's Doctrine enforcement, and Himlensendt's Scar accumulation), Cardinals activate as independent actors per §2.13. Jarnstal's Schism behavior: "Deploys Templars aggressively; prioritises territorial defence over theological expansion."

What "aggressive deployment" looks like in practice: Jarnstal does not attack. He pre-positions. He moves Templar Commander units from interior Cathedral settlements to the contested perimeter settlements (T10, T6 where applicable). He is not violating Baralta's Doctrine (he stays within Cathedral perimeters). He is maximizing defensive presence at the locations where Church's territorial claims are most contested. Anyone looking at the map sees a militarized Church frontier. Anyone who understands Jarnstal knows: he is building a defensive posture, not an offensive one.

The political effect is indistinguishable from the military effect: the Church's contested territories now have significantly more Templar presence. Whether this is defense or intimidation depends on interpretation. Jarnstal doesn't care which interpretation is used. The result is the same either way.

**S16 — Olafsson's challenge:**
Olafsson proposes using the Church Stability crisis to accelerate Heresy Proceedings in territories where TC advancement has been blocked by Baralta's Doctrine. His argument: the crisis is an opportunity to assert Church authority where it has been constrained. Jarnstal disagrees, privately and explicitly: "A Heresy Proceeding during a Stability crisis is an institutional overreach that will invite exactly the Parliamentary counter-response that made the crisis worse. Defend what we hold. Do not advance while we are bleeding."

This is the sharpest disagreement between the two most capable Cardinals. Olafsson: procedure and enforcement. Jarnstal: position and attrition. At the College vote (Himlensendt is in Scar 2 crisis, casts a weak vote): Jarnstal and Olafsson 2-2 (Klapp with Jarnstal, Tormann with neither firmly). Himlensendt's weak casting vote: defensive posture (Jarnstal wins by default — the Confessor, in uncertainty, chooses the less aggressive option). Olafsson files the result and says nothing further. The silence is significant.

## NPC 05 — THORVALD HANN (Eastern March Jarl, Varfell Inner Circle)

### Who He Is

Thorvald Hann controls Varfell's largest military bloc. Order/Authority Conviction, Certainty 5. Bloodline-focused succession advocacy. Disposition toward Maret Uln: −1. He is not anti-Einhir as a personal position — he is pro-institution, and he believes that institutions depend on established succession criteria, and established succession criteria in Varfell favor hereditary bloodline claims.

His argument is not that Maret Uln is unworthy. His argument is that if Varfell abandons hereditary succession criteria in favor of merit-based Deed-Claims, it has changed what it is. The Assembly that elects on merit is not the Assembly that sustained Varfell through the post-Calamity period. He is defending a principle, not attacking a person.

He is also wrong, and he knows he is in a structural minority. Stenskald will not endorse the bloodline claim over the Deed-Claim. Björn Holdar (Western Highlands) is resource-focused and will vote for whoever promises continued settlement development. Maret Uln has his former boss's ear (she served as Huscarl Captain — she has access to Vaynard that Hann does not).

He is a conservative who is losing the argument in slow motion and knows it. Playing him means playing that position with full commitment and the dignity of genuine belief.

### Season Structure

**Council of the March:**
Hann chairs the Council of the March (military frontier). This is Varfell's equivalent of the Kriegsamt — Eastern military frontier management, Altonian invasion preparation. He has 3 squadrons under direct command and authority to conscript additional forces from his March territories in crisis conditions.

**The Readiness Track:**
Council of the March Readiness 0–3. Hann maintains it at Readiness 2 as baseline (adequate preparation, normal function) and pushes to Readiness 3 when Institutional Pressure (IP) reaches 40+ (Schoenland Priority 2 fires; Altonian Preparation beginning). At Readiness 3: Hann may authorize action in the Council's jurisdiction without Vaynard's approval.

**His intelligence position:**
The Eastern March borders on territories relevant to multiple factions. He receives intelligence about Altonian Vanguard deployment patterns, Schoenland trade routes, and (crucially) Niflhel activity in the eastern supply corridor. He is, in some ways, the best-positioned Jarl to understand the external threat to Valoria — and this shapes his political conservatism. He believes Varfell must maintain internal institutional coherence specifically because the external threat is real and growing.

### Phase 1 (S1–8): The Military Jarl

**S1–4 — Readiness building:**
Hann's early game is pure military professionalism. Rotate garrison assignments. Train new officers. Maintain supply lines. Review Schoenland border intelligence (from his March position he receives reports that no other Jarl has direct access to — Schoenland's diplomatic mission occasionally passes through Eastern March territory, and the passage generates observational intelligence about mission composition and timing).

**His S3 intelligence finding:**
Schoenland diplomatic mission passes through T4 Grauwald (adjacent to Eastern March). The mission's composition has changed: there are two additional advisors with Altonian imperial livery this season who were not present in prior seasons. This is the first visible signal that Altonian interest in Valoria is intensifying. Hann reports it through the Jarl Assembly's Council of Voices (diplomatic intelligence channel). It reaches Vaynard.

**S7 — The genealogical research:**
Hann engages a Skald-adjacent scholar (a figure who knows Varfell's oral tradition but operates in the space between formal oral law and academic genealogy) to trace Vaynard's collateral bloodline. This is not a secret project — he mentions it to the Assembly as "standard succession preparedness research." Stenskald notes the research with quiet attention. Hann finds his candidate: a nephew of a figure two generations removed from Vaynard's bloodline, currently farming in T11 Halvardshelm. He has never held any Assembly position. He is a hereditary claimant with no institutional presence.

This is Hann's fundamental problem: the best hereditary candidate is someone who has no institutional preparation for leadership. His Deed-Claim opponent (Maret Uln) has served as Huscarl Captain, understands Thread practice, has relationships across the Assembly, and has a coherent governance vision. The genealogical claimant has a bloodline and nothing else.

**[FINDING HANN-SIM-01: Hann's genealogical research produces his own strongest counter-argument. By finding the best available hereditary candidate and discovering that candidate's institutional limitations, he has demonstrated precisely the problem with rigid hereditary succession: it produces claimants without capacity. His Certainty 5 prevents him from processing this as a refutation of his position. He processes it as a problem to be solved — if the candidate lacks institutional preparation, build institutional preparation. He begins quietly positioning the nephew in T11 governance roles. This will take 8–10 seasons. He does not have 8–10 seasons. He does not know this.]**

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Constituency Problem

**S12 — Björn Holdar's question:**
Holdar (Western Highlands Jarl) approaches Hann privately: "Your genealogical candidate has no presence in the Western Highlands. He has never worked with anyone west of T12. If I am going to vote for him at Assembly, I need to know he understands what the western territories require." Hann: "I can arrange an introductory mission." Holdar: "Not a mission. Understanding. From working in the Highlands, not visiting."

This is the thing Hann cannot provide. He is asking Assembly members to support a candidate they do not know, for reasons that are abstract (bloodline continuity), against a candidate they have worked alongside. His political coalition is formal (2 of 5 Jarls initially aligned) and fragile (neither of the 2 knows the candidate personally).

**S14 — Maret Uln's Council presence:**
Maret Uln, as Huscarl Captain, begins attending Council of the Warden sessions (she is eligible per her dual role). Hann observes: she is building her own Council presence — not challenging his, but creating an institutional track record that would support a succession case. He raises this at Assembly: "The Huscarl Captain is attending multiple Council sessions outside her formal role." Stenskald: "The Councils are open to Assembly members. The Huscarl Captain holds inner circle status. Her attendance is within protocol." Hann has no counter. The observation is noted. Nothing changes.

**The diminishing constituency:**
By S18, Hann's genealogical candidate has been positioned in T11 in a minor administrative role (settlement governance, 1 season). He has made 3 appearances at Assembly functions. He is still unknown to the Western Highlands. Holdar has not committed. Hann is running out of Assembly seasons to build the coalition he needs.

### Phase 3 (S19–28): The Assembly

**S22 — Vaynard eliminated:**
SUC-03 fires. Hann has 2 of 5 Jarls (himself and one allied Jarl from the Eastern March). He needs 3. Holdar has not committed. Stenskald will not endorse either candidate. The fifth Jarl (Maret Uln's aligned Warden-Council member) is likely for Maret Uln.

He knows the math. He calls a private meeting with Holdar before the formal Assembly convenes. His argument: "The bloodline continuity is not about this candidate specifically. It is about what Varfell commits to when it makes this decision. If we elect a Deed-Claim candidate, we have changed what the Assembly is. The next time there is a succession question, the precedent will be: merit matters more than bloodline. That is a different Varfell than the one that survived the Calamity."

Holdar listens. Then: "And if we elect a bloodline candidate who cannot govern the Western Highlands effectively, what have we preserved? The form without the function?" Long pause. "I am going to abstain." 

Abstention means: 2 Hann, 1 Maret Uln's aligned, 2 abstentions (Holdar and Stenskald). Majority not achieved by either. Assembly goes to a formal vote with secondary conditions. Under oral law (Stenskald's jurisdiction), a tied Assembly defaults to a trial to determine readiness — not combat, but a Grand Contest (all modes), Ob 3. Maret Uln vs. the genealogical claimant. Thread awareness, governance experience, relationship to oral tradition: she wins 2 of 3 modes. The genealogical claimant wins 1.

Hann's candidate has a better bloodline and a worse Grand Contest result. Maret Uln becomes Hochjarl.

**Hann's response:** He affirms the Assembly's decision. He serves the new Hochjarl. His Order Conviction is the thing that makes this possible: the Assembly process is legitimate, and its outcome is therefore legitimate. He does not agree with the outcome. He accepts its authority. He begins working with Maret Uln on Eastern March military readiness — because the Altonian threat is real and her governance needs his Council's full operational capacity.

His private record: "S22: Grand Contest result determines succession. Oral law invoked correctly. The principle of merit has been formalized in Varfell succession precedent. This is not what Varfell was. It may be what Varfell needs."

## NPC 06 — MARET ULN (Varfell — Pre-Succession Self-Construction)

### The Campaign Before the Campaign

Previous simulations treated Maret Uln as an inheritor — someone who takes over after Vaynard's elimination. This simulation runs her as an agent: from Season 1, she is building the conditions in which her eventual claim will be recognized as legitimate. She does not know Vaynard will be eliminated. She is not plotting against him. She is preparing for a future that her Equity Conviction and Reason secondary tell her is coming: eventually, Varfell's Thread-acquisitive model will produce a crisis, and when it does, someone needs to be ready to redirect the faction.

She is making herself that person before the need is visible.

**Starting position:**
- Huscarl Captain (inner circle, Vaynard's personal guard and loyalty signal)
- TS 35, Coherence 10 (Active tier practitioner — the highest TS of anyone in Varfell's current inner circle)
- Conviction: Equity (primary), Reason (secondary)
- Certainty 2 (direct Thread experience has destabilised worldview)
- Her dual affiliation: Varfell institutional loyalty AND Southern Einhir community solidarity (per npc_roster §14)

**What "pre-succession self-construction" means:**
She is building the three things she will need when succession comes:
1. **Institutional track record** — Assembly Councils participation, governance presence, Duty completion across multiple Varfell contexts
2. **Coalition** — relationships with Jarls and inner circle members that produce Assembly support without appearing calculated
3. **Thread legitimacy** — the one thing Hann's bloodline candidate cannot match: she is a practicing Thread-worker at Active tier, which is the only thing that makes Varfell's VTM-based victory paths achievable. Any Hochjarl who cannot engage with Thread work is structurally limited.

### Phase 1 (S1–8): Building Without Asking

**Her core discipline:**
Maret Uln never asks for anything directly. Every position she holds comes from demonstrating value in the role she already has. This is not false modesty — it is strategic restraint. She has watched Vaynard long enough to know: he promotes people who make him look good, not people who compete with him. She makes him look good. This preserves her access.

**Huscarl Captain responsibilities:**
The Huscarl Captain guards Vaynard personally. This means she accompanies him to every significant event: Jarl Assembly, diplomatic meetings, Thread research sessions. She has observed more of Vaynard's actual decision-making than any other inner circle member. She knows where his Consequentialist Framework produces blind spots (long-term investments that don't have measurable outcomes within one season get systematically underweighted). She files these observations in her own internal record.

**S3 — First Thread operation:**
Vaynard's Private Collection deployment triggers a Discovery Event check. His Spirit check fails (from other simulation analysis). Maret Uln is present. She manages his physiological response to the Thread sensitivity expansion — not dramatic, but she has been trained (TS 35, Coherence 10, trained practitioner) for exactly this transition. She provides grounding contact.

Vaynard notices. He asks afterward: "How did you know what to do?" Her answer: "I have done this before. For myself, and for others." He looks at her for a moment. He files this information but does not immediately change anything. She has revealed her practitioner depth to him at exactly the moment when that depth was needed — not as a credential presentation, but as an action.

**[FINDING MULN-SIM-01: Maret Uln's revelation of practitioner depth at Vaynard's Discovery Event is her most important early-game relationship investment. She did not introduce herself as a practitioner. She demonstrated it when it was useful, in a way that served him rather than herself. This is the Solidarity RS expressed institutionally: she built the relationship through action at the right moment. Vaynard's trust in her will be substantially grounded in this single scene for the next 20 seasons. She has made herself necessary before asking for anything.]**

**S5 — Council of the Warden:**
Maret Uln requests formal assignment to the Council of the Warden (the Jarl Assembly Council handling Thread-related matters and Warden liaison — currently Readiness 0, effectively inoperative). Her argument to Vaynard: "VTM advancement requires coordinated Warden liaison work. The Council should be operationally capable. I have the Thread background to build its Readiness." Vaynard authorizes it.

She builds Council of the Warden from Readiness 0 to Readiness 2 over 4 seasons. This produces: formal Varfell-Warden communication protocols (from Haelgrund's Protocol 3 work — she connects with Edeyja before Vaynard does, through the Council's formal mandate). WR track advancement begins through Council operations rather than pure Expedition.

**Her communication with Edeyja:**
She approaches Edeyja differently from Vaynard. Vaynard comes with data. Maret Uln comes as a practitioner: "I work at TS 35. I have read your monitoring reports through the Protocol 3 channel. What do you need that Varfell could provide without requiring you to come to us?" Edeyja's response: supply continuity and the cessation of Thread extraction operations in T4's eastern district (where Niflhel is running quiet operations that increase Thread Tension). Two things. Maret Uln delivers both within 3 seasons (supply via Council logistics; extraction cessation via Vaynard authorization, sold to him as "reducing Thread Tension to prevent Discovery Event frequency increase"). WR track: substantial advancement, faster than Vaynard's direct Expedition approach.

### Phase 2 (S9–18): The Coalition

**S10 — Stenskald's observation:**
From Stenskald's simulation (Batch 4): she observed Maret Uln's authentic response to the oral law recitation at the S4 Assembly. By S10, Stenskald has added a second observation: Maret Uln has built the Council of the Warden from Readiness 0 to Readiness 2 — institutional construction work that demonstrates governance capacity independent of her Thread background.

Stenskald requests a private meeting with Maret Uln. The meeting: Stenskald asks Maret Uln to recite a specific oral law passage from memory — one that is not usually taught outside the formal Skald tradition. It was taught in Southern Einhir oral tradition in slightly different form. Maret Uln recites the Varfell version, then the Southern Einhir variant. She notes the difference: "The Southern Einhir version preserves the original metric. The Varfell version adapted to the northern tongue pattern. They both say the same thing." Stenskald: "Not many people would know both." Maret Uln: "My grandmother taught me. She said the division was political, not cultural."

The meeting ends without formal conclusion. Stenskald has confirmed what she observed at the S4 Assembly: the connection is genuine, not performed.

**S14 — Hann's research becomes visible:**
The genealogical research Hann is conducting becomes visible at Assembly. Maret Uln observes it without visible reaction. Her internal assessment: Hann will produce a candidate who is genealogically valid and institutionally empty. When that candidate appears, the Assembly will see the contrast without her having to create it. Her strategy: continue building the Council, continue the WR track, continue being useful to Vaynard. Do not compete with what Hann is doing. Do not respond to it. Let the work speak.

**S16 — Thread Supremacy path visible:**
Vaynard is pursuing Path C (Thread Supremacy) based on Maret Uln's Council infrastructure. She has built the operational framework that makes Path C viable — the Warden liaison, the RS maintenance commitment, the Thread Tension management in eastern T4. She has made herself structurally indispensable to the victory condition her nominal leader is pursuing. If Vaynard wins on Path C, a significant portion of the infrastructure is hers. If Vaynard fails or is eliminated, the infrastructure continues under her leadership.

**[FINDING MULN-SIM-02: Maret Uln has built a win-win position across all Vaynard arc outcomes. If he wins: she contributed substantially and her Council standing is publicly recognized. If he fails or is eliminated: she is the most capable institutional actor to continue the work. If he enters Arc C (Consumed): her practitioner training makes her the only person who can help him — and helping him demonstrates her capacity even if he cannot be saved. She has aligned her personal development with Vaynard's success in a way that makes her indispensable regardless of whether he succeeds. This is the Equity Conviction applied to institutional construction: the work should serve everyone, and when it does, it serves her as well.]**

### Phase 3 (S19–28): The Succession and After

**S22 — Vaynard's elimination:**
The succession crisis fires. Maret Uln is ready. She has: Assembly coalition approaching (Stenskald's equal-validity ruling, Holdar's abstention path identified through her Highlands relationship work in S18–20), Council of the Warden at Readiness 3, WR track at 3 (Edeyja's active collaboration), Thread operation capacity at Active tier.

Her Grand Contest performance (from Stenskald's simulation): she wins 2 of 3 modes. Not because she is superior in raw capability to the genealogical claimant — she is not genealogically superior. She is institutionally, practically, and relationally superior. The oral law recognizes this, because Stenskald structured the trial to test exactly those things.

**Post-succession governance:**
Maret Uln as Hochjarl is not a departure from who she was as Huscarl Captain. She has been building Varfell governance capacity for 22 seasons. The transition is the moment she had always been preparing for. Her first Assembly as Hochjarl: she affirms the oral law recitation. She completes it from memory, both versions. She addresses Thorvald Hann directly: "The Council of the March needs to be at Readiness 3 within 2 seasons. Institutional Pressure is at 55. You are the only person who can build it to operational capacity that fast. I need you for this." He serves.

## CROSS-SIMULATION FINDINGS

### FINDING CROSS-B5-01: The Advisor Becomes the Bottleneck

In Batch 5, two characters (Heljason and Linder) serve as information processors between principals. Both face the same structural problem: the information they handle is more consequential than the principals recognize. Heljason knows Baralta's constitutional case is built on a brief he wouldn't sign as accurate. Linder knows Himlensendt's questions to him have become more uncertain. Both filter this knowledge institutionally — neither tells the principal the full picture. The advisor becomes the place where inconvenient truths are managed rather than delivered. This is not failure; it is the structural consequence of the advisor's position. The principal needs confidence to act. The advisor manages information to preserve confidence. The bottleneck is systemic.

### FINDING CROSS-B5-02: Coalition Logic Is Asymmetric

Hann needs 3 of 5 Assembly votes. He builds 2. His marginal vote (Holdar) abstains rather than supporting him. Maret Uln needs only to not-lose — abstentions send the question to oral law trial, which she wins. Her coalition strategy is therefore: secure 1 vote (her Warden-Council ally), prevent Hann from securing Holdar, and trust Stenskald's equal-validity ruling to ensure the oral law trial occurs. She is playing a negative-coalition game (prevent his majority) rather than a positive-coalition game (build her majority). This is more efficient and less visible. Building a majority requires active solicitation; preventing a majority requires only that you have done your work well enough that the middle votes don't feel safe committing to the other side.

### FINDING CROSS-B5-03: Long-Horizon Investment Wins Mid-Range Crises

Every character in this batch who succeeds does so by investing in a longer horizon than their opponents:
- Reichard builds Haushalt Competence 3 in S1–4, which produces Wealth buffer at S12+ when the Coup threatens
- Jarnstal maintains Templar Readiness so that the Stability crisis (S12) does not trigger his Independence Card
- Heljason writes the counter-argument addendum in S6, which he uses when the T3 jurisdiction argument is challenged in S14
- Maret Uln builds Council of the Warden from S5, which is the infrastructure for Vaynard's Path C win and her own succession legitimacy at S22

The character who is managing only this season's problem loses to the character who has been building for two seasons from now.

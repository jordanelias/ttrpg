## SIMULATION 37 — E-01: TS 100 PRACTITIONER AT COHERENCE 0 (Mode D)

Per §9.16: Coherence 0 prevents Thread operations. Per §9.17: another practitioner may perform corrective Weaving at Ob 5 to restore Coherence to 1.

**Edge case: Can the TS 100 practitioner perform corrective Weaving on themselves?**

§3.4 Community Intervention: "another practitioner may perform corrective Weaving... At Coherence 4–3. Below Coherence 3: requires the degraded practitioner's active cooperation, which their loosened categories make increasingly unlikely."

§9.17: "another practitioner" — the corrective Weaving rule explicitly requires a different practitioner. Self-targeted corrective Weaving at Coherence 0 is not permitted by the text.

**Why this matters:** A TS 100 practitioner alone (last surviving practitioner, isolated) cannot self-rescue from Rendering Crisis. They must find another practitioner — which may be narratively impossible.

**Finding (SIM7-F-01, P2):** The corrective Weaving "another practitioner" requirement creates a hard dependency — solo practitioners cannot recover from Rendering Crisis without assistance. This is philosophically coherent (you cannot render yourself back into rendering — that requires an external rendering agent). But the rule should be explicit about why, not just that. Add design note to §9.17.

**The TS 100 detail:** TS makes no difference. Coherence 0 = incapacity regardless of TS. The most sensitive practitioner who ever lived cannot perform Thread operations at Coherence 0. ✓

## SIMULATION 38 — E-02: COLLECTIVE OF 5 FRAGMENTED PRACTITIONERS (Mode C)

### Setup

5 practitioners, all at Coherence 4 (Fragmented). Attempting a Collective Structural Weaving (Ob 5).

**Individual pools:**
- Anchor (Spirit 5, Cog 4, TPS 9): 5+3+9 = 17D base. Fragmented +1 Ob. Ob = 5+1 = 6.
- Helpers ×4: floor(Cog 4 ÷ 2) = +2D each. Total helper contribution: +8D.
- **Collective pool: 25D vs Ob 6**

P(≥6 net at 25D): ~97%

**Coherence costs:**
- All 5 practitioners: Structural scale −1 (cap). Each: Coherence 4→3.
- All 5 cross into Fragmented Fallout territory... wait — they're already in Fragmented (4–3). They stay in Fragmented. No new threshold crossed at 3. ✓

**Lattice fracture threshold:** Half Anchor's pool = 17D ÷ 2 = 8.5 → 9D. With 8D helpers, pool = 25D. If 3 helpers drop: pool = 17 + 2 = 19D. Still above 9D. If all 4 helpers drop: pool = 17D = exactly above 9D (threshold is "below half" = below 8.5 = 8D or less). At 17D solo: no fracture. ✓

**Fragmented −1D to social rolls:** This applies to social operations, not Thread pools. Thread pool construction (Spirit + History + TPS) is unaffected by Fragmented's −1D social penalty. The +1 Ob (Thread operations) does apply. ✓

**Finding: A collective of Fragmented practitioners is highly effective at high Obs.** 25D at Ob 6 = 97% success. Five degraded practitioners compensate for individual impairment through collective pooling. The system doesn't penalise collective operations for participant Coherence state (beyond each participant's +1 Ob contribution to the Anchor's Ob). 

**New finding (SIM7-F-02, P2):** Fragmented +1 Ob applies to the Anchor's Ob, but the Helpers' Fragmented state adds no additional Ob penalty. Five Fragmented helpers vs five healthy helpers: identical contribution. The helpers' cognitive impairment (Fragmented: Memory rolls penalised, social rolls penalised) has no mechanical effect on their Thread contribution (floor(Cognition ÷ 2) — Cognition itself is not penalised by Fragmented). 

**Proposed:** This is probably correct — the helpers' contribution is their configuration's intentionality alignment, not their conscious cognitive state. Fragmented affects rendering-based faculties (social, memory), not the pre-conscious intentionality contribution. Flag as P3 — design note only.

## SIMULATION 39 — E-03: PAST-ORIENTED PULL ON THE EINHIR CATASTROPHE (Mode A)

**Attempting to displace a Foundational event 245 years ago.**

**Eligibility:**
- TS 70+ ✓ (required)
- RS ≤ 60: At RS 60 exactly — barely eligible. At RS 61: cannot attempt. This is the first eligibility cliff: campaign RS must be degraded enough for temporal depth to be accessible.
- Diagnosis mandatory ✓

**Pool (Option B):** Spirit + History + TPS÷2. Maximum conceivable: Spirit 6 + History 5 + TPS 10÷2 = 16D.

**Ob by recency (245 years = 10+ seasons/generational):** Ob 7.

But wait — the Einhir Catastrophe is not merely old. It is a **Foundational-scale event**. The Pulling table shows Ob by actualization level, not recency for non-Past-Oriented Pulling. Past-Oriented Pulling uses recency. The Catastrophe is the maximum recency tier (Ob 7) but is also Foundational scale.

**Gap (SIM7-F-03, P1):** No rule for Past-Oriented Pulling at Foundational scale. The operation exists for personal/territorial events. The Einhir Catastrophe is Foundational. Does scale add Ob? Is it even possible?

**Proposed:** Past-Oriented Pulling is bounded by scale accessibility. The Pulling table shows minimum TS by actualization for standard Pulling — Foundational requires TS 70+ for standard Pulling, and Past-Oriented Pulling additionally requires RS ≤ 60. At Foundational scale, an additional prerequisite should apply: the Einhir ritual framework (§9.15). Without it, the practitioner cannot form intentionality directed at Foundational temporal depth. The scale is too far below the waterline.

With Einhir framework: possible. Ob = 7 (recency) + 2 (Foundational scale surcharge, proposed) = Ob 9. But Mending Ob ceiling is 8 — **Past-Oriented Pulling has no ceiling**. P(≥9 net at 16D): ~5%.

**This is intentional near-impossibility.** Displacing the Einhir Catastrophe is a campaign-ending act — the kind of thing that defines a generation of practitioners. 5% success with the most capable conceivable practitioner using the Einhir framework. ✓

**RS consequence on success:** RS −3 minimum. A Foundational displacement would likely produce RS −10 or more (Foundational-scale temporal interaction). **P1 FLAG (SIM7-F-03):** Add Foundational-scale Past-Oriented Pulling rules: requires Einhir framework, +2 Ob surcharge, RS consequence ×3 (−9 minimum on success). And explicitly state: displacing the Einhir Catastrophe is mechanically possible at 5% probability — it is a legitimate (if near-mythic) campaign endpoint.

## SIMULATION 40 — E-04: RS 1 — ONE OPERATION FROM RUPTURE (Mode D)

**RS: 1. One Thread operation failure = Rupture.**

Any operation at RS 1 carries Rupture risk:

**Weaving Failure:** RS −2 (Object/Personal scale minimum per degree table with patched P-25 scaling). RS 1 − 2 = −1 → **Rupture**.

**Mending Failure:** RS −2. Rupture.

**Dissolution Success:** RS −5 (Personal) to −8 (Structural). Rupture regardless of scale.

**Dissolution Partial:** RS −6. Rupture.

**Lock Failure:** RS −3. Rupture.

**Only safe operations at RS 1:**
- Weaving Success/Overwhelming (Object/Personal): RS unchanged or +1. Safe.
- Pulling Success: RS unchanged. Safe.
- Mending Overwhelming: RS +2. Safe — but P(Overwhelming at Catastrophic Gap, RS 1 = Critical band +1 Ob) ≈ 5%.

**Finding (SIM7-F-04, P2):** At RS 1, almost every Thread operation carries Rupture risk on Failure. The world has become so fragile that practitioners are forced into a terrible choice: stop operating (and let the world continue degrading from Locks, Gaps, winter drift) or operate and risk Rupture on every roll.

**The natural floor problem:** RS 1 without active operations still reaches 0 within 1–3 seasons from Lock drift + winter. Not operating doesn't prevent Rupture — it delays it.

**No rule addresses this endgame trap.** The RS Critical design note (§5.3) describes the trajectory but doesn't provide any mechanical escape valve. The only structural exit is: remove all Locks (stops drift), Mend all Gaps (stops −4/season), then operate only at Object/Personal scale until RS recovers via Mending. But at RS 1: Mending Failure = Rupture. The escape valve requires successful Mending — which is ~17–34% depending on practitioner skill.

**This is the Einhir Catastrophe.** They reached this point. There was no escape. The game correctly replicates this. ✓ Document explicitly in §5.3 Critical design note (extend the note to address RS 1 specifically).

## SIMULATION 41 — E-05: FOCUS 1 PRACTITIONER ATTEMPTING STRUCTURAL WEAVING (Mode D)

**Focus 1:** Contact duration 1 round. Round 1 = Leap round (no operation). Contact expires. Zero operations.

**Structural Weaving requires at minimum Focus 2** (1 operation round). At Focus 1: the practitioner achieves contact but cannot operate at any scale. Per §2.3: "At Focus 1, the practitioner gains the experience of contact... but cannot perform operations."

**This is already defined.** Focus 1 = no operations regardless of operation scale. ✓

**The degenerate case:** A practitioner with Focus 1 and TS 70+ (eligible for Structural operations) can never perform Structural Weaving. Their TS opens the scale; their Focus prevents contact duration. The gate is Focus, not TS.

**Adjacent finding (SIM7-F-05, P3):** No advancement pathway is explicitly labelled "Focus improvement." Focus is an attribute — it improves via CP (presumably). But no character advancement rule in v2.5 specifies Focus as improvable, or at what CP cost. This is likely in the core character advancement section (not in v2.5 scope), but the Leap rules assume Focus improvement is possible without citing it. Cross-reference needed.

## SIMULATION 42 — E-06: THREADCUT BEING AS PRACTITIONER (Mode C)

**Setup:** A threadcut being (Solmund, TS 100) attempts Thread operations on external threads — not self-maintenance, but directed work on other configurations.

**Eligibility:**
- Approach Training: does a threadcut being require Approach Training? The being IS a permanent Leap. Their existence is originary intentionality. The concept of "suspending rendering to enter contact" doesn't apply — they have no ordinary rendering to suspend.

**Gap (SIM7-F-06, P1):** No rules for threadcut beings performing Thread operations on external configurations. All Thread operation rules presuppose an organic being who renders, Diagnoses, Leaps (suspends rendering), operates, and returns. A threadcut being's existence IS the Leap state — they have never been outside originary contact. Can they direct their intentionality toward external threads, or are they locked into self-maintenance?

**Proposed framework:**
- Threadcut beings can perform Thread operations — their permanent originary state IS the operational state. No Leap required (they're already there).
- No Coherence track — confirmed in §6.3.
- No Leap roll — they don't suspend rendering; they direct already-suspended intentionality.
- Operations: declare target and type; roll operation pool directly (Spirit + History + TPS).
- Cost: each external operation costs 1 Rendering Strain (the effort of directing intentionality outward rather than inward strains self-maintenance). This makes external Thread work dangerous for threadcut beings — it accelerates their De-Actualisation.

**Implications:** Solmund performing Structural Weaving costs 1 Rendering Strain per operation. If Solmund has Rendering Strain 5 and Health 6: one more external operation triggers De-Actualisation. The being who could save the world by one act of Structural Weaving faces the choice: operate (and begin De-Actualisation) or preserve self (and withhold the act).

This is philosophically rich and mechanically consistent. Flag as **SIM7-F-06 (P1)** — high design value, needs explicit rules.

## SIMULATION 43 — E-07: SIMULTANEOUS DISSOLUTION + MENDING ON SAME CONFIGURATION (Mode D)

**Setup:** Practitioner A (Dissolution, Ob 5) and Practitioner B (Mending — targeting a Shifting Object at the same site, Ob 2) both in contact simultaneously.

**§9.13** covers opposing Thread operations. Dissolution and Mending are not simply opposing — they are metaphysically contradictory: Dissolution tears the intelligible face; Mending repairs the substrate. Operating on the same configuration simultaneously.

**Is this covered by §9.13?** §9.13 addresses opposing *intentionalities* (Weave vs Pull). Dissolution vs Mending is a different conflict: one tears, one repairs. §9.13's "both succeed" produces a Shifting Object. But if Dissolution succeeds and Mending succeeds on the same configuration simultaneously:

- Dissolution success: configuration torn, Gap forms.
- Mending success: substrate repaired, Gap closes.

These are sequential in logic but simultaneous in the rules. The Gap forms and closes in the same instant.

**Finding (SIM7-F-07, P2):** Simultaneous Dissolution + Mending on the same configuration is undefined. §9.13 covers Weave vs Pull. It does not address operations where the targets are logically different (Dissolution targets the configuration; Mending targets the substrate/absence). They may not even be the same "configuration" in the mechanical sense.

**Proposed resolution:** Dissolution and Mending on the same site are not opposing operations under §9.13. They fire sequentially by pool size (larger pool resolves first). If Dissolution resolves first and succeeds: Gap forms; Mending now targets a Gap (not a Shifting Object), Ob increases to Standard Gap (5). If Mending resolves first and succeeds: Shifting Object closed; Dissolution now targets a repaired configuration, Ob unchanged. If both fail: Shifting Object persists, RS damage from both failures.

## SIMULATION 44 — E-08: WOUND AT EXACTLY INCAPACITATION THRESHOLD DURING LEAP (Mode D)

**Incapacitation threshold:** Wounds = Health ÷ 2 (round up? round down? — see below).

**Eligibility rule:** "Not at or above incapacitation Wound threshold."

**Edge case:** Health 6, Wounds 3. Threshold = Health ÷ 2 = 3. At exactly 3 Wounds: at threshold (not above). Eligible or not?

**Text says "at or above."** At exactly 3 = at threshold = ineligible. ✓ Clear.

**Second edge case:** Health 7, Wounds 3. Threshold = 7 ÷ 2 = 3.5. Round up = 4? Round down = 3?

**Gap (SIM7-F-08, P2):** Incapacitation threshold rounding not specified. If round down: threshold = 3, character at 3 Wounds is ineligible. If round up: threshold = 4, character at 3 Wounds is eligible (not yet at 4).

**Proposed:** Round up for incapacitation threshold (ceiling). Health 7: threshold = 4 Wounds. Health 5: threshold = 3. This is more generous (characters fight slightly longer before incapacitation) and consistent with most wound system conventions. Needs explicit statement in core engine.

## SIMULATION 45 — E-09: CE AT MAXIMUM — INQUISITOR CASCADE (Mode C)

**CE track:** Assumed range 0–10 (from compiled Stage 2, CE accumulates from Thread operation witnessing and investigation).

**Inquisitor Veth at CE 9:** One more witnessed Thread operation = CE 10.

**Gap (SIM7-F-09, P2):** CE ceiling behavior not defined in v2.5. What happens at CE 10 (or whatever the maximum is)? Does CE cap? Does it trigger a Church action? Does the Inquisitor automatically act?

**Proposed:** CE ceiling should trigger a mandatory Church institutional response — Veth must file a formal report, triggering Church action (TC +1 or domain action). CE resets to 0 after institutional action. This creates a CE cycle: accumulate → report → reset → accumulate. If Veth cannot report (isolated, imprisoned, dead): CE overflows → Veth acts unilaterally (GM determines action based on Belief). Flag as SIM7-F-09 (P2) — CE ceiling rule absent from v2.5, needs cross-reference to Church mechanics.

## SIMULATION 46 — E-10: ALL FACTIONS AT MANDATE 0 (Mode C)

**Setup:** RS 15 (Critical). Six seasons of Stability failures. All factions at Mandate 0.

**Mandate 0 per faction:** No legitimate authority. Domain Actions that require Mandate cannot be taken. Faction cannot confer legitimacy on PC actions. Institutional collapse.

**Thread environment at RS 15:**
- Critical band: spontaneous Gaps (40% per season), worldwide +1 Ob, faction Stability checks Ob 1 every season (all fail at Mandate 0 — what happens when there's no Mandate to lose?).

**Gap (SIM7-F-10, P2):** Stability check at Mandate 0: what is the consequence of failing a Stability check when Mandate is already 0? The rule says "Mandate −1" — below 0 is undefined.

**Proposed:** Mandate cannot go below 0. Stability check failure at Mandate 0 instead produces: Faction fractures. One sub-faction splinters off (GM creates a new minor faction from the remnants). This prevents Mandate from becoming a meaningless track at the endgame and creates narrative consequences for total institutional collapse.

**Thread operations in a Mandate-0 world:**
All Thread ops +1 Ob (Critical). But practitioners are no longer operating on behalf of institutions — no patron, no cover, no political protection. CE accumulation continues (Inquisitors still operate even without institutional authority — they're running on Belief alone). IP may be irrelevant (no intelligence networks functioning at scale).

**Faction with Thread capability (Revolution) at Mandate 0:**
Revolution Mandate 0 = their community networks have collapsed. Community Weaving (requires "affiliated TS 30+ practitioner" and community substrate) — is the community substrate still present without Mandate? 

**Finding (SIM7-F-11, P3):** Revolution's Community Weaving prerequisite (community substrate) may fail at Mandate 0. The community that provides the Thread substrate is the same community that confers Mandate. If Mandate collapses, does Community Weaving become impossible?

**Proposed:** Community Weaving requires Mandate ≥ 1. At Mandate 0, Revolution cannot perform Community Weaving — the community has fractured. This creates a feedback loop: low RS → Stability failures → Mandate loss → cannot Mend via Community Weaving → RS continues falling. The Revolution's primary Thread contribution becomes unavailable precisely when it's most needed. Intentional design echo of the Einhir trajectory.

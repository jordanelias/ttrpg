## SIMULATION 47 — FIRST LEAP AS EVENT SCENE (Mode C)

### Setup: Character first achieving TS 30+ and Approach Training

**Character:** Elske (Spirit 4, TS 32 — just crossed threshold via Discovery Event)

**First Leap procedure (§2.3):** Full event scene. GM describes the approach and perceptual boundary.

**Leap roll:** Attunement 3 + History 1 + TPS 3 = 7D, Ob 2 (TS 30–49).
P(Success): ~83%. P(Failure): ~17%.

**On success:** Character experiences Being beyond their own being. Returns. Certainty −1 (permanent). TS revealed to player.

**Certainty interaction:** Elske at Certainty 4 (Spirit score). After First Leap: Certainty 3. Now permanently reduced ceiling — the old framework can never feel complete. This is irreversible.

**On failure (17%):** Dissociation. Cannot attempt Thread operations for one season. TS frozen.

**Repeated First Leap failure:** Elske fails again next season. Second Dissociation. Rules don't cap attempts — she can keep trying. Each attempt at Ob 2 with 7D: 17% failure rate. P(failing 3 consecutive attempts): 0.17³ ≈ 0.5%. Rare but possible.

**Finding (SIM8-F-01, P3):** No rule for accumulated Dissociation from repeated First Leap failures. Each failure freezes TS for one season and prohibits attempts — that's the consequence. But psychological/narrative consequences of repeatedly failing the First Leap are unaddressed. Minor — GM handles narratively.

## SIMULATION 49 — BARALTA: DEVOUT + CONSTITUTIONAL LEGALIST (Mode C)

### NPC: Baralta (Hafenmark, Devout + constitutional legalist, divine authority claim vs Confessor jurisdiction)

**Unique mechanic:** Devout Constraint + constitutional legalism creates a character who cannot pursue TS growth but actively resists Confessor jurisdiction over Thread matters on constitutional grounds.

**Thread environment interaction:**
Baralta at TS 0 (Devout, no Thread perception). A Dissolution occurs in Hafenmark territory. Gap forms.

Baralta's institutional response: constitutional challenge to Church jurisdiction over the Gap. Argues the Gap is a natural phenomenon under civil authority, not a theological matter.

**Mechanical consequence:** Church attempts CE-based investigation. Baralta's constitutional resistance: Circles/Influence contest. No Thread mechanics required on Baralta's part. ✓

**Discovery Event trigger:** Gap in the same territory. Baralta witnesses the Gap's effects. Per Devout rules: Discovery Event triggers involuntary Spirit check. P(success): 73%. On success: +5 TS, Theological Dissonance Event immediately.

**Theological Dissonance on success:** Spirit TN 7 Ob 1 + Devout +2D bonus = 6D. P(pass): ~92%. Framework holds. Baralta gains TS 5 but Devout Constraint re-engages. Certainty −1.

**Finding:** Baralta can accumulate TS 5 from a single Discovery Event despite Devout Constraint — but the Constraint re-engages immediately after. She perceives the Thread for a moment (involuntary) then her framework reabsorbs it. The moment of perception is real; its implications are suppressed. ✓ Philosophically correct.

**New finding (SIM8-F-03, P2):** Baralta at TS 5 (Dormant) has passive Thread awareness ("vague wrongness near Gaps") but her Devout Constraint prevents voluntary TS growth. She will perceive wrongness near Gaps without being able to understand or act on it. No rule for how a Devout character at TS 5–29 (Dormant range) processes passive Thread perception. Proposed: Devout characters in Dormant range attribute passive Thread perception to theological causes (divine unease, spiritual contamination) without recognising it as Thread sensitivity. No mechanical effect — purely narrative framing.

## SIMULATION 50 — ANCHOR LEAP FAILURE WITH COMMITTED HELPERS (Mode C)

### Setup: Collective operation. Helpers commit to the Leap. Anchor fails.

**Procedure per §2.5:** Anchor rolls Leap. Helpers each roll their own Leap. All must succeed for collective contact.

**Wait — §2.5 doesn't specify that helpers make their own Leap rolls.** The section says "Each practitioner must have their own active contact window" — implying helpers Leap independently. But the Leap roll mechanics are only described for individual practitioners.

**Gap (SIM8-F-04, P1):** Collective Leap procedure is undefined. Does each practitioner roll their own Leap? Does the Anchor's Leap carry the collective? If the Anchor fails, do helpers' successful Leaps result in contact without an Anchor?

**Proposed framework:**
- All practitioners roll their own Leap (same round, Priority 5)
- If Anchor fails: collective operation fails. Helpers who succeeded are in individual contact (their own Focus windows) but the collective lattice never formed — no shared intentionality, no Anchor pool. Each helper can perform individual operations within their contact window, but the collective's pooled dice do not apply.
- If Anchor succeeds but helpers fail: subtract failed helpers' contributed dice. If remaining pool drops below half Anchor's solo pool: +1 Ob (lattice fractures per §2.5). Otherwise proceed with reduced pool.
- If all fail: all take individual Leap failure consequences.

Flag as **SIM8-F-04 (P1)**.

## SIMULATION 51 — HEALTH 1 THREAD OPERATIONS (Mode D)

### Boundary condition: Practitioner at Health 1 (one Wound = incapacitation)

**Health 1, Wounds 0:** At incapacitation threshold = ceiling(1 ÷ 2) = 1. Already at threshold. **Ineligible for Leap.** Cannot attempt Thread operations at all.

**Wait** — Wounds 0 at Health 1: threshold = ceiling(1/2) = 1 Wound. Character has 0 Wounds. "At or above incapacitation threshold" means Wounds ≥ threshold. 0 Wounds < 1 threshold. **Eligible.**

**Leap Ob:** +0 Wounds (no Wounds yet). Standard Leap eligible.

**During contact: takes 1 Wound.** Wounds 0→1. Attunement disruption check fires. At Attunement 3: P(pass) ≈ 73%. 

On fail: rendering reasserts. Contact ends.
On pass: contact continues. But now: Wounds 1 = threshold. If another Wound hits during contact: Wounds 1→2. Wounds > Health (impossible at Health 1 — Wounds can't exceed Health without death/incapacitation). 

**Actually:** At Health 1: Wounds 1 = Health. Character is incapacitated. Contact must end — incapacitation supersedes active contact. 

**Gap (SIM8-F-05, P2):** No rule for automatic contact termination on incapacitation. The Wound disruption check handles Wound-taken-during-contact, but incapacitation is a more severe state than just taking a Wound. A character incapacitated during contact should lose contact immediately — their rendering hasn't just been disrupted, their capacity to function has collapsed.

**Proposed:** Add to §2.3 Wound disruption: "If a Wound incapacitates the practitioner during contact (Wounds equal to or exceed Health), contact terminates immediately regardless of the disruption check result."

## SIMULATION 52 — MULTIPLE SIMULTANEOUS GAPS IN ONE TERRITORY (Mode C)

### Setup: RS 25 (Fractured). Territory has: 1 Entrenched Gap (3 seasons old) + 1 Standard Gap (just formed from failed Dissolution). Both in the same territory.

**RS effects, both Gaps:**
- Entrenched Gap persisting: RS −4/season
- Standard Gap persisting: RS −4/season
- Both: RS −8/season from Gaps alone

**Mending priority question:** Which Gap does the practitioner Mend first? Entrenched is more severe (Ob 6 vs Ob 5) but both drain RS equally (−4/season each).

**Optimal strategy:** Mend Standard Gap first (lower Ob, higher P(success)), then Entrenched. Even though the RS drain is equal, closing the easier Gap first preserves the practitioner's Coherence and reduces sequential failure risk before tackling the harder one.

**Territory effects stacking:**
Both Gaps active = two sources of Monstrous Incursion risk (at RS Fractured: entities can emerge from either Gap independently). Two Gaps in one territory also means: Mending in the territory has Ob +1 (RS Fractured) on all Thread ops. No additional stacking for multiple Gaps — the RS threshold applies once regardless of Gap count.

**Gap (SIM8-F-06, P3):** No rule for whether multiple Gaps in the same territory produce stacked effects or a single consolidated effect. Proposed: Gap effects (Monstrous Incursion risk, RS drain) apply per Gap — two Gaps = double the drain and double the Incursion risk. Threshold-band penalties (+1 Ob) apply once regardless of Gap count.

## SIMULATION 53 — CERTAINTY 0 FOR NON-PRACTITIONERS (Mode C)

### Setup: Non-practitioner at Certainty 0 (Rendering Crisis for non-practitioners)

**Non-practitioners track Certainty** (§4.6 from Stage 2 compilation). Starting value = Spirit score. Maximum = Spirit score. Certainty 0 = Rendering Crisis (scene event).

**How a non-practitioner reaches Certainty 0:**
Triggers: Successful Leap (−1), witnessing monstrous entity (−1, Spirit check to resist), non-consensual Thread work on them (−1), Intelligibility/Coherence thresholds affecting Certainty maximum.

A non-practitioner with Spirit 3: Certainty starts at 3. Three witnessed monstrous entities (failed Certainty checks each time): Certainty 0.

**Rendering Crisis for non-practitioners:**
Per §4.6: "Certainty 0 — Rendering Crisis: A scene event, not a passive state. The character's rendering of reality has become unstable. They must resolve the dissonance narratively: revise a Belief, withdraw from Thread-active situations, or find a new framework for what they have experienced."

**Gap (SIM8-F-07, P2):** No mechanical rule for what a non-practitioner at Certainty 0 actually cannot do. For practitioners: Coherence 0 = cannot perform Thread operations (explicit). For non-practitioners: Certainty 0 = "rendering unstable" but no mechanical penalty beyond the narrative resolution requirement.

**Proposed:** Non-practitioner at Certainty 0: −2D on all rolls (rendering instability impairs all action) until Rendering Crisis resolved. Resolution requires: Belief revision OR one full season of withdrawal from Thread-active situations OR a significant relational anchoring scene (Bonds TN 7 Ob 3). This parallels Coherence 0 for practitioners without the Thread-operation-specific incapacity.

## SIMULATION 54 — TS GROWTH UNDER SUSTAINED PRESSURE (Mode C)

### Scenario: Three characters pursuing TS growth simultaneously under RS Fractured conditions

**Characters:**
- Scholar (non-practitioner, Einhir texts path): Spirit 4, 1 qualifying study season completed
- Soldier (confrontation path): Spirit 3, witnessed 1 Gap in battle
- Inquisitor (CE accumulation, Devout constraint): Spirit 4, witnessed multiple Thread ops

**TS growth check: Spirit TN 7 Ob 1**

**Scholar (Spirit 4):** P(success) ≈ 80% per qualifying season. After 2 seasons: likely TS 10. After 6: likely TS 30 (Stirring). Clear, slow path. ✓

**Soldier (Spirit 3):** P(success) ≈ 73% per confrontation. Gap witnessed in battle: qualifying event. Soldier holds the confrontation (doesn't flee). TS 0→5. Next confrontation opportunity: next Gap or monstrous entity. In RS Fractured campaign: ~1–2 per season. Expected path to TS 30: 6 qualifying events × average 1.4 attempts each ≈ 8–9 seasons. Slower than Scholar but more organic.

**Inquisitor (Devout constraint, Spirit 4):**
Discovery Events bypass Devout Constraint for initial check. Inquisitor witnesses Thread operations constantly (CE accumulation). Does witnessing a Thread operation qualify as a confrontation/Discovery Event for TS growth?

**Gap (SIM8-F-08, P2):** Witnessing a Thread operation vs. a Discovery Event vs. a confrontation: the qualifications for TS growth checks are inconsistently defined across character types. The core rule says "held confrontation" with qualifying events (monstrous incursion, Originary Lock, Gap, Locked Zone, proximity to Resonant practitioner). Witnessing a Thread operation is not listed as a qualifying event — but Inquisitors encounter Thread operations constantly. Does the Inquisitor's exposure accumulate into a qualifying event, or only the listed triggers?

**Proposed:** Witnessing a Thread operation is not itself a qualifying event for TS growth. The qualifying events require direct confrontation with Thread reality — a Gap, a monstrous entity, handling an Originary Lock. Observing an operation from the outside (which is the Inquisitor's usual position) does not qualify. The Inquisitor must directly encounter Thread phenomena to grow. This keeps TS growth rare and meaningful for non-practitioners. Note: close-proximity witnessing of a major Dissolution (Gap formation in the same room) may qualify at GM discretion.

## SIMULATION 55 — NIFLHEL THREAD HARVEST FULL SCENARIO (Mode C)

### Setup: Niflhel operative harvesting dissolution residue from an active Gap site

**Board game Thread Harvest order:**
Intelligence vs Ob 2. +1 Wealth, RS −0.5 on success.

**TTRPG equivalent (no BG order):**
Niflhel has TS 0 operatives. They don't perform Thread operations. Residue harvesting is a physical act — finding residue deposits near Gap sites and extracting them.

**Mechanical procedure:**
No Thread operation. Resources check or Circles check to acquire residue. Niflhel's Intelligence attribute used for logistics. The residue itself has Potency 1–5 — determined by Gap size/age.

**Gap (SIM8-F-09, P2):** No TTRPG procedure for residue harvesting. The board game has a Harvest order. TTRPG has no equivalent. A Niflhel PC who wants to harvest residue in TTRPG mode has no rules.

**Proposed:** Residue harvesting in TTRPG: Resources or Circles check Ob 2 (fresh Gap) to Ob 4 (well-known Gap, competition for residue). Success: acquire residue sample, Potency = 1d3 (fresh Gap) to 1d6 (entrenched Gap, more residue accumulated). No Thread operation required — it's a physical extraction act, not Thread work. Coherence risk: handling concentrated residue without Thread sensitivity = no Coherence track. However: sustained proximity to high-Potency residue (Potency 4–5) without TS protection may trigger Certainty checks (GM discretion, per SIM8-F-03 framing for passive Thread exposure).

## SIMULATION 56 — KLAPP NPC: CARDINAL MECHANICS + CHURCH EXPANSION (Mode C)

### NPC: Klapp (Cardinal, Church expansion, intelligence operations)

**Thread relevance:** Klapp manages Church intelligence operations. CE accumulation through his network (distributed — multiple Inquisitors reporting to him).

**Cardinal CE pooling:** If Klapp receives reports from multiple Inquisitors (each contributing CE), does Klapp himself accumulate CE from secondhand reports?

**Gap (SIM8-F-10, P2):** No rule for CE pooling through institutional hierarchy. Proposed: CE accumulates for the Inquisitor who witnesses directly. Reports to superiors (Klapp) translate into: TC +1 (institutional knowledge), IP −1 (information consumed by Church network), but not direct CE to the superior. Klapp has institutional access to investigation findings without personal CE accumulation.

**Church expansion interaction with Thread environment:**
At RS Fractured: Church TC +1 from Co-Movement cards (18-card deck, cards 10 and 17 specifically). Church expansion is mechanically accelerated by RS degradation — substrate damage generates theological alarm, which the Church channels into institutional growth.

**Finding:** The Church is the faction that benefits most mechanically from RS degradation (TC growth from substrate stress). This creates a perverse incentive: Church factions may have passive interest in maintaining a degraded RS. ✓ Philosophically coherent — institutionalised theology thrives on the inexplicable.

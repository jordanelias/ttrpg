## Key Findings — Run 3 vs Run 2 Comparison

### F-SIM-3-001 — Versatile-Heavy reversal confirms Str 3 bracket issue (Design confirmation)
Run 2 (Str 3): Vers-Heavy worst build, 5.6% avg win rate.  
Run 3 (Str 4): Vers-Heavy/Heavy **best build**, 60.1% avg win rate.

The Versatile-Heavy penalty is not broken — it requires Str 4 to function. At Str 3, the −2D pool penalty from armour + Versatile −1D + TN 7 weapon stacked catastrophically on a pool-8 base. At Str 4, no penalties apply and the build excels. **No patch to Versatile needed.** Close PP-SIM-001 — invalid finding, stat-bracket artefact.

---

### F-SIM-3-002 — Heavy armour dominance intensifies at Str 4 (P1 BALANCE)
At Str 4, Heavy armour costs nothing (no pool penalty). DR 3 with full pool 8 is overwhelmingly powerful. Short-Light/Heavy beats Short-Medium/None and Short-Heavy/None at **100%**. Light weapon + Heavy armour consistently beats heavier weapons with no armour.

Same pattern as Run 1 and Run 2 but now more extreme because the −2D penalty that previously checked Heavy armour at Str 3 is gone.

Root cause: DR 3 neutralises most damage at Health 10. Expected damage per hit with Medium weapon (+2) against Heavy armour (DR 3): 2 + excess − 3 = excess − 1. Most hits with small excess deal 0 damage. Heavy armour near-immunises against Light/Medium weapons unless the attacker consistently rolls high excess.

**Patch options:**
- A: Reduce Heavy armour DR from 3 → 2; shift Medium to 1 (compress the DR range)
- B: Add Stamina cost to Heavy armour (Stamina max End−2 instead of End−1) — staying in Heavy armour exhausts faster
- C: Heavy armour −1D penalty applies at ALL Str levels (not just one below minimum) — consistent cost regardless of Str

---

### F-SIM-3-003 — No-armour Heavy weapons remain weak (P2, confirmed across brackets)
Both Run 2 and Run 3: Long-Heavy/None, Short-Heavy/None ≈ 15–16% avg win rate. Heavy weapon TN 7 produces low hit rates (≈35% per attack) that don't compensate for the +3 damage bonus when the opponent has any armour. The High-risk/high-reward profile doesn't pay off at pool 8.

At Str 4 with no armour penalties, Heavy/None builds should improve — but they don't. Confirms this is a weapon TN issue, not a Str-bracket issue.

**Patch options:**
- A: Reduce Heavy weapon TN from 7 → 6 (same as Medium); differentiate by damage only
- B: Heavy weapon TN 7 attack but TN 6 parry (easier to defend with, harder to use offensively — tradeoff weapon)
- C: Accept as intentional — Heavy weapons are specialist tools requiring specific builds; document accordingly

---

### F-SIM-3-004 — Medium weapon + Heavy armour is dominant at Str 4 (P1 BALANCE)
Long-Medium/Heavy (50.8%) and Short-Medium/Heavy (49.4%) are top-5 builds. Medium weapon's balanced hit rate (TN 6, ≈50% per die) combined with DR 3 full-pool defence is the sweet spot. This is the "knight in armour" build and it's too strong relative to lighter builds.

This finding and F-SIM-3-002 share a root cause: Heavy armour DR is too high relative to damage output at this pool size.

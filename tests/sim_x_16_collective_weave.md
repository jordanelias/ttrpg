## Collective Leap Procedure

**Step 1: Collective Diagnosis.** Both practitioners Diagnose the Varfell estate territory simultaneously (shared GM exchange). Target: the substrate near Locked Zone border. Configuration: strained, near-Dormant (RS 20), with persistent micro-Gap from X-14. Ob for W-04: base 4 + 1 (Locked Zone proximity, from X-14 F-59) = **Ob 5**.

**Step 2: Both Leap (Priority 5, same round).**

Maret Leap: 9D TN7 Ob1. P(success) ≈ 86%. Most likely: succeeds.
Klapp Leap: 8D TN7 Ob1. P(success) ≈ 83%. Most likely: succeeds.

**Collective result (both succeed):** Lattice forms. Anchor Maret proceeds with operation.
Klapp contributes: +2D bonus dice to Maret's operation pool.

**Klapp Certainty:** Successful Leap → Certainty −1. Klapp Certainty: 1→**0**. **Rendering Crisis triggered.**

### Rendering Crisis (Klapp, mid-operation)

Certainty 0: "A scene event, not a passive state. The character's rendering of reality has become unstable. Must resolve the dissonance narratively — revise a Belief, withdraw from Thread-active situations, or find a new framework."

**Timing:** The Rendering Crisis fires on Certainty dropping to 0 from the Leap. But Klapp is now in contact — suspended rendering. He cannot experience the Rendering Crisis during contact (he is not consciously present; rendering is suspended). The Crisis fires **when he returns from contact** — at the end of the Leap.

**Does this interrupt the operation?** No. Contact is maintained once the Leap succeeds. The Crisis is a post-contact event. The collective operation proceeds.

**Finding F-66 [P2]:** Rendering Crisis from Certainty 0 fires post-Leap, not during contact. The practitioner is not present to experience the crisis during the Leap. This is mechanically coherent but creates a design question: does the Rendering Crisis affect the operation's outcome retroactively? The rules say "resolved narratively" — this suggests the crisis is a scene event after the operation resolves, not an interrupt. Taking this interpretation: operation proceeds normally.

## Operation: W-04 Territorial Calm (Collective)

**Maret's pool:** 15D (solo) + 2D (Klapp helper) = **17D TN7**. Ob 5.

P(17D TN7 ≥5 net):
Expected net = 17×0.33 = 5.6. P(≥5) estimated from reference:
- 15D: P(≥5) ≈ 57%.
- Each additional die adds ~0.33 expected net. 17D: expected 5.6 vs Ob5.
- P(≥5 from 17D) ≈ 65%.

| Degree | Outcome | RS | P |
|--------|---------|-----|---|
| Overwhelming (≥8) | All Thread ops in territory −1 Ob for 1 season. RS +1. | +1 | ~27% |
| Success (5–7) | All Thread ops in territory −1 Ob for 1 season. RS unchanged. | 0 | ~38% |
| Partial (2–4) | Partial effect (GM sets scope). RS −1. Coherence −1. | −1 | ~25% |
| Failure (≤1) | Interaction collapses. RS −2. Coherence −1. | −2 | ~10% |

**Most likely: Success (38%).** All Thread ops in Varfell territory at −1 Ob for 1 season. RS unchanged.

**Collective operation RS:** Territorial Weave. Standard Weave RS consequence on Success: RS unchanged. But collective operation: "Co-movement scales with participant count." Does this increase RS cost? Stage3 §5.1.5: "Co-movement scales with participant count. Multiple configurations interacting simultaneously produces correspondingly significant consequences across all three dimensions." This is narrative; it says co-movement consequences are larger, but does not specify a mechanical RS multiplier for collective Weaving (the multiplier is specified for mass battle operations, not standard collective ops).

**Ruling:** Standard RS (unchanged on Success). Co-movement produces larger temporal/epistemic narrative effects but no mechanical RS increase beyond the base operation.

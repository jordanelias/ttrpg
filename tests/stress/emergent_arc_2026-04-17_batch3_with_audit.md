## H2: SMOOTH — INTEGRATION WITHOUT FRICTION POINTS, ZOOM IN/OUT, SYSTEM TRANSITIONS

**ASSESSMENT: ◑ (moderate, several friction points)**

**Strengths:**
The Scene Slate as unified Zoom In trigger system (integration_proposal, Batch 1) correctly handles the majority of Zoom In occasions. Domain Echo from personal actions to faction-level is well-specified in the new Domain Echo Reference Table (Batch 2, NEW-S26 demonstrates it firing correctly across multiple systems). The Obligation system (Batch 2, §6.1) integrates cleanly with the NPC priority tree (blocking actions for 4 seasons) without requiring new mechanics.

**Friction Point 1 — Settlement Order → Province Accord derivation:**
The mathematical derivation creates non-obvious edge cases (NEW-OI-12: Order 0 contribution; NEW-OI-11: Institutional Consolidation Accord +1 vs settlement-derived Accord). Two systems — faction_layer's direct Accord grants and settlement_layer's Order-derived Accord — currently operate in parallel without a specified interaction rule. **Priority: P1 — the derivation is central to the Universal Victory condition.**

**Friction Point 2 — Dialogue Lattice → Social Contest handoff:**
The Lattice session's Style choices (Revealing/Obscuring × Memory/Projection) are not specified to carry into the Contest that follows (NEW-OI-20). When a Lattice session escalates to Contest, the genre is re-established at GM Setup (Step 2). This creates discontinuity: the player made stylistic choices throughout the Lattice, then those choices reset at Contest start. The Lattice-to-Contest transition should carry the accumulated stylistic frame as the Contest's starting genre. This is a minor friction point but produces narrative discontinuity.

**Friction Point 3 — Remote Investigation through Knots:**
Knot-mediated remote investigation (fieldwork §2.6) + companion governor creates a geography-collapsing capability (Batch 2, NEW-S31) whose Coherence cost is unspecified relative to standard Thread-Read (NEW-OI-16). If remote investigation carries no additional Coherence cost over a physical Thread-Read: it's strictly superior (saves 3 scene actions + no Exposure). It should carry the same Coherence cost as a Perceptive Leap to maintain the Remote vs Physical tradeoff.

**Friction Point 4 — Style Bonus Fix × Adjudicator Type Shift:**
When a Dialogue Lattice escalates to Contest in a Parliament setting, the adjudicator type shifts from "No adjudicator" (Attunement-primary, Lattice) to "Crowd" (Charisma-primary, Parliament). The player's optimized pool construction for the Lattice doesn't transfer. This adjudicator shift is mechanically correct but creates a skill mismatch that should be flagged explicitly at the escalation moment (Batch 2, NEW-S37 surface).

**Friction Point 5 — Subnational Outreach competition with national Outreach:**
The §8.11.6 subnational Outreach entries count toward the 3-entry maximum (competing with national). In a province with an active Guild settlement, RM outpost, and Warden post, a player could receive 3 subnational Outreach entries alone — displacing all national NPC Outreach. The volume control (§8.11.5) prioritizes by highest Disposition, but subnational NPCs may have very high Disposition (settlements the player governs). **This could crowd out named NPC arcs during critical arc moments.** Propose: subnational Outreach occupies a separate sub-count (max 1 subnational per 3-entry maximum) to prevent crowding.

## H3: ELEGANT — LOGICALLY SIMPLE, CLEAR APPROACH, NO UNNECESSARY OVERHEAD

**ASSESSMENT: ◕ (strong, one complexity spike)**

**Strengths:**
The Domain Echo table (Batch 2, NEW-S26 appendix) is a masterwork of elegant design: one formula (personal action → faction consequence), two directions (up only), one seasonal cap (±2), one Standing amplifier. PP-632 (Disposition/Knot via Bonds) remains the gold standard: one formula (floor(Bonds÷2)+1), two applications (Disposition ceiling, Knot capacity), zero overhead. The Conviction symmetry (Batch 1, NEW-S7) — player Convictions and NPC Convictions share the same name, mechanism, and targeting system — is one of the cleanest design principles in the project.

**Complexity Spike — The Investigation Systems Proposal:**
The four-system investigation architecture (NPE → Investigation Interface → Dialogue Lattice → Response Matrix) is intellectually coherent but produces seven interaction surfaces within a single investigative scene (7 gate types, 5 filters, 5 outcome types, 2 scene graph types, 3 investigative registers). A player navigating a complex investigation simultaneously manages: Certainty gates, Evidence gates, Belief gates, History gates, Disposition gates, TS gates, and Thread Sensitivity gates — then runs the 5-filter chain for each utterance. Even experienced players will find individual filter outputs uncertain without the filter being labeled.

**Proposed resolution:** The filter chain CANNOT be eliminated (it produces the richest investigative fiction in the design). But the presentation must be visual-only: filter failures manifest as specific conversational registers (see integration_proposal Part 5 Gap 5) that players learn to READ rather than calculate. The design should commit explicitly to this presentation method and NOT expose the filter chain as labeled UI elements. The gate types (7) can be reduced: Attribute Gate and History Gate share the same "prior character investment" logic — merge into a single "Background Gate" that covers both attribute thresholds and History tags. This reduces 7 gates to 6 (merged Attribute+History, Evidence, Belief, Certainty, Disposition, Thread Sensitivity) without any mechanical loss.

# PART II: GRANULAR AUDIT — SCENARIO-LEVEL ISSUES

## G1: PROVINCE ACCORD DERIVATION — ARITHMETIC INCONSISTENCY [P1]

The province-level Accord now derives from floor(average(settlement Order)) per settlement_layer §1.3. But faction_layer §1.3 grants "Accord +1 in one territory" as an Institutional Consolidation recovery path. These two mechanisms are incompatible as stated:

- If Accord +1 is a direct province-level grant: it bypasses the Order average calculation
- If Accord +1 requires increasing one settlement's Order: it's a different mechanic entirely

**Additionally:** peninsular_strain §2.3 specifies Accord change rules that modify Accord directly (e.g., "Accord +1 from Govern success"). These rules now require translation to settlement Order changes. The existing Accord change rules (§2.3 gaining, §2.4 losing) should be reprinted in settlement_layer terms: "Govern success → Order +1 in governing settlement" rather than "Accord +1 in territory."

**Resolution:** Province Accord is DERIVED (read-only) from settlement Order average. All rules that previously granted or reduced province Accord are rewritten as settlement Order changes. The only exception: Institutional Consolidation Accord +1 should be reframed as "Order +1 in one settlement of the controlling faction's choice in that territory."

## G2: HOLISTIC CLOCK INTERACTION — IP HALVED BASELINE VS SIEGE DURATION [P2]

The IP baseline has been halved (settlement_layer §7.1: from +1/season to +1/2 seasons baseline) to accommodate 20-30 year games. BUT: the Altonian Invasion Pressure escalation table in emergent_scenarios.md (Scenario 11) still uses the original rate benchmarks (IP 30+ fires Tutoring Demand, IP 100 = Invasion). With the halved baseline: IP 30 is now reached in 60 seasons (15 years) instead of 30 seasons (7.5 years). IP 100 = 200 seasons (50 years) — well beyond any campaign length.

This means: the IP milestones fire only from non-baseline sources (Thread events, succession delays, CI cross-clock effects). The clock now functions as an accumulator of bad political decisions rather than a passive timer. This is likely INTENDED (correct design for the 30-year game) but should be stated explicitly in emergent_scenarios.md as a design note update.

## G3: NIFLHEL PRIORITY 1 × RENOWN 9 INTERACTION [P2]

From Batch 1 (NEW-S21): Renown 9+ means "all factions evaluate player at Priority 2." But Niflhel Priority Tree Priority 1 fires regardless: "Stability ≤ 2 → Quiet Intelligence mode against strongest rival." Niflhel evaluating the player at Priority 2 (as all factions do at Renown 9+) means what? Niflhel's Priority 2 is "Exposure threat" — if Niflhel perceives the Renown 9+ player as having an exposure threat against Niflhel: Priority 2 fires against the player. This is mechanically dangerous: a Renown 9+ player automatically becomes a Priority 2 evaluation for the most covert faction, potentially triggering counter-intelligence action against a player who hasn't investigated Niflhel at all. **Resolution needed:** "all factions evaluate player at Priority 2" should specify that it means faction social outreach evaluation (Outreach Priority 3 level), not their operational Priority Tree evaluation.

## G4: COMPANION DEPARTURE + ACTIVE SETTLEMENT GOVERNANCE — TRANSITION RULES [P2]

When a companion departs (for any reason) while serving as settlement governor, the settlement is immediately unmanaged. But: the departure is preceded by a mandatory departure scene (§3.1), which takes 1 scene action (or is free — this is ambiguous). During the departure scene, is the companion still governing? If yes: the Order decay starts at Accounting, not at departure scene. If no: Order decay starts immediately (mid-season). The difference matters for planning: does the player have the rest of the season to find a new governor, or must they act within the departure scene itself?

**Resolution:** Governor status ends at the departure scene's conclusion. Order decay starts at the NEXT Accounting after the departure scene. This gives the player exactly 1 season (the remaining seasons in the current season after departure + 1 Accounting) to assign a new governor before Order begins declining.

## G5: DISTRICT-VERSUS-TERRITORY SCOPE OF SUBNATIONAL OUTREACH [P3]

The §8.11.6 subnational Outreach condition specifies "player is in or adjacent to their settlement" for Guild Council members. But the Scene Slate system operates at settlement-level (not province-level) after the settlement_layer update. "Adjacent to their settlement" is not the same as "adjacent province" — within a province, all settlements are adjacent. Does a Guild member in S-017 Gransol Market Quarter generate Outreach when the player is in S-015 Gransol Parliament? If yes: all three Gransol settlements' subnational factions generate Outreach simultaneously for any player in the province. If no: settlement-level adjacency creates very tight Outreach geography.

**Resolution:** Subnational Outreach triggers when the player is in the same settlement OR in any other settlement in the same province. This preserves the province-level social texture while maintaining settlement-level specificity.

## G6: ALMUD ARC B AUTHORITY RESONANT STYLE — WHAT CONSTITUTES "COMPETENCE WITNESSED" [P2]

Almud Arc B (Fortress) shifts his Resonant Style to "Authority only — competence witnessed by him personally." The scope of "competence" is undefined.

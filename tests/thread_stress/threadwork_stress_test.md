# 2. ED-SETT-04 — Siege Duration Calibration

## 2.1 Lock as Siege Defense [EXPLOIT — MAJOR]

Settlement spec: siege reduces Order by −1/season. A Fortress with Order 4 surrenders in 4 seasons.

A practitioner defender can Lock the settlement's social/institutional fabric at Relational or Field scale:

- Success: The community's loyalty is frozen at its current actualization — it cannot become less loyal. This directly blocks siege-induced Order decay.
- Chronic drift: RS −1/season from season 2+. Adjacent Thread ops +1 Ob.

**Mechanical break:** A successful Relational Lock on settlement loyalty makes the siege-Order-decay mechanic inoperative for as long as the Lock holds. A Fortress with a practitioner defender + successful Relational Lock can hold indefinitely until the Lock is reversed. Attacker response options:
1. Pull the Lock (Ob = original practitioner TS ÷ 10 − 2, minimum 1; e.g. TS 70 defender = Ob 5). 
2. Dissolve the Lock (RO — TS 50+ required, TN 8, same Ob table). On Success: RS −5, Gap forms.
3. Use their own practitioner to oppose the Lock (Contested Intentionality — Weave/Pull vs Lock is in the opposing pairs).

**The siege duration becomes a Thread practitioner standoff, not an attrition curve.** 4-season base duration is irrelevant once a Lock is placed. Siege duration design (ED-SETT-04) cannot be finalized without addressing whether Locks can prevent Order decay.

**Required ruling:** Define whether a Relational-scale Lock on settlement loyalty prevents siege-induced Order decay. Proposed: Yes, a Lock prevents seasonal Order decay from siege pressure. However: the Lock's chronic RS drain (−1/season from Season 2) becomes the attacker's "timer" — they don't need to break the Lock, they can simply wait for the RS consequence to force the defender to choose between maintaining the Lock (RS drain) and surrendering (releasing the Lock). This creates a three-way tension: siege attrition (Order decay), Lock maintenance (RS drain), and defender Coherence depletion (repeated Locking).

## 2.2 Relational Pull as Siege Acceleration [EXPLOIT]

An attacking practitioner (TS 50+) can Pull the settlement's Order configuration:
- Ob: Depth 3 (Relational) + Breadth 2 (Formation) + Distance 1 (Near — attacker outside walls, 10-100m) = **Ob 6, TN 7**.

E[net] for 21D at TN7 is 6.3 — that's ~0.3 above Ob 6, meaning average net surplus is just slightly above 0. So on average, this Pull gets end-of-session duration. That's significant but not a one-shot siege. However, a practitioner with Spirit 5, TPS 7, History +8D: pool = 25D. E[net] = 7.5. Average surplus above Ob 6 = 1.5 → consistent end-of-session, sometimes end-of-season.

**Conclusion:** Relational Pull on settlement Order is not quite an instant-win exploit at typical practitioner stats, but it is a significant siege accelerator that bypasses the Order-decay-per-season calibration entirely. A Relational Pull cannot be the answer to every siege — but one successful Pull equals 1–4 seasons of siege pressure. **The settlement layer needs an explicit ruling on whether governance stats (Order) are Thread-eligible targets.**

# 5. ED-COMP-02 — Companion Combat AI

## 5.1 Companion Dissonance During Player Thread Contact [GAP — REQUIRES RULING]

During the player's Thread contact window (rounds 2+, operations firing), the companion is in the same scene. Dissonance rules: "When Thread operations fire nearby, character enters Thread-disrupted space... Spirit check TN7 vs Dissonance Factor."

Dissonance Factor for a "Thread operation nearby (significant)": 2. A companion in the same combat scene as the player's Thread operation makes a Spirit check TN7, Ob 2. 

**At Spirit 3 (average), 6D TN7 Ob2: P(≥2) ≈ 70%.** So ~30% of rounds with Thread operations, the companion fails their Dissonance check: Spirit −1D for rest of scene. This is not catastrophic, but it accumulates. Over a 3-round contact window with 2 operations: two Dissonance checks. At 30% failure each: ~51% chance the companion fails at least one. A Spirit 2 companion (low): 4D TN7 Ob2 → P(≥2) ≈ 50%, so ~75% chance of at least one failure across two operations.

**Companion combat AI design must account for this.** The companion should not be treated as a fixed-stat combatant during Thread-active rounds — their Spirit pool is volatile. TTRPG: GM runs companion, can apply Dissonance checks directly. Videogame: the companion AI needs Dissonance as an explicit state that reduces their effective combat pool when the player is in Thread contact. This is not currently specified in either the companion spec or the combat doc.

## 5.2 Dissolution of Living Being — Companion Conviction Trigger [GAP — CRITICAL]

## 5.3 Companion Incapacitation + Knot Consequences [GAP]

Companion spec §4.2: "Companion incapacitation → forced departure at season end unless Healed or Mended."

If the companion is Knotted (Close, Medium, or Loose), the departure constitutes... what, exactly? Knot breaking rules define:
- **Rupture:** practitioner's Belief/action directly severs the bond. Points return, Disposition →−4, Composure damage = tier cost.
- **Loss:** companion dies or departs permanently outside player control. Points return, Coherence −1, Composure damage = tier cost.

Incapacitation-induced departure is not clearly Rupture (no belief violation) or Loss (companion may return if healed). **The PP-632 note says "P-12 suggests Knots are constitutive and do not break by choice."** Departure by incapacitation is not a choice — it is a physical crisis. 

**Required ruling:** Companion incapacitation departure = Knot enters Dormant state (not Loss, not Rupture). Dormant: Knot mechanics suspended, points remain invested, no Coherence cost. When companion returns (Healed/Mended, next season): Knot reactivates at its previous tier. If companion does NOT return (Healing not possible): Dormant → Loss at season end. This preserves the metaphysical coherence of Knots while handling the practical interruption of travel companionship.

# 8. Obligation Degenerate Loop Simulation

## 8.1 Obligation as Relational Thread Configuration [GAP — NEEDS RULING]

Obligations (feudal duty, debt of honor, contracted loyalty) between characters are relational configurations — they constitute the binding between two entities, their roles, their mutual actualization as obligated parties. This makes them Thread-eligible targets.

On Success: the obligation is over-actualized. What does OA on an obligation produce? The OA modifier means: subsequent Thread operations targeting this same config +1 Ob. **Pull an obligation:** Opening it = releasing the party from duty. If the obligation has been Woven (OA active): Pull Ob = standard (Firmly actualised, Ob 3) + OA (+1) = Ob 4. If OA has cleared (one season): back to Ob 3.

## 8.2 The Self-Terminating Loop [OK — elegant]

**Proposed degenerate loop:** Practitioner A Weaves an obligation binding NPC B each season → obligation is maintained by Thread force indefinitely.

**Why it terminates:** Each Relational Weave costs Coherence −1. After 10 seasons: Coherence 0 → Rendering Crisis → NPC conversion or recovery arc. The practitioner who maintains an obligation by Thread force for 10 seasons destroys their own rendering. The loop is self-terminating via Coherence depletion.

**This is a significant balance problem.** Either: (a) threadcut being Health must be much higher (50+) for a campaign-active threadcut being to survive 30 years, or (b) external Thread operation frequency must be extremely low (1 per 5+ years), or (c) the Rendering Strain recovery rule (−1/season rest) provides sufficient offset. **[GAP]** Rendering Strain recovery rate (−1/season rest) vs accumulation rate from external ops needs explicit calibration for long-campaign threadcut beings.

# TEST S-07: THE TEMPORAL KNOT PARADOX

**Setup:** A TS 75 practitioner (Coherence 7/Dissonant, Focus 4) performs Past-Oriented Pulling to undo an event that occurred last session: the assassination of a minor Crown diplomat. The diplomat's death created a Knot Crisis for the PC faction leader (Close Knot, now at Crisis). The practitioner's intentionality: undo the assassination so the Knot un-Crises. RS is 55 (Fragile, and critically: RS ≤ 60, so Past-Oriented Pulling is available). The practitioner has History "Crown Loyalist" (5 points). The assassination occurred in a territory with an over-actualised Relational Weaving from S-04 (the Crown-Hafenmark agreement, +1 Ob to subsequent ops on that configuration).

**Layer 1: Prerequisites check.**
- TS 75 ✓ (≥70)
- RS 55 ✓ (≤60)
- Diagnosis mandatory ✓

**Layer 2: Diagnosis (pre-Leap).**
- Practitioner Diagnoses the diplomatic configuration — the web of relationships around the assassination. This is a Personal-scale thread (one person's existence/death) but its consequences are Relational (the death affected multiple relationships and a political agreement).
- Scale of Past-Oriented Pull: the target is the event itself (the assassination). The physical facts of one person dying = Personal scale. But the temporal weight extends into the Relational scale because the event cascaded.
- **ISSUE: What scale is a Past-Oriented Pull?** The Pull targets an event, not a configuration. The event's scale depends on what it affected. The assassination of one person is Personal. But pulling it means pulling ALL its consequences — the Knot Crisis, the political fallout, the factional response. Does the Pull operate at Personal scale (the event itself) or Relational scale (its consequences)?

**Resolution:** Past-Oriented Pulling operates at the scale of the event being pulled, not its consequences. The assassination is Personal. The consequences unwind as a natural result of the temporal displacement, not as part of the operation itself. The practitioner's intentionality is "this event did not fully actualise." The cascade of un-consequences follows from that displacement, not from additional Thread work.

**However:** The consequences have ALREADY been rendered. The Knot Crisis is a fact. The political fallout is a fact. These are themselves actualised configurations. When the Pull displaces the assassination, it creates a Temporal Disjunction: the physical facts are removed (the diplomat is alive) but the memories remain (everyone remembers the assassination). The Knot Crisis was a rendered consequence of the assassination. Does it persist (because the memory persists) or resolve (because the physical cause is removed)?

**ISSUE FOUND: Temporal Disjunction and downstream mechanical states.** Past-Oriented Pulling removes physical facts but preserves memories. A Knot Crisis was triggered by the physical event (the death). If the death is un-actualised but the memory persists, is the Knot still in Crisis? The Crisis was triggered by the rendered event. The event is now un-rendered. But the Knot's strain was a mechanical change applied when the event occurred. Do mechanical states revert when their triggering event is temporally displaced?

**Resolution options:**
(a) **Mechanical states revert.** The Knot strain that pushed it to Crisis was caused by the assassination. The assassination is now un-actualised. The strain reverts. The Knot exits Crisis. But the Knot-holder REMEMBERS the Crisis — they feel the emotional weight of the Crisis without its cause. This is the Temporal Disjunction expressing through the Knot system: mechanical state says "not in Crisis," but the character's experience says "I went through a Crisis."
(b) **Mechanical states persist.** The strain was applied. The system doesn't track WHY strain was applied. The Knot remains in Crisis. The diplomat is alive but the relationship damage persists because the memory persists.
(c) **GM judgment per case.** Some mechanical states are directly tied to physical facts (the diplomat is dead → strain from external event). If the physical fact is removed, the state reverts. Others are tied to experience (the character suffered through the Crisis). Experience-based states persist because memory persists.

**Resolution:** Option (c) is correct. This is a GM determination, not a universal rule. The GM asks: was this mechanical state caused by a physical fact or by an experience? The Knot strain from external events (§4.7: "External events: Territory conquest affecting the Knot's entity") is caused by the physical fact of the event. If the fact is displaced, the strain reverts. But Knot strain from the character's own response (calling the Knot, using it as Composure buffer) is experiential — the character remembers doing it. That strain persists.

**PATCH NEEDED: Past-Oriented Pulling and mechanical state reversion — GM guidance. Physical-fact-triggered mechanical states revert when the fact is displaced. Experience-triggered mechanical states persist because memories persist. The Temporal Disjunction manifests as a gap between mechanical state and experienced state.**

**Layer 3: The Pull itself.**
- Ob by recency: assassination occurred last session = "Same session" equivalent? No — it occurred last session. "Same week" = Ob 5.
- +1 Ob from Fragile territory effect: Ob 6.
- Does the over-actualised Crown-Hafenmark agreement in the same territory affect this Pull? The Pull doesn't target that configuration. But the over-actualisation makes the territory's configurational traffic rigid. **Per P-18 (GM guidance): the over-actualised agreement doesn't mechanically affect the Pull, but the GM may note that the territory's rigidity makes the temporal displacement harder to integrate.**
- Pool: Spirit (5) + History "Crown Loyalist" (5+3=8) = 13 dice. (No TPS for Past-Oriented Pull per §2.6.)
- TN 7. Ob 6. Expected successes: ~3.9. Likely Partial or Failure.

**Assume Success (lucky roll):**
- Temporal Disjunction complete. Physical facts removed: the diplomat is alive. Memories remain: everyone remembers the assassination.
- RS: −3 minimum. RS 55 → 52.
- Coherence: −1 (Relational scale, since the Pull reaches Relational depth) + −1 (Past-Oriented additional) = −2. Coherence: 7 → 5. Enters Dissonant.
- The Fraying check: has the practitioner performed Past-Oriented Pull or FR Dissolution 3+ times this season? If this is the first: no Fraying Bane.

**Co-movement cascade:**
- Temporal auto-effect: Coherence −2 (already counted). History "Crown Loyalist" resonates — the practitioner pulled against a Crown-relevant event. Bonus die stored.
- Epistemic: Witnesses who remember the assassination retain both memories (the original and the altered). The diplomat is alive. Everyone remembers them dying. Inert Knowledge paradox forms: they know something is wrong but cannot determine what.
  - **The Knot-holder (PC faction leader):** Has the strongest memory of the Crisis. The diplomat is alive. The Crisis happened but its cause didn't. Spirit check TN 7 Ob 1 for the faction leader (non-practitioner, experiencing Temporal Disjunction): failure = Certainty −1. The leader's rendering of reality has been compromised.
  - **The assassin (if still alive):** Remembers committing the murder. The victim is alive. The assassin's rendering is severely compromised. Spirit check TN 7 Ob 2 (direct participant): failure = Certainty −2.
  - **Church investigators:** If the Church had begun investigating the assassination (Inquisitor procedure), they now have evidence of an event that didn't happen. Their investigation files describe a corpse that is alive. This is mechanically detectable as Thread work by an Inquisitor with CE 2+ (they feel the wrongness). TC +1 (heterodox activity detected).
- Actual (d6): Roll. Say 6 — delayed manifestation. The full displacement completes 1d3 scenes later. **ISSUE: What does "delayed manifestation" mean for Past-Oriented Pulling?** The diplomat is alive NOW or in 1d3 scenes? If delayed: the diplomat remains dead for 1d3 more scenes, then is alive. During that delay, the world contains both states — the Pull has been performed but hasn't fully manifested. This is a short-lived Temporal Disjunction within a Temporal Disjunction.

**ISSUE FOUND: Delayed manifestation (d6=6) interaction with Past-Oriented Pulling.** The actual co-movement effect "delayed manifestation" was designed for operations where the physical change can sensibly delay (a healed wound that seals gradually, a Pulled lock that opens slowly). For Past-Oriented Pulling, delay creates a paradox: the temporal displacement has occurred at the Thread level but the physical facts haven't caught up. For 1d3 scenes, the diplomat is both dead and not-dead at the Thread level. The physical world hasn't updated yet.

**Resolution:** This is correct and should be kept. The delay IS the paradox expressing. The d6 result means the Pull's physical manifestation takes time to render — the world's substrate is processing the displacement. During the delay: practitioners with TS 30+ perceive the paradox (the diplomat's thread reads as both present and absent). Non-practitioners perceive nothing unusual yet. When the manifestation completes, the physical facts snap to the new state. This is narratively rich — a scene where the body is still in the morgue but practitioners can see the thread already alive, waiting for the rendering to catch up.

**FINDING: Delayed manifestation + Past-Oriented Pulling = legitimate paradox window. No patch needed. Document as intended interaction.**

# TEST S-08: BOARD GAME CO-MOVEMENT AVALANCHE

**Setup:** Board game mode. Season 7 (Autumn). RS 48 (Fragile). 5 players: Crown, Church, Hafenmark, Varfell, Revolution.

**This season's orders (all Thread-related):**
1. Revolution: Community Weaving (RS recovery attempt)
2. Varfell: Thread Harvest (Wealth extraction)
3. Crown: Mend order on Standard Gap in Kronmark
4. Church: Excommunicate (targets Varfell leader for heretical Thread use)
5. Hafenmark: Trade (non-Thread, but adjacent to Crown's Mend territory)

**Resolution order (per priority table from G-060):**
Priority 1: Excommunicate (political act, Priority 2 in resolution order)
Priority 2: Trade (Priority 4)
Priority 3: Mend (Thread-related = Priority 12, last in order)
Priority 4: Community Weaving (Thread-related = Priority 12, tie → Influence order)
Priority 5: Thread Harvest (Thread-related = Priority 12, tie → Influence order)

Wait — the priority table resolves Thread operations LAST (Priority 12). So Excommunicate resolves first, then Trade, then all three Thread orders resolve in Influence order.

**Step 1: Excommunicate resolves.**
- Church Mandate (6) vs target (Varfell leader). Single roll. Say success.
- Varfell leader excommunicated. Social/political consequences. Varfell Stability check Ob 1 (institutional shock). Say Varfell passes.
- TC +1 (Church authority demonstrated). TC was... let's say TC 65. Now 66.
- **Does excommunication affect Thread Harvest?** The Varfell leader is excommunicated but Thread Harvest is a faction-level order, not a personal action. The Private Collection still exists. The order proceeds.

**Step 2: Trade resolves.**
- Hafenmark Trade in territory adjacent to Crown's Mend target. Summer Trade bonus (+1D) expired — it's Autumn. Autumn = Harvest season (Prosperity 4+: +1 Wealth). Standard Trade roll. Resolves normally. No Thread interaction.

**Step 3: Thread orders resolve (Influence order).**
- Highest Influence among Revolution, Varfell, Crown: say Crown (5), Revolution (4), Varfell (3).
- Crown Mend first. Standard Gap in Kronmark. Board game Ob: 2 (Standard Gap per P-15 board game table).
- Crown Intelligence: 4. Roll 4 dice TN 7. Expected: ~1.2 successes. Ob 2 is tight.
- Say Partial (1 success, needed 2). **ISSUE: Board game degree system.** The board game Thread orders don't have the full TTRPG degree system (Overwhelming/Success/Partial/Failure). They have binary Success/Failure with some having Overwhelming. Do board game Mend orders have Partial results?

**ISSUE FOUND: Board game Thread order degree resolution.** The TTRPG has 4 degrees. The board game Weave order (from G-057) only specifies Success and Failure (and implicitly Overwhelming: "Overwhelming: TT −2"). The Mend order as designed in v2.2 says "Success: Gap closed. RS +1. Failure: RS −1." No Partial. But what if the faction rolls net successes > 0 but < Ob? The core engine says Partial = "net > 0 but < Ob." This should apply to board game rolls too.

**Resolution:** Board game Thread orders use the standard degree system. Add Partial results:

| Order | Overwhelming | Success | Partial | Failure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Weave | RS +2 | RS +1 | RS unchanged, draw Co-Movement Card | RS −1, draw Co-Movement Card |
| Mend | Gap closed, RS +2 | Gap closed, RS +1 | Gap reduced one category, RS unchanged | Gap unchanged, RS −1 |
| Investigate | Learn RS value + Gap locations | Learn RS value | Learn RS value (approximate: "above/below 50") | Learn nothing |
| Harvest | +1 Wealth, RS −0.5 | +1 Wealth, RS −0.5 | +0.5 Wealth (round down at Accounting), RS −0.5 | No Wealth, RS −1 |

**PATCH NEEDED: Board game Thread order degree table (4 degrees, consistent with core engine).**

**Continuing with Partial result on Crown Mend:**
- Gap reduced from Standard to Micro-Gap equivalent. RS unchanged. Draw Co-Movement Card.
- Card drawn: say 13 — "Two territories: swap Prosperity values." Kronmark (Prosperity 5) swaps with... random selection... Grauwald (Prosperity 4). Kronmark → 4, Grauwald → 5. Minor but affects Autumn harvest (Kronmark drops below Prosperity 4 threshold? No, it's now exactly 4, so Autumn harvest still applies).

**Revolution Community Weaving:**
- Roll: faction Influence (4) vs Ob = RS inverted... wait. Old formula: Ob = TT ÷ 20. In RS terms: Ob = (100 − RS) ÷ 20 = (100 − 48) ÷ 20 = 52 ÷ 20 = 2.6 → round up → Ob 3.
- 4 dice vs Ob 3. Expected ~1.2 successes. Likely Failure.
- Say Failure: RS −1. RS: 48 → 47. Draw Co-Movement Card.
- Card drawn: say 2 — "TT +1." In RS terms: RS −1. RS: 47 → 46.
- **Double RS hit from one failed operation.** RS dropped from 48 to 46 in one resolution.

**Varfell Thread Harvest:**
- Roll: Intelligence (5) vs Ob 2. Expected ~1.5 successes. Likely success.
- Success: +1 Wealth. RS −0.5. RS: 46 → 45.5.
- Draw Co-Movement Card. Say card 10: "Church TC +1." TC: 66 → 67.

**Step 4: Accounting.**
- RS: 45.5. Round at Accounting: 45.5 → 45 (half-points round against acting faction for RS).

Wait — which faction's half-point? Thread Harvest is Varfell. The 0.5 is Varfell's contribution. "Round up" in TT terms = "round down" in RS terms = against Varfell = RS decreases. 45.5 → 45.

- RS 45 = Fragile. Spontaneous Shifting Object check: 1 per season. GM places.
- Autumn Harvest: all territories with Prosperity 4+: +1 Wealth to controlling faction.
- TC 67. No threshold crossed.
- IP 58 (unchanged this season). Check IP drift: +2/season base. IP: 58 → 60. **IP 60 threshold: Altonian army visible. Diplomatic Resolution possible.** This is a MAJOR campaign event triggered by the automatic IP drift during a season where nobody was paying attention to Altonia.

Five factions acted. Seven consequences cascaded. The system resolved everything sequentially without contradiction.

**FINDING: Board game mode handles multi-faction Thread order resolution correctly. The Co-Movement Card system produces emergent cascades (double RS hits from failed Weaving + bad card). The automatic clock drift (IP +2/season) creates time pressure that fires regardless of player action. No mechanical failures. One patch needed (board game degree table).**

# TEST S-09: HYBRID PHASE CONFLICT — ORDER INVALIDATION

**Setup:** Hybrid mode. Season 4. RS 52 (Fragile).

**Personal Phase (TTRPG):** A PC practitioner performs Territorial-scale Weaving to stabilise Eisengrund (Varfell territory). Success. RS +1. Over-actualisation: +1 Ob to subsequent ops on Eisengrund's configurational stability + Diagnosis +1 Ob in Eisengrund.

**Strategic Phase (board game):** Varfell player (same PC) had placed a Mend order on a Standard Gap in Eisengrund before the Personal Phase.

**The conflict:** The Personal Phase Weaving changed the territory's configurational state. The over-actualisation makes all subsequent Thread operations in Eisengrund +1 Ob. The Strategic Phase Mend order was placed BEFORE the Personal Phase (orders are placed face-down in Planning phase, which precedes both Personal and Strategic phases in hybrid).

**ISSUE FOUND: Hybrid order timing.** In hybrid mode, the session structure is: Personal Phase → Strategic Phase → Cascade Phase. But board game orders are placed during Planning (before Personal Phase). The orders were set based on the board state BEFORE the Personal Phase changed it. By the time Strategic Phase resolves, the state has changed.

**Resolution options:**
(a) **Orders are binding.** The player placed the order based on pre-Personal-Phase state. The order resolves as placed, but at the modified Ob (the territory's state has changed). The +1 Ob from over-actualisation applies because it's a real configurational change, regardless of when the order was placed.
(b) **Orders can be revised.** After Personal Phase, players may swap one order (representing adaptive leadership). This adds a mini-phase between Personal and Strategic.
(c) **Orders resolve against current state.** No revision, but modifiers from Personal Phase apply. This is option (a) restated.

**Resolution:** Option (a)/(c). Orders are binding. The Strategic Phase resolves against the current state, which includes Personal Phase changes. The Mend order now faces +1 Ob from the over-actualisation. The player made a strategic error by Weaving the same territory they planned to Mend — the over-actualisation made their own Mend harder. **This is correct. It teaches players that TTRPG actions in Personal Phase have Strategic Phase consequences. The two phases are not independent — they interact through the game state.**

But wait — did the player know this would happen? The over-actualisation hazard is a new mechanic. The player may not have anticipated that a successful Territorial Weaving would penalise their own Mend order. The first time this happens, it should be a learning moment, not a gotcha.

**FINDING: No mechanical failure. The system correctly penalises the player for not anticipating cross-phase interactions. GM guidance note recommended: during Session Zero, explain that Personal Phase Thread operations can affect Strategic Phase order resolution through territory state changes.**

# TEST S-10: THE DEVOUT CASCADE

**Setup:** Himlensendt (Church leader NPC, TS 0, Devout Constraint active, Certainty 6/max 7, Spirit 5, 2 Dissonance Marks) is present in Himmelstift when a collective Thread operation occurs at Relational scale. A Gap exists in an adjacent territory. A seasonal Event Card ("Thread Tremor") fires simultaneously, adding +2 RS degradation and +1 Ob to all orders in a random territory.

**Layer 1: Discovery Event trigger.**
- Himlensendt witnesses a Relational-scale Thread operation. This is a qualifying event for TS growth (witnessing a Thread operation at close range).
- Himlensendt is Devout. Per Devout Constraint: "Discovery Events bypass this for the initial check (the experience is involuntary)."
- Spirit check TN 7 Ob 1. Spirit 5: 5 dice. Expected ~1.5 successes. Likely success.
- Success: +5 TS. Himlensendt now has TS 5 (from 0). Still Inert range (0-9).

**Layer 2: Theological Dissonance Event (immediate).**
- After a Devout character's successful Discovery Event: Spirit TN 7 Ob 1.
- Spirit 5: same roll profile. Say success: framework holds. Devout Constraint re-engages. Certainty −1. Certainty: 6 → 5.
- Himlensendt now has 2 Dissonance Marks (unchanged — success means no new Mark).
- **What if Failure?** Framework cracks. +1 Dissonance Mark → 3 total. **At 3 Dissonance Marks: Devout Constraint collapses regardless of Belief status.** Belief must be revised or lost.

**Layer 2b: Assume Failure on Theological Dissonance.**
- 3 Dissonance Marks. Devout Constraint collapses. Certainty −2. Certainty: 6 → 4.
- Himlensendt's essentialist theological Belief ("The works of Solmund are divine truth; the Thread is heresy") must be revised or lost.
- **This is the leader of the Church losing their theological framework.** The institutional consequences are enormous.
- Can Himlensendt attempt TS growth now? The Devout Constraint has collapsed. They are no longer blocked from TS growth checks. But TS 5 is still Inert — they need qualifying events to grow further.

**Layer 3: Gap proximity.**
- Adjacent territory has a Gap. Himlensendt is not in that territory (they're in Himmelstift). Does Gap proximity affect them? Per Gap rules: "Certainty −1 per scene near a Gap (no Spirit check to resist)." Adjacent territory is not "near" — you need to be in the same territory or scene. Himlensendt is not affected by the adjacent Gap. Correct.

**Layer 4: Thread Tremor event card.**
- RS −2 (was part of the Accounting, but narratively fires during the scene). One random territory: all orders +1 Ob.
- If the random territory is Himmelstift: TC implications. The Thread Tremor in the Cathedral city is visible evidence of Thread instability. Non-practitioners in Himmelstift experience rendering failures (RS is Fragile). Himlensendt, with their newly-collapsed Devout Constraint and TS 5, might perceive... something. At TS 5 (Inert): no Thread perception. They perceive nothing Thread-specific. But they DO experience the rendering failure as a non-practitioner: déjà vu, objects in wrong places, a sense of wrongness.
- **Himlensendt's Certainty is already at 4.** The rendering failure in Himmelstift: is this a Certainty check? Per RS Fractured effects (if RS is in Fractured range): "Non-practitioners experience rendering failures — inconsistent memories, déjà vu, objects in wrong places." But RS is Fragile (52 before Thread Tremor, 50 after). At Fragile: "Occasional wrongness in territories with Thread history." Not a Certainty check trigger, just narrative description.

**Layer 5: Institutional cascade.**
- Himlensendt's Devout Constraint collapse is a private psychological event. Nobody else knows yet. But mechanically, the Church leader's theological framework has cracked. When does this become public?
- If Himlensendt's Belief is revised: the revision affects how they make Domain Actions. Church Domain Actions guided by essentialist theology ("heresy must be suppressed") may shift. Himlensendt might hesitate on Excommunication orders. Inquisitor dispatches might slow.
- Per faction card design (G-020/G-032): Institutional Tendency vs. Leadership Deviation. The Church's institutional tendency is to suppress heresy. Himlensendt's deviation from that tendency (post-Constraint-collapse) produces friction. Domain Actions that align with the old orthodoxy: +1 Ob for Himlensendt (they no longer fully believe). Domain Actions that deviate: Stability check for the Church (the institution resists the leader's new direction).
- **This is the TC clock's hidden engine.** TC tracks Church authority externally. Himlensendt's internal collapse is the mirror: the leader's faith failing while the institution continues. The TC number doesn't reflect this — it measures external perception, not internal belief. The Church can continue seizing territories while its leader privately doubts.

**ISSUE: Does Devout Constraint collapse for an NPC faction leader trigger any mechanical change to the faction, or is it purely narrative?**

**Resolution:** It IS narrative, but narrative drives mechanics through the Leadership Deviation system. The GM begins playing Himlensendt as a leader whose actions are no longer fully aligned with the Church's institutional tendency. This manifests as: Domain Echoes from Himlensendt are slightly off — the Church's public position and its leader's private actions diverge. PCs who interact with Himlensendt may notice the shift (social perception rolls). The TC clock continues advancing from institutional momentum regardless of Himlensendt's personal state. **No mechanical patch needed — the existing Institutional Tendency / Leadership Deviation framework handles this. But GM guidance should note: NPC Devout Constraint collapse is a major narrative inflection point that changes how the GM plays that NPC.**

**FINDING: Devout Constraint collapse cascade is handled by existing systems. No mechanical failures. The cascade from Discovery Event → Theological Dissonance → Constraint Collapse → Belief revision → Leadership Deviation is a legitimate emergent narrative arc that the mechanics generate without contradiction. The TC clock's independence from the leader's personal faith is philosophically correct — institutions persist beyond individual belief.**

# TEST S-11: THE RESIDUE ADDICT — FULL CAMPAIGN ARC

**Setup:** Track a practitioner who uses dissolution residue at every opportunity across a 30-session campaign. Starting stats: TS 45, Coherence 10, Spirit 4, Focus 3, Attunement 4. They use residue (Potency 2-3) in every Relational+ operation. Goal: determine when the system renders them non-functional vs. the old Taint track.

**Old system comparison:** Taint 0-10. +1 Taint per residue use. Effects at Taint 1-3 (minor), 4-6 (moderate), 7-8 (severe), 9 (choice point), 10 (NPC). A practitioner using residue 10 times across a campaign would hit Taint 10 — full transformation.

**New system: Coherence 10→0 with residue as −1 additional per use.**

**Campaign tracking (30 sessions, ~10 seasons):**

| Session Block | Operations | Residue Uses | Coherence Impact | Running Coherence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sessions 1-3 (Season 1) | 2 Relational Weavings | 2 uses | −2 (scale) + −2 (residue) = −4 | 10 → 6 |
| Sessions 4-6 (Season 2) | 1 Territorial Weaving, 1 Relational Pull | 2 uses | −2 (scale) + −2 (residue) = −4 | 6 → 2 |

**The practitioner hits Coherence 2 (Fractured) by session 6.** In the old system, they'd be at Taint 4 (moderate effects: +2D Thread, −1D social). In the new system, they're at Fractured: −2D social, −2D Memory, Belief Co-Authorship, dissociative episodes, +1 Ob on all Thread operations.

**ISSUE: The new system is significantly harsher than the old Taint track for residue users.** Old Taint 4 = mild social penalty + Thread bonus. New Coherence 2 = severe penalties across the board + no Thread bonus.

**Wait — dissolution residue still provides bonus dice.** Potency 2-3 = +2-3 dice that explode on 9-10. The bonus is in the operation pool, not in a track modifier. So the practitioner at Coherence 2 gets: +2-3 bonus dice per use, but +1 Ob from Fragmented state, −2D to social. The bonus dice partially offset the Ob increase. But at Coherence 2, the practitioner also has Belief Co-Authorship — the GM is rewriting their Beliefs.

**Is the acceleration too fast?** 4 residue uses in 6 sessions → Fractured. The old system would have them at Taint 4 after 4 uses — uncomfortable but functional. The new system has them at Fractured after the SAME 4 uses — barely functional.

**Root cause:** In the old system, Taint was a SEPARATE track from ThS. Taint accumulated from residue. ThS accumulated from operations. They didn't stack. A practitioner could use residue heavily (high Taint, low ThS) or operate heavily without residue (low Taint, high ThS). The tracks were independent decay paths.

In the new system, both sources feed into Coherence. Residue use AND operation scale BOTH drain the same pool. This is philosophically correct — both are expressions of the same process (repeated suspension of rendering, with residue intensifying the exposure). But mechanically, it means residue users are penalised much more severely because they're draining a pool that's already being drained by normal operations.

**Is this intended?** The user cut Taint and said residue is just Coherence loss. The implication is: residue use should be MORE dangerous, not a parallel track with its own progression. The old Taint track gave residue users BENEFITS (+1D to +3D to Thread operations) that partially compensated for the penalties. The new system gives the bonus dice but no protective benefit track — it's pure acceleration of the same degradation that normal operations cause.

**Assessment:** This IS correct. Residue use should be a genuine sacrifice — you get bonus dice for this operation at the cost of permanent Coherence loss. The old system's "Taint as a power track" framing was philosophically wrong (it implied a separate transformation mechanic, which the Foundations reject). The new system's unified Coherence track means residue use accelerates you toward the same crisis that normal practice produces, just faster. The practitioner must choose: do I need +3 dice RIGHT NOW badly enough to lose 10% of my remaining Coherence?

**But the campaign math says the residue-heavy practitioner is non-functional by mid-campaign.** Is there a viable residue-use pattern that doesn't crater?

**Conservative residue use:** 1 use per season (10 uses across the campaign). Each costs −1 Coherence (residue) + −1 (operation scale, assuming Relational). = −2 per use from residue-accompanied operations, but the −1 for scale would have applied anyway. Net additional from residue: −1 per use × 10 = −10 additional across the campaign.

Without residue: 10 Relational operations = −10. With recovery (3 seasons): +3. Final: Coherence 3 (Fragmented).

With residue: 10 Relational operations = −10 (scale) + −10 (residue) = −20. Impossible — Coherence can't go below 0. They'd hit 0 around operation 5 (after which they're in Rendering Crisis and can't operate). With recovery (2 seasons between heavy use): +2. They'd hit 0 around operation 6-7.

**FINDING: The new system makes habitual residue use a 6-7 operation career. The practitioner can use residue ~6 times across a campaign before hitting Rendering Crisis (assuming they also do the operations without residue). This is dramatically shorter than the old Taint track (which allowed ~9 uses before transformation). But the old track also had a Taint 9 "choice point" where the player could accept correction or continue. The new system has Coherence 2 (Belief Co-Authorship) and 0 (Rendering Crisis / NPC) as the equivalent inflection points.**

**Is 6-7 uses enough for dramatic content?** Yes. Each use is a genuine, weighty decision. The player is choosing to sacrifice 10-15% of their remaining Coherence for a momentary advantage. This should feel like reaching for a loaded gun — powerful but devastating. The old system's 9-use runway made residue feel like a slow corruption mechanic. The new system makes it feel like a cliff.

**FINDING: No patch needed. The acceleration is intended. The system is correctly brutal toward habitual residue use. 6-7 uses across a campaign is the residue-use budget. Each use is a meaningful sacrifice. Document this in GM guidance: "Dissolution residue is not a power progression. It is an emergency measure. Each use permanently narrows the practitioner's remaining career."**

**D. Church practitioner Thread operation (Weave to reinforce walls):**

**ISSUE FOUND: Opposing simultaneous Thread operations.** Two practitioners in contact, targeting the same configuration with opposite intentionalities (one Pulling, one Weaving). What happens?

**Resolution options:**
(a) **Opposed roll.** Both roll their pools. Higher net successes "wins." The losing operation fails with snap-back/collapse consequences.
(b) **Sequential resolution.** The operation that resolves first sets the configuration's state. The second operation faces modified Ob (if the first succeeded, the second is working against a changed state).
(c) **Configuration conflict.** Both operations partially succeed. The thread is being pulled in two directions simultaneously. The configuration becomes a Shifting Object (oscillating between states). Both practitioners take Coherence damage from the conflicted interaction.

**Option (c) is the most philosophically correct.** Two configurations in originary intentionality, directing opposite outcomes on the same thread, simultaneously. Neither can "win" cleanly because both are operating beyond rendering — they can't perceive each other's intentionality during contact. The thread receives contradictory direction. It splits. Both operations partially succeed and the thread oscillates.

**Proposed resolution for opposing simultaneous operations:**
1. Both practitioners roll their pools against their respective Obs.
2. If both succeed: the thread becomes a Shifting Object at its scale. Both operations produce partial effects. Both practitioners: Coherence −1 additional (conflicted interaction). The Shifting Object persists until Mended or until one practitioner re-operates without opposition.
3. If one succeeds and one fails: the successful operation resolves normally. The failing practitioner takes standard failure consequences PLUS +1 Coherence loss (their configuration encountered unexpected resistance from the opposing intentionality).
4. If both fail: the thread is destabilised. Shifting Object forms. Both take failure consequences. RS −2 (substrate strained by contradictory forces).

## T11: INQUISITOR CE ARC → CRISIS OF FAITH

### Mechanic IDs: M-80, M-28 Devout Constraint, M-27 TS growth, CE track

### Scenario
Valdis (CE 1) is 18 months into the Maret investigation. He has built Stage 1 of his file (Church Reach 5D vs Ob 3: verified prior session). Now he is assigned to execute Stage 2: Formal Accusation. Before the accusation can be filed, he must review physical evidence from the Maret scene at the Niflhel warehouse — a site where a Mode 1 FR Dissolution occurred. He also receives a dissolution residue object logged as evidence: an artefact that reacted to the Dissolution.

### Initial State
```
Valdis — Cog4, End4, Pre4, Spi4
  Combat Pool: 10D; Debate Pool: 10D
  CE: 1 (interrogated Maret's associate, passive TS exposure)
  Certainty: 5
  Devout Constraint: active (1 Dissonance Mark)
  TS: 5 (Dormant — involuntary from prior Discovery Event)
  Belief: "The Arrogance is the origin of the Calamity. I will excise it from Valoria."

Tracks
  TC: 17 | TT: 31 | IP: 20
```

### Firing Sequence

**Step 1 — Review physical evidence from a Thread operation site (+1 CE):**
Valdis handles case records from the warehouse. Classified forensic descriptions: objects that moved against their natural configuration, a witness testimony about "wrongness in the air," one partially dissolved artefact catalogued.
CE: 1→2.

At CE 2: no threshold effects yet. But Devout Constraint check — sustained evidence review is a qualifying confrontation event. Spirit TN7 Ob1:

*Valdis Spirit roll:* 4D TN7 Ob1: 9✓,7✓,3,4 = 2. Success. Framework holds. No Dissonance Mark from this pass. Certainty −0 (Spirit success means no Certainty cost; the Devout bonus (§C6) doesn't apply here — this is an evidentiary confrontation, not a monstrous entity encounter).

**RULING CHECK:** Devout bonus (+2D) specifically applies to monstrous entity encounters, not all CE events. Evidence review is not a monstrous encounter. Confirmed — no bonus. Spirit 4D stands.

**Step 2 — Handling originary lock (dissolution residue object) as evidence (+2 CE):**
The artefact is bagged and tagged. Standard procedure: Valdis opens the evidence container to inspect it. The object is a dissolution residue class 2 (partial Dissolution, object partially reconfigured). Per §5.11: "Inquisitors with CE 2+: feel wrongness near residue even without TS."

CE is now at 2 before this step. After handling: CE 2+2 = **CE 4.**

**CE 4 exceeds threshold (CE 3):**
Threshold trigger fires immediately: TS growth check — Cognition TN7, Ob2 (Devout formation raises Ob).

*Valdis TS growth check:* Cog4D TN7 Ob2: 8✓,3,7✓,4 = 2. Net 2. Success (exactly Ob2). TS: 5+5 = **TS 10.** Dormant tier (5–29).

**Immediate consequence:** The felt sense upon handling the residue was not like the theological descriptions of the Arrogance. It was not alien. It was familiar — a wrongness that felt like something *remembered*. This is the **crisis of faith trigger.**

**Theological Dissonance Event (immediate, involuntary TS gain at CE 3+):**
Spirit TN7 Ob1: 4D: 9✓,4,3,7✓ = 2. Success. Framework holds — Valdis interprets the felt wrongness as evidence of the Arrogance's proximity, not as evidence that the Arrogance framework is wrong. **Doubling Down trajectory** begins.
Certainty −1 (4→3... wait — Certainty loss triggers on failed Theological Dissonance check. Success means no loss). Actually re-reading §C6: on Dissonance check Success: "framework holds, Certainty −1." On Failure: "Dissonance Mark, Certainty −2."

Success → Certainty 5→4. No Dissonance Mark.

**Dissonance Mark count:** 1 (from prior session). Still 1. Constraint intact.

**Step 3 — Stage 2: Formal Accusation roll:**
Church Reach 5D TN7 vs Ob4 (requires completed File):

*Roll:* 5D TN7 Ob4: 9✓,8✓,3,7✓,1 = 3, −1 = 2. Failure (net 2 < Ob4).

**Failure on Stage 2:** File incomplete — the forensic evidence is contested by the Parliament legal office (Hafenmark-appointed. Baralta's proxy objection to Inquisitor jurisdiction is the obstacle). The Accusation is suspended. Church Stability check Ob1 at accounting (procedural failure).

The file failure is not Valdis's personal failure — it is an institutional setback. But it means Valdis must return to investigate. He will encounter Maret again.

**Step 4 — Extended interrogation of Maret (hypothetical Stage 2 retry):**
Maret is TS 66 — Attuned tier. Extended interrogation of an Attuned-tier practitioner: +3 CE.

CE 4 + 3 = **CE 7.** This exceeds any listed threshold. The table runs to CE 3+ only. 

**RULING NEEDED (R-T11-A):** CE track cap: The table lists events up to "Extended interrogation of Resonant-tier practitioner: +3 CE." No CE maximum is stated. CE is effectively an event accumulation counter. Beyond CE 3, no new threshold events are listed — the trajectory (Doubling Down, Fracture, Conversion) was already determined at CE 3. CE beyond 3 represents deepening exposure but does not retrigger the growth check. The trajectory's consequences unfold through narrative, not further mechanic triggers.

**Valdis trajectory: Doubling Down (confirmed at CE 3):**
- He interprets the felt wrongness as evidence of the Arrogance's proximity.
- He becomes more effective as an investigator because he is now a low-sensitivity Thread-detector without knowing it.
- TC: +1 (per §9.3 Inquisitor trajectory: Doubling Down → TC +1 at accounting).
- TC: 17→18.

**Devout Constraint status:** Active (1 Dissonance Mark). He remains functional. But his TS is now 10 (Dormant). He can perform passive perception in TS 10–29 range: "sense wrongness near Dissolution residue automatically." He has been doing this. He does not recognise it as Thread sensitivity — he reads it as spiritual discernment granted by Galbados.

**P-08 COMPLIANCE CHECK (Epistemological Barrier):** Valdis's interpretation of his felt wrongness as divine discernment rather than Thread sensitivity directly expresses P-08. The barrier is maintained by his Devout formation even as his TS climbs. CLEAN.

**P-03 COMPLIANCE CHECK (Rendering = consciousness):** Valdis's increasing TS means the Thread reality is incrementally rendering to him. His essentialist framework is a rendering-side interpretation of the same phenomenon. CLEAN — the system correctly shows that rendering doesn't guarantee accurate interpretation.

### Stress Test Dimensions

**Boundary:** CE threshold at 3 → TS growth check fires correctly. Ob 2 (not 1) due to Devout formation. Fires once, not repeatedly.
**Cascade:** CE accumulation does not cascade infinitely. Growth check fires once at threshold; trajectory locks; no further mechanics trigger.
**Deadlock:** Valdis cannot advance his file (Stage 2 failed). But he has narrative avenues: retry via Parliament appeal, or redirect the investigation. No deadlock.
**Optimal play:** "Run Inquisitors at low CE forever" — not viable. Investigating practitioners always accumulates CE. The Doubling Down trajectory creates a dangerous NPC who becomes more functionally effective but is headed toward crisis.
**Crunch load:** CE tracking requires 1 number per Inquisitor NPC. One decision point at CE 3. Low cognitive load. ✓

### Findings

**RULING CONFIRMED (R-T11-A):** CE tracks accumulation events. Threshold at CE 3 fires once. No retrigger above 3. Trajectory determines subsequent arc through narrative consequence.

**RULING NEEDED (R-T11-B):** Does Doubling Down trajectory grant a mechanical bonus to Inquisitor investigation rolls? §9.3 says "becomes functionally a low-sensitivity Thread-detector." This means: in scenes where the Inquisitor is searching for Thread evidence, the GM applies +1D to their Cognition (investigation) rolls, as if they had a practical but unrecognised tool. This is the mechanical expression of the Doubling Down trajectory and should be codified.

**Design concern (P2):** The Inquisitor CE track only has one threshold (CE 3). A very active Inquisitor might hit CE 7–10 without any additional mechanical framework. The trajectory labels (Doubling Down/Fracture/Conversion) describe arc direction but have no stat changes beyond the initial TS growth. This is probably intentional — the arc unfolds through narrative, not mechanics — but should be confirmed in GM guidelines.

**VERDICT:** CLEAN. CE track functions correctly. Devout Constraint correctly interacts with TS growth at CE threshold. The theological interpretation of Thread sensitivity (P-08) is modelled accurately. One ruling codified (R-T11-A). One codification needed (R-T11-B).

**P-09 COMPLIANCE:** The "messiness" of the extraction — the informant who spotted Ryx, the network damage, the political exposure — all correctly express P-09's principle that covert operations leave traces that cannot be fully controlled. CLEAN.

**VERDICT:** CLEAN. DD track functions correctly. Circles network damage accumulates with correct granularity. Two rulings codified.

## T13: EHRENWALL COUP THRESHOLD ASSESSMENT

### Mechanic IDs: M-76 Coup trigger, M-30 Beliefs, M-50 Leader deviation

### Scenario
Ehrenwall (Grandmaster, Löwenritter) has observed: Crown signs a treaty with Church granting Inquisitor jurisdiction in Löwenritter-held Ehrenfeld (Fortification 3). Crown Mandate has declined to 3. Torben Loyalty is 6 (Adapting). IP = 48. This scene tests whether Ehrenwall's Belief crosses the coup threshold.

### Ehrenwall's Belief Structure
**Active Belief:** "The Crown is the institution through which Valoria governs itself. I will protect that institution even at personal cost."

**Secondary Belief:** "No foreign authority or Church body supersedes the Crown's military command within Valoria's borders. I will enforce this principle."

**The friction:** The Crown just granted Church jurisdiction within Löwenritter-held territory. This directly contradicts Ehrenwall's Secondary Belief. Does this constitute institutional failure warranting a coup assessment?

### Firing Sequence

**Step 1 — Coup trigger mechanics (§8.9 Löwenritter):**
Per the ruleset: Ehrenwall "has not yet concluded the Crown has failed the nation. When she does, she will act without hesitation."

Coup triggers are tied to Torben Loyalty and IP thresholds. Current state: Loyalty 6 (no coup trigger 2 at ≥6; trigger 2 fires at Loyalty ≤4). IP 48 (Hostile tier: IP 40–59 = Löwenritter prepares). 

**Current trigger status:**
- Coup Trigger 1: IP ≥ 65 AND no active defense preparation → NOT met (IP 48 < 65).
- Coup Trigger 2: Loyalty ≤ 4 → NOT met (Loyalty 6).
- Coup Trigger 3: TC ≥ 80 AND Church holds Crown territories → NOT met (TC 17).

**Conclusion:** No coup fires mechanically. But does the Crown-Church treaty constitute a **Belief contradiction event** for Ehrenwall?

**Step 2 — Belief contradiction (Leader Deviation):**
Ehrenwall's institution (Löwenritter) has a default: "protect Crown sovereignty." The Crown has acted against Löwenritter institutional interest (granting Church jurisdiction in Ehrenfeld). This is **Leadership Deviation from the Crown's perspective** (Crown deviated from virtue ethics — Courage axis: failing to defend Crown military sovereignty).

For Ehrenwall, this is a **Belief challenged** event:
- Her Primary Belief ("protect the institution") is being tested — is an institution that surrenders military sovereignty worth protecting?
- CP awarded: +1 for engaging the challenge.

**Step 3 — Ehrenwall's decision:**
Ehrenwall does NOT revise her Primary Belief (she's not convinced the Crown is fully lost yet — one treaty is insufficient). She holds.

But she issues a private ultimatum to Almud: the Löwenritter will not honour Church jurisdiction in Ehrenfeld regardless of the treaty's formal status. This is a **Leader Deviation** for the Löwenritter institution.

**Leader Deviation procedure:**
Stability check at seasonal accounting. Ehrenwall's Löwenritter: Stability 5. Ob for deviation from default (refusing to implement a Crown directive): Ob 2.

*Stability roll (Löwenritter):* Stability 5D TN7 Ob2: 9✓,3,7✓,4,8✓ = 3. Success. Löwenritter Stability holds. Deviation cost: minimal. The institution absorbs her position without fracture.

**Step 4 — Almud's response:**
Crown receives Ehrenwall's ultimatum. Almud's options:
a) Capitulate: rescind the Church jurisdiction clause. TC consequence (Church views this as breach of agreement: TC +1). Crown Mandate +1 (public display of independence from Church pressure — virtue ethics aligned: −0 Ob bonus on next Domain Action).
b) Enforce: send Crown military to Ehrenfeld to enforce Church jurisdiction. Löwenritter: Stability check Ob 3 (direct threat to institutional sovereignty). Probable Stability −1 or worse. Coup trigger 2 window opens faster.
c) Negotiate: Offer Ehrenwall a compromise (Inquisitor access limited to specific buildings, not military compounds). Ob 2 Influence vs Ehrenwall (Disposition: Cool, Ob 3).

**P-01 COMPLIANCE:** The inseparability principle applied institutionally — Crown's compromise with Church produces an equal-magnitude pull from Löwenritter. Every political concession creates a counter-force. CLEAN.

### Stress Test Dimensions

**Boundary:** Coup trigger thresholds are clear. No trigger fires here because numeric conditions aren't met. The Belief system correctly creates narrative pressure short of mechanical threshold.

**Cascade:** If Almud enforces (option b): Löwenritter Stability −1 (5→4), which approaches the coup trigger proximity window, and Ehrenwall's Belief is further tested. This is correct escalation, not cascade.

**Deadlock:** None. All three of Almud's options are viable.

**Optimal play:** Players observing this will understand they must prevent any two coup triggers from approaching simultaneously. The mechanical coupling between Loyalty, IP, and TC creates a multi-front management problem. This is correct design — the Löwenritter threat should feel structurally present even when no single trigger is near.

**VERDICT:** CLEAN. Coup triggers correctly gate to numeric thresholds. Belief contradiction + Leader Deviation creates organic political pressure below the threshold. No rulings needed — existing procedures handle this precisely.

## Pre-History

**Pre-Collapse Era (date unknown)**
The Einhir civilisation establishes the Southernmost site-network — a coordinated system of practitioners maintaining thread-configurations across the peninsula. For generations, they perform sustained Weaving that actively stabilises the world's ontical base.

**The Deterioration (~generation before collapse)**
The Southernmost's thread-configuration begins exhibiting near-unintelligible behaviour. The Einhir recognise this as systemic degradation and plan a foundational-scale response: a coordinated working across the full site-network involving every significant practitioner on the peninsula.

**The Einhir Catastrophe (~250 years before 0 AG)**
The mass working fails catastrophically. At the moment of maximum actualization pressure, the configuration inverts — converting the entire Weaving output toward maximum potential rather than actualization. Every thread the network had built releases simultaneously. Gaps of unprecedented scale open across the peninsula. Through these Gaps, Galbados emerges — a fully configured being from the unintelligible ground, organised and self-aware. The Einhir civilisation collapses. The Locked Zones form. The Forgetting begins.

The mechanism is entirely rendered-side: the Einhir over-drew the threads, and the structural limits of the substrate were exceeded. The Ein Sof's surfeit of positive being flooded through the breach. What came through is from the ground, but the tear itself is explained by the rendered world's actions on its own substrate.

**Galbados's Presence (~250 to ~30 before 0 AG)**
Galbados exists in the rendered world as a threadcut being of the third mode: radically present without organic becoming, maintaining his rendering through continuous Thread work, existing through existential-essential excess. He does not found the Church — he serves as a catalyst around which institutional formation occurs over centuries. The human interpretive community builds the theology; Galbados's teachings are filtered through followers' perceptual capacities. The perceptual prophylaxis (essentialist theology that forecloses Thread sensitivity) is emergent, not designed.

**Galbados's Dissolution (0 AG)**
Galbados dissolves. The circumstances are theologically interpreted but not empirically understood. The Church's institutional maturation accelerates in the centuries following — the dissolution is sufficiently traumatic to generate a sustained interpretive tradition.

## Post-Dissolution Era

**0-200 AG (approximate): Church Institutional Maturation**
The Church of Galbados develops from catalyst-generated movement to mature institutional apparatus over approximately two centuries. Cardinals, diocesan structure, Knights Templar, educational monopoly, and theological orthodoxy all emerge during this period. The Church's relationship with secular governance is initially cooperative — both Crown and Church benefit from post-Catastrophe stability.

---

## The Secession Wars and Settlement

**~Year -10 to 0 AG (approximate, pre-game-era reckoning)**
Altonia controls the Valorian peninsula as a colonial territory. The Secession Wars are fought to achieve Valorian independence. The wars end with a settlement that establishes:
- The Valorian Crown as sovereign authority
- The Church of Galbados as the peninsula's dominant religious institution (carried over from Altonian period)
- The suppression of Einhir cultural practices as part of the post-war political bargain
- The informal social caste system relegating southern Einhir to second-class status
- The Löwenritter as the Crown's standing military order

**Year 0 AG (game-era reckoning): Post-War Settlement**
The AG calendar as used in the game timeline begins here. Almud Almqvist's predecessor (or early-reign Almud) governs the newly independent Valoria.

| ~15-18 AG | **The hunting accident (~18 AG).** A key political figure is killed in circumstances officially ruled accidental. It is an open secret that this was an assassination. The perpetrator is undefined — a campaign revelation candidate. [EDITORIAL E-01: perpetrator TBD.] |

Economy stabilises. The Church objects publicly; its Altonian missionaries quietly benefit from normalised movement. The Crown knows. The Church knows the Crown knows. Neither raises it. Schoenland profits enormously. |

| 38 AG | **Direct militarised trade routes opened between Valoria and Altonia.** Ratified by political marriage: Almud's daughter Elske marries an Altonian Duke holding border territory adjacent to Valoria. The marriage is a de facto security arrangement as much as a trade ratification. Elske lives in Altonia. Schoenland is partially cut out — they pivot to arms sales and deliberate tension-stoking, profiting from sustained feuding while preventing resolution. |
| 38-45 AG | **Uneasy direct relationship.** Official trade routes operate under military escort — functional but visibly armed. Altonia's Church minority continues growing. Altonian authorities watch Church clergy more closely. Schoenland operates in the grey space the official routes won't touch, selling arms and stoking grievances on both sides. Prince Torben remains in Royal Court. Altonia begins eyeing him as leverage. |

### Schoenland
Not a faction — a spoiler actor. Schoenland is a neutral trade port that profits from tension. At game start: pro-war in terms of arms sales, anti-conquest because they benefit from sustained feuding. They actively work to keep tensions alive without triggering resolution. Mechanically: Schoenland appears in territory 15 (Schoenland trade port) and modifies Trade orders. Schoenland's actions are GM-driven, not player-controlled.

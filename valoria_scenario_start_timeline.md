## Canonical Timeline: Pre-Game History

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Pre-AG | Altonian colonial rule. Einhir sites destroyed. The Forgetting takes hold. The Church of Galbados organises under Altonian rule — predates independence, congregations on both sides of what will become the border. Tolerated by Altonia as a legal minority: watched, not suppressed. |
| Year 0 AG | **The Secession begins.** Three ducal coalitions break simultaneously. The Church positions above the conflict — ministers to both sides, handles prisoners, mediates truces. Schoenland trades with both sides throughout. |
| 0–10 AG | **The Secession Wars.** A decade of grinding conflict. Mass casualties accumulate TT. The Church emerges more powerful than any faction — it held when everything else broke. Schoenland emerges commercially indispensable to everyone. |
| 10 AG | **Wars end.** Altonia withdraws. IP clock starts at ~20. Peninsula sovereign, economically wrecked, institutionally fragile. Church cross-border presence now a structural fact. |
| 10–20 AG | **Post-war collapse decade.** Crown economy falters. Church fills governance vacuum — dispute resolution, poor relief, record-keeping. By 20 AG structurally indispensable. Characters now ~65+ have direct war memories; ~55+ remember this decade as genuine hardship. |
| ~18 AG | **The hunting accident.** The reigning monarch dies during a hunt on Valorian lands attended by local and foreign dignitaries. Officially ruled an accident. Privately, most political actors believe it was arranged — an open secret 27 years later. Almud, mid-20s, inherits the succession. A small number privately suspect Almud himself arranged it. He suspects foul play but has no proof and cannot identify who. |
| ~18–20 AG | **Almud consolidates power.** Takes the throne of a broke kingdom with a Church that spent the post-war decade becoming indispensable, and a predecessor's death he cannot resolve. His early reign is defined by economic survival and political stabilisation. |
| 20–35 AG | **Almud opens trade via Schoenland.** Politically toxic domestically — Church, war veterans, and nationalists all object. Goods move through Schoenland's ports as a fig leaf. Economy stabilises. The Church objects publicly; its Altonian missionaries quietly benefit from normalised movement. The Crown knows. The Church knows the Crown knows. Neither raises it. Schoenland profits enormously. |
| 38 AG | **Direct militarised trade routes opened between Valoria and Altonia.** Ratified by political marriage: Almud's daughter, a Valorian royal princess, marries an Altonian prince — not in direct line of succession, but holding border territory adjacent to Valoria. The marriage is a de facto security arrangement as much as a trade ratification. The princess lives in Altonia. Publicly the marriage is warm; privately, something else. Seven years in at game start, no children — a growing diplomatic problem for both crowns. **Schoenland is partially cut out.** They pivot: pro-war in terms of arms sales, anti-conquest because they benefit from sustained feuding. They actively work to keep tensions alive without triggering resolution. |
| 38–45 AG | **Uneasy direct relationship.** Official trade routes operate under military escort — functional but visibly armed. Altonia's Church minority continues growing. Altonian authorities watch Church clergy more closely. The childless marriage erodes the treaty's long-term value. Schoenland operates in the grey space the official routes won't touch, selling arms and stoking grievances on both sides. |
| **45 AG — Game start** | TT: 28 (Stirring). TC: 0. IP: 20. Parliament Integrity: 7. Almud: early 50s, ~27 years on the throne. The princess is in Altonia, seven years married, no heirs. Schoenland is a spoiler actor. The hunting accident of ~18 AG is an open secret with no resolution. The Southernmost is unexamined folklore. |

## Key Actors at Game Start

**Almud (Crown)**
- Early 50s. ~27 years on the throne.
- Inherited after the hunting accident of ~18 AG, mid-20s at the time.
- Suspects the accident was arranged. Has no proof. Cannot identify the perpetrator.
- A small number of political actors suspect Almud himself arranged it.
- Reopened Altonian trade via Schoenland (20–35 AG) despite domestic opposition.
- Ratified direct trade with Altonia via his daughter's political marriage (38 AG).

**The Princess (Crown/Altonia)**
- Almud's daughter. Married ~38 AG to an Altonian prince (border territory, not in direct succession line).
- Lives in Altonia. Seven years married. No children — a growing diplomatic problem.
- Publicly warm toward the marriage. Privately: something else (undefined).

**The Altonian Prince**
- Not in direct line of Altonian succession.
- Holds border territory adjacent to Valoria.
- The marriage is as much a security arrangement as a trade ratification.

**Schoenland**
- Genuinely independent small merchant state.
- Profited enormously from serving as Crown–Altonia trade proxy (20–35 AG).
- Partially cut out by the 38 AG direct trade agreement.
- Current posture: pro-war (arms sales), anti-conquest (benefits from sustained feuding).
- Actively works to keep Crown–Altonia tensions alive without triggering resolution.

## Church of Galbados — Cross-Border Status Notes

- Organised under Altonian colonial rule — predates Valorian independence.
- Operated on both sides during the Secession Wars without openly siding with either party.
- Converted wartime neutrality into post-war institutional dominance.
- Legal minority in Altonia — tolerated, watched, growing.
- For the Crown: simultaneously asset (soft power, potential intelligence network, administrative capacity), liability (independent political actor with supranational loyalty), and potential weapon (missionaries as network) depending on context.
- Church's high Influence (6) and Stability (5) reflect its longer institutional build than any other faction.
